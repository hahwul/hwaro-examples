There is meaning in the error. When a digital signal corrupts, it does not simply lose information -- it transforms it. The original intent collides with entropy, producing something neither the sender nor the medium intended.

This is what makes glitch art compelling. It occupies the space between control and chaos, between the designed and the accidental. The artist sets conditions. The machine produces the unexpected.

The viewer encounters something that feels simultaneously broken and intentional.
