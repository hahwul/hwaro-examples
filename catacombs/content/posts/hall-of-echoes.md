We found a chamber unlike any other. A vast hall where every sound echoes endlessly, layer upon layer, creating a symphony of whispers.

<!-- more -->

## The Discovery

The passage opened suddenly into a massive space. Our torches couldn't reach the ceiling—the darkness above was absolute. But when we spoke, our voices returned to us transformed.

One word became many. A single footstep became a rhythm. The chamber had perfect acoustics, designed—or perhaps naturally formed—to amplify and repeat every sound.

## The Acoustic Properties

We experimented carefully:

* A whisper traveled across the entire hall
* A shout created a cascade of echoes lasting nearly a minute
* Stone dropped on stone produced a bell-like tone that resonated through the space

Ancient architects must have known of this place. Perhaps it was used for ceremonies, for music, for communication across the vast underground network.

## What the Echoes Revealed

As we listened to our own sounds returning to us, we heard something else—sounds that weren't ours. Footsteps from another passage. Voices too distant to understand. Evidence that we are not alone down here.

### The Question

Are these echoes from other explorers, somewhere else in the catacombs? Or are they echoes from the past, sounds trapped in stone and released only when new vibrations awaken them?

We cannot say for certain. The catacombs hold many mysteries.

## Moving On

We marked the Hall of Echoes carefully on our map. We will return here—there is more to learn from this place. But for now, we follow the passages deeper, toward the sound of running water that grows louder with each step.

The underground river awaits.
