## The Nature of the Maze

The catacombs are not a simple network of tunnels. They are a labyrinth—a maze designed to confuse and disorient. At each intersection, we face choices:

* The left passage slopes downward, deeper into the earth
* The center passage continues level, parallel to the surface far above
* The right passage curves upward, perhaps toward an exit

But which is the right path? Or is there even a "right" path at all?

## Markers and Signs

Previous explorers left markers—scratches in the stone, piles of small rocks, symbols we're learning to recognize. Some warn of dead ends. Others mark paths that loop back on themselves.

> "In the darkness, trust the markers of those who came before." — Ancient inscription found on the walls

## Our Method

We've developed a system:

1. Mark each passage we explore with our own symbol
2. Create a map, though the scale is uncertain
3. Follow the current of air—somewhere, there must be ventilation
4. Listen for the sound of running water

The underground river we heard yesterday has become our guide. Water flows downward, always seeking the lowest point. If we follow it, we will eventually find... something.

## Tomorrow's Expedition

We've chosen the left passage. Deeper we go, following the sound of distant water, trusting our instincts and the wisdom of those who explored before us.

The torches burn low. We must rest before continuing.
