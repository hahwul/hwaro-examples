Carved into a wall at a major intersection, we found it—a compass made of bone fragments, pointing in eight directions.

<!-- more -->

## The Find

Not a compass in the modern sense, but a direction marker nonetheless. Eight passages radiate from this central chamber, and above each entrance, a symbol:

* The skull facing upward → ascent toward the surface
* The skull facing downward → descent to the deeper levels
* The twin skulls → passages that loop back to known territory
* The cracked skull → dangerous passages, unstable stone
* The hollow skull → passages leading to large chambers
* The crossed bones → passages that dead-end
* The spiral pattern → passages that wind in circles
* The unmarked passage → territory unexplored by those who carved these signs

## Decoding the System

This was a navigation system, developed by early explorers or perhaps by those who built the catacombs. Each symbol warns future travelers, preserves knowledge, prevents others from making the same mistakes.

We photographed every marking, documenting this ancient wisdom.

## The Deeper Implications

If such a sophisticated system exists, it means:

1. The catacombs were actively used for a long period
2. Many people passed through, enough to warrant permanent markers
3. There is structure to this apparent chaos
4. We are following paths walked thousands of times before

### Respect for the Ancients

We leave our own marks respectfully, adding to the record rather than defacing it. Future explorers will read our symbols as we read those of the past.

## Following the Hollow Skull

We chose the passage marked with the hollow skull—the sign promising large chambers. If the bone compass speaks true, we will find something significant.

Tomorrow, we venture deeper with new understanding. The catacombs are not random tunnels, but a designed space, purposeful and mapped by those long gone.

Their knowledge lights our way as surely as our torches.
