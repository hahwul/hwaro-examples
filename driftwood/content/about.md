## The Journal

Driftwood is a coastal photo journal dedicated to the quiet beauty of shorelines. Each entry documents a moment along the edge --- where land meets water, where light changes by the minute, where weather writes its own story on sand and stone.

## Approach

The images here are made slowly. Most are captured during the shoulder hours of early morning or late afternoon, when the coast is emptiest and the light most nuanced. Equipment varies, but the method stays consistent: arrive early, stay patient, respond to what the shore offers.

Subject matter ranges from broad seascapes to the smallest details --- the geometry of a tide pool, the texture of bleached wood, the way fog softens a headland into near-abstraction.

## About the Photographer

This journal is maintained by a single photographer working primarily along the northern Pacific coast. The images are presented without heavy processing. Color palettes tend toward the muted and desaturated, reflecting the coast as it actually appears on overcast days and in diffused maritime light.
