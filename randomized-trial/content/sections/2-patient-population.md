## 2.1 Eligibility criteria

Adults aged 18 to 75 years were eligible for enrolment if they met all of the following inclusion criteria:

- Diagnosis of rheumatoid arthritis according to the 2010 ACR/EULAR classification criteria, with disease duration of at least 6 months
- Moderate-to-severe disease activity defined as DAS28-CRP above 3.2 with at least 4 tender and 4 swollen joints (based on the 28-joint count)
- Inadequate response to at least one csDMARD at an adequate dose for at least 12 weeks
- CRP above 0.6 mg/dL or ESR above 28 mm/h at screening

Key exclusion criteria included active or latent tuberculosis (by interferon-gamma release assay and chest radiograph), chronic hepatitis B or C infection, history of severe allergic or anaphylactic reactions to monoclonal antibodies, absolute neutrophil count below 2,000 per microlitre, platelet count below 100,000 per microlitre, and ALT or AST above 1.5 times the upper limit of normal.

## 2.2 Screening and enrolment

Screening was conducted over a 28-day window. A total of 1,284 patients were screened between March 2023 and February 2024. Of these, 442 (34.4 pct) did not meet eligibility criteria or declined participation. The most common reason for screen failure was insufficient disease activity at the screening visit (n = 148, 33.5 pct of exclusions), followed by an inadequate washout of prior biologic therapy (n = 82), and positive tuberculosis screening (n = 68). A total of 842 patients were randomised, 421 to each arm.

## 2.4 Geographic distribution

Patients were enrolled across six geographic regions: East Asia (Japan, South Korea; n = 186, 22.1 pct), Western Europe (Netherlands, Sweden, Germany, United Kingdom; n = 218, 25.9 pct), North America (United States, Canada; n = 164, 19.5 pct), South Asia (India; n = 98, 11.6 pct), Sub-Saharan Africa (Ghana, South Africa; n = 84, 10.0 pct), and Latin America (Brazil, Mexico; n = 92, 10.9 pct). The geographic distribution was balanced between treatment arms within each region.

## 2.5 Patient disposition

Of the 842 randomised patients, 837 received at least one dose of study medication (418 VEL-401, 419 MTX) and constituted the safety population. Five patients were randomised but never received study medication (3 VEL-401, 2 MTX) due to withdrawal of consent before the first dose. A total of 770 patients (91.4 pct) completed the 24-week treatment period (393 VEL-401 [93.3 pct] and 377 MTX [89.5 pct]). The most common reasons for early discontinuation were adverse events (VEL-401: n = 9, 2.1 pct; MTX: n = 8, 1.9 pct), patient withdrawal of consent (VEL-401: n = 5, 1.2 pct; MTX: n = 14, 3.3 pct), and loss to follow-up (VEL-401: n = 14, 3.3 pct; MTX: n = 22, 5.2 pct).
