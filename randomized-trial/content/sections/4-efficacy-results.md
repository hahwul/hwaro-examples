## 4.1 Primary endpoint

The primary endpoint was the proportion of patients achieving ACR50 response at week 24. In the ITT population with non-responder imputation, ACR50 was achieved by 215 of 421 patients (51.2 pct) in the VEL-401 group and 121 of 421 patients (28.7 pct) in the MTX group. The absolute difference was 22.5 percentage points (95 pct CI: 16.1 to 28.9; P less than 0.001 by stratified Cochran-Mantel-Haenszel test). The number needed to treat (NNT) was 4.4 (95 pct CI: 3.5 to 6.2).

Results were consistent in the per-protocol population (54.7 pct vs 30.2 pct; P less than 0.001) and with multiple imputation for missing data (52.1 pct vs 29.4 pct; P less than 0.001). A tipping-point analysis demonstrated that the primary endpoint remained statistically significant even when all missing data in the VEL-401 arm were imputed as non-responders and up to 60 pct of missing data in the MTX arm were imputed as responders.

## 4.4 Subgroup analyses

Pre-specified subgroup analyses of the primary endpoint (ACR50 at week 24) showed consistent results across all subgroups. The treatment effect favoured VEL-401 regardless of baseline DAS28-CRP category (moderate: 48.1 pct vs 27.1 pct; high: 54.6 pct vs 30.5 pct), prior biologic use (yes: 44.9 pct vs 24.2 pct; no: 52.8 pct vs 29.9 pct), RF serostatus (positive: 52.2 pct vs 29.0 pct; negative: 47.2 pct vs 27.4 pct), geographic region, sex, age group, and body mass index category. No statistically significant treatment-by-subgroup interactions were observed (all interaction P-values above 0.10).

## 4.5 Time course of response

ACR50 response rates in the VEL-401 arm separated from the MTX arm as early as week 4 (18.1 pct vs 9.7 pct; nominal P = 0.001). The difference increased progressively through week 12 (38.7 pct vs 20.9 pct) and week 16 (45.4 pct vs 24.9 pct) before reaching the primary timepoint at week 24 (51.2 pct vs 28.7 pct). The time course of response suggests ongoing benefit with continued treatment.
