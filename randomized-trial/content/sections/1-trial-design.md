## 1.1 Study design overview

The VELOCITY trial was a multicentre, double-blind, double-dummy, active-controlled, parallel-group Phase III randomised controlled trial conducted at 68 sites across 14 countries (Japan, Ghana, the Netherlands, India, Sweden, United States, United Kingdom, Germany, South Korea, Brazil, Canada, Australia, South Africa, and Mexico). The trial compared subcutaneous VEL-401 200 mg administered every two weeks with oral methotrexate 15-25 mg administered weekly in adult patients with moderate-to-severe rheumatoid arthritis.

The study was conducted in accordance with the Declaration of Helsinki, the International Council for Harmonisation Good Clinical Practice guidelines (ICH E6-R2), and all applicable local regulations. The trial protocol was approved by the institutional review board or independent ethics committee at each participating site. All patients provided written informed consent before any study-related procedures.

## 1.2 Randomisation

Patients were randomised in a 1:1 ratio to the VEL-401 or MTX arm using a computer-generated permuted block randomisation sequence with variable block sizes of 4 and 6. Randomisation was stratified by two factors:

- **Baseline disease activity:** DAS28-CRP 3.2 to 5.1 (moderate) versus above 5.1 (high)
- **Prior biologic DMARD use:** yes versus no

The randomisation sequence was generated by an independent statistician at an academic coordinating centre (Uppsala University Clinical Trial Centre) using validated software (Proc Plan in SAS version 9.4). Treatment allocation was administered centrally via an interactive web response system (IWRS), ensuring concealment of allocation from investigators and patients at all times.

## 1.3 Blinding

The double-blind, double-dummy design ensured that all patients received both a subcutaneous injection and an oral capsule at the specified intervals, regardless of treatment assignment:

- **VEL-401 arm:** active VEL-401 200 mg subcutaneous injection every two weeks plus matching oral MTX placebo capsules weekly
- **MTX arm:** matching subcutaneous placebo injection every two weeks plus active oral MTX capsules weekly

All study medications (active and placebo) were identical in appearance, volume, and packaging. Unblinding was permitted only in the event of a medical emergency, following the pre-specified procedures in the data safety monitoring board charter. No unblinding events occurred during the 24-week treatment period.

## 1.4 Sample size determination

The sample size calculation was based on the primary endpoint of ACR50 response rate at week 24. Assuming an ACR50 response rate of 50 pct in the VEL-401 group and 35 pct in the MTX group (a 15 percentage-point difference), a two-sided chi-squared test at the 5 pct significance level with 90 pct power required 198 evaluable patients per arm. To account for an anticipated dropout rate of 10 pct, the target enrolment was increased to 220 per arm. Following the recommendation of the independent data monitoring committee after a pre-planned blinded sample size re-estimation at the midpoint of enrolment, the sample size was increased to 420 per arm to ensure adequate power for key secondary endpoints.

## 1.5 Statistical analysis plan

The statistical analysis plan was finalised and signed before database lock. All primary and secondary efficacy analyses were conducted in the intention-to-treat (ITT) population, defined as all randomised patients regardless of treatment received or adherence. The per-protocol (PP) population, defined as all randomised patients who completed the 24-week treatment period without major protocol deviations, was used for sensitivity analyses.

The primary endpoint was analysed using a Cochran-Mantel-Haenszel test stratified by the two randomisation stratification factors (baseline DAS28-CRP category and prior biologic use). The primary comparison was tested at the two-sided 5 pct significance level. Missing data were handled using non-responder imputation (NRI) as the primary analysis, with multiple imputation and tipping-point analyses as sensitivity methods.

A hierarchical testing procedure was used to control the family-wise type I error rate across the primary endpoint and three key secondary endpoints (ACR20 at week 24, ACR70 at week 24, and change from baseline in DAS28-CRP at week 24). Each endpoint was tested sequentially at the 5 pct significance level; if any endpoint failed to reach significance, all subsequent endpoints in the hierarchy were considered exploratory.

## 1.6 Trial oversight

An independent data safety monitoring board (DSMB) comprising three external clinical experts and one biostatistician reviewed unblinded safety data at pre-specified intervals (after 25 pct, 50 pct, and 75 pct of patients completed the week 12 visit). The DSMB charter included pre-defined stopping rules for safety and futility.

The trial was registered on ClinicalTrials.gov (NCT04829174) before the first patient was enrolled.
