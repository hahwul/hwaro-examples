## 3.1 VEL-401 regimen

VEL-401 is a fully humanised IgG1 monoclonal antibody directed against the interleukin-6 receptor alpha subunit (IL-6R-alpha). It binds with high affinity (KD = 0.8 nM) to both the membrane-bound and soluble forms of IL-6R-alpha, inhibiting IL-6-mediated JAK-STAT signalling. The molecule was engineered with a modified Fc region to extend its half-life to approximately 14 days, supporting biweekly dosing.

Patients randomised to the VEL-401 arm received 200 mg by subcutaneous injection every two weeks for 24 weeks (13 injections total). The dose was selected based on the Phase II dose-ranging study (VELOCITY-02, NCT04518712), which demonstrated that 200 mg every two weeks provided the optimal balance of efficacy and tolerability. Injections were administered at the clinical site by trained study personnel. The injection site was rotated between the abdomen, thigh, and upper arm according to a pre-defined schedule.

## 3.2 Methotrexate regimen

Patients randomised to the MTX arm received oral methotrexate starting at 15 mg weekly. The dose was escalated to 20 mg at week 4 and to 25 mg at week 8, provided that tolerability was acceptable and ALT/AST remained below 3 times the upper limit of normal. Dose reductions were permitted for toxicity according to a standardised dose-modification algorithm. All patients in the MTX arm received folic acid 5 mg weekly (administered at least 24 hours after the MTX dose) to mitigate folate-related adverse effects.

## 3.3 Double-dummy blinding

To maintain blinding, the following double-dummy approach was used:

- VEL-401 arm: active VEL-401 subcutaneous injection + matching oral placebo capsules
- MTX arm: matching subcutaneous placebo injection + active oral MTX capsules

The placebo injection was identical in appearance, volume (1.0 mL), and syringe to the active VEL-401 injection. The placebo capsules were identical in size, colour, and packaging to the active MTX capsules. The dose-escalation schedule for the MTX placebo in the VEL-401 arm mirrored the active MTX dose-escalation protocol to maintain the blind.

## 3.4 Concomitant medications

The following concomitant medications were permitted, provided that doses were stable for at least 4 weeks before baseline and remained stable through week 24:

- Oral corticosteroids at a dose equivalent to prednisone 10 mg per day or less
- Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) at stable doses
- One additional csDMARD other than methotrexate (e.g., hydroxychloroquine, sulfasalazine)

Prohibited concomitant medications included any biologic DMARD other than VEL-401, targeted synthetic DMARDs (JAK inhibitors), cyclophosphamide, and live vaccines. Intra-articular corticosteroid injections were limited to one injection in a single joint during the study period and were considered a protocol deviation if administered more than once.

## 3.5 Rescue therapy

Patients who did not achieve at least a 20 pct improvement in both tender and swollen joint counts by week 16 were eligible for open-label rescue therapy. Rescue therapy consisted of the addition of background csDMARD therapy at the investigator's discretion. Patients who received rescue therapy were classified as non-responders for the primary endpoint analysis from the time of rescue forward.

A total of 34 patients in the VEL-401 arm (8.1 pct) and 72 patients in the MTX arm (17.1 pct) received rescue therapy (P less than 0.001 by chi-squared test).

## 3.6 Adherence monitoring

Adherence to the subcutaneous injection regimen was monitored through IWRS-based drug accountability and direct observation at study visits. Adherence to the oral capsule regimen was monitored through capsule counts at each visit. Mean adherence rates were 97.2 pct in the VEL-401 arm (subcutaneous injections) and 93.8 pct in the MTX arm (oral capsules). A pre-specified per-protocol analysis excluded patients with overall adherence below 80 pct, which affected 8 patients in the VEL-401 arm and 19 patients in the MTX arm.
