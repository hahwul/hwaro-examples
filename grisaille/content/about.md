# About Grisaille

Grisaille is a method of painting executed entirely in shades of grey or another neutral tone. The technique was widely used in the medieval period and throughout the Renaissance, often to imitate sculpture or to serve as underpaintings before the application of colour.

This journal borrows from that tradition. It strips away the distractions of colour to focus on what remains: form, structure, contrast, and the deliberate interplay of light and dark.

## What You Will Find Here

Writing on art, design, and the philosophy of restraint. Each piece examines how limitations shape creative output -- from the monochrome canvases of the old masters to the deliberate constraints of modern design systems.

> "Colour is an accessory to form. When you remove it, you are left with the truth of the shape."
