## Search Strategy

The systematic search was conducted across four electronic databases from inception through 31 December 2025:

- **PubMed/MEDLINE** (1,204 records)
- **PsycINFO** (892 records)
- **Cochrane CENTRAL** (1,024 records)
- **Embase** (722 records)

Search terms combined three concept blocks using Boolean operators: (1) insomnia OR sleep disorder OR sleep disturbance; (2) cognitive behavioral therapy OR CBT OR CBT-I; (3) digital OR online OR internet OR app OR computerized OR automated. No language or date restrictions were applied.

The full search strategy for each database is available in Supplementary Table S1.

## Eligibility Criteria

### Inclusion criteria (PICOS)

- **Population:** Adults (age 18+) with self-reported or clinically diagnosed insomnia symptoms
- **Intervention:** Fully digital CBT-I delivered via web platform, mobile app, or automated program (with or without therapist support)
- **Comparator:** Waitlist, treatment-as-usual, sleep hygiene education, or active control
- **Outcome:** Validated insomnia measure (ISI, PSQI, or sleep diary) as primary outcome
- **Study design:** Randomized controlled trial with at least 2 arms

### Exclusion criteria

- Non-randomized studies, case reports, or quasi-experimental designs
- Interventions requiring synchronous therapist delivery (i.e., video therapy sessions)
- Pediatric or adolescent populations (age < 18)
- Conference abstracts without full-text publication
- Duplicate samples from the same research group (latest publication retained)

## Study Selection Process

Two reviewers (KB and AOM) independently screened titles and abstracts, followed by full-text assessment. Disagreements were resolved by consensus or adjudication by a third reviewer (IP). Inter-rater agreement was kappa = 0.84 at title/abstract stage and kappa = 0.91 at full-text stage.
