Deconstructivism is a movement of postmodern architecture which appeared in the 1980s. It gives the impression of the fragmentation of the constructed building, characterized by an absence of harmony, continuity, or symmetry.

## The Core Concept

The core concept is to break apart the structure, to dislocate the form, and to challenge the traditional ideas of order and balance. Yet, in this chaos, a new kind of balance emerges.

### Implementation

In this theme, we implement these ideas through:
*   **Asymmetrical Layouts**: Elements do not align perfectly.
*   **Rotated Forms**: Text and containers are slightly skewed using CSS transforms.
*   **High Contrast**: Stark black and white color schemes with occasional bold, aggressive colors like red or yellow.
*   **Fragmented Typography**: Headings that break lines unexpectedly or overlap.
*   **Overlapping Elements**: Layers interacting in non-standard ways using negative margins and z-indexes.

This is an example of a blockquote that breaks the grid:
> "Architecture is the will of an epoch translated into space." — Mies van der Rohe

And another thought:
> "Form follows function—that has been misunderstood. Form and function should be one, joined in a spiritual union." — Frank Lloyd Wright

The chaos is intentional. It forces the reader to actively engage with the content, breaking the passive consumption model of traditional web design.
