# About This Journal

Dune is a travel journal dedicated to the deserts of the world. It exists because the desert teaches things that no other landscape can.

## Why the Desert

There is a reason that every major spiritual tradition includes a period of wandering in the wilderness. The desert strips away distraction. It reduces the world to its essentials: light, shadow, heat, cold, thirst, and the slow movement of sand.

For the traveler, this reduction is a gift. Without the noise of the modern world, you begin to hear things you had forgotten were there -- the sound of wind across a ridge, the creak of a saddle, the silence between stars.

## The Journeys

The entries in this journal span multiple expeditions across different deserts, from week-long treks in the Moroccan erg to longer crossings of the Mauritanian Adrar. Some journeys are solo. Others follow traditional caravan routes with local guides whose families have traveled these paths for generations.

## About This Site

This site is built with [Hwaro](https://github.com/hahwul/hwaro), a fast static site generator. The design reflects the landscape it documents -- warm tones for the daylight hours, cool tones for the desert night, and the S-curves of dune ridges woven throughout.

## A Note on Travel

The deserts described here are real places with real communities. If these writings inspire you to visit, please do so with respect -- for the land, for the people who call it home, and for the silence that makes it sacred.
