Volcanic glass possesses a distinctive set of physical properties that have made it valuable to humans for millennia and continue to make it useful in modern applications.

## Mechanical Properties

Obsidian has a Mohs hardness of approximately 5 to 5.5, placing it between apatite and orthoclase feldspar. Despite this moderate hardness, its most remarkable mechanical property is its fracture behavior.

When fractured, obsidian breaks with a **conchoidal fracture pattern** -- smooth, curved surfaces that radiate from the point of impact. This occurs because the amorphous structure has no crystal planes along which to cleave. The fracture surface can be extraordinarily thin at its edges, sometimes only a few nanometers in width.

## Optical Properties

Obsidian is typically opaque in hand specimens but can be translucent to transparent in thin sections. The optical properties vary with composition:

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Refractive index | 1.48 - 1.51 |
| Specific gravity | 2.35 - 2.60 |
| Luster | Vitreous |
| Streak | White |
| Transparency | Opaque to translucent |

## Edge Sharpness

The conchoidal fracture of obsidian can produce edges far sharper than any metal blade. Freshly knapped obsidian edges have been measured at 30 angstroms (3 nanometers) in width -- roughly 500 times thinner than a steel surgical scalpel.

This property has led to experimental use of obsidian blades in surgical contexts, where studies have shown reduced scarring and faster healing compared to steel instruments.

## Hydration

When exposed to the atmosphere, obsidian slowly absorbs water. This process, called **obsidian hydration**, forms a measurable rind on exposed surfaces. The thickness of this hydration rind can be used as a dating technique in archaeology, known as obsidian hydration dating.

```
Hydration rate factors:
  - Temperature (primary)
  - Relative humidity
  - Chemical composition of the glass
  - Intrinsic water content
```

The hydration rate varies by source and climate, making calibration essential for accurate dating.
