The earliest known writing system, cuneiform, emerged in the Sumerian city-states of southern Mesopotamia. Initially developed for accounting and record-keeping, these wedge-shaped marks pressed into clay tablets would evolve into a full literary medium.

<!-- more -->

Writing transformed human civilization by enabling the accumulation and transmission of knowledge across generations. Administrative records, legal codes, epic poetry, and scientific observations could now outlast their creators. The scribal tradition that grew around cuneiform became one of the first specialized professions, and the libraries that housed these tablets -- such as the later Library of Ashurbanipal -- represent humanity's earliest attempts to organize and preserve collective knowledge.
