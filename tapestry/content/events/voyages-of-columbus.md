Christopher Columbus's arrival in the Caribbean, sponsored by the Spanish Crown, initiated sustained contact between Europe and the Americas. This encounter set in motion centuries of exchange, colonization, and transformation on both sides of the Atlantic.

<!-- more -->

The consequences of 1492 were vast and uneven. For Europe, the "discovery" opened new horizons of wealth, territory, and scientific knowledge. For the indigenous peoples of the Americas, it brought catastrophic population decline through disease, displacement, and violence. The Columbian Exchange -- the transfer of plants, animals, diseases, and ideas between hemispheres -- reshaped ecosystems and economies worldwide, making this moment one of the most consequential turning points in human history.
