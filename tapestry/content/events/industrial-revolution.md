Beginning in Britain's textile mills and iron foundries, the Industrial Revolution replaced manual labor with machine-based manufacturing. Steam power, factory production, and new transportation networks reshaped not only the economy but the very fabric of daily life.

<!-- more -->

The shift from agrarian to industrial society was perhaps the most profound transformation in human history since the adoption of agriculture. Urbanization accelerated as workers migrated to factory towns. New social classes emerged, and with them new political tensions. The environmental consequences -- pollution, resource extraction, and landscape alteration -- set in motion challenges that persist today. Yet the revolution also brought rising living standards, medical advances, and the technological foundation of the modern world.
