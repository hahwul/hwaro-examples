In design, white space is often referred to as negative space. It is the portion of a page left unmarked, the space between graphics, margins, and gutters. It is the space between columns, between lines of type, or figures that provides visual breathing room for the eye.

### Purpose Over Decoration

We often feel compelled to fill every available area with content or ornamentation. This urge stems from a fear of emptiness, a belief that more equates to greater value. However, true elegance is found in restraint.

> "Perfection is achieved, not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away."
> — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

### Finding Focus

By removing the superfluous, we elevate the essential. A minimal approach does not mean a lack of design; rather, it is design in its purest form. It requires a deep understanding of what truly matters and the courage to discard the rest.

- **Clarity:** Messages are communicated more effectively.
- **Calm:** A less cluttered environment reduces cognitive load.
- **Elegance:** Simplicity possesses an inherent sophistication.

Let the content breathe. Let the words speak for themselves.
