## THE CITATION

*Read from the reviewing stand at City Hall, 14:02, to sustained applause.*

Forty years ago, a young librarian took over the Civic Library &mdash; a single-branch institution with 14,000 books, a leaking roof, and fewer than 800 active card-holders. Today she retires from a thirty-branch network with more than four million books, audio recordings, maker spaces, and a board-approved budget that has been honored by three consecutive mayors from two different parties.

### What She Built

The Vargas years saw the library expand by a factor of thirty. But that is not the citation. The citation is for the specific decision, in her second year, to make every branch's Wi-Fi free and unlogged. That decision, made before free Wi-Fi was a line item anyone had considered, made the library into the backup civic infrastructure of this city for four decades.

### What She Did With Every Snow Day

The library was the emergency warming center. Every single snow day. Every power outage. Every summer cooling crisis. Every time the public schools closed without warning and a parent had nowhere to send a child who still needed lunch.

Helena Vargas built the thing we only remembered we had when everything else had failed.

> "A library is the only building that does not ask you why you are there." &mdash; from the citation

### The Parade

She rides first in the open car. Her granddaughter drives. Ticker tape falls.
