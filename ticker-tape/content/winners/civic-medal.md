## THE CITATION

*Read from the reviewing stand at City Hall, 14:28, to cheers and a chorus of horns from the adjacent construction float.*

The Lighthouse Project broke ground on April 1st of last year. By March 31st of this year, two thousand new homes &mdash; genuine homes, with full kitchens, real square footage, and permitted construction &mdash; had been completed and occupied. No one of any significance believed this was possible.

### The Work

The project combined a prefabrication facility in the Navy Yard with a permitting fast-track that the City Council passed unanimously in January of last year. The fast-track was set to expire in twelve months. The Lighthouse team did not ask for an extension.

### What It Took

A hundred and forty construction workers who drove to the site every weekday from February through January. Three shifts. Weather-independent panelized assembly. Building inspectors who worked Saturdays without complaining. A community board that held twenty-eight public meetings and voted yes twenty-eight times.

### Two Thousand Homes

One for every family on the wait list as of March of last year. Empty apartments remained empty only long enough for someone to sign a lease.

> "The wait list is now nine families long. We are taking that as a win." &mdash; Project Lead, from the stage

### The Parade

The Lighthouse float carries a life-size model of a two-bedroom unit. A real kitchen. Running water. The float is delayed by twelve minutes because they had to stop and fix a hinge on the front door. Nobody minded.
