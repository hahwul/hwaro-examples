## Conditions

Arrived at the site with an incoming current running northeast along the wall. Surface chop was mild, but below 5 meters the water was calm and clear. Visibility opened up past 30 meters once we cleared the thermocline.

Water temperature held steady at 29C through the entire dive profile. No significant temperature gradient until the final safety stop.

## The Dive

Descended to 18 meters and immediately hooked into the reef edge to observe the channel below. Within minutes, a group of six **grey reef sharks** appeared from the blue, moving in a loose formation along the wall.

At 25 meters, a large **Napoleon wrasse** -- easily 1.2 meters in length -- drifted past with characteristic slow, deliberate movements. It circled twice before disappearing over the wall edge.

Released the hooks at 20 minutes and began a slow drift along the wall face. The current carried us at a steady pace, allowing long observation windows of the reef structure.

## Notable Observations

- Grey reef sharks (6 individuals) at 22-28m depth
- Napoleon wrasse, single adult male, approximately 1.2m
- School of barracuda (estimated 200+) forming a cylinder at 15m
- Several hawksbill turtle tracks visible on the sand shelf
- Large table corals in excellent condition along the wall top

## Post-Dive Notes

The drift section offered exceptional visibility for photography. Current strength was moderate -- manageable without hooks for experienced divers but recommended for comfort during shark observation.

Second dive of the day at this site. Morning dive had weaker current and fewer pelagic sightings, suggesting afternoon timing aligns with feeding patterns in this area.
