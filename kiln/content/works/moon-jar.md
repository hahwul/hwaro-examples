The moon jar (달항아리, dalhangari) is a type of traditional Korean white porcelain which was made during the Joseon dynasty (1392–1910). The name comes from its shape and milky glaze color to remind people of a full moon.

Due to its large size, it cannot be made in one piece on the wheel. Instead, it is thrown in two separate halves – top and bottom. These are then joined together in the middle before firing. This technique results in a subtle asymmetry, which is considered one of its most beautiful characteristics.

This piece was fired in an oxidation atmosphere to achieve a pure white tone with a subtle, soft matte finish.
