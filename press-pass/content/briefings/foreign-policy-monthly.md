The Political Director briefed correspondents Thursday on the monthly canvass of the three regional desks. A senior Political Affairs official characterised the current regional landscape as &ldquo;in motion but not in flux,&rdquo; noting that the pace of diplomatic activity has increased but that underlying positions have remained largely stable.

## Regional One

The first regional desk reported that ongoing bilateral consultations with two principal partners are progressing on the expected timeline. The official emphasised that the consultations are &ldquo;substantive, not cosmetic,&rdquo; and that the department expects a joint statement within the next four weeks. No specific commitments are anticipated in the joint statement; its purpose is to establish the framework for a subsequent ministerial meeting in early June.

> On background: the purpose of this cycle of consultations is to align expectations ahead of the ministerial. The substantive decisions will be made at the ministerial level, not by the working-level delegations.

Correspondents should read the joint statement, when it issues, as a procedural document rather than a substantive one. Reporting that presents the statement as a breakthrough would miss the dynamic.

## Regional Two

The second regional desk reported continued engagement on the previously announced trilateral framework. The official confirmed that the framework's second working group has been convened and is meeting twice monthly, with a mandate to produce a draft text by the end of the calendar year.

Two elements remain unresolved:

- The **scope** of the framework &mdash; specifically, whether it extends to two additional subject areas currently under consideration
- The **dispute resolution mechanism** &mdash; the three parties have aligned on the principle but not on the specific mechanism

The official indicated that neither issue is &ldquo;close to resolution,&rdquo; but that both are &ldquo;being handled at the appropriate level.&rdquo; Correspondents should not expect a breakthrough on either before the summer.

## Regional Three

The third regional desk is the most active. The official characterised the current situation as requiring &ldquo;continuous attention at the senior level,&rdquo; and confirmed that the Political Director has made three trips to the region in the past six weeks. No further visits are currently scheduled but the official did not rule them out.

Specific items under active diplomacy include:

- **Credential review** of a new principal representative, which the Department has accepted and which is proceeding through the expected procedural stages
- **Technical dialogue** on three subject-matter areas, all of which are active and two of which are expected to produce working-level agreements in the near term
- **A scheduled visit** by a senior cabinet official, details to be released through the Department's public affairs channel when the visit is confirmed

The official declined to characterise the posture of the third regional relationship beyond &ldquo;requiring continuous senior attention,&rdquo; and specifically declined to confirm or deny speculation about the scope of the scheduled cabinet visit.

## Looking Ahead

Correspondents should watch:

1. The joint statement from the first regional desk, expected within four weeks
2. The working-group draft text from the second regional desk, expected before end of year
3. The scheduled cabinet visit from the third regional desk, timing to be announced

The next monthly foreign policy briefing is scheduled for 17 April at the standard Thursday time.
