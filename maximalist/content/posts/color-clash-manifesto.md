Forget color theory harmonies. Maximalism embraces the **clash** — the electric tension between colors that were never meant to sit side by side.

<!-- more -->

## Rules of the Clash

1. **Go saturated** — pastels are for whispers; we are here to shout
2. **Mix warm and cool** — magenta next to teal, orange against violet
3. **Use black as punctuation** — it grounds the chaos without taming it
4. **Layer gradients** — why pick one transition when you can have three?

## The Palette Mindset

Think of your palette not as a set of matching swatches, but as a cast of characters. Each color has a personality. Let them argue, let them clash, let them dance.

Hot pink meets electric blue. Sunshine yellow crashes into deep violet. Lime green shakes hands with coral. The tension is the point.

## In Practice

Start with one bold anchor color. Then add its opposite. Then add something that surprises even you. Step back. If it makes you slightly uncomfortable — you are on the right track.
