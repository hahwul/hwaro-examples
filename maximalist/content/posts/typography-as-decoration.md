In maximalist design, typography is not merely functional — it is **ornamental**. Letters become shapes, words become patterns, and headlines become the centerpiece.

<!-- more -->

## Go Big or Go Home

Maximalist typography favors:

- **Oversized headlines** that dominate the viewport
- **Mixed typefaces** — pair a heavy serif with a playful sans-serif
- **Decorative fonts** for accents and flourishes
- **Colored and gradient text** that demands attention

## The Anti-Hierarchy

Traditional design creates clear hierarchies: heading, subheading, body. Maximalist typography blurs those lines. A pull quote might be bigger than the headline. A caption might be bolder than the body text.

The goal is not confusion — it is **surprise**.

## Letter as Art

When you scale a letter to 500px tall and layer it behind your content, it stops being text and starts being texture. It stops communicating language and starts communicating emotion.

This is typography at its most liberated.
