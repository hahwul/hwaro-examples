The use of dramatic lighting transformed the art of portraiture, bringing a lifelike depth to subjects that had previously felt flat and uninspired. By contrasting deep shadows with intense highlights, artists of this era created an illusion of volume and soul.

> "The painter who draws merely by practice and by eye, without any reason, is like a mirror which copies every thing placed in front of it without being conscious of their existence."
— Leonardo da Vinci

Consider the way the eyes capture the single light source in the room, reflecting not just the environment but the inner life of the sitter. The rich, dark backgrounds serve merely to push the figure forward into our space, forcing a confrontation between viewer and subject.

It is in these subtle gradations of shadow that the true mastery of the medium is revealed, demonstrating a profound understanding of anatomy and light that continues to captivate audiences centuries later.
