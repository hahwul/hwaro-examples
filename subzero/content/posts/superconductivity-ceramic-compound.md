# Superconductivity Observed in Novel Ceramic Compound

**Experiment ID:** SZ-2025-001
**Temperature:** -196°C (77K)
**Material:** Yttrium Barium Copper Oxide (YBa₂Cu₃O₇)

## Abstract

Today we successfully demonstrated superconductivity in a ceramic compound at liquid nitrogen temperatures. This represents a significant advancement in accessible superconductivity research, as liquid nitrogen is far more practical and economical than liquid helium.

<!-- more -->

## Experimental Setup

The sample was submerged in a liquid nitrogen bath at 77K (-196°C). Resistance measurements were taken using a four-point probe method to eliminate contact resistance effects.

## Observations

At temperatures above 92K, the material behaved as a normal conductor with measurable resistance. However, as the temperature decreased below the critical temperature (Tc ≈ 92K), resistance dropped to unmeasurable levels, confirming the superconducting transition.

### Visual Confirmation

The Meissner effect was dramatically demonstrated by observing a small neodymium magnet levitating above the superconducting disk, repelled by the perfect diamagnetism of the superconducting state.

## Implications

This experiment confirms that practical superconductivity doesn't require the extreme cost and complexity of liquid helium systems. Applications in power transmission, magnetic resonance imaging, and maglev transportation become more feasible with liquid nitrogen-cooled superconductors.

## Next Steps

- Investigate current-carrying capacity at various temperatures
- Test mechanical strength under thermal cycling
- Explore doping effects on critical temperature
- Scale up synthesis for larger samples
