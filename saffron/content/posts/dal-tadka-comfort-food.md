Dal tadka is the quiet backbone of Indian home cooking. It appears on tables across the country every single day, in countless variations, and yet it never grows tiresome. The dish is built on the simplest of foundations -- split lentils cooked until they break down into a thick, nourishing porridge -- and then elevated by the tadka, a sizzling tempering of spices and aromatics poured over the top at the last moment.

Rinse a cup of toor dal or a mixture of toor and masoor dal until the water runs clear. Pressure cook or simmer the lentils with turmeric, salt, and enough water to cover them by two inches. The lentils should be completely soft and easily mashed with the back of a spoon. Meanwhile, prepare the tadka: heat ghee in a small pan until it shimmers, then add cumin seeds and let them crackle. Follow with mustard seeds, a pinch of asafoetida, sliced garlic, and two or three dried red chilies broken in half.

When the garlic turns golden and the chilies darken, add a chopped tomato and cook until it softens. Pour this sizzling tempering directly over the cooked dal and cover the pot immediately to trap the aromatic steam. Stir it through after a minute, adjust the consistency with hot water if needed, and finish with a squeeze of lemon and fresh cilantro. Serve with steamed rice or warm roti for a meal that is both humble and deeply satisfying.
