## Eligibility Criteria

### Inclusion criteria

- Age 18-25 years at time of enrollment
- Enrolled full-time at one of the four participating university sites
- Registered for introductory biology (BIO 101 or equivalent) in the Spring 2026 semester
- No prior completion of the target course (first-time enrollees only)
- Able to provide written informed consent in English

### Exclusion criteria

- Prior enrollment in the same course at any institution (repeat students)
- Concurrent enrollment in another educational intervention study
- Documented learning disability requiring alternative assessment formats that preclude standardized testing
- Planned withdrawal from the course before week 10

## Recruitment

Recruitment will proceed through course announcements at each of the four sites during the first week of the semester (week 1). Research assistants blinded to allocation will present a standardized recruitment script during lecture. Interested students will complete an online screening form and provide informed consent through REDCap.

## Randomization Procedure

Randomization will use a computer-generated sequence with permuted blocks (block sizes of 6 and 9, randomly varied) stratified by:

1. **Site** (4 levels: Uppsala, Toronto, Tokyo, Cape Town)
2. **Prior GPA quartile** (4 levels: Q1 through Q4, based on cumulative GPA at enrollment)

The randomization sequence will be generated by an independent statistician (not a member of the investigative team) using R version 4.3 with the `blockrand` package. The sequence will be uploaded to the REDCap randomization module before the first participant is enrolled.

### Allocation concealment

Allocation is concealed within the REDCap randomization module. Site coordinators enter participant demographics and stratification variables; REDCap returns the allocation. Neither the site coordinator nor the participant can predict the next assignment.

### Blinding

- **Participants:** Blinded to arm assignment. Both testing and re-study sessions are presented as "active learning sessions" with identical scheduling and duration.
- **Instructors:** Blinded to individual participant assignments. All sections receive identical lectures.
- **Outcome assessors:** The end-of-semester exam is machine-scored; no subjective grading is involved.
- **Analysts:** The primary analysis will be conducted by the independent statistician on a dataset with arms coded as X, Y, Z. Unblinding occurs only after the primary analysis is locked.

## Sample Size Justification

The sample size of N = 960 (320 per arm) provides 90 pct power to detect a standardized mean difference of d = 0.25 between any active arm and control, assuming:

- Two-sided alpha = 0.025 (Bonferroni correction for two primary comparisons)
- Intraclass correlation within sites: ICC = 0.02
- Attrition rate: 10 pct
- Design effect for clustering within course sections: DEFF = 1.08

Power calculations were performed using the `pwr` package in R and confirmed by simulation (10,000 iterations). The minimum detectable effect of d = 0.25 is informed by the meta-analytic estimate of d = 0.40 (95 pct CI: 0.30-0.50) from Rowland (2014) for retrieval practice in educational settings, discounted by 35 pct for real-world implementation.
