## Tier I &#183; Patron

The Amaranth is the first of the four Circles. It is named for the old French word *amarante*, an unfading red &mdash; the colour of the seat cushions in rows three through six of the principal hall, which have been re-upholstered in the same dye since 1878.

## What the contract includes

- **Reserved seating** in rows three through six of the principal hall for six named evenings per season.
- **First-release access** to the season programme, four weeks before public announcement.
- **Two named guest invitations** per principal evening.
- **Annual patrons&rsquo; reception** at the Villa Cornelis, held each June under the plane trees.
- **Printed programme** delivered by hand in advance of each principal evening.

## What the contract does not include

The Amaranth tier does not include a private box, a named dedication in the season programme, or access to the Vermilion Salon. Members seeking these privileges should consult the Lacquer or Vermilion tiers.

## Application

Applications for the Amaranth Circle open on the first Monday of September each year and remain open until the end of October. Each application requires the signature of two existing members and is reviewed by the Artistic Director and the Board of Patrons over the course of November.

Successful applicants are notified in writing by the second week of December, in time to plan for the spring season.

## The seat

A member&rsquo;s seat is assigned for the full season at the time of admission. The house does not rotate seats between members. If for any reason a member is unable to attend a principal evening, the seat is held empty unless the member formally releases it by noon on the day.

The seat is yours for the season. It is not yours in the sense of private ownership, but in the older sense: it is kept for you, and it is not given to anyone else, even in your absence.
