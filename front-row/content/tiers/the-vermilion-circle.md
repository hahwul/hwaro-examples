## Tier III &#183; Laureate

The Vermilion is the third of the four Circles. It is named for the red lacquer door at the south end of the first balcony, which admits only Laureate members and opens onto a small private lounge built in 1904 for the donor who endowed the orchestra&rsquo;s pension fund.

## What the contract includes

All privileges of the Lacquer tier, with the following additions:

- **Access to every evening** in the season, including previews, salons, and non-public rehearsals.
- **A named dedication** in the season programme, in the form the member prefers.
- **An annual house call** from the Artistic Director, at the member&rsquo;s home, for a conversation about the season&rsquo;s repertoire.
- **Access to the Laureate Lounge**, open at every evening performance, serving a long wine list and a short supper menu.
- **Standing invitation** to the Vermilion Salon, a private fortnightly gathering at the Villa Cornelis.

## The Vermilion Salon

The Salon is held on alternating Friday evenings from eight until midnight, at the Villa Cornelis. It is convened by the Artistic Director and is attended by Vermilion and Imperial members, a small number of resident artists, and such guests as the Director wishes to introduce.

The Salon is a private conversation. It is not minuted. What is said in the Salon does not leave the Salon &mdash; this is the single, standing rule, and it has been kept without exception since 1904.

## The annual house call

Once each year, at a date of the member&rsquo;s choosing, the Artistic Director attends the member&rsquo;s home for a private conversation about the season&rsquo;s repertoire, the artists in residence, and any concerns or suggestions the member wishes to raise. The call lasts approximately an hour and is unhurried. The Director brings nothing with her except a single bound copy of the forthcoming programme.

## Admission

The Vermilion Circle admits eighteen members. Vacancies arise when an existing member withdraws, relocates, or passes to the bequest clause. Applications are received at any time by the Board of Patrons and are considered at the annual November meeting.
