## Tier II &#183; Benefactor

The Lacquer is the second of the four Circles. It takes its name from the six private boxes on the first balcony of the principal hall, which are panelled in japanned black lacquer &mdash; the oldest surviving element of the 1878 interior.

## What the contract includes

All privileges of the Amaranth tier, with the following additions:

- **Private box seating** (four or six seats, depending on election) for twelve named evenings per season.
- **Two guest invitations** to the Annual Gala in July.
- **Standing reservation** at the Verandah Room, the members&rsquo; supper club above the foyer, for the duration of the season.
- **Subscription to the house archive**, a quarterly monograph on the season&rsquo;s repertoire, delivered by post.
- **First refusal** on any ticket returned by a Vermilion or Imperial member, at no additional charge.

## Box allocation

Boxes are allocated in October by the Board of Patrons on the basis of seniority and, where necessary, the order of application. A box is held for the full season; boxes are not rotated or re-assigned between members. Members may elect a four-seat or six-seat box at the time of admission, subject to availability.

## The Verandah Room

The Verandah Room is open from six in the evening until the hour of curtain, and again at the interval. It serves a short menu of supper dishes and a short list of wines. Members may reserve the room for private parties on nights without performance, subject to arrangement with the concierge.

The room is named for the long glazed gallery that runs along its north side, which looks out over the roof garden of the Villa Cornelis.
