Dark themes are the perfect canvas for bold color. Just as a sunset appears most dramatic against the darkening sky, warm accent colors reach their maximum visual impact on dark backgrounds.

## The Warm–Cool Contrast

The most striking color combinations pair warm accents with cool dark bases:

- **Magenta on deep indigo** — intense and glamorous
- **Burnt orange on midnight blue** — warm and inviting
- **Gold on charcoal** — elegant and rich
- **Coral on deep purple** — vibrant and modern

## Saturation Strategy

On dark backgrounds, fully saturated colors can feel overwhelming. The Magenta Sunset approach uses strategic saturation:

- **Headings**: High saturation for maximum impact
- **Body accents**: Medium saturation for comfortable reading
- **Backgrounds**: Low saturation for depth without distraction
- **Hover states**: Boost saturation for interactive feedback

## Glow Effects

The secret weapon of warm colors on dark themes is the glow effect. A magenta heading with layered `text-shadow` doesn't just display text — it radiates warmth, creating an atmosphere that flat color alone cannot achieve.

## The Golden Hour Rule

In photography, the golden hour produces the most beautiful warm light. In dark theme design, the same principle applies: use warm colors sparingly and intentionally, letting the darkness frame each moment of warmth like the twilight sky frames the setting sun.
