The sunset is nature's greatest gradient. From the fiery oranges at the horizon to the deep violets overhead, twilight creates a color symphony that designers have tried to capture for centuries.

## Nature's Gradient Palette

A real sunset moves through a breathtaking spectrum:

- **Molten gold** — the last direct rays touching the horizon
- **Burnt orange** — the warm glow just above the sun's path
- **Deep magenta** — the rich middle band where warmth meets cool
- **Twilight violet** — the transition zone into night
- **Indigo darkness** — the deep sky overhead

## From Sky to Screen

Translating sunset gradients to CSS means understanding layered color transitions:

### Linear Gradients for the Horizon

The classic sunset effect uses a vertical linear gradient that moves from warm tones at the bottom to cool tones at the top — just like looking at the real horizon.

### Radial Gradients for the Sun

The sun itself creates a radial glow. A centered radial gradient with warm yellows fading to oranges captures the feeling of a sun disk.

### Layering for Depth

Real sunsets have depth — clouds catch light differently, atmospheric haze creates soft transitions. In CSS, stacking multiple semi-transparent gradients achieves this layered richness.

## The Magenta Moment

The most dramatic sunsets feature a deep magenta band — that rare, intense pink that appears when atmospheric conditions are just right. This is the signature color of our theme, and it drives every design choice.

Magenta isn't just pink. It's the color of maximum twilight drama.
