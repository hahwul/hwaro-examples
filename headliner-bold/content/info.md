## Gates, entry, re-entry

Gates open at 13:00 on Friday and 12:30 on Saturday and Sunday. Re-entry is allowed on the same wristband as long as you scan out and back in through an official gate. Wristbands are non-transferable.

## Accessibility

Each stage has a dedicated accessibility viewing platform with room for a companion. Accessible camping is a separate zone with quiet hours, hardstanding for accessible vehicles, and an accessible toilet block staffed at all times. Request the accessible zone when booking &mdash; it is not available on the day.

## Cashless payments

The entire site operates cashless. Top up your festival wristband at any top-up station or through the festival app. Unused balance is refunded within 72 hours after the festival closes; the refund window stays open for six weeks.

## What to bring

Warm layers after dark, a reusable water bottle (free taps across the site), and sensible shoes for the meadow stages. What not to bring: glass bottles, professional camera rigs without a press pass, drones of any size, or your own alcohol through the gates.

## Camping

Camping is limited to 24,000 pitches. Sold out for 2026. Campervan and live-in vehicle pitches are a separate allocation &mdash; those sold out in November 2025. Day tickets give you access to a 04:30 last-shuttle service back to the city centre; campers do not need to leave until 14:00 on Monday.

## Lost and found

Lost items are collected at the Welfare tent on the west side of the Parade Grounds. After the festival, unclaimed items are photographed and listed on the festival app for four weeks before being donated to a local charity.
