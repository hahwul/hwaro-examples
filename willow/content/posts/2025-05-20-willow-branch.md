title = "Willow Branch"

The willow does not argue with the wind.
It bends. It yields.
And in yielding, holds.

Where the oak stands rigid
and risks the breaking,
the willow surrenders its shape
to the shape of the air.

Is this weakness?

Watch it after the storm.
Every branch intact.
Every leaf accounted for.

---

There is a kind of strength
that looks like softness.
A way of moving through the world
that does not announce itself.

The willow knows this.
Its roots go deep.
Its branches reach down
toward the water
as though in gratitude.

---

I have spent years
learning to stand firm.
Now I am learning
something harder:
how to bend.
