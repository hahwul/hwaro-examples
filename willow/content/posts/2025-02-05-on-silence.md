Silence is not empty. Anyone who has sat alone in a room long enough knows this. It fills the space the way water fills a glass, taking the shape of whatever contains it.

We speak of silence as though it were a lack, something to be broken or filled. But silence is its own kind of speech. It is the language of things that do not need to be said.

## Listening

The best listeners are not those who wait for their turn to speak. They are the ones who understand that some things can only be heard in the pauses. A conversation is not a sequence of words. It is a rhythm of speaking and not-speaking, and both halves carry meaning.

## The Page

A poem lives as much in its white space as in its ink. The line break is a form of silence. The stanza break, a longer one. The best poets know when to stop, when the next word would be one too many.

> "Silence is the language of God; all else is poor translation."
> -- Rumi

## Practice

Try this: sit for ten minutes and listen. Not to anything in particular. Just listen. You will hear the house settling, your own breath, the distant hum of the world going about its business. In that listening, something shifts. The noise does not go away, but your relationship to it changes.

Silence, in the end, is not about the absence of sound. It is about the quality of attention we bring to whatever is already here.
