## About Acid Graphics

Acid Graphics is a visual language born from the underground. It pulls from rave flyers of the early 90s, psychedelic poster art of the 60s, and the raw energy of warehouse parties.

This site is a tribute to that aesthetic — warped type, melting gradients, hallucinatory color, and unrestrained visual intensity.

## The Aesthetic

Acid graphics reject clean minimalism. They embrace distortion, saturation, and sensory overload. Every element is pushed past its comfortable threshold:

- **Typography** gets stretched, skewed, and layered until it vibrates
- **Color** is cranked to maximum — fluorescent greens, hot magentas, electric yellows
- **Layout** breaks the grid, overlaps, and challenges readability on purpose
- **Texture** comes from halftone dots, noise, and analog imperfections

## Influences

The style draws from decades of countercultural graphic design:

- **1960s psychedelia** — concert posters by Wes Wilson and Victor Moscoso
- **1990s rave flyers** — anonymous designers working under deadline for warehouse events
- **Y2K aesthetics** — chrome, translucency, and digital maximalism
- **Contemporary acid graphics** — a revival movement pushing these ideas through modern tools

## Built With

This site runs on [Hwaro](https://github.com/hahwul/hwaro), a static site generator written in Crystal. The design is pure CSS — no JavaScript frameworks, no build tools. Just raw markup and style.
