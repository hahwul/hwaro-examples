## 0630 Local

Arrived at Ny-Alesund after two days navigating heavy pack ice through Kongsfjorden. The settlement sits low against the hillside, a handful of structures barely distinguishable from the snow cover at distance. Only the radio mast and the orange hull of the supply vessel at dock gave any indication of human presence.

Temperature at landfall was negative twenty-seven. Wind from the north-northwest at eighteen knots, producing an effective windchill well below negative forty. Visibility had dropped to four hundred meters by the time we secured the vessel and began offloading equipment.

## Station Assessment

The primary research building remains structurally sound. Heating systems operational. The exterior instrument array requires recalibration -- several anemometers show drift consistent with prolonged icing. Solar panels are buried under seventy centimeters of compacted snow and will need clearing before they contribute any meaningful charge.

## Ice Conditions

Fjord ice is thicker than predicted for this date. Satellite imagery from last week showed open leads to the west, but these have since closed. We observed pressure ridging along the northern shore, some formations reaching two meters above the surface. This suggests active compression from wind-driven pack ice entering the fjord mouth.

## Notes

The silence here has a specific quality. It is not the absence of sound so much as the presence of stillness. The wind stops and the world becomes a held breath. Then it returns and everything moves again.

Provisions are adequate for thirty days. Next transmission scheduled in seventy-two hours.
