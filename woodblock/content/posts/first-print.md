The first time paper meets ink and wood, the result is always a surprise. No matter how carefully the block was carved, the way the ink bleeds into the fibers of the paper adds a layer of character that cannot be fully predicted.

<!-- more -->

### The Reveal

Lifting the paper from the block is a moment of pure anticipation. In this first impression, we see where the gouges were too shallow, where the ink was too thick, and where the grain of the wood decided to make itself known.

### Lessons Learned

*   **Consistency**: Achieving a uniform layer of ink requires patience.
*   **Pressure**: Too much pressure can blur the fine lines.
*   **Texture**: The wood itself is a collaborator in the final piece.

Each print is a step towards understanding the medium more deeply.
