## Kenji Okonkwo &mdash; Gold

Twenty-four, born in Suva, trained in Apia. Kenji entered the final as the second fastest qualifier but executed the cleanest start of his career: a 0.128 reaction, an upright transition at 35 metres, and a final ten-metre split of 0.81 seconds. He crossed the line with the slimmest possible smile, leaning only because form demanded it.

His coach, Ioane Faleolo, described the race simply: "He did exactly what we drew on the whiteboard. The stopwatch agreed."

## Temi Adewale &mdash; Silver

Four-time continental champion from Lagos, Temi ran the race of her life and still finished four-hundredths short. Her 9.62 becomes the fastest non-winning time in championship history, and her name now sits above three of the four previous world records on the all-time list.

## Aleksei Kovac &mdash; Bronze

The only finalist over thirty, Kovac anchored the Baltic 4x100m relay at three previous championships. Bronze here completes the set &mdash; he has now medalled in sprint, hurdles, and relay events across his career. He announced his retirement from individual sprints in the mixed zone seven minutes after the race.
