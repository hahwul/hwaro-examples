The visual vocabulary of burlesque is built on contrast: the softness of velvet against the flash of sequins, the depth of shadow against the brilliance of spotlight.

<!-- more -->

## Material Language

Every material in burlesque tells a story. Velvet speaks of luxury, intimacy, and the weight of tradition. Sequins catch the light and scatter it in a thousand directions, demanding attention. Feathers suggest movement, grace, and a touch of the fantastical.

## Texture in Digital Design

On the screen, we translate these physical textures into visual ones. Deep gradients mimic the pile of velvet. Subtle shimmer effects capture the sparkle of sequins. Soft shadows and glow effects recreate the interplay of stage lighting with richly textured costumes.

## The Palette of Glamour

A true burlesque palette goes beyond simple color choices. It embraces the full spectrum of burgundy — from near-black to rose — layered with gold that ranges from antique brass to champagne. Black provides the stage, the frame, the negative space that makes everything else shine.
