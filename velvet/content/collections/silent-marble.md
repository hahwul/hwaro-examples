## The Vision

Architecture is the starting point. The columns of a Roman temple, the curve of a Brancusi sculpture, the negative space in a Noguchi garden. Silent Marble takes these forms and renders them in fabric.

The palette is monochromatic: ivory, cream, stone, and ash. Without colour to distract, the cut of each piece becomes the entire statement.

## Key Pieces

**The Column Dress** -- Duchess satin cut on the bias, falling in a single unbroken line from shoulder to floor. There are no seams visible from the front. The construction is entirely concealed.

**The Plinth Coat** -- Structured wool with an exaggerated shoulder and minimal collar. The coat stands on its own when placed on a hanger, a testament to the rigour of its tailoring.

**The Chisel Trouser** -- High-waisted crepe with a knife-edge crease and a slightly cropped hem. The fit is exact. Alterations are included with every purchase.

## Process

Each piece in Silent Marble begins as a clay maquette sculpted by hand in our Paris atelier. Only after the three-dimensional form is approved does the pattern-making begin.
