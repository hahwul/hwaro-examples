In chemistry, reaction kinetics is the study of how fast reactions occur and what factors influence their rate. The same principles apply remarkably well to software product development.

## Rate-Determining Steps

Every reaction has a rate-determining step -- the slowest step that controls the overall speed. In your product pipeline, this might be:

- **Code review bottlenecks** that queue up pull requests for days
- **Design handoff friction** that creates misalignment between intent and implementation
- **Deployment ceremonies** that batch releases into risky, infrequent events

Identifying your rate-determining step is the first act of optimization.

## Concentration and Collision Theory

Reactions proceed faster when reactant molecules collide more frequently. In product terms, this means increasing the frequency and quality of interactions between the people and information that need to come together.

## Temperature and Energy

Raising the temperature increases molecular motion and reaction rates. In teams, temperature is analogous to urgency, focus, and energy. But too much heat causes decomposition -- burnout, shortcuts, and technical debt.

The art is finding the optimal operating temperature for your team. Catalyst's Titration Dashboard helps you monitor and adjust.

## Applying the Framework

Start by mapping your current product pipeline as a reaction mechanism. Identify each step, measure its duration, and look for the bottleneck. Then apply targeted catalysis -- the right tool, process change, or structural adjustment -- to accelerate that specific step.

The results compound. Small improvements to your rate-determining step produce outsized gains in overall throughput.
