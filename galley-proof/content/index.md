## What is a galley proof?

A <span class="annot">galley proof<span class="proof-number">1</span></span> is the first complete typeset impression of a text, printed in a single long column (the "galley" of the compositor's tray) before the pages are broken and numbered. It is the stage at which most editorial corrections are made &mdash; the text is set, but the layout is not yet committed. Corrections made here cost little; corrections made after pagination cost dearly.

The present website is a visual essay in the form of a galley proof: the main text runs in a single justified column, with the proofreader's margin to the right. The marginal marks are drawn in the hand and the inks of the trade &mdash; the red of the copy-editor, the blue of the query, the dotted leader from the mark to the line it concerns.

## The rules of the galley

<p class="annotated">Every correction is a <span class="dele">very</span> deliberate act. The copy-editor does not <span class="insert-caret">&#x2227;</span>merely<span class="insert-caret">&#x2227;</span> tidy; she interrogates. Each mark asks the author a question, and each answer &mdash; <span class="stet">stet</span> or amend &mdash; is entered in a numbered list at the end of the galley.<span class="proof-number">2</span></p>

<p class="annotated">The margin is the site of the conversation. Read outward from the column &mdash; the leader line carries your eye from the offending word to the note that concerns it.<span class="proof-number">3</span> Leader lines are always drawn in the same colour as the mark; dotted, for a query; solid, for a correction; wavy, for an emphasis or tonal note.</p>

<p class="annotated">The body face is set in <span class="annot">Crimson Text</span>, with leading fixed to the baseline grid. The margin is set in the editor's own hand &mdash; a cursive, unrepeatable thing &mdash; and is treated not as part of the typography, but as an occasion.<span class="proof-number">4</span></p>
