<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">I.</span>
Come gather round, ye prentices bold,
And hearken to this tale untold,
Of a pressman stout and greatly wise,
Who set his forme before his eyes.
</div>

<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">II.</span>
He took his rule and took his quoin,
He measured every letter fine,
Yet when the sheet came from the press,
The whole was pied, a sorry mess.
</div>

<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">III.</span>
So learn ye well, both young and old:
No broadside printed can be sold
Without a patient hand and eye,
Nor ballad sung beneath the sky.
</div>

## Fresh from the Press

The present sheet gathers verse and curiosity after the manner of the old street-papers &mdash; the flimsy broadsides once pasted to alehouse walls and hawked by travelling chapmen from Cheapside to the provinces. Each item is set in true Roman letter, with displayed lines in the blackletter face, after the usage of the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries.

Read on, good reader, and mark well the [Ballads](/ballads/) entered in our register.
