The distance between Sol and its nearest neighbor, Proxima Centauri, is 4.24 light-years. Written in kilometers, that number loses all meaning: 40,113,000,000,000. The human mind was not built to hold such quantities.

## Not Empty

The interstellar medium is often described as a vacuum, but this is imprecise. Every cubic centimeter of interstellar space contains, on average, one atom. Mostly hydrogen. Occasionally helium. These atoms drift in darkness for billions of years, rarely encountering another particle.

Consider the loneliness of a single hydrogen atom in the void between stars. It exists. It has mass, spin, charge. But it has no context. No molecule to belong to, no surface to rest upon. It is the most minimal form of matter -- a single proton orbited by a single electron, suspended in nothing.

## The Space Between

We speak of space as if it were a container. A box that holds stars and planets and dust. But space is not a container. Space is the relationship between things. Where there are no things, there is only geometry -- the curvature of spacetime folding over itself, waiting.

## Dark Energy

Seventy percent of the universe is made of something we cannot see, touch, or directly detect. We call it dark energy because we have no better name. It pushes the universe apart, accelerating the expansion, ensuring that the distances between stars will only ever increase.

The void grows. This is perhaps the most unsettling fact in all of cosmology. The nothing between everything is getting larger.
