## About this publication

This publication was composed and produced using Hwaro, a static site generator. Its design reflects the material and aesthetic qualities of handmade paper, drawing on the traditions of the Arts and Crafts movement and the private press tradition.

## Typography

**Display faces.** Headings are set in Vollkorn, a warm, versatile serif face whose generous proportions and slightly organic character evoke the Arts and Crafts revival typefaces inspired by William Morris and the Kelmscott Press. Its bold weight carries the authority needed for chapter titles while maintaining the approachable warmth appropriate to a craft publication.

**Body text.** The running text is set in Vollkorn at regular weight, with Merriweather as a fallback. Both faces were designed for extended reading on screen and in print, with open counters, generous x-heights, and carefully balanced stroke contrast that supports sustained attention.

## Visual design

The color palette is drawn from the materials of the paper workshop -- the warm cream of rag paper, the umber of wooden moulds, the gold-brown of dried plant fibers. All decorative borders use SVG deckle-edge patterns that echo the irregular torn edge of handmade paper. SVG paper fiber mesh textures provide subtle page backgrounds that evoke the visible fiber structure of a handmade sheet held to the light.

No CSS gradients are used in the design. All decorative elements are produced with inline SVG.
