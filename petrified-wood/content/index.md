Welcome to the Petrified Wood. Here, time has stopped, transforming ancient trees into solid, colorful minerals over millions of years.

## The Transformation

The process begins when wood is buried under sediment, protecting it from decay. Over immense stretches of time, mineral-laden groundwater percolates through the porous structure of the wood.

- **Silica** forms the bulk of the replacement, often crystallizing as quartz or opal.
- **Iron oxides** create the vivid reds, oranges, and earthy browns.
- **Manganese** streaks the wood in deep blacks and purples.
- **Copper** deposits bring flashes of green and blue.

## Enduring Beauty

Unlike living wood that bends and decays, these fossilized remnants are dense and unyielding. They serve as stone monuments to ancient forests that once breathed under skies we have never seen.

> "A piece of petrified wood is not just a stone; it is a clock that has recorded a million years in its heart."

Explore the dense rings, the crystalline intrusions, and the stark contrast between organic form and inorganic substance.
