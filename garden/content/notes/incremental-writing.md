Incremental writing is the practice of building a piece of writing through many small sessions rather than one concentrated effort. Each session adds a paragraph, refines an argument, or fills in a gap.

## The Approach

Instead of sitting down to write a complete essay from scratch, you:

- Start with a rough outline or a single paragraph
- Return to the piece regularly, adding and revising
- Let ideas develop naturally over days or weeks
- Publish when the note reaches a useful threshold, not when it is "perfect"

## Why It Works for Gardens

This approach aligns naturally with [digital gardens](/notes/digital-gardens/), where content is expected to evolve. A note begins as a seedling -- perhaps just a title and a few bullet points -- and grows through incremental additions into a budding or evergreen piece.

## Reducing Friction

The biggest advantage of incremental writing is reduced friction. Starting a blank page from scratch is psychologically difficult. Adding a paragraph to an existing note is easy. Over time, these small additions compound into substantial bodies of work.
