## A Note From the Keeper

These pages serve as the interior face-plate of a private collection &#8212; a modest library that began with
three volumes carried home from an estate sale in the winter of 1972 and has since grown, unevenly
and without apology, into something like a household. Every book that enters the shelves is
inscribed with its own bookplate: a small ceremony, a signature of possession, an invitation to its
eventual finder to return the volume should it ever wander.

What follows is a record of those plates. Each entry names a book, its accession number, the shelf
where it resides, and the circumstances under which it arrived. Readers may browse by subject register,
consult the keeper&#39;s notes, or simply let their eye drift across the catalogue and see what catches.
