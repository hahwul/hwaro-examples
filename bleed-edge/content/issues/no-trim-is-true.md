Walk onto any bindery floor and ask the operator where the trim falls. He will tell you the truth: it falls wherever the stack happens to settle when the blade comes down. The guillotine is a precision instrument measured against a stack of 500 sheets; by sheet 247, the blade has drifted by 0.3 millimetres. By sheet 498, it has drifted by nearly a full millimetre.

## The Acceptable Drift

Commercial print treats drift as a defect to be minimised. Our editorial position is the inverse: drift is the medium. A magazine in which every copy has an identical trim is a magazine that has been re-printed digitally, page by page, which is a different object entirely.

> A stack of 500 hand-cut books is 500 books. A stack of 500 perfectly identical digital prints is one book, printed 500 times.

When you receive your copy of BLEED EDGE, the trim on your copy is not the trim on any other copy. Your magazine is 210mm wide in some places, 209.4mm in others, 210.6mm in still others. The safe area &mdash; the rectangle in which we guarantee text will be visible &mdash; is drawn 12mm from the trim line, which is to say 12mm from wherever your particular trim happens to fall.

## How We Design For This

Our layout is governed by three rules:

1. **Images always bleed.** Every photograph, every flat fill, every illustration extends at least 6mm past the trim on every side it touches. This is twice the specified bleed, because we do not trust the guillotine.
2. **Text is set inside the safe area.** Our safe area is larger than the industry standard &mdash; 14mm from the trim, rather than 12mm &mdash; because drift can be cumulative across a signature.
3. **Page numbers live in the bleed.** They print in fluorescent orange, along the outer edge of the page, and are intentionally cut through. Half a numeral is still a numeral.

### The Compromise of the Spine

The one place where we do not run to bleed is the gutter. A perfect binding with 4mm glue cannot accept ink at the inner margin; the glue will bleed through the paper and discolour the ink. We leave the innermost 8mm of the gutter empty of design, which is to say: the only white space in the magazine is at the spine.

This is a production constraint, not an aesthetic choice, and we make no secret of it.

## The Colophon

This issue is set in Anton for display and Inter for body, with occasional incursions from Noto Sans for non-Latin script. Printed in Seoul on a Heidelberg Speedmaster XL 106, 175 lpi, CMYK plus Pantone 021 U fluorescent orange. Run length: 2,400 copies, of which an estimated 127 were discarded for trim drift in excess of 1.2mm.
