## the set

Forty-five minutes on the dot. If the horn section is late taking their spots, the conductor counts in without them and they join on song two. That is the rule. The set is rehearsed as a single continuous piece with four named movements — there are no gaps for applause between them and the band does not stop to tune.

### stage layout

Twelve players across a single-tier riser. Horn section stage-right, three trumpets and a trombone, all on dynamic mics. Drum line center, four snares and a floor tom. Strings stage-left, sharing two wedges. The conductor stands downstage-center on a small podium, facing the band, back to the house.

### what to listen for

- The low-end does not come from a bass guitar. It comes from the floor tom.
- The horns play in unison for the first three minutes and do not split into harmony until the second movement.
- Every piece resolves to the same major chord. It is the only thing the group agrees on.

### changeover notes

Holdfast leaves the stage at 20:50 sharp. The riser stays. The wedges get repatched. The drum world stays struck until the downstage crew pulls it upstage for the headliner. Ten minutes. Go.
