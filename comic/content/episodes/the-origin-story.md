Every great adventure begins somewhere. For this site, it started with a simple idea: what if a blog could feel like flipping through your favorite comic book?

## The Spark

It began with a sketch on a napkin. A few rectangles, some bold lines, and suddenly the page had *energy*. Traditional blog layouts are wonderful, but they can feel static. Comics are dynamic. They guide your eye, control pacing, and make every panel count.

## Panels as Structure

In comics, panels aren't just containers. They're storytelling devices:

- **Wide panels** slow the reader down, creating atmosphere
- **Narrow panels** speed things up, building tension
- **Overlapping panels** create depth and urgency

> This is not just a blog. This is a story told one panel at a time.

We brought this philosophy to the web. Each content block is framed with intention, each section given its own visual weight.

## The Rules

Three rules guide this design:

1. **Bold borders define space** - every element knows where it stands
2. **Color communicates mood** - bright accents draw the eye to what matters
3. **White space is breathing room** - even comics need gutters between panels

The origin story is just the beginning. In the next episodes, we'll explore the technical details of building panel layouts for the web and how speech bubbles can transform how we present quotes and commentary.
