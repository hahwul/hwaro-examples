## The Art of Subtle Depth

Bas-relief is a sculptural technique in which figures are carved with a shallow overall depth against a flat background. Unlike high relief, where forms project boldly into space, bas-relief achieves its power through restraint --- through the quiet insistence that depth need not be dramatic to be felt.

This theme draws from that same principle. Every element on these pages exists at a carefully considered elevation. Shadows are not decoration; they are the medium through which structure becomes legible. The interplay of light and surface creates hierarchy without excess.

## Design Principles

**Elevation as meaning.** Each component occupies a distinct layer above the surface. Cards rise. Headers anchor. Content breathes between them.

**Material honesty.** The palette is drawn from limestone, sandstone, and marble --- warm neutrals that carry centuries of carved tradition. No artificial color. No visual noise.

**Typographic carving.** Headings are set in Playfair Display, a typeface whose high contrast and sharp serifs evoke the precision of a chisel. Body text uses Source Sans for clarity at every scale.

**Shadow as structure.** Rather than borders or backgrounds, this theme uses layered box-shadows to define relationships between elements. Light falls consistently from above-left, creating a unified sense of physical space.
