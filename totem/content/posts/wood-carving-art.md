## Project Overview

The Cedar Spirit Carving is a standing figure carved from a single section of old-growth Western Red Cedar, salvaged from a storm-fallen tree on Vancouver Island. The piece stands 48 inches tall and depicts a guardian spirit figure with outstretched wings and an open mouth -- a welcoming gesture in the tradition of coastal longhouse entrance figures.

## Process

### Selection of the Wood

Choosing the right piece of cedar is the first and most critical decision. The wood must be straight-grained, free of excessive knots, and properly aged. This particular section had been drying naturally for three years after the tree fell, giving it the ideal moisture content for carving -- firm enough to hold detail, soft enough to work with hand tools.

### Roughing Out

The initial form was established using a D-adze, removing large sections of wood to reveal the basic silhouette of the figure. At this stage, the carver works fast and bold, thinking in large planes and volumes. The figure must emerge from the cylinder of the log -- arms from the sides, face from the front flat, wings sweeping back.

### Detail Carving

With the form roughed out, the straight knife becomes the primary tool. Every formline was carefully incised, following the traditional rules of weight and flow. The eyes -- the most important feature -- were carved last, as they bring the figure to life. Ovoid eye sockets frame circular irises, with the eyelid line giving expression and character.

### Surface Treatment

The finished surface retains subtle tool marks from the straight knife, giving the piece a handcrafted texture that catches light differently from every angle. No sandpaper was used -- only sharp steel on clean cedar.

### Painting

Natural pigments were mixed with a binder of salmon egg oil, following traditional practice:

- **Black** -- charcoal ground fine and mixed smooth, applied to primary formlines
- **Earth Red** -- ochre clay collected and processed, filling secondary design areas
- **Turquoise** -- copper carbonate mineral, used sparingly for accent areas and eye pupils

## Dimensions

- Height: 48 inches
- Width: 14 inches at widest (wing span)
- Depth: 10 inches
- Weight: approximately 22 pounds
- Wood: Western Red Cedar (*Thuja plicata*)

## Techniques Referenced

The carving draws from the northern style tradition, particularly the bold formline approach of Haida master carvers. The proportional system follows the classical three-head-height ratio for standing figures, with the face occupying the top third and the wings and body filling the lower two-thirds.

The open mouth and outstretched arms reference the welcoming figures traditionally placed at the entrances of great longhouses, inviting guests to enter and share in the warmth and stories within.
