The Prime Meridian passes through the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, making London the symbolic center of global timekeeping. GMT (Greenwich Mean Time) has served as the reference point for all other time zones since 1884.

<!-- more -->

## Timekeeping Heritage

The Royal Observatory was established in 1675 by Charles II. The famous red time ball atop the observatory has dropped at precisely 13:00 every day since 1833, originally allowing ships on the Thames to set their marine chronometers.

## The City and Time

London operates on GMT in winter and BST (British Summer Time, UTC+1) from the last Sunday in March to the last Sunday in October. Big Ben's clock mechanism, designed by Edmund Beckett Denison, has kept time since 1859 with remarkable accuracy.

## Key Meridian Facts

- **Longitude:** 0 degrees (Prime Meridian)
- **Standard Time:** GMT (UTC+0)
- **Summer Time:** BST (UTC+1)
- **Observatory Founded:** 1675
