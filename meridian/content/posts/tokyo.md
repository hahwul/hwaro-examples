Japan Standard Time is defined by the 135th meridian east, passing through Akashi in Hyogo Prefecture. Japan does not observe daylight saving time, making JST constant throughout the year.

<!-- more -->

## Precision Culture

Japan's relationship with time is deeply cultural. The Shinkansen bullet train system operates with an average delay of less than one minute per year. Train stations display departure times to the second, and a delay of even 30 seconds warrants a formal delay certificate.

## The 135th Meridian

The Akashi Municipal Planetarium marks the exact location where the 135th meridian east crosses Japan. A monument there serves as a reminder that this line determines the time for over 125 million people.

## Key Timezone Facts

- **Longitude:** 139.6503 E (Tokyo) / 135 E (Standard Meridian)
- **Standard Time:** JST (UTC+9)
- **Daylight Saving:** Not observed
- **Time Signal:** JJY radio station broadcasts from Fukushima
