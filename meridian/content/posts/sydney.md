Sydney operates on Australian Eastern Standard Time, ten hours ahead of UTC. Among major world cities, it is one of the first to greet each new day, sitting near the International Date Line.

<!-- more -->

## The Date Line Proximity

Australia's eastern seaboard sits close to the International Date Line at 180 degrees longitude. When it is Monday morning in Sydney, much of the world is still in Sunday. This forward position gives Australian markets an early look at the week's trading.

## Sydney Observatory

Established in 1858, the Sydney Observatory on Observatory Hill once served as the city's official timekeeper. A time ball, similar to Greenwich's, was dropped daily at 13:00 to allow ships in the harbor to calibrate their chronometers.

## Key Timezone Facts

- **Longitude:** 151.2093 E
- **Standard Time:** AEST (UTC+10)
- **Summer Time:** AEDT (UTC+11)
- **DST Change:** First Sunday of October / First Sunday of April
