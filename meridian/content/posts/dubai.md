Dubai operates on Gulf Standard Time, four hours ahead of UTC. The city never observes daylight saving time, providing a fixed reference point in a region that bridges European and Asian business hours.

<!-- more -->

## The Crossroads Clock

Dubai's position at UTC+4 places it in a unique temporal window. When London's markets close at 16:30 GMT, it is 20:30 in Dubai. When Tokyo opens at 09:00 JST, it is 04:00 in Dubai. This overlap makes the city a natural hub for international commerce.

## Dubai International Airport

DXB is one of the world's busiest airports by international passenger traffic. Its flight information display systems track arrivals and departures across dozens of time zones simultaneously, a constant reminder of the city's role as a global connector.

## Key Timezone Facts

- **Longitude:** 55.2708 E
- **Standard Time:** GST (UTC+4)
- **Daylight Saving:** Not observed
- **Business Hours Overlap:** Bridges London and Tokyo trading sessions
