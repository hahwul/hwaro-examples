<h4>Fira Mono 11/14 Setting Sample</h4>

The rain in Spain falls mainly on the plain. Fira Mono sets true at 6.6pt advance, yielding 32 characters per pica at this size; a 28-pica measure carries 44 characters per line, sufficient for code and for any tabular setting in which alignment must be exact to the character.

<div class="forme">
<span class="forme-label">SPECIMEN &middot; COURIER PRIME &middot; 11 PT</span>

<h4>Courier Prime 11/15 at 28 picas</h4>

Courier Prime, the more open of our two monospaced faces, is reserved for italic quotation and for long passages in which readability at small sizes matters. Set here at 11 on 15, leaded to preserve the horizontal texture of the block. In practice we use it only in editorial apparatus: footnotes, captions, and the occasional block quotation.

The rain in Spain falls mainly on the plain. Courier Prime offers italic as well, which Fira Mono does not, and we depend on this for cases where italic is required and the monospace is not negotiable.

## Faces We Do Not Hold

We get asked, regularly, about faces we do not hold. For the record, we do not hold any sans-serif face, any blackletter, any script face beyond the simplest italic, any type larger than seventy-two point, any type smaller than eight point, or any non-Latin alphabet. We have a standing arrangement with a firm in Edinburgh (Gaelic) and with a firm in Paris (Greek) if a commission requires it.

We do not plan to acquire a sans-serif. We have not planned to acquire a sans-serif since 1972. The absence is considered a matter of house policy rather than of shop budget, though the two shade into one another over the decades.
