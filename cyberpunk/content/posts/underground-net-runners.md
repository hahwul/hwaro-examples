# Inside the Ghost Circuit: How Underground Netrunners Evade Corporate ICE

In the lower levels of District 7, behind a noodle shop with no name, there is a door that leads to one of the sprawl's last independent netrunning dens. The people who work here call themselves the Ghost Circuit, and they have been breaching corporate firewalls for over a decade without a single member being flatlined by ICE.

## The Evolution of Corporate ICE

Intrusion Countermeasure Electronics -- ICE -- has evolved dramatically since the early days of corporate network defense. Modern black ICE does not just disconnect intruders; it traces neural links back to the source and delivers targeted biofeedback designed to incapacitate or kill.

The megacorps invest billions annually in these defenses. Militech alone employs over 12,000 ICE architects. Breaking through these systems without getting burned requires a combination of custom hardware, social engineering, and raw skill that few possess.

## Tools of the Trade

The Ghost Circuit relies on three core technologies:

**Signal Ghosting** -- A technique that routes neural traffic through dozens of compromised IoT devices, making the runner's true location effectively untraceable. The lag is brutal, requiring runners to compensate with predictive algorithms.

**Polymorphic Decks** -- Custom cyberdecks with firmware that rewrites itself every 0.3 seconds, presenting a different hardware signature to ICE scanners on each pulse. Building one requires components from at least four different manufacturers to avoid pattern recognition.

**Organic Buffers** -- Perhaps the most controversial tool: bioengineered neural tissue that sits between the runner's brain and their cyberdeck, absorbing the worst of any ICE feedback before it reaches organic neurons. The tissue degrades with each hit and must be regularly replaced.

## The Risk

Despite their precautions, netrunning remains extraordinarily dangerous. The Ghost Circuit has lost members -- not to ICE, but to the cumulative neurological damage that comes from years of jacking into hostile networks.

"Everyone has a shelf life in this business," one senior runner told me. "The trick is knowing when to unplug for good."

## Why They Do It

The Ghost Circuit is not a criminal operation in the traditional sense. Their primary targets are corporate data vaults containing information they believe the public has a right to access: environmental contamination reports, worker safety violations, political manipulation campaigns.

"Someone has to keep the corps honest," the runner said. "And it is not going to be the people they own."
