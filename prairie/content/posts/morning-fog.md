Before the sun burns through, the fog settles low across the gravel roads. Fence posts disappear into white. The world shrinks to just a few feet ahead.

<!-- more -->

## Driving Through Silence

On foggy mornings, the prairie feels like a different place entirely. Sound is muffled. Headlights barely cut through the white. You slow down, grip the wheel, and trust the road.

The familiar landmarks vanish — the old oak at the crossroads, the red barn on the hill. You navigate by instinct and memory.

## When the Fog Lifts

Around mid-morning, the sun wins. The fog peels back in layers, revealing dew-soaked fields that catch the light like glass. Spiderwebs glitter on fence wire. The world returns, sharp and bright.

These are the mornings I live for.
