## Summary of Contributions

This dissertation has made three principal contributions to the understanding of topological phases in twisted bilayer graphene:

**First**, we have developed a comprehensive theoretical framework connecting the flat-band topology of the Bistritzer-MacDonald model to the symmetry-breaking patterns of correlated insulating states. Our Hartree-Fock calculations predict quantum anomalous Hall states at odd integer fillings (nu = +1, +3) driven purely by electron-electron interactions, without any external magnetic field.

**Second**, we have provided direct spectroscopic evidence for the correlated insulating state at nu = +2 through scanning tunneling spectroscopy at 300 mK. The measured gap magnitude (5.2 meV), spatial modulation pattern, and spectral line shape are in quantitative agreement with the valley-polarized insulator predicted by our calculations.

**Third**, we have identified signatures of topologically-protected edge states near structural boundaries, consistent with the nontrivial topology of the correlated ground states. While not yet definitive, these observations provide the strongest experimental indication to date that the correlated insulators in magic-angle TBG carry nonzero topological invariants.

## Broader Implications

The results of this thesis extend beyond twisted bilayer graphene. The demonstration that flat-band topology and electron correlations conspire to produce topologically nontrivial ground states suggests that the broader family of moire materials -- including twisted trilayer graphene, twisted transition metal dichalcogenides, and moire heterostructures -- may host an even richer variety of topological correlated phases.

## Future Directions

1. **Nonlocal transport:** Hall bar measurements on gated devices at nu = +1 should directly test the QAH prediction through quantized Hall conductance.

2. **Higher magic angles:** The second magic angle (approximately 0.5 degrees) is predicted to have even flatter bands and stronger correlations. Devices at this angle are now within fabrication reach.

3. **Moire of moire:** Stacking three layers with incommensurate twist angles creates a second-order moire pattern that may realize even more exotic phases, including fractional Chern insulators without any external magnetic field.
