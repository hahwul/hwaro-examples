  <h1 class="paper-title">Topological Phases and Correlated States in Twisted Bilayer Graphene</h1>
</header>

Twisted bilayer graphene at the magic angle of approximately 1.1 degrees hosts extremely flat electronic bands that serve as a platform for strongly correlated physics. This dissertation investigates the topological properties of the correlated insulating states that emerge at integer fillings of these flat bands, combining analytical theory, numerical simulation, and scanning tunneling spectroscopy.

We develop a comprehensive Hartree-Fock framework for the interacting flat bands and identify quantum anomalous Hall states at odd integer fillings (nu = +1, +3) characterized by spontaneous time-reversal symmetry breaking and nonzero Chern numbers. These states arise from the interplay between the fragile topology of the noninteracting flat bands and the Coulomb interaction projected into the flat-band manifold.

Experimentally, we perform scanning tunneling microscopy and spectroscopy at 300 mK on magic-angle devices. At nu = +2 filling, we observe a correlated gap of 5.2 meV with spatial modulation matching the theoretical prediction of a valley-polarized insulator. Near structural boundaries, we identify in-gap states consistent with topologically-protected edge modes.

Our results establish a direct connection between flat-band topology and the symmetry-breaking patterns of correlated ground states in moire systems, with implications for the broader family of van der Waals heterostructures.

**Keywords:** *twisted bilayer graphene; magic angle; topological phases; correlated insulators; quantum anomalous Hall effect; scanning tunneling spectroscopy; moire materials*
