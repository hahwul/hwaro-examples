## On the Annex

Room B (the Annex) is the quieter and slower counterpart to the Ballroom. The room holds 180 dancers comfortably, has a lower ceiling which keeps the bass close, and does not have a disco ball. Pilar's three-hour set moves across a much wider tempo range than would be possible in the main room; the middle hour sits at 116 to 118 bpm and opens into considerable space for vocal dub, minimal house, and extended edits of the Portuguese and Angolan catalogues which Pilar knows as well as anyone in the world.

## A Short Biography

Pilar Ito began as a selector at Lux in Lisbon in 2014 and became a resident at Plano B the following year. She relocated to London in 2020 and joined the Reserve rotation in 2022 after a series of guest sets which established her as an unusually literate selector in the broader Lusophone tradition.

Her collection is exceptional in its depth of Kizomba, Semba, Angolan electronic productions of the 1990s, and the Cape Verdean and Mozambican productions which sit alongside them. She resists the label "world music" and insists that all of this material is simply contemporary dance music, the same as anything played in Berlin or Chicago.

## A Note from the Resident

> "The Annex is a reading room for dancers. If you can't hear the bass vibrate inside your sternum, you are not in the right room. Come in, stand still for a moment, feel the air. Then you can start."

Pilar ordinarily includes thirty to forty minutes of Portuguese-language vocal records in each set. The records are not translated; listeners who do not speak Portuguese are invited to hear them as purely melodic and rhythmic material, which is how a good half of her audience has always heard them in any case.
