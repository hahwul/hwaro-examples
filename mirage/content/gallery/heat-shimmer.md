Heat shimmer is the desert made kinetic. While dunes and rock formations hold still, the air above them writhes. Thermal convection creates cells of rising hot air and sinking cool air, and each boundary between them acts as a lens, bending light unpredictably.

Through a telephoto lens, the effect is dramatic. Distant mountains wobble and stretch. The horizon line pulses like a slow heartbeat. Objects that should have sharp edges dissolve into trembling outlines. The world beyond a hundred meters becomes an impressionist painting, rendered not in brushstrokes but in turbulent air.

This collection explores heat shimmer as both phenomenon and metaphor. The photographs capture moments when the distortion is at its most intense -- midday in summer, over dark rock surfaces that absorb and radiate heat with particular ferocity. They are images of the invisible made visible: the atmosphere itself, caught in the act of reshaping what we see.
