The dunes at midday are an exercise in geometry. Each ridge casts a blade of shadow that shifts imperceptibly as the sun tracks overhead. From a distance, the horizon line between sand and sky dissolves entirely -- the two merge into a single pale expanse that seems to breathe.

These photographs were taken in the hour when the wind drops and the sand holds perfectly still. Every grain catches light at a slightly different angle, turning the surface into something closer to fabric than earth. The ridgelines stretch unbroken for miles, each one a horizon unto itself.

What strikes you most is the silence made visible. No trees, no structures, no interruptions. Just the long, patient curve of sand meeting sky, repeated endlessly, each iteration subtly different from the last.
