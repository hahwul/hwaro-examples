## Primary Outcome: Digital Competence Scale for Older Adults (DCS-OA)

The DCS-OA is a 24-item validated self-report scale measuring digital literacy across four domains:

- **Information (6 items):** ability to search, evaluate, and manage digital information
- **Communication (6 items):** email, messaging, video calls, social media use
- **Content creation (6 items):** editing documents, photos, using online forms
- **Safety (6 items):** password management, privacy settings, recognizing threats

Each item is scored 0-4 (never, rarely, sometimes, often, always), yielding a total score range of 0-96. The scale was validated in 8 European languages (Cronbach alpha 0.91-0.94 across language versions).

## Data Collection

Data were collected via structured face-to-face interviews conducted in participants' homes by trained research assistants. The interview lasted approximately 35 minutes and included:

1. Cognitive screening (6-item CIT, 2 minutes)
2. DCS-OA administration (15 minutes)
3. Demographic questionnaire (10 minutes)
4. Technology access inventory (5 minutes)
5. Health status questions (3 minutes)

All data were entered into REDCap on tablets during the interview, with automatic range checks and skip logic. Quality assurance included 5 pct re-interviews within 2 weeks (ICC for DCS-OA total: 0.94).
