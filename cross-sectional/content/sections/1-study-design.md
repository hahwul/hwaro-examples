## Design

This is a cross-sectional, population-based survey conducted at a single time point across six European cities. The cross-sectional design captures a snapshot of digital literacy prevalence and its correlates among community-dwelling older adults, without attempting to establish temporal or causal relationships.

## Study Setting

The six cities were selected to represent geographic and socioeconomic diversity within Western and Central-Eastern Europe:

- **Geneva, Switzerland** -- high income, multilingual (French/German), strong digital infrastructure
- **Warsaw, Poland** -- middle income, rapid digital transformation since EU accession
- **Barcelona, Spain** -- Mediterranean context, mixed digital adoption patterns
- **Amsterdam, Netherlands** -- high income, consistently high digital adoption rates
- **Helsinki, Finland** -- Nordic welfare state, national digital literacy programs
- **Lisbon, Portugal** -- lower-middle income among Western European capitals, older population

## Ethical Approval

The study was approved by the University of Geneva Research Ethics Committee (ref. 2025-0621) and by local ethics boards at each participating institution. All participants provided written informed consent.
