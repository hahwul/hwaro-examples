A journey through layered landscapes, from the first light of dawn to the silence of the night sky.

I. Morning

The Meadow at Dawn

The grass was silver with dew. It bent in slow waves under a wind that carried the smell of wet earth and wild thyme. The sun had not yet cleared the eastern ridge, but its light was already spreading -- a pale wash that turned the mist into something luminous.

II. Forest

Into the Forest Deep

The canopy closed overhead. Light did not arrive directly but was filtered, scattered, and redirected by thousands of leaves. It reached the forest floor in patches and coins, shifting as the wind moved the branches above. The effect was of being underwater.

III. Mountain

The Mountain Pass

Above the treeline, the rules changed. Wind arrived unobstructed, constant and cold. Vegetation clung close to the ground -- alpine grasses, cushion plants, lichens that had taken decades to cover a single stone. Everything here had learned to be small.

IV. Twilight

The Twilight Ridge

The sun was low now, touching the western peaks and turning them into dark cutouts against an orange sky. Colors deepened through a sequence -- pale yellow to deep amber to a red so saturated it seemed impossible.

V. Night

Under the Stars

The first stars appeared while there was still color in the west. They arrived one at a time, tentatively, as though checking whether it was dark enough. Then they emerged in crowds -- hundreds, then thousands, filling the darkness with a cold, steady light.
