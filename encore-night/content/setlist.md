## Per-Song Notes

### Old Porch Light

The opener, the one that begins with thirty seconds of single-string picking in the dark. The house lights come up as the rhythm enters, and the band comes in one at a time.

### Hazel, If You're Listening

Maeve's solo. Ollie sits this one out. Written in 2011 after a long phone call. If you are sitting near the front, watch the second verse, where the dynamics drop almost to a whisper.

### Carolina Evening

Acoustic solo, Maeve only. The one the audience sometimes joins on the chorus. The band agreed years ago not to amplify the audience microphones during this song, so the singing from the room stays in the room.

### Walking The Dog At Dusk

Extended version tonight. Six minutes, with an instrumental outro we have not played in seven years. Duncan has a bass solo in the bridge.

### One More Song &mdash; Encore I

The obvious one. Title track of our third record. The song this night is named after. We have not played it at a closing show in eight years, because we were saving it for this one.

### Goodnight Irene &mdash; Encore II

The traditional, unamplified, the band standing in a semicircle around a single microphone at the edge of the stage. We have closed with it every Old Hall show since 2004.

## Changes &amp; Swaps

If the room feels quiet enough in the first set, we may swap *The Long Commute* for *Kitchen Table*, which has not been on a setlist since 2019. If the room feels big, we will keep to the written set. Ollie carries a backup capo for the swap.

The encore is not negotiable. Do not ask.
