## Maeve Kilgore

Born in Cork, raised on the coast, moved to Manchester at eighteen for music college and never went back. Maeve writes most of the lyrics, usually in the car, always on paper. Her guitar is a 1968 Martin D-28 that she bought from an estate sale in 1999 for three hundred pounds, which was then all the money she had. It has been to every Old Hall show.

She sings in three registers: the speaking voice, the singing voice, and the third one, which only comes out on the high harmony of *Hazel, If You're Listening*. We do not know where that voice lives during the rest of the year.

## Ollie Aspin

Ollie is the steady one. He plays the rhythm, sings the seconds, and has never missed an Old Hall show, not even the year he had appendicitis. He came to that one directly from the hospital, still on fluids, and finished the first set before the doctor in the audience dragged him to the bar and ordered him to sit down. He sat down for the interval and came back for the second set. The doctor is still annoyed about it.

He plays a 1972 Guild F-50, in standard tuning, with a medium pick. Capo two is his default. He does not believe in open tunings.

## Duncan Wray

Duncan came to us from the Manchester Jazz Collective in 2009 when our previous bassist went to teach in Dublin. He was supposed to play three shows. He has played every show since. He plays a 1930s King Mortone upright, which he restored himself. He calls it Ethel.

On the other six nights of the week, Duncan still plays jazz. He maintains that our band is his rest cure. We are not sure whether that is a compliment.

## Rosie Gallagher

Rosie joined in 2013 when we realised the room deserved percussion. She plays a minimal kit -- kick, snare, one floor tom, one crash, two tambourines, a shaker, and a single Turkish finger cymbal that she rings on the last note of *Goodnight Irene*. The finger cymbal is the last sound of every Old Hall show, and we have argued about replacing it and we have never done so.

She grew up in a Clare pipe-band family and still sometimes plays bodhran in a session on the first Thursday of the month. We have tried to book her for more Old Hall shows, and she has always been available.

## Who Else

The band is a four-piece. For specific songs we have sometimes had a fifth, the late Peter Conran on fiddle, who played with us from 2006 until 2018. Peter passed in early 2019. We have not replaced him; we play the songs he played on as a quartet, with his parts left out and a small silence where his fiddle used to enter. Tonight, those silences are still there.

Martin has mixed every show we have ever played at The Old Hall. Willa runs the stage with a spreadsheet and a military calm. Sol designed the amber rig that you will see tonight and has never repeated a lighting cue twice. Teresa keeps the merch table, where we have, each year, printed a very small run of hand-numbered setlists.
