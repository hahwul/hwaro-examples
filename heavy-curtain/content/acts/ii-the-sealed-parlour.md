## Scene and Atmosphere

The full set of the front parlour: heavy burgundy drapes fixed to iron rods, a tea service set for two but taken from three, a writing desk, a locked cabinet. A single practical candelabrum stands on the sideboard.

The stage is lit from above by a single source that, at a cue in the fifteenth minute, is allowed to fail. The gaslight does not return for the remainder of the act; the scene plays out by candelabrum.

## Characters Present

- **Helene** and **Pietra**, through the act
- **Nikolai**, returning at minute thirty-two
- **The Voice**, heard through the east wall

## Staging Notes

> The wall is not real, but the voice must be. Mic the speaker on a cardioid and place her behind the flat. Do not amplify the wall.

The audience should be uncertain whether the Voice is Helene's memory, Pietra's guilt, or a third presence in the house. The script does not resolve this ambiguity; the production should not either.

## Key Exchange

**Pietra.** There is someone here.
**Helene.** The house is empty.
**Pietra.** The house is not empty. The house is only quiet.

## End of Act

The candelabrum is lifted from the sideboard and taken through the double doors stage-left. The stage returns to black while the drapes are still. A second interval is taken.
