There is a specific window of time -- roughly 5:30 to 7:00 PM -- when radio becomes something different. Listeners are not choosing to listen. They are stuck in traffic, tired from work, and the radio becomes a companion rather than entertainment.

## The Golden Hour

Every radio programmer knows this window. The songs you play at 6 PM are not the songs you play at noon. They need to carry weight without demanding attention. They need to feel familiar without being predictable.

## Tonight's Set

We built tonight's show around a single idea: songs that sound better in a car with the windows down.
