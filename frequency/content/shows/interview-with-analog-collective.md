The Analog Collective is a three-person operation based out of a converted garage in the warehouse district. They press cassette tapes -- not as novelty items, but as a deliberate artistic choice. Marcus sat down with founders Rena Okafor, Tomás Herrera, and Yuki Tanabe to talk about why tape still matters.

## Why Cassettes

**Rena**: "A cassette is intimate. It degrades. It warps. It carries the fingerprints of everyone who handled it. Digital files are perfect, and perfection is boring."

**Tomás**: "We are not anti-digital. We use digital tools in our mastering process. But the final product -- the thing you hold, the thing you give to someone -- that should feel like it was made by a human."

## The Process

The Collective runs two Tascam duplicators and a bulk eraser. Each tape is hand-labeled with a stamp kit they bought at a flea market. Runs are limited to fifty copies, sometimes fewer.

## What They Are Working On

Their next release is a split tape featuring two local ambient artists. Expected in April.

**Yuki**: "We have a waiting list of thirty artists. We could release something every week, but that defeats the purpose. Each tape should feel like an event."

## Where to Find Them

The Analog Collective sells exclusively through their mailing list and at local markets. No streaming, no online store.
