There is something irreplaceable about holding a record that nobody has played in forty years. The dust on the sleeve, the slight warp of the vinyl, the mystery of what sound it holds. This week on Vinyl Archaeology, we explore three recently unearthed collections.

## The Warehouse Find

Last month, a collector in Portland stumbled upon two thousand records in the basement of a former distribution warehouse. Most were unsold stock from the late 1970s -- pressings that never made it to store shelves. Among them were several test pressings from labels that no longer exist.

## Grading and Preservation

Not every old record is worth saving, but every record tells a story. We discuss the basics of vinyl grading -- from Mint to Poor -- and explain why a record in Very Good condition can still sound extraordinary on the right turntable.

## What We Played

- **Side A**: A 1974 pressing of an unreleased soul session from Detroit
- **Side B**: A Japanese import of a funk compilation, still sealed
- **Closer**: A hand-labeled acetate with no artist information -- pure mystery
