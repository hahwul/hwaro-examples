The world of glacial ice is a study in light and pressure. As snow compresses over centuries, the air is squeezed out, resulting in ice that absorbs red light and reflects a deep, mesmerizing blue.

## The Glassmorphism Effect

In this design, we mimic the properties of ice using CSS. The core technique is `backdrop-filter: blur()`, which creates the frosted glass effect, making elements look like they are encased in ice.

### Characteristics of Ice

*   **Translucency:** Allows light to pass through, but diffuses it.
*   **Refraction:** Bends light, creating inner glows and sharp shadows.
*   **Fractures:** Internal stress creates beautiful, chaotic cracked patterns.

> "Ice is essentially a mineral, and a glacier is just a huge rock composed of that mineral."

We use SVG filters to simulate these fractures and the frosty surface texture, giving the interface a raw, tactile feel without relying on heavy background images.
