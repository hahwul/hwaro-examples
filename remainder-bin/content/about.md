## The Rooms

The shop occupies four rooms upon a single floor. The front room is the new stock: current titles at full trade price, arranged by section in the ordinary way. The middle room is the secondhand: used books in various conditions, priced by the proprietor's judgement and reviewed seasonally. The back room is the remainder bin itself: publisher's overstock, bookshop returns, and review copies, stickered once and sometimes twice. The fourth room, across the corridor, is the reading room: a few chairs, a small table, a window upon the yard, and the policy that you may sit in any of the chairs for any length of time whether or not you have bought a book.

## The History

The shop has operated from this address since 1987, when our founder - who wishes not to be named in the published description, which he regards as tending to self-importance - opened it in the space formerly occupied by a typewriter repair shop. (The sign over the door still bears the faint ghost of the previous occupation beneath three coats of fresh paint.) The founder retired from day-to-day management in 2015 and sold the business to his longest-serving counter staff, who have continued to operate it upon the same principles.

## The Principles

**A bookshop is a shop, not a museum.** Stock turns. Books that do not sell are reduced; books that still do not sell are reduced again; books that will not sell at any price are given away. This is not disrespectful to the books - it is how the books find their readers.

**A bookshop is a counter, not a website.** We do not maintain an inventory online, we do not operate a search for our own stock, and we do not take orders by email. The serendipity of browsing is the principal pleasure the shop offers, and we will not dilute it with a searchable database.

**A bookshop is where readers come to discover, not to confirm.** We recommend books when asked; we do not recommend books unasked. We do not curate tables by theme. We stock what interests us at the prices we can get and leave the reader to browse.

**A remainder book is still a book.** The remainder spray does not diminish the contents; the stickered price does not alter the prose. The only difference between a remaindered book and the full-price equivalent is that the publisher could not sell it at full price - which is often more a reflection upon the publisher than upon the book.

## Contact

The shop does not offer email correspondence on matters of stock. For matters of business - invoices, bulk trade orders, or the rare correspondence from a publisher or author - letters may be addressed by post to the shop.

The shop telephone rings but is not generally answered; if you wish to establish that we are open, consult the opening hours in the footer of this page.
