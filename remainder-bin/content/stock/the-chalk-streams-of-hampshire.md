## About this Lot

Two copies, each with the bookplate of a former owner - a single Hampshire angler who apparently acquired both upon publication and passed them to us upon downsizing his library earlier this year. One of the two copies carries pencil marginalia throughout, evidently the notes of a reader who knew the streams intimately and occasionally disagreed with the authors on matters of small local fact. The marginalia enhance rather than detract from the reading, and the copy with the pencil is to my mind the more interesting of the two.

## On the Subject

The book gathers the natural history, history, and present condition of the twelve principal chalk streams of Hampshire - the Itchen, the Test, the Meon, the Avon above Fordingbridge, the Hamble, and others. Each stream is treated through a structure of opening landscape essay, historical survey from the Domesday record onwards, an ecological chapter upon the characteristic flora and fauna, and a closing consideration of current pressures upon the system (abstraction, agricultural runoff, invasive signal crayfish, and suchlike).

> The chapters on the Test and the Itchen are standard. The chapter on the Meon is superb and has become, since publication, the reference work upon that system. The authors are too kind to the water companies in their closing chapter, but readers who know the politics will make their own corrections.

> Previous owner's pencil: "Redfern knows the Hamble perfectly; Askew is guessing about the Meon. Trust one and verify the other." Hard to argue with.

## Why it is in the Bin

These two copies came to us through a private purchase from the estate of the former owner. They are priced to move on the merits of the book itself, without any premium for the bookplate or the marginalia, which are not to everyone's taste. The remainder spray to the top edge is the ordinary mark of a trade remainder; the original copies were acquired by the previous owner from the publisher's clearance sale of 2023.
