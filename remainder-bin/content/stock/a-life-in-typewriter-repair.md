## About this Lot

Five copies, all of them publisher's overstock from Fountain & Nib's 2022 first printing. The books were never in bookshops and were purchased direct from the publisher at trade remainder rate. All five are in very good condition, with clean interiors and covers free of creases or bumps.

Harry van Eck operated a typewriter repair shop in Amsterdam from 1968 until his retirement in 2011, specialising in the Olivetti and Hermes machines favoured by the Dutch literary press. His memoir, written in collaboration with his daughter, recounts not only the craft of the trade but the parade of writers, journalists, and clerks who came through the shop across four decades.

## What is Inside

The book moves chronologically through the life, with digressions into the history of particular machines (the Olivetti Lettera 22, the Hermes 3000, the Olympia SM9) as each is introduced by a customer. Van Eck's voice is wry, affectionate, and unsentimental; he has a particular gift for the dialogue of counter exchanges.

> Van Eck writes better than most of the writers whose machines he repaired. He is also generous about those writers, where perhaps a more acid memoirist might not have been.

> The closing chapter, on the difficulty of sourcing ribbons in the present century, has been reprinted in three anthologies of writing on the trades since the book's original publication.

## Why it is in the Bin

The press printed four thousand copies and sold approximately two thousand four hundred through the ordinary trade. The remaining one thousand six hundred were remaindered; we acquired seventy-five copies at trade remainder and have steadily moved them over the last year. Five are left. They are not going up in price.

Ideal reading for anyone with an affection for well-made objects of the obsolete sort.
