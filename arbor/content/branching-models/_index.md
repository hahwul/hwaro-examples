A branching model defines how your team creates, names, and merges branches. The right choice depends on your release cadence, team size, and deployment strategy.

This section covers three widely adopted models:

- **GitFlow** -- a structured model with dedicated branches for features, releases, and hotfixes.
- **Trunk-Based Development** -- a model that favors short-lived branches and continuous integration into a single trunk.
- **GitHub Flow** -- a lightweight model built around `main` and feature branches with pull requests.
