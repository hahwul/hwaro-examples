Typography in cyberpunk aesthetics often blends **Japanese characters** with **Latin scripts** — creating a visual language that feels both foreign and familiar.

## タイポグラフィの融合

The mixing of writing systems is not merely decorative. It reflects a worldview where cultures collide, merge, and create something entirely new.

### Key Principles

**Contrast is everything.** The most effective cyberpunk typography pairs:

- **Bold geometric sans-serifs** (Orbitron, Rajdhani) with **flowing Japanese typefaces** (Noto Sans JP)
- **Large display text** with **small annotation text**
- **Bright neon colors** against **deep dark backgrounds**

### The Role of Katakana

Katakana (カタカナ) is particularly suited to cyberpunk design. Its angular, mechanical shapes naturally complement the geometric nature of futuristic interfaces:

| Katakana | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| ネオン | Neon | Neon |
| サイバー | Saibā | Cyber |
| テクノ | Tekuno | Techno |
| データ | Dēta | Data |
| システム | Shisutemu | System |

### Practical Application

When designing for a cyberpunk theme, consider these guidelines:

1. **Hierarchy through script mixing** — Use Japanese for decorative headers, Latin for body text
2. **Weight contrast** — Heavy weights for impact, light weights for technical feel
3. **Color coding** — Assign different neon colors to different information levels
4. **Spacing** — Generous letter-spacing creates a futuristic, broadcast feel

```css
.heading-jp {
  font-family: 'Noto Sans JP', sans-serif;
  font-weight: 900;
  letter-spacing: 0.15em;
}

.heading-en {
  font-family: 'Orbitron', sans-serif;
  font-weight: 700;
  text-transform: uppercase;
  letter-spacing: 0.3em;
}
```

The fusion of scripts is what gives cyberpunk its unique visual identity — a future shaped by the meeting of East and West.
