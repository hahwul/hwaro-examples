The neon sign is more than a light source — it is a **visual language** that has defined Tokyo's identity for decades.

## ネオンサインの歴史

From the post-war economic boom to the present day, neon signage has evolved alongside Tokyo itself. What began as simple advertising became an art form, a cultural statement, and eventually an aesthetic movement that influenced everything from cinema to web design.

### The Craft

Creating a neon sign is a meticulous process:

1. **Design** — The lettering is drafted, balancing readability with artistic flair
2. **Bending** — Glass tubes are heated and shaped by hand into letterforms
3. **Filling** — Noble gases (neon for red, argon for blue) are sealed inside
4. **Mounting** — The finished sign is installed, often in layers for depth

### Digital Neon

In web design, we recreate the neon effect through CSS:

```css
.neon-text {
  color: #ff2d78;
  text-shadow:
    0 0 7px #ff2d78,
    0 0 10px #ff2d78,
    0 0 21px #ff2d78,
    0 0 42px #ff007f,
    0 0 82px #ff007f;
}
```

The layered `text-shadow` property creates the characteristic glow that fades outward — just like real neon gas emitting light through glass tubing.

### Why Neon Endures

Despite LED technology largely replacing traditional neon tubes, the aesthetic persists because it carries emotional weight. Neon represents:

- **Energy** — The vibrant pulse of urban nightlife
- **Nostalgia** — A connection to the golden age of city signage
- **Mystery** — The allure of dimly lit streets punctuated by brilliant color

> ネオンの光は、言葉以上のことを語る。
