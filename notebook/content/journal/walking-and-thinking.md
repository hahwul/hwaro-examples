Went for a long walk today without my phone. Two hours, no destination, no podcast, no music. Just walking and thinking.

I used to think of walks as exercise -- something to do for my body. Somewhere along the way I realized they are much more important for my mind. The best ideas I have had in the past year all came while walking.

## Why It Works

There is something about the rhythm of walking that loosens up stuck thoughts. When I sit at a desk and try to solve a problem, I tense up. My thinking becomes narrow and forced. Walking opens it back up.

I think it has to do with peripheral attention. When you walk, your brain is processing the environment -- trees, people, sounds, weather -- and that low-level processing somehow frees up the higher-level thinking. You stop trying to force an answer and the answer just shows up.

## The Phone Problem (Again)

Walking with a phone is not really walking. It is sitting at your desk in a different location. The moment I put in earbuds and start a podcast, I lose the benefit. My mind fills up with someone else's thoughts instead of generating its own.

I am trying to walk without my phone at least three times a week. It is harder than it sounds. The first ten minutes feel restless -- my hand keeps reaching for a pocket that is empty. After that, something shifts. The restlessness becomes spaciousness.

## Today's Walk

Walked through the park, then along the river, then through the old neighborhood with the big oak trees. Thought about a project I have been stuck on for weeks. By the time I got home, I had a plan. Not a perfect plan, but a starting point. That is usually all you need.
