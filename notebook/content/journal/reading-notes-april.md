Just finished Verlyn Klinkenborg's *Several Short Sentences About Writing*. The title is literal -- the whole book is written in short sentences, each one making a single point about how to write better.

## Key Takeaways

**Sentences are not just containers for ideas.** They are the ideas. The structure of a sentence shapes the thought it carries. Revising a sentence is revising the thought itself.

**Most people write too long.** Not because they have too much to say, but because they are afraid of silence. Short sentences require you to know exactly what you mean.

**Read your work aloud.** Your ear catches things your eye misses. Rhythm matters. A clunky sentence will sound clunky before it looks clunky.

## Passages I Marked

On revision:

> You are never merely rearranging words. You are rearranging the way you think.

On simplicity:

> A short sentence is not a simplification. It is a clarification.

## My Reaction

This book made me uncomfortable in the best way. I realized how much filler I put in my writing -- hedging phrases, unnecessary transitions, words that exist only to soften what I actually want to say.

I have been editing my journal entries since reading this. They are getting shorter. I think they are getting better.
