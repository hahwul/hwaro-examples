For over a decade, flat design dominated the digital landscape. Clean lines, solid colors, minimal shadows. It was a necessary correction from the excesses of skeuomorphism, but it left something behind — **visual excitement**.

<!-- more -->

## The Return of Richness

Today's design trends are bringing texture back:

- **Glassmorphism** — Frosted glass effects with backdrop blur
- **Aurora gradients** — Soft, flowing color transitions
- **3D elements** — Depth and dimension in UI components
- **Prismatic effects** — Rainbow refractions and spectral highlights

These aren't a return to skeuomorphism. They're a new synthesis — combining flat design's clarity with rich visual texture.

## Glamour as a Design Value

There's a place for minimalism. But there's also a place for **glamour** — design that doesn't just communicate, but dazzles. A diamond doesn't apologize for being brilliant. Why should a website?

Glamorous design:

- Draws attention and creates memorable first impressions
- Signals quality and craftsmanship
- Creates emotional engagement beyond simple usability
- Differentiates in a sea of identical minimal templates

## Balance Is Key

The challenge is maintaining readability and usability while pushing visual boundaries. The best glamorous designs use bold effects as **accents** — a prismatic gradient on a heading, a faceted card border, a spectral glow on hover — while keeping body text clean and legible.

Like a well-cut diamond, the goal is maximum fire without sacrificing clarity.
