Patch 4.7 dropped at midnight and it is already reshaping the competitive meta. The development team has addressed months of community feedback, particularly around Warlord-class dominance and the underperformance of Ashcaller units.

## Warlord Class Rebalance

The Warlord class has dominated top-tier play since patch 4.2. The new changes dial back their scaling without gutting their identity.

**Key Changes:**

- Hellbrand passive reduced from 35% lifesteal to 22%
- Siege Aura now affects only Warlord and Knight units (previously all)
- Infernal Charge cooldown increased: 14s to 18s
- Ruinous Stomp damage reduced by 12% but stun duration increased to 2.4s

## Ashcaller Buffs

Ashcallers have been statistically the weakest ranged unit for two full patches. Patch 4.7 gives them meaningful upgrades.

- Base fire damage increased from 48 to 64
- Ember Wall now persists for 6 seconds (up from 4)
- New passive: Scorchmark — every third hit applies a 3-second burn debuff

## New Mode: Scorched Earth

A limited-time 3v3 elimination mode where terrain permanently burns as the match progresses. The map shrinks organically as fire spreads outward from contested zones. Available April 1–30, 2026.

## Economy Adjustments

Soul Shard conversion rates have been adjusted to reduce end-game hoarding and encourage active play. Players logging in daily during April receive double Ember Coins on weekends.

## Known Issues

The Ashcaller Ember Wall sometimes clips through elevated terrain. A hotfix is expected within 72 hours. Players encountering this in ranked matches should report through the in-game bug tool.

The forge burns hotter now. Adapt or be consumed.
