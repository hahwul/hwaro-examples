Torres del Paine National Park in Chilean Patagonia is home to one of the world's most spectacular trekking routes. The W Circuit showcases glaciers, turquoise lakes, and the iconic granite towers that give the park its name.

<!-- more -->

## The W Circuit

Named for its shape on the map, the W Circuit is approximately 80 kilometers of stunning Patagonian wilderness. Most hikers complete it in 4-5 days, though we took our time over 5 days to fully appreciate the landscape.

### Route Highlights

The circuit visits three major valleys:
1. **Valle Ascencio** - Base of the Torres
2. **Valle Francés** - French Valley
3. **Grey Valley** - Grey Glacier

## Daily Log

### Day 1: Hotel Las Torres to Campamento Torres (18 km round trip)

We started with arguably the most challenging and rewarding section - the approach to Base Las Torres. The final hour involves a steep boulder field, but the payoff is worth every step.

Standing at the base of the three granite towers, with a glacial lake reflecting their imposing faces, is a moment that stays with you forever. We arrived at sunrise, watching as the first light painted the towers in shades of pink and orange.

### Day 2: Hotel Las Torres to Refugio Los Cuernos (11 km)

The trail hugs the shore of Lago Nordenskjöld, offering stunning views of the Cuernos (horns) across the turquoise water. The contrast between the sedimentary black peaks and the granite walls is striking.

Patagonian weather lived up to its reputation - we experienced sun, rain, and fierce wind all in one afternoon.

### Day 3: Los Cuernos to Valle Francés to Refugio Paine Grande (25 km)

This long day includes the spectacular French Valley. The trail ascends into a glacial cirque surrounded by peaks on three sides. The sound of avalanches echoing off distant walls reminded us of nature's power.

The hanging glacier of Frances dominated the valley head, while sharp granite spires rose on either side.

### Day 4: Refugio Paine Grande to Grey Glacier (22 km round trip)

The trail to Grey Glacier follows the shoreline of Lago Grey, where massive icebergs drift lazily past. The glacier itself, fed by the Southern Patagonian Ice Field, stretches several kilometers wide.

We took a catamaran trip to get closer to the glacier face, watching chunks of ice calve into the lake with thunderous splashes.

### Day 5: Exit to Park Entrance (18 km)

The final day involved retracing our steps to the park entrance. Though the weather turned stormy, with horizontal rain and 80 km/h winds, it felt like a fitting conclusion to our Patagonian adventure.

## Weather Considerations

Patagonian weather is notoriously unpredictable. During our 5 days, we experienced:
- Beautiful sunshine
- Driving rain
- Fierce winds (80+ km/h)
- Brief hail
- Temperatures from 2°C to 20°C

**Essential gear**:
- High-quality rain jacket and pants
- Windproof layers
- Multiple base layers
- Waterproof pack cover
- Trekking poles for stability in wind

## Camping vs Refugios

The circuit offers both camping and refugio (hostel) options. We used refugios for convenience, though they book out months in advance during peak season (December-February).

Camping provides more flexibility and solitude, but requires carrying more gear and dealing with high winds.

## Wildlife

Despite the harsh environment, we encountered:
- Guanacos (wild relatives of llamas)
- Andean condors soaring overhead
- Patagonian grey foxes
- Various bird species including ibis and caracaras

## Final Thoughts

The W Circuit is justifiably famous. The combination of massive granite peaks, electric blue glaciers, and constantly changing weather creates an unforgettable trekking experience.

**Difficulty**: Moderate to challenging, primarily due to weather and distance rather than technical difficulty.

**Best time**: November-March (Southern Hemisphere summer), though expect crowds in December-January.

**Permits**: Required and must be booked in advance along with accommodations.

This trek reinforced why Patagonia has such a magnetic pull on adventurers worldwide. The raw, windswept beauty is unlike anywhere else on Earth.
