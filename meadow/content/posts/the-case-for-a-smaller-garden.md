Every spring, the catalogues arrive and the ambition swells. More varieties, more beds, more square footage under cultivation. It is a familiar pattern, and it leads, almost without exception, to the same place: a garden that is half-tended by July and abandoned by August.

This year, I am arguing for the opposite. Grow less. Tend more.

## The Arithmetic of Attention

A single raised bed, four feet by eight feet, properly maintained, will produce an astonishing quantity of food. Succession-planted lettuce, a row of beans, a few tomato plants, some herbs along the edge. If you give that thirty-two square feet your full attention -- consistent watering, timely harvesting, prompt weeding -- it will outperform a plot ten times its size that receives only sporadic care.

The limiting factor in most home gardens is not space. It is time. And time, once honestly accounted for, usually argues for restraint.

## What to Keep

If you are reducing, keep the things that satisfy most. For me, the non-negotiables are:

**Salad greens.** Nothing from a shop compares to lettuce cut five minutes before eating. The variety of textures and flavours available -- butterhead, oak leaf, mizuna, rocket -- makes a garden salad an entirely different experience from a supermarket one.

**Herbs.** A single pot of basil, a patch of thyme, a rosemary bush by the door. Fresh herbs transform the simplest cooking, and they require almost no space.

**One ambitious crop.** Each year, choose one thing that challenges you. This year it might be garlic. Next year, winter squash. This keeps the garden interesting without overwhelming it.

## The Empty Space

There is something unsettling, at first, about a garden with empty space. We are conditioned to fill. But empty space in a garden is not wasted space. It is room for the soil to rest. It is a path you can walk without stepping on seedlings. It is a place where a child can sit, or a cat can sleep, or you can kneel and simply look at what is growing.

A smaller garden, well loved, is a more beautiful thing than a large garden, neglected. And it leaves time for all the other parts of life that deserve attention too.
