## About Meadow

Meadow is a journal about the unhurried life. It began as a collection of handwritten notes -- observations from long walks through pasture land, recipes scribbled on the backs of seed packets, thoughts on what it means to live in rhythm with the seasons rather than against them.

This is not a guide to productivity or self-improvement. It is, if anything, the opposite: a quiet argument for doing less, noticing more, and finding satisfaction in the kinds of tasks that have no measurable output -- tending a row of beans, watching the light change across a field, letting bread rise in its own time.

### What You Will Find Here

- **Seasonal Living** -- Reflections on adapting daily life to the character of each season
- **Kitchen Notes** -- Simple recipes drawn from what grows nearby and what the weather demands
- **Field Observations** -- Writing about landscape, weather, soil, and the creatures that share this place
- **Slow Craft** -- Thoughts on handwork, preservation, and the satisfaction of making things by hand

### A Note on Pace

The entries here arrive when they are ready, not on a schedule. Some weeks bring several; others bring silence. That unevenness is part of the point. A meadow does not bloom on command. Neither does good writing, or a well-considered life.

You are welcome to stay as long as you like.
