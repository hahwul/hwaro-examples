<p class="lead">We are a small house, interested chiefly in the typography of the book's outside &mdash; the jacket, the spine, the wrapper, the case. We are not a literary agency; we do not edit manuscripts. We design and print the thing that surrounds the book.</p>

## Our house style

We believe in a simple, forceful jacket. We do not illustrate; we do not photograph; we do not use pattern or ornament beyond what a single ornament figure can provide. Our display face is **Abril Fatface** &mdash; an ultra-heavy didone whose weight requires no supporting image. Our subtitle face is **Playfair Display** in italic. Our body faces are the plainest we can find: **Georgia** on the web, **Times New Roman** on the page. We do not use the same face for display and body. We do not mix italics.

The result is a jacket that reads as loudly as a road sign, and an interior that reads as quietly as a letter. The jacket claims the book; the text delivers it.

## Our imprint mark

Our imprint mark is a simple square, divided by its diagonals, with a small circle at the centre. It appears in the lower-right corner of every front panel, at a size no larger than the body face would allow. It is not a logo. It is a signature.

## Why we do not restore jackets

A jacket that has been lived with is a jacket that has been read. The marks of handling &mdash; a thumb-smudge on the flap, a slight fade on the spine, a small nick in the front corner &mdash; are the record of the book's life with its reader. To restore them is to pretend the book is new, which it is not.

We do, however, make new jackets for our own out-of-print titles, where a collector asks us to. These are clearly marked as reissues. We do not pretend they are anything other than what they are.

## Getting in touch

Write to `imprint@dustjacket.example` with a query. We answer slowly. If you are writing to ask us to design a jacket for a book we have not published, the answer is almost always no &mdash; not because we dislike the request, but because we cannot design a jacket for a book we have not read.
