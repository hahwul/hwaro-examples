# Roles and Responsibilities

**Document ID:** GOV-001 | **Effective Date:** 2025-06-15 | **Owner:** Chief Compliance Officer

**Classification:** Internal | **Review Cycle:** Annual | **Next Review:** 2026-06-15

---

## Article 1. Governance Hierarchy

1. **Board of Directors** -- Ultimate oversight authority
2. **Governance Committee** -- Policy approval and strategic direction
3. **Chief Compliance Officer** -- Day-to-day governance operations
4. **Policy Owners** -- Responsible for individual policy domains
5. **Department Heads** -- Implementation within their departments
6. **All Personnel** -- Compliance with applicable policies

## Article 2. Role Definitions

### Section 2.1 Governance Committee

Standing body meeting quarterly. Approves new policies and major amendments, sets the annual review schedule, resolves interpretation disputes, and reports governance status to the Board.

### Section 2.2 Chief Compliance Officer

Chairs the Governance Committee, oversees the OGC, ensures regulatory alignment, and reports compliance metrics to the Board.

### Section 2.3 Policy Owners

Hold domain expertise for assigned policies. Draft and maintain content, conduct periodic reviews, coordinate stakeholder input, and submit amendments for approval.

### Section 2.4 Department Heads

Accountable for implementation within their teams. Communicate policies, ensure training completion, report compliance gaps, and participate in reviews.

## Article 3. RACI Matrix

| Activity | Board | Gov. Committee | CCO | Policy Owner | Dept. Head |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Approve policies | I | A | R | C | I |
| Draft policies | I | I | C | R | C |
| Implement policies | I | I | I | C | R |
| Monitor compliance | I | C | A | R | R |
| Annual review | I | A | R | R | C |
| Exception requests | I | A | R | C | R |

**Legend:** R = Responsible, A = Accountable, C = Consulted, I = Informed

## Article 4. Delegation of Authority

- Authority may only be delegated one level down in the hierarchy
- Delegations shall be documented in writing and time-limited
- The delegating party retains accountability
- Delegation of Governance Committee authority requires Board approval
