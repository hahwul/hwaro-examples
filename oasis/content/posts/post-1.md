There is a quality to the early morning that cannot be found at any other hour. The light is softer. The air carries a coolness that will burn away by midday. The sounds of the world have not yet gathered their full volume.

This is the window for stillness.

## Why the Morning Matters

The first minutes after waking set the tone for everything that follows. When we reach immediately for a screen, we hand over our attention before we have had a chance to gather it. The mind fills with other people's words, other people's urgencies, and by the time we rise from bed we are already behind.

A morning stillness practice reverses this pattern. It is not complicated. It does not require special equipment or training. It asks only for a few minutes and the willingness to sit quietly.

## A Simple Approach

Find a comfortable seat. It does not need to be on the floor -- a chair works well. Close your eyes or soften your gaze downward. Breathe naturally. Do not try to control the breath or empty the mind. Simply notice what is already happening.

The thoughts will come. Let them pass like clouds crossing a wide desert sky. You are not trying to stop thinking. You are practicing the act of not following every thought to its conclusion.

## Five Minutes Is Enough

The most common objection is time. But five minutes is enough. Five minutes of genuine stillness is worth more than thirty minutes of distracted sitting. Start small. Be consistent. The practice builds itself over time.

## What Changes

After a few weeks of morning stillness, most people notice a subtle shift. Reactions become responses. The space between stimulus and action widens. There is a little more room to choose how you meet the day.

This is not dramatic transformation. It is something quieter -- a gradual settling, like sediment finding the bottom of a still pool.
