## About Sundew

Sundew is a science blog dedicated to the study of carnivorous plants -- their taxonomy, trapping mechanisms, ecology, and the ongoing research that continues to reveal their remarkable adaptations.

## What We Cover

- **Taxonomy and Classification** -- Systematic descriptions of carnivorous plant genera, from *Drosera* to *Nepenthes*
- **Trapping Mechanisms** -- Detailed analysis of flypaper, snap, pitfall, bladder, and lobster-pot traps
- **Ecology and Habitat** -- Field observations from peat bogs, tropical highlands, and sandy coastal plains
- **Microscopy** -- Close-up examinations of trichomes, glands, and digestive structures
- **Conservation** -- Status updates on threatened species and habitat preservation efforts

## About the Name

The sundew (*Drosera*) is one of the largest genera of carnivorous plants, with over 190 species found on every continent except Antarctica. The name refers to the glistening droplets of mucilage on the tips of their tentacles, which early botanists likened to morning dew sparkling in the sun.
