Jeju in April is green in a way that the mainland is not. The volcanic soil holds moisture, and everything that can grow does grow, densely and urgently. The Eorimok trailhead sits at about 970 meters, already above most of the island's development, surrounded by temperate forest.

## Lower Forest

The first section of the trail passes through old-growth forest. The trees here are tall and closely spaced, their trunks covered in moss. The understory is thick with ferns and early wildflowers. The path is a mix of wooden boardwalk and bare volcanic rock, dark and porous.

The sound environment is remarkable. Running water somewhere below the trail, birdsong from multiple directions, and the creak of branches in the wind. No traffic noise reaches this elevation.

## The Rocky Middle Section

Above 1,200 meters the forest changes. The trees become shorter and more wind-shaped. The trail surface shifts to larger volcanic rocks, requiring careful foot placement. The terrain opens up, and on clear days you can see the coastline far below, a thin white line where the island meets the sea.

Patches of snow lingered in shadowed hollows. In April. The contrast between the snow and the new green growth around it was stark.

## Witse Oreum

The trail reaches Witse Oreum, a subsidiary peak and the turnaround point for the Eorimok route. The vegetation here is sub-alpine: low scrub, hardy grasses, and the famous Hallasan azaleas that were just beginning to bud.

From Witse Oreum, the main Hallasan summit is visible, its crater rim sharp against the sky. The scale of the mountain becomes apparent here in a way it does not from the coast.

## Descent

The return follows the same path. Going down, I noticed things I had missed on the way up: a small stone shrine tucked into a rock face, a section where tree roots had formed a natural staircase, the way the light filtered through the canopy in shifting patterns.

## Trail Notes

- The Eorimok trail closes at certain times for ecological protection; check the schedule
- Start early; afternoon clouds often obscure the views above 1,200 meters
- The wooden boardwalks can be slippery when wet
- Bring layers; the temperature drops about one degree per 100 meters of elevation
