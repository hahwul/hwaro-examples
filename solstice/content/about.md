## The Turning of Light

Solstice is a meditation on the two great pivots of the year: the summer solstice, when daylight reaches its zenith, and the winter solstice, when darkness holds its longest dominion. These two moments define the rhythm of the natural world and, by extension, the rhythm of human experience.

This site itself embodies that duality. During the daylight hours, you see the warm palette of midsummer -- golden tones, the soft glow of peach, the clarity of a sun-drenched afternoon. As night falls, the design shifts to the deep blues and silvers of a winter midnight, the quiet authority of indigo and ice.

## Why Duality Matters

We tend to think of light and dark as opposites, as adversaries in a cosmic struggle. But the solstices teach us something different. They remind us that light and dark are partners, each defining and giving meaning to the other. The longest day contains within it the promise of shortening. The longest night carries the seed of returning light.

This blog explores that interplay through essays on nature, time, perception, and the quiet turning of seasons. Each piece examines some aspect of the relationship between illumination and shadow, presence and absence, warmth and cold.

## The Design

The automatic theme switching is not merely decorative. It is an invitation to notice the time of day, to become aware of where you stand in the daily cycle of light. The sun that orbits through the navigation is a small reminder that we live within patterns far older than ourselves, and that there is wisdom in attending to them.
