## The question

Does the modern firm still need the office &mdash; or has the office outgrown the firm?

The question is put as a yes-or-no, with the understanding that the panel will, as usual, find the question itself contested. The audience votes at 14:00 and again at 15:30.

Positions are declared in advance by the panelists. A panelist may revise their position on stage; that counts for the panel.

## Sample positions

**Dr. Saskia Nordstrom** has written that &ldquo;the office, once removed, does not reappear &mdash; it is replaced by individually negotiated contracts with commercial real estate operators, which is a different thing.&rdquo; Her position is that the firm without an office is not worse; it is a contract pool, and we should stop pretending otherwise.

**Aleksandr Moussa** has argued that the office is where firms learn to disagree in the same room, and that video calls select for politeness over correctness. His position is that the office is not a lease; it is a decision.

**Prof. Jun Heo** has pointed out that labour protections attach to addresses. Remove the address and the protection goes with it. His position will likely turn on specific cases where this has already occurred.

**Sofia Carpentieri** will speak from the position of a factory-plus-engineering firm, where the factory is fixed and the engineering team is distributed. Her position is that the firm is the thing that keeps them a single company.

## After the panel

The panel closes at 15:30. Audience questions from 15:30 until 15:50 in Stage A. The vote is tallied and announced at 15:55.
