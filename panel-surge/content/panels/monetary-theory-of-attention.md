## The question

Does attention behave enough like a currency &mdash; issuable, inflatable, hoarded, redenominated &mdash; for a monetary framework to be useful, or is the analogy actively misleading?

## Panelists

- **01. Dr. Esteban Ramirez** &mdash; Monetary economist, Banco de Espa&ntilde;a
- **02. Cordelia Fen** &mdash; Author, *Platform Reserves*
- **03. Prof. Ogden Stackhouse** &mdash; Media theorist, NYU
- **04. Alix Jankowski** &mdash; Former central-bank researcher, now product strategist at Terrace

## Moderator

Iris Sato, economics editor of the *Kyoto Monthly* and a Panel Surge moderator since 2019.

## Format

Four positions, six minutes each, followed by an hour of structured exchange. Each panelist may challenge one other panelist per round, with the moderator holding the queue. The fourth round is free-for-all.

## Moderator&rsquo;s brief

> &ldquo;The analogy is interesting or useless &mdash; it is never neutral. I want every panelist to pick an outcome measurement: what policy changes or decision changes if their position is right. If nobody can name a consequence, we close early.&rdquo;

&mdash; Iris Sato

## Required reading

The panelists have all agreed to the following as the shared starting point:

- Fen, C. (2024). *Platform Reserves: What if Attention Worked Like a Dollar?*
- Ramirez, E. (2025). &ldquo;Attention aggregates do not respond to interest-rate proxies.&rdquo; *Journal of Monetary Economics* 167.
- Stackhouse, O. (2022). *The Fourth Fungibility*.
- Jankowski, A. (2025). Internal paper, Terrace, released publicly for this panel.

## Audience

No audience vote on this one. This panel is a discussion, not a debate.
