The spring transfer window has closed, and the deals done -- or not done -- could shape the title race, the relegation battle, and everything in between. Here is our assessment of the key business.

## Winners

### Thunderbolts FC

The league leaders added depth where they needed it most, signing defensive midfielder Kofi Mensah from overseas. At 26, Mensah arrives in his prime and gives the squad a reliable rotation option for the run-in.

### Royal City

Two shrewd loan signings bolster their midfield and attack. Both players come with a point to prove, and early signs in training suggest they will integrate quickly into the system.

## Losers

### Phoenix Rising

Despite their league position, Phoenix failed to address their well-documented defensive issues. Manager Andrei Petrov publicly stated he wanted a centre-back, but the club could not agree terms with any targets. With a difficult run of fixtures ahead, this could prove costly.

### Iron Athletic

The departure of their top scorer on loan, with no replacement signed, leaves Iron Athletic significantly weakened. The club insists the move was the player's decision, but the lack of a contingency plan is concerning.

## The Verdict

Overall, this was a quiet window. Most clubs opted for minor tweaks rather than wholesale changes, reflecting the financial caution prevalent across the league. The real question is whether Phoenix's failure to strengthen at the back will cost them in the final stretch.
