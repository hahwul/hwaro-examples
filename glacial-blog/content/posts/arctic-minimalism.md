Refinement through subtraction. This is the core of Arctic design.

When we look at the frozen North, we see a landscape that has been carved by time and elements until only the essential remains. This "glacial" aesthetic is defined by:

1.  **Frosted Glass Textures**: Mimicking the translucency of deep ice.
2.  **Monochromatic Palettes**: Using varying shades of blue, white, and grey.
3.  **Expansive Spacing**: Giving elements room to breathe, like the vast tundra.

## Why it works

In a world filled with high-contrast, noisy interfaces, the Arctic approach provides a sense of calm and sophistication. It's trendy yet timeless, cool yet inviting.
