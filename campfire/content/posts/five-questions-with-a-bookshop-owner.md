Marcus Bell opened Bell Books in a side street in Bath fourteen years ago. The shop has no website. It has no social media presence. It does not sell online. Despite this --- or because of it --- there is a six-month waiting list for his curated reading boxes, and people travel from across the country to visit.

<!-- more -->

**Campfire:** Why no website?

> **Marcus Bell:** Because a website would turn the shop into a brand. A shop is a place. A brand is a performance. I am not interested in performing. I am interested in putting the right book into the right hands. That requires a conversation, not a search bar.

**Campfire:** How do you decide what to stock?

> **Marcus Bell:** I read everything before it goes on the shelf. Not all of it, but enough. If I cannot tell someone something true about a book --- not a blurb, not a review, but something I actually think --- then I do not carry it. The shop is small. Every book on every shelf has to earn its place.

**Campfire:** What is the most common thing customers say when they walk in?

> **Marcus Bell:** "It smells like a bookshop should." Which tells you something about what people are actually looking for. They do not come here because they cannot find books elsewhere. They come because they want the experience of being in a room full of books that someone cared about. They want to be in a space where slowness is the point.

**Campfire:** Do you worry about the future of physical bookshops?

> **Marcus Bell:** No. I worry about the future of attention. Bookshops will survive as long as people want to hold a physical object and sit quietly with it. What I worry about is whether people will continue to want that. Reading a book is an act of sustained attention, and sustained attention is becoming a radical act. I sell radical acts for twelve to twenty pounds each.

**Campfire:** What are you reading right now?

> **Marcus Bell:** I never answer that question. If I tell you what I am reading, you will judge the shop by my taste. The whole point of this place is that it is not about my taste. It is about yours. Come in, tell me what you are feeling, and I will find you something. That is the deal.

---

*Bell Books is located in Bath, England. Visits are by appointment only from November through February.*
