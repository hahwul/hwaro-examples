## Differential Diagnosis

The initial differential for altered mental status in a collapsed endurance athlete includes:

- **Exercise-associated hyponatraemia** (confirmed): Excessive free water intake during prolonged exertion
- **Exertional heat stroke**: Rectal temperature was 37.8C at field assessment, making this unlikely
- **Hypoglycaemia**: Point-of-care glucose was 62 mg/dL -- low but not explanatory of seizure
- **Cardiac arrhythmia**: ECG showed sinus tachycardia only
- **Cerebrovascular event**: CT head was unremarkable; rapid improvement with sodium correction argues against

## Management Challenges

This case presented three specific management challenges:

**1. Refractory sodium decline.** Despite cessation of oral intake and initial 100 mL bolus of 3% NaCl, serum sodium continued to fall from 118 to 114 mmol/L over the first 2 hours. This was attributed to ongoing absorption of hypotonic fluid from the gastrointestinal tract -- the patient had consumed approximately 8 litres of water and dilute sports drink during the preceding 8 hours of racing.

**2. Rate-limited correction.** The target correction rate of 10 mmol/L per 24 hours was adhered to strictly to avoid osmotic demyelination syndrome (ODS). This required continuous 3% NaCl infusion at 15-30 mL/h with 2-hourly sodium monitoring and dose adjustment. The sodium correction rate was temporarily excessive between hours 14-20 (6 mmol/L rise in 6 hours), requiring brief administration of desmopressin to slow the correction.

**3. Multi-organ monitoring.** Concurrent rhabdomyolysis (CK peak 4,200 U/L) and acute kidney injury (creatinine 1.6 mg/dL) required careful fluid management that conflicted with the goal of sodium correction through fluid restriction.

## Learning Points

1. Severe EAH (Na+ < 120 mmol/L with neurological symptoms) is a medical emergency requiring immediate hypertonic saline administration
2. Ongoing GI absorption of previously ingested hypotonic fluid can cause refractory sodium decline despite treatment
3. Correction must be rate-limited to avoid ODS -- target < 10 mmol/L per 24 hours
4. Desmopressin can be used as a "safety net" if correction exceeds the target rate
5. Hydration education for endurance athletes should emphasise drinking to thirst, not to a predetermined volume
