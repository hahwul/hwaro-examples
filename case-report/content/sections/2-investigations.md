### Imaging

**CT Head (non-contrast):** No acute intracranial pathology. No evidence of cerebral oedema on initial imaging.

**Chest X-ray:** Clear lung fields bilaterally. No evidence of pulmonary oedema. Endotracheal tube position confirmed.

**ECG:** Sinus tachycardia at 128 bpm. Normal axis. No ST-segment changes. QTc 440 ms (borderline, attributed to electrolyte disturbance).

### Diagnostic Summary

The clinical picture is consistent with severe exercise-associated hyponatraemia (EAH) caused by excessive hypotonic fluid intake during prolonged endurance exercise. The concentrated urine (520 mOsm/kg) in the setting of low serum osmolality (236 mOsm/kg) indicates inappropriate antidiuresis -- likely a combination of non-osmotic AVP release (from exercise, nausea, and volume depletion) and the physiological stress response.

Secondary diagnoses include:
- Exertional rhabdomyolysis (CK 2,850 U/L, peaking at 4,200 U/L)
- Acute kidney injury (KDIGO Stage 1)
- Moderate hypokalaemia (K+ 2.8 mmol/L)
