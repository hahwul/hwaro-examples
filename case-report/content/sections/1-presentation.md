## Patient Demographics

A 34-year-old male presented to the medical tent at kilometre 68 of a 100-km mountain ultramarathon in the White Mountains, New Hampshire, in September 2025. The race had been underway for approximately 8 hours in cool, overcast conditions (ambient temperature 12-16C, humidity 75-85%).

## History of Presenting Complaint

The patient was found by fellow runners in a confused state approximately 200 metres off the marked trail. Witnesses reported that he had been running well through the 50-km checkpoint but appeared increasingly disoriented at the 60-km aid station, where he was noted to be "stumbling and talking nonsense." He consumed approximately 500 mL of water at that aid station.

Shortly after leaving the 60-km aid station, he experienced a witnessed generalised tonic-clonic seizure lasting approximately 90 seconds that terminated spontaneously.

## Past Medical History

- No significant past medical history
- No regular medications
- No known allergies
- Previous ultramarathon experience: 12 races over 5 years, no prior medical events
- Self-reported fluid intake during the race: approximately 1 litre per hour (8 litres total)

## Field Assessment

The race medical team responded within 8 minutes. Initial field assessment:

- **Airway:** Patent, no vomitus
- **Breathing:** RR 24, SpO2 96% on room air
- **Circulation:** HR 132, BP 98/62 mmHg, peripheral pulses palpable
- **Disability:** GCS 11 (E3 V3 M5), pupils equal and reactive
- **Exposure:** Temp 37.8C (rectal), no external injuries

- K+ 3.1 mmol/L <span class="badge-warning">LOW</span>
- Glucose 62 mg/dL <span class="badge-warning">LOW</span>
