## On the Design

The Jerusalem classical style takes its principal ornament from the eight-pointed geometric rosette, a form which has adorned the architecture and bookwork of that city since the mediaeval period. The studio master has rendered it here in concentric forms, each petal turning about the next in a manner which rewards long looking and repays the patience of the student.

The ruled border is triple: an outer line in heavier umber, an inner line in gilt, and a third line closer to the lettering block in the same umber at a lighter weight. The four corners carry minor rosettes which echo the central device.

## On the Lettering

The script is the Sephardic square, preferred by this studio for its geometric clarity and its harmony with the ruled border. Lines are ruled in dry point before the ink is laid, and each line is lettered without lifting the pen from the commencement of the verse to its termination.

## Provenance

Commissioned by the Stern and Arad families in the spring of 2025 for a ceremony held in Tel Aviv upon the fourteenth of September. The families granted permission for reproduction of the ornamental design for the studio's portfolio, with the personal inscriptions redacted in this reproduction.
