<div class="prose">

A contract drawn by this studio is the product of approximately three months of attention, from the first consultation through the final sealing of the leaf. The stages below describe a typical progress, though no two commissions proceed in the same order; the particular wishes of a couple, the urgency of their date, and the complexity of their ornamental requirements all modify the method.

## On the Materials

We use paper and vellum of the finest available grade - Arches, Fabriano, and kid vellum prepared to our specification by a single supplier in Rome - and pigments ground in the studio from mineral earths (ochre, sienna, umber, lapis, malachite) and from plant materials (woad, madder, gamboge) where the contract calls for these. Iron gall ink is made each season from our own recipe; oak galls are collected in the autumn from known trees near the studio.

## On Conservation

A contract so made is expected to outlast its owners. We provide with every delivery a small pamphlet upon the conservation of the document - the avoidance of direct sunlight, the requirement for stable humidity, the recommendation of acid-free mounting - and the studio offers a perpetual service of conservation and repair to its own work. Should a contract suffer damage by flood, fire, or accident, it may be returned to the studio for such restoration as may be possible.
