Composting is one of the simplest and most impactful things you can do to reduce household waste. About 30% of what we throw away could be composted instead, diverting material from landfills and creating nutrient-rich soil.

## What Can Be Composted

Compost needs a balance of "greens" (nitrogen-rich) and "browns" (carbon-rich):

**Greens:**
- Fruit and vegetable scraps
- Coffee grounds and tea leaves
- Fresh grass clippings
- Plant trimmings

**Browns:**
- Dry leaves
- Cardboard and paper (uncoated)
- Straw and hay
- Wood chips and sawdust

## Getting Started

You don't need special equipment. A simple bin or even a designated corner of your garden will work.

1. Choose a dry, shaded spot near a water source
2. Layer browns and greens alternately
3. Keep the pile moist but not waterlogged
4. Turn the pile every few weeks to aerate it
5. Wait 2-6 months for finished compost

## Common Mistakes

**Too wet.** Add more browns and turn the pile. A soggy pile becomes anaerobic and produces unpleasant odors.

**Too dry.** Sprinkle water when turning. Microorganisms need moisture to break down material.

**Wrong materials.** Avoid meat, dairy, and oils. These attract pests and decompose poorly in home systems.

> The best time to start composting was years ago. The second best time is today.

Finished compost is dark, crumbly, and smells like fresh earth. Use it to enrich garden beds, potted plants, or share it with neighbors.
