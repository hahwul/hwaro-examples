# Sculptor Profile: Chen Wei and the Weight of Stillness

Chen Wei works slowly. In a contemporary art world that rewards prolific output and constant reinvention, the Shanghai-born, Pietrasanta-based sculptor produces perhaps three major works per year. Each one takes months of preparation before chisel touches stone, and further months of careful, deliberate carving.

"I am not interested in speed," Chen explains from his studio in the historic marble-working town. "The stone existed for millions of years before I arrived. What is another six months?"

## Training and Approach

Chen studied classical sculpture techniques in Florence before relocating to Pietrasanta, where he has maintained a studio for fifteen years. His early work was figurative, but over the past decade he has moved toward abstraction -- or, more precisely, toward forms that occupy the boundary between representation and pure geometry.

His signature works are large-scale pieces in which recognizable elements -- a shoulder, a horizon line, the curve of a vessel -- emerge from and dissolve back into unworked stone. The transition points are handled with extraordinary subtlety, creating surfaces that shift between polished and rough, defined and ambiguous.

## Material as Collaborator

Chen is particular about his stone. He visits the quarries personally, spending days examining blocks before selecting material. He looks for what he calls "character" -- natural veining, inclusions, even small flaws that he can incorporate into the finished work.

"The stone tells you what it wants to become, if you listen," he says. "I have abandoned pieces because the material resisted. You cannot force marble. It will break before it bends."

## Recent Work

His most recent completed piece, "Threshold," stands two meters tall and weighs approximately four tons. It suggests a doorway or portal, with interior surfaces polished to a mirror finish while the exterior retains the raw texture of the quarry face. The contrast is striking -- industrial precision meeting geological time.

"Threshold" is currently on loan to the Fondazione Prada and represents a new scale for Chen's practice. Two further monumental works are reportedly in progress, though he declines to discuss unfinished pieces.

## Collecting

Chen Wei's work is represented by three galleries internationally. Recent auction results have placed finished pieces in the mid-six-figure range, with strong demand from institutional collectors. Given his limited output, works rarely appear on the secondary market.
