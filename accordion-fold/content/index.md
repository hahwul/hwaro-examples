<div class="section-header">
  <h2>About This Publication</h2>
  <div class="section-header-mark">Editor&#39;s Note</div>
</div>

Accordion Fold is a small quarterly concertina, printed on long-grain Kozo paper and folded by hand in a studio above the old tramline. It is distributed in signed numbered editions of three hundred. Each number concerns a single subject examined across eight continuous panels, to be read front-to-back or opened out all at once against a wall.

The publication takes its name and its form from the Japanese accordion-book tradition (*orihon*), where a long strip of paper is folded into alternating mountains and valleys to produce a bound object with no spine. The form was used for sutras, for painted hand-scrolls, and for private commonplace books.

The current number concerns the geometry of folding itself.
