## Production

The Blackbird Festival is produced by a small core team of six: the production manager,
the stage manager, the head of security, the head of technical, the head of hospitality,
and the production coordinator. All six carry Tier 01 (ALL ACCESS) passes. They may be
reached at any point during the event on channel 2 of the crew radio; in an emergency,
any of them will step in for any of the others.

## Shift Crew

Beyond production, the event is staffed by approximately one hundred and twenty shift
crew across four departments:

- **Stage** (riggers, monitor engineers, front-of-house engineers, spot ops).
- **Security** (perimeter, pit, crowd, backstage).
- **Hospitality** (talent runners, catering, dressing room dressers).
- **Site** (gate, box office, parking, power).

Shift crew carry Tier 02 and Tier 03 passes, which permit access to the zones appropriate
to their work but exclude talent greenrooms and the production office after hours.

## Media Pool

A small pool of accredited press work from Zone Media, which is a railed-off area at the
rear of the pit. Press passes are Tier 04 and do not permit access to backstage, dock,
or talent zones. Press are instructed to direct all questions to the production
coordinator; talent interviews are arranged in advance by management.

## Volunteers

A small number of volunteers assist with site setup and load-out. They carry day-pass
credentials that must be handed back at the end of each working day.

## Lost Pass Protocol

> If you lose your pass, stop working and walk to the production office.

A lost pass is not a minor matter. Between the moment it is lost and the moment it is
reported, any person may pick it up and walk into a restricted zone. For this reason, a
lost pass is treated as a security incident: the pass is immediately voided at the
perimeter, the bearer is issued a temporary replacement, and &#8212; if the lost pass is
not recovered by the end of the day &#8212; a full perimeter sweep is conducted before
the gates re-open.
