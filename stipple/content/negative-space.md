In this composition, the subject is rendered in reverse. The surrounding field is densely stippled while the central form remains bare paper. The subject exists as a void --- defined entirely by what surrounds it.

This approach inverts the usual logic of mark-making. Instead of building up the subject, the artist builds up everything else. The form is carved from a field of dots.

## Inversion

Traditional stipple constructs the subject through accumulation. Here, accumulation constructs the background. The subject is absence made visible. It challenges the assumption that representation requires direct depiction.

## Resonance

The untouched paper carries a different quality than white paint or erased graphite. It is genuinely blank --- never touched by the instrument. This purity gives the negative-space form a luminous presence that stippled highlights cannot replicate.
