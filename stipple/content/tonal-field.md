This piece explores how uniform dots, placed with varying density, produce the illusion of continuous tone. No line is drawn. No stroke is made. Only points --- thousands of them --- building from sparse highlight to near-solid shadow.

The viewer's eye does the rest, blending discrete marks into smooth transitions. It is a study in the threshold between mark and field, between presence and absence.

## Technique

The foundation is a grid of equidistant points. Tonal variation is achieved solely by adjusting spacing: tighter clusters darken, wider spacing reveals the paper beneath. Each dot is placed by hand, demanding unwavering focus and a steady instrument.

## Observation

Stand close and the structure dissolves into scattered marks. Step back and the form coheres. This duality --- chaos at one scale, order at another --- is the central tension of stipple art.
