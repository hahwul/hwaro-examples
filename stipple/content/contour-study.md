Where does an edge begin when there are no lines? In this study, contour is implied through the abrupt shift in dot density. The eye perceives a boundary that the hand never drew.

Stipple forces the artist to think in fields rather than edges. The silhouette is not outlined but emerges from the stark contrast between dense ink and untouched paper.

## Process

Beginning from the darkest region, dots are placed in tight clusters, nearly overlapping. As the form recedes, the spacing widens gradually. The contour appears where this transition is sharpest --- a ridge of perception created entirely by rhythm and repetition.

## On Patience

A single square centimeter of deep shadow may require hundreds of individual marks. There is no shortcut. Each dot is a commitment, and the cumulative result cannot be achieved any other way.
