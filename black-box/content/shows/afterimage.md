## the closing piece of the weekend

Thirty-three minutes total. The spotlight is on for seven of them. The rest is voice in the dark.

### the first seven minutes

The performer is in the light. She is arranging a set of five objects on the floor &mdash; a cup, a key, a folded piece of paper, a coin, and a small stone. She is speaking, but not to the audience.

### the remaining twenty-six minutes

The light fades. The performer continues. The audience hears her voice. Occasionally they hear the cup against the floor, the key against the cup, the paper unfolding. What she is doing is not a mystery. It is just unlit.

### the last ten seconds

The light returns, very briefly, at 32:50. The objects are arranged differently than they were when the light went out. The performer is no longer in the pool. The audience has seven seconds to see the new arrangement before the light fades for the last time.

> "An afterimage is what you see after you stop looking. That is the piece." &mdash; director's note
