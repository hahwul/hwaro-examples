## a description, before the lights go

One chair. One performer. One pool of light about two meters across. The rest of the room is dark. The audience sits in the dark too.

### the arc

The performer enters and sits. For the first four minutes, nothing happens. The audience adjusts. Then a voice begins to speak &mdash; not necessarily the performer's voice &mdash; and the piece begins in earnest.

### what the piece is about

It is not about the chair. It is not about the light. It is about the thing that is left behind when everything else has been removed &mdash; the performer's attention, the audience's attention, and the relationship between the two.

> "The point is not what remains in the center. The point is what is worn away from the edges." &mdash; from the rehearsal notes

### running time

Forty minutes, without interval. The lights fade to black for approximately nine seconds before the house lights come up. Please remain seated during the fade.
