A simple pasta where good tomatoes and fresh basil do all the work. No cream, no complications.

<!-- more -->

## Ingredients

- 200g spaghetti or linguine
- 400g canned whole tomatoes (San Marzano if possible)
- 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- A generous handful of fresh basil leaves
- 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Parmesan for serving

## Steps

1. Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a boil. Cook the pasta until just short of al dente — it will finish in the sauce.
2. While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a wide pan over medium-low heat. Add the garlic slices and cook slowly until they turn pale gold, about 2 minutes. Do not let them brown.
3. Crush the canned tomatoes by hand directly into the pan. Season with salt and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly.
4. Transfer the pasta into the sauce using tongs, bringing along a splash of pasta water. Toss over medium heat for 1-2 minutes.
5. Remove from heat. Tear the basil leaves over the top and toss once more.
6. Serve with freshly grated Parmesan.

## Notes

- The quality of tomatoes matters enormously here. This is not the recipe to use bargain canned tomatoes.
- Reserve at least a cup of pasta water before draining — it is the secret to a sauce that clings.
