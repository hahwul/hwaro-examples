Chewy rice cakes in a sweet-spicy gochujang sauce. A Korean street food staple that takes 20 minutes from start to finish.

<!-- more -->

## Ingredients

- 300g Korean rice cakes (tteok), cylindrical type
- 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
- 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 400ml water or anchovy stock
- 2 stalks green onion, cut into 5cm pieces

## Steps

1. If using refrigerated or frozen rice cakes, soak them in warm water for 10 minutes to soften.
2. Combine the water, gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, and sugar in a wide pan. Stir well and bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
3. Add the drained rice cakes to the boiling sauce. Stir to coat evenly.
4. Cook at a steady simmer for 8-10 minutes, stirring frequently. The sauce will thicken as the starch releases from the rice cakes. If it gets too thick before the rice cakes are tender, add a splash of water.
5. When the rice cakes are soft and chewy and the sauce coats them in a glossy layer, add the green onion pieces. Stir for another minute.
6. Serve hot, directly from the pan.

## Notes

- Fresh rice cakes from a Korean market are best. Frozen ones work after soaking.
- For a richer version, add a handful of fish cakes or a hard-boiled egg.
