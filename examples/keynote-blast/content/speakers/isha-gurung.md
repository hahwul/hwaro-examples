## the talk

**"The Compound Cost of Five-Minute Decisions."** The closing keynote. Isha has spent fifteen years inside one codebase. She has watched the compound interest on early decisions accrue and she knows exactly which ones paid off and which ones keep the on-call schedule full a decade later.

### why she is in slot 03

Slot 03 is the anchor. The audience is peak-engaged. The room is loud. This is where you put the talk that people will quote for the next year. Isha's talk is that talk.

### what she will argue

- The decision that looks small in a stand-up is rarely small in retrospect
- Shortcuts compound; they do not stay local
- The best engineers aren't the fastest; they're the ones who buy the team cheap optionality
- Ten specific shortcuts she regrets and what the fix would have cost up front
