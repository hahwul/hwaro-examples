# VENUE

One room. No second stage. No breakouts. The Magazine is a former printing warehouse rigged as a 680-seat auditorium with a single stage at the short end, one FOH booth, and one row of stage lights focused on the podium. If you want a multi-track experience, go to a different conference.

## room specs

| SPEC | VALUE |
| --- | --- |
| Capacity | 680 seated |
| Stage size | 24 ft wide &middot; 16 ft deep |
| Ceiling | 28 ft |
| Rig | 12 moving heads &middot; 6 fixed spots &middot; 4 strobes (audience) |
| PA | L-Acoustics K2 &middot; four per side flown |
| Console | Avid S6L-32D &middot; 32-fader |
| Video | LED wall 40 ft x 14 ft upstage &middot; 4K feed |

## stage spotlight pattern

The entire stage is lit by a single upstage rig focused on a seven-foot diameter circle centered on the podium. Step outside the circle and you walk into deep shadow. That is on purpose.

## seats

First five rows are reserved for speakers, production, and press. Rows 6 through 18 are general admission and are first-come, first-served. The balcony is 160 seats; ticket upgrades available at check-in if inventory remains at T-00:45.

## not-in-the-room

The Magazine is not accessible by public transit after 22:00. Rideshare surge pricing triggers inside the venue geofence from T+02:00 to T+03:15. Plan accordingly. A conference-run shuttle runs from the venue to the city center from 21:30 to 23:00.
