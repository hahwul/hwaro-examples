The codex treats the medieval illuminated book as an object of continuing relevance. Its concerns are those of the scribe and the illuminator: the shape of letters, the interlace of ornament, the economy of gold leaf, the relation of text to image. Each folio offers a single small article, an illumination, or a reproduction of an initial from the historical record.

## De Rebus Huius Libri

The codex was inspired most directly by the *Codex Aureus of Echternach*, an eleventh-century Gospel book written entirely in gold ink upon purple-dyed parchment. It draws also from the *Book of Kells*, the *Lindisfarne Gospels*, and the *Book of Durrow* &#8212; the great works of the insular illumination tradition. These codices are, properly speaking, out of reach: their materials, their techniques, their religious functions all belong to another world. But their formal ambitions &#8212; a book that is also an object of devotion, a page that is also a window &#8212; remain worth the studying.
