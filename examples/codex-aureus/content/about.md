## The Hands

The present codex is the work of three hands: a senior scribe responsible for the running
text, a junior scribe who rules the pages and prepares the vellum, and an illuminator who
works the initials, marginalia, and coloured capitals. On great feasts all three hands
sit in the same room; on ordinary days the work happens by post.

## The Materials

Our codex is copied on vellum of moderate thickness. It is ruled with a hard point and a
lead line before the ink is applied. The ink is oak-gall with ferrous sulphate and a
little gum arabic. The gold leaf is of the usual 23 carat. The blue is lapis lazuli
ground with a small quantity of honey, in the manner of Cennino Cennini. The red is
vermilion, warmed with a little lead-tin yellow for the rubrications.

## The Scripts

Two scripts are used in the codex. The running text is a modernised Carolingian minuscule,
close to what a scribe of the ninth century would have written. The rubrications are an
insular majuscule in the manner of the eighth-century Irish scriptoria. The two scripts
sit together without quarrel.

## Corresponding

Letters may be addressed to the scriptorium via the ordinary post. Subscriptions, when we
have any folios left to send out, are on a one-per-reader basis.
