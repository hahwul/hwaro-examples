# About Windmill

Windmill is a blog dedicated to the beauty and traditions of the Dutch and European countryside. From the iconic tulip fields of South Holland to the centuries-old cheese markets of Alkmaar, this space celebrates the pastoral rhythms that have defined life in the Low Countries for generations.

Here you will find stories about cycling along quiet canals, visits to historic windmill sites, reflections on the Golden Age masters, and explorations of the customs that make the Netherlands such a distinctive corner of Europe. Whether you are a lifelong resident or a curious traveler, we hope these writings bring you closer to the unhurried charm of Dutch rural life.

The name "Windmill" pays homage to the structures that have shaped the Dutch landscape since the Middle Ages -- engineering marvels that drained lakes, milled grain, and came to symbolize an entire nation. Like those windmills, this blog aims to be a steady presence, turning with the seasons, capturing what makes each one worth savoring.
