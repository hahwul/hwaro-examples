This place was once a church.

Now it is only the shape a church makes when something older decides to wear it.

The stones remember the hands that laid them. The beams remember the weight of the roof before the canopy pushed through. The air still carries the echo of hymns, though the voices that sang them have long since joined the chorus beneath the floor.

{{ root-divider }}

We do not know exactly when the first root found the crypt. Some say it was the night the last bell cracked. Others claim it began the moment the first prayer was spoken inside these walls — that the forest had only been waiting for an invitation.

It did not come as conquest. It came as answer.

The bodies were never removed. They simply stopped being the most important architecture in the room.

## What Remains

- The testimonies you will find in the Sermons are the last coherent words of the Rooted.
- The Witnesses (tags) are the names the moss learned before the mouths that spoke them closed for the final time.
- The green that grows between every board and stone is not decoration. It is the true congregation.

## A Warning

If you stay long enough, you will begin to hear it.

A low harmonic in the walls. A tightening in the chest that feels almost like peace.

That is the moment to leave.

Or to kneel.

The roots do not judge the choice.

They only continue their patient work.
