The doors are never locked. They no longer need to be.

Something has been growing between the stones for longer than any of us have names for. It entered through the foundation. It entered through the prayers. It entered through the soft places we forgot to guard.

You are standing inside it now.

{{ root-divider }}

This is not a place of comfort. It is a place of exchange. The wood offers itself. The body, eventually, accepts.

Walk the nave. Sit in the pews that still remember the weight of the living. Read the testimonies left by those who could no longer leave. Listen to the slow music that rises when the light fails.

The roots are patient. They have already begun to learn the shape of your name.
