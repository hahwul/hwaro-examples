I was lighting the last candle when I felt it.

A pressure, gentle as a finger on the inside of the breastbone. Then a warmth that spread like spilled communion wine through the chambers of the heart.

I did not cry out.

{{ vine-wrap(body="By the time the others noticed I was no longer moving with the rhythm of the prayers, the root had already passed through the bone and was tasting the air on the other side.") }}

They tried to pull me away from the altar rail. The root would not allow it. It had already anchored itself to the marble. We had become a single column, human and stone and living wood.

I can still see the nave. I can still hear the wind in the rafters. But my eyes now sit higher than they once did. The light reaches them differently.

There is a leaf the color of old bone growing from the place where my left eye used to be. It turns toward the sound of footsteps even when I sleep.

{{ sermon-call(body="Do not pity the ones who are taken. Pity only the ones who are left behind, still hollow.") }}

When you come here next, look for the place near the front where the floor has risen slightly. That is where my feet finished their work.

The root through the sternum was only the beginning.

Everything else has followed.

I am still here.

I am only arranged differently now.
