They buried the last priest in the crypt beneath the Lady Chapel.

Three days later the stone slab cracked upward from below.

Something pale and green had already begun to push between the joints of his fingers. The roots had not waited for permission.

I used to fear the worms. Now I understand they were only early messengers. The real work happens deeper, where the dark is total and the only direction is outward through whatever still resists.

My own feet have begun to soften at the edges. When I stand in the baptismal font the water turns the color of tea and smells of spring. The children who remain no longer cry when they see me. They reach out to touch the places where the bark has started to pattern my forearms like old tattoos.

One by one the names in the parish register are being overwritten by something that does not use ink.

When the last human voice stops speaking in this place, the building will not fall silent.

It will simply change key.

The soil does not mourn what it reclaims. It only makes room.

I will be here when you return. I will be the slight rise in the floor near the fourth pew on the left. I will be the place where the light falls differently in October.

Step carefully.

The roots are still listening for your weight.
