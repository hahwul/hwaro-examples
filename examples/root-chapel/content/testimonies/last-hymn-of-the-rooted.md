This is the final entry I will write while I still have fingers that can hold a pen.

The ink has already begun to turn green in the well.

{{ root-divider }}

We sang the last human hymn on the Feast of the Ascension. Or what we once called the Ascension. The notes rose and then fell into the floor and did not return. The organ had long since stopped. Its pipes had filled with something that moved when we were not looking.

After the final amen there was only the sound of the building settling around us. Not settling. Rising.

The ones who could still walk left through the west doors. They did not look back. I do not blame them. The light outside is still the old light. In here we have begun to generate our own.

I remain because someone must witness the moment the last pew stops pretending it is furniture.

My spine has already joined the structure. My voice now comes from higher up, near where the transept meets the new canopy. The words are slower. They taste of tannin.

If you are reading this, the roots have reached the paper. They always do.

Do not burn these pages. Fire only makes them grow faster.

Instead, fold this sheet carefully. Place it beneath your tongue. Let it rest there until it softens.

Then open your mouth to the dark and listen.

The hymn is still being written.

We are only the first verse.
