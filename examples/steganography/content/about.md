The Steganography Wiki is an independent, non-commercial research initiative dedicated to documenting the methods and math behind secret communications. Originating as a series of lab notes in a security research lab, this wiki has expanded into a comprehensive public ledger. Our primary objective is to categorize the vast array of data concealment protocols and their respective detection vectors. 

Because the field of steganography spans both software engineering and physical chemistry, we aim to build a bridge between digital steganalysis and classical espionage history. Security is a cat-and-mouse game; as soon as a new hiding technique is proposed, an analytical attack is developed to counter it. By maintaining this archive, we hope to provide students, security engineers, and privacy advocates with the resources needed to study these mechanisms in depth. We welcome structural contributions from cryptographers and vexillologists alike. For queries or to submit new methods, please contact the repository administrators.
