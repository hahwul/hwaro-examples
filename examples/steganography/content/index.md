Welcome to the Steganography Wiki, a curated directory and repository dedicated to the science and art of hidden writing. Unlike cryptography, which scrambles a message so that its contents cannot be understood, steganography hides the very existence of the communication. By embedding secret payloads within innocuous carrier mediums—such as digital images, audio tracks, formatted text, or physical paper—stego-analysts and practitioners ensure that private messages pass unnoticed through hostile channels.

This archive catalogues both historical ciphers, like the invisible ink used by ancient espionage networks, and modern mathematical techniques, such as Least Significant Bit (LSB) replacement in high-resolution digital files. We document the theoretical foundations, implementation steps, and analytical tools used to detect hidden payloads (steganalysis). By analyzing statistical anomalies, noise distribution, and file structures, steganalysis aims to expose concealed data and evaluate the strength of steganographic algorithms. 

We encourage researchers to explore both sides of this field to better understand security limits. Read more on our [About page](/about/), or explore the directories below to review modern [concealment methods](/methods/) and [steganalysis attacks](/steganalysis/).
