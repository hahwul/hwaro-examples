Illot Fenc needs less exposure than the other coves to be worth a visit, because its lower pools sit in a natural basin that only drains on the better tides of a neap week — this one barely qualified, and the window was short enough that the notebook stayed dry only because it was written standing up.

<!-- more -->

The two anemone pools nearest the basin's mouth are as reliable as anything on this shore. Beadlet anemones (*Actinia equina*) crowd the shallower of the two in every colour variant the species carries here — red, green, and the strawberry form with pale spots, all within arm's reach of each other. The deeper pool belongs to snakelocks anemones (*Anemonia viridis*), tentacles fully extended in the still water, more of them than in any previous visit to this cove.

Lifting one loose slab at the basin's edge — replaced exactly as found, per the method — turned up a black brittle star (*Ophioderma longicauda*), arms coiled tight around the slab's underside, the first logged at this cove rather than at Roig or Cala Ombrina. It moved off into a crevice within seconds of the light hitting it, too quickly to check for regenerating arms.

Star limpets (*Patella caerulea*) sit in loose clusters on the basin's rim, distinguishable from the rough limpets logged elsewhere by the sharper radiating ribs on the shell. The water was already sheeting back over the rim by the time this entry was finished; Illot Fenc gives you less time than it promises.
