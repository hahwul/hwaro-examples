title = "Why there are no pictures"
description = "The case for writing about night photographs instead of publishing them."
+++

For six years I photographed cities after dark and published almost none of it. Not out of secrecy — the negatives are filed, dated, indexed by street and lamp type like anyone's. I stopped publishing the pictures because the pictures kept losing the argument I was actually trying to make, which was never really about how a place looked. It was about what it took to stand there long enough to see it.

A night exposure is a negotiation with time that a daylight snapshot never has to have. You are not freezing a moment; you are accumulating one, sometimes for thirteen minutes, while the sodium lamp overhead does something to color that the eye edits out and the sensor refuses to. Writing that negotiation down — the actual minutes, the actual arguments with a light meter that wanted to average a scene with no true middle gray — turned out to say more about the city than the finished frame did. The photograph is a compromise between the scene and the film's opinion of it. The sentence can just tell you what happened.

So this is what Sundog is: essays instead of prints. Each one runs under a small line of exposure data — the settings that produced the picture I am not showing you, left in as a kind of caption for an absent image, and as a promise that the details are real and not reconstructed from memory. I keep them because a camera's decisions are as much the subject as the street is.

I shoot with old manual bodies and a headlamp I am embarrassed by. I work mostly within walking distance of wherever I happen to be sleeping, which over six years has meant a rotating cast of mid-sized cities with more sodium lighting than sense — overpasses, interchanges, the kind of empty civic architecture that only looks considered once everyone has gone home.

New essays post when there's something worth the trip out, which averages to roughly one a season. There is no schedule to subscribe against, only [the RSS feed](/essays/rss.xml) and the [essays index](/essays/) itself.
