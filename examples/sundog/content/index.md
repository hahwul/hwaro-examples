Sundog keeps no photographs. Every essay here describes a picture that was made and then left in the camera, because the picture was never the point of standing outside at one in the morning — the noticing was. What appears instead is the record: where the tripod legs went, what the meter argued for, what the eye kept insisting it saw that the sensor did not agree with.

City light after dark is a short list of unnatural colors trying to pass as ambience — sodium orange, mercury green-white, the flat blue-white of an LED retrofit nobody asked for. A photograph settles that argument into a single frame. A sentence can hold it open: how long the shutter stayed open, what moved during that time, what kept still that shouldn't have.

This is a site about method more than subject — waiting, metering, the difference between a city that is asleep and a city that is merely quiet at 3am. Start with [the essays](/essays/), or read about [why there are no pictures](/about/).
