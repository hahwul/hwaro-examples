Nature's most stunning color combinations often come from gemstones. Each stone tells a story through its unique palette, and these palettes translate beautifully into digital design.

<!-- more -->

## Moonstone Palette

The moonstone palette centers on cool, ethereal tones:

- **Deep indigo** (`#0a0e1a`) — The dark base, like a midnight sky
- **Adularescent blue** (`#6b8fc7`) — The signature floating sheen
- **Silver mist** (`#b8c6db`) — Soft highlights and text
- **Lavender haze** (`#a8b5d6`) — Secondary accents
- **Pearl white** (`#e8ecf4`) — Primary text and emphasis

## Complementary Stone Palettes

Other gemstones offer equally compelling palettes:

- **Labradorite** — Dark grey base with flashes of electric blue and green
- **Opal** — White or black base with rainbow fire
- **Alexandrite** — Green in daylight, red under incandescent light

## Applying Natural Palettes

When borrowing from nature, the key principles are:

1. **Respect the ratios** — In a moonstone, the blue sheen is a small fraction of the whole
2. **Maintain harmony** — Natural palettes are inherently harmonious
3. **Use accent sparingly** — The most vivid color should be the rarest

These principles help create designs that feel grounded and intentional rather than arbitrary.
