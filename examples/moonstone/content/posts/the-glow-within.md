Adularescence is the optical phenomenon that gives moonstone its distinctive floating light. This blue-white sheen appears to glide across the surface of the stone as it is moved, creating an almost supernatural glow.

<!-- more -->

## What Causes Adularescence?

The effect is caused by the scattering of light between microscopic layers of orthoclase and albite feldspar within the stone. As light enters the moonstone, it is diffracted and scattered by these thin layers, producing the characteristic blue sheen.

## Translating Light to Design

When designing digital experiences inspired by moonstone, we can capture this quality through:

- **Layered transparency** — overlapping elements with varying opacity
- **Subtle gradients** — shifting between cool blues and soft whites
- **Animated glow effects** — gentle pulsing that mimics the stone's inner light

```css
.adularescent {
  background: linear-gradient(
    135deg,
    rgba(107, 143, 199, 0.15),
    rgba(168, 181, 214, 0.08)
  );
  box-shadow: 0 0 40px rgba(107, 143, 199, 0.1);
}
```

The key is restraint — moonstone's beauty lies in its subtlety. The glow should feel discovered, not forced.
