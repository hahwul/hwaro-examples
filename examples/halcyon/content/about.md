Halcyon is not a club in the sense of dues and meetings. It is a shared log, kept online since 2019 by a loose circle of canoe trippers who happen to favor the same handful of northern rivers and the same unhurried pace on them. Nobody owns it. Whoever paddled last writes the report — browse the [full voyage log](/voyages/) to see what that has produced.

## What gets logged

A trip qualifies for the log if it was run under paddle power, self-supported, with the party carrying its own gear over every portage on the route. We are not precious about speed or mileage — some of the best entries in the log cover forty kilometres in three days, and some cover a hundred and forty in nine. What we ask for is a route ribbon, an honest portage count, and whatever camp lore survived the trip intact.

## The route ribbon

Every trip report opens with a small wavy line tracing the shape of the river run, with a tick mark crossing it at each portage. It is a convention borrowed from the hand-drawn route maps that used to circulate before anyone thought to put a canoe log online, and we have kept it because it does, at a glance, what a paragraph of description takes longer to do: it shows you where the water stops and the walking starts.

## Filing a report

If you have run one of the rivers in this log, or a river that belongs beside them, write it up the way the rest of the log is written — route, distance, days, every portage by name and length, and whatever happened at camp that is worth remembering next season. Send it to the address on the trip you are replying to, or to whoever in the circle you paddled with last. The log has never had a formal editor and does not intend to start now.
