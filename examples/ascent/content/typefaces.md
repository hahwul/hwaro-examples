Every typeface in the catalog starts as a photograph. We go out with a ladder, a stepladder-length tape measure, and a camera that can handle low afternoon light, and we shoot the lettering straight-on, panel by panel, before anyone repaints the fascia.

Back at the studio, we trace the letters at full size, correct for the lens and the angle of the wall, and rebuild the forms as vector outlines. We keep the imperfections that came from the brush or the router bit and discard the ones that came from forty years of weather. What ships is not a filter over a photograph &mdash; it is a typeface, spaced and kerned and tested at body-copy sizes the original sign never needed to survive.
