Resonance started as a shared spreadsheet. Two friends — you can read more about [Marisol and Devon here](/hosts/) — kept comparing notes on their growing plots — one on a rooftop, one in a fenced lot behind a church — and realized the numbers they were trading (load limits, ratios, spacing) were more useful than anything either of them found in a book. The show grew out of that spreadsheet, and it still shows: we publish the specifics, not just the vibes.

## What we cover

Every episode is built around one working plot and one problem that plot forced someone to solve. That has meant compost temperatures, structural load calculations, companion-planting spacing, and the exact brand of milk jug that survives a whole winter outside. We are not interested in inspiration for its own sake. If a number is unknown, we go find it before we publish.

## How episodes get made

We record on location whenever a site allows it — on the roof, in the bed, next to the compost pile — because the ambient sound tells you as much as the conversation does. A typical episode runs four to six weeks from first call to release: one scouting visit, one recording session, and a week of fact-checking any measurement or ratio a guest gives us on tape. If a number turns out to be wrong in the edit, we cut it rather than let it stand.

## Get in touch

We take plot recommendations and correction requests from anyone growing food in a place it technically was not designed for. The fastest way to reach us is through the show notes on any [recent episode](/episodes/); we read every message before the next recording session.
