Light enters a diamond and something extraordinary happens. The precisely angled facets bend each wavelength at a slightly different angle, splitting a single beam of white light into every color of the visible spectrum.

<!-- more -->

## Dispersion and Fire

In gemology, this effect is called **fire** — the play of spectral colors that dances across a diamond's surface as it moves. It's what makes a well-cut diamond so captivating: not just brilliance, but a living rainbow trapped inside geometry.

## From Nature to Design

The same principles that make diamonds sparkle can inspire digital design:

- **Angular layouts** mimic the sharp facets of a cut stone
- **Gradient transitions** reproduce the smooth color flow of refracted light
- **Transparency effects** evoke the crystalline clarity of a gem
- **Bold color palettes** capture the intensity of spectral dispersion

## The Perfect Cut

A diamond's beauty depends entirely on precision. The angles of each facet must be exact to maximize light return. In design, the same principle applies — every pixel, every gradient stop, every shadow matters.

When you look at a well-crafted interface, you're seeing the same kind of intentional precision that makes a round brilliant cut diamond return 95% of the light that enters it.

> "A diamond is a chunk of coal that did well under pressure." — Henry Kissinger

The best designs, like the best diamonds, transform raw materials into something extraordinary through careful, deliberate craftsmanship.
