Gradient mesh is more than a visual technique — it's a philosophy of color that embraces fluidity and organic transitions. Unlike flat colors or simple linear gradients, mesh gradients blend multiple color nodes into a seamless, painterly surface.

## Why Gradient Mesh?

Traditional gradients move in a single direction. Mesh gradients break free from that constraint, allowing colors to flow in every direction simultaneously. The result is something that feels alive.

```css
background:
  radial-gradient(at 20% 30%, #ff6b9d 0%, transparent 50%),
  radial-gradient(at 80% 20%, #c084fc 0%, transparent 50%),
  radial-gradient(at 50% 80%, #06b6d4 0%, transparent 50%);
```

## Principles of Good Mesh Design

1. **Contrast with purpose** — Bold colors need breathing room
2. **Organic flow** — Let colors transition naturally
3. **Depth through layers** — Stack translucent gradients
4. **Motion with restraint** — Animate slowly for elegance

The key is balancing visual impact with readability. A stunning background means nothing if your content disappears into it.

## Looking Forward

As CSS continues to evolve, native mesh gradient support may arrive in browsers. Until then, layered radial gradients with careful animation give us a powerful approximation of this beautiful technique.
