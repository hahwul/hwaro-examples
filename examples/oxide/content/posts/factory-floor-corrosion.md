# Factory Floor: Corrosion Patterns

The steel floor plates in abandoned factories develop intricate corrosion patterns—natural fractals created by water flow, chemical gradients, and time.

## Pattern Formation

Corrosion patterns reveal the history of water flow:

- **Radial patterns** around bolt holes and drainage points
- **Linear streaks** following floor slopes
- **Bubble patterns** where water pooled
- **Edge corrosion** where different metals met (galvanic corrosion)

Each pattern tells a story of decades of weather, leaking roofs, and the slow movement of water across steel surfaces.

## Macro Photography

Shot with a 100mm macro lens, these corrosion patterns reveal complexity at every scale:

- Millimeter scale: Individual rust crystals forming
- Centimeter scale: Overlapping layers of oxidation
- Meter scale: Water-flow patterns across entire floor sections

{{ alert(type="info", message="Best shooting conditions: overcast days for even lighting, or early morning when dew emphasizes texture.") }}

## The Rust Palette

Within a single square meter of corroded floor, you can find:

- Bright orange (fresh rust)
- Deep red-brown (aged rust)
- Purple-brown (manganese oxide)
- Yellow-orange (rust in early stages)
- Black (magnetite from deep corrosion)

## Technical Considerations

Focus stacking required for most shots—the texture has too much depth for a single focal plane. 10-30 images combined for full detail from front to back.
