# Steel Mill Ruins: Layers of Rust

The abandoned Carrie Furnace outside Pittsburgh offers a masterclass in oxidation. Massive steel structures, once heated to thousands of degrees, now cool slowly under decades of rain and snow.

## The Rust Palette

Walking through the mill, you encounter every shade of rust:

- **Surface Rust**: Bright orange, powdery, the beginning of decay
- **Deep Corrosion**: Rich browns and reds where oxidation has penetrated deep
- **Scale Patterns**: Flaking layers that create three-dimensional textures
- **Bleed Marks**: Orange streaks running down from rivets and joints

> "Rust never sleeps—it works slowly, transforming iron into iron oxide one molecule at a time."

## Photographic Notes

The texture is everything here. Shot in natural light during the golden hour, when the sun's angle emphasizes the three-dimensional quality of corroded surfaces. Macro lens for detail shots, wide angle for environmental context.

### Technical Details

- Morning light, side-angle illumination
- Shallow depth of field for texture emphasis
- Natural patina—no post-processing color adjustments needed
- The rust provides all the warmth the image requires

## The Beauty of Industrial Decay

These structures were designed to last decades while in use. Abandoned, they'll last decades more as they slowly oxidize. There's something honest about this transformation—no maintenance, no preservation, just natural processes at work.
