# About Oxide

Oxide was founded to document the aesthetic transformation that occurs when human-made structures are reclaimed by time and the elements. We focus on the textures, colors, and patterns that emerge through oxidation, corrosion, and decay.

## Our Mission

Industrial sites and abandoned structures offer a unique visual landscape. The rust-orange of iron oxide, the turquoise-green of copper patina, and the complex browns of surface corrosion create rich, organic textures that tell stories of time, weather, and entropy.

## Photographic Approach

We photograph with respect for these spaces, documenting without disturbing. Our work captures:

- Macro details of rust patterns and surface textures
- Wide angles of abandoned industrial spaces
- The interplay of decay and remaining structural integrity
- Natural light as it interacts with corroded surfaces

## The Beauty of Decay

There's a raw honesty in abandoned industrial spaces. No facade, no pretense—just materials slowly transforming according to the laws of chemistry and physics. Oxide celebrates this process as a form of natural art.
