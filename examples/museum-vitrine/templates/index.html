OBJ-{{ current_year }}.0009
CABINET A

Aegerita candida / paper wasp comb, fragment

Accession {{ current_year }}.0009 Acquired Winter, by exchange Provenance Schoolhouse attic, Saint-Cirq Period Recorded 1907

This fragment, no larger than a child's hand, is the only surviving comb from a colony observed by the schoolmaster F. Aubain over four summers between 1903 and 1907. The schoolmaster kept a small diary of the colony's expansions and quiet collapses, written on the inside flaps of an arithmetic exercise book, and the diary is held with the object as a single accession lot.

The fragment was prepared for the case using a method the conservator does not name in his report — only that it involved the careful warming, in a copper bath, of beeswax thinned with turpentine, applied with a sable brush along the cell walls. The brush is in the cabinet beside the object, and is itself given a separate accession number, as the curator believes a tool used to preserve a specimen earns its own line in the record.

Aegerita combs of this size, dating from the early twentieth century and accompanied by a primary written record, are uncommon in French regional collections; only three are known. Visitors are reminded that the case is lit at twenty-five lux during reading hours and may not be photographed with flash, in keeping with the conservation directive of XI · {{ current_year }}.
