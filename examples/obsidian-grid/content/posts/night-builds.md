# Night Builds

The best code I have ever written was written between midnight and three. Not because I was inspired. Because I was alone.

## Silence as a build step

Silence is the only library that cannot be imported. It arrives when the house is asleep, when the kettle has already boiled and cooled, and it asks nothing of you but to type.

Most engineering meetings are attempts to reconstitute that silence in a room full of people. They always fail. The silence at 2am is not the absence of speech. It is the absence of stakes.

## A short defense of the dark terminal

A dark terminal is not a style. It is a correction. When you lift your eyes from it to a dark window, nothing changes. The contrast is already gone. The computer has graciously turned itself into the wall behind it.

A light terminal at 2am is an act of hostility.

## The other thing about night builds

Night builds will betray you in daylight. Before a commit, read the diff as if you slept through the session. If a reasonable, caffeinated version of yourself would not understand, delete it and start over in the morning.

The best code of the night was still only ever a draft.
