This monograph is dedicated to the preservation of the ancient and heirloom orchard, wherein lies a rich repository of botanical history and flavor. In an era dominated by industrial standardization and uniform, transport-hardened cultivars, the legacy of the old trees remains vulnerable. We seek to document the rare, the forgotten, and the extraordinary pomological specimens that have graced heritage orchards for centuries.

From the sharp, tannic cider apples of the West Country to the crisp, russeted dessert fruits of New England, each variety tells a story of terroir, husbandry, and selection. Through careful grafting, soil care, and pruning, we can ensure that these living monuments of agriculture continue to blossom and bear fruit for generations to come. May this manual serve as a practical guide and a historical testament for the modern orchardist, cider maker, and preservationist who hears the whisper of the old trees.
