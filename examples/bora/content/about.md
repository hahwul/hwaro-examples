My name is Jonah Merrick. I build furniture with hand tools in a one-car garage behind my house in the foothills, and this journal is where the shavings get swept into words.

Bora is the name of a wind — the cold, dry katabatic wind that pours down off the karst and out to sea. The first winter I stacked lumber in this shop, a week of that kind of weather took my rack of green ash from dripping to workable, and the name stuck. A shop, like a wind, is mostly about patience and pressure applied in one direction for a long time.

## What "slow" means here

Slow is not a speed; it is a sequence. Stock is bought rough, stickered, and left alone for seasons, not days. Boards are flattened by hand because the plane tells you things a planer keeps to itself — where the grain reverses, where tension hides, where a board wants to be a door panel instead of a tabletop. Joints are cut to scribed lines and fitted until they close off the saw or admit they need one more shaving.

There is no table saw in this shop, no router, no jointer. This is not a purity oath — machines are wonderful and I use a bandsaw at a friend's shop for resawing — it is an acoustic and a temperamental preference. A quiet shop lets you hear the cut, and hearing the cut is half of sawing straight.

## The journal

Every finished piece gets a build log in the [projects section](/projects/), with the joinery and the species recorded up front the way a recipe lists its ingredients. If you build something from one of these notes, or find an error in one, write to me at the shop: jonah at this domain, spelled the way you would expect.
