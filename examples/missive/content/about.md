Missive is written from the Fenwick Test Floor, an independent materials-testing bench that takes on failure-analysis and qualification work for clients who would rather not see their name in a newsletter. What is left over after the client reports go out — the interesting fractures, the specimens that surprised us, the tests that ran longer than they should have — becomes an issue.

Every issue follows the same discipline. A material comes onto the floor with a batch number. It is loaded, heated, cycled, or struck until it does the thing we are trying to learn about: yields, cracks, ruptures, or — occasionally — simply refuses to fail within the time we had for it. We write down the number. The specimen label on every issue is not decoration; it is the actual batch record, trimmed for print.

<!-- more -->

Missive is not peer-reviewed and is not a substitute for a certified test report. It is closer to a field notebook: specific, dated, and willing to say when a material underperformed its datasheet. We name real test standards (ASTM, ISO) and real failure mechanisms, but batch numbers, client names, and lab identities are fictionalized throughout.

New issues are filed roughly monthly, whenever a specimen on the floor finishes its test program. There is no comment section and no promise of a fixed schedule — only the record, once the test is done.
