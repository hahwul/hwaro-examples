Missive is a letter about the materials that hold the built world together, and about the deliberate, instrumented, sometimes destructive process of finding out whether they actually will. Each issue takes one specimen — an alloy, a ceramic, a glassy metal that refuses to crystallize — through the test that defines it: a tensile pull to yield, a fatigue floor found at two million cycles, a creep-rupture clock run out at 650 degrees. We report the number the material failed at, not just the one it was rated for.

There is no vendor copy here and no marketing spec sheet dressed up as journalism. Every issue is filed from a test floor, with a batch number, a specimen chip, and a verdict. Materials science makes the built world possible; Missive is interested in the moment each material's promise runs out.
