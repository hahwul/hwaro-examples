## How The Archive Is Organized

Three rough buckets keep the archive navigable. Posts are sorted into one of them when they are filed, and a piece can sit in only one bucket to keep the listing clean.

### Long Reads

Anything over fifteen hundred words. Long reads are usually research notes, conference write-ups, or annotated reading lists. The oldest piece in this bucket dates from the spring of 2025.

### Short Notes

Quick observations, link round-ups, and sketch posts. Short notes age out of the main feed faster, which is why a sizable share of the archive lives here. Most pieces are between two and five hundred words.

### Working Drafts

Public drafts that the writer wants to keep visible without giving them feature placement. Drafts that mature into polished essays move into the long-reads bucket on republication, which has happened three times so far in 2026.
