The walls of the red drawing room have begun to pulse.

At first it was subtle — a slow, arrhythmic thrum that could have been the pipes. But the velvet panels now visibly rise and fall. When I place my palm against them I feel heat, and beneath the nap, something that moves like muscle.

Three nights ago the largest panel split along an old seam. Inside was not horsehair or plaster. There were veins. Wet, dark, and unmistakably alive. They branched outward in the shape of an enormous heart that had never belonged to any creature that walked on two legs.

I have ordered the room sealed. The servants refused. They say the room has begun to whisper their names when they pass, and that the whispers sound like old lovers calling them home.

I do not know what will happen when the velvet finally splits completely. I only know that whatever is growing inside it has learned the rhythm of our footsteps.

It is learning to dance.

And it is hungry for partners.
