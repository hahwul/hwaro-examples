My dearest,

The stairs leading down have grown longer again. Last night I counted two hundred and seventeen steps. This morning there were two hundred and nineteen. The extra two smelled of wet earth and something sweet, like lilies left too long in a vase.

I have stopped trying to find the bottom.

The bottles here are not wine. They are labeled in a hand I recognize as my own from years ago, though I have no memory of writing them. Each contains a different silence. When I hold one to my ear I hear conversations I have not yet had.

<!-- more -->

There is one bottle with your name on it. I have not opened it. I am afraid it will contain the sound of you saying goodbye.

If the stairs ever shorten again, I will bring this letter with me. If they do not, then know that I danced well, even here in the dark, and that the last thing I tasted was the memory of your mouth on the night we believed the house could still be escaped.

Do not come looking.

The cellar has already begun to write your name on the next bottle.

Yours, until the glass breaks,

— The one who stayed too long
