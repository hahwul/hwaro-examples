Epistle collects one letter each month from Josiah Teal, keeper of Verge Light, addressed to his sister Constance in Aldercombe. What we publish here are second copies, kept faithfully among a family's papers for the better part of a lifetime before they came into an editor's hands. We have corrected nothing but spelling, and we have added nothing but the titles, which are ours and not Josiah's — he never gave his letters names, only dates.

## Why by letter, and why monthly

Verge Light receives the mail boat roughly once a fortnight, weather permitting, and a letter takes its own time to become worth sending. Josiah wrote when he had a month's worth of the light to report, no faster and no slower, and we have kept that rhythm rather than smoothing it into something more convenient for a reading schedule. One installment arrives here on the first working day of each month, in the order it was written, with nothing held back for effect and nothing moved up for suspense.

## Reading the archive

The full run to date lives in [the letters](/issues/), oldest first, each one carrying its installment number and the serial's running word count in the outer margin — a small bookkeeping habit borrowed from Josiah himself, who numbered his pages against the light's log the same way.

## Subscribing

The form on the home page is a static example: no letter is actually sent, and no address is stored anywhere. If this were a working subscription, a new installment would reach your inbox the same morning it is added to the archive here, plain text, unannotated, exactly as Constance received it.

## A note on the correspondence

Where a letter mentions people or places we have been unable to verify against any harbor record — Fenhollow, Aldercombe, the Lucinda Mare, a keeper named Aldous Mire — we have left them as written. Whatever else these letters are, they are consistent with themselves across five years of monthly delivery, which is more than most correspondence manages.
