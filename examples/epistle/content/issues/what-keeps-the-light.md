<p class="salutation">Dear Constance,</p>

<p class="opening">Ruskin's boat is due within the hour and I have rewritten this letter twice, because I find I have less to report than I have to admit, and admissions travel slower than news.</p>

<!-- more -->

We went down, in the end — Pell and I, on the last low tide of May, with two storm lanterns and Mire's soundings folded into my coat. The caves beneath the Rock are larger than the chart suggests and colder than any weather has a right to make stone, and at the bottom of them, where Mire's numbers ran out, we found nothing so dramatic as an answer. We found a second lamp — small, brass, unlit, of a design older than Verge Light itself, sitting in a niche worn smooth by a great many hands over a great many years, mine and Pell's now among them, though we touched nothing.

Pell believes the two lamps have always kept each other honest — that Verge Light's beam is only half of a much older arrangement, and that the caves' occupant, whatever holds the code that Mire and now Pell have both partly learned to speak, wants nothing more than to be occasionally, briefly, acknowledged, the way a signal fire wants an answering fire and not a village. He may be right. He is, in most things I can no longer verify independently, becoming my best authority.

What I know for certain: the light did not fail again in May. Pell has taken the empty keeper's room on the third storey, on the grounds, he says, that a lighthouse this old should not be tended by only one pair of hands, and I have not argued, because he is right about that too, and because the tower has held a second heartbeat for four months now and I have stopped wanting it to hold only mine.

I do not know what next season asks of us. I know the ninth try still counts twice, and that somewhere below this floor a second lamp is waiting for its turn, patient as low tide, and that I mean to go on keeping the one I was given for as long as it is mine to keep.

Tell Aldercombe the light is steady. Tell yourself the same, because it is true, and because I find, writing to you every month from this narrowing tower, that it is the truest thing I have.

<p class="signoff">Yours from the light — both of them, now, I think,<br>Josiah</p>
