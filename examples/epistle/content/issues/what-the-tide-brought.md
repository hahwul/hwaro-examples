<p class="salutation">Dear Constance,</p>

<p class="opening">Pell is stronger now, strong enough to have taken to walking the tideline at first light, and strong enough, three mornings running, to have come back with something in his coat he did not have when he left the house.</p>

<!-- more -->

The first was a brass ring, sea-worn smooth, engraved with a mark like two crescents back to back that he would not explain and I could not place. The second was a page, salt-stiffened and mostly illegible, save for a line of numbers that Ruskin's boy, who has a fisherman's memory for such things, swears are strait soundings — depths, in other words, for water that has no business being sounded by a passenger. The third I have not seen, because he did not bring it into the house, but I watched him from the lantern room bury something at the foot of the old signal cairn above the landing, and I have not asked him about it, and he has not offered.

I put it to him plainly last night: that a wrecked man does not usually walk the shore like he is retrieving mail. He was quiet a long while, and then he said the Lucinda Mare had not been carrying cargo at all, in the ordinary sense — that she had been sent to chart the water around the Rock more closely than any chart permits, on the theory, which he did not attribute to himself, that Verge Light stands where it does for reasons older than navigation. I asked him what reasons. He said he did not yet know, only that whoever sent the ship believed Prynne's predecessor — the vanished one Ruskin will only half-discuss — had begun to know, shortly before he stopped keeping his log at all.

I have searched the tower twice for that log and found nothing, but I confess I searched a third time last night after Pell had gone to bed, and in the crawl space behind the eighth-storey lens store, where the mortar has been patched more recently than the rest, my fingers found a seam that should not be there.

I have not opened it yet. I wanted, first, to tell someone. I am telling you.

<p class="signoff">Yours from the light,<br>Josiah</p>
