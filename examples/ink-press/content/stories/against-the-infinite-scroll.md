The first sin of the social feed is not that it is addictive, but that it is indefinite. You never finish. You reach the end of what the reader has been given and you are shown more anyway.

## A counter-proposal

The next reading surface should have an ending. Not a pagination boundary that secretly loads — a real ending. A page number that ticks up once. A bar that fills, and then does not reset.

## Why this matters

A thing with a known length is a thing the reader can finish. A finished thing produces closure. Closure produces return — the reader comes back not out of compulsion but out of interest. The feed has trained us to forget this.

## What to do

If you are building a reading surface this quarter, give the reader a visible end. Call it "Edition 48." Call it "Issue 12." Call it "You are done." Give them somewhere to stop.
