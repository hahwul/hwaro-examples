Corten steel, also known as weathering steel, is a group of steel alloys developed to eliminate the need for painting. When exposed to weather, it forms a stable rust-like appearance over several years. The outer layer protects the steel beneath from further corrosion.

## A Material With Character

What makes Corten remarkable is that its appearance changes with time. Each panel develops a unique patina based on its orientation, exposure to rain, and local climate. No two facades age identically.

## Notable Examples

The Angel of the North by Antony Gormley stands 20 meters tall in Gateshead, England. Its Corten surface has become a deep, rich brown that shifts with the seasons. The Barclays Center in Brooklyn features a pre-oxidized Corten facade whose warm tones contrast sharply with the surrounding urban environment.

## The Design Philosophy

Choosing Corten is a statement against the polished permanence of glass and brushed aluminum. It embraces impermanence and accepts that materials have a life of their own. The architect surrenders a degree of control to nature, and the result is a structure that tells its own story over decades.
