Electroplate is built on the principle that depth can be conjured without a single gradient. Every reflective edge in this template is the product of stacked box-shadows, paired insets, and carefully tuned text-shadow values that simulate light catching a polished surface.

The process begins with a base tone, often a near-black or muted graphite. Layered above are highlight strokes using semi-transparent whites, then sunken shadows that imply the bevel of a stamped plate. The result is type that feels embossed and panels that feel pressed, all rendered through standard CSS properties available in any modern browser.

We avoid gradients deliberately. Gradient fills, while convenient, tend to flatten the perception of weight. Stacked shadows preserve the directionality of imagined light and let the surface read as a real material rather than a gradient swatch. Each shadow is tuned for offset, blur, and opacity to keep the metallic illusion stable across screen sizes and color modes.

Designers adapting this template can swap the base palette to chrome, brass, or gunmetal by adjusting only a handful of CSS variables. The plating recipe stays the same.
