Every surface in Electroplate references a real-world finish. Understanding the vocabulary makes it easier to extend the theme into new components without losing the metallic feel.

Chrome is the brightest finish in the system. It uses near-white highlights and tight black shadows to suggest a mirror polish. Use chrome sparingly, on accent elements or call-to-action panels, where the high contrast can carry visual weight without overwhelming surrounding type.

Brushed steel softens the highlights and widens the shadow blur. The effect is industrial and quieter, which makes it the right choice for body containers, sidebars, and listing cards. Brushed steel pairs well with serif body type and longer reading passages.

Gunmetal sits on the dark end. Its highlights are muted blues and its shadows lean toward true black. Gunmetal is best for footers, masthead bars, and anywhere the page needs gravity. Combined with engraved text shadows, it produces a stamped-plate effect that anchors the layout.

These three materials, used in rotation, give Electroplate its consistent voice: structured, reflective, and unmistakably metallic.
