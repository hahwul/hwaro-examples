Raveneau's Les Clos is the definitive expression of Chablis — uncompromising, mineral-driven, and deeply serious.

<!-- more -->

## First Impressions

Pale gold with green reflections. The nose is reticent at first, then slowly reveals crushed oyster shell, flint struck against steel, green apple, and a whisper of white flowers. There is zero oak influence — this is Chardonnay stripped to its purest essence.

## On the Palate

The entry is taut and bracing. This is not a wine that flatters immediately; it demands your attention. Flavors of citrus peel, wet chalk, and green almond unfold across a palate of extraordinary precision.

The minerality here is not metaphor — you can almost taste the Kimmeridgian limestone that defines Chablis. The texture is lean, almost austere, but never thin. There is a coiled intensity that suggests enormous aging potential.

## Acidity as Architecture

The acidity is the defining feature. It is razor-sharp, almost electric, providing the spine around which every other element is organized. This is acidity as architecture — structural, necessary, beautiful.

## The Finish

The finish is long and saline, recalling a brisk ocean breeze. Chalk, lemon zest, and a faintly bitter almond note persist for well over a minute.

## Pairing Suggestions

- Fresh oysters on the half shell
- Grilled Dover sole with caper butter
- Young goat cheese with herbs

## Cellaring Potential

This wine will develop beautifully over the next 15-20 years, gaining richness and complexity while retaining its mineral core. Already striking, but patience will be rewarded.
