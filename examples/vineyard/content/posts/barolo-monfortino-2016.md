Monfortino is a wine that demands patience and rewards it tenfold. The 2016 is among the finest releases in the estate's storied history.

<!-- more -->

## First Impressions

The color is a luminous garnet with characteristic orange-tinged reflections — Nebbiolo announcing itself with quiet confidence. The nose is at once ethereal and profound: dried rose petals, warm tar, red cherry, and a distinct ferrous mineral quality that recalls wet stone.

## On the Palate

The attack is deceptively gentle before the wine reveals its full scope. There is an almost crystalline purity to the fruit — dried Marasca cherry, pomegranate, and blood orange. The mid-palate is where the wine truly stuns: wave upon wave of flavor, each more complex than the last.

The tannins are formidable but impeccably polished after years of extended cask aging. They provide a frame of iron wrapped in silk.

## Structure and Balance

What sets this vintage apart is the tension between power and elegance. The acidity is electric — a driving force that carries the wine across the palate and into an impossibly long finish. There is nothing heavy or ponderous about this Barolo; it floats with the lightness of great Burgundy while maintaining Piedmontese gravitas.

## The Finish

Minutes. The finish simply does not end. Tar, rose, camphor, and dried herbs linger in shifting patterns.

## Pairing Suggestions

- Braised veal cheeks with truffle
- Fresh egg pasta with butter and white truffle
- Aged Parmigiano-Reggiano (36+ months)

## Cellaring Potential

Easily 40-50 years of development ahead. This is a wine for patience. If opening now, decant for at least 3-4 hours.
