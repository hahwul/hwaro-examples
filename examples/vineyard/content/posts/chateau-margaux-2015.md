The 2015 vintage at Chateau Margaux is nothing short of magnificent. From the moment the cork is drawn, this wine commands attention.

<!-- more -->

## First Impressions

The color is a deep, almost opaque ruby with violet reflections at the rim. Swirling the glass releases an initial burst of crushed blackcurrant and fresh violets, followed by more subtle notes of graphite, cigar box, and crushed stone.

## On the Palate

The entry is remarkably silky — the hallmark of great Margaux. There is an immediate sense of precision and poise. Dark fruit flavors unfold in layers: blackcurrant, then black cherry, then something darker and more brooding. The mid-palate reveals mineral tones and a subtle savory quality that adds complexity.

The tannins deserve special mention. They are extraordinarily fine-grained, present but never obtrusive, providing the architecture for what will be decades of graceful evolution.

## The Finish

The finish extends for well over a minute, with a persistent echo of violets and graphite. It is at once powerful and delicate — a paradox that only the finest wines can sustain.

## Pairing Suggestions

- Rack of lamb with rosemary and thyme
- Aged Comte cheese
- Seared duck breast with blackberry jus

## Cellaring Potential

This wine is magnificent now but has at least 30-40 years ahead of it. Decant for 2-3 hours if drinking young.
