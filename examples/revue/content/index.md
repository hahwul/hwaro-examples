Revue is filed once a month by a small, argumentative desk that cares more about how a print survives than about what won at any festival this year. We cover restoration labs and the choices their colorists make with no reference print to check against; we cover the repertory houses that still book 35mm on faith; and we cover the unglamorous administrative layer underneath both — ledgers, condition reports, and the projectionists who write them. Every essay runs with a kicker naming its beat, a byline in the house's letterspaced caps, and no photographs, because the film is the only image that gets to be the image.
