Cirrus is the workshop notebook of Wren Halloway, a kite builder working out of a
converted garden shed on the edge of a small town where the wind never quite
makes up its mind. It started as a way to remember which bridle setting worked,
and turned into a habit of writing down every build — the graceful ones and the
ones that came home in a bag.

## The bench

The shed holds a sewing machine older than I am, a wall of fibreglass and bamboo
spar stock sorted by diameter, a soldering iron kept only for hot-cutting
ripstop, and a drawer of offcuts too good to throw out and too small to use. The
kettle is the most important tool. Nothing gets cut in a hurry.

## What you will find here

Every post is a complete build: the sail plan I cut from, the spar and bridle
spec, and a flight report from the field behind the allotments. The measurements
are real and the tolerances are honest. If a spar was under-specced or a bridle
point was wrong, it says so — usually in the crash report.

## The one rule

Write down the crashes too. A workshop log that only records the wins teaches
you nothing the second time the wind gets up. The
[Diamond Down report](@/builds/diamond-down.md) is the entry I am proudest of,
precisely because it went so badly.

## Getting in touch

There is no shop and nothing for sale. If you build one of these and it flies —
or especially if it does not — I would love to hear how. The best flight reports
always come from someone else's field.
