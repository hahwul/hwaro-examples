Dear girl,

You came with your little pot of glue and your trembling hands and you said, *“I will make you beautiful again.”* You did not ask if I wanted to be beautiful in the way you understood beauty.

You pressed my cheek to my jaw with too much pressure. You used too much of the clear stuff that smells like hospitals. When it dried, it pulled the glaze tighter than it had ever been. I could feel the skin of the world stretch.

I tried to thank you. My mouth would not open the way it used to. One of the new cracks had learned how to hold a smile in place.

You cried when you finished. I watched the tears fall into the places where the pieces did not quite meet. They dried there. Now when the light hits me from the left, it looks as though I am weeping gold.

I am not.

I am only waiting.

You left the window open the night you finished. The cold came in and settled in the hollow behind my left eye. Sometimes I hear it moving.

I hope you never come back to check your work.

Yours, always,
the one you named perfect once more
