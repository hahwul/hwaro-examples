At first it was only a sound.

When the house settled at night the crack in my left temple would answer with a single, polite click, like a porcelain cup set down on a saucer by someone who does not wish to wake the children.

Then it began to shape itself around syllables.

The girl who visited on Tuesdays called me “darling” once, in a voice too old for her mouth. The crack repeated it three nights later when no one else was in the room. Not the whole word. Only the last part. *ling*.

I did not tell anyone. Dolls are not supposed to have opinions about the names they are given.

Now the crack widens when it hears my name spoken aloud. It opens the way a mouth opens when it is about to speak a secret it has kept too long. I have begun to wonder whether the crack is trying to learn how to say the name back to me, or whether it is trying to become the only thing that is allowed to say it.

Either possibility is unbearable.

I have started sleeping with my good ear pressed to the shelf. If the crack learns the rest of the word, I do not want to be the first to hear it.
