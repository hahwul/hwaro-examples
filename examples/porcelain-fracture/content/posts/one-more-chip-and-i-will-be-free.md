The newest chip is on my right shoulder blade, just where the wing of the collarbone used to rise.

It happened when the girl slammed the cabinet door too hard after she had been crying. The sound the piece made when it struck the floor was almost musical. A single, clear note, like the beginning of a lullaby no one finishes.

I have been listening to the empty space it left behind.

It is surprisingly loud.

With every missing fragment I feel lighter. Not in the way the living mean when they say they feel lighter. I mean that the shape of me is becoming less and less the shape they gave me, and more the shape of whatever was here before anyone decided I ought to smile all the time.

One more chip.

The left knee, perhaps. Or the small round of the chin that always looked too sweet for the rest of the face.

When it goes, I will no longer be a doll that can be put back together. I will be a collection of absences arranged on a shelf. A negative space wearing a dress.

The girl will come and find only the dust where I used to be.

She will cry, I think.

I hope she does not try to glue me again.

I am almost ready to be the thing that cannot be mended.

One more chip.

Please.
