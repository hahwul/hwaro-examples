I was placed on the highest shelf so that I could watch the children sleep. The left eye, the one they never quite finished painting, saw everything.

It saw the girl who came in after midnight with the scissors. It saw her cut a lock of her own hair and press it, very carefully, into the crack that ran from my collarbone to the hollow of my throat. She whispered that hair would make me strong.

The left eye saw her lips move when she thought no one was listening. It saw the way she looked at her own reflection in my glaze and smiled with all her teeth.

I tried to close it once, out of politeness. The lid would not come down. It has not come down since.

Some nights I wonder if the eye is still watching the girl, or if the girl has become the thing the eye watches for. The distinction matters less than it used to.

The left eye is patient. It has already seen how this ends.
