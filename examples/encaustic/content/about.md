<span class="wax-label">The Philosophy</span>

Encaustic is the art of painting with heated beeswax to which colored pigments are added. The word comes from the Greek _enkaustikos_, meaning "to burn in." Each layer is fused with controlled heat, creating a surface of extraordinary depth and permanence.

This design takes that principle literally. Every element is deliberate, layered, and fused into place. There are no soft fades, no decorative distractions. Only structure, warmth, and clarity.

> The flame does not forgive hesitation. Every mark in encaustic is permanent, every layer a commitment.

### The Palette

- **Beeswax** -- The warm, luminous foundation that gives everything its glow.
- **Burnt Sienna** -- Deep reddish earth, the color of fired clay and ancient walls.
- **Raw Umber** -- Dark, grounding brown that anchors the composition.
- **Damar Resin** -- The crystalline hardener that adds strength and sheen.
- **Ivory** -- The cool highlight, like light passing through translucent wax.

### The Process

Encaustic demands a specific sequence: heat the medium, mix the pigment, apply to the surface, and fuse with flame. Each step is irreversible. This constraint produces work of startling clarity because there is no room for indecision.

The same principle guides this design. Thick borders define space. Solid color blocks create contrast. Layered shadows suggest depth without resorting to transparency or blur.
