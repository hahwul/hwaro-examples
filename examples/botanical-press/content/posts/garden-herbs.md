An apothecary garden is both a sanctuary and a source of healing. Identifying the herbs within it requires an eye for detail and an appreciation for the subtle differences in leaf shape, aroma, and bloom.

### Rosemary (*Salvia rosmarinus*)

Recognizable by its needle-like leaves and woody stems, Rosemary is a staple of the Mediterranean garden. When crushed, its leaves release a pungent, pine-like fragrance that has been associated with memory and remembrance for centuries.

### Lavender (*Lavandula*)

The silvery-green foliage and tall spikes of purple flowers make Lavender unmistakable. Beyond its beauty, it is prized for its calming essential oils and its ability to attract pollinators to the garden.

### Mint (*Mentha*)

Mint is a vigorous grower with square stems and serrated leaves. It comes in many varieties—from Peppermint to Spearmint—each offering a refreshing burst of flavor and scent. Be warned: its roots like to wander, so it is often best grown in a dedicated container or a controlled patch.

By learning to identify these botanical treasures, we connect more deeply with the land and the ancient wisdom of herbalism.
