Psychedelic color is not random. It follows a deliberate anti-logic — choosing combinations that conventional design theory warns against, then pushing them to extremes.

## The Rules of Breaking Rules

Traditional color theory teaches harmony: complementary pairs, analogous schemes, triads. Psychedelic design starts with these relationships, then distorts them:

- **Complementary clash** — instead of balancing red and green, push both to fluorescent intensity until they vibrate against each other
- **Analogous overload** — stack five shades of hot pink to magenta to purple until the boundary between them dissolves
- **Simultaneous contrast** — place colors side by side that create optical interference patterns

## The Fluorescent Palette

Fluorescent colors are central to acid graphics because they exceed the normal gamut of what screens and printers can reproduce. In physical media, fluorescent inks absorb UV light and re-emit it as visible light — they literally glow.

On screen, we approximate this with maximum saturation:

```css
:root {
  --acid-green: #39ff14;
  --acid-pink: #ff6ec7;
  --acid-yellow: #ffff00;
  --acid-orange: #ff5f1f;
  --acid-violet: #bf00ff;
}
```

## Optical Effects

When high-saturation colors meet at sharp boundaries, the human visual system produces artifacts — edges shimmer, colors bleed, and surfaces appear to pulse. This is not a bug. It is the defining feature of psychedelic design.

The viewer's perception becomes part of the artwork. What you see depends on how long you look, how close you are, and what your eyes did five seconds ago.

## Digital vs. Analog

Digital screens have an advantage for psychedelic color: they emit light directly. A fluorescent green on screen is generated by actual photon emission, not by reflected light from ink on paper. This means screen-based acid graphics can achieve intensities that print cannot match.

The tradeoff is that screens have a fixed gamut. Fluorescent inks in physical media can exceed sRGB, producing colors that no monitor can display. The best acid graphics artists understand both mediums and design for the strengths of each.
