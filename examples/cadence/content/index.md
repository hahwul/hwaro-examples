Cadence is a biweekly show about the English that's still moving: the
vowel a grandchild says differently than their grandparent without either
one noticing, the pronoun quietly falling out of casual speech, the
loanword worn smooth at the edges after two generations. Every episode
title below carries its own transcription — the show's way of pinning one
exact pronunciation to the page, the same way it discusses drift on air.
