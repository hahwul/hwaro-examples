## What happens after you write

<ol class="method-steps">
<li><h3>Intake reply, within two business days</h3><p>We confirm we have capacity for your timeline and send our standard mutual NDA if you have not already signed one.</p></li>
<li><h3>Scoping call, thirty to forty-five minutes</h3><p>We ask about the systems in scope, your launch or renewal timeline, and what has already been reviewed. No slide deck, no pricing pitch — just enough to write an accurate proposal.</p></li>
<li><h3>Written proposal, within a week of the call</h3><p>Scope, duration, team, price, and start date, in one document. We do not require a signature to hold a start date for more than two weeks.</p></li>
<li><h3>Engagement kickoff</h3><p>Source access, environment mirroring, and the first step of the methodology begin on the agreed start date, not whenever we get around to it.</p></li>
</ol>

## Fit, honestly

We turn down more engagements than we accept, usually because the timeline does not allow for real manual review or because the work is better suited to a larger firm with more auditors to throw at a very large codebase. If we are not the right fit, we will say so on the scoping call and, where we can, point you toward someone who is.
