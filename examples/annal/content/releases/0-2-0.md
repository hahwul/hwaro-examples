The second folio turns wikilinks into something bidirectional. Every note now
carries a quiet ledger of who points to it — and, more usefully, who *almost*
points to it but hasn't yet.

<!-- more -->

### Added

- A backlinks panel docked under the editor lists every note that links here,
  with the linking sentence quoted for context so you don't have to open each
  one to remember why it's connected.
- Unlinked mentions: Commonplace scans the vault for plain-text occurrences
  of a note's title and surfaces them separately, with a one-click action to
  turn the mention into a real `[[wikilink]]`.
- Tags are now indexed at parse time and browsable from a sidebar tree,
  including nested tags written as `#project/annal-ledger`.
- `commonplace vault stats` prints note count, link count, and orphan count
  (notes with zero inbound or outbound links) for the current vault.

### Changed

- The editor pane now reflows to make room for the backlinks panel on
  windows narrower than 1000px, collapsing it behind a toggle rather than
  overlapping the text.

### Fixed

- Wikilinks to notes with punctuation in the title (`What's Next?`) no
  longer silently fail to resolve.
- Front matter written with tabs instead of spaces parsed inconsistently;
  the YAML parser now normalizes indentation before reading.

Orphan count turned out to be the most-quoted number from this release in
early feedback — nothing motivates linking your notes like watching that
number in the corner refuse to go down.
