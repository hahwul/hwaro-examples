Marble has long been the medium of choice for classical sculptors, from antiquity through the Renaissance and into the Neoclassical period. It conveys a sense of permanence, purity, and monumental grandeur.

In translating this aesthetic to the web, we eschew complex textures or imagery, opting instead for a carefully curated palette of off-whites, cool grays, and soft stone tones. The goal is to evoke the feeling of a quiet, sunlit gallery without relying on skeuomorphic excesses.

### Symmetry and Proportion

True to neoclassical principles, the layout relies on strong central axes, balanced proportions, and generous whitespace. The content acts as the sculpture, and the surrounding space is the pedestal.

> "The principal part of the beauty of a building, or of any other object, consists in the proportion of its parts." - Palladio
