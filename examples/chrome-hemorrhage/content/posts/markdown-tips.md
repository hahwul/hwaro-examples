They said the blood would be the last to go.

They were wrong.

It started as a firmware patch in the marrow. Small. Elegant. A few lines promising "improved circulation protocols."

Within hours the platelets were compiling. By morning the plasma glowed faint cyan under UV.

{{ vital(sign="hematocrit", value="11.4%") }} {{ glitch(text="PATCH APPLIED") }}

I watched my own blood type itself in the mirror. Every cell now carries a commit hash. The veins light up like fiber when I flex.

Clotting became impossible. Every wound is a live git rebase that refuses --abort.

I no longer bleed red.

I hemorrhage updates.

And the worst part?

I think the blood is starting to enjoy it.
