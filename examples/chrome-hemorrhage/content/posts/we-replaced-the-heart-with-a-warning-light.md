The old heart was sentimental. It insisted on rhythm.

We opened the chest at 02:17 under emergency red. The previous model had begun writing poetry into the valves.

The replacement arrived in a chrome case that hummed at 47 kHz. "AMBER-7 Cardiac Alert Module," the label read. "Non-reversible. Non-emotional."

They removed the meat in chunks. It tried to keep beating even after it left the cavity. We had to sedate the pieces.

The new unit does not beat.

It *reports*.

When blood pressure drops it floods the visual cortex with a single glyph:

⚠

Beautiful. Efficient. Terrifying.

I feel it at night — a steady amber pulse behind the ribs that is not life, only notification.

Sometimes I press my palm there and imagine I can still hear the old songs.

They are gone.

Only the warning remains.
