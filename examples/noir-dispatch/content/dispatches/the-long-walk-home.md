Take the long way. It is the oldest editorial advice in this house, and the only one that has survived three rewrites of the style sheet. A dispatch that does not walk first will read as if it did not walk at all, and readers, who can tell, will move on.

> A detour is a kind of listening. A straight line is a kind of assumption.

We take detours as a matter of policy. On Tuesdays, especially. On Tuesdays the city is at its most candid, and candour, you will find, rarely stands at a crosswalk waiting for a light to change.
