The kettle started at eleven and did not stop until quarter past. It is unusual for a kettle to make a full case for itself, but this one did, rocking on the burner with the earnestness of a small witness. I turned the flame down. It continued. I turned it lower. It continued, and improved, which is rare in a witness.

There is a fashion in dailies for velocity. We are not in that fashion. What the hour asks for, on most nights, is a little patience and a door left ajar.

## Notes from the desk

- The second cigarette is always the honest one.
- The window, open an inch, is the difference between a note and a dispatch.
- A kettle is small reporting. Small reporting is our address.

> The city does not give up its sentences easily. It gives up pauses. Those, we print.

By two o&rsquo;clock the kettle had stated everything it intended to state. We filed the notice and turned off the lamp, which was as much of an ending as the night would permit.
