She said: *I wanted the long way around.* He said: *It&rsquo;s Tuesday.* The napkin took note. The napkin, in a good bar, is the most honest stenographer in the room.

Tuesday is not a bad night for long ways around. It is a better night for walking home, which is what we did afterwards, slowly and without a column in mind. A column has a way of arriving on its own, if you walk long enough, and we did.

- Two sentences.
- One napkin, pocketed.
- A page filled, later.

That is the whole of it. Dispatches, sometimes, are small.
