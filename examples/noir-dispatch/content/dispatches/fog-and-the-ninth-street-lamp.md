Fog arrived around ten and stayed on. The lamp at Ninth and Laurel has a habit of flickering in the middle of a thought, as if reconsidering the street. Tonight it reconsidered three times and then settled.

We walked the block twice. The first pass was for weather; the second, for company. A man in a grey coat nodded in the way men in grey coats nod when they have nothing to sell and nothing to refuse.

## Inventory

1. One bicycle, chained to the wrong lamp.
2. A letter, unopened, left on a window ledge.
3. A cat, uninterested.

None of it made a headline. All of it made a block.
