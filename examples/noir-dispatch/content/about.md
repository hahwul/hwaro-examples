The Noir Dispatch is an imagined broadsheet for the small hours. It is pressed, folded, and delivered by a staff of one, in a room with a chair that creaks and a lamp that has seen things. We are not in competition with the morning dailies. We are in an agreement with the lamps.

## House Rules

We publish what we can verify, and we verify what we can stand behind. Anything that cannot be stood behind is returned to the envelope it arrived in.

- Short paragraphs for impatient readers.
- Long paragraphs for patient ones.
- No dispatch printed before the second cup.

## The Desk

The desk is at the window. The window looks onto a street that has forgotten most of its own names. That is material enough for a year.

> A good story, read carefully, leaves the reader quieter than it found them.

Write in. We reply after midnight.
