*Squall* is the passage log kept aboard [*Petrel*](/about/), a full-keel cutter sailed single-handed out of Corvid Harbor along the Ferrow Strait and the Ansel Islands. Every entry is written the morning after making harbor, with the barometer and the wind noted the way they looked at the helm — not corrected afterward against a forecast that turned out to be right, or wrong.

There's no itinerary here, no cruising guide dressed up as a blog. Just the passages as they happened, gale warnings and flat calms both, logged with the same honesty either way. A falling glass gets written down as falling, even when the passage that follows turns out easier than the number promised.

The instruments are simple and few: a barometer read at the chart table, a compass, a wind estimate made by eye and years of guessing rather than by an anemometer anyone should fully trust. What's below is the record those instruments kept, one gusty stretch of coast at a time.
