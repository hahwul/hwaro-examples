## Flight Control
Foundational coordination within the complex structural environment. Controls integrate hydraulic, fly-by-wire, and digital channels through a single command surface, allowing the pilot to balance authority and feedback under variable load conditions.

## Instrumentation
Primary displays consolidate altitude, attitude, airspeed, and heading into a unified attention field. Secondary panels handle fuel, engine, and electrical telemetry, while annunciators escalate only when threshold logic warrants pilot intervention.

## Procedures
Checklist discipline anchors every phase of operation. Pre-flight, taxi, takeoff, cruise, descent, and shutdown each follow a fixed cadence, with cross-references to abnormal and emergency procedures recorded in the supplementary manual.

## Recovery Modes
When automation degrades, manual reversion paths preserve flight control without external dependencies. The recovery hierarchy assumes progressive failure and prefers conservative authority limits over restored automation, until the airframe and crew confirm a stable corridor.
