A machined case doesn't wear out the way a molded one can, but the
[switches](/switches/), the cable, and the electronics underneath it will
eventually need attention. This page covers what's guaranteed, what it
costs to fix outside that guarantee, and how to reach us when something
needs a second look.

## Warranty

The case itself — the aluminum, the machining, the anodize — carries a
five-year structural warranty against manufacturing defects: a mounting
hole cut off-spec, a finish that flakes rather than scratches, a plate that
doesn't seat flush. It does not cover drops, spills, or the ordinary wear
of an anodized surface thinning at the palm rest over years of use.
Electronics — the PCB, the USB-C daughterboard, the controller — carry a
one-year warranty, which is shorter because that's where genuine component
failure actually shows up, if it's going to.

| Coverage | Duration | What's included |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Case and machining | 5 years | Manufacturing defects in material or cut |
| Electronics | 1 year | PCB, controller, USB-C port |
| Switches | 1 year | Manufacturing defects, not wear |
| Anodize finish | 1 year | Flaking or peeling, not scratching |

## Repair program

Outside of warranty, we run a flat-fee mail-in repair program rather than
quoting each case individually. Send the board in with the machining
drawing that shipped in its original box — it has the serial and finish
code we need to match refinishing stock — and we'll refinish worn anodize,
replace a cracked screw boss, or swap a damaged plate for a flat $45,
turnaround usually two weeks from the day it arrives on our bench.

{{ alert(type="note", body="No drawing on hand? Send us the serial number stamped on the underside of the case instead — every drawing we've ever printed is on file, indexed by serial, going back to the first TQ-60 batch.") }}

## Getting in touch

For warranty claims, repair intake, or anything not answered here, write to
`shop@torque-mfg.example`. We read every message ourselves — there is no
support queue or ticketing layer between you and the two people who run the
mill — and typically reply within two business days, longer during a
finish-tank backlog. The shop itself sits outside Two Harbors, Minnesota,
and mail-in repairs should be addressed there; we'll send the exact address
once a repair is confirmed so nothing goes to an old building we've since
outgrown.
