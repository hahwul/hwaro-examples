We tried sourcing low-profile switches from outside suppliers for the first
prototype run and gave up after the third batch arrived with a different
bottom-out feel than the one before it. A case [machined](/materials/) to
±0.02mm tolerance is wasted on a switch that can't hold its own spec, so we
now design and spec the switch ourselves and have it built to our tooling
by a contract manufacturer we've worked with since the first
[TQ-60](/keyboards/) run. The result is the Signal line — three variants,
one shared mechanical platform.

## Signal Linear

The default across every model. A short pole stem rides in a POM housing
with no leaf and no click bar, so the only feedback is the spring building
resistance until the stem bottoms out against the base. Factory lubed with
a light grease on the stem legs and spring coils before assembly.

| Spec | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Feel | Linear |
| Actuation force | 45g |
| Bottom-out force | 62g |
| Pretravel | 0.5mm |
| Total travel | 1.2mm |
| Housing | POM upper, nylon lower |

## Signal Tactile

Same housing and travel as the Linear, with a small tactile bump built into
the stem profile instead of the leaf-and-click-bar mechanism a full-height
tactile switch would use — there isn't room for one at 1.2mm of travel. The
bump sits early, just past a third of the way down, so it registers before
your finger has committed much force.

| Spec | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Feel | Tactile, no click |
| Actuation force | 52g |
| Bump position | 0.4mm |
| Bottom-out force | 65g |
| Total travel | 1.2mm |
| Housing | POM upper, nylon lower |

## Signal Silent Linear

The Linear's spring and stem, with a thin silicone pad bonded inside the
base to absorb the bottom-out strike. It costs a small amount of travel
consistency switch to switch — the pads compress at very slightly different
rates — which is the one place we accept a wider tolerance band in exchange
for an office-appropriate board.

| Spec | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Feel | Linear, dampened |
| Actuation force | 45g |
| Bottom-out force | 58g |
| Total travel | 1.15–1.2mm |
| Housing | POM upper, nylon lower, silicone pad |

## Hot-swap and sockets

Every board ships with five-pin hot-swap sockets under the plate, so any
Signal variant can be pulled and replaced with a switch puller and no
solder. We sell loose switches in bags of ten through the spare parts store,
and every board's box includes two spares of whatever variant it shipped
with — enough to cover the two or three positions that see the most wear
over a few years of daily typing.

## Lubrication and run-in

Springs and stems are lubed on the assembly line, not by hand per unit, so
consistency across a batch matters more to us than a hand-tuned feel on any
single switch. New boards can feel very slightly stiffer for the first one
to two weeks of typing as the lube distributes evenly across the stem legs;
this is normal and not something we consider a defect during that window.
