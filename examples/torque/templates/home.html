LOW-PROFILE STACK HEIGHT

1.2MM

Total Key Travel

A standard mechanical switch travels four millimeters before it bottoms out. Ours travels 1.2 — the whole stack, case through keycap, measures 18.4mm at the front edge, close to the height of two stacked keycaps on a full-travel board. Less distance between intent and input, and a wrist angle that doesn't need a wrist rest to stay level.

ONE BILLET, NO SEAMS

6063-T5

Aircraft-Grade Billet

Each case starts as a 1.4-kilogram block of 6063-T5 aluminum, rolled and pre-aged, sourced from a mill two states over. A three-axis mill removes better than two-thirds of that mass over a fifty-five-minute rough-and-finish cycle, leaving one continuous shell with no bonded seam, no plastic insert, and no parting line to eventually crack. The offcuts go back to the same supplier as recycled stock.

Every board ships with its own drawing.

Each Torque leaves the shop with a printed copy of its own machining drawing — the same file the mill read to cut it, dimensioned and dated, folded into the box under the keyboard. It is not a warranty card. It is proof of what you own and how it was made, and it's the same document we keep on file if a part ever needs replacing.
