## Featured Chronicles

The scriptorium burns low. Three candles remain upon the writing desk, each one casting long shadows across parchment grown yellowed by the passage of many winters. Within this cathedral of stone and silence, the chroniclers labor without ceasing to preserve what the living world would otherwise lose to oblivion.

These are not tales of valor and triumphant armies. These are the accounts no herald dare announce, the histories scratched in the margins where daylight does not reach. They concern themselves with the weight of old decisions, the slow hunger of dark places, and the men and women who looked into those depths and did not look away.

---

### The Three Pillars of This Scriptorium

**The Undying City** concerns itself with a settlement that refuses to submit to time, whose stones remember kings whose names have been scraped from every other record. Its population does not age in the expected manner, and the cathedral at its center has never required a single repair despite standing for eleven centuries.

**The Cartographer of Forgotten Places** follows a woman who draws maps of locations that do not exist yet---or no longer exist, or exist in states of probability that resist conventional notation. Her most recent commission came unsigned, the payment left in a locked room to which no one held a key.

**The Doctrine of Hollow Saints** examines what becomes of faith when the objects of veneration are proven, beyond reasonable disputation, to be counterfeit. The relics are false, the miracles manufactured, and yet the healings continue. What force, then, does the cathedral actually serve?

## About the Scriptorium

This chronicle is maintained by a single writer working in the tradition of the monastic scribes: long hours, poor light, the occasional crisis of faith, and the persistent conviction that the work matters even when the world outside the cloister walls suggests otherwise.

New chronicles are inscribed when the darkness speaks clearly enough to be transcribed. Silence is not absence. It is preparation.

The candle in the window burns for those still finding their way through the dark.
