Most of the civic buildings built between 1955 and 1980 are past the age where anyone defends them out of nostalgia and not yet old enough to be protected out of reflex. That gap is where Masthead works. We send critics into courthouses, pools, libraries, parking structures, and trade schools that a public agency once paid to build and ask a narrow question: what is this building's second life actually like to use, and does it deserve one?

Some of what we cover has been converted wholesale into something its architect never imagined. Some has simply been restored, quietly, by people who never called it preservation. A few pieces here cover buildings still arguing for their own survival, deferred from demolition by a vote that could easily go the other way next cycle. We try not to arrive with a verdict already decided by the style label on the building. Concrete built a lot of very different arguments in this period, and so did brick.
