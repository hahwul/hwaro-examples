Every patch below is a complete, reproducible signal path for the Karst-6 — not a starting point to tweak, but a destination you can dial in from a cold patch bay in a few minutes. Each entry documents its sources, its modulation routing, and its final settings, alongside a signal-flow diagram of the exact cabling, so you can see the routing before you read a single parameter.

The six entries were chosen to cover the instrument's range rather than to flatter it: a drone that never resolves, a sequence built to drift out of phase with its own clock, a pad too slow to notice moving, a lead voice with no softness in its attack, a bassline locked to an eight-step pulse, and a percussion patch that samples a fresh pitch on every hit. Between them, all ten Karst-6 modules make at least one appearance.

Patches are organized by family in the sidebar — Drones, Basslines, Sequences, Pads, Leads, and Percussion. Ctrl+K (Cmd+K) opens the command palette if you already know the name you are looking for, and every patch carries one or two tags for browsing by mood instead of family.
