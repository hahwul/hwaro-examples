Six modules generate signal or noise. Two shape it. One sequences it, one samples it, and one mixes it out — that is the entire fixed architecture of the Karst-6, and every sound in this encyclopedia is built from nothing more than those ten blocks patched in a different order.

Baobab exists because the Karst-6 has no preset memory. Once you unpatch it, a sound is gone unless it is written down precisely enough to rebuild — which module fed which, what each knob was set to, and what was patched into what CV input at what depth. That is what every page here does: sources, modulation, and settings, in that order, with a signal-flow diagram above them so you can see the routing before you read a single parameter.

Six families are catalogued so far — drones that never resolve, basslines locked to an eight-step clock, sequences that drift out of phase with themselves, pads too slow to notice moving, one lead voice, and one percussion patch built from sampled noise. Start from the sidebar, or press Ctrl+K to jump straight to a patch by name.
