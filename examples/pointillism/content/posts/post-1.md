title = "The Origins of Pointillism"
date = "2025-01-12"
+++
Pointillism began as an innovative artistic movement in the late 19th century. By applying small, distinct dots of pure color, artists allowed the viewer's eye to blend the colors optically, creating a luminous and vibrant effect unlike traditional mixing.

This technique, pioneered by artists like Georges Seurat, challenged the conventional methods of painting by relying on the science of optics.
