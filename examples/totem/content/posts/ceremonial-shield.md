## Project Overview

The Orca Shield is a bent-cedar ceremonial shield bearing the image of Killer Whale rendered in the formline design tradition. The shield measures 30 inches in diameter and is constructed from steam-bent cedar planks laced together with rawhide. The Orca design spans the full face of the shield, its body curving in the characteristic S-form that suggests powerful movement through dark waters.

## The Orca in Pacific Northwest Art

Orca -- Killer Whale -- holds a place of profound respect in coastal cultures. Known as the "ruler of the undersea world," Orca is the marine counterpart to the wolf on land. Orcas are associated with family bonds, strength, intelligence, and safe travel across water. In Haida tradition, the Orca is connected to the souls of drowned chiefs, who are said to transform into these great creatures.

The formline representation of Orca is one of the most recognizable images in Northwest Coast art. Key identifying features include:

- **Prominent dorsal fin** -- tall and sweeping, often stylized as a separate design element
- **Rounded head** with a distinctive eye patch rendered as an ovoid
- **Pectoral fins** shown as U-form elements extending from the body
- **Tail flukes** split and curved, often containing secondary faces
- **Blowhole** sometimes depicted as a small ovoid on top of the head

## Construction

### Bent-Wood Technique

The shield body was constructed using the ancient bent-wood technique. Cedar planks were cut to thickness, soaked in water for several days, then slowly steamed over a rock-lined pit fire. As the wood became pliable, it was pressed into a curved form and held with temporary lashings until dry. Three planks were shaped this way and then joined edge-to-edge.

### Joining and Lacing

The planks were joined using a combination of drilled holes and rawhide lacing. Wet rawhide was threaded through paired holes along each seam, then allowed to dry and contract, pulling the joints tight. This creates an extremely strong bond that also allows slight flexibility -- important for a shield that must absorb impact without shattering.

### Surface Preparation

The front face was planed smooth using a straight knife, creating a flat painting surface. The back was left with visible adze marks for grip texture and to reduce weight. A rawhide arm loop and hand grip were lashed to the back using the same drilling and lacing technique.

### Painting the Orca

The Orca design was first sketched in charcoal directly on the shield face, working out the proportional relationships between the head, body, fins, and tail. The formline rules demand that every element connect to every other element -- there are no floating shapes, no disconnected lines. The entire Orca is one continuous flowing design.

Painting followed the traditional sequence:

1. **Primary formlines** in black -- the bold outlines defining the major body shapes
2. **Secondary formlines** in earth red -- filling the interior of the primary shapes with flowing U-forms and split-U forms
3. **Tertiary areas** in turquoise -- small accent shapes in the negative spaces, particularly around the eye, blowhole, and fin joints
4. **Negative space** left as natural cedar -- the warm wood tone serves as a fourth color in the composition

## Design Details

The Orca on this shield is shown in a diving posture, its body curving downward from upper left to lower right. The dorsal fin rises at the top of the composition, acting as the visual apex. The eye is the emotional center -- a large ovoid containing a circle and crescent that gives the Orca an expression of calm authority.

Within the tail flukes, a secondary face appears -- a smaller being contained within the larger. This "face within a form" technique is characteristic of the most sophisticated formline work, suggesting the spiritual depth of the creature and the idea that all beings contain other beings within them.

## Specifications

- Material: Western Red Cedar planks, rawhide lacing
- Diameter: 30 inches
- Depth: 4 inches (curvature)
- Weight: 8.5 pounds
- Pigments: Charcoal black, earth red ochre, copper carbonate turquoise
- Back fittings: Rawhide arm loop and hand grip
