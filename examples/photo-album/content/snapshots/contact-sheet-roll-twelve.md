## The Roll

Roll twelve was loaded into the camera on a Tuesday and finished the following Sunday. Six exposures were made on a walk through the neighborhood. Twelve frames came from a family lunch on Saturday. The remaining eighteen exposures were made indoors over four evenings, mostly at table-top distances under tungsten lighting.

The film was Ilford HP5 Plus, rated at box speed of ISO 400. It was developed in Kodak HC-110, dilution B, for 7 minutes 30 seconds at 20 degrees Celsius, with continuous agitation for the first 30 seconds and then five inversions every minute. This is a working development scheme that produces moderately contrasty negatives well-suited for darkroom printing on grade 2 paper.

## The Sheet

The contact sheet was printed at grade 2 on Ilford Multigrade IV RC paper, exposed at f/8 for 12 seconds under a 75-watt enlarger bulb. Each frame is reproduced at the actual negative size — 24 by 36 millimeters. The full sheet shows all 36 exposures plus the leader and tail of the roll.

Reading the sheet in sequence reveals the rhythm of a week. The first exposures are bright outdoor frames with deep shadows. The middle of the roll is family portraits — even tonality, faces well-lit by window light. The final third is darker: lamps, books on a desk, a hand resting on a wooden table. The exposure interval lengthens as the subject matter grows quieter.

## Selection

Three frames were marked for printing. They are circled in red wax pencil on the sheet itself. The first is frame 9, a portrait of a child at the lunch table. The second is frame 22, a still life of a teacup and a book. The third is frame 31, made on the Saturday evening — a window reflecting the corner lamp.

The contact sheet itself is filed in a binder behind the prints it produced. This is the standard archive practice for the album: each finished print can be traced back to its negative, its development batch, and the visual context of the roll it came from.
