The antique mirror in the guest room has begun to show the room behind me instead of the room in front of me.

At first I thought it was a trick of the light. Then I noticed the furniture was arranged the way it was last winter, before I moved the reading chair. Before she would have known the layout.

<span style="color: var(--crack); text-shadow: 0 0 3px rgba(240,224,208,0.4);" class="crack">The glass has a hairline fracture now that follows the exact path of the vein in my left wrist.</span>

When I stand in front of it long enough, I begin to feel the pull. Not physical. Something older. Like the surface tension of a lake that has already decided you belong beneath it.

Last Tuesday I put my hand against the glass.

My fingers passed through up to the second knuckle before I pulled back. On the other side, her fingers met mine. They were warmer. They were not pulling. They were offering.

She did not try to drag me through. That would have been mercy.

Instead she simply stood there, holding the opening open, letting me feel how easy it would be to finish the crossing myself.

I have started sleeping in the guest room. Not because I want to cross. Because I am afraid of what will happen if I am not there to refuse.

Every morning the fracture is a little longer. Every morning her expression is a little more patient.

I keep telling myself I will have the mirror removed.

I keep telling myself I still want to be the one who decides when the crossing happens.

I am no longer sure which side of the sentence is true.
