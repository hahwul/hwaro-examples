I moved to a new apartment with no mirrors.

I covered the bathroom mirror with a sheet. I removed the mirrored closet doors. I threw away every compact and polished surface. For three weeks I lived in a world without my own face.

Then the hallway mirror appeared.

It was not there when I signed the lease. It is bolted to the wall beside the front door now, exactly where a mirror would be if someone wanted you to check your face one last time before leaving.

I tested it. I left for six hours and returned without turning on any lights. The mirror was empty when I opened the door. The moment the light from the hallway touched it, she was already standing inside, waiting.

She was not wearing the clothes I had left in. She was wearing the dress I keep meaning to donate.

She waved at me with my hand. The wave was small and perfect. Mine are always slightly too eager.

I have begun leaving through the bedroom window to avoid passing her. The neighbors think I am having an affair or a breakdown. Both would be simpler than what is actually happening.

Yesterday I found a note on the kitchen table written in my handwriting, only neater.

"It is easier if you stop fighting the glass. The silvering is almost complete."

The handwriting was better than mine has ever been.

I am starting to forget what my actual face looks like when no one is watching. I only remember the version that remains behind after I walk away.

She never leaves. She is always there, holding the space I used to occupy, improving it one breath at a time.
