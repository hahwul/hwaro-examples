There is one mirror left in the apartment.

It hangs in the narrow hallway between the bedroom and the bathroom. It is small. Unremarkable. The kind of mirror you glance into only to confirm you still exist before you leave the house.

I have not looked directly into it for seventeen days.

Instead I watch it from the corner of my eye when I pass. Sometimes the movement I catch is mine. Sometimes it is not.

  <div class="mirror-surface" style="padding: 2.1rem 2.4rem;">
    The frame is simple wood, painted the color of old bone. The glass itself has begun to yellow at the very edges, like paper left too long in sunlight.
  </div>
</div>

I have decided that when I finally look straight into it, I will not look away.

I will stand there until one of us blinks first.

If it is me, I will accept what that means.

If it is her, I will ask the only question that still matters:

"Was I ever the original?"

She will not answer with words. She will answer by remaining perfectly, beautifully still while I continue to tremble.

I think that is the kindest lie she has left to tell me.

The last mirror is waiting.

I am almost ready to meet it.
