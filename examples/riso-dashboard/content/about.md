Riso Studio is an indie print shop that runs everything in two inks. There is no full-color process here, no four-plate separation, no glossy coating. Just fluorescent pink, federal blue, and uncoated paper.

The trick of the whole place is **overprint**. We lay pink down first, then pull blue over it. Where the two solid inks cross, they multiply into a darker third color we never had to mix. It is not a gradient and it is not a tint &mdash; it is two flat layers that happen to overlap.

<div class="riso-alert">
<strong>Note</strong>
<span>Every dark accent on this dashboard is an overprint, not a separate ink. Two drums, one richer palette.</span>
</div>

## what the studio measures

1. **Impressions** &mdash; how many sheets pass under the drums each week.
2. **Registration** &mdash; how far the second ink lands from the first. A little drift is the look; too much is a fault.
3. **Ink and drum levels** &mdash; tracked as halftone meters so a near-empty drum reads at a glance.

## the press parameters

- **Inks**: Riso Fluorescent Pink and Riso Federal Blue, spot only.
- **Paper**: warm uncoated stock, visible grain left intact.
- **Screen**: an 8px halftone dot for fills and meters.
- **Engine**: the dashboard itself is statically compiled with Hwaro in Crystal, no gradients, no emoji, just solid ink.
