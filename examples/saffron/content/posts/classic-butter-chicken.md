Butter chicken, or murgh makhani, is one of the most beloved dishes to emerge from the tandoors of Old Delhi. The secret lies not in complexity but in patience: allowing the yogurt-marinated chicken to absorb the warmth of Kashmiri chili and garam masala overnight, then cooking it slowly in a sauce built from ripe tomatoes, butter, and cream. The fenugreek leaves, stirred in at the very end, give the dish its distinctive earthy sweetness that no restaurant imitation can quite replicate.

Begin by marinating the chicken in a mixture of yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, Kashmiri chili powder, and a squeeze of lemon. The marinade should coat every piece thoroughly, and ideally the chicken rests in the refrigerator for at least four hours, though overnight is best. For the sauce, char whole tomatoes over an open flame or under a broiler until their skins blister, then blend them smooth. This step adds a subtle smokiness that elevates the entire dish.

Cook the tomato puree with whole spices -- a cinnamon stick, green cardamom pods, and a bay leaf -- until the oil separates and the raw smell disappears. Add the marinated chicken pieces and let them simmer gently until cooked through. Finish with cold butter stirred in off the heat, a pour of cream, and a generous handful of crushed dried fenugreek leaves. Serve over steamed basmati rice or with warm, freshly made naan.
