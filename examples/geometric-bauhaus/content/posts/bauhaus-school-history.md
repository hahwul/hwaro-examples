Founded in Weimar in 1919 by architect Walter Gropius, the **Bauhaus** school of design represented a revolutionary attempt to unify fine art, craft, and technology. The school's ultimate goal was to integrate all artistic disciplines into a single cohesive architectural framework.

How does Gropius' vision translate to modern static web systems?

## form follows function in code

The famous modernist maxim, *form follows function*, is highly applicable to programming and software architecture.

In static site development, we translate this philosophy by ensuring that our layout structure matches our content requirements perfectly:

1. **Semantic HTML**: Using structural HTML tags (`<article>`, `<header>`, `<main>`) to define layout meaning.
2. **Minimal Styling**: Refusing to load general-purpose styling scripts that add visual clutter and overhead.
3. **Speed as Form**: In static site generation, speed is the ultimate form of user experience. Instantaneous compilation and loading align perfectly with Bauhaus functionalism.

## modularity and craftsmanship

At Bauhaus, students worked in physical workshops (wood, metal, weaving) to master their materials. As digital developers, our materials are source code, stylesheet sheets, and markup files.

By refining our static templates, keeping our CSS lightweight, and optimizing our compilation cycles, we honor the modernist heritage of pure craftsmanship.
