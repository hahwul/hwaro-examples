Mireille Kessler's poem is the newest broadside off Hazel, pulled in May, and the first Carmine Press has set entirely in italic, top to bottom, because the poem is a set of instructions and instructions, Eleanor decided, are always spoken a little sideways.

<!-- more -->

> Leave the porch light on past when you mean to.
> Leave it on through the part where you decide
> they are not coming, and the part after that,
> where you decide it again, differently.
> A light left on is not a question.
> It is closer to a fact you are willing to keep being true.
>
> In the morning, turn it off without ceremony.
> This is also an instruction.

## Notes from the chase

Forty copies, the smallest run of the year, set entirely in 16pt Alder Italic on Bellhaven Mill's cotton stock. Eleanor printed the carmine pass first — a single rule beneath the final line, standing in for the porch light — then the black. The edition went up for order the day it came off the press and is Carmine Press's most recent broadside.
