T. Ferris Lund inherited a barn along with a farmhouse in 2023 and spent a winter cataloguing everything left inside it before deciding what to keep. The poem is close to a literal list.

<!-- more -->

> A hay rake, rusted into a single gesture.
> Six mason jars of nails, sorted by a hand
> I never met and can only guess at.
> A saddle no horse alive remembers.
> My father's name, written once in pencil
> on a rafter, in a hand younger than mine is now.
>
> I kept the rafter. The rest went to auction,
> which is its own kind of keeping,
> if you squint.

## Notes from the chase

The title was pulled in 24pt Hollow Antique and the body set entirely in Alder Italic, the shop's only italic face and one Eleanor rarely uses for more than a line at a time — this poem asked for the whole thing slanted. Fifty copies were printed on Corvid Paper Co.'s laid stock in January, timed to the poet's request that it arrive before the anniversary of his father's death, which it did, by four days.
