## About This Workshop

Timber is a woodworking journal dedicated to the patient craft of building things by hand. It began as a set of notes kept in a sawdust-covered notebook on the edge of a workbench and grew into something worth sharing. Every project documented here has been built, every technique tested, every mistake made firsthand.

The philosophy is straightforward: good woodworking takes time, attention, and a willingness to let the material guide the work. We do not rush to finish. We do not cut corners when a joint deserves care. The goal is never just a finished piece --- it is the understanding gained in making it.

## What You Will Find Here

These pages cover a range of topics for woodworkers at every level. You will find detailed project walkthroughs with step-by-step breakdowns, discussions of hand tools and power tools and when each serves best, explorations of wood species and their characteristics, and occasional essays on what this craft teaches us about patience and intention.

Whether you are building your first cutting board or restoring a century-old workbench, the principles remain the same: measure carefully, cut deliberately, and never underestimate the importance of a sharp blade.

> "The wood teaches the woodworker. You can fight the grain or you can follow it. The ones who last are the ones who learn to listen."

## A Note on the Craft

Woodworking is one of the oldest human skills. Long before screens and keyboards, people shaped their world with chisels and saws. There is something grounding about returning to that work --- about feeling the resistance of oak under a hand plane, about watching a dovetail joint close tight for the first time. This journal exists to honor that tradition and to pass along what has been learned at the bench.
