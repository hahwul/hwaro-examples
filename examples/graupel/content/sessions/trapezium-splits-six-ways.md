A graupel shower rattled through at half eight and took every scrap of moisture with it. By nine the air was so still that Rigel sat at 264× like a drafting-compass point, diffraction rings and all. Nights like this arrive maybe twice a winter, and the rule of the ledger is: when the seeing is a five, you drop the plan and go to the Trapezium.

<!-- more -->

The scope had been cooling in the shed since dinner, which mattered tonight more than most. At 119× the four bright components of the Trapezium were cleanly split before my eye had even settled. In went the 4.5 mm ortho — 264×, the eyepiece of last resort — and there was **E**, a firm pinprick between A and B, holding steady rather than winking. F took longer. It hides in the glare of C, and the trick that worked was moving Theta-1 just out of the field and letting F sit in the darker rim. Ten minutes of that and I could hold it with direct vision for seconds at a time. Six stars in the box. First time from this garden.

The nebula itself was almost a distraction, which is an absurd thing to write about M42. With the narrowband filter at 50× the wings swept wider than the field, and the dark bay of the Fish's Mouth cut in with a hard, drawn edge. The green cast was obvious; on the best moments I convinced myself of a brick-red fringe on the western wing, which I will not swear to in daylight.

Finished on Sigma Orionis because it was there and the air was too good to waste: a tidy quadruple, the two faint companions strung east like ducklings. The raw ledger line, for the record:

```text
24 Jan 2025  21:10-01:40  T -6C  S5/T3  M42, Trap E+F @264x, sigma Ori
             10" f/4.7 + 4.5mm ortho; graupel 20:30, cleared hard after
```

Frost on the tube an inch thick by the end. Worth every degree.
