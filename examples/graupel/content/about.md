Graupel is the observing log of Marta Ilves, kept from a back garden on the edge of Harrow Fen, where the streetlights give out three roads before the farmland does. The sky here rates Bortle 4½ on a good winter night — the Milky Way is real but shy, and M33 is a maybe. It is not a dark-sky site. It is a *convenient* site, which in practice beats a dark one you never drive to.

## The instrument

The telescope is a 254 mm f/4.7 dobsonian, bought secondhand with a cracked base and rebuilt over one autumn on the kitchen table. It has no motors, no encoders, and no opinions. Finding things is done the old way: a red-dot finder, a 9×50 straight-through, and a stack of photocopied charts held down with a mug. The eyepiece case holds four: 24 mm (50×), 10 mm (119×), 6 mm (198×), and a 4.5 mm ortho (264×) that only comes out when the air deserves it. A narrowband nebula filter does more work per gram than anything else in the kit.

## Why “graupel”

Graupel is soft hail — rimed snow pellets that fall in short, violent showers from convective winter sky. Where there is graupel there is unstable air, and unstable air clears hard and sudden: twenty minutes after a shower rattles the shed roof, the sky can be the most transparent it will be all month. The first entry in this log was written on exactly that kind of night, so the name stuck.

## The seeing scale

Every session is filed with a seeing grade from 1 to 5, and the star glyph that marks each entry on the ledger's spine encodes it: **the number of points is the seeing plus three**. A four-pointed star is a night of boiling air; an eight-pointed one is the rare night the atmosphere forgets to exist.

| Grade | Star | At the eyepiece |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| 1 | 4 points | Boiling. Stars are woolly blobs; nothing above 80× survives. |
| 2 | 5 points | Poor. Moments of focus between long rolling blurs. |
| 3 | 6 points | Fair. Steady at medium power, soft above 180×. |
| 4 | 7 points | Good. Diffraction rings visible in glimpses, 264× usable. |
| 5 | 8 points | Rock steady. The telescope, not the sky, is the limit. |

## The ledger format

Notes are jotted at the eyepiece in pencil on a clipboard, one line per hour, then typed up the next morning. The raw line format has not changed since the first winter:

```text
DD MMM YYYY  HH:MM-HH:MM  T <air>  S<seeing>/T<transparency>  targets @power
             instrument + eyepiece; weather remarks, failures, resolutions
```

Anything that survives the morning read-through becomes a session entry here. Anything that does not — the misidentified field, the hour lost to a dew-fogged secondary — usually becomes a footnote, because the failures are half the point of keeping a log at all.

Every new entry lands in [the session ledger](/sessions/) and its RSS feed, linked in the footer. Clear skies, and dress warmer than you think you need to.
