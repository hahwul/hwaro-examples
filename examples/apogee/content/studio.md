Apogee is three people. We are based in Toulouse, a city that has been building aircraft and satellites for longer than most design studios have existed, and we picked the neighborhood on purpose — half our clients are a twenty-minute tram ride away, which means we sit in on engineering reviews instead of dialing into them.

We started the studio in 2019 after each independently deciding that generalist branding work was a poor fit for hardware companies. A launch provider does not need a mood board; it needs a documentation system that survives a supplier audit. A climate-data startup does not need a gradient; it needs a chart type its own scientists trust. Narrowing the practice to two adjacent industries let us get genuinely fast at the parts of the work that are actually hard: information density, technical accuracy, and systems that hold up under people who read specifications for a living.

We keep the studio small on purpose. No account managers sit between you and the person doing the work, and no project is quietly handed to a subcontractor once the contract is signed. See recent [work](/work/) or read how a [project moves through the studio](/process/).
