Apogee works exclusively with companies that build hardware meant to leave the atmosphere, and companies racing to keep it habitable. Satellite operators, launch providers, grid-battery manufacturers, direct-air-capture startups — the brief is different every time, but the standard is not. We treat a brand guideline the way our clients treat an interface control document: versioned, testable, and useless if nobody on the engineering team can find it at three in the morning.

Most of what we ship is unglamorous by design. Type specimens instead of mood boards. Grid systems instead of texture. A single accent color, used sparingly, because a control room does not need six of them competing for attention. We write documentation because founders under-invest in it, and we sit in on engineering reviews because a brand system nobody understands at the component level is not a system — it is a PDF.

We are small on purpose. Three designers, no account managers, no junior work quietly outsourced overnight. If we take on a project, one of the three of us designed every screen, mark, and page you will see. Read more about how we [work](/process/), or meet the [studio](/studio/) itself.
