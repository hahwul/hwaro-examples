We take on a narrow set of engagements, on purpose. Every service below has shipped inside an active engineering organization — none of it is theoretical, and none of it comes with a slide deck full of case studies from other industries. If your team is not building hardware or working on the climate, we are probably not the right studio.

1. **Identity systems.** Marks, type, and color built to survive a paint job, a rocket fairing, and a hundred vendor decks before anyone at your company sees them again. We deliver a living guideline, not a PDF that goes stale the week it ships.
2. **Interfaces.** Mission-control consoles, telemetry dashboards, and field tools designed for people under load. We design for the 3 a.m. shift as carefully as the investor demo, because the same interface has to serve both.
3. **Technical documentation.** Interface control documents, integration handbooks, and onboarding guides written by people who read the spec first and the style guide second. Good documentation is a design deliverable, not an afterthought handed to whoever is free.
4. **Pitch & investor materials.** Decks, one-pagers, and data rooms built from the same visual system as your product, so a Series B partner and a new engineer see the same company.
5. **Environmental & wayfinding graphics.** Signage, cabin markings, and facility graphics for hangars, launch sites, and data-center floors — physical work held to the same grid as the digital system.

Every engagement starts with a two-week audit before any of the above begins. We do not estimate a system we have not seen the inside of — see the full [process](/process/) for how that audit fits into the rest of a project, or browse [past work](/work/) for examples of each service.
