Mizzle is a practice journal, nothing more. Each week I sit down at the same low table with the same teacher, make a bowl of tea badly, and write down what happened. A temae I worked through, a utensil that behaved differently in my hands than in hers, and one small thing I understood that I had not understood the week before.

I keep the seasonal notes in the margin because that is where they belong. A tea room answers to the calendar more than to the clock, and the sweets on the plate tell you the month more honestly than any wall does. Read down the rule and you can watch a beginner's year turn, from [the first fumbled fold of the fukusa](@/sessions/first-fold.md) to [the first time the kettle sounded](@/sessions/first-kettle.md), to me, like wind in pines.
