The cherry outside the stationer's had opened over the week, and Sensei had set a branch of it in the alcove. Today, finally, tea. Thin tea, *usucha*, whisked to a froth, the first drink I would make that someone actually meant to swallow.

<!-- more -->

## The temae

The choreography of *hira-date* is the tray temae grown up: the same purifications, but now standing water, a real kettle, and a guest — the student to my left — who would judge my bowl. Sensei taught me to warm the chawan with the first ladle of hot water, whisk it empty to heat the bamboo, then pour that water away before the tea ever arrives. I had thought this was hygiene. It is thermal: a cold bowl steals the heat the tea needs to bloom, and thin tea made in a cold bowl lies flat and grey.

## Utensils

- **Chawan** — a wide, shallow spring bowl, chosen to let the tea cool quickly as the days warm.
- **Chasen** — whisked in a light *m*-shaped stroke from the wrist, never the arm.
- **Natsume** — the lacquered caddy for thin tea, lid lifted straight up so nothing spills across the rim.
- **Hishaku** — the bamboo ladle, held so the water falls without a sound.

## One lesson

> A froth is not something you beat into the tea. It is already waiting in the matcha; the whisk only lets it out. I whisked harder and got less. I whisked lighter and the surface turned to jade cream.
