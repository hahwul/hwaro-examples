I arrived twenty minutes early and still felt late. Sensei sat me on the tatami, gave me a square of orange silk, and said we would not touch tea today. We would fold cloth. For an hour I folded and unfolded the fukusa while she watched my thumbs.

<!-- more -->

## The temae

We worked through *ryakubon*, the tray temae, which exists so that beginners have somewhere to put their hands. Everything the full procedure will one day ask for is here in miniature: purify the tea container, purify the scoop, ladle the water, whisk, present the bowl. I did each step half a beat behind Sensei's count, and twice I whisked before I had added the tea, which is a way of making very hot green water.

The point of ryakubon, she said, is not the tea. It is learning that every object has a place it lives and a path it travels, and that the path never crosses itself.

## Utensils

- **Fukusa** — the silk purifying cloth, folded perhaps forty times before it behaved.
- **Chawan** — a plain, warm-brown winter bowl, deep enough to keep the heat.
- **Chasen** — the bamboo whisk, softened first in warm water so the tines would not snap.
- **Chashaku** — the slender tea scoop, carved from a single length of bamboo.

## One lesson

> The fold is not preparation for the tea. The fold is the tea. If I hurry the cloth to get to the good part, there is no good part left to get to.
