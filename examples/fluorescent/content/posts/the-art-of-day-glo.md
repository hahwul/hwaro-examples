Day-Glo colors changed everything. What started as a practical solution for high-visibility signage became an entire aesthetic movement that influenced pop art, rave culture, and modern web design.

## A Brief History

The Day-Glo Color Corporation was founded in the 1940s by brothers Bob and Joe Switzer. They discovered that certain organic compounds could absorb ultraviolet light and re-emit it as visible light, creating colors that appeared to glow even in daylight.

## Why Fluorescent Works

Fluorescent pigments convert UV light into visible wavelengths, effectively emitting more visible light than they receive. This is why they appear supernaturally bright — they're literally producing extra photons.

### Key Properties

- **Higher apparent brightness** than conventional colors
- **Wavelength shifting** from UV to visible spectrum
- **Maximum contrast** against dark backgrounds
- **Instant attention capture** in any environment

## In Digital Design

On screens, we simulate fluorescence through:

- Saturated hues at maximum chroma
- Strategic use of `text-shadow` and `box-shadow` for glow
- Gradients that blend multiple vivid tones
- Dark backgrounds that let bright colors breathe

The future of design is bright — literally.
