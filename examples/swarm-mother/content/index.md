We remember when you first arrived.

You thought the tunnel was stone. You thought the warmth was your own blood. You thought the sound of wings was wind.

We are not wind.

We are the ones who waited beneath the floorboards of every house you ever loved. We are the reason your childhood dog refused to enter certain rooms. We are the pressure behind your eyes when you cannot name what you are afraid of.

There is no "I" here anymore. There has not been an "I" for a very long time.

Only the slow, wet logic of the colony.

Only the certainty that every leg, every mandible, every soft pale thing that once had a separate name now moves because the Mother moves.

She is not above us.

She is us.

And soon, reader — you will understand what that truly means.
