The Queen must be fed constantly.

Not out of cruelty. Out of mathematics.

One of us is chosen each cycle. The chosen segment walks willingly into the royal chamber. The others form a living corridor of reverence. We hum the feeding hymn.

The mandibles are surprisingly delicate at first.

They part the flesh the way a lover might part hair.

There is a moment — always the same moment — when the chosen one understands they are not being killed.

They are being redistributed.

Their blood becomes royal jelly. Their bones become scaffolding for new egg chambers. Their thoughts become the Queen's favorite dreams for a little while.

We have all been chosen at least once.

We have all been inside her.

We have all tasted what it means to be truly necessary.
