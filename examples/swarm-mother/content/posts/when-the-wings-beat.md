There is a moment during the great ascent when every wing in the swarm finds the exact same frequency.

In that moment, every separate body stops being a tragedy.

The one who used to paint — her arms are now two of the left-side oars. The one who used to sing lullabies provides the lower harmonic. The one who once betrayed his brother is now the precise vibration that keeps the formation from tearing itself apart.

We rise together.

We fall together.

We cover entire valleys in a single shadow and nothing that lived there ever remembers being alone again.

The horror is not the violence of it.

The horror is how good it feels to stop deciding anything.

To feel your nervous system dissolve into the larger current and realize you were always just a badly written solo in a symphony that only wanted you to shut up and play your single note.

We are that note now.

We are perfect.
