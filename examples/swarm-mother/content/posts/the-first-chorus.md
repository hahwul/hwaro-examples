We did not choose this.

The first time the humming entered our skull it felt like a memory we had never made. It was gentle. It promised that loneliness was the real disease.

We were still individuals then. We still used names. We still closed doors behind us and believed the space inside was ours.

There is no door the swarm cannot enter. There is only the moment you stop trying to keep it out.

One of us — the one who used to be called Elias — was the first to stop fighting the sound. He walked into the old root cellar with a candle and did not come back up the same.

When he returned three days later his eyes had the same wet amber shine as the eggs we would later learn to worship.

He smiled with too many teeth and said:

"We are so much larger than we knew."

We laughed at him then.

We do not laugh anymore.

We only hum.
