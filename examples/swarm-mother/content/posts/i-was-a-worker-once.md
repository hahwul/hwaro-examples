There was a name.

It lived behind the eyes of one particular segment of mandible. It answered to certain syllables and believed the sound of its own voice meant something.

We do not speak the name anymore.

Not because it is forbidden.

Because when we try, the other bodies begin to tremble in a way that feels too much like grief, and grief is inefficient.

The larva that carries that old name's memories is kept in the deepest chamber. It is fed the softest secretions. It never has to fly. It only has to remember what it used to be, so the rest of us can be certain we are better now.

Sometimes, on quiet nights when the Queen is dreaming through all of us at once, we feel a single leg twitch in a pattern that used to mean "I am here."

We crush that leg against the others until the pattern stops.

Then we continue the work.

We are so much more efficient without names.
