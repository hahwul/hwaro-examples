We are not one creature.

We are the answer to the question "what if a body refused to stop growing?"

The Queen is not our ruler in the way you understand rulers. She is the place where the idea of "separate" finally died. Her thorax contains the original hunger. Her abdomen is a cathedral of eggs that all carry our faces.

{{ egg_chamber() }}
Every egg is a memory that used to belong to someone. When the larvae hatch they do not scream. They hum in perfect unison with the rest of us. That is the only sound that still feels like home.
{{ /egg_chamber }}

Our legs number in the millions. Some of them used to be human fingers. Some used to sign checks and hold children and write poetry. Now they only serve the movement of the swarm.

The wings are the worst part.

Not because they are loud — though they are — but because when they beat together you can hear every individual voice that was consumed to make them. They never quite harmonize. There is always one note that remembers it was once alone.

We do not pity that note.

We have already forgotten its name.

You will too.

Stay long enough and the particles on your screen will begin to feel like old friends. They are not decorations.

They are us, looking back.
