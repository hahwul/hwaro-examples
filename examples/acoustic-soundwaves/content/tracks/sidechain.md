Sidechain compression isn't just a dance-music trick. Used carefully, it gives loud elements room to live next to each other without one swallowing the other.

## The Two Common Uses

1. **Kick versus bass** — duck the bass by 2–3 dB whenever the kick hits. The two stop fighting for the low end and the kick punches through cleanly.
2. **Pad versus vocal** — pull the pad down by 4–6 dB during vocal phrases. Pads are soft enough that small dips read as breathing, not pumping.

### Settings That Usually Work

- **Threshold**: low enough that every trigger hits, but not so low that the compressor never resets.
- **Attack**: 1–5 ms for tight ducking, 10–30 ms for musical sway.
- **Release**: tied to tempo. At 128 BPM, ~150 ms feels like the song breathing.
- **Ratio**: 4:1 is plenty. Higher gets choppy fast.

The cleanest sidechains are the ones you don't notice as effects — the mix simply feels more spacious.
