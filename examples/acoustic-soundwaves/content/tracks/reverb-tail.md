The reverb tail is the part of a sound that lingers after the source has stopped. Its length is the single most powerful cue for how large a space *feels*, more so than predelay or early reflections.

## A Simple Mental Model

- **0.4–0.8s tail** — small room, intimate vocal space.
- **1.2–1.8s tail** — concert hall, generous but still controlled.
- **2.5s+** — cathedral or pad-like ambience; long enough to bloom but easy to overdo.

Tail length interacts with tempo. At 120 BPM a 1.5s tail will smear three beats of overlap, which is fine for a sustained pad but disastrous on a busy drum loop. Always check your tail against the song's grid.

```
predelay  | early refls | tail
~30 ms    |   80 ms     |  1.4 s
```

A short predelay glues the source into the room; a longer one lets the dry signal punch first and then sit inside the space.
