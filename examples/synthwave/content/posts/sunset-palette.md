A synthwave palette only works when each hue carries a specific job. Without that discipline the screen reads as nostalgia rather than interface.

Start from the indigo backdrop. The base canvas sits between hue 245 and 260 at low lightness. This is the night sky against which the foreground hues are measured. Drop the saturation by ten percent on large surfaces to avoid eye strain over a long session.

Magenta is the headline color. Reserve it for titles, primary buttons, and active states. Saturation runs at ninety percent, lightness at sixty, which keeps it bright on dark backgrounds without bleeding into pink. Use it sparingly so the eye registers the signal.

Cyan handles structural hierarchy. Borders, dividers, secondary text accents, and link underlines pull from a single cyan value at seventy percent saturation. Because cyan reads as cooler than magenta, the interface gains a clear depth axis even before any blur is applied.

Yellow appears only in caution states. The hue sits around 50 degrees, with lightness pulled high enough to clear contrast thresholds against the indigo base. Body copy never uses yellow, since the eye fatigues quickly at that wavelength.

Test each combination at three contrast tiers: text on background, text on glass, and text on hover state. Anything below 4.5 contrast for body copy is rejected. Headlines may run lower, but only when paired with weight 700 or above.

Constrained palettes age better than expansive ones.
