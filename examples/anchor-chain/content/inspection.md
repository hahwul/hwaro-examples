# Chain Inspection

A chain is a sequence of links, and the failure of a single link is the failure of the assembly. The inspection routine documented here covers the visual checks performed at each scheduled service.

## Visual Pass

Walk the length of the chain under steady light. Look for surface scoring, flattening of the link profile, and any visible elongation at high-load segments. Mark suspect links with chalk so a closer measurement can be taken without needing to relocate them.

## Measured Pass

Use the gauge specified in the maintenance manual to confirm pitch and inner-link width. Record the values in the chain log. A single out-of-tolerance link is replaced. Multiple out-of-tolerance links in the same segment indicate the chain has reached the end of its service life and should be retired entirely.

## Anchor Connection

The shackle that joins the chain to the anchor receives a separate inspection. Check the pin for deformation, confirm the seizing wire is intact, and verify that the moused split pin is still seated. The connection sees the highest concentrated load in the system and benefits from being treated as its own component.
