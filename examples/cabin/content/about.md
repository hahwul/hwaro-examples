## About This Place

This journal is kept in a small cabin set among old-growth pines at the edge of a mountain ridge. The nearest town lies a winding hour down the valley road, and the nearest neighbor is the red fox who visits the woodpile each evening at dusk.

Life here follows the rhythm of the seasons rather than the clock. Spring brings the meltwater and the first green shoots along the creek. Summer is long days of light, the scent of warm pine needles, and the sound of wind through open windows. Autumn turns the mountainside to copper and gold, filling the cellar with preserved harvest. Winter draws everything inward, to the fireside, to books, to the slow work of hands and the comfort of a well-stocked kitchen.

## Why Write

There is something worth recording in the ordinary passages of a day spent close to the land. The way morning light falls across a wooden table. The particular silence after a heavy snowfall. The satisfaction of a shelf stocked with jars of preserves you put up yourself. These small things, taken together, compose a life of substance and intention.

This journal is an invitation to slow down, to notice what is near at hand, and to find warmth in the fundamental acts of dwelling well.
