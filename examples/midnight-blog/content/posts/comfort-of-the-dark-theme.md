We live in an age of bright screens and constant illumination. The comfort of a dark theme goes beyond mere aesthetics; it's a physiological relief for tired eyes.

When designing for the night, pure black `#000000` against pure white `#FFFFFF` creates a harsh halation effect. The text appears to bleed into the background, causing eye strain and fatigue.

<!-- more -->

### The Soft Contrast Approach

Instead of stark contrasts, a thoughtful dark theme uses soft off-blacks (like our `#1a1a1c`) paired with warm, off-white text (`#e5e5e0`). This subtle reduction in contrast significantly improves readability during long sessions.

> "A good dark theme doesn't turn off the lights; it simply dims them to a comfortable level."

### Typography Matters

In a dark environment, the choice of typeface becomes even more critical. Serifs like Lora provide a structured, classic reading experience that anchors the eye as it scans across the line. Pairing this with a clean sans-serif like Inter for headings creates a clear hierarchy without relying on color or aggressive weight changes.

Line height must be generous. When reading light text on a dark background, the eye needs more vertical space to track comfortably. We use a `1.8` line height here to ensure every word breathes.

The goal is simple: comfort. Nothing should distract from the words themselves.
