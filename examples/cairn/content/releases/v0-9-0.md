Slate is the foundation stone — the first release Cairn shipped to anyone besides
its author. It boots on a handful of common split boards, drives both halves over
a wired TRRS link, and gets the fundamentals right before anything clever is
built on top.

<!-- more -->

## Added

- **Split keyboard support** over a wired serial link. One half is the central
  controller, the other a peripheral; the central half owns USB and merges the
  peripheral matrix into a single logical grid.
- **Home-row mods** as a built-in behavior. Hold a home-row key for a modifier,
  tap it for the letter, with a per-key tapping term.

  ```lua
  layer("base", function(k)
    k.home_row_mods({
      a = mod.GUI, s = mod.ALT, d = mod.CTRL, f = mod.SHIFT,
      j = mod.SHIFT, kk = mod.CTRL, l = mod.ALT,
    })
  end)
  ```

- **Tunable debouncer.** Choose the algorithm and window in `board.toml` instead
  of recompiling.

  ```toml
  [debounce]
  algorithm = "sym_defer"   # sym_eager | sym_defer
  window_ms = 5
  ```

- **Momentary and toggle layers** (`layer.hold`, `layer.toggle`).
- A minimal **`cairn flash`** wrapping the common bootloaders.

## Changed

- The matrix scan rate is configurable; the default moved from 500 Hz to 1 kHz
  after latency testing on a 5×12 split.

## Fixed

- Corrected column ordering on boards wired `COL2ROW` so the right half no longer
  mirrored its outer column.

## Upgrade notes

There is nothing to upgrade from — this is the first tagged release. Boards and
pin maps live in `board.toml`; start from the sample for your controller and
adjust the matrix size. Wireless, encoders, and the compiler all come later; Slate
is deliberately small.
