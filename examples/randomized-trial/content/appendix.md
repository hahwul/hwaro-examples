## Data Availability

Individual patient data from the VELOCITY trial will be made available to qualified researchers upon reasonable request, subject to a data sharing agreement, after the primary manuscript and all pre-specified secondary analyses have been published. Requests should be directed to the corresponding author. De-identified data will be provided through a secure data-sharing platform with controlled access. Summary-level efficacy and safety data are deposited in the YODA Project repository and are freely available for download. The ClinicalTrials.gov record (NCT04829174) includes the full results summary.

## Protocol Access

The full trial protocol, statistical analysis plan, and informed consent form are available as supplementary materials at the journal website. The protocol is also registered and available through the EU Clinical Trials Register (EudraCT 2022-004821-15).

## Code Availability

Statistical analysis scripts for the primary and secondary endpoint analyses, including the CONSORT flow diagram generation code and the subgroup analysis forest plot scripts, are available at the following repository: [https://github.com/velocity-trial/velocity-phase3](https://github.com/velocity-trial/velocity-phase3). The repository includes SAS programs (version 9.4), R scripts (version 4.3.2), and the randomisation program used to generate the allocation sequence.

## Ethics Statement

This study was approved by the institutional review board or independent ethics committee at each of the 68 participating sites. All participants provided written informed consent before any study-related procedures were performed. The study was conducted in accordance with the Declaration of Helsinki (2013 revision), the International Council for Harmonisation Good Clinical Practice guidelines (ICH E6-R2), and all applicable national and local regulations. An independent data safety monitoring board oversaw the trial and reviewed unblinded safety data at pre-specified intervals.
