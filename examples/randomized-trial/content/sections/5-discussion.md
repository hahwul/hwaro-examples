## 5.1 Principal findings

The VELOCITY Phase III trial demonstrated that VEL-401 200 mg administered subcutaneously every two weeks achieved statistically significant and clinically meaningful superiority over methotrexate across all primary and key secondary efficacy endpoints in patients with moderate-to-severe rheumatoid arthritis who had an inadequate response to csDMARDs. The primary endpoint of ACR50 at week 24 was met with a 22.5 percentage-point absolute difference (51.2 pct vs 28.7 pct; P less than 0.001), corresponding to a number needed to treat of 4.4. These results were robust across multiple sensitivity analyses and consistent across all pre-specified patient subgroups.

The magnitude of the treatment effect observed with VEL-401 compares favourably with results from pivotal trials of other approved IL-6 pathway inhibitors. The AMBITION trial of tocilizumab monotherapy demonstrated an ACR50 response rate of 44 pct versus 34 pct for methotrexate at week 24. The MONARCH trial of sarilumab versus adalimumab showed an ACR50 rate of 29 pct versus 18 pct at week 24. The larger treatment effect observed with VEL-401 may reflect its extended half-life and sustained receptor occupancy between doses, though cross-trial comparisons must be interpreted with caution given differences in patient populations, disease severity at baseline, and comparator regimens.

## 5.2 Safety profile

The safety profile of VEL-401 was consistent with the known class effects of IL-6 receptor inhibitors. Injection site reactions occurred more frequently with VEL-401 (8.9 pct vs 0.2 pct), as expected for a subcutaneous biologic. The rates of serious adverse events (6.4 pct vs 5.8 pct) and serious infections (1.9 pct vs 1.7 pct) were comparable between groups. Nausea and hepatic transaminase elevations were more frequent in the MTX arm, reflecting the known gastrointestinal and hepatic toxicity profile of methotrexate.

Neutropenia of grade 3 or 4 severity occurred in 2.4 pct of patients receiving VEL-401 compared with 1.0 pct of those receiving MTX. All episodes resolved with temporary dose interruption. Hyperlipidaemia was observed more frequently with VEL-401 (6.0 pct vs 2.9 pct), consistent with the known effect of IL-6 inhibition on lipid metabolism. None of the lipid elevations were associated with cardiovascular events during the 24-week treatment period. Long-term cardiovascular safety will be assessed in the ongoing open-label extension study.

## 5.3 Radiographic outcomes

VEL-401 significantly reduced radiographic progression as measured by the change from baseline in the modified total Sharp score (0.42 vs 1.28; P less than 0.001). The proportion of patients with no radiographic progression (change of 0.5 or less) was higher in the VEL-401 group (72.4 pct vs 54.2 pct; P less than 0.001). These findings suggest that VEL-401 provides structural benefit beyond symptom control, consistent with the established role of IL-6 in osteoclast-mediated bone erosion and cartilage degradation.

## 5.4 Limitations

Several limitations should be acknowledged. First, the 24-week treatment period, while adequate for demonstrating efficacy, does not capture the long-term safety and durability of response. The ongoing open-label extension study (VELOCITY-OLE, NCT04829181) will provide data through 104 weeks. Second, the active comparator was methotrexate rather than another biologic DMARD, which limits direct comparison with existing biologic therapies. A head-to-head trial against tocilizumab (VELOCITY-HEAD, NCT04829198) is currently in planning. Third, the study excluded patients with significant comorbidities (decompensated heart failure, uncontrolled diabetes, active malignancy), and the results may not generalise to all patients seen in clinical practice. Fourth, while the trial enrolled patients from diverse geographic regions, the representation of some populations, particularly from sub-Saharan Africa, was relatively limited.

## 5.5 Clinical implications

The results of the VELOCITY trial position VEL-401 as a potential new treatment option for moderate-to-severe RA. The biweekly subcutaneous administration schedule offers a practical advantage over agents requiring more frequent dosing. The consistent efficacy across subgroups, including patients with prior biologic exposure, suggests broad applicability in clinical practice. Based on these results, a regulatory submission to the US Food and Drug Administration and the European Medicines Agency is anticipated in the third quarter of 2026.

## 5.6 Conclusion

VEL-401 200 mg subcutaneously every two weeks demonstrated statistically significant and clinically meaningful superiority over methotrexate in patients with moderate-to-severe rheumatoid arthritis inadequately controlled by csDMARDs. The safety profile was consistent with the class of IL-6 receptor inhibitors. These findings support the continued development of VEL-401 for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis.
