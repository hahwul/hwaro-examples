<section class="abstract-box">
  <h2>Abstract</h2>
  <dl>
    <dt>Objective</dt>
    <dd>To evaluate the efficacy and safety of VEL-401, a novel anti-IL-6 receptor monoclonal antibody, compared with methotrexate (MTX) in patients with moderate-to-severe rheumatoid arthritis (RA) who had an inadequate response to conventional synthetic disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (csDMARDs).</dd>
    <dt>Methods</dt>
    <dd>This multicentre, double-blind, active-controlled, parallel-group Phase III trial randomised 842 adults (1:1) with moderate-to-severe RA (DAS28-CRP above 3.2) to receive either subcutaneous VEL-401 200 mg every two weeks or oral MTX 15 mg weekly, titrated to 25 mg by week 8. The primary endpoint was the proportion of patients achieving ACR50 response at week 24. Secondary endpoints included ACR20, ACR70, change in DAS28-CRP, HAQ-DI improvement, and radiographic progression (modified Sharp score). Randomisation was stratified by baseline DAS28-CRP (3.2-5.1 vs above 5.1) and prior biologic use (yes vs no).</dd>
    <dt>Results</dt>
    <dd>At week 24, ACR50 was achieved by 51.2 pct of patients in the VEL-401 group versus 28.7 pct in the MTX group (absolute difference 22.5 pct; 95 pct CI 16.1 to 28.9; P &lt; 0.001). ACR20 response rates were 71.8 pct versus 49.3 pct (P &lt; 0.001) and ACR70 rates were 26.4 pct versus 11.9 pct (P &lt; 0.001), both favouring VEL-401. Mean change in DAS28-CRP was -2.89 versus -1.74 (P &lt; 0.001). The incidence of serious adverse events was 6.4 pct in the VEL-401 group and 5.8 pct in the MTX group. Rates of serious infection were comparable (1.9 pct vs 1.7 pct).</dd>
    <dt>Conclusion</dt>
    <dd>VEL-401 demonstrated statistically significant and clinically meaningful superiority over MTX across all primary and key secondary efficacy endpoints at 24 weeks, with an acceptable safety profile. These results support VEL-401 as a treatment option for moderate-to-severe RA in csDMARD-inadequate responders.</dd>
    <dt>Keywords</dt>
    <dd><em>randomized controlled trial; rheumatoid arthritis; monoclonal antibody; anti-IL-6 receptor; ACR50; CONSORT; phase III; VEL-401</em></dd>
  </dl>
</section>
