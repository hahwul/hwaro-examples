Fog arrived before dawn and stayed through midmorning, reducing the visible world to perhaps two hundred metres. The sea stacks that normally anchor the southern view became faint suggestions, then vanished entirely. Sound carried strangely --- the surf seemed closer and more diffuse at the same time.

These conditions eliminate colour almost completely. The photographs are functionally monochrome: warm greys, cool greys, the faintest blue where the ocean surface catches some remnant of sky. The sand darkens near the waterline and lightens inland, providing the only tonal gradient in an otherwise flat field.

What fog gives in exchange for visibility is atmosphere. Figures on the beach --- a distant walker, a dog at the surf line --- become silhouettes without sharp edges. The boundary between sand and water softens. Driftwood logs emerge from the mist as dark forms, their shapes simplified to essential geometry.

The exposure challenge is real. Metering wants to render everything as middle grey, which loses the luminous quality of the fog itself. Slight overexposure preserved the brightness while keeping enough shadow detail in the foreground elements to maintain depth.

Eight frames from this morning. Each one quieter than the last.
