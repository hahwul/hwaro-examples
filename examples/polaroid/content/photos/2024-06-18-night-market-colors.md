The night market is sensory overload in the best possible way. Red lanterns casting warm pools of light. The sizzle of something delicious on a grill. Vendors calling out in three different languages. Music from somewhere you can't quite locate.

I ate my way through four blocks and photographed everything in between bites.

## Highlights

- **Best food:** Grilled skewers from the third stall on the left. The sauce is a secret recipe.
- **Best photo spot:** Under the lantern tunnel near the entrance
- **Biggest surprise:** A street artist doing live calligraphy on rice paper
- **Biggest regret:** Not bringing a bigger memory card

## The Art of Night Photography

Shooting in markets is tricky. The light is mixed - warm tungsten bulbs, cool fluorescent tubes, colored neons. But that chaos is also what makes the photos interesting. I shoot in manual, slightly overexpose, and let the colors do their thing.

Every visit is different. Every frame is unrepeatable. That's the magic of street photography.
