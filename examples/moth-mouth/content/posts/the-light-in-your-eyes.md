I brought someone home.

He had the kind of eyes that catch lamplight and hold it like a secret. When he laughed the sound was warm and ordinary and I felt something in my chest loosen for the first time in weeks.

They did not come while he was here. I thought perhaps they had moved on.

After he left I stood in the dark for a long time, listening. The room was so quiet I could hear the small sounds my own body makes when it believes it is alone.

Then the lamp flickered once, as if something had passed in front of it from the inside.

On the pillow where his head had rested there was a perfect ring of fine gray dust, no larger than a wedding band. In the center of the ring, one single scale, iridescent in the way oil on water is iridescent — beautiful only because it is dying.

I have not turned the lights on since.

The light in your eyes was the last good meal.

I am trying to remember what my own eyes looked like before I started keeping them closed.
