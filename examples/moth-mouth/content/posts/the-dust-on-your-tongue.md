I woke with my tongue coated in something that was not sleep. It tasted of old paper and something sweeter, almost floral, but wrong in the way funeral flowers are wrong.

When I brushed my teeth the water ran slightly iridescent. I told myself it was the new toothpaste. I have always been good at telling myself useful lies.

{{ scale_dust() }} Later, while speaking, I felt something shift against the roof of my mouth — not pain, not a sore, but a *settling*. Like a small weight deciding it belonged there.

In the mirror the back of my throat looked darker than usual. Not bruised. Dusted. As if someone had taken a moth by the wings and shaken it gently over my open mouth while I dreamed.

I have begun keeping my lips pressed together even when alone. It does not help. They are already learning the shape of the inside of me the way cartographers once learned coastlines — by walking them slowly, again and again.

The dust on your tongue is not yours.

It is only visiting. For now.
