There is a theory I have been testing on myself.

I placed three lamps in the room: one bare and ugly, one frosted and modest, and one with a hand-painted porcelain shade depicting a woman whose eyes had been worn away by time and careless handling.

They ignored the first two completely.

{{ open_mouth() }} The third they circled for two hours without landing. When they finally touched down it was not on the bulb. It was on the painted mouth of the woman, where the glaze had begun to crack.

I have since noticed the pattern in everything they approach: a chipped teacup with a single rose still visible, a letter written in a hand so beautiful it hurts to read the ordinary words, the small mirror I keep beside the bed whose silver has foxed into a map of some other country.

They are not drawn to light.

They are drawn to the places where light once was, and where something lovely is now decaying.

I have stopped wearing the necklace with the tiny enamel moth. It was the first gift he ever gave me. Last night I found one of them resting inside the empty locket, wings moving in a rhythm too slow to be flight, too deliberate to be anything but reading.

They only eat beautiful things.

I am trying very hard not to be beautiful anymore.
