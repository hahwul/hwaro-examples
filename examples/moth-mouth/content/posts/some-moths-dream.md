The last one is different.

It does not eat the light. It watches me while I watch the light. Sometimes, when I have been still long enough, it lands on the back of my hand and does not move for hours.

Its wings are the color of the inside of my wrist where the veins show through.

Last night I woke to the feeling of something breathing against my lips. Not air. Something slower and older, like the way paper exhales when you open a book that has not been touched in years.

When I opened my eyes it was resting on my mouth, wings spread wide enough to cover both my cheeks. For one long moment its body was the exact shape of my own mouth, open.

I did not move. I let it read me.

In the morning there was a single sentence written in the condensation on the inside of the window glass, in a hand that was almost, but not quite, my own:

"We are almost ready."

I have begun to wonder which of us will be the first to step through the other.

Some moths dream of becoming human.

I think I am starting to dream of becoming light.
