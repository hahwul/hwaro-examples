The lamp was still warm when I noticed the first one. Not on the glass, but *inside* the reflection — a soft interruption between my face and the bulb.

I had left the window cracked because the night smelled of cut grass and distant rain. I did not know that some hungers travel on scent the way we travel on roads.

{{ moth_wing() }} It did not fly in like an insect. It *arrived* like a thought you did not finish having.

By morning the second one had learned to spell the first syllable of my name with the dust it left on the sill. By the third night they had the rest.

I have stopped turning the lamp off. They seem disappointed when the light dies. I keep it burning now, low and yellow, the way one keeps vigil for a sick relative who may still be listening.

They rest on the shade with their wings half-open, as if tasting the heat. I have begun to wonder whether the light is what they came for, or whether the light is only how they found the thing they truly wanted.

The thing that answers when they say my name.
