The wings were not mine.

I felt them first as pressure, like two dry pages pressed between my molars. When I opened my mouth in the dark the sound was the sound of something delicate tearing very slowly.

I did not scream. I have learned that sound travels differently when they are near.

{{ moth_wing() }} By the time I reached the bathroom the wings had already begun to curl at the edges from the dryness of the air. They were the exact color of the inside of my eyelids when I press my fingers against them too hard — that bruised, private pink.

I did not pull them out. I stood very still and let them dry against my tongue until they felt like two pressed flowers I had once kept in a book of poems I no longer read.

In the morning they were gone. In their place was a faint taste of something like powdered sugar and regret.

I have started keeping my mouth slightly open while I sleep. Not because I want them to return.

Because I am afraid of what will happen if they try to come back and find the door closed.

Some doors, once opened, grow teeth of their own.
