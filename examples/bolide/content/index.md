## What Bolide is

Bolide is a three-day demoscene party held in a cold sports hall in Vantaa,
just outside Helsinki. Bring a machine, a monitor, and something you made in
too few bytes. We run one big screen, one PA that is louder than it should be,
and a compo schedule that respects nobody's sleep.

The idea is old and simple: people write tiny programs that draw impossible
things, then we watch them together on a projector at 2 a.m. and vote. A
**4K intro** is a self-contained executable no bigger than a small email. A
**256-byte intro** is smaller than this paragraph. Tracker music is composed
one sample at a time. And the live shader battle puts two coders on stage with
a blank fragment shader and forty minutes.

## Who shows up

Sceners, students, chiptune nerds, and graphics programmers who spend their
day jobs on spreadsheets and their weekends on sine scrollers. First-timers
are welcome and encouraged to enter something rough. A wild entry that crashes
on the second frame is worth more to us than a no-show.

## The deal

Entry to the hall is free. A bring-your-own-computer seat with a table, power,
and wired network is a small fee that pays for the electricity and the coffee.
Deadlines are real and enforced by an organiser with a stopwatch and no mercy.
Read the [compo rules](/compos/), figure out [how to get here](/venue/), then
[register your seat](/register/) before the good tables are gone.
