A single frame. No animation, no sound, no bundled textures. You get 4096
bytes of code that must paint one still image, and that image goes on the wall.
The winning entry gets printed at A2 and taped up in the hall for the rest of
the weekend.

## Rules

- **4096 bytes** of source or executable, your choice of pipeline as long as
  it runs on the compo PC.
- No raster input. Every pixel comes from your math. Noise functions are
  allowed; loading a `.png` of clouds is not.
- Fixed output resolution 1920x1080. We screenshot the compo machine; you do
  not get to hand us a pre-rendered file.

## Technique

Executable graphics lives and dies on material and light. A raymarched scene
with one interesting surface, real shadows, and a considered palette will beat
a busy fractal every time. Spend your bytes on the look, not the geometry.

A tiny signed-distance scene fits in surprisingly little code:

```glsl
float map(vec3 p){
  float d = length(p) - 1.0;          // a sphere
  d = min(d, p.y + 1.0);              // a floor
  return d - 0.04*sin(8.0*p.x)*sin(8.0*p.z);
}
```

From there it is all lighting: one sun, soft occlusion, a horizon that is not
pure black. Grade the final color like a photographer. The judges are looking
at a print, so contrast and composition carry more weight than raw shader
cleverness.
