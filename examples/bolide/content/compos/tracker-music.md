Music the old way: patterns of rows, each row a note or a command, samples you
chopped yourself. Bring a module file and headphones. The compo plays over the
big PA at an hour when the hall is dark and everyone is finally listening.

<!-- more -->

## Rules

- **Original composition.** Your notes, your arrangement. Samples may be from
  royalty-free libraries, but the song must be yours.
- Delivered as a **module** (`.xm`, `.it`, `.mod`, `.s3m`) up to 8 MB, playable
  in OpenMPT. No rendered `.mp3` or `.wav` masters; this is a tracker compo.
- Two to five minutes. Fade or end cleanly. A song that just stops mid-pattern
  reads as unfinished to the voters.

## Getting it to land on a big system

The hall PA has real low end, which is a trap. A kick that thumps in your
earbuds can turn to mud on a subwoofer. Reference your mix on something with
bass before Saturday, and leave headroom: master to about -1 dBFS peak so the
compo playback does not clip.

Structure matters more than sound design. Give the piece an intro, a lift, and
a payoff. Sceners have heard ten thousand loops that never go anywhere; a
32-bar idea that actually develops will out-vote a gorgeous eight-bar loop on
repeat. Label your patterns so the compo team can cue you without guessing, and
name the module with your handle and the track title so it does not vanish into
a folder of `untitled-final-2.it`.
