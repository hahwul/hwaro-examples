Bolide runs at **Kuusela sports hall** in Vantaa, a suburb north of Helsinki.
It is a real gym: a wooden floor, high ceilings, and lighting we will turn off
the moment the compos start. Doors open Friday 3 April at 18:00 and the hall
stays open around the clock until teardown on Sunday afternoon.

## Getting there

- **From Helsinki Airport (HEL):** fifteen minutes by local bus. The airport
  is closer to the hall than the city center is, which surprises people every
  year.
- **From Helsinki Central:** commuter train to Tikkurila, then one bus. Around
  forty minutes door to door. A transit day pass covers the whole trip.
- **By car:** free parking behind the hall. The entrance for gear is the loading
  door on the north side; do not carry a full rig through the front.

Exact stop names and a live map link go out in the party channel the week
before. If you are flying in, land Friday afternoon so you are not setting up a
machine at three in the morning.

## The BYOC area

A bring-your-own-computer seat gets you a table section, a power socket on a
metered strip, and a wired gigabit port. Bring your own extension lead and a
power strip, because there are never enough sockets and the ones by the wall go
first. Wi-Fi exists but it is for phones, not for pulling a demo off a server
five minutes before the deadline.

Forty-two seats, allocated in the order people [register](/register/). When
they are gone they are gone, but the floor is open and plenty of people come
with just a laptop and a sleeping bag.

## Sleeping, eating, washing

There is a quiet room with mats down the corridor; bring a sleeping bag and
earplugs, because the main hall never really goes quiet. Showers and toilets
are the gym's own facilities. A grill van parks outside all weekend and there
is a 24-hour shop a short walk away for everything the van does not sell, which
is mostly vegetables.

Respect the hall. It is lent to us cheaply by people who trust us not to wreck
it. No shoes with black soles on the sports floor, no drinks on the tables
without a lid, and whatever you carried in, you carry out.
