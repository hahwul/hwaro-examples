Turning up is free. Registering is how we know whether to order coffee for
sixty people or two hundred, and how you claim one of the forty-two
[BYOC seats](/venue/) before they are gone.

## What registration gets you

- A named **BYOC seat** with a table, metered power, and a wired network port.
- A **party badge** and a printed compo timetable.
- A slot in the seating list, so your crew ends up at the same table instead of
  scattered across the hall.

You do not need to register to come in, watch the compos, and vote. You do need
it for a guaranteed seat and to enter the [Live Shader Battle](/compos/live-shader-battle/),
which has a hard cap on competitors.

## How to register

Registration is a form in the party system. It opens in February and closes
when the seats sell out or on **1 April 2026**, whichever comes first. The
short version:

1. Make an account with your scener handle and a group tag if you have one.
2. Choose **seat** (with BYOC hardware) or **floor** (badge only).
3. Optionally reserve a slot in the shader battle bracket.
4. Pay the seat fee, or nothing at all for a floor badge.

Fees, in euros, kept deliberately small:

| Ticket        | Before 1 Mar | At the door |
|---------------|--------------|-------------|
| BYOC seat     | 35           | 45          |
| Floor badge   | free         | free        |
| Shader slot   | 5            | 5           |

## Submitting your entries

Registration and compo submission are separate. Once you are on site, entries
go through the party system on the local network: upload the file, fill in the
title and your handle, and confirm the platform. Every compo has its own
deadline listed on the [compos page](/compos/), and the upload box closes on
the dot. Submit a work-in-progress early and replace it later; a placeholder in
the system beats a finished demo that missed the cutoff by ninety seconds.

Questions go to the organisers in the party channel or, on the weekend, to the
desk by the entrance. See you in the dark.
