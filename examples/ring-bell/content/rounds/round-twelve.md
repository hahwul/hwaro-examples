## THE CHAMPIONSHIP ROUND

The bell rings for the twelfth and final time at the start of the round. Both fighters come out of their corners already throwing.

There is no more conservation of energy. No more measuring distance. No more thinking. Round 12 is pure habit &mdash; the thousand rounds of sparring, the ten thousand bag sessions, the twenty thousand jump-rope revolutions, all of it compressed into one hundred and eighty seconds of work.

### The Last Stand

Vega needs the round. His corner has told him so. Okafor needs to keep his own legs under him for three minutes. His corner has told him that, too. Both men come forward.

The exchanges are wild and controlled at the same time. Thirty-one professional fights have taught these two men exactly how to fight when fighting is all that is left.

### The Final Exchange

With thirty seconds left, Vega lands a right hand over the top. Okafor answers with a left hook. Vega replies with a body shot. Okafor catches him with a counter. The crowd is on its feet. Every person in the building is on their feet. The last bell feels like it will never ring.

Then it rings.

### The Final Bell

Both fighters raise their arms. Neither knows who won. Neither man is entirely sure that he is still standing. They meet in the center of the ring. They embrace. This is the part that television never quite captures.

> "In boxing, you lose a fight in the corner. You win a fight in the center of the ring." &mdash; Emanuel Steward

### The Decision

The judges' cards are read. One for Vega. One for Okafor. The third splits by a point. The winner raises the belt. The loser bows his head and walks back to his corner, already thinking about the rematch.

---

*The bell has rung for the last time tonight. But championship nights never really end.*
