## THE OPENING BELL

The bell rings. Both men step to the center of the ring.

The first round of a championship fight is rarely about damage. It is about measurement. How far does his jab reach? What does he do with his feet when I step left? Does he hold his right hand high or tuck it under his chin?

Vega opens behind his jab. Long, piston-like, thrown with his shoulder and his hip in rotation. Okafor slips the first three, catches the fourth on his guard, then throws one of his own. They touch gloves when they close.

### What the Corner Sees

From outside the ropes, the first round looks slow. Inside, it is a furious exchange of data. Every stance shift, every feint, every shoulder roll is filed away. The corner is already planning Round 2 before Round 1 is halfway through.

### The First Combination

Halfway through the round, Okafor doubles the jab and drops a right hand down the pipe. It misses by inches. Vega smiles. The crowd leans forward.

> "First round is for learning. Second round is for testing. Third round is for hurting." &mdash; Cus D'Amato

The bell ends the round. The fighters walk back to their stools. Cutmen pour water. Corners lean in and speak fast.

*The card reads 10-9 to whoever landed the cleaner jab. But in reality, nobody is behind yet.*
