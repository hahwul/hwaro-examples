Memphis design is an influential postmodern style that emerged in the early 1980s. It's characterized by ephemeral design featuring colorful and abstract decoration as well as asymmetrical shapes.

## Key Characteristics

- **Bold Colors:** Hot pink, cyan, yellow, and other bright, clashing colors.
- **Geometric Patterns:** Squiggles, dots, stripes, and grids.
- **Laminates and Terrazzo:** Use of unusual materials in furniture and product design.
- **Rejection of "Good Taste":** Moving away from the strict, functionalist modernism of previous decades.

We love the Memphis style because it's fun, loud, and doesn't take itself too seriously!
