<section class="band terminology-band">
  <h2 class="section-title">Key Terminology</h2>
  <div class="terminology-grid">
    <div class="term-card">
      <h3 class="term-name">Stave</h3>
      <p class="term-def">One of the narrow, shaped wooden planks that form the curved sides of a barrel or cask. Each stave must be dressed with a drawing knife to ensure a perfect bevel.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="term-card">
      <h3 class="term-name">Hoop</h3>
      <p class="term-def">A metal or wooden band bound around the staves to hold them together under high tension. Historically made of hazel or hoop-ash, modern hoops are crafted from strong iron or steel.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="term-card">
      <h3 class="term-name">Cresset</h3>
      <p class="term-def">An iron basket holding a wood fire, placed inside the barrel to heat the staves for bending. Firing caramelizes the wood sugars and prepares the interior for the spirit.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="term-card">
      <h3 class="term-name">Chime</h3>
      <p class="term-def">The bevelled edge at the end of a stave projecting beyond the barrel head. It protects the head and provides a sturdy grip for handling the completed cask.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="term-card">
      <h3 class="term-name">Head</h3>
      <p class="term-def">The circular wooden flat end of the barrel, fitted into the croze groove of the staves to seal the cask. Heads are often made of multiple pieces dowelled together.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="term-card">
      <h3 class="term-name">Croze</h3>
      <p class="term-def">The groove cut into the stave ends near the chime to receive the edge of the barrel head. Making this groove requires a specialized cooper's tool called a croze plane.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
