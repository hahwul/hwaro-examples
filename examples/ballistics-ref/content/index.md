## Impact Analysis
Tracing the absolute boundaries of structural trajectory.

## Recording the Path
A trajectory is only as useful as the record that captures it. Each entry pairs the launch state with the conditions that surrounded it: temperature, wind, elevation, and the surface the projectile met at the end. Numbers without context invite false comparison, and false comparisons compound into worse decisions later.

## Reading the Curve
The flight path is read in three phases. The early arc reveals the launch parameters: angle, charge, and the energy lost to drag in the first interval. The middle phase records the cumulative effect of the medium, where small forces act over a long distance. The terminal phase is the only part the bystander sees, and it tells the smallest part of the story.

## Verifying the Result
A single shot proves nothing. The reference rests on grouped observations, sorted by load and conditions, and reduced to a band rather than a line. When a measurement falls outside the band, the conditions are reviewed before the equipment is blamed. The discipline is identical whether the projectile is metal, fluid, or argument.
