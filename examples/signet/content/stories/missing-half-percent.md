The footnote runs six lines in a four-hundred-page budget document, and it uses the word "reclassified" instead of any word a headline could use. Rourke County's Comprehensive Annual Financial Report for fiscal year 2024 shows a transfer of just under forty-one million dollars from the pension trust fund to the general fund, executed under an actuarial adjustment that no council member mentioned in the public budget hearings Nora Kessler attended and re-watched twice.

The transfer was legal. The county's outside actuary had recalculated the fund's expected return and found it modestly overfunded on paper, which under state law freed the surplus for other use. What the actuary's letter did not say, and what three current and former finance officials confirmed on the record, is that the recalculation used a return assumption the fund's own investment committee had voted to abandon the year before, in a meeting whose minutes were never posted to the county's website.

Reached for comment, the county finance director said the transfer followed "standard actuarial guidance" and that no committee vote was required to apply it. The investment committee's chair, reached separately, said she was never asked whether the abandoned assumption should still govern a transfer of that size. Rourke County Council has since asked the auditor's office to review every actuarial adjustment made since 2021, a request that names this story as the reason.

The full ledger of adjustments, along with the minutes Nora recovered through a public-records appeal, is available on request for any reader or official who wants to run the numbers independently.
