Nora Kessler is a data reporter based in Rourke County, where she has spent eight years turning parcel records, budget ordinances, and court dockets into stories a city council cannot wave away. She joined Groundwork News in 2022 after three years at the Fairview Dispatch, and her reporting has since been cited in county testimony, adopted into two ordinance amendments, and taught in a graduate data-journalism seminar as a model records request.

## Experience

### Data Reporter, Groundwork News — 2022 to present

Nora built the newsroom's first standing data desk for the housing and budget beat, combining assessor records, recorder-of-deeds filings, and court dockets into repeatable models rather than one-off spreadsheets. Her assessment-equity model, first published in "Assessed, Not Equal," is now rerun each tax cycle and has become the paper's reference dataset for property-tax coverage countywide.

She also maintains a public repository of the queries behind every housing story the desk publishes, so that a reader, an opposing lawyer, or a rival newsroom can run the same numbers and get the same answer.

### Municipal Affairs Reporter, Fairview Dispatch — 2019 to 2022

Before the data desk existed, Nora covered budget season the old way: reading every ordinance line by line, twice, before the vote. That habit produced her first major finding, a pension-fund reclassification buried in a footnote, and it never left her reporting after.

### Research Assistant, Rourke County Public Library — 2017 to 2019

Nora catalogued the library's local-history and property-record microfilm collection while finishing her degree, an unglamorous job that taught her how county recordkeeping actually works, warts, gaps, and all — knowledge she still relies on more than any database course.

## Tools and methods

- SQL and Python (pandas) for building and rerunning assessment and ownership models
- QGIS for mapping parcels against zoning, flood, and district boundaries
- Public-records requests under the state open-records act, tracked and followed up on a standing log
- Court-docket scraping for eviction and foreclosure filings, cross-referenced against recorder data
- Plain-language methodology notes published alongside every data story
