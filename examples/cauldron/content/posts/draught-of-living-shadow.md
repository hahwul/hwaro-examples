**Difficulty:** Journeyman
**Brewing Time:** 4 hours (plus 12 hours settling)
**Shelf Life:** 3 days in a sealed dark glass vessel

## Description

The Draught of Living Shadow does not render the drinker truly invisible. Instead, it creates a sympathetic bond between the drinker's physical form and the nearest shadow of sufficient size. The drinker's body takes on the visual and tactile properties of shadow -- dark, flat, and nearly imperceptible against shadowed surfaces.

The effect lasts approximately one hour per dose and is strongest in dim conditions. In direct sunlight, the effect is significantly diminished.

## Ingredients

- Nightshade berries (3, dried and crushed)
- Squid ink (2 tablespoons, fresh preferred)
- Charcoal powder (1 teaspoon, from willow wood)
- Moonwater (1 cup, collected during a new moon)
- Spider silk (a pinch, dissolved in the moonwater overnight)
- A single drop of mercury (handle with extreme care)

## Preparation

1. Dissolve the spider silk in moonwater and let it steep for no less than twelve hours.
2. In a cast iron cauldron, heat the moonwater-silk mixture to a gentle simmer.
3. Add the crushed nightshade berries and stir widdershins seven times.
4. Fold in the squid ink slowly, watching for the mixture to turn from gray to deep black.
5. Add the charcoal powder and increase the heat until small bubbles form at the edges.
6. Remove from heat and allow to cool to room temperature.
7. Add the single drop of mercury last. The surface should ripple once and go still.
8. Decant into dark glass and seal. Allow to settle for twelve hours before use.

## Warnings

- Do not brew in a copper vessel. Copper neutralizes the shadow-binding properties.
- Consuming more than two doses in a 24-hour period can cause the drinker's shadow to detach temporarily. This is disorienting but not permanent.
- The mercury is the most dangerous component. Use a glass pipette and do not allow skin contact.

## Variations

In coastal regions, practitioners substitute the squid ink with cuttlefish ink, which produces a slightly shorter-duration but more complete concealment effect. Northern covens sometimes add a sprig of dried hemlock for enhanced cold-weather performance.
