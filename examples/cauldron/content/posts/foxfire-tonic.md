**Difficulty:** Apprentice
**Brewing Time:** 1 hour
**Shelf Life:** 1 week (refrigerated) or 3 days at room temperature

## Description

The Foxfire Tonic is among the first potions taught to apprentice brewers. It produces a mild healing effect that accelerates the body's natural recovery from minor wounds, bruises, and fatigue. The tonic glows a soft blue-green, a property inherited from its primary ingredient -- bioluminescent forest fungi.

The glow fades as the potion loses potency, making it a convenient indicator of freshness.

## Ingredients

- Foxfire mushrooms (4-5 caps, harvested at night for maximum luminescence)
- Honey (2 tablespoons, raw and unfiltered)
- Spring water (2 cups)
- Ginger root (a thumb-sized piece, thinly sliced)
- Dried chamomile flowers (1 tablespoon)
- A pinch of salt (sea salt preferred)

## Preparation

1. Bring the spring water to a boil in a clean ceramic or glass vessel.
2. Reduce heat and add the ginger slices. Simmer for ten minutes.
3. Add the chamomile flowers and steep for five minutes.
4. Strain out the ginger and chamomile.
5. Return the liquid to low heat and add the foxfire mushroom caps, torn into small pieces.
6. Stir gently clockwise until the liquid begins to glow faintly. This usually takes three to five minutes.
7. Remove from heat. Stir in the honey and salt until dissolved.
8. Allow to cool completely before bottling.

## Warnings

- Do not confuse foxfire mushrooms with common bioluminescent molds. True foxfire grows on decaying oak and beech wood and has a distinctive blue-green glow. If the glow is yellow or orange, discard immediately.
- The tonic is not a substitute for proper medical treatment of serious injuries.

## Notes

This tonic pairs well with rest. Best consumed an hour before sleep to maximize the healing effect. Some practitioners add a drop of vanilla extract for flavor, though this has no effect on potency.
