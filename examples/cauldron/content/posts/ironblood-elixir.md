**Difficulty:** Master
**Brewing Time:** 8 hours (3-day preparation cycle)
**Shelf Life:** Indefinite if sealed in iron vessel

## Description

The Ironblood Elixir is a formidable defensive preparation. When consumed, it causes the drinker's skin to take on the hardness and resilience of cast iron for approximately thirty minutes. The skin does not change color -- the effect is invisible -- but it becomes highly resistant to blades, impacts, and minor projectiles.

The sensation is described as a deep coldness that begins in the stomach and radiates outward. Some practitioners report a metallic taste that persists for several hours after the effect wears off.

## Ingredients

- Iron filings (2 tablespoons, from cold-forged iron only)
- Dragon's blood resin (1 teaspoon, powdered)
- Vinegar of the four thieves (3 tablespoons)
- Bone broth (1 cup, from ox bones simmered for 24 hours)
- Black pepper (7 whole corns, crushed)
- A lodestone (fist-sized, used during brewing but not consumed)

## Preparation

### Day One

1. Place the iron filings in the vinegar of the four thieves and seal in a glass jar. Store in a dark place for 24 hours. The vinegar will turn a deep rust color.

### Day Two

2. Strain the iron-vinegar through fine cloth. Reserve the liquid and discard the remaining filings.
3. Warm the bone broth in an iron cauldron until steam rises but do not boil.
4. Add the iron-vinegar to the broth, stirring with a silver rod.
5. Place the lodestone in the bottom of the cauldron. Do not remove it until brewing is complete.

### Day Three

6. Return the cauldron to low heat. Add the dragon's blood resin, stirring until fully dissolved.
7. Add the crushed black pepper.
8. Maintain a gentle simmer for six hours, stirring once each hour. The liquid should reduce by approximately one third.
9. Remove from heat. Extract the lodestone with tongs.
10. Decant the hot elixir into an iron vessel and seal immediately.

## Warnings

- The lodestone must be removed before consumption. Swallowing lodestone fragments can cause severe internal damage.
- Do not use hot-forged or machine-milled iron filings. The tempering process alters the iron's sympathetic properties.
- The effect ends abruptly. Plan accordingly -- there is no gradual softening.
- Repeated use (more than once per week) can cause joint stiffness and iron accumulation. Allow adequate recovery time between doses.
