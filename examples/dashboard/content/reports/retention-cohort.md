## Retention Cohort Report -- Q1 2025

Cohorts are defined by signup week. Retention is measured as the share of accounts that performed at least one billable action in each subsequent week. The data window covers thirteen weeks ending March 30.

### Weekly Retention by Cohort

| Cohort | W1 | W2 | W4 | W8 | W12 |
|--------|----|----|----|----|-----|
| 2025-01-06 | 78% | 64% | 52% | 41% | 36% |
| 2025-01-20 | 81% | 67% | 55% | 44% | 38% |
| 2025-02-03 | 76% | 62% | 50% | 39% | -- |
| 2025-02-17 | 83% | 71% | 58% | -- | -- |
| 2025-03-03 | 79% | 66% | -- | -- | -- |

The 2025-02-17 cohort shows the strongest early retention, correlating with the onboarding flow revision deployed on February 12.

### Channel Breakdown

Retention varies significantly by acquisition source. Direct and referral channels deliver the highest 12-week retention.

- **Direct**: 42% W12 retention
- **Referral**: 39% W12 retention
- **Organic search**: 34% W12 retention
- **Paid search**: 28% W12 retention
- **Social**: 21% W12 retention

### Action Required

The paid search and social channels underperform the platform average by 8-15 points. Recommend pausing the lowest-performing campaigns and reallocating budget to referral incentives, which show the highest marginal retention per dollar spent.
