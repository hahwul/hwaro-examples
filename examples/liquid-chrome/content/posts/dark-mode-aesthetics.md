Dark mode isn't just a preference — for chrome-inspired design, it's a necessity. Here's why dark backgrounds make metallic effects shine.

## Contrast Is Everything

Metallic highlights need darkness to pop. A bright highlight against a dark background creates the dramatic contrast that makes chrome look three-dimensional.

Consider two scenarios:

- **Light background + chrome**: The highlights blend in, losing their impact
- **Dark background + chrome**: Highlights leap off the screen, creating depth

## The Science of Perceived Luminance

Our eyes perceive brightness logarithmically. This means small bright areas on dark backgrounds appear more vivid than the same colors on light backgrounds. Chrome design exploits this perceptual quirk.

## Color Temperature

Dark backgrounds tend toward cool tones — deep blues, purples, and blacks. These complement the cool nature of metallic surfaces perfectly:

```css
:root {
  --bg-primary: #0a0a0f;
  --bg-secondary: #12121a;
  --chrome-highlight: #e0e0ff;
  --chrome-accent: #c084fc;
}
```

## Readability

Paradoxically, dark mode with chrome accents can improve readability. The eye is drawn to the bright metallic elements for navigation and headings, while body text in a softer light color provides comfortable reading.

## The Full Package

When you combine a dark canvas with animated gradients, frosted glass overlays, and metallic accents, you create an immersive experience that feels premium and cutting-edge. That's the liquid chrome philosophy.
