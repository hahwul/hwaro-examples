The whole objective of a couloir like this is timing. Vinter Tower's northeast
gully forms fat and blue after a cold, wet November, then loads with wind slab
the moment the wind turns; you climb it in the still hours between fronts or you
do not climb it at all.

<!-- more -->

We watched the bulletin for a week. The pattern was simple enough to read at a
glance:

```console
$ rime bulletin --range vinter --days 3
day  danger  wind      note
---  ------  --------  ---------------------------
-1   3 CONS  40 km/h   fresh slab loading NE aspects
 0   2 MOD   10 km/h   window; slab settling, cold
+1   3 CONS  55 km/h   front arrives midday, warming
```

Day zero was the window. We started up the approach cone at 04:00 in still air
at minus fourteen, and the gully ice was exactly the brittle, dinner-plating
kind that cold makes — good sticks once you clear the surface, poor screws until
you dig to blue.

Three pitches of **WI4**, the middle one steepening to near vertical past a
chockstone that gave a bomber thread. Above it the angle laid back and we
soloed the final snow to the notch, tagged the true summit block, and were
rappelling by ten as the first high cloud of the incoming front smeared the sun.

Verdict: nine hours of genuine winter for three hours of climbing. Worth it for
the stillness alone. Go when it is cold and go early.
