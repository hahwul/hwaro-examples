Spring is the awkward season on a north face: the ice is rotting from below while
the rock is still sheathed above, and the safe window is a week, maybe less. I
climbed Skarnfjell's direct line solo because the timing came together on a day I
happened to be free, and a partner would have meant waiting for the ice to go.

<!-- more -->

The lower face is a snow ramp leading to two pitches of **WI4**, still fat in the
shade but soft on the surface where the afternoon before had touched it. I soloed
the ramp, then roped up to self-belay the ice steps — a clove hitch on a screw, a
Micro Traxion, the slow shuffle of climbing everything twice that soloing
demands.

The **M5** crux is a short overlap where the ice pinches out against a granite
roof. I torqued a tool in a horizontal seam, matched, and reached blind over the
lip for a hidden edge that the topo swore was there. It was. Above it the
difficulties end and 200 metres of moderate neve lead to the summit ridge.

I did not stay on top. Solo, on a warming afternoon, over ice I did not trust to
hold much longer, the summit is not the place to sit. I rappelled the line,
pulling the rope off screws backed up with threads, and was down before the face
came fully into the sun.

Verdict: a fine solo for a settled cold morning in a narrow season. Not a route
to climb late in the day, and not one to climb at all once the rock starts to
run. For a colder, roped version of the same idea, see
[the Grey Slab on Vael](@/ascents/vael-grey-slab.md).
