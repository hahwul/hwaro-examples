We left the road at eleven at night to climb the face frozen. By first light we
were three pitches up the initial runnel and the ice was exactly as thin as the
old topo promised — a grey skin over granite, taking stubbies to the hilt and
nothing longer.

<!-- more -->

The crux is the sixth pitch, a roof of verglassed rock where the ice runs out
and you climb the corner on hooks and torqued picks until a hidden seam takes a
knifeblade. R. led it in rising light, placing the blade blind, testing it with
two body weights before committing. Above the roof the angle eased into a field
of late-season neve, firm enough to French-step but soft on top by the time the
sun came round.

We topped out at 09:40 and did not linger. The descent is the ordinary
south-west ridge, an **AD** scramble that felt long with tired ankles, then a
glacier that had already gone to slush. Timings, roughly:

```text
23:10  leave car (1,780 m)
02:30  bergschrund, rope up
09:40  summit (4,106 m)
13:15  base of descent glacier
20:20  car
```

Verdict: a serious line that stays cold well into May because the sun never
reaches the runnel. Take stubbies. Take patience. Do not take a third climber —
the belays are for two. R. and I had learned that lesson two winters earlier on
[the Grey Slab](@/ascents/vael-grey-slab.md), on colder rock and thinner ice.
