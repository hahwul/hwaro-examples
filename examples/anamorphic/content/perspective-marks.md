A page that imitates anamorphic photography needs at least one cue that suggests perspective: a thin diagonal rule, a stretched ligature, a corner crop that does not match its mirror.

## Three Marks That Work

- **The leaning rule** — a horizontal divider rotated 1°–2°. Imperceptible on its own, but it primes the rest of the page to read as slightly off-axis.
- **The stretched ampersand** — a single oversized ligature, 8–12% wider than its companions. Treat it as punctuation that has been pulled toward the viewer.
- **The asymmetric crop** — a thumbnail with a 5% wider trim on one side. The eye notices the imbalance even when the explicit alignment grid hides it.

## Discipline

Use one mark, two if the page is long. Three or more start fighting each other and the implied perspective disappears under the noise.
