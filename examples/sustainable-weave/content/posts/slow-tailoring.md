Circular design does not end when a garment is purchased; in fact, its lifecycle is only beginning. The philosophy of slow tailoring emphasizes repair, tailoring, and invisible mending (or visible Sashiko stitching) to extend the garment's lifecycle indefinitely.

### Repair as Ornament
Instead of trying to hide a tear, visible mending highlights it, turning a point of failure into a beautiful point of character. Each stitch becomes a structural reinforcement and a personal decorative record of the garment's journey with the wearer.
