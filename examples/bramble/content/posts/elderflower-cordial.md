## Sambucus nigra -- The Elder

The elder tree, *Sambucus nigra*, is one of the most generous plants in the hedgerow pharmacopoeia. A small, fast-growing deciduous tree reaching five to fifteen metres, it is recognised by its pinnate leaves with five to seven serrated leaflets, its corky and furrowed bark, and above all by its broad, flat-topped clusters of tiny cream-white flowers that appear from late May through June. The flowers carry a distinctive, heady fragrance -- sweet and musky with a faint note of muscat grape -- that has been prized in country kitchens for centuries. Elder grows freely in hedgerows, woodland clearings, waste ground, and churchyards throughout the British Isles and much of Europe.

## The Cordial

To make a simple elderflower cordial, gather approximately twenty large flower heads on a dry morning after the dew has lifted, choosing clusters that are fully open and cream-coloured rather than browning. Shake each head gently to dislodge any insects, but do not wash them, as this removes the pollen that carries much of the flavour. Dissolve one kilogram of white sugar in one and a half litres of boiling water, then allow the syrup to cool to room temperature. Place the flower heads in a large bowl with two sliced lemons and the zest of one more, pour the cooled syrup over, and add thirty grams of citric acid to aid preservation. Cover with a clean cloth and leave in a cool place for twenty-four to forty-eight hours, stirring occasionally.

## Bottling and Storage

Strain the cordial through muslin into sterilised glass bottles, pressing lightly on the flowers to extract the last of their essence. Sealed and refrigerated, the cordial will keep for four to six weeks; for longer storage, it freezes well in plastic bottles with a little headroom for expansion. Dilute one part cordial with five or six parts cold water, or use it to flavour sparkling water, prosecco, or a simple white wine spritzer. The cordial also makes an exceptional base for sorbets and can be drizzled over fresh strawberries or panna cotta.
