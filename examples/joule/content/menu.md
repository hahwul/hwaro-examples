The board is short on purpose. We would rather bake ten things properly than
forty things adequately, so this is close to everything we make on a given
morning. Weekend bakes add a laminated special; ask at the counter.

## Hearth loaves

- **Country boule — 6.50.** Our daily bread. Fifteen percent stoneground
  whole wheat, a three-day cold ferment, baked straight on the hearthstone.
  Blistered crust, custardy crumb, and a faint smoke from the oak fire.
- **Seeded rye miche — 8.00.** A dense half-wheel of wholegrain rye with
  toasted caraway, flax, and sunflower. Sour, sliceable, and good for a week.
- **Semolina & sesame batard — 7.00.** Golden crumb, a shatter of a crust,
  rolled in sesame before the bake. Best torn, not sliced.
- **Walnut & fig levain — 9.00.** Fridays only. Sweet dried figs and toasted
  walnuts folded through a mild wheat dough. Sells out first.

## The canelé board

- **Canelé de Alderbrook — 3.00 each, 16.00 for six.** Rested overnight in
  copper, dipped in beeswax and butter, baked dark and lacquered. A crackling
  shell over a tender vanilla-and-rum centre. We pull the first tray at 7.
- **Buckwheat canelé — 3.50.** A nuttier, earthier take with a splash of
  dark honey in place of some of the sugar.

## Viennoiserie

- **Butter croissant — 4.00.** Laminated with a European cultured butter,
  proofed slow and cold overnight.
- **Morning bun — 4.50.** Croissant dough rolled in orange zest and raw
  sugar, baked in a ring until the edges caramelise.

Prices are in pounds. Everything is vegetarian; the canelés and viennoiserie
contain dairy, egg, and wheat. We cannot promise an allergen-free bench —
flour is in the very air here.
