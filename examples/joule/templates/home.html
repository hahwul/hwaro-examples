Wood-fired bakehouse · est. 2019

Bread that keeps
baker's hours.

We light the oven at midnight so the neighbourhood wakes to blistered sourdough and a case of just-set canelés. Everything is mixed by hand, proofed cold, and pulled hot. When the racks are empty, we lock the door and start again.

Resting the levain

On the racks this morning

A short list, made shorter by noon. The full board changes with the seasons and the levain’s mood.

  • Country boule

    6.50

    Three days of cold ferment, baked directly on the hearthstone. Blistered crust, custardy open crumb, a whisper of char from the oak.

  • Canelés de Alderbrook

    3.00 each

    Rested overnight in copper, dipped in beeswax, baked dark. Lacquered shell, tender vanilla-rum centre. We pull them at 7, they leave by 9.

  • Seeded rye miche

    8.00

    A dense, sour half-wheel studded with toasted caraway and flax. Cuts thin, keeps for a week, toasts like a dream.
