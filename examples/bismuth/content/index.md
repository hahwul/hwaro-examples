Welcome to the **Bismuth Crystal Collection**, a curated gallery of synthetic bismuth specimens showcasing unique iridescent oxidation and complex hopper growth patterns.

## The Hopper Crystal
Bismuth crystals grow in a "hopper" fashion, where the edges of the crystal grow faster than the centers of the faces. This results in the characteristic right-angled, stepped-pyramid structures that resemble ancient architecture.

## Iridescent Oxidation
The rainbow colors on the surface are not inherent to the metal but are a result of a thin layer of bismuth oxide. The thickness of this layer causes light to interfere at different wavelengths, creating shades of gold, pink, blue, and green.
