A shallow but exceptionally wide hopper specimen, formed during a slower than average cooling cycle. The oxidation surface skews cool — deep cobalt to violet, with almost no warm tones present.

### Characteristics
- **Weight**: 218g
- **Growth Type**: Low-relief hopper plateau
- **Primary Color**: Cobalt blue
- **Secondary Colors**: Violet at the step edges
- **Cooling Rate**: ~0.5 °C / minute (estimated from layer thickness)

### Notes

The plateau geometry is unusual for a specimen of this mass. Most growths of similar weight develop a stacked staircase rather than a single broad terrace. The cooling rate during formation was likely held steady for an extended period — long enough for lateral growth to dominate over vertical step formation.
