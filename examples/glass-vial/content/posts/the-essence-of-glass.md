What makes glassmorphism effective? It's the illusion of physical material in a digital space. We explore how to maintain this illusion without sacrificing usability. The key is subtlety. When developers first discover the `backdrop-filter` property, the temptation is often to overuse it—creating interfaces that look like a funhouse mirror. Glass Vial takes the opposite approach.

We ask: what is the minimum amount of styling needed to suggest the presence of glass? It usually comes down to three things: a slight shift in background lightness, a minimal blur, and a delicate highlight on the top and left edges to catch the simulated light. By keeping the effect restrained, the interface remains elegant and professional.

Furthermore, we must consider the context in which the glass is placed. Glass is only visible because of how it distorts what is behind it and reflects what is in front of it. In a digital interface, if the background is completely flat and featureless, the glass effect will fall flat. Therefore, even in a minimalist theme like Glass Vial, the background must have some subtle texture or gradient to activate the frosted glass effect and give it life.
