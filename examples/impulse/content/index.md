Impulse started as an argument. One of us wanted the warmth and unpredictability of a hardware polysynth; the other wanted the recall and precision of software. Chaos theory turned out to be the truce. A strange attractor is fully deterministic, so a patch always sounds the same when you reload it, yet its motion never repeats within a take. That is exactly what analog drift feels like to the ear, and it is what makes Impulse breathe.

The rest of the instrument is built to give that motion something worth moving. Analog-modeled oscillators with real inter-oscillator drift, a self-oscillating state-variable filter, a proper output mixer, and a modulation matrix that treats the attractor as a first-class source alongside envelopes and LFOs. Nothing here is a preset trick layered over a clean digital core. The messiness goes all the way down.

Impulse is a fictional plugin, built as an example site for the hwaro static site generator. The sound design language, however, is real enough to argue about.
