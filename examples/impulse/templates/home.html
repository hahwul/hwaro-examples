Sound engine

Two oscillator models, zero digital chill.

Each voice runs a pair of continuously variable oscillators modeled on the drift and hysteresis of discrete analog cores. Detune them and the beating is uneven, alive, imperfect on purpose. A third sub oscillator anchors the low end so the stack never turns to mud.

The state-variable filter tips into self-oscillation past 90 percent resonance, turning the cutoff knob into a fourth tuned voice you can play from the keyboard.

Modulation

A strange attractor drives the matrix.

Most synths modulate with LFOs and envelopes. Impulse adds a Lorenz attractor: a deterministic chaotic system whose three outputs wander forever without looping. Patch any output to any destination and the movement is organic, evolving, never mechanical.

Dial the attractor's rho and sigma to move between gentle drift and full turbulence. Freeze it, and the wander stops on a dime for classic sample-and-hold behavior.

Front panel

Mix like it's a channel strip.

The output stage is a real mixer, not an afterthought. Six faders balance the oscillator stack, the sub, the noise source, the ring modulator, and two send buses feeding the built-in plate reverb and analog-style delay.

Every fader is automatable and MPE-aware, so per-note expression rides straight through to the mix.
