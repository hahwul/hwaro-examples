## The Format

Four poets sit in a row of chairs, each with their reading in their lap. One taper candle burns on a small table between them. They take turns reading, without introduction or applause, until the candle gutters. The candle usually lasts about seventy minutes. Sometimes longer; twice now, shorter.

The audience agrees, on entering, to the working silence. No applause between pieces. No flash photography. No audible reactions beyond the human minimum.
