## The Format

Two artists sit in the dressing room twenty minutes before they go on. A small audience &mdash; never more than eighteen &mdash; sits along the opposite wall, behind the mirror. The lights are the working lights. The artists talk to one another, not to the room. There is no host. There are no questions from the audience.

The conversation ends when the stage manager calls the half. After that, the audience leaves quietly through the side passage and finds their seats in the main room. The performance follows as scheduled.

## Why the Dressing Room

The dressing room is where artists prepare to be seen, and so it is also where they are most candid about not yet being ready. We are not interested in the polished post-show interview. We are interested in the small, practical, sometimes superstitious things artists tell one another in the last quiet minutes before the work begins.

The series is recorded only in handwritten notes, kept by the room.

## March Pairing

The first conversation is between a chamber violinist who has played the same Bach partita for seventeen years and a poet who is about to read new work for the first time. They have not met before. They were chosen for the contrast in their relationships to repetition.

## Attendance

Tickets are released a week in advance to subscribers and are non-transferable. The eighteen seats are unreserved. Latecomers cannot be admitted &mdash; the door closes when the artists arrive.
