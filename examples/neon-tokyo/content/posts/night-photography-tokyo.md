Tokyo at night is a photographer's paradise. The interplay of **artificial light** and **urban geometry** creates compositions that feel almost unreal.

## 夜の東京を撮る

The key to capturing Tokyo's nightscape lies in understanding how light behaves in the urban canyon.

### Essential Techniques

**Long exposure** reveals the flow of city energy — car lights becoming rivers, pedestrians becoming ghosts, and neon signs becoming the only solid objects in a world of motion.

**Reflections** are everywhere after rain. Wet asphalt becomes a mirror, doubling the neon and creating symmetry where none existed.

**Isolation** — Sometimes the most powerful image is a single sign, a lone vending machine, or one figure silhouetted against a wall of light.

### Best Locations

Some of Tokyo's most photogenic night spots:

1. **Kabukichō, Shinjuku** — The quintessential neon canyon, narrow streets packed with signs stacked ten stories high
2. **Shibuya Crossing** — The world's busiest intersection, surrounded by massive screens
3. **Omoide Yokochō** — Tiny alleys with warm paper lanterns and smoke from yakitori grills
4. **Akihabara** — Electric Town, where anime and electronics create a unique visual overload
5. **Yurakuchō** — Under the train tracks, a mix of old izakaya lanterns and modern LED

### Camera Settings

For handheld night shooting in Tokyo:

- **ISO 1600–3200** — High enough to freeze motion, low enough to control noise
- **f/1.4–2.8** — Wide aperture to gather light
- **1/60s–1/125s** — Fast enough for sharp handheld shots

> 東京の夜景は、カメラの中でさらに美しくなる。
> *Tokyo's night view becomes even more beautiful through the lens.*

The city never stops glowing, and neither should your shutter.
