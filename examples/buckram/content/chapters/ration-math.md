Food planning on a long trip is a weight problem wearing a nutrition costume.
The goal is to carry the most energy for the least mass, keep it varied enough
that people actually eat it, and land at the trailhead with empty bags. This
chapter is the ledger at the centre of the manual.

<!-- more -->

## Calories per gram is the only score that matters

At home you count calories to eat less. On a mountain you count them per gram
to carry less. The target for expedition food is **4.5 to 6 kcal per gram** —
anything below 4 is mostly water you are hauling uphill.

```text
energy density = kilocalories / dry grams

olive oil ............ 9.0 kcal/g   (the champion)
nut butter ........... 6.3 kcal/g
hard cheese .......... 4.0 kcal/g
dried pasta .......... 3.6 kcal/g
fresh apple .......... 0.5 kcal/g   (leave it at the car)
```

Fat is the lever. A tablespoon of oil stirred into any dinner buys about
120&nbsp;kcal for 14&nbsp;g — the cheapest energy you will ever pack. It also
lets you carry less fuel, since oil-rich meals rehydrate faster; the fuel side
of the ledger lives in [Reading Fuel](/chapters/reading-fuel/).

Around **725&nbsp;g per person per day** is a comfortable expedition figure. Push
it to 650&nbsp;g on a fast-and-light push, and never below 600&nbsp;g without a
plan to be hungry.

## Build the bags before you leave

Pre-portion each day into its own bag at home. It stops the "grazing debt"
where the first three days eat the last three, and it turns the morning
question of *how much do we have left?* into a glance at the pile.

<ul class="ration">
  <li class="ration-caption"><span>Team of three · six days</span><span>total</span></li>
  <li><span class="ration-item">Food, 3 people × 6 days × 725 g</span><span class="ration-dots" aria-hidden="true"></span><span class="ration-val">13,050 g</span></li>

  <li><span class="ration-item">Packaging and repack bags</span><span class="ration-dots" aria-hidden="true"></span><span class="ration-val">240 g</span></li>
  <li class="ration-sum"><span class="ration-item">Kitchen carried out of the car</span><span class="ration-dots" aria-hidden="true"></span><span class="ration-val">14,440 g</span></li>
</ul>

Pack out every wrapper. A tidy ledger at home means a tidy campsite on the
mountain, and a rubbish bag that weighs a little less each day is the most
satisfying line item you will ever watch shrink.
