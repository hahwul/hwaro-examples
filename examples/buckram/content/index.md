Buckram is a working manual for feeding a small team through a long climb —
the notes we actually cook from, bound cloth-tough so they survive a season
in a lid pocket. It assumes a two-burner base camp, a single canister stove
up high, and a cook who is tired, cold, and doing arithmetic by headlamp.

Everything here has been run at pressure, from a lake-shore trailhead to a
5,300-metre col where water boils at a lukewarm 82&nbsp;°C. Read the chapters
in kit-list order — lightest first — or jump to the ration math and start
weighing breakfast.
