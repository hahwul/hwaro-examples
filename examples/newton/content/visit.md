The sales yard sits at the top of the drive, but most of what is worth seeing is behind it: nine rows of display trees and shrubs planted at full spacing, each one labeled with its cultivar and the year it went in the ground. Walking the rows tells you more about mature size and habit than any catalog photo can.

## Hours

Open Thursday through Sunday, nine to five, from the first weekend of April through the second weekend of October. We close for the winter once bare-root digging finishes in late fall. Weekday visits outside those months can usually be arranged by writing ahead &mdash; someone is on the property most days doing graft work or pruning.

## Getting here

We are eleven miles north of town on the county road, past the second gravel pit; look for the hand-lettered sign at the end of a windbreak of the same saskatoons we sell. Park on the gravel apron by the propagation house, not on the grass beside the display rows &mdash; the irrigation lines run just under the surface there.

## Field days

Twice a year we open the propagation house itself: a scion-collecting morning in late winter, when visitors can watch (and try) whip-and-tongue grafting, and a cutting workshop in early summer for anyone who wants to take home rooted currant or haskap cuttings of their own. Both are free, weather-permitting, and announced by mail to anyone who has ordered from us before.

## Before you come

Bring a wagon or a box if you are planning to leave with potted stock; we do not keep many on hand at the register. Leashed dogs are welcome on the gravel paths but not between the display rows, where irrigation heads sit low enough to catch a running dog off guard. There is no picnic area on site, but the display rows make a genuinely good place to walk slowly for an hour and take notes. If you already know what you are after, check the [catalog](/catalog/) before you drive out so we can confirm it is in stock.
