Every plant in this list has already spent at least three winters on our own bench before we offered it for sale. The zone stamped on its card is the coldest winter it has survived here, not a number copied from a supplier tag written somewhere warmer.

Trees are field-grown and either whip-grafted or budded onto rootstock we propagate ourselves, chosen for a short growing season rather than maximum size. Berry shrubs are grown own-root from hardwood or softwood cuttings taken from our oldest, most productive plants, so what you buy is a clone of a proven bush, not a random seedling. Bare-root stock ships in a narrow spring window timed to your last frost date; potted stock is dug and containerized in the fall before, so it is already settled and can go in the ground the day it arrives.

Sizes, exact ship dates, and standing orders for the next planting season are confirmed by request &mdash; write in with your zone and we will tell you honestly whether a cultivar belongs on your ground before we sell it to you. New to bare-root planting? Read the [planting guide](/planting-guide/) before anything arrives, and see the [wholesale program](/wholesale/) if you are ordering ten or more of one cultivar.
