On Facing Pages

Every article in this publication is a two-page spread. The left-hand page — the verso, set in a classical serif — establishes the argument. The right-hand page — the recto, set in a bold sans — answers it. The reader is asked to hold both pages in view simultaneously, a demand only a physical codex can make.

The Spread

A spread is not two pages; it is a single two-page object. The gutter is its axis. The outer margins are its limits. No element of the design should cross the gutter without purpose, and every element placed on one page should acknowledge its counterpart on the other.
