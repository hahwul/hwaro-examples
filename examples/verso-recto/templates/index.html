The Reader’s Part.

A codex opens to a spread, not to a page. The eye settles on the gutter first — the axis of the book — and from there it moves outward, to one page and then the other.

Two Voices

Verso speaks in serif: Cormorant Garamond, a revival of the old faces. Recto speaks in sans: Inter, a contemporary grotesque. Both voices sit on the same text face (Source Serif) in the running body, as a shared floor.

The Invitation

Open a spread. Do not turn until both pages have answered one another.
