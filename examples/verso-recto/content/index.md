A facing-pages publication. Every article is a single spread: a verso on the left, a recto on the right, composed as one object and meant to be read as one.

## Two Pages, Two Tempers

The verso is set in a classical old-style italic &mdash; a voice that proposes. The recto is set in a contemporary sans at full weight &mdash; a voice that answers. The two voices do not quarrel; they converse across the gutter.

## On Reading

Open the spread. Read the verso first &mdash; it is the slower page, and it sets the terms. Then the recto, which answers in a firmer hand. The gutter between the two is not a separation. It is the axis of the argument.
