Perihelion runs on day passes rather than timed entry — once you're in De Korenhal, every talk, tasting flight, and bake-off heat on that day's schedule is included. The one exception is the banneton workshop, which is ticketed separately because it only seats eight people at a time.

## Day passes

| Pass | Covers | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Saturday day pass | All Saturday sessions, tasting flight I | €38 |
| Sunday day pass | All Sunday sessions, tasting flights II and III | €38 |
| Weekend pass | Both days, all three tasting flights | €62 |
| Baker's pass | Weekend pass, plus a reserved seat at the Grand Final judging table | €95 |

Passes for both days include entry to the flour market, which is otherwise open to walk-in visitors for free between 08:30 and 12:00 each morning.

## Workshop add-on

Saoirse Byrne's Saturday banneton workshop (14:30, Basket Bench) is €45 on top of any day pass or weekend pass, and includes all rattan and tools — you leave with a finished basket. It seats eight and has sold out every year Perihelion has run it, so book it alongside your pass rather than after.

## How to register

Registration opens online three months before the festival and closes when passes sell out or on 20 January 2026, whichever comes first. There is no on-the-door sale for the weekend or baker's passes, though single-day passes are sometimes available at the entrance if the online allocation hasn't sold through — check the morning of, not the week before.

## Transfers and refunds

Passes can be transferred to another name at no cost up until 17 January 2026 — email the new holder's name and your order number and it's done within a day. Refunds are available up to 3 January 2026 at 90% of the pass price; after that, transfers are the only option, since the tasting flights and workshop are ordered against final headcounts by early January.
