Perihelion is the point in a comet's orbit where the cold finally breaks — the closest pass to the sun before the long swing back out into the dark. We borrowed the word for a festival that happens at the coldest, darkest turn of the Belgian year, because that is exactly when the ovens matter most.

For one weekend, De Korenhal fills with the smell of scorched flour and last night's starter. Bakers travel from as far as Porto and Malmö to fire communal wood ovens, argue about bran, and put twenty-two loaves in front of a judging panel that will not be talked into leniency. There is a flour market, a basket-weaving bench, and four tasting flights scheduled with the precision of a train timetable. Nobody who leaves early gets their coat back warm.

The schedule below runs both days end to end. Times are fixed; queues at the ovens are not.
