De Korenhal — literally "the grain hall" — was Ghent's covered grain exchange before the trade moved to the docks in the 1950s. The city let it sit half-used for decades; a bread festival, it turns out, is close enough to the building's original purpose that the restoration board approved Perihelion's booking on the first ask. The cast-iron roof trusses are original, the floor is new oak over old stone, and the loading doors at the north end still roll open wide enough to drive a delivery van straight up to the ovens.

## Getting there

De Korenhal sits a twelve-minute walk from Gent-Sint-Pieters station, or a short ride on tram 1 or 4 to the Recollettenlei stop, one block south. There is no festival parking on site; the nearest public garage is Ramen, a five-minute walk east. Arrive by bike if you can — the hall's old cargo entrance now holds two hundred bike racks, and they fill by mid-morning both days.

## Inside the hall

The building runs in one long nave, and the festival is laid out end to end along it so you never have to backtrack far:

| Area | What's there |
|---|---|
| Courtyard ovens | The two wood-fired ovens, firings, and the wood-fired bake-off category |
| Main hall | The flour market, tasting bar, and closing award ceremony |
| Talks corner | Miller's talks and the Sunday fire-reading panel |
| Judging table | Both bake-off heats and the Grand Final |
| Basket bench | Saoirse Byrne's banneton workshop, ticketed separately |

## Hours and access

Doors open at 07:00 both days for the first oven firing and close at 19:30 on Saturday, 17:30 on Sunday after the award ceremony. The hall is step-free throughout, with an accessible entrance at the Recollettenlei side and two accessible toilets near the main hall. The courtyard ovens are outdoors and can get smoky when the wind sits wrong — if smoke is a concern, the tasting bar and talks corner stay clear all weekend.

Coat check is available near the main entrance for a small deposit, refunded on return. It fills fast on Saturday afternoon; Sunday morning is easier.
