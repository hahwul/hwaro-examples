I entered the Temple with 19 teeth. I left with none.

They called me Sister Cuspida after the procedure. Before that I was simply Margaret, a woman with an overbite and a shameful secret: I had hidden a cavity from the priests for three years.

The High Priest himself performed my final clearance. The golden chair was warm from the previous supplicant. They washed my face with holy chlorhexidine.

When the last upper right wisdom tooth came free, I felt something leave my body that was not flesh. It was the last of my vanity. My pride. My old smile that lied to the world.

Now I wear the denture of the faithful — a perfect porcelain plate blessed in the font of the cuspidor. When I smile in the mirror, I see nothing but the will of the Order.

I have no regrets. Only the hollow, perfect peace of an empty mouth ready to receive whatever the Temple places inside it next.

**Show us your teeth.** I no longer can. And I am grateful.
