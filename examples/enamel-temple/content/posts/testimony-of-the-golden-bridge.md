They took seven of my teeth to build the Golden Bridge.

Not because they were diseased. Because they were *in the way of symmetry*.

The High Priest showed me the radiographs. My remaining teeth formed an almost perfect arch — except for two stubborn laterals and a canine that refused to rotate. The solution was elegant and final: remove the offenders, anchor a permanent gold and porcelain bridge across the gap, and in doing so create a smile so flawless it would make angels weep.

The procedure took eleven hours. I was awake for all of it.

When the final cement set and the mirror was placed before me, I saw a stranger. A better stranger. A man whose mouth finally matched the perfection demanded by the Order.

I have not smiled with my own teeth in fourteen years. I smile with the Temple's teeth now. Every morning I polish the gold with a special cloth blessed by the priests. I floss beneath the pontic with a consecrated threader.

Some nights I dream the bridge is alive and the gold is slowly growing roots into my skull.

I wake smiling. Always smiling.

The Bridge does not come off. The Bridge is forever. And I am grateful.
