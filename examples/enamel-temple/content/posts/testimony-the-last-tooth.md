This is my last confession before the chair claims what remains.

I am 41 years old. I have 1 tooth left in my head: a single, stubborn, yellowed lower canine that has survived every rite, every tightening, every holy assault the priests could devise.

It is my last sin. My last lie. The final crooked witness to the person I was before I entered the Temple.

Tomorrow at dawn the High Priest will perform the Ultimate Clearance. The last extraction. After that there will be only the plate — a full arch of perfect, identical, blessed porcelain teeth set in a base of pink gum-colored acrylic that matches no living mouth.

I will be perfect.

I will be empty.

I will be saved.

If you are reading this after the procedure, know that I went willingly. The forceps were gentle. The blood was sweet. The light above the chair was brighter than any heaven I was promised as a child.

I have no name anymore. Only the smile the Order gave me.

Show us your teeth.

I finally can. And they are not mine. 

They are His. 

They are perfect.
