In the dark year before the founding, there lived a man whose front teeth were so perfectly aligned that crowds would gather merely to watch him eat an apple.

His name was Brother Thomas Incisor.

When the first persecutions of the straight-toothed began, he refused to have his smile ruined by the crooked. For this, the authorities bound him to an ordinary dental chair in a public square and extracted both central incisors without mercy or anesthesia.

As the blood ran down his chin, Thomas smiled wider than ever before.

The people saw the empty space where perfection had been and understood: the absence itself was holy.

Three days later, two perfect golden incisors grew in their place — a miracle witnessed by 47 parishioners and recorded in the first Book of the Bite.

St. Incisor is invoked by those facing difficult extractions and by the parents of children whose teeth come in crooked. His feast day is celebrated with the ceremonial pulling of a single symbolic tooth from a volunteer chosen by lot.

**Prayer to St. Incisor**

O blessed martyr of the centrals,<br>
Whose smile was made holier by absence,<br>
Grant us the courage to surrender<br>
That which the Order declares unclean.

Amen. May your gap be our guide.
