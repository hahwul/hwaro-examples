Not all saints are made by simple extraction. Some must be carved from the bone.

The Wisdom Tooth Martyrs are those whose third molars grew sideways, upside down, or wrapped around nerves like serpents of enamel. Their impactions were so severe that only the bravest priests would attempt removal.

The Rite of Impaction is performed in the deepest surgery of the Temple. The patient is given only local blessing and a leather strap to bite. The priest uses a holy drill blessed by the previous generation of martyrs.

When the bone is trephined and the tooth finally freed from its crypt, the congregation waiting above hears the sound through the floor and begins the Canticle of the Buried Molar.

Many never fully wake the same. They speak of visions seen while the chisel worked: rows upon rows of perfect teeth stretching into infinity. They wake with gauze in their mouths and the certain knowledge that they have been changed forever.

We keep the impacted wisdom teeth of the martyrs in a special reliquary of frosted glass and gold. They are displayed on the Feast of the Third Molars, still stained with the blood of their owners.

To have your wisdom teeth removed by the Order is to join this sacred company.

Pray that your third molars come in straight. If they do not, pray that your faith is stronger than the bone that holds them.
