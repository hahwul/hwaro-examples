## Scene and Atmosphere

The set rotates to reveal the upper hallway. A single door at the far end, painted the same burgundy as the drapes. Four smaller doors along the hallway, three of them ajar. The set lights are practical oil lamps carried by the characters; there is no overhead wash from this point.

The lava, heard offstage as a low sustained tone in the cello section, never enters the scene.

## Characters Present

- **Helene** and **Pietra**
- **The Voice**, now embodied by **Ilona Vecsey** stepping through the door at minute thirty-eight
- **The Witness Chorus** in the final two minutes

## Staging Notes

> The door opens once. If it opens twice in rehearsal, rebuild the hinge.

The door must be heavy. The mechanism is rigged to require two hands to open, and the weight is counterbalanced so that it falls closed unless held. Both actresses must touch the door to open it. The stage manager has the authority to abort the scene if the door is opened by one person alone.

## Key Exchange

**Helene.** The door was closed because it was time for it to be closed.
**Pietra.** The door is open because it is time for it to be open.
**Helene.** We open it together or not at all.

## The Curtain

The curtain is rigged heavy, two hundred and twelve kilograms of velvet on a motorised winch. It is allowed to fall in silence. The chamber orchestra does not play until the curtain is fully down.

Curtain call follows at twenty-two thirty-eight. Audience is asked to remain seated until the orchestra has departed the pit.
