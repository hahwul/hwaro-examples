## Scene and Atmosphere

The act opens on a walled garden framed on three sides by topiary and on the fourth by a low wrought-iron gate. A bench stands centre-right. Smoke is visible above the wall; the audience should understand, without being told, that something is burning in the middle distance.

The stage is lit by a single practical brazier and by a rising wash of warm amber from the east. No house-lit scene changes; the gaslight takes thirty seconds to full and another forty to extinguish.

## Characters Present

- **Helene Caldera**, in mourning dress, unbonneted
- **Pietra Caldera**, newly arrived, travel-worn
- **Nikolai**, the steward, present only in the opening three minutes
- **The Witness Chorus**, unseen, heard in the breath between lines

## Staging Notes

> The brazier is practical. The brazier is always practical. If the brazier fails, the act fails.

The actors should treat the falling ash as real. A gram falls each minute from a rigged line above the set. By the close of the act a thin grey film has settled on Helene's black sleeves.

## Key Exchange

**Pietra.** You wrote that the garden was ruined.
**Helene.** The garden is not ruined. The garden is only quiet.
**Pietra.** I do not know the difference anymore.

## End of Act

The act ends with the gate closing. The lights do not change. The audience is asked to stay seated during the first interval.
