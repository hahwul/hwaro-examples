## A shop, not a service desk

Pascal is a two-person print room on the ground floor of an old kiln building.
We run a single risograph drum set and a proofing press that predates most of
the people who use it. We do not do 10,000-unit jobs, matte lamination, or
same-day everything. We do short runs, done properly, on paper you can feel.

The work that suits us: zines, poetry pamphlets, band posters, gig flyers,
recipe cards, exhibition handouts, wedding stationery for people who hate
wedding stationery, and the occasional stubborn idea that no trade shop would
quote. If it wants texture, misregistration, and a colour that glows a little,
it belongs here.

Bring artwork as flat PDFs with the colours already split into layers, or bring
a sketch and a strong opinion. We will tell you honestly whether riso or
letterpress serves it better, what it costs, and how long the ink needs to sit
before you can stack the sheets without offsetting. No account managers, no
minimum you cannot afford.
