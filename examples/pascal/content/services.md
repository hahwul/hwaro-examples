Everything below happens in one room, on machines we maintain ourselves. That
means we can walk over mid-run and change a colour, pull a proof, or tell you a
gradient will band before you waste a hundred sheets.

## Risograph

Two-colour riso is our house speciality. The drum lays down a flat, slightly
translucent ink that overprints into a third colour where two layers meet. It
loves bold shapes, halftones, and grain; it forgives nothing that needs
photographic accuracy.

- Standard drums in stock: **Fluorescent Pink, Riso Red, Federal Blue, Sunflower, Black, Green**.
- Max sheet size: **A3**, single or double sided.
- Runs from **25 to 1,000** sheets. Below 25 it is cheaper to photocopy, and we
  will say so.

Send each colour as its own 100% black layer in a single PDF, named by ink.
There is a layer-splitting note on the [Papers](/papers/) page for the
paper-and-ink pairings that actually sing.

## Letterpress

Our proofing press handles polymer plates for deep-bite impression on cotton
stock. This is the slow work: hand-mixed ink, one colour per pass, registration
by eye. Best for cards, invitations, and covers where the dent in the paper is
half the point.

## Finishing

Scoring, folding, saddle-stitching, hand-trimming on the guillotine, and gold or
copper foil on the letterpress. We bind zines up to 48 pages in-house. Anything
thicker goes to a binder we trust two streets over.
