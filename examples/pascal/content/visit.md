The shop is a working press room, not a gallery, so it helps to know what to
expect before you turn up with a tote bag of ideas.

## Where

**Pascal Press & Riso** — Unit 4, Kiln Row, Marrow Ward. Ground floor, blue
roller door, look for the drying rack in the window. The nearest tram stop is
Marrow Cross, three minutes on foot. There is loading space out front for
fifteen minutes if you are dropping off boxes of stock.

## When

- **Wednesday to Saturday, 10:00 to 18:00** for drop-in proofs and collections.
- **Monday and Tuesday** we run booked jobs only; the door stays shut.
- Press slots are booked in two-hour blocks. Email what you want to make and we
  will hold the drum.

## Bringing artwork

Come with a print-ready PDF on a USB stick or share a link ahead of time. If you
want to proof on the day, arrive in the first hour so the ink has time to dry
before you leave.

> Bring the actual thing your print will live next to — the record sleeve, the
> book cover, the wall. Riso colour shifts against its neighbours, and a proof in
> your hand beats a swatch on a screen every time.

If you are new to riso, book a **half-hour paper-and-ink consult** first. We will
pull sample chips on three stocks so you can see how Fluorescent Pink behaves on
newsprint versus cotton rag before you commit a whole run. No charge if the job
goes ahead.
