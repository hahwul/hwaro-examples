Pascal started in 2019 when two of us pooled a redundancy cheque and a stubborn
grudge against beige print. We named the shop after Blaise Pascal, who spent his
short life proving that pressure moves through a closed system evenly — which is
either a fact about hydraulics or a decent description of a printing press,
depending on how the day is going.

## Who

**Rennie Halm** runs the risograph and mixes every letterpress ink by eye. She
learned the drum at a cooperative in the north and can tell you why your gradient
will band before you finish describing it.

**Osei Tan** keeps the presses alive, splits the artwork files, and handles the
finishing table. If a fold looks wrong, it is his fault and he will fix it while
you wait.

## What we believe

- **Short runs deserve care.** A run of fifty is not a rounding error; it is
  someone's whole idea. We treat it that way.
- **Constraint is a gift.** Two colours, one paper, a fixed sheet size. Limits
  make people braver, not poorer.
- **Nothing leaves the room half-dry.** Riso ink offsets if you stack it too
  soon, so we let sheets rest on the rack overnight rather than hand you a smudge.

We teach, too. Once a month we run a **Saturday riso clinic** for six people:
split a file, mount a drum, pull your own poster, and take home the misregistered
proofs as well as the good ones. Dates go up on the door and to the small mailing
list we actually keep short on purpose.
