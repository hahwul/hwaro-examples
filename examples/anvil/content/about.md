## The Space

The workshop is a converted garage -- 400 square feet of concrete floor, steel shelving, and decades of accumulated dust. It houses a 150-pound anvil, a coal forge, a drill press, a bandsaw, and more hand tools than any reasonable person needs.

## Philosophy

Build things that last. Use the simplest technique that works. Measure twice. Accept that the first attempt will have flaws, and that the flaws are part of the record.

Every project in this journal is documented honestly -- including the mistakes, the rework, and the parts that didn't go as planned. The goal isn't perfection. It's understanding.

## Materials

The work here focuses on three primary materials:

- **Steel** -- Mild steel, tool steel, and reclaimed stock. Forged, welded, ground, and finished by hand.
- **Wood** -- Hardwoods like oak, walnut, and ash. Shaped with hand tools and power tools alike.
- **Leather** -- Vegetable-tanned cowhide. Cut, stitched, and finished for tool rolls, sheaths, and accessories.
