Thunderbolts FC secured a crucial 3-2 victory over Phoenix Rising in a match that had everything -- early drama, a second-half comeback, and a stoppage-time winner that sent the home fans into delirium.

## First Half: Phoenix Strike First

Phoenix Rising came out with clear intent, pressing high and disrupting Thunderbolts' build-up from the opening whistle. Their pressure paid off in the 18th minute when Leo Tanaka latched onto a through ball from midfield and slotted past the keeper with composure.

The visitors doubled their advantage just before the half-hour mark. A set piece was only partially cleared, and Dimitri Volkov's strike from the edge of the box deflected off a defender and looped into the net. At 2-0, Phoenix looked firmly in control.

## Second Half: The Comeback

Thunderbolts manager Sofia Navarro made a tactical switch at halftime, bringing on an extra midfielder and shifting to a 3-4-3 formation. The change was immediate.

Kai Chen pulled one back in the 52nd minute with a header from a corner, and the momentum shifted decisively. Thunderbolts were relentless, and when Adaeze Okafor was brought down in the box on 71 minutes, she stepped up to convert the penalty herself.

## The Winner

With the match seemingly heading for a draw, Marcus Reid received the ball on the edge of the area in the third minute of stoppage time. His curling effort beat the goalkeeper at the far post, and the stadium erupted.

> "We showed incredible character tonight. Going 2-0 down at home is never easy, but this squad never gives up." -- Sofia Navarro, post-match press conference

The result moves Thunderbolts five points clear at the top of the table with eight matches remaining.
