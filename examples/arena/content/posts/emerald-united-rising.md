Few predicted Emerald United would be anywhere near the top four at this stage of the season. Promoted as runners-up last year, most analysts tipped them for a relegation fight. Instead, they sit fourth and are genuine contenders for continental qualification.

## The Foundation: Organisation

Manager Priya Sharma has built this side on a foundation of tactical discipline. Emerald concede fewer than a goal per game on average, the second-best record in the league behind Arctic Wolves. Their defensive shape out of possession is among the most compact in the division.

## Key Players

**Liam Gallagher (GK)** -- Not the most spectacular keeper, but consistently reliable. His save percentage of 78% leads all regular starters.

**Nadia Torres (CM)** -- The metronome in midfield. Torres completes more passes per 90 minutes than any other player in the league, and her ability to control tempo has been the platform for Emerald's success.

**Jamal Wright (ST)** -- A bargain signing from the lower leagues, Wright has 12 goals this season and leads the line with intelligent movement and clinical finishing.

## What Comes Next

The run-in is kind to Emerald United. Four of their remaining eight matches are at home, and they avoid the top three entirely until the final day of the season. If they maintain their current form -- averaging 1.95 points per game -- they are on course for 72 points, historically enough for a top-four finish.

> "Nobody gave us a chance at the start of the season. That's fine. We just keep working." -- Priya Sharma
