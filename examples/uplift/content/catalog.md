Every title here passed the same test before it was acquired: the author had to be willing, and reasonably able, to read the whole book aloud themselves. Some spend a week in a professional booth. Others — Odile Ferrand, notably — insisted on recording at home, in the room where the book was written, on the theory that the room's particular quiet belongs in the file.

<!-- more -->

We don't re-cast a book if the author's read is uneven. A stammer on a hard paragraph, a laugh that breaks the fourth wall, a long pause before a name that still costs something to say — we leave it in. That is the whole premise of the catalog: not performance, testimony.

Below is everything currently available, sorted by newest release first. Runtimes are the finished audio length; chapter counts match the dashes under each cover, and the first one or two dashes are always free to sample before you buy.
