A professional audiobook narrator is a skill in itself, and most of the industry runs on people who are extremely good at inhabiting someone else's sentences. Uplift runs on the opposite skill: authors who already know exactly where their own sentences want to breathe, because they built them that way, word by word, over months or years.

<!-- more -->

That means our narrators are not always the smoothest read in the room. Priya Anand Vance talks faster when she's near a punchline. Marcus Odell drops half an octave on hard paragraphs, the way he used to on actual collection calls. None of that gets smoothed out in post — we mix for clarity, not for consistency with a studio standard.

Every narrator below wrote the book they're reading. Follow their name to hear the title, or read on for a short account, in their own words, of why they didn't hand the job to someone else.
