Uplift started in 2024 with a complaint, not a business plan. Our founder had just finished listening to her own memoir on audio, professionally narrated, and couldn't recognize her own sentences in someone else's mouth — not because the narrator did anything wrong, but because a stranger's competence had ironed out every deliberate rough edge she'd spent a year putting in.

<!-- more -->

So the rule became the whole product: if you can't or won't read your own book aloud, Uplift won't publish the audio edition. That has turned away more manuscripts than you'd expect — recording a full-length book is genuinely hard, and not every author who can write one can sit in a booth for six hours and read it back convincingly. The ones who can produce something a hired narrator, however good, cannot: a reading with the author's actual doubts still audible in it.

We release one new title every Friday, at 6 a.m. in the catalog's local time, which is currently Central European. There is no algorithmic recommendation engine deciding what you hear about next — just a small editorial team, a release calendar, and the belief that a book read by the person who wrote it is a different object than the same book read by anyone else, however good they are at reading.

## How a Friday release gets made

Manuscripts arrive from agents or directly from authors roughly four months before release. Editorial and production run in parallel: a developmental pass on the text alongside two or three studio sessions, usually in the author's home city rather than ours, so the room stays familiar. Mixing and mastering take the final three weeks. Nothing skips the queue, including titles from authors who have published with us before.

## Where to start

New to the catalog, start with whichever runtime fits your week: *Six Ways to Leave a Room* is under four hours, and *The Cartographer's Daughter* is closer to eleven. Both were read by the person who lived the material, which is the only thing every title on Uplift has in common — browse the full shelf on the [catalog page](/catalog/), or read about the people doing the reading on [narrators](/narrators/).
