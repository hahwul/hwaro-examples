<div class="content-block">
  <h1>A Legacy of Complexity</h1>
  <p>
    Founded in the late 20th century, Deconstructed was born out of a desire to 
    challenge the "International Style" that had dominated urban planning for 
    decades. We draw inspiration from the works of Zaha Hadid, Frank Gehry, and 
    Daniel Libeskind, continuing their legacy by pushing the boundaries of what 
    is structurally possible and socially meaningful.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="overlapping-block" style="background: var(--text); color: var(--bg);">
  <h2>Our Team</h2>
  <p>
    Our studio brings together expertise in computational design, structural 
    engineering, and social anthropology. Every project is a research-led 
    inquiry into the nature of the site and the needs of its future inhabitants.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="content-block">
  <h2>Our Process</h2>
  <p>
    We don't start with a sketch; we start with a set of constraints. By 
    intentionally disrupting these constraints, we arrive at forms that 
    are both functionally sound and aesthetically radical.
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Deconstruct traditional program requirements.</li>
    <li>Apply non-Euclidean spatial logic.</li>
    <li>Utilize advanced structural modeling for fabrication.</li>
    <li>Integrate architectural drawing lines into the final aesthetic.</li>
  </ul>
</div>
