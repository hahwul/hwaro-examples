In the realm of high fashion, the human body is no longer merely a hanger for fabric; it has become an architectural axis. Contemporary designers are increasingly discarding the organic contour in favor of sharp, geometric abstractions.

> "A silhouette should be a sculpture that moves."

### The Linear Outline
By utilizing heavily structured materials—such as double-faced neoprene, boiled wool, and bonded cotton—designers can construct garments that retain their own shape independently of the wearer. This shifts the focus from body shape to sculptural form. 

Stark black lines define edges that slice through three-dimensional space, transforming a simple walk down the runway into a sequence of shifting planar compositions.
