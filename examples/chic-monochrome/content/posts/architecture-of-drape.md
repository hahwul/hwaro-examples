Draping is perhaps the most ancient form of garment construction. From the Greek chiton to the Indian sari, it relies on the natural fall of fabric rather than complex patterns and seams.

### The Modernist Reinterpretation
When draping is rendered entirely in absolute black or clinical white, its shadows become deep, high-contrast crevices. Designers are now using laser-cut synthetic blends to create pleats that never lose their crispness, resulting in drapes that appear cast in marble or molded in plaster.

This fusion of classic elegance with space-age precision represents the pinnacle of modern minimalist couture.
