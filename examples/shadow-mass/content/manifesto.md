## I. Mass over ornament

Every block on the page has a reason to exist. If a rule, a border, or a margin can be removed without losing meaning, it is removed before the press runs.

## II. One ink, one accent

We commit to a single ink color and a single accent. No gradients, no shadows, no faux glass. A page is a sheet, not a window.

## III. Type as architecture

We treat headlines as load-bearing walls. They are heavy because they carry weight. They are loud because the room demands it.

## IV. Speed is the only animation

We do not animate decoration. Our only motion is the jump cut from one solid state to the next. If a transition takes more than the time of a blink, it does not earn its keep.

## V. No popups, ever

There is no modal demanding your email. There is no banner mourning your cookies. The text is the offer.

## VI. Print arrives all at once

A page is delivered like a sheet of paper — fully composed, no skeletons, no spinners. If you see a loading state on this site, the press is broken.

## VII. Color carries meaning

Sulfur yellow is reserved for emphasis the page itself cannot ignore. Blood red is reserved for errors. Mute grey is reserved for the things the eye should pass over. Color is a verb, not a decoration.

## VIII. Margins are weapons

A wide margin is a refusal. It says: the page does not need to be full. The page can breathe even when the type is heavy.

## IX. Anonymous readers are still readers

We track nothing. We measure nothing. We do not need to know if you finished the article — you know.

## X. Static is sufficient

Every page is a flat file. Every link is a hard link. We build with Hwaro because the simplest engine is the strongest one.

## XI. We do not chase the algorithm

The algorithm is not our editor. The reader is. We will never optimize a headline for a machine that does not read.

## XII. The press continues

If this manifesto is still legible to you in ten years, we did our job. If it is forgotten, we did our job. Either way, the press continues.
