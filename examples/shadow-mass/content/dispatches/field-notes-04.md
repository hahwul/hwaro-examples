Field notes from the editor's desk. Books we have on the table, recommended in no particular order.

## Books

1. **Towards a New Architecture** — Le Corbusier (1923). Still the founding document of the modern poster. Read it for its furious clarity, not its conclusions.

2. **Programming Type** — Karen Cheng (2005). The grammar of letterforms. Reading it changed the way we thought about the gap between an `n` and an `h`.

3. **The Form of the Book** — Jan Tschichold (1975). A reactionary text, written by a man who had abandoned modernism. Still the best argument for the centered title page we have read.

4. **Notes on the Synthesis of Form** — Christopher Alexander (1964). Not about design, technically. About how problems decompose into sub-problems. Required reading for anyone laying out a complex page.

5. **Concrete: The Building Material** — Adrian Forty (2012). A history of the most maligned and most consequential building material of the twentieth century. We borrow more from architecture than from graphic design.

6. **The Anti-Aesthetic** — Hal Foster, ed. (1983). A collection of essays on postmodern critique. We disagree with most of it. We think about it constantly.

## Pamphlets

- **Massin and the Theatre of the Page** — a slim Pompidou catalogue we found in a Paris bookshop in 2019. Out of print, worth tracking down.
- **Wolfgang Weingart: My Way to Typography** — too expensive new. Find it used.

## And one bus ticket

A receipt from a Berlin night bus, kept on the wall above the editor's desk. We do not know what it means. We refuse to throw it away.
