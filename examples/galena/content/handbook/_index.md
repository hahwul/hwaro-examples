Six chapters, meant to be read in order. The first two establish the scheduling model and the cron grammar Galena accepts. The middle three cover the features that separate Galena from a plain cron daemon: jittered fire times, backfill runs, and dead-letter queues. The last chapter is for whoever carries the pager.

Every chapter opens with the cron expression that best captures its subject. Hover any field to see which of the five positions it is.
