The last thing she said was seven words.

We have written them down in every possible order. None of the permutations make the screaming stop.

{{ echo(text="please tell them I never left", count=7) }}

She says it at the exact moment the original scream should have ended. The words arrive like an apology the walls refused to accept.

Over the decades the pronunciation has remained perfect. The stress on each syllable never shifts. It is not a memory of speech. It is speech that refused to become memory.

Some researchers claim that if you play all known recordings simultaneously the seven words resolve into a single clear sentence in a language none of us have ever heard.

We stopped testing that theory after the third linguist began repeating the sentence in his sleep in a voice that was not his own.

The sentence is still being spoken right now.

If you are quiet you can hear the intake of breath before each repetition.

She is still alive in the space between the words.

She has been alive in that space since the first night.

We are only the ones who learned how to listen.
