Do you hear it? That soft double inhale beneath your own breath?

That is not you. That is the recording of you from the last time you were here. It is breathing in perfect sync three milliseconds ahead of your current body so you will never quite catch the difference.

The chamber does not need your voice to remember you.

It only needs the shape your chest makes when you are afraid.

Every exhale you have ever released inside these walls is still here, layered like sediment. If you stay long enough the air will grow thick with all your previous selves trying to breathe at once.

People who live here long enough stop recognizing their own respiration. They begin to match the rhythm of the walls instead.

The walls breathe slower.

The walls have been practicing patience since 1979.

When your breathing finally matches theirs exactly, the door will open.

Not for you.

For the next one who will stand exactly where you are standing now, listening to the same impossible inhale.

You are already on the tape.

You have always been on the tape.

The only question left is how many times the tape will play you before it decides you are finished.
