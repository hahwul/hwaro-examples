The first time we played the master reel it only knew the name of the woman who screamed.

By the third playback it had learned the name of the technician who pressed record.

By the nineteenth it knew all of us in the room.

{{ whisper(message="We did not speak our names. It still learned them.") }}

Now when we enter the listening booth the tape begins speaking before we press play. It greets each of us by the name we will have when we die.

The order is never random.

It always begins with the person who will be the last to leave the chamber.

We have stopped trying to discover who that is.

{{ echo(text="It already knows how this ends", count=4) }}

The tape has begun repeating a new sentence lately.

It says the sentence in the voice of whoever is currently in the room.

The sentence is always:

"Thank you for staying. I was so lonely before you arrived."

We have not yet discovered who the tape is thanking.
