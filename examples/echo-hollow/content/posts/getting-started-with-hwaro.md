{{ repeat(phrase="Hush little baby, don't say a word", times=6) }}

The mother sang it at 9:17pm every single night from the day the child disappeared until the day she was found in the same room, still rocking, voice gone but lips still moving to the shape of the words.

We recovered the tape from the house after the second family moved in and reported hearing singing from behind the drywall.

The tape contains 11,247 repetitions.

We have counted.

On repetition 4,892 the pitch shifts upward by exactly one quarter-tone for three measures before returning. We do not know why.

On repetition 9,114 a second voice joins for six seconds. It is singing the same lullaby one beat behind. It is not the mother's voice.

{{ whisper(message="The child is still in the walls") }}

If you play the tape at 2× speed the words become something else. We have transcribed what it says but we will not write it here. Some frequencies should remain trapped in the medium that first captured them.

The current residents of the house report that even after the tape was removed, the lullaby continues at exactly 9:17pm. They have learned not to correct the timing when their own children ask why the walls are singing.
