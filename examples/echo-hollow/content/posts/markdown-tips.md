At 2:14:03 every night something walks the length of the corridor outside room 17.

The footsteps are heavy but the floorboards do not creak. They stopped creaking in 1981.

At 2:14:11 the handle turns.

At 2:14:14 the door opens exactly six inches. No more. No less.

The sentence is always spoken in your own voice. Even the first time you hear it.

We have placed microphones. We have placed cameras. We have placed people who swore they would stay awake.

None of them ever remember leaving the room.

The tape from 1994 ends at 2:14:47.

Every subsequent recording of that same night ends at 2:14:47.

The thing in the doorway has learned to reach the microphone before the tape can capture what happens next.

If you are reading this after midnight, do not turn around to check the door.

It opened six inches three seconds ago. It always does.
