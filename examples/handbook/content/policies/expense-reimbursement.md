# Expense Reimbursement

Employees who incur out-of-pocket costs for approved business activities can request reimbursement through the standard finance workflow. This page describes what is reimbursable, the documentation required, and the timing of payments.

## Reimbursable Categories

The following categories are reimbursable when they support a documented business purpose:

*   **Travel.** Airfare in economy class, ground transportation, lodging at the standard nightly rate, and meals within the daily allowance.
*   **Equipment.** Pre-approved peripherals or office supplies that are not provided through the central procurement channel.
*   **Training.** Conference fees, online course tuition, and books that are directly related to the employee's current role.
*   **Client engagement.** Reasonable meal and venue costs incurred while hosting external participants.

Items that fall outside these categories require written approval from a department head before purchase.

## Submission Process

All requests are submitted in the expense system within thirty days of the transaction. Each line item must include:

1.  A clear description of the business purpose.
2.  An itemized receipt or invoice in PDF or image format.
3.  The associated project code, if applicable.

Requests submitted without receipts are returned to the employee for correction.

## Review and Payment

Finance reviews submitted requests on a weekly cycle. Approved reimbursements are issued through the next regular payroll run. Rejected items receive a written explanation, and the employee may appeal to their department head within fourteen days.

Receipts and supporting records are retained for seven years to satisfy audit requirements. Employees should keep their own copies for at least one year.
