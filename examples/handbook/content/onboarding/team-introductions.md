# Team Introductions and Stakeholders

The first two weeks are reserved for structured introductions. Your manager will schedule short one-to-one sessions with the people you will collaborate with most often. The goal is to give you a working map of the organization before you take on independent assignments.

## Recommended Format

Each meeting is thirty minutes. Use the time to cover three areas:

1.  **Scope of work.** Ask the other person to describe their current responsibilities and the projects they own.
2.  **Points of contact.** Note when their team is the right escalation path and when another team should be approached first.
3.  **Working preferences.** Document whether they prefer asynchronous messages, scheduled calls, or shared documents for review.

A short written summary after each meeting is encouraged. Most new joiners keep these notes in a personal handbook page that is shared only with their manager.

## Cross-Functional Touchpoints

Beyond your direct team, plan introductions with at least one person from:

*   **Engineering operations.** They handle deployment timelines and release windows.
*   **Finance.** They review budget requests and procurement.
*   **Legal.** They coordinate vendor agreements and compliance reviews.

## Closing the Loop

At the end of week two, your manager will review your contact map with you. Any gaps will be filled before you begin your first independent deliverable. Persistent confusion about ownership is the most common cause of delivery slippage in the first quarter, and a complete contact map is the simplest preventive measure.
