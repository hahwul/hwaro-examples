We did not discover the temple. We became it.

Each pillar is a femur straightened by will. The altar is a sternum cracked open and re-set. The roof is woven from hair that never grew back.

{{ sigil(name="azathoth") }}

When the last stone was placed we realized the scaffolding was gone. There was no way down. Only further inward.

The mortar is not lime. The mortar is memory pressed until it forgets mercy.

We built it because the world outside refused to hold our breaking. So we gave the breaking a roof. We gave it walls thick enough that even god could not hear us scream.

Now the temple breathes. It has learned the rhythm of our oldest fear.

And every night the ribs sing in the wind.
