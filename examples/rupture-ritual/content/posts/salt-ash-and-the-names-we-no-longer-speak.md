We do not speak the old names anymore.

Not because we forgot them. Because they remember us too well.

{{ sigil(name="triangle") }}

Salt was poured first — white as bone, sharp as the last honest sentence. Then the fire. Then the careful scattering of what remained so that nothing could ever grow in that place again.

The names were not evil. They were simply true in a way that made living unbearable.

Now the only language left is the one made of absences. The pause between two heartbeats. The space where a word used to live.

When you stand on that ground at night you can still hear them. Not as voices. As pressure behind the eyes. As the sudden taste of metal.

We keep the salt in our pockets like rosaries.
