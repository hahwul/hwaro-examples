The knife does not negotiate.

It arrives already inside the skin. Already past the first layer of refusal. You will feel the cold only after the heat begins to pour.

{{ bloodline() }}

Every covenant begins with a violation that we later call destiny. The wound is not the price — it is the proof.

We spend lifetimes learning how to flinch. The knife teaches the opposite motion: to open.

Do not ask what it wants. It wants what you have already given without knowing you gave it.

There is no clean break. There is only the moment the steel decides you are ready and the slow realization that you have always been waiting for it.

The knife keeps no ledger. It does not remember your name. It only remembers the shape it left behind.
