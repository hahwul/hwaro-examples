Building highly complex systems that can successfully withstand the test of time, unexpected traffic surges, and inevitable hardware failures requires a foundational shift in engineering culture and architectural design. This in-depth talk covers the core tenets of modern reliability engineering, focusing on the practical application of chaos engineering, comprehensive observability, and the fundamental principles of graceful degradation.

Attendees will learn how to proactively design architectures that not only anticipate failure but also recover autonomously, thereby minimizing downtime and mitigating negative user impact. We will explore advanced deployment strategies, automated rollback mechanisms, and the importance of establishing robust service level objectives (SLOs). Through detailed technical case studies, we will dissect historical system outages, identifying the root causes and the architectural changes implemented to prevent recurrence. This session is designed for engineers and operations professionals who are tasked with maintaining mission-critical infrastructure and who wish to cultivate a culture of continuous improvement and uncompromising reliability within their organizations.
