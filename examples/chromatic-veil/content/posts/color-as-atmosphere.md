# Weather Over Wallpaper

When I started building interfaces I treated color the way a child treats stickers. Pick the brightest one, place it where it draws the most attention, repeat. The result was loud rooms with nowhere to sit.

The shift happened slowly. I began to think of color the way a landscape painter thinks of light. Not a label applied to an object, but a condition the object exists within. A red button is not red. A red button is a red button at noon, in a humid summer, with the curtains half drawn.

## Practical Notes

- Pick a single ambient hue and let it bleed into every surface, even the ones you would normally paint white.
- Reserve high saturation for the smallest possible elements. A pinprick of pure color does more work than a full panel.
- Allow the page to feel cold or warm. Neutrality reads as indecision.

## On Restraint

The hardest part is leaving things alone. A page with three colors looks intentional. A page with seven looks anxious. When in doubt, remove one.

The veil is the point. Let it hang.
