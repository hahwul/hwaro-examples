# Reading Through Glass

Frosted surfaces are easy to admire and difficult to use. The trouble is not the blur itself, but what sits beneath it. Place a frosted panel above busy content and the eye loses purchase. Place it above a calm field and the panel feels weightless.

I keep three rules taped to the wall above my desk.

## Rules of Thumb

1. Never stack more than two translucent layers. The third surface always looks like a mistake.
2. Reserve the strongest blur for the topmost layer. Lower layers should hint, not assert.
3. Borders matter. A single hairline rule along the edge tells the eye where one plane ends and the next begins.

## Notes from the Field

The best examples I have seen this year all share one trait: the designers were willing to be boring underneath. A loud background eats every ounce of subtlety the frosted layer offers. A quiet background gives the layer somewhere to breathe.

Save the drama for the type. Let the surfaces be still.
