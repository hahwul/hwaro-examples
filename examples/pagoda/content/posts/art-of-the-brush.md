In the stroke of a brush, there is more than just ink. There is the breath, the intent, and the silence. Traditional ink and wash painting, often referred to as Sumi-e, is an art form that captures the essence of a subject with minimal strokes.

It is not about realism, but about the spirit. The interaction between the ink and the Hanji (traditional paper) is a dance of spontaneity and control.

As you scroll through this digital space, imagine the slow unfolding of a traditional handscroll.
