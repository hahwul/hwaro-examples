Propel Mail Inc. started in 2023 with a stopwatch, not a mockup. Our co-founders, Iris Nakamura and Teodor Vasquez, spent a week timing how long it actually took to clear a hundred-message inbox in the email clients they used at their old jobs — mouse in hand, hovering over archive buttons, waiting for hover states to confirm a click had registered. The number that stuck with them was twenty-six minutes. Twenty-six minutes to make a hundred small decisions that, individually, took less than a second each. The gap wasn't decision-making. It was travel time — the mouse moving back and forth between the message and the button meant to act on it.

Propel is the answer to that gap. Every interaction we design starts from the same question: what is the shortest keyboard path from "message arrives" to "message handled," and can we make that path memorable in under a week of use? Most of the time the answer is a single letter key, chosen for how it reads rather than how it looks on a cheat sheet.

## How we work

The team is deliberately small — nineteen people as of this year, split evenly between engineering and a support group that answers every ticket personally rather than routing through a bot first. We ship weekly, in public, with a changelog that reads more like a diary than a press release. If a shortcut we shipped turns out to fight muscle memory instead of building it, we hear about it fast and we fix it faster.

## Where we're based

Propel Mail Inc. is headquartered in Porto, Portugal, with a second small studio in Halifax, Nova Scotia, keeping support coverage close to both European and North American business hours without requiring anyone to work a genuine night shift.
