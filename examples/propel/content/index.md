## How triage works

Propel tracks the row under your cursor, not the row you clicked last. Every key acts on it immediately, and the cursor advances on its own, so a full pass never touches the mouse.

1. **Land on a message.** Arrow keys or `j` / `k` move the cursor down the list. Nothing opens until you tell it to.
2. **Decide in one key.** `e` archives, `h` snoozes, `r` opens a reply, `⌫` deletes. None of them ask you to confirm.
3. **Move on automatically.** The cursor steps to the next unread thread the instant the last one clears, so momentum never breaks.

Most Propel users clear a hundred-message backlog in under ten minutes once the habit sets in. The trick isn't reading faster — it's never re-reading the same subject line twice while deciding what to do with it.

## Day mode, night mode, your call

The toggle in the header does more than swap colors. It mirrors the reading mode built into the Propel client itself: night mode dims message backgrounds and holds the accent color steady, so switching between a terminal at 11 p.m. and an inbox doesn't mean re-adjusting your eyes twice. Whatever you pick here previews exactly what opening the app will look like.
