## About this Lot

Four copies, all of them publisher's overstock from the 2023 first edition, in as-new condition. The jackets are crisp; the boards beneath unmarked; the interiors unopened save by ourselves to check. The remainder mark to the top edge is the only indication that the books have had an existence outside a warehouse.

Dandelot's book is a no-nonsense cookery handbook organised not by course or occasion but by the condition of the cook - the tired, the hungry, the alone, the entertaining, the short of time, the short of ingredients. Each section opens with a short essay upon the particular condition and proceeds through eight or twelve recipes appropriate to it.

## What is Inside

Recipes are written in the old English manner of continuous prose rather than bullet lists, and are generally brief - four to eight lines apiece, with quantities expressed in ordinary measures (a handful, a tablespoon, a dash) rather than metric precision. The book presumes a basic competence and does not offer hand-holding.

> Dandelot does not condescend and does not over-explain. The section on cooking for oneself, when tired, is the finest of its kind since Elizabeth David's corresponding chapter in French Provincial Cooking.

> Best used as a day-to-day reference rather than a from-cover-to-cover read. Keep a copy in the kitchen and let it open to whichever page presents itself.

## Why it is in the Bin

The book was over-printed; the publisher planned a run of six thousand against a first-month order book of two thousand, and the rest gathered in the warehouse for the better part of a year before being remaindered. The reviews were excellent but the marketing was modest, and the book simply did not find its shelf at full price. At seven pounds fifty it will move.
