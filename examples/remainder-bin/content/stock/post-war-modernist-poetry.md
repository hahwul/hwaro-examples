## About this Lot

Three copies arrived this week, all of them from the publisher's overstock rather than bookshop returns. The jackets show very minor shelf-wear at the head and tail; the boards beneath are in excellent condition; the interiors are entirely clean and unmarked.

The anthology gathers poems from forty-four writers in English, German, Italian, and Polish (the latter two in parallel translation) across the decades from 1946 to 1972. The editor has supplied biographical notes upon each poet, and an opening essay upon the conditions under which post-war European poetry came to prominence.

## Contents in Brief

The volume is divided into four principal sections: the elegiac, occupying the first third of the volume; the political, occupying the second; the experimental, occupying the third; and a final section of shorter lyric which the editor has placed last as an intentional quieting.

> The editor's hand has been light with respect to the Italian poets, who are perhaps not quite so well represented as their weight in the period would merit, but he has been generous with the translations of the Polish poets, whose work is here for the first time available in English without recourse to the German intermediary editions.

> The selection from Celan is particularly strong and makes this volume worth its full list price even before the bin marking.

## Why it is in the Bin

The book was published in a first run of four thousand copies, of which approximately six hundred remained unsold after three years upon the publisher's backlist. These six hundred were remaindered at twenty-five per cent of the trade price; we took three copies at the trade remainder rate and priced them at six pounds to move.

There is nothing wrong with the book. It simply did not find its audience in the ordinary course of bookselling. That audience may yet come by our shop.
