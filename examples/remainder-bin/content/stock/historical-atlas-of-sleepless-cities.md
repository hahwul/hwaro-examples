## About this Lot

Two copies, both in slipcases which show light bumping at the top corners but are otherwise clean. The boards beneath are unblemished. The fold-out maps - the principal attraction of the volume - are in pristine condition, having never been opened more than once per copy.

Hollinghead's atlas traces the development of twenty-four cities which, for reasons of industry, commerce, ritual, or illumination, have operated through the night hours as well as the day: Kyoto, Naples, Manchester, Saint Petersburg, Las Vegas, and others. Each city is treated to a dozen pages of text and five or six maps at varying scales, from the entire metropolitan region down to the individual block.

## On the Cartography

The maps were drawn by Isobel Tracy, whose cartographic work has appeared in the Penguin historical atlases of the last fifteen years. The drafting is clean and restrained; the colour palette limited to four inks and their overlays; the typography set in a revised Monotype Bell from which the antique-serif baseline roughness has been removed. The whole effect is of a book printed with exacting care.

> Hollinghead's text is not the equal of Tracy's maps, alas. The chapters upon Manchester and Las Vegas in particular depend too heavily upon secondary sources and the author's enthusiasms rather than primary research. But the maps alone are worth the sticker.

> Note: the Kyoto fold-out at page 94 has been printed with a minor registration error in the red plate upon approximately one per cent of the print run. Our two copies appear to be correctly registered.

## Why it is in the Bin

The Lambent House was a small press operating from Bristol which closed in 2023 after the death of its founding publisher. The stock was dispersed by the executors of the estate to the trade; we acquired a lot of twelve atlases at auction and have sold ten. The remaining two are marked to twelve pounds.
