## About this Copy

A single copy, rear-boarded only (no jacket survives). The rear board shows a clear corner bruise of approximately ten millimetres where the book was dropped upon its edge prior to entering our stock. The corner has not split; the cloth is rubbed but intact. All plates are perfectly clean.

The Pilot & Cutter Press field guides are among the finest natural history reference works published in the last decade, distinguished by the accuracy of their colour plates (printed at the Frome works upon coated matt stock) and by the rigour of their identification keys. The Mollusca volume was issued in 2019 at thirty-eight pounds; the press discontinued the series in 2023 when its principal editor retired, and the remaining copies were remaindered.

## What is Inside

The volume covers approximately two hundred and ninety species of British coastal mollusc, with full colour plates of each, habitat notes, distribution maps at a county level, and an opening key which leads the user from shell shape to family in not more than eight dichotomous choices.

> Wallis's key is considerably less frustrating than the equivalent key in Pelseneer's Handbook, which is the book this volume was designed to replace. The plates are also better.

> The distribution maps were compiled from the National Biodiversity Network data to 2018 and should be cross-referenced against the current NBN for any recent records.

## Why it is in the Bin

The series was discontinued. This copy was part of a lot of fifteen we acquired at auction from the Pilot & Cutter warehouse disposal of 2024; the other fourteen have sold, this is the last. The corner bruise means it will not suit a collector, but it will suit the practical field naturalist admirably.
