A quire, in the bindery sense, is a set of sheets folded together into a
single signature before they're sewn into a book — four sheets folded once
make an octavo quire of eight leaves. It's a unit of **making**, not just of
paper, which is why it seemed like the right name for a book about the craft
underneath the words rather than the words themselves.

This page is the back matter every printed book used to carry as a matter of
course: a plain account of how the thing in your hands was actually made.

## Typefaces

Display type — chapter titles, the specimen labels, this book's own name on
the cover — is set in **Spectral**, a serif drawn for long-form screen
reading, used here at weights 500 through 700 with an italic reserved for the
one flourish described in the restraint chapter.

Running text is set in **IBM Plex Sans**, chosen for the same reason most of
this book's rules exist: it holds up at small sizes without special pleading,
and its numerals stay legible in the specimen captions where a lot of this
book's actual argument lives.

## Palette

Six colors, none of them decorative: a warm paper background, a slightly
deeper surface tone for specimen boxes and code, a near-black foreground, a
muted brown-grey for captions and metadata, and one red accent — the color of
a proofreader's pencil — spent only on links and rules, per the rule laid out
in [Weight](/chapters/weight/) and enforced in full in [Restraint](/chapters/restraint/).

## The pica ruler

Every specimen box in this book is ruled the way a compositor would mark a
galley: a strip along the top edge ticked in inches, with a single accent
bracket sized in picas under the sample it's measuring. A pica here is held
at a clean 16px — twelve points, six to the inch, the same conversion a
Monotype caster used before pixels existed at all. The bracket isn't
decoration; it's the actual width of the box below it, stated as a number so
the claim can be checked rather than taken on faith.

## Printing history

Drafted and set between January and August 2025, with the screens chapter
finished last, once the rest of the book's own rules had been tested against
a phone screen in direct sun. This edition is maintained as a living
reference rather than a fixed printing — chapters are corrected in place when
the working advice in them changes.
