Every screen is a composing stick now — a phone, a laptop lid, a car dashboard
waiting at a red light. The rules that made pages readable when they were cut
from lead haven't gone anywhere; they've just lost their compositor. This book
puts the compositor back. Six short chapters, each one a single working habit:
set your measure before you set your mood, give lines room to breathe, let
scale do the arguing that boldface shouldn't have to, and spend your one
accent color like it's rationed — because on the page you're about to read, it is.

Start with [Measure](/chapters/measure/) if you're reading straight through,
or jump to [the colophon](/colophon/) first if you want to know how this
book itself is set before you trust its advice.

None of this is theory for its own sake. Every chapter carries at least one
specimen box, ruled in picas the way a compositor would mark a galley, so you
can see the measurement before you take our word for it — a habit that starts
right here, on the cover.
