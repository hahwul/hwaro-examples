The largest snake plant had cracked its plastic pot along the seam, so I moved it into a 25 cm terracotta planter today. Two thick rhizomes were already pressing against the wall, and a smaller offset had pushed itself almost out of the soil.

I divided one rhizome cluster while it was out of the pot and set the offset aside in a separate container. The cut surface should callus over a few days before the first watering. Soil mix was two parts cactus blend, one part perlite, and a thin layer of pumice at the bottom for drainage.

No watering for at least a week. The leaves looked firm, so the roots have plenty of stored moisture to ride out the transition. Will check next weekend for any signs of wilting at the base of the cut.
