Six species, one notebook, no shortcuts. Each entry below follows the same shape: a cap-size range and habitat note drawn from real specimens, a spore print colour sampled from a dried print rather than a book plate, and — where one exists — the look-alike responsible for the most emergency-room calls involving that shape of mushroom. Entries are sorted alphabetically by common name rather than by date or danger, on the theory that a guide you can't find a page in quickly is a guide you'll stop opening in the field.

Three of the six are considered good eating once positively identified. Three are not, for reasons ranging from "merely too tough to bother with" to "will fail your liver within a week." The corner badge tells you which is which at a glance; the entry itself tells you why, and what to check before you trust the badge over your own specimen. New to the guide? Start with [how this guide is compiled](@/about.md) before you start with the mushrooms.
