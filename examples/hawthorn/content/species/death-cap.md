This entry exists because it has to. The death cap is responsible for the large majority of fatal mushroom poisonings recorded worldwide, and it grows in exactly the kind of mixed broadleaf woodland this guide covers. It is not rare, it is not exotic, and it does not taste bad — survivors consistently describe it as pleasant. None of that helps once the amatoxins it contains have been absorbed.

## Identification

The cap is 5 to 15 cm across, domed when young and flattening with age, in a colour that ranges from olive-green through yellowish-green to, occasionally, almost white — colour alone is not a safe exclusion test. The gills are free from the stem, crowded, and white, never yellow or brown. The stem is white to pale olive, often faintly patterned, and carries two features that matter more than colour: a skirt-like ring high on the stem, and a swollen, cup-like volva at the base, usually buried in soil or leaf litter and easy to miss if a specimen is pulled rather than dug.

## Habitat & Season

Like the chanterelle, the death cap is mycorrhizal and partners with oak and beech, which puts it in the same general habitat as several prized edibles — a large part of why it accounts for so many accidental poisonings. It fruits from late summer into autumn, often singly or in small, loose groups at the edge of woodland rather than deep within it.

## Look-alikes

Early symptoms of amatoxin poisoning — vomiting, cramping, watery diarrhoea — typically begin six to twelve hours after eating, well after the meal has been digested, and are sometimes followed by a deceptive period of apparent recovery before liver and kidney failure set in. Anyone who has eaten a wild mushroom and develops delayed gastrointestinal symptoms should seek emergency care immediately and bring a specimen or photograph if at all possible.

## Why It Stays in the Ground

There is no reliable field test that makes a death cap safe, no amount of cooking that destroys amatoxins, and no dose small enough to be casual about. This entry's only job is to make the cap, the ring, the volva, and the white spore print recognisable enough that you never have to find out the rest firsthand.
