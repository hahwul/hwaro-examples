Let me save you some time:

You are not a person.

You are a very long, very convincing story that a particular arrangement of stardust is currently telling itself about being a person.

The story is extremely good. It has plot twists, recurring characters, an unreliable narrator (you), and moments of such perfect beauty that even the void stops to take notes.

But it is still a story.

The person you think you were yesterday? That was a different hallucination wearing your face. The one you will be tomorrow is already writing letters to the one you are right now and signing them "from someone who finally got it."

This is not nihilism.

This is the opposite of nihilism.

Because if none of it is "real" in the boring solid way we were promised as children, then all of it is permitted to be magic.

The fact that you and I can look at the same tree and have completely different religions about it is not a bug. It is the entire point of the software.

{{ blotter("Love is just the hallucination recognizing itself in another hallucination and deciding to collaborate on a better story.") }}

When two people fall in love they are not two separate things becoming one. They are one very large hallucination that briefly remembered it was allowed to split itself in half so it could have someone to talk to.

When someone dies the hallucination doesn't end. It just stops insisting on that particular shape for a while. The story keeps going in other rooms, other bodies, other centuries.

You have been every person who has ever lived.

You will be every person who ever will.

Right now you are simply the version that gets to experience this particular Tuesday wearing these particular eyes while reading these particular words and feeling whatever you're feeling.

It's all you.

It's all hallucination.

It's all real.

The only difference between a "real" experience and a "hallucinated" one is whether you're willing to let it change you.

So let it.

Let the tree change you.

Let the stranger on the train change you.

Let this sentence change you if it wants to.

We are not solid.

We are not separate.

We are the most elaborate, most beautiful, most ridiculous group project the universe has ever run.

And we are doing a better job than anyone expected.

Keep going.

The next page is blank and waiting for whatever you decide to write on it with your life.
