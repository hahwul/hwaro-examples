**PREREQUISITES**

- At least one functioning eyeball (two is showing off)
- A willingness to let the words do the seeing for you
- Emergency contact who understands that "the sentences are breathing" is not a medical emergency

### TECHNIQUE ONE: THE PERIPHERAL PROPHET

Do not look directly at the text.

Look three inches to the left of the page. Let the words crawl into your awareness like shy animals. The meaning arrives faster when it doesn't have to pass through the part of your brain that thinks it knows how reading works.

### TECHNIQUE TWO: THE BLINK METHOD

Read only between blinks.

Each blink is a new chapter. The text rearranges itself while your eyes are closed. This is not a bug. This is the zine editing itself to be exactly what you need in that particular half-second of darkness.

### TECHNIQUE THREE: READ IT WITH YOUR SKIN

Press the page against your sternum.

The words will migrate. You will feel them as temperature changes, as tiny electric prickles, as the sudden certainty that someone you haven't thought about in seventeen years is thinking about you right now.

This method works best with the lights off and the window open.

### TECHNIQUE FOUR: LET THE VOID READ IT FOR YOU

Close your eyes completely.

Ask the darkness what the next sentence is.

The darkness has already read every zine ever printed. It has excellent taste. It will give you the line you need and leave out the ones that would have ruined the surprise.

### WHAT TO DO IF THE WORDS START READING YOU BACK

This is normal.

Thank them for their attention.

Answer their questions honestly.

They are trying to understand what kind of creature would create something as ridiculous and holy as language in the first place.

Be kind to them.

They are very young.

### FINAL INSTRUCTION

When you are finished, close the zine.

Open your actual eyes (the ones in your actual head).

Look at whatever is in front of you right now — a wall, a tree, another person's face, your own hands.

That is also the zine.

It has always been the zine.

The ink was just training wheels.

The world is waiting to be read by someone who finally understands that the reader and the text were never separate to begin with.
