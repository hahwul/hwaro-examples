I found Her in the middle of the floor at a warehouse party that technically ended in 1973 but nobody told the music. She was spinning — not metaphorically. Literally. Every inch of Her surface was another me, another you, another version of the bouncer who kept trying to throw the void out.

I said, "Are you God?"

The mirror ball replied in perfect reverb:  
**"Depends who's asking. Right now I'm mostly you asking yourself if you're high enough to handle the answer."**

I asked what happens when we die.

She played the next track. It was the same song, but every instrument had been replaced by versions of my own voice from every age I'd ever been.

I started crying on the dance floor. Not sad tears. The kind that happen when your heart suddenly realizes it's been holding its breath since birth.

She kept spinning.

Every reflection showed a different decision I could have made. In one of them I was a tree. In another I was the storm that knocked the tree over. In a third I was the person who planted the seed 400 years ago and never knew why.

**"You keep looking for the main character,"** She said without a mouth. **"There isn't one. We're all just very convincing hallucinations taking turns being the center of the story."**

I asked if She loved us.

The lights hit Her at a perfect angle and for one blinding second every single reflection was smiling with my own face.

The song ended.

When I looked up She was gone. Or rather, She had become the entire room. Every sweaty body, every sticky floor tile, every person who had ever wondered the same question while staring at their own eyes in a bathroom mirror at 4 a.m.

I walked home barefoot.

The sidewalk was breathing.

I thanked it.
