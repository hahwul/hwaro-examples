I didn't even know it was looking for work.

One morning I woke up and it was already dressed — tiny government-issue button-down, name tag that just said "EYE" in all caps, sensible shoes. It had made coffee for both of us.

**"Big day,"** it said. **"Orientation."**

I asked what the hell it was talking about.

**"I got the job. Postal Service. Third district. They said my ability to see through time and space would be an asset for sorting packages that were mailed from the future."**

It kissed me on the forehead (the regular two eyes) and left.

I spent the entire day trying to function with only binocular vision. Everything felt flat. Jokes weren't funny. Music was just noise. Food tasted like texture.

Around 5:40 p.m. it came home looking exhausted but strangely peaceful.

**"You would not believe the backlog,"** it told me while massaging its cornea with a tiny warm washcloth. **"There's a whole warehouse of letters people wrote to their future selves in 1997 that never got delivered because the future self moved and didn't leave a forwarding address in the space-time continuum."**

It showed me one. The handwriting was mine. The date on the envelope was 1997. The letter inside said:

> Dear Future Me,
> Please remember that the scary thing you are avoiding is actually the only door that leads anywhere interesting. Also buy Apple stock. Also tell Mom you love her before it's too late.

I started crying again.

The third eye patted my hand with its tiny government-issued glove.

**"We delivered it anyway,"** it said. **"We always deliver it anyway. That's the whole point of the job."**

Now it leaves every morning at 7:12 with a little canvas bag full of unsent apologies and unopened miracles. Sometimes it brings home stories:

- A package addressed to "The Person Who Will Finally Forgive Themselves" that keeps rerouting itself to different houses until someone opens it
- A certified letter from 1971 that simply says "YOU WERE RIGHT ABOUT THE RAIN"
- An entire crate of postcards from people who are currently dreaming this exact moment and don't know it yet

I asked it once if it ever gets tired of seeing everything.

It thought for a long time.

**"I used to,"** it said. **"Now I think the seeing is the only reason any of this is bearable. Someone has to witness the packages. Someone has to make sure the meaning gets where it's supposed to go."**

It adjusted its little blue shirt.

**"Tomorrow we're delivering a box of spare courage to a kid who's about to need it in 2009. Want to come with?"**

I said yes.

We left together.

The regular two eyes stayed home and watered the plants.

They needed it.
