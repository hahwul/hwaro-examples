Tramontana is a working sketchbook, kept on trains and terrace tables from Genoa to Cartagena. Every entry is one sitting — thirty minutes, an hour, whatever the light allowed before it moved — drawn on the spot and scanned later with the coffee rings left in.

I chase two subjects above all: the plazas, where a whole town arranges itself around shade, and the rooftops, where the tiles argue about which century they belong to. The pages here are unedited in spirit, complete with the wrong first lines and the buildings I ran out of paper for. Leaf through them the way you would a real block — sideways, out of order, stopping at whatever catches.
