By noon the plaza in front of the old town hall had emptied out the way southern plazas do — everyone folded into the shade of the arcades, waiting for the sun to lose interest. That emptiness is the drawing. A plaza at midday is mostly the shape of its own shadow.

<!-- more -->

The Modernista facades here are gorgeous and, frankly, exhausting — every window framed, garlanded, and topped with something. Draw them straight and you will still be drawing at nightfall. So I made a deal with myself: full ink attention on the ground-floor arcade, where the arches march along in deep shadow, and the lightest possible touch above, just enough marks to say *ornate* without cataloguing it. The eye reads the detailed part as sharp and generously fills in the rest.

The wash was almost all about one thing: the temperature of shade. Arcade shadow in strong Mediterranean sun is not grey and not black — it is a warm, glowing brown, lit from underneath by light bouncing off the pale paving. I mixed it deliberately hot, burnt sienna leading, and let it go cool only at the very back of the arches. Two pigeons and a waiter setting out chairs wandered into the composition and earned their place. I stopped when the shade I was standing in moved on without me.
