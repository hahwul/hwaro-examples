# Encounter with the Leviathan

It is with a trembling hand that I record today's events. We were becalmed for three days in the doldrums, the sea as smooth as glass. This afternoon, the water beneath our hull began to darken, as if a great shadow were rising from the depths.

A massive serpentine form breached the surface less than a cable's length off our starboard bow. The creature was larger than our ship, its scales the color of midnight and seaweed. It paid us little mind, thankfully, merely exhaling a spout of water that smelled of ancient brine before slipping back beneath the waves.

The crew is shaken. Some claim it was an omen, others a sign that we have sailed too far into the unknown. I have ordered a double ration of rum to steady nerves, but I fear sleep will elude us all tonight. The ocean holds secrets we are entirely unequipped to face.
