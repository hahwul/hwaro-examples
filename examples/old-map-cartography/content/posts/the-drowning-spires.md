# The Drowning Spires

We encountered the Drowning Spires at first light. The fog was thick, obscuring the horizon and muffling the sound of the waves. Without warning, dark, jagged shapes emerged from the mist, piercing the surface of the sea like the fingers of a colossal drowned hand.

According to the old legends, these are the remains of an ancient kingdom swallowed by the ocean millennia ago. Navigating the narrow channels between the spires is treacherous work. The currents here are unpredictable, swirling in dangerous eddies that threaten to pull our vessel against the unforgiving stone.

Our cartographer has been furiously sketching the formations, attempting to update our outdated maps. We have observed strange, luminescent flora clinging to the submerged portions of the spires, glowing with an eerie, pale green light. We shall not linger long; this place feels cursed.
