# Voyage to Ultima Thule

The crew has been restless since we crossed the 60th parallel. The air is so cold it burns the lungs, and the rigging is thick with ice. Our navigator, Mr. Higgins, insists we are on the correct heading for Ultima Thule, the mythical northern landmass.

Yesterday, the lookout spotted what appeared to be a mountain range composed entirely of blue ice. As we drew nearer, the sheer scale of these frozen leviathans became apparent. We are navigating carefully through the berg-strewn waters, taking soundings every hour.

The ship's cat has refused to leave the galley, and I cannot blame the creature. There is an unnatural stillness to the air here, broken only by the sharp crack of distant ice splitting. If the charts are to be believed, we should make landfall within the fortnight. I only hope our supplies hold out.
