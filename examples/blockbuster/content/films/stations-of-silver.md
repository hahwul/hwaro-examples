## Premiere

Aero Theatre, Santa Monica, Friday 25 April, 20:30. Press and industry only; a public opening follows on 02 May in wide release.

## Synopsis

Nevada, 1883. A Pinkerton agent pursuing a bullion theft and a railway bookkeeper running from an altogether different liability share a freight car for forty miles of single-track line. The last thirty-one minutes of the picture are one unbroken take, filmed on 65mm in a single railcar built for the purpose and dismantled afterwards.

## The unbroken take

The final act was planned over eleven months with the cinematographer and the railway safety board. It was shot eight times over the course of six days; the seventh take is the one in the picture. No visual effects were used to join cuts; the shot is technically a single camera roll.

## Running time

131 minutes. The studio advises that the final reel begins approximately ninety minutes into the picture and asks audiences to remain seated from that point.
