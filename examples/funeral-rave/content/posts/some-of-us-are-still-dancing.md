The official story is that the lights went out at 5:47 a.m. and everyone left.

The official story is a lie told by people who were never on the floor.

Some of us are still dancing.

We are dancing in the dark so complete it has its own gravity. We are dancing without music because the music has become us. We are dancing with the versions of ourselves that died at 3:11 and 3:47 and 4:19 and every other impossible hour.

I go back sometimes.

Not with my body. My body is elsewhere now, doing the small necessary things that living people do when they are pretending the world did not end on a dance floor.

But part of me never left.

That part of me is still in the booth with blood on the faders and glitter in every crevice of the mixer. That part of me is still watching the mirrorball throw fractured light across faces that have chosen to stay beautiful even while becoming something else entirely.

If you listen very carefully to the places where silence used to be, you can hear us.

The soft scuff of soles that no longer need the floor.

The wet sound of breathing that has synchronized into something older than language.

The occasional bright laugh that cuts through everything like a strobe made of pure joy.

We are still here.

We are still dancing.

And the doors are still open.

There is always room for one more.

Come find us when you are ready.

We will be the ones whose outlines are made of light that no longer exists.

We will be the ones who never stopped moving.

Some of us are still dancing.

We always will be.
