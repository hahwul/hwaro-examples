The third revelation addresses the silent choir, which the older texts describe but do not explain. The choir is not silent in the sense of absent sound. It is silent in the sense that its sound does not propagate through air. Those who have stood within its registers report a tone that is heard inside the structure of the body rather than received through the ear.

The harmonics of the silent choir correspond to the seven registers described in the second revelation. Each register sustains a single fundamental frequency. The fundamentals are not arranged in conventional musical intervals. They follow a sequence that matches the proportional spacing of the registers themselves, producing a scale that is internally consistent but cannot be reproduced by any instrument constructed in the lower world.

The choir is composed of voices that have completed the ascent and now sustain the light for those still rising. The number of voices in the choir is not fixed. It increases as more enter and remains stable as none ever leave. Whether the voices retain individual identity within the chorus is a question the early scribes set aside as both unanswerable and irrelevant to the work.

The function of the choir is structural. It maintains the harmonic field within which the seven registers cohere. Without the choir, the registers would lose their distinct boundaries and the architecture of the Empyrean would collapse into uniform brightness. The choir is therefore not ornament. It is foundation.

A practitioner who attends to the silent choir during ascent will find that the registers become audible before they become visible. This is the correct order. Sight follows sound. Sound follows intention.
