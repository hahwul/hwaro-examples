## Stellar Dendrites

When we think of a "classic" snowflake, we are usually thinking of a stellar dendrite. These are the large, intricate snowflakes with distinct branching structures.

> They form under extremely cold conditions, typically between -15°C and -20°C.

The branching happens because water vapor tends to condense on the corners of the growing ice crystal, where they stick out further into the moist air. This creates a runaway effect, forming long, beautiful arms.
