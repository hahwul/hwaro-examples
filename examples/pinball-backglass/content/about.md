## INSERT COIN

This blog is painted like a backglass: flat color blocks, heavy black keylines, a white pinstripe to make the whole thing pop. The look comes from the golden age of electromechanical and early solid-state pinball — roughly 1968 to 1985 — when the most beautiful thing in any bowling alley was the lit panel of glass standing upright at the back of the machine.

**Pinball Backglass** is a place to write about that craft: the silkscreened art, the score reels that clacked over by hand, the relay logic, the plumb-bob tilt, and the design instinct that turned a metal ball rolling downhill into a spectacle worth a quarter.

---

## About the Operator

I am *the operator*. I keep a small route of restored machines — a few wedgeheads, a couple of early Williams solid-state games, one stubborn Gottlieb that eats its own fuses. I write under that name because operators were the people who actually lived with these machines: setting the replay score, adjusting the tilt bob, swapping the #44 lamps behind the glass when a face went dark.

I write about:

- **Backglass art and illustration** — how flat color and a black keyline carry a whole mood
- **Score reels and display logic** — counting in base ten with motors and relays
- **The tilt mechanism** — the small ethics of the nudge and fair play
- **Multiball and escalation** — why more balls is more theatre, and how designers earn it

> "A backglass is a poster that keeps score."
