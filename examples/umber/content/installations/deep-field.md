Deep Field scores a 28-minute survey through a long-exposure deep-field image, widening from a single quiet patch of sky to a frame holding ten thousand galaxies. It was commissioned to close Fennimore Observatory's centennial season alongside a new visualization built from the observatory's own archival plates.

<!-- more -->

## Speaker topology

The dome runs a 32-speaker full array in eight rings of four, each ring independently amplified so a texture bed can migrate slowly around the room rather than sitting fixed at one bearing — useful here, because the visualization pans and rotates through the field constantly.

```
ZONE1-8  dome.zone[1-8].zenith   -5.0 dB  LPF 11kHz
ZONE1-8  dome.zone[1-8].mid      -5.0 dB  LPF 11kHz
ZONE1-8  dome.zone[1-8].horizon  -6.5 dB  LPF 11kHz
```

## Mix notes

The granular bed is built from detuned recordings of the observatory's original 1961 dome-drive motor, still in occasional service, run through a grain cloud whose density is keyed to the galaxy count visible in the corresponding visualization frame — more galaxies, denser grain, a direct if invisible link between the sound and the data on screen.

Dynamic range stayed wide: eighteen decibels between the starfield hush and the telescope-firing cues that punctuate the survey. The whole piece is an argument about scale, and scale needs somewhere quiet to fall from.
