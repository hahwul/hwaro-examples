Patience,
Discretion
& Care. An address to the membership — Volume XIV, Spring

For ninety-eight years the Syndicate has answered to one constituency: the people we already know. We invest slowly, write honestly, and refuse the room when the room is wrong.

I.Patience over pace. We wait for the right business at the right price. II.Plain language. Every letter is one page. Every page is signed. III.Membership over scale. We have closed the junior roll twice this century.

Four Disciplines, kept narrow.

The Syndicate operates in four disciplines and no more. We have declined adjacent mandates twice in the last decade. Each discipline reports to the full membership at the annual.

I. Permanent Capital

Companies for the century.

Founding-family businesses, taken into joint stewardship. No exit clock; no quarterly drumbeat.

11— portfolio holdings
II. Credit, Carefully

Lending where the line is honest.

Senior secured paper to operators we know by name. The deck stays small; the diligence stays long.

4·2— USD bn outstanding
III. The Library

Research & correspondence.

The Library publishes one quarterly letter and one annual address. Both arrive on paper.

98— quarterly letters published
IV. The Roll

Members & stewardship.

One hundred and twelve members. No marketing. No conference circuit. We answer the letters we are sent.

112— members of record
§ IIThe Spring Address
— Augustus M. HallowayChairman of the House

To the membership: another year, another quiet account. We invested in three businesses and declined twenty-seven. We added one member and removed none. We answered every letter sent to us in working hours, and most of those sent at night.

The world is not slowing on our behalf. We will continue to. We will write the next letter when there is something to say, and not before.
