Overview of the deployed seismic monitoring network along the primary fault line.

We have established a network of twelve broad-band seismometers spaced at approximately 5-kilometer intervals. These stations continuously record ground motion across three axes. Data is telemetered in near real-time to the central processing facility for immediate analysis and integration into the early warning system.

Stations alpha through delta form the core array in the high-risk zone, featuring redundant power supplies and reinforced enclosures to ensure continuous operation during significant seismic events.

To augment this core array, we are currently evaluating the deployment of several low-cost, MEMS-based sensors in the urban periphery. These sensors, while lacking the sensitivity of the broad-band units, can provide vital high-density strong-motion data during a major rupture, significantly improving our ShakeMap generation capabilities. The integration of these disparate sensor types into a unified data stream represents a key objective for the current operational quarter. All telemetry links undergo weekly diagnostic checks to confirm latency remains below the required 500-millisecond threshold for early warning efficacy.
