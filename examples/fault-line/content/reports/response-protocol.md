date = "2025-05-24"
+++

Standard operating procedure upon detection of a significant seismic event.

1.  **Immediate Assessment:**
    Upon triggering of the threshold alert (Mw > 5.0), the automated system immediately disseminates preliminary magnitude and location estimates to relevant emergency management agencies.

2.  **Network Validation:**
    Duty seismologists manually verify the automated parameters within 5 minutes of the event, updating estimates based on full waveform data as it becomes available.

3.  **Aftershock Deployment:**
    If the mainshock exceeds Mw 6.0, deploy the mobile rapid-response array to the epicentral region within 24 hours to capture critical near-source aftershock data.

4.  **Public Communication:**
    Issue standardized public advisories detailing the event parameters and potential aftershock forecasts. Maintain continuous coordination with civil defense authorities.

5.  **Damage Assessment Surveys:**
    Coordinate with local authorities to conduct rapid structural damage assessments in areas experiencing peak ground acceleration exceeding 0.2g. Utilize satellite imagery and drone reconnaissance where ground access is compromised.
6.  **Data Archiving and Dissemination:**
    Ensure all raw waveform data and preliminary event catalogs are securely archived and made available to the broader research community within 72 hours of the event. This rapid dissemination is crucial for collaborative post-event analysis and refinement of regional hazard models.
