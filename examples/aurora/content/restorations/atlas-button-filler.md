Button fillers are lever fillers turned inward: all the mechanism, none of the visible hardware, and one spring steel part doing the entire job in the dark. When the Meridian 62 arrived, its blind cap concealed a button that pushed against nothing. The pressure bar had sheared at its fold — a fatigue break, clean as a guillotine cut, sixty-odd years of flexing finally cashed in.

<!-- more -->

## The parts problem

Atlas Stationers vanished in the 1950s and took its spare-parts drawers with it. No donor pens surface at sane prices, because every surviving Meridian needs the same bar. This is the moment a restoration journal earns its keep: the repair that follows is fabrication, documented so the next bench does not start from zero.

## Making the bar

The replacement began as 0.20 mm spring steel shim stock, cut to a 4.5 mm ribbon with tin snips and deburred on a stone. The geometry that matters:

```
length (folded)   61 mm    barrel depth minus button throw
fold radius        1.5 mm  tighter cracks the temper
free angle        12 deg   preload against the sac
tail hook          2 mm    seats under the section lip
```

The fold was made cold around a mandrel — heating spring steel to bend it is how you make a second broken bar. The tail hook seats under the section so the bar cannot ride up the barrel when the button releases; the original's hook had polished a witness mark inside the barrel wall, which located the new one exactly.

## Assembly and verdict

A size 18 sac went on with shellac and talc, exactly per the procedure recorded under [Plate No. 01](/restorations/corvid-cadet-sac/), and the new bar slid in alongside it, hook first. The button now travels four millimeters and the bar folds flat against the sac with a firm, even collapse — no twist, which would wring the sac like a dishcloth and kill it inside a year.

The plate records one caution for future benches: the fabricated bar is 0.02 mm thicker than the factory part, so the fill is slightly stiffer under the thumb. The owner calls it reassuring. The record calls it a tolerance.
