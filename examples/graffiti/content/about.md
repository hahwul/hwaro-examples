# About This Project

Graffiti is a blog built to document and celebrate the culture of street art worldwide. The walls of our cities carry decades of creative expression -- art that is democratic, accessible, and unapologetically bold.

## Why Street Art Matters

Street art is one of the few art forms that belongs to everyone. It does not require a ticket, a membership, or an invitation. It exists in the shared spaces of daily life -- on the walk to work, at the bus stop, under the bridge you drive past every day.

It is also one of the most ephemeral art forms. A piece can survive for years or be painted over in hours. That impermanence is part of its power.

## About This Site

This site is built with [Hwaro](https://github.com/hahwul/hwaro), a fast static site generator. The design draws inspiration from the concrete walls and brick facades that serve as the canvas for so much of the art discussed here.

## Get in Touch

If you have stories, corrections, or tips about street art in your city, reach out. The walls are always talking -- we just need to listen.
