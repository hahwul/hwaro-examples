Cinema posters were Art Deco's loudest voice. Where a building or a piece of furniture is a single object, a poster reproduces in the thousands and pastes itself onto the street.

## What the Posters Did

The poster artists — Cassandre, Hohlwein, and the studio illustrators of MGM and UFA — compressed Deco geometry into images that read at thirty paces. A locomotive became a wedge of black and red. A swimmer became a ribbon of stylized motion.

The trick was to subtract until only the silhouette remained, then re-add a single color accent. A poster of an ocean liner had a black hull, two red funnels, and the curve of a wake — nothing else.

## Typography as Architecture

Deco posters set their typography like buildings. Letters were tall and narrow, often with internal striping that suggested a stepped façade. Headlines stacked vertically were common; the eye learned to read them as a column.

```
S
T
A
T
E
N D U S T R I A
```

Posters from the period are still legible at glance because the type was treated as part of the image, not as commentary on it.
