A solid case with an open handle loop standing above it, which is either the plainest or the most literal pictogram in the whole set depending on how generously you read "literal": there was, in fact, exactly one lost property office on the entire system, in the Meridian Central mezzanine, and this plate marked the door to it and nothing else.

<!-- more -->

No designer's name is attached to this plate in any surviving document. The construction sheet, if one was ever drawn, did not survive; the plate itself carries no sign shop numbering on the reverse, unlike every other item in this catalog. It is catalogued here as unattributed rather than guessed at, on the reasoning that a wrong credit is worse than an honest gap — a policy this catalog holds to throughout, even where circumstantial evidence might point toward Halvorsen or Wren.

The office it marked closed in 1988 when its functions were absorbed into the general information booths, and this plate came down with the door itself during the relocation. It sat unlabeled in a maintenance depot for thirty-six years before a routine inventory in 2024 turned it up, still screwed to a fragment of the original door frame, which is the only reason its provenance is known at all — the depot's own inventory tag, not the plate, records where it was found.
