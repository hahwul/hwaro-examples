Four steps climbing in strict thirty-two-module increments, capped by a small arrowhead that Wren's notes describe only as "the nudge" — not a full arrow, not a chevron, just enough of a mark to say the stair keeps moving even after the eye stops counting steps. It is the only pictogram in the surviving set attributed solely to Wren rather than Halvorsen, and the only one with a documented second version.

<!-- more -->

The first version, cut and installed at two stations in early 1974 before the manual was finalized, drew five steps rather than four. Maintenance crews reported the extra step made the symbol read as a full staircase rather than a moving one, undercutting the entire point of distinguishing it from the plain stair mark hung beside it. Wren's revision — four steps, tighter riser height, the added directional head — went to print within a month and every later installation used it, though the two original five-step plates were apparently never swapped out and may still exist somewhere in the system, unrecorded.

This plate hung at Harrow Cross East for twenty-nine years, longer in service than all but one other plate in this catalog, and shows the wear to prove it: a hairline scuff along the second step where a maintenance ladder rested against the headwall for years without anyone thinking to move it six inches to the left.
