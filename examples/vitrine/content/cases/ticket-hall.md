A ticket stub broken by a dashed perforation and a single round punch hole: the one plate in the program credited jointly to both designers, and, as of this cataloguing, the last one to have come down from an original station wall rather than out of a skip or a depot.

<!-- more -->

Halvorsen and Wren's joint credit on this plate is unusual enough that the archive's correspondence addresses it directly — a short note from Halvorsen describing the ticket hall symbol as "the one we couldn't stop redrawing together," since neither designer would sign off on the punch hole's position without the other's agreement. Six iterations are referenced in surviving correspondence, though only the final construction sheet itself survives; the punch hole's position, dead center on the right third of the stub rather than the corner a real ticket punch would use, was the last detail settled.

This plate hung at Anneslie Road for fifty-two years, longer than any other pictogram in this catalog and longer than the paper ticketing system it depicted, which the authority phased out decades before the plate itself came down. It was removed and photographed in the same week in early 2026 ahead of the station's modernization, which makes it the only plate in this exhibition catalogued while its physical twin was, briefly, still bolted to a wall.
