Seven surviving pictograms from the Meridian Metro's 1974 signage program, redrawn as vector line work and mounted, museum-fashion, behind glass.

<!-- more -->

The Systems Graphics Unit drew these glyphs on a fixed thirty-two module grid — the small, unglamorous marks that told a rider where the stairs were, where the telephone was, where the line split in two. They outlived three renovations and one near-total decommissioning; the design office that made them did not. Porcelain enamel and acrylic light-box plates are durable and unfashionable in roughly equal measure, and that mismatch is the only reason any of this survives to be catalogued. Where a construction sheet from the original 1974 manual survives, the plate below is redrawn from its measurements exactly. Where only the physical object survives, it is redrawn from careful photography, cross-checked against the fixed grid module the design office held to across the entire program. Read the [curatorial note](@/about.md) for the full account of how the archive was assembled.
