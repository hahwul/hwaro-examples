Niello is a black metallic alloy of sulfur, copper, silver, and sometimes lead. It has been used since antiquity as a decorative inlay on engraved or etched metal surfaces. The alloy is applied as a powder or paste, heated until it flows into the recessed lines of the design, then cooled and polished to reveal bright metal patterns against a deep black ground.

## This Site

This is a demonstration of the Niello theme for Hwaro. The design draws from the craft itself: high contrast, precise lines, silver on black. No ornament without purpose. Every element serves the text.

## Principles

- **Contrast over color.** White and silver against near-black. Nothing else needed.
- **Precision over decoration.** Thin rules, exact spacing, deliberate hierarchy.
- **Legibility first.** Type is sized for reading, not for show.
- **No noise.** No gradients, no shadows, no animation for its own sake.
