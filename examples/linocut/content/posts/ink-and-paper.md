Choosing the right ink and paper combination is essential for a successful print. Oil-based
inks provide rich, deep colors and an excellent working time, but require solvents for
cleanup. Water-based inks are easier to clean up and dry faster, though they can sometimes
lack the opacity of their oil-based counterparts. We must balance these characteristics
against the requirements of the project.

Paper selection is equally nuanced. A heavy, textured paper like Somerset or Rives BFK
will hold the ink beautifully and add a tactile quality to the print. Lighter papers, such
as Japanese kozo or mulberry papers, offer a delicate, translucent effect. The interaction
between the ink, the paper, and the pressure applied during the printing process determines
the final appearance. Experimentation is key to discovering the perfect combination for
each unique block. Different papers absorb ink in varying ways, dramatically altering the
mood and impact of the final artwork.
