The culmination of the linocut process occurs at the press. This mechanical marvel applies
even, consistent pressure across the block, transferring the inked image to the paper. Setting
the pressure correctly is a matter of feel and experience. Too much pressure can cause the
ink to bleed or the paper to tear, while too little will result in a faint, incomplete print.

We must carefully align the paper over the inked block, a step known as registration. This
ensures the image is centered and square. Once aligned, we roll the block and paper through
the press. The moment of revelation—peeling back the paper to reveal the print—is always
exciting. It is the final translation of our carved lines into a tangible piece of art. The
press is a powerful tool, demanding respect and careful operation to achieve consistent,
high-quality results. Maintaining the press, keeping the bed clean and the rollers lubricated,
is just as important as the printing process itself.
