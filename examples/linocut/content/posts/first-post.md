This is the first post detailing the meticulous process of traditional block printing.
Every carve into the linoleum surface requires focus and intent. The tools we use dictate
the final texture of the print. Sharp gouges create clean lines, while wider tools clear
away the negative space. The relationship between positive and negative space is everything
in linocut. We are essentially drawing with light, deciding what will remain untouched to
catch the ink.

The preparation phase is critical. Transferring the design accurately ensures the final
image aligns with the initial vision. We use soft graphite for the transfer, rubbing the
back of the tracing paper to leave a ghost image on the block. From there, the cutting
begins. The scent of the linoleum and the satisfying sound of the blade slicing through
the material are integral to the experience. This sensory engagement is what makes the
process so rewarding. Each block becomes a unique topography, shaped by the artist's hand
and the resistance of the material itself.
