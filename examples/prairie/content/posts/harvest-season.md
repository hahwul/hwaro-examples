The combines are running from dawn to dusk. Dust rises from the fields in golden clouds, and the air smells of cut wheat and dry earth.

<!-- more -->

## The Rhythm of Fall

Harvest season is the heartbeat of the prairie. Every family knows the drill — early mornings, long days, late suppers. The machinery hums across miles of open land, and the grain trucks line up at the elevator before sunrise.

### What We Gather

- **Winter wheat** — the backbone of the region
- **Corn** — tall and golden by September
- **Soybeans** — filling the lowland fields

There is something deeply satisfying about watching a full grain cart empty into the truck. The weight of the year's work, measured in bushels.

## Evening on the Prairie

When the sun sets over the stubble fields, the sky turns amber and pink. Crickets begin their chorus. The porch light comes on, and supper is waiting.

> "The prairie teaches patience. You plant, you tend, you wait — and then the land gives back."

This is the season that makes every early morning worth it.
