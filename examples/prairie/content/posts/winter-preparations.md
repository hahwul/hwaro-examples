The first hard frost came last night. Time to check the wood pile, seal the windows, and make sure the storm cellar is stocked.

<!-- more -->

## The Checklist

Winter on the prairie is no joke. When a blizzard rolls in, you might not leave the house for days. Preparation is everything.

### Essential Tasks

1. **Stack firewood** under the lean-to, enough for two months
2. **Check the generator** — fuel, oil, and test run
3. **Seal windows and doors** with weatherstripping
4. **Stock the pantry** — canned goods, flour, salt, coffee
5. **Inspect the roof** for loose shingles before the snow load

### The Storm Cellar

Every prairie homestead has one. Ours is stocked with blankets, water, a battery radio, and a shelf of home-canned vegetables. When the sirens sound, you don't think — you just go.

## Finding Beauty in the Cold

Winter is harsh, but it has its own kind of beauty. The silence after a snowfall. The way the light turns blue at dusk. Smoke rising straight from the chimney into still, frozen air.

The prairie doesn't stop being beautiful just because it's cold.
