A page full of glow needs typography that does not also try to glow. The light belongs to the accents, not the words.

## What Calm Type Looks Like

- Body copy in a steady serif or low-contrast sans, set at 1.05–1.15rem with line-height around 1.55.
- Headings reserved for genuinely structural breaks. Two H2s on a page is usually right; six is a sign the text needs to be split.
- Letter-spacing close to zero. Loose tracking on body text reads as decorative, which competes with the glow.

The reader's eye should slow down on this page, not feel chased through it. Quiet typography is the contract that keeps the rest of the design from feeling busy.
