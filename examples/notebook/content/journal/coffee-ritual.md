I switched from a drip machine to a pour-over setup about six months ago. Everyone told me it would be too much work. It is five extra minutes. Five minutes that have become the best part of my morning.

## The Process

Boil water. Grind beans. Rinse the filter. Pour slowly in circles, starting from the center. Wait. Pour again. The whole thing takes about four minutes of active attention.

That is the point. Four minutes where I am not thinking about email or deadlines or what I need to do today. I am just watching water flow through coffee grounds and paying attention to the stream.

## Why It Matters

We do not have many rituals left. Most of our daily actions are optimized for speed -- instant coffee, fast food, one-click ordering. Speed is useful, but it strips away the experience of doing things.

Making coffee by hand is a small rebellion against that. It says: this moment is worth my full attention. This simple thing deserves to be done carefully.

## The Taste

It does taste better. Not dramatically, life-changingly better. But noticeably better. I think part of it is the attention -- when you make something with care, you taste it with more care too.

The real benefit is not in the cup. It is in the four minutes of presence that come before it.
