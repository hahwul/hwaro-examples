I have been reading too many articles about morning routines. Every one of them promises that waking up at 5 AM and drinking lemon water will change my life. I tried it for a week. It did not change my life.

What actually works for me is much simpler: wake up without an alarm when my body is ready, make coffee, and sit with a blank notebook for ten minutes before opening any screens. That is it.

## The Screen Problem

The moment I check my phone, my attention scatters. Emails pull me toward other people's priorities. Social media fills my head with noise. By the time I sit down to do real work, I have already spent my best mental energy on reactions instead of creation.

## What I Actually Do Now

1. Wake up naturally (usually around 7)
2. Coffee, black, no negotiation
3. Ten minutes of writing -- anything, stream of consciousness
4. Review my three priorities for the day
5. Then and only then: open the laptop

It is not revolutionary. It is not Instagram-worthy. But it works.

> The best morning routine is the one you actually follow, not the one you read about.

I think the real lesson here is that consistency matters more than optimization. A mediocre routine done daily beats a perfect routine abandoned after three days.
