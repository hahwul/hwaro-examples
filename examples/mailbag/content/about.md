The Mailbag is written by Wren Halloway, a field correspondent who spends migration season moving between banding stations, ranger posts, and other people's spreadsheets. Each issue takes one flyway or herd route — never more — and tries to say something true about it in under a thousand words: where the animals started, where they ended up, how long it took, and what nearly went wrong along the way.

Nothing here is guesswork dressed as narration. Distances are drawn from published tracking studies, banding recoveries, and agency migration reports; coordinates mark real staging grounds, calving grounds, and wintering ranges, rounded to a tenth of a degree so the letter reads like a letter and not a data table. Where a figure is disputed between sources, the dispatch says so rather than picking the more dramatic number.

## How the season works

A season runs six issues, one per route, roughly one every four to five weeks from January to June. That cadence isn't arbitrary — it follows the actual staggering of departure windows across flyways, so an issue tends to land close to when its subject is mid-migration rather than long after the fact. The roster closes once the sixth letter ships and reopens the following January with six new routes.

## Joining the roster

The homepage carries a static roster form for demonstration — this example site doesn't send mail. In the live version, joining the roster adds your address to a plain delivery list; no segmentation, no tracking pixels, one email per issue, unsubscribe in the footer of every letter.

## Corrections

Field data gets revised. When a distance or window changes materially between drafting and publication, the correction is noted at the bottom of the issue it affects rather than silently edited — the record should show what was known when the letter was filed.
