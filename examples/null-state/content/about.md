## On the practice

null-state was founded in 2017 after a long run inside larger product companies. The premise: that the most valuable design work happens not on the first version of a product but on the second and third — when patterns harden into systems, when single-screen decisions ripple across hundreds of dependent surfaces, when small typographic mistakes become permanent infrastructure.

We took the name *null-state* because the founding insight was that most products are not designed *for* their primary task. They are designed for the empty case — the first launch, the screenshot in the press release. Real users live in the messy, populated middle. The studio set out to design for that.

## How we work

A typical engagement runs three to six months. We embed with one or two designers and one or two engineers. We produce three artifacts:

1. **A system,** documented as code in your repository — not as a Figma library.
2. **A reference application** that demonstrates the system on a real workflow.
3. **A short written brief** explaining the decisions, so the next person to inherit the work knows why.

We do not produce decks. We do not produce mood boards. We do not produce concept videos. If a deliverable cannot be shipped to a user, we do not make it.

## Who we work with

Past clients have been startups in the seed-to-series-B range, research institutions, and one government agency. We turn down agency work and we turn down anything that requires us to use a particular CMS.

We will not work on:

- Loyalty programs.
- Adtech.
- Crypto exchanges.
- Generative-AI consumer apps that mislead the user about provenance.
