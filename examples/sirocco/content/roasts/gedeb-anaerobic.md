The importer's sample sheet used the word "loud" to describe this lot's cup and I laughed, then bought two bags, because that is exactly the kind of note that means someone actually tasted the coffee before writing about it.

<!-- more -->

**Green:** 210 g, moisture 10.3%. Anaerobic-processed beans carry a sharper, almost carbonic smell straight out of the bag — sealed-tank fermentation does that, and it usually means the roast needs to work harder to fold the funk into something drinkable rather than fight it.

**Charge:** 201°C.

**Turning point:** 1:16.

**First crack:** 9:05, steady and even across the batch — good sign, since anaerobic lots can roast unevenly if the fermentation wasn't consistent bean to bean.

**Development time ratio:** 15.9%, dropped at 10:46, 216°C. I aimed for Full City specifically to let the funk mellow into fruit rather than staying sharp — a shorter roast leaves this one tasting more like fermentation than coffee, which I learned the hard way on an earlier batch not in this log because I didn't think it deserved an entry.

Cupped at four days: pineapple and a tropical, almost mango-skin sweetness up front, settling into a caramelized sugar finish that keeps the wilder notes in check. Body sits between the washed and natural lots — round but not heavy. This is the one I hand to people who say they don't like "funky" coffee, because by Full City the funk has become fruit, and fruit is an easier sell.
