Bought this one on smell alone — the green beans still carried a faint ferment sweetness through the bag, which is either a very good sign or a warning, and with naturals it is usually the former if the drying beds were turned enough.

<!-- more -->

**Green:** 200 g, moisture 9.8%, visibly uneven bean color, which is normal for a natural and not something to sort out before roasting.

**Charge:** 202°C.

**Turning point:** 1:19.

**First crack:** 8:02 — early, and I let it go only 40 seconds past before pulling the drop lever. Naturals this fruit-forward turn to jam and then to ash fast once development runs long, and I was not going to lose the strawberry.

**Development time ratio:** 8.3%. The shortest DTR in this log so far, and it shows in an uneven bean surface — patchy, almost tiger-striped. I used to fight that look. I've stopped; the cup tells the real story.

Cupped on day three because I couldn't wait: strawberry and red grape skin on the nose, a ferment tang that reads as wine rather than vinegar, and a body syrupy enough that it drinks almost like a cordial in a small cup. Not a coffee for a big mug and a workday — this is a five-minute pour you sit with on purpose.

Buying a full box of this next harvest if the co-op will hold a lot back for me.
