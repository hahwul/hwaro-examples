First lot from the Ortiz family this season, bought through the same importer who sold me last year's Huila that I still miss. Same farm, different picking — this one leans sweeter.

<!-- more -->

**Green:** 230 g, moisture 11.1%.

**Charge:** 200°C, a touch cooler than the Ethiopia batch to slow the front half of the curve — Colombian washed coffee this dense rewards patience early on.

**Turning point:** 1:28.

**First crack:** 9:52, quieter than I expected for the density, more of a crackle than a pop.

**Development time ratio:** 17.4%, dropped at 11:38, 214°C. This is the roast where I finally trust my own notes instead of a published Full City reference — the bean color at drop looked a shade lighter than the charts say it should, but the log from the last three Huila batches all point the same direction, so I went with the log.

Cupped at day four: caramel and a soft plum note that firms up into brown sugar as it cools, body heavier than the Ethiopia, finish shorter but rounder. Good in a moka pot, better in a batch brewer where the extra contact time draws out more of the stone fruit.

Logging a note for next time — try dropping five degrees earlier and see if the plum holds longer before it turns to straight caramel.
