Honey-processed lots are the ones I'm still learning. The mucilage left on the parchment during drying holds heat differently in the drum than a washed or natural bean, and this batch scorched a little on my first pass in August — this is the redo, and the fix worked.

<!-- more -->

**Green:** 235 g, moisture 10.9%, tacky surface even after months in the bag — honey-processed coffee stays a little sticky, which is normal.

**Charge:** 196°C, twelve degrees cooler than my usual — the fix from the scorched batch. Slower ramp through the yellowing stage kept the surface sugars from caramelizing before the inside of the bean caught up.

**Turning point:** 1:34.

**First crack:** 10:15, later than the washed lots, as expected from the lower charge.

**Development time ratio:** 21.6%, dropped at 12:58, 221°C — well into second crack territory by sound, though I pulled before it went audible in earnest. Full City+ is where this variety wants to live; lighter and the mucilage sugars taste raw instead of caramelized.

Cupped at five days: milk chocolate and toasted pecan, a thick body that coats the cup, and almost no acidity left, which some tasters would call a flaw and I'd call the entire point of a honey-processed roast this dark. Makes an excellent everyday drip coffee — the kind you can hand to someone who says they don't like "fancy coffee" and watch them come around.
