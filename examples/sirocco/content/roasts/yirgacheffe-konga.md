Konga again this year, and it drank the same way it did last January — I keep buying it hoping it will finally get boring, and it never does. This is the batch that started the year's log.

<!-- more -->

**Green:** 220 g, moisture 10.6%, density on the high side for the region.

**Charge:** 204°C drum, beans in at 0:00.

**Turning point:** 1:12, a little slow — I'd underfed the burner on the previous heat-up and didn't correct until charge.

**First crack:** 8:44, rolling within twenty seconds. I let it run 1:05 past the first pop and dropped at 9:49, 196°C at the bean probe.

**Development time ratio:** 11.1% — deliberately short. Ethiopian washed lots this clean don't need coaxing; they need protecting.

Rested five days before the first cup. Dry fragrance is jasmine and lemon verbena, and it holds through the crack — no baked or flat patches on the outside of the bean, which tells me the airflow curve I've been running since November is finally right for this drum. In the cup: bergamot up front, a long finish that reads as black tea rather than fruit juice, and a body thinner than the Huila but honestly better suited to a pour-over than anything heavier would be.

Next bag is already ordered. If Konga keeps showing up this consistent I may stop treating it as a seasonal purchase and just keep it in rotation year-round.
