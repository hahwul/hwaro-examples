## About This Journal

**Tartan Weave** is a journal about cloth that carries meaning. Not fashion, not nostalgia — the actual structure of a woven tartan: the warp and the weft, the count of threads, the crossing of solid colour that builds a *sett*, and the thin overcheck line that lifts a heavy pattern out of mud.

Tartan looks loud from across a room and resolves into quiet order the closer you stand. That is the tension we write about here. A pattern can be bold and disciplined at the same time. The colours shout; the maths whispers.

---

## What You Will Find Here

- **Construction** — how a sett is specified, counted, and reproduced thread for thread
- **History** — where the idea of a "clan tartan" actually came from, and how recently
- **Detail** — the overcheck, the selvage, the guard threads, and the small decisions that separate a designed cloth from a striped blanket
- **Colour** — the difference between a dye recipe and a marketing swatch

We care about getting it right. When a story is a romantic invention dressed as ancient fact, we say so — and the invention is usually more interesting than the myth it replaced.

## About the Author

I write under the name **the-weaver**. I keep a four-shaft floor loom in a cold room, a shelf of dye notebooks, and far too many partial warps waiting to be threaded. I learned to read a thread count before I learned to read a tartan register, and I still trust the count more than the register.

> "A tartan is not a picture of a colour. It is an instruction for crossing one colour with another until a third appears."

If you weave, dye, or simply like knowing why the cloth on your shoulder is built the way it is, you are in the right place. Pull up a stool by the loom.
