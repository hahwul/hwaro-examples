Editor's Note

On filing late, and filing clean.

Late but right is better than early but wrong. We will hold the page if the desk asks. We will not run a sentence we are unsure of.

The features desk filed at 11pm Thursday and the copy desk read it cold. We held the page. The piece runs Monday with one footnote and a corrected dateline.

— M. Han, Friday morning
From the Morgue

A note found in the May 1958 box.

A two-page memo from the founding editor on the question of how often to print a correction. The answer, in his hand: whenever a reader writes in good faith. Pinned to the city desk this morning.

— Archive Drawer 47, Bin C
