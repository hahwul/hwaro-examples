In design, what you *don't* include is often as important as what you do. White space—or negative space—is the breathing room around elements. In a typography-focused blog like Typeface, space is our most valuable tool.

<!-- more -->

When we provide generous margins and loose line-heights, we aren't just making things look "minimal." We are reducing cognitive load and inviting the reader to focus on the nuances of the letterforms.

## Spacing Principles

*   **Macro Space:** The margins between sections that define the overall structure.
*   **Micro Space:** The kerning between letters and the leading between lines that determines readability.

A sophisticated design knows when to be quiet so that the type can truly be heard.
