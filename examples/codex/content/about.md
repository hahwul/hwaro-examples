## About This Codex

Codex Illuminatus is an original theme for [Hwaro](https://github.com/hahwul/hwaro), inspired by the illuminated manuscripts produced in medieval European scriptoria between the 6th and 15th centuries. Every element of this theme pays homage to the book arts of that era while remaining a fully functional modern blog.

### Design Principles

- **Blackletter headings** using the UnifrakturMaguntia typeface, evoking the hand-lettered titles of medieval codices
- **Drop cap initials** on the opening of each chapter, reminiscent of the decorated versals painted by monastic scribes
- **Parchment-toned background** with a warm, aged palette drawn from vellum and oak gall ink
- **Ornamental borders** framing the manuscript page, inspired by carpet pages and canon tables
- **Two-column layout** with a main text column and a margin for glosses and annotations
- **Illuminated dividers** using floral ornaments to mark section breaks
- **IM Fell English SC** for navigation and labels, recalling the small capitals of early printed books

### Setup

1. Clone or download the files into your Hwaro project
2. Run `hwaro serve` from the terminal
3. Open your browser to `http://localhost:3000/`

*Deo gratias.*
