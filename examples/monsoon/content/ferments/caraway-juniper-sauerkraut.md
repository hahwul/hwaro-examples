Sauerkraut is where everyone should start, and the crock I come back to when I
want to remind myself that fermentation is mostly just salt, cabbage, and
keeping your nerve. This one is built for the dark months: caraway and juniper
give it a resinous, almost gin-like edge that stands up to anything roasted.

<!-- more -->

The only ratio that matters here is salt to cabbage by weight. Two percent is
the number I trust — enough to hold back spoilage, gentle enough to let the
lactic bacteria do their slow work.

```text
cabbage, shredded ......... 2000 g
sea salt (2%) .............. 40 g
caraway seed ............... 2 tsp, lightly crushed
juniper berries ............ 12, cracked
tart apple, grated ......... 1 small
```

Shred the cabbage thin, weigh it, and scatter the salt through in handfuls.
Then you wait ten minutes and start squeezing. It feels like nothing is
happening right up until the moment it does: the leaves collapse, water beads,
and suddenly there is enough brine to submerge the lot. Pack it tight into the
jar, press until the liquid rises over the top, and weigh it down.

## Reading it

Days one to three it sputters and hisses as the ferment gets going — keep it on
a plate, it will overflow. By the end of the first week the smell shifts from
raw cabbage to something bright and sour. I start tasting at day ten. This batch
came into its own around day twenty-one: crisp, clean, with the juniper coming
through late like a cold walk. Once it tastes right, it goes into the cold end
of the larder where it holds for months, only getting rounder.
