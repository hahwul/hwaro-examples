Monsoon is written by Wren Okafor, a former line cook who traded a hot kitchen
for a cold one. The pantry is a converted stone larder off the back of a house
in Cregg Vale, dug half into the hillside so it holds around sixteen degrees
without any help from a plug. That steadiness is the whole point. Warm rooms
rush a ferment and teach you bad habits; a cool dark shelf makes you slow down
and pay attention to what the salt and the microbes are actually doing.

## How the day counts work

Every entry carries the date its jar was packed. The oversized number you see
on each ferment is simply today minus that date — the age of the batch in days,
recalculated every time the site is rebuilt. A kraut on day 14 and a miso on
day 500 are both, in their own time, exactly where they should be. The counter
is not a countdown. It is a reminder that the good stuff is measured in
patience, not minutes.

## The method, briefly

- **Weigh, don't guess.** Salt is a percentage of vegetable weight, always.
- **Submerge everything.** What sits above the brine is what spoils.
- **Taste on a schedule, decide by the tongue.** The calendar is a suggestion.
- **Label the lid.** Future you will not remember what past you packed.
