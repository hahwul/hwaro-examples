Courier is a letter about the tools novelists actually reach for at five in the morning — outliners, typewriters, dictation software, index-card systems, the unglamorous spreadsheets that track where a manuscript has been sent. Nothing here is tested for a weekend. Every review runs alongside a real draft, through however many passes it takes to know if the thing earns its place on the desk.

No sponsored issues, no affiliate links, no roundups of ten apps nobody uses past the trial period. One tool or workflow per letter, reviewed the way you would revise a chapter: closely, and more than once.
