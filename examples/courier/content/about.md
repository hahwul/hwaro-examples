Courier is written by Rosalind Ferry, a novelist who has finished four books and abandoned considerably more, and who has therefore tried nearly every tool that claims to make the finishing part easier. She started keeping notes on what actually helped — as opposed to what merely felt productive — somewhere around her second manuscript, and Courier is those notes cleaned up and sent out roughly once a month.

## What gets reviewed

Outlining software, drafting apps, dictation tools, typewriters both electric and manual, notebooks, index-card systems, submission trackers, and the occasional piece of furniture that turns out to matter more than the software does. If it sits between a novelist and a finished draft, it is fair game.

## How a review gets written

Nothing runs through a weekend trial. Each tool or workflow gets tested against a manuscript that is actually in progress — usually Rosalind's own, occasionally a volunteer's — for as many drafts as it takes to know whether the thing holds up under real revision pressure rather than a demo's best behavior. That is what the slugline at the top of every issue records: not an issue number, but the draft the review itself went through and the length it settled at, the way you would slug your own pages before sending them out.

## What this letter will not do

It will not run sponsored issues. It will not publish a "ten best apps for writers" roundup, because nine of the ten would be filler. It will not recommend a tool Rosalind has not personally put a full draft through. When something does not work, the issue says so plainly, including the expensive things.

## Reaching the letter

Courier goes out by [feed](/issues/rss.xml) and by the one-field form on the front page — no account, no password, just an address the next issue lands in. Replies reach Rosalind directly, and the ones with genuine tool suggestions tend to become the next issue.
