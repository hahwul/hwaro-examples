Marl is named for the soft, layered sediment that forms where lime and clay
settle together over long stretches of time. The database borrows the idea
literally: data is deposited at high resolution near the surface, then
compacted downward into coarser, longer-lived layers as it ages. You never
migrate or expire a series by hand — you describe the strata once, and the
engine does the deposition. This manual reads the same way, from the surface
down to the deep store.
