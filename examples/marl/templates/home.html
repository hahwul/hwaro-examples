Marl

Marl stores metrics the way ground stores time: newest at the surface, older data pressed into deeper, coarser layers. Tiered retention, automatic downsampling, and gap-aware queries are built into the storage engine, not bolted on top.

Tiered retention

Each series flows through named tiers — hot, warm, cold — with its own resolution and lifetime. A point that starts at one-second precision settles into hourly rollups without a cron job.

Automatic downsampling

Continuous rollups aggregate as data ages: mean, max, p99, and counts are computed once at the tier boundary and reused by every query that reaches for them.

Gap-aware queries

Missing intervals are first-class. Queries distinguish a real zero from an absent sample, and fill policies let you carry, interpolate, or leave holes exactly where sensors went quiet.
