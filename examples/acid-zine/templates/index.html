Manifesto

Ten things we do and six we refuse.

  1. Write fast, edit slow, staple anyway.
  2. Interview the people nobody books.
  3. Print what we can stand behind.
  4. Correct ourselves on the same page.
  5. Run the classifieds unedited.
  6. Mail to readers we have never met.
  7. Take pay in coffee and cigarettes.
  8. Respect the reader’s time.
  9. Miss no deadline we set ourselves.
  10. Keep the price at pay-what-you-like.
  11. Refuse advertisers. All of them.
  12. Refuse polite adjectives.
  13. Refuse the second draft that loses the point.
  14. Refuse the explanation that excuses the crime.
  15. Refuse the headline that undersells the piece.
  16. Refuse the version that is too clean.

Interview

Transcribed from a minidisc
PLATE 01 · FIG 2.A

“We ran out of toner and kept printing.”

Q / On the second issue.
We mailed them at midnight. The toner was striping by page three and we didn’t stop. The stripes became the issue. We put a note in the margin explaining nothing.
Q / On criticism.
We get two kinds. The kind that wants us cleaner, and the kind that wants us louder. We take the second to heart and the first to the recycling.
Q / On next issue.
Staple. Mail. Repeat. That is the editorial calendar. We will not dress it up.
