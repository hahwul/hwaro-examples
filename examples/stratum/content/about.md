## The Layers Beneath

Stratum is a journey through deep time, presented as a cross-section of the Earth itself. Each layer you encounter represents millions of years of geological and biological history, compressed into readable strata that you can explore at your own pace.

The name comes from the Latin *stratum*, meaning "something spread or laid down" -- the fundamental unit of sedimentary geology. Just as geologists read the history of the Earth by examining rock layers exposed in cliff faces and canyon walls, this site invites you to read history by scrolling through time.

## How to Read the Layers

The timeline flows from the present at the surface to the ancient past in the deepest layers. Each stratum is color-coded to reflect its geological character:

- **Topsoil and humus** mark the modern era, rich and dark with organic matter.
- **Sandstone and limestone** represent the middle ages of Earth, when shallow seas covered the continents.
- **Igneous and metamorphic rock** speak to the violent origins of our world, when magma shaped the crust.

## Sources and Methodology

The geological dates and descriptions presented here draw from the International Commission on Stratigraphy's chronostratigraphic chart, supplemented by paleontological literature and field geology references. While we have simplified the timescales for readability, the relative ordering and major events are accurate to current scientific understanding.
