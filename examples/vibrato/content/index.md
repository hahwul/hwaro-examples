Every episode of Vibrato takes apart one recording session — a single afternoon, a single reel of tape, sometimes a single take — and puts it back together from the engineer's notebook up. Not a survey of careers. Not a ranked list. One room, one band, one decision that only made sense at the time.

We work from session logs, studio correspondence, and whatever the surviving engineers were willing to tell us, then press the story to the same 78-rpm-style label you'll find in every episode's session card: the running time on the wax, the pressing number where the episode count used to be.

New sessions arrive every few weeks, chosen less for fame than for a good story behind the tape — a borrowed microphone, a flooded basement, a foghorn that wandered into the mix and stayed. Start with whatever's pressing now, or drop into the archive and pick a room, any room.
