Vibrato is a fortnightly-ish show about the rooms jazz records were actually made in — the studios, the borrowed microphones, the tape machines that jammed at the worst possible moment. It is hosted by Odell Marsh, a jazz historian who spent a decade cataloguing independent-label session sheets before turning the collection into a podcast, and Priya Anand, a mastering engineer who handles the technical breakdowns: mic placement, tape speed, why a room sounds the way it sounds.

## How an episode gets made

Each session starts as a folder of primary sources: studio logbooks, union contractor sheets, correspondence between labels and engineers, and — where we're lucky — surviving interview tape with the musicians themselves. Odell builds the narrative timeline; Priya listens back with an engineer's ear, flagging the technical choices a casual listen would miss. An episode does not go into production until both of us agree we can source the central claim, not just repeat the legend that's grown up around it.

We are upfront when a detail can't be confirmed. Several episodes carry a line noting where the record disagrees with itself — a contractor sheet that lists a different bassist than the one credited on the sleeve, a session date that doesn't match the union paperwork. We'd rather flag the gap than paper over it.

## The pressing labels

Every session card on this site is built to look like the label glued to a 78-rpm pressing: running time where a record would print its side length, and the episode's catalog number — VB, then the episode count — standing in for the pressing code a label would have stamped into the wax. It is decoration with a point: a reminder that these sessions were, eventually, physical objects that had to survive being cut, pressed, and shipped before anyone could hear them.

## Get in touch

Corrections, session leads, and studio-logbook donations are always welcome. If you were in one of these rooms, or you know someone who was, we want to hear from you before the next episode locks.

Start with the [full session archive](/episodes/) if you want to hear where we've been, or jump straight into [The Amber Room Session](/episodes/amber-room-session/), the episode that started the show's habit of pressing running time onto a label.
