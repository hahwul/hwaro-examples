We loaded a portable receiver, a pair of shielded cables, and a directional loop antenna into the back of an old hatchback and drove past the city limits. The plan was simple: park where the streetlights end, sweep the AM band slowly from 540 to 1700 kilohertz, and capture whatever was there.

What we found was a layered, slow-moving texture. Distant stations bled into one another with a gentle wash of carrier hum. A weather broadcast from three states away surfaced for a few seconds, then receded. Between the strong stations, we recorded long stretches of pink noise punctuated by the faint clicks of atmospheric discharges. The receiver's automatic gain control fought us the entire time, so we switched to manual and rode the level meters by ear.
