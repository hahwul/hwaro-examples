## Concerning the Plate

This volume entered the collection on a Tuesday in February, wrapped in brown paper and tied
with butcher's string, with no accompanying note save a postcard from a small town in
southern Vermont that read, merely: *&#8220;Take good care of her. She has travelled.&#8221;*
The plate was cut that evening on a quarter-sheet of copper. The central device shows a
mountain ash in winter, its berries rendered in stipple; the motto, *UBI BENE IBI PATRIA*,
appears beneath the branches on a ribbon scroll.

## Physical Description

A quarto volume, ix + 228 pages, sewn in four signatures with linen thread. The binding is
half-cloth over paper-covered boards, with a brown calf spine stamped in blind. Edges
untrimmed. Two previous plates have been removed from the interior front board, leaving
faint ghost-marks; the present plate is affixed above these in the accepted ex-libris style.

## The Reader's Note

I read the whole book through in the week after it arrived, slowly, in the evenings, with a
cup of weak tea at the elbow. The commentary is longer than the poem and contains at least
three kingdoms, two murders, and a recipe for beet soup that is reproduced here exactly as
it would have been served in Zembla before the revolution.

> Life everlasting &#8212; based on a misprint!

A line that, having read it, one cannot unread. The book is now shelved between an
early Nabokov and a collection of Edwardian errata. It belongs there.
