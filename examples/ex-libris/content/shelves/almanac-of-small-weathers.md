## Concerning the Plate

The plate prepared for this volume is a simple device: a single weather-vane rendered in
three lines, with the cardinal points inscribed in small capitals and an open scroll
beneath bearing the motto *SECUNDUM DIEM* &#8212; &#8220;day by day.&#8221; It seemed
fitting for a book whose entire ambition is to mark one single year from one single window.

## Physical Description

An octavo diary volume, 366 numbered pages, the last an errata leaf correcting an earlier
edition's confusion between *stratus* and *stratocumulus*. Original publisher's cloth,
now badly foxed; the spine lettering nearly gone. Previous owner's signature on the
front free endpaper (&#8220;H. Dove, 1893&#8221;) preserved and transcribed in the
accession slip.

## The Reader&#39;s Note

Miss Dove's entries are short, often only a line. They describe what the sky was doing at
seven in the morning and what the kitchen garden was doing at noon. Taken day by day they
are unremarkable. Read across a season they become almost unbearable in their accumulated
attention.

> *April 14.* A thin rain from the west. The starlings have not come back.

> *April 15.* The starlings have come back.
