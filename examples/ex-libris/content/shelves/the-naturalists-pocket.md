## Concerning the Plate

An oval plate, four inches on the long axis, showing a rowan leaf and a curlew&#39;s feather
crossed as though they were arms of a quarter. The motto, *VADE MECUM*, is set in small
capitals along the upper curve. A number-field at the lower edge has been filled in, by
hand, with the accession number.

## Physical Description

A small pocket-sized volume bound in limp vellum, stained around the edges in such a way
that the front cover faintly smells of wet heather even now. The interior is half printed
text and half manuscript: plates of wildflowers alternate with pages onto which the previous
owner has pressed the actual plants and recorded the place and date of collection in a
careful, slightly cramped, copperplate hand.

## On the Pleasures of the Book

There is no single passage to quote, because the book is not of that kind. Its pleasures
are accumulative: the way a cotton-grass from 1896 sits opposite a printed plate of the
same plant, and the two fail to match except in the most general outline. The book is the
record of a person comparing the world to a book and deciding, carefully and without
resentment, in favour of the world.
