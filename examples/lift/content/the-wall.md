The depot gave us four bays and we gave each one a different job. Together they hold a little over 130 problems at any given time, graded V0 through V10, taped in five colors that rotate with the reset — pick a color at the desk and every hold in that color, on that bay, belongs to the same route.

## The four bays

**Bay One — Slab, 15°.** Low-angle and footwork-heavy, where balance matters more than strength. Where we send new climbers first, and where a lot of strong climbers get quietly humbled.

**Bay Two — Vertical, 90°.** The depot's original tram entrance, now dead straight up. The widest grade spread in the building, from beginner-friendly to genuinely hard.

**Bay Three — Overhang, 20° past vertical.** Power and core, short problems that ask a lot in six or seven moves. Where most of the grunting happens.

**Bay Four — The Cave.** A horizontal roof traverse under the lowest run of the old truss line, climbed sideways more than up. Not for a first session, but the bay most regulars name as their favorite.

## The reset

We reset one bay every week on a rotating four-week cycle, so the whole gym turns over roughly once a month without ever going stale all at once. Reset happens Monday, which is why we're closed — the crew needs the wall to themselves, and honestly, so does the tape gun. Follow the reset board by the front desk to see which bay is due, or check [visiting hours](@/visit.md) before you come by.

## Grading, briefly

We grade on the V-scale, same as most gyms: V0 for a first attempt, V10 for the setting crew's idea of a joke. Grades are a starting point, not a promise — a V3 slab problem and a V3 overhang problem can feel like they belong to different sports. Ask the desk if a grade seems off; we adjust the board based on what actually happens on the wall, not just what looked hard on paper. New to grading altogether? Our [classes](@/classes.md) cover it in the first ten minutes.
