Lift is easiest to find by looking for the building nothing else on Kettle Street looks like — a long brick shed with three rows of sawtooth skylights and a set of old loading doors, now glass, propped open on warm evenings. There's no sign taller than the doorway; the depot does most of the advertising.

## Hours

Tuesday to Friday, 7:00am to 10:00pm. Saturday and Sunday, 9:00am to 8:00pm. Closed Monday for reset — the crew rebuilds one bay of routes and the tape gun gets the day it deserves.

## Getting here

**14 Kettle Street, Depot Quarter.** The depot sits two blocks north of the old rail junction; if you're walking from the river path, follow the exposed brick wall until you hit the loading doors. Street parking on Kettle is free after 6:00pm and all day Sunday; a small lot behind the building covers the rest of the week for a flat $4.

## What to bring

Nothing, if it's your first time — shoes and a chalk bag are included with every visit, [day pass or membership](@/membership.md). If you climb elsewhere already, bring your own shoes and a brush; we'll skip the intro talk and point you straight at the [reset board](@/the-wall.md).

## First-timers

Budget five minutes at the desk for a waiver and, if you want it, a free orientation covering how to fall, how the tape system works, and where the easier problems are on whichever bay reset most recently. No booking required — just come by during open hours and ask for a walk-through, or read [more about Lift](@/about.md) first.
