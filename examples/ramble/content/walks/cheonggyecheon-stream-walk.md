There is something unusual about walking below street level in the middle of a city. The Cheonggyecheon stream runs through a channel cut below the roads, and once you descend the steps, the traffic noise fades to a dull murmur.

## Along the Water

The stream is shallow and clear, flowing over a bed of carefully placed stones. Stepping stones cross the water at intervals, and in the evening light, the surface catches reflections of the buildings above. The walkway runs along both banks, smooth concrete edged with low plantings.

In February the vegetation is mostly dormant. Brown grasses line the banks, and the trees are bare frameworks against the sky. But the water itself is alive, moving steadily eastward with a quiet persistence.

## The Bridges

Twenty-two bridges cross the Cheonggyecheon along its length, each one different. Some are stone, some steel, some decorated with historical motifs. Walking beneath them creates a rhythm, a steady punctuation to the flow of the walk. The light changes under each bridge, cooler and blue-tinged, before opening up again.

## Evening Observations

- Office workers in long coats walking briskly in the opposite direction
- A photographer with a tripod capturing the last light on the water
- Two herons standing motionless in the shallows near Gwanggyo
- The smell of roasting chestnuts drifting down from a street vendor above

## Notes

- The path is well-lit and suitable for evening walks
- Flat and fully paved, accessible to all fitness levels
- Restrooms are available at several points along the route
- The walk is best started from Cheonggye Plaza near Gwanghwamun
