November in Seoul belongs to the trees. The ginkgo along the northern approach to Namsan had turned a uniform bright yellow, and the maples on the southern slope were deep red, almost brown at the edges.

## The Northern Approach

I started at the Namsan Library entrance and took the paved path that curves upward through dense forest. The canopy overhead was a patchwork of color: yellow ginkgo, red maple, the persistent dark green of pine. Fallen leaves covered the edges of the path in drifts.

The air smelled of damp earth and decomposing leaves, a clean autumn smell that is distinctive and brief. In a few weeks the trees would be bare and the smell would be gone until next year.

## The Wall Path

The old Seoul city wall traces a partial circuit around Namsan. The stones are dark and uneven, some sections original and some restored. Walking alongside the wall, you can look down into the neighborhoods below, where the city presses close on all sides.

The wall path is quieter than the main summit route. Most visitors take the cable car or the direct road. Here, the only company was a few runners and an older couple walking slowly with cameras.

## Summit Circuit

At the top, the observation area offered a panoramic view of Seoul under thin autumn haze. The Han River was a silver line in the distance. The mountains ringing the city were in full color, each ridge a slightly different shade.

I skipped the tower and continued on the southern descent path, which drops steeply through an older section of forest. The maples here were at their peak, heavy with color, branches nearly meeting overhead.

## Notes

- The full loop takes about two hours at a comfortable pace
- Peak autumn color in Seoul typically falls in the first two weeks of November
- The northern approach from Namsan Library is less crowded than the Myeongdong side
- Bring a light layer; the summit is noticeably cooler and windier
