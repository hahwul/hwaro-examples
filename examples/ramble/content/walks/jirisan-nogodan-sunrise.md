The alarm went off at 4:30 AM. Outside the shelter, the air was below freezing and the sky was perfectly clear, which is exactly what you want for a sunrise hike.

## The Dark Ascent

Headlamp on, I started up the Nogodan trail from the Seongsamjae parking area. The path is well-maintained, wide enough for two people side by side, and the grade is steady rather than punishing. In the dark, the world shrinks to the circle of light ahead and the sound of boots on frozen earth.

Other headlamps moved ahead and behind me on the trail, small points of light bobbing through the black forest. Nobody talked. The only sounds were footsteps, breathing, and the occasional crack of a frozen branch.

## The Summit

I reached the Nogodan summit platform forty minutes before sunrise. The temperature gauge on my pack read minus eight degrees. The wind was moderate but persistent, finding every gap in layers.

The eastern horizon was still dark when I arrived, but by the time I had settled into a spot behind the windbreak, a faint band of grey had appeared along the mountain line. It grew slowly: grey to pale blue, pale blue to amber.

The sun broke the horizon at 7:14 AM. It came fast once it started, a bright point that swelled into a disc in what felt like seconds. The ridgelines of the Jirisan range were backlit in sharp silhouette, layer after layer receding into the distance. The valley floors were filled with cloud, white and still, as though the mountains were islands in a frozen sea.

## The Return

The descent was warmer and brighter. The frost on the trail had begun to melt, making the wooden steps slippery in places. The forest looked entirely different in daylight: bare oaks, dark pines, and the occasional flash of a jay moving between branches.

Back at the trailhead by 8:30 AM. A thermos of hot barley tea in the car completed the morning.

## Trail Notes

- Reservation required for Nogodan access; book online in advance
- Bring thermal layers, gloves, and a balaclava for winter sunrise hikes
- The summit platform has a windbreak but no shelter
- Microspikes recommended from December through February
