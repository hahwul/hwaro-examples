The documents compiled in this archive represent the complete paper trail of the late nineteenth-century conspiracy that took place between Transylvania and England. Bram Stoker's novel is unique in its epistolary structure, composed entirely of diary entries, letters, telegrams, ship logs, and newspaper clippings. By presenting the narrative through these fragmented, personal mediums, the story achieves a striking sense of realism and psychological immediacy. 

This digital recreation is designed to evoke the tactile feel of a clerk's private file or a Victorian scrapbook. The parchment tones and traditional typography reflect the era in which the events occurred, while the dark theme invokes the midnight atmosphere of gothic horror. The transcription of these documents preserves the dates of recording (running from May to November) and the medium of communication. We invite researchers and enthusiasts of gothic literature to explore the files, follow the chronological order of events, and experience the dread of the Harker expedition anew. May the truth recorded in these pages serve as a reminder of the darkness that lurks just beyond the boundaries of our reason.
