My dearest Lucy,—Forgive me that I have not written to you for so long, but I have been very busy. I am working hard at my shorthand and typing, for I want to be useful to Jonathan when we are married. He writes to me that he has arrived safely in Transylvania, but his letters are very short, and he seems to have so much business to attend to that he has little time for correspondence. I sometimes feel a strange anxiety about him, though I know it is foolish, as he is so capable and the Count seems to treat him with great hospitality.

<!-- more -->

How I wish you were here in London with me! I long to hear all about your social season and the suitors who are surely vying for your hand. You must tell me everything in your next letter. London is damp and crowded as usual, but my studies keep me occupied during the long days. I have been practicing reading railway timetables as well, so that I can help Jonathan plan his travels when he returns. Write to me soon, my dear, and tell me of your health and happiness.
