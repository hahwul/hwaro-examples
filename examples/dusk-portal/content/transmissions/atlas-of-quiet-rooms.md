A handheld atlas for wayfinding in overlit civic buildings. The brief was unusual: visitors were having trouble finding *quiet rooms* in a building designed entirely around its atriums. The atlas answers the question *where can I sit and not be asked anything for twenty minutes?*

## Scope

- Cover and twelve fold-out plates.
- Lavender and amber, on uncoated paper.
- Pocket-sized. Prints at the reception desk.

## Outcome

The reception desk reported a forty-percent reduction in wayfinding questions in the first week. The atlas now ships with every visitor pass.

> Maps do not only tell you where things are. Good maps tell you where you might recover.

This remains one of the studio&rsquo;s favourite transmissions.
