In modern web design, gradients have become ubiquitous. They are an easy way to add depth and interest to an otherwise flat interface. However, for the Ultraviolet theme, we made a conscious decision to ban them entirely.

## The Power of Pure Color

When you remove the ability to blend colors, you are forced to make strong, definitive choices. A solid block of neon purple against a near-black background creates a visual vibration that a gradient simply cannot match. It feels raw, energetic, and slightly rebellious.

### Creating Depth Without Gradients

Without gradients, we rely on other techniques to create a sense of space:
*   **Layering:** Placing elements over structural background lines.
*   **Borders:** Using varying border widths and colors to define boundaries.
*   **Shadows:** Using hard, offset box-shadows (e.g., `box-shadow: 15px 15px 0 var(--primary-color)`) instead of soft, blurred ones. This adds a physical, almost tactile quality to interactive elements like our article cards.

By constraining our toolset, we've found a new, distinct voice for this design.
