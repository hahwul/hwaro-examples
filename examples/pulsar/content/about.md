## The Observatory

Pulsar is a deep-space radio observatory blog dedicated to the rhythms hidden in the cosmos. We listen to the universe and translate its signals into sound, music, and meaning.

Every star has a heartbeat. Every nebula hums at a frequency. Every black hole resonates with a tone that stretches across light-years. We capture those transmissions and share them here.

## Our Mission

We exist at the intersection of astrophysics and music. Our writers are astronomers, musicians, sound designers, and signal processing engineers who share a common obsession: the music of the spheres.

## What We Cover

- **Stellar Rhythms** -- Periodic signals from pulsars, variable stars, and binary systems translated into audible frequencies.
- **Cosmic Composition** -- Original music inspired by radio telescope data and spectral analysis.
- **Signal Processing** -- The technical craft of converting electromagnetic radiation into waveforms the human ear can appreciate.
- **Observatory Reports** -- Field notes from radio telescope arrays around the world.
