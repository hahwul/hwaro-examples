Daguerreotypes were usually enclosed in a protective case, often made of leather and lined with velvet or silk. The image itself was protected by a cover glass and a brass mat, which served as a frame.

The mat often featured an oval, arch, or geometric opening, creating a vignette effect that focused attention on the subject. The intricate stamping and texture of these brass mats are a hallmark of the daguerreotype aesthetic, something we aim to replicate here with solid borders and complex box shadows.
