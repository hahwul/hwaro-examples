Noise as a genre has a low tolerance for the word "intense" used as a synonym for "loud," and *Everything Bites Back* leans on that conflation more than its maker probably realizes. Feral Static works almost entirely with contact microphones on scrap metal, run through a chain of cheap distortion pedals, and the record is committed to that palette with genuine discipline. What it lacks, across most of its thirty-one minutes, is the dynamic range that makes hostile music actually land as hostile rather than just present.

<!-- more -->

The exception is "Doorframe," track four, which drops the distortion almost entirely for ninety seconds and lets the raw contact-mic signal — metal groaning under real physical pressure, audibly un-processed — sit in near silence before the pedal chain slams back in. It is the single best moment on the record because it understands something the rest of the album does not: that noise music needs contrast to register as violent. Without a quiet register to violate, loud just becomes the ambient condition, and the ear adjusts within a minute.

Fenn's desk has heard worse records built on this exact mistake, and Feral Static clearly has the raw material — the tone here is genuinely unpleasant in the way this genre wants — to make something more dynamically considered next time. *Everything Bites Back* is a demo of intent more than a fully realized statement, competent but rarely surprising, loud in a way that stops registering as loud about six tracks in.
