Concrete Fathom knows exactly what dub techno is supposed to sound like, and *Low Tide Index* delivers it without a single wasted gesture: chords that arrive soaked in reverb and refuse to resolve, low end mixed with the physical weight the genre demands, hi-hats placed with the patience of someone who has clearly logged serious hours in a proper room system. As a technical exercise in the idiom, this is close to flawless.

<!-- more -->

The trouble is that "close to flawless idiom exercise" is also a fair description of at least a dozen records released this year, and *Low Tide Index* rarely steps outside the idiom long enough to distinguish itself. "Slack Water" is the clear highlight, built around a detuned chord stab that sits a few cents outside where the ear expects it, generating a genuine low-level tension that the rest of the record could have used more of. Elsewhere, tracks like "Neap" and "Ballast Line" are so faithful to the genre's established grammar that they start to feel less like compositions than well-executed citations.

None of this makes *Low Tide Index* a bad record — it is a genuinely good one, well-sequenced and mixed with real care, the kind of album that will sound excellent on a system built for it. But dub techno at its best makes the familiar feel newly strange, and this record is content to make the familiar feel merely correct. Voss's desk expects Concrete Fathom to take the actual risk next time.
