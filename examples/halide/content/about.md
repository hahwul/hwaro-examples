Halide Press started in the back half of a rented storefront darkroom that a co-op of five printers shares by the hour. The lease came with an enlarger nobody used anymore, a stack of trays gone brittle at the corners, and a safelight bulb that had clearly been burning red over the same square yard of counter for longer than anyone currently paying rent had been a member. The first poster in [the series](/posters/) — Developer Bath — was drawn directly from that counter, on a night when the darkroom was booked but the desk wasn't.

## Two Inks, No Exceptions

Every print in the series is screen-printed by hand in exactly two passes: a black plate laid down first and heavy, then a safelight-red plate registered on top for whatever in the original scene would actually glow, drip, stain, or burn. The constraint is not an aesthetic default — early proofs experimented with a warm gray third ink for shadow, and every one of them looked less like a darkroom than the two-color originals did. Chemistry doesn't need a midtone. It needs contrast, and enough red to remind you the whole room the objects live in is lit by exactly one color.

## Editions

Runs are small on purpose: forty impressions per plate, numbered, on a 100-pound uncoated stock heavy enough to survive being pinned rather than framed. Once a run sells out, the screens are reclaimed and the design is retired — nothing in this series gets reprinted. The six posters currently up on the site represent the whole run to date; a seventh plate is being drawn now, from a stop-bath tray nobody has gotten around to photographing yet.

## Visiting

The co-op darkroom keeps open printing hours on weekend afternoons, and the current poster run hangs on the drying line along the back wall for anyone who wants to see the ink before it ships. There is no mailing list and no storefront beyond this site — editions move by word of mouth, one darkroom to the next, which has so far kept the run sizes honest.
