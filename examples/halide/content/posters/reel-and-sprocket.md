Everything before this poster in the series happens in usable light, however dim. Loading a stainless developing reel happens in absolute darkness, by feel alone, which makes it the one step every printer remembers learning badly before they learned it well — film buckling off the track, a whole roll ruined by a kink nobody could see coming.

<!-- more -->

The reel itself is a spiral of wire track wound around a central hub, sized so that a strip of film threaded in at the outer edge follows the spiral inward without ever touching itself, leaving both sides of the emulsion fully exposed to chemistry once it goes into the tank. The perforations along the film's edge — the sprocket holes drawn down every poster in this series — exist for exactly this kind of mechanical handling: the same teeth that pulled the film through a camera one frame at a time now keep it tracking straight onto the reel.

We put this print last in the release order for a reason that has nothing to do with the darkroom sequence, where loading obviously comes first. It is the piece that names the signature running through the whole series — that red punched strip down the left edge of every poster here started as a literal drawing of this reel's sprocket track, before it became the mark that ties the other five prints together, [Developer Bath](/posters/developer-bath/) included.
