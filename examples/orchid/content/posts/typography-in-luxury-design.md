In luxury design, typography carries enormous weight. The right typeface pairing can elevate a simple blog into something that feels curated and refined.

## The Dual-Font Approach

This theme pairs two complementary typefaces:

- **Cormorant Garamond** for headings — a high-contrast serif with elegant proportions that whisper sophistication and old-world charm
- **Nunito Sans** for body text — a rounded, friendly sans-serif that ensures effortless readability on screens

The contrast between ornate headings and clean body text creates a visual rhythm that keeps readers engaged.

## Sizing for Impact

Luxury typography favors generous sizing and spacing:

1. **Hero titles** — oversized, with dramatic letter-spacing for maximum presence
2. **Section headings** — bold with subtle glow effects to establish hierarchy
3. **Body text** — comfortable `1.75` line-height for extended reading sessions
4. **Captions and metadata** — refined, small, with muted colors for visual quiet

## Glow as Typography Enhancement

On dark backgrounds, a carefully tuned text-shadow can make headings appear to float above the page:

```css
h1 {
  text-shadow: 0 0 20px rgba(218, 112, 214, 0.6),
               0 0 60px rgba(218, 112, 214, 0.2);
}
```

The key is restraint — reserve strong glow effects for primary headings only. Secondary headings use lighter touches, and body text uses none.

## The Result

When font pairing, sizing, and effects work in concert, the typography itself becomes a design element — not just a vehicle for content, but part of the aesthetic experience.
