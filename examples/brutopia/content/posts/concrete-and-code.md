There is an old argument in architecture: should a building reveal its structure or conceal it behind ornament? Brutalism answered clearly. The concrete is the design.

<!-- more -->

## Structure as Interface

In software, the same question surfaces. Should code be wrapped in abstraction layers, or should its logic remain visible and direct? Brutopia takes the architectural stance: show the load-bearing walls.

## Honest Materials

Brutalist buildings use concrete, steel, and glass without apology. In this theme, the materials are equally plain:

- **Monospace type** -- no variable-width illusions
- **Flat color** -- no depth tricks, no artificial dimension
- **Hard borders** -- structural dividers, not decorative lines

## Why Dark

The dark palette is not mere preference. It inverts the default assumption that the web must be white. A dark ground forces content to earn its visibility. Text that appears on a dark surface has been deliberately placed there.

> Good design is as little design as possible. -- Dieter Rams

The same principle applies to color. Where a single red accent appears, it marks something structural: a heading border, a hover state, a navigational signal. Nothing more.
