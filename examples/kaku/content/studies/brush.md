The calligraphy brush (fude) is a highly specialized instrument, crafted by combining different types of animal hair to achieve a balance between stiffness and water retention. The outer hairs (uwa-ge) are selected for their smoothness and ability to form a fine tip, while the core hairs (shin-ge) provide structural strength and hold the reservoir of sumi ink.

Common hair types include sheep (yogo), which is soft and holds a large volume of ink, and weasel (itachi), which is stiff, springy, and provides precise control. Many brushes are hybrid blends (kembou), designed to offer the best of both worlds. A calligrapher must choose a brush that fits their specific script style and hand strength: softer brushes require a highly developed technique to maintain structure, while stiffer brushes are more forgiving for beginners.

Proper maintenance of the fude is essential to prevent the hairs from rotting or shedding. After every session, the brush must be washed thoroughly in cool or lukewarm water. Gently massage the head of the brush under running water to release the dried glue and ink accumulated near the handle. Never use soap or hot water, as they strip the natural oils from the hair. Hang the brush tip-down in a well-ventilated area until it is completely dry. Storing a damp brush in a closed box will lead to mold and ruin the delicate hairs.
