Welcome to Kaku, a calligrapher's notebook and digital garden. This project is a curated repository of notes, recipes, and reflections on the practice of Shodo (traditional Japanese calligraphy), sumi-ink preparation, and character studies. The term *kaku* means "to write" or "to paint" in Japanese, reflecting the dual nature of calligraphy as both literary and visual art.

The garden is maintained by Kenji Sato, a practitioner of traditional ink arts based in the quiet hills of Kamakura. After years of study under master calligraphers, this digital space was established to compile loose notes on brushwork techniques, the behavior of different washi papers, and the chemistry of pine soot ink. Unlike a structured textbook, this garden is designed to grow organically, with interlinked pages tracing the relationships between materials, bodily posture, and artistic expression. 

Whether you are a beginner picking up a brush for the first time or a seasoned practitioner researching the properties of animal-glue sizing, we hope these studies offer a quiet space for reflection and a practical guide for your Shodo practice.
