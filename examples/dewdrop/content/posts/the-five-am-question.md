There is a common misconception that waking at five in the morning requires extraordinary discipline. In truth, it requires something far simpler: a reason to get up.

## Reframing the Alarm

When the alarm sounds in the dark, the body does not ask "what time is it?" It asks "why should I move?" If the only answer is obligation -- work, commute, deadlines -- the pillow will always win. But if the answer is something you genuinely look forward to, the equation changes entirely.

## Building a Morning Worth Waking For

Consider what brings you quiet joy. Perhaps it is:

- Twenty minutes with a book before the house stirs
- A slow pour-over coffee made with attention
- A short walk while the streets are still empty
- Ten minutes of stretching in the half-light

The specific activity matters less than the quality of attention you bring to it. The goal is not productivity. The goal is presence.

## Starting Small

You do not need to leap from 7:30 to 5:00 in a single night. Move your alarm back by fifteen minutes each week. Let your body adjust. Let the new rhythm feel natural rather than forced.

The morning will meet you where you are. You only need to show up.
