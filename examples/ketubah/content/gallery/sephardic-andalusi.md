## On the Design

The Andalusi manner, inherited from the Jewish communities of mediaeval Spain and carried by the expulsion of 1492 into the Maghreb and the Levant, is characterised by a rigorous geometric interlace - the strapwork of eight-pointed and sixteen-pointed stars, woven over and under in apparent infinity. The present contract uses a central eight-pointed star, constructed from a sixteen-point division of the circle, with the interior subdivided into three concentric stars of decreasing size.

The border interlace along top and bottom is of the classical Andalusi type, where parallel strands cross and recross in a rhythm which continues indefinitely - indicated here by its truncation only at the border of the plate. A fuller execution would continue the interlace around all four sides.

## On the Lettering

The text is in the Sephardic square script, with majuscules drawn after the Arabic calligraphic tradition which has long coexisted with the Hebrew letterforms in the books and documents of al-Andalus and the Maghreb. The couple requested that a brief inscription in Arabic be included upon the verso of the document, and this was lettered separately in the same week.

## Provenance

Commissioned by the Toledano and Benaim families for a ceremony held in Tetouan in the north of Morocco during the summer of 2025. The families descend, upon both sides, from those expelled from Spain in 1492 who took refuge in the Maghrebi cities; the design was requested explicitly to honour that heritage.
