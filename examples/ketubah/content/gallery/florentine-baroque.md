## On the Design

The Florentine Baroque style is characterised by the swelling cartouche: a scrolling foliate frame which turns and returns upon itself, enclosing the lettering block in a moment of architectural repose. The studio has adapted this ornament from the printed books of the seventeenth century, where the engraver's art produced similar cartouches for the title pages of botanical and theological works.

A pair of heraldic supporters - here rendered as abstracted birds in lapis - flank the cartouche and recall the ancient Roman and Florentine traditions of paired symbolic figures upon funerary and commemorative inscriptions. The pomegranate finial at the crown is both a traditional symbol of abundance and a playful echo of the Medici arms.

## On the Lettering

The text is set in the Italian humanist manner, with majuscules raised in shell gilt at the commencement of each paragraph, and the running text in oak-gall ink. The couple requested a trilingual text - the formal declarations in Hebrew and Latin, and a personal note in Italian - and the studio accommodated the three scripts upon a single ruled sheet with particular attention to their visual balance.

## Provenance

Commissioned by the Levi and Rossi families for a ceremony at the Villa di Lappeggi in Tuscany upon the summer solstice of 2025. The families kindly consented to reproduction of the design in the studio portfolio.
