## On the Design

This contract demonstrates the studio's contemporary manner - an approach which has grown in demand over the last decade and which pairs the rigour of traditional lettering with the restraint of modernist design. The ornamental frame is reduced to a single ruled rectangle; the decorative device at the head is reduced to an asterisk of eight strokes; and the signature band is a single horizontal ruling with the names in italic beneath.

The design is not an absence of ornament but an assertion of it. Every element has been placed with the same care as the more exuberant designs in this portfolio - the asterisk is struck upon a grid; the rectangle is ruled to a precise mathematical ratio; the lettering is calibrated to the golden section between the horizontal lines.

## On the Lettering

The text is lettered in Sabon italic, a typeface designed by Jan Tschichold in the 1960s after the sixteenth-century forms of Claude Garamond. Its clarity and warmth make it particularly well-suited to the modern minimal style, where the letterforms must carry much of the emotional weight of the document.

## Provenance

Commissioned by a couple who met at the Rietveld Academie and who specified that the contract should "contain no ornament which does not serve the text." The studio took this instruction as a creative constraint and produced a design which has since become a reference piece for subsequent modern commissions.
