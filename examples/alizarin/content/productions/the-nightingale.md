Opera Klein tours this reduced-orchestration Nightingale to houses that were never built for opera, which meant the rig had to be small, fast to hang, and forgiving of ceilings that change from one venue to the next. It also meant that the production's single boldest choice — a full company wash in R026 Bright Red at the moment the Emperor faces Death — had to read the same in a black-box studio and a nineteenth-century proscenium.

<!-- more -->

The solution was to keep the entire rest of the evening deliberately cool and underlit: R60 No Color Blue and open white hold the court in a restrained, almost archival palette for eighty-eight minutes, so that when the red system finally fires, there is no other saturated color anywhere in the rig's memory to compete with it. Twelve Source Four profiles carry the red wash, gelled in the field rather than pre-cut, because Opera Klein's touring stock rotates between venues and the designer wanted control over exactly which units carried the color on any given night.

**Gel schedule**

| Position | Fixture | Gel | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| FOH, 6x | Source Four 36° | R60 No Color Blue | Court wash, entire evening |
| Overhead, 12x | Source Four 26° | R026 Bright Red | Death cue, held in reserve |
| Side booms, 4x | Fresnel 6" | Open white, ND filter | Fisherman and Nightingale isolations |
| Practical, 1x | Par 16 | R026 Bright Red | Mechanical bird's eye, throughout |

**Sample cue**

| Cue | Trigger | Look | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 41 | Death's entrance | No change — court wash holds | Deliberately withheld to build tension |
| 47 | "Death, give me back my emperor's regalia" | Overhead red snaps to full, court wash cuts | The evening's only hard cue-to-cue snap |
| 48 | Nightingale's song resumes | Red bleeds down to 20% over 45 counts | Longest fade in the show |
| 52 | Final tableau | Court wash restores, red holds at 8% as residual | Red never fully leaves the stage again |

Fifty-four cues across a ninety-minute run, and the plot book flags cue 47 in the margin with a single instruction to future re-lighters: "don't soften this. It should feel like a mistake in the board." For an earlier study in the same restraint-then-release structure, see [Winterreise](/productions/winterreise/).
