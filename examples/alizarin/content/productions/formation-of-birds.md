Talia Osei's piece for nine dancers is structured as a single forty-minute approach to a sunrise that the choreography withholds until the final ninety seconds. The lighting had to withhold with it — every earlier cue in the piece is a variation on the same amber system, never brighter than 60%, so that the eventual full release reads as an event rather than a plateau.

<!-- more -->

The rig leans on eight LED wash fixtures hung low and raked at flat angles across the floor, gelled and gated through R019 Fire even though they're color-mixing units — the designer's note in the plot book is blunt about why: "tungsten-warm LED read as clinical under this ceiling height; gel it like a conventional and it stops looking like a fixture." Six overhead Source Four profiles carry the formation blocking in hard-edged shafts that shift position between sections, tracking the birds' changing geometry from scatter to wedge to column.

**Gel schedule**

| Position | Fixture | Gel | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low floor wash, 8x | LED wash (gelled) | R019 Fire | Base amber system, all sections |
| Overhead, 6x | Source Four 26° | R02 Bastard Amber | Formation shafts, tracked per section |
| Cyc, 3x | Cyc LED | R019 Fire, capped | Horizon line, sections 3 and 5 only |
| Booms, 2x | Fresnel 6" | R60 No Color Blue | Cold edge, final ninety seconds only |

**Sample cue**

| Cue | Trigger | Look | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 8 | Section 2 downbeat | Overhead shafts reconfigure to wedge | Six-count snap, no crossfade |
| 21 | Section 4 stillness | Wash drops to 30%, formation shafts hold | Longest static look in the piece, 90 seconds |
| 33 | Final approach | Cyc uncaps, blue booms cross-fade in against amber | The only cue where two colors share the stage |
| 35 | Last beat | Full release to 100% across all amber systems | Sunrise arrives with 4 seconds left in the piece |

Thirty-eight cues, most of them level changes inside the same amber palette rather than color changes — the plot book calls this "a dimmer piece wearing a lighting designer's clothes," which is as close to a mission statement as this rig gets.
