Kammeroper Noord staged Schubert's cycle in the round, baritone and pianist sharing a nine-meter circle of floor with the audience seated on all four sides. There was nowhere to hide a lighting rig and nothing to hide it behind, so the design became the only scenery the production allowed itself.

<!-- more -->

The rig is eight units total: four Source Four 19-degree profiles on a low truss ring for the traveling pool, and four side-light booms gelled full blue to hold the perimeter at near-black. The pool starts at a warm 2900K equivalent for "Gute Nacht" and cools by measured steps across the cycle's twenty-four songs, crossing into L201 full blue by "Der Leiermann" so that the wanderer ends the evening standing in a color with no warmth left in it at all.

**Gel schedule**

| Position | Fixture | Gel | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Truss ring, 4x | Source Four 19° | R3407 Hamburg Frost + dimmer-only warm | Traveling pool, songs 1–14 |
| Truss ring, 4x | Source Four 19° | L201 Full C.T. Blue | Traveling pool, songs 15–24 |
| Booms, 4x | Fresnel 6" | L201 Full C.T. Blue | Perimeter hold at 8% |
| Floor, 1x | Par can | R026 Bright Red | Practical strike, "Der Leiermann" only |

**Sample cue**

| Cue | Trigger | Look | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 14 | End of "Rast" | Pool cools one step, perimeter lifts to 11% | First visible blue in the pool gel |
| 19 | Top of "Die Nebensonnen" | Perimeter cuts to 4%, pool holds | Isolates baritone from pianist for the only time in the cycle |
| 24 | Final bar | Pool locks full L201, red practical fades in over 40 counts | House holds three seconds before blackout |

Twenty-six cues across seventy minutes, no cue longer than a six-count fade. The design note pinned inside the plot book reads: "the room gets colder, not darker — keep the audience able to see his face until the last page."
