Ingrid Solberg's evening-length work asks a simple question of the lighting: what does it look like to hold the ten minutes of dusk — after sunset, before streetlights matter — for a full seventy-minute run without ever resolving into either daylight or night. The answer the plot settled on was a single lavender-to-blue system with no other color anywhere in the rig, moved so slowly that most audience members never catch it changing.

<!-- more -->

Fourteen fixtures across three positions carry G780 Lavender at varying densities, cross-faded against each other in fades that run as long as six minutes — well past the point where a human eye tracks a lighting change as a cue rather than as weather. The company's twelve dancers spend the piece in near-continuous motion through a space that never stops almost-changing, which is the effect Solberg wanted: a dusk that behaves like a held breath rather than a countdown.

**Gel schedule**

| Position | Fixture | Gel | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Overhead grid, 8x | Source Four 36° | G780 Lavender | Primary wash, slow cross-fades throughout |
| Side booms, 4x | Fresnel 6" | G780 Lavender, half CTB | Density variation, opposite the overhead grid |
| Cyc, 2x | Cyc LED | Matched lavender-to-blue, no gel | Horizon line, degrades toward blue after minute 40 |

**Sample cue**

| Cue | Trigger | Look | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 | Opening | Overhead grid at 70%, cyc at first density | Six-minute fade in from cue 1, felt more than seen |
| 27 | Midpoint | Booms overtake overhead grid as primary source | Slowest cue in the piece, eight-minute cross-fade |
| 41 | Minute 40 mark | Cyc begins degrading toward blue | First moment the palette leaves true lavender |
| 55 | Final section | All systems converge at low, even density | No cue after this one — the piece ends inside the fade |

Fifty-six cues logged, average fade time just under three minutes — the longest average hold time of any plot in this book, and the one the plot book's cover page calls "the one where the board op has the least to do and the most to answer for."

Vantage Dance Collective previously commissioned [Formation of Birds](/productions/formation-of-birds/), a faster, harder-edged piece built on the opposite instinct: change constantly, resolve rarely. Reading the two plots side by side is the clearest record of how the same room and the same company can ask for entirely different light.
