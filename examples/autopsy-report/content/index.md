## Post-Mortem Analysis
Structural integrity within the post-observational environment.

## Case Notes
Each report begins with a clear chronology: the observed condition, the prior state, and the interval between them. Before any cause is named, the available evidence is laid out in plain order so that a reader can retrace the path without having to take the author on trust. Conclusions reached without this scaffolding rarely survive a second reading.

## Method of Inquiry
The instrument matters less than the discipline. A flashlight and a notebook, used consistently, will outperform a sophisticated rig used only when convenient. The aim is not novelty but coverage: every surface examined, every measurement recorded, every assumption named. Findings that do not fit the working theory are recorded separately and revisited later.

## Reading the Record
A finished report should be legible to someone who was not in the room. Diagrams are labelled, terms are defined on first use, and each numbered observation can be located in the underlying notes. The reader should be able to disagree with the conclusion while still find the path to it intact.
