The standard staggered keyboard is a relic of typewriter design. Modern ergonomic layouts, such as ortholinear or split grids, align the keys with the natural movement of human fingers.

Transitioning to an ergonomic layout requires a period of adjustment, but the long-term benefits in reducing wrist strain are undeniable. It represents a functional evolution in how we interact with our machines.
