Minimalism is not about a lack of detail; it is about an extreme concentration of intent. When you strip away the distraction of color, a garment is forced to speak entirely through its silhouette, material weight, and drape.

### The Power of Stark Opposites
By utilizing deep, light-absorbing matte blacks and clinical, reflective optical whites, designers are constructing silhouettes that slice through three-dimensional space with space-age precision. 

> "Color is a distraction. Contrast is an absolute truth."

This season's runways are a stark validation of this philosophy, presenting heavy double-faced wool structures that retain their sculptural form independently of the wearer's organic contour.
