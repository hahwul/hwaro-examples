# Hexagonal Metamorphosis

This tessellation demonstrates the classical Escher technique of metamorphosis, where regular geometric shapes gradually transform into recognizable creatures. The pattern begins with simple hexagons at the periphery and evolves into interlocking lizards toward the center.

<!-- more -->

## Pattern Analysis

The hexagonal grid provides the underlying structure, with each hexagon subdivided into three diamond shapes. These diamonds then morph through several intermediate stages:

1. **Pure Geometry**: Regular hexagons
2. **Suggestion**: Subtle curves hint at organic forms
3. **Emergence**: Recognizable limbs and features appear
4. **Full Form**: Complete lizard figures interlock seamlessly

## Mathematical Foundation

This design employs a **P6M wallpaper group** symmetry, featuring:
- 6-fold rotational symmetry
- Mirror lines through vertices
- Perfect tiling with no gaps or overlaps

The transformation maintains the tessellation properties throughout, ensuring that even at maximum abstraction, the pattern tiles infinitely without interruption.

## Color Theory

The piece uses Escher's signature palette:
- Background: Light grey (#d4d4d4)
- Primary forms: Black (#1a1a1a)
- Negative space: White (#f8f8f8)
- Accent details: Gold (#d4af37)

This high-contrast approach emphasizes the figure-ground relationship, a key element in Escher's work where positive and negative spaces have equal visual weight.
