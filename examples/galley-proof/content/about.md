## Editorial staff

**Editor-in-chief.** Marguerite Holroyd.
*In her seventeenth year in the chair; unwilling to relinquish the blue pencil.*

**Copy chief.** L. Teasdale.
*Responsible for all typographical matters; maintains the house style sheet.*

**Proofreaders.** J. Okafor, R. Bhattacharya, T. Sinclair.
*Three passes per galley, in rotation. Queries referred to the editor when unresolved.*

**Compositor.** The Leighton Press.
*Sets all text hot-metal for the print edition; facsimile for the present web galley.*

## To contributors

All manuscripts are to be submitted in clean copy, double-spaced, with the author's name and the working title on the first folio. Manuscripts exceeding six thousand words are considered "long," and will be scheduled for a later issue.

Upon acceptance, the manuscript will pass through the following stages &mdash;

1. Edit, sent to the author for approval of the blue-pencil marks.
2. First galley, set in type and returned with the first pass of corrections.
3. Second galley, this being the present form, with the second pass and a query list.
4. Page proof, after pagination has been fixed.
5. Revise, if necessary; blueline; to press.

Authors are asked to answer every query. A query left unanswered is entered on the record as <span class="stet">stet</span> by default.

## House style

- Spellings follow the 1998 revision of the house sheet, copies of which are held by the copy chief.
- Em rules are em rules, not hyphens. Spaces around em rules are the author's prerogative.
- Block quotations run more than forty words; shorter quotations take quote marks.
- Footnotes are set at the foot of the page where possible; endnotes where not.

## To correctors

The marks in the margin are the correctors' own. Use the hand you have; the editors have grown accustomed to most hands. When a mark is ambiguous, prefer a clarifying word in the margin over a fresh symbol. The leader line is a courtesy to the eye, not a rule.

## To the compositor

Hold the forme until all queries are returned. Set the errata on the tail folio in small caps. Do not re-justify paragraphs without consulting the editor. The baseline grid is sacred.
