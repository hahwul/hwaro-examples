In the vast expanse of the hall,
The loudest sound is the absence of a call.
Stone pillars catch the fading light,
As shadows stretch into the night.

We speak our truths to empty air,
Hoping the silence might begin to care.
But walls of marble, cold and gray,
Only echo back the things we cannot say.
