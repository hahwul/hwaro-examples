# About the Archive

<div class="rune-divider">&#5765; --- &#5819;</div>

The Rune Archive exists to preserve and transmit the knowledge systems of the ancient North. It is a gathering place for scholarship on runic writing, Norse mythology, Viking-age history, and the cultural practices of the Scandinavian peoples who shaped the medieval world.

## Purpose

The inscriptions collected here serve several ends. First, to present the runic alphabets -- Elder Futhark, Younger Futhark, and the Anglo-Saxon Futhorc -- in their historical context, not as objects of mysticism but as functional writing systems created by practical people for practical purposes.

Second, to explore the mythology and cosmology that informed the worldview of the Norse peoples. The stories of Odin, Thor, and the other Aesir were not mere entertainment. They were frameworks for understanding the natural world, the passage of time, the cycles of life and death, and the obligations that bind a community together.

Third, to document the material achievements of the Viking age -- shipbuilding, navigation, metalwork, textile production, and the complex trade networks that connected Scandinavia to Byzantium, the Islamic world, and the furthest reaches of the North Atlantic.

## The Name

A rune, in the Old Norse, means "secret" or "mystery." The word carries within it the idea that writing itself is a form of hidden knowledge -- that to mark a symbol upon wood or stone is to encode meaning that only the initiated can unlock. The Rune Archive takes this idea seriously. Knowledge preserved is knowledge that endures.

## Method

Every inscription in this archive draws from primary sources where possible: the Poetic Edda, the Prose Edda, the sagas, archaeological evidence, and the runestones themselves. We present this material with care, distinguishing between what is known, what is probable, and what remains uncertain.

The runes deserve accuracy. They have survived more than a thousand years of weather, neglect, and misinterpretation. The least we can offer in return is honesty about what they tell us and what they do not.
