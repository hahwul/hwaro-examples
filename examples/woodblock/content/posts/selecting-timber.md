Not all wood is created equal when it comes to the block. The hardness of the grain, the density of the fibers, and even the moisture content can dramatically affect the carving experience and the final print quality.

<!-- more -->

### Popular Choices

1.  **Cherry**: The gold standard for fine detail. Its tight grain allows for incredibly thin lines.
2.  **Pear**: Very similar to cherry, often used for smaller, intricate works.
3.  **Boxwood**: Exceptionally hard, used for wood engraving where the detail is paramount.
4.  **Plywood**: A more accessible option, but the layers can sometimes splinter if not handled carefully.

Choosing the wood is the first artistic decision in the process. It sets the limits of what can be achieved with the tools at hand.
