<section class="abstract-box">
  <h2>Abstract</h2>
  <dl>
    <dt>Objective</dt>
    <dd>To develop and psychometrically validate a 25-item self-report instrument (BASIS-25) capturing five theoretically distinct subscales for use in routine health-services research surveys of adult respondents.</dd>
    <dt>Methods</dt>
    <dd>A three-stage instrument development process: item generation (literature review, expert Delphi), cognitive pretesting (n = 24), and field administration (n = 1,412). Exploratory factor analysis on a split-half calibration sample (n = 706) and confirmatory factor analysis on a validation sample (n = 706). Internal consistency, test-retest reliability, and construct validity evaluated.</dd>
    <dt>Results</dt>
    <dd>A five-factor solution explained 64.2 pct of variance. Confirmatory fit was acceptable (CFI = 0.947, TLI = 0.941, RMSEA = 0.048, SRMR = 0.041). Cronbach <em>alpha</em> ranged from <strong>0.78 to 0.91</strong> across subscales. Test-retest reliability (<em>ICC</em> = 0.82) and construct validity (convergent and discriminant) were supported.</dd>
    <dt>Conclusion</dt>
    <dd>The BASIS-25 demonstrates adequate psychometric properties to support its use in routine health-services research. The instrument and scoring algorithm are made available under CC BY 4.0.</dd>
    <dt>Keywords</dt>
    <dd><em>questionnaire development; psychometric validation; factor analysis; Cronbach alpha; Likert scale; cognitive pretesting; health-services research</em></dd>
  </dl>
</section>

## Structure of the Paper

1. **Section 1. Instrument Development** &mdash; item generation, expert Delphi, cognitive pretesting
2. **Section 2. Factor Structure** &mdash; EFA, CFA, factor loadings, cross-loadings
3. **Section 3. Reliability** &mdash; internal consistency, test-retest, inter-item correlations
4. **Section 4. Validity** &mdash; construct, convergent, discriminant, known-groups
5. **Section 5. Discussion** &mdash; strengths, limitations, implementation guidance
