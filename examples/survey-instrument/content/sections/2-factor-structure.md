## 2.1 Sample and split

The field administration sample (n = 1,412) was randomly split into a calibration sample (n = 706) for exploratory factor analysis and a validation sample (n = 706) for confirmatory factor analysis. Demographic characteristics did not differ significantly between samples (all p &gt; 0.20).

## 2.2 Exploratory factor analysis

Exploratory factor analysis was conducted on the calibration sample using principal axis factoring with oblique (promax) rotation. Parallel analysis and the Kaiser criterion both supported a five-factor solution, which together explained 64.2 pct of item variance. The scree plot elbow occurred at five factors.

## 2.3 Factor loading pattern

Primary loadings ranged from 0.58 to 0.84. Cross-loadings were all below 0.30 except for Q18 (Autonomy primary = 0.62, Communication cross = 0.31). Q18 was retained after discussion because its content validity was high and its cross-loading was near the preregistered threshold.

## 2.5 Confirmatory factor analysis

A five-factor CFA was estimated on the validation sample (n = 706) with maximum-likelihood robust (MLR) estimation. Fit indices were: <strong>CFI = 0.947, TLI = 0.941, RMSEA = 0.048 (90 pct CI 0.042 &ndash; 0.054), SRMR = 0.041</strong>. All indices met preregistered thresholds for acceptable fit (CFI &geq; 0.93, RMSEA &lt; 0.06, SRMR &lt; 0.08).

Inter-factor correlations ranged from 0.35 to 0.58, supporting the oblique rotation choice and confirming that the five domains are conceptually distinct but not independent.
