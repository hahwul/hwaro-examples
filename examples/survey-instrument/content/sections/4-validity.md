## 4.1 Convergent validity

Convergent validity was evaluated by correlating BASIS-25 subscale scores with conceptually related scales from two external instruments administered on the same respondents. Hypothesized moderate-to-strong correlations were observed across all five subscales.

## 4.2 Discriminant validity

Discriminant validity was supported by the heterotrait-monotrait (HTMT) ratio: all HTMT ratios were below 0.85, indicating subscales measure distinct constructs despite moderate inter-correlations (0.35 &ndash; 0.58).

## 4.3 Known-groups validity

BASIS-25 subscale scores differentiated between groups known a priori to differ on each construct. For example, respondents reporting a recent negative service episode scored significantly lower (mean difference = 0.61 SD units, p &lt; 0.001) on the Communication subscale than respondents reporting no such episode. Equivalent effects were observed for all five subscales.
