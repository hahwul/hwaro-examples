## 5.1 Principal findings

The BASIS-25 demonstrates adequate psychometric properties to support its use in routine health-services research. Factor structure aligned with the preregistered five-domain framework, internal consistency exceeded 0.78 for every subscale, and construct validity was supported through convergent, discriminant, and known-groups evidence.

The overall composite <em>alpha</em> of 0.93 should not be interpreted as justifying a unidimensional use of the instrument. Inter-factor correlations (0.35 &ndash; 0.58) indicate that the five subscales capture related but distinct constructs, and we recommend reporting subscale scores separately except where a single composite is explicitly preregistered.

## 5.2 Implementation guidance

1. **Use subscale scores, not a single composite, for most research purposes.** The composite should only be used for descriptive summaries or when a preregistered analysis specifies it.
2. **Calibrate the response scale to the population.** Our sample was primarily English-speaking adults in a single country; translation and validation are required before deployment in other languages.
3. **Choose the right administration block.** The short block (Q21 &ndash; Q25, Environment only) is appropriate when respondents have not had a recent care episode but can still evaluate the physical environment of a visit.
4. **Report Cronbach <em>alpha</em> from the administered sample.** Do not quote our alpha estimates as sample-specific estimates; always recompute and report.
5. **Missing-data strategy.** Multiple imputation is recommended over listwise deletion for item-level missingness. Subscale scores should not be computed when more than two of five items are missing within a subscale.

## 5.3 Limitations

1. **Sample context.** The calibration and validation samples were drawn from a single country and primarily from English-speaking respondents. Cross-cultural validation is a clear next step.
2. **Cross-sectional data.** Our test-retest sample was 214 respondents at a two-week interval. Longer-interval stability has not been evaluated.
3. **Self-report.** The BASIS-25 captures subjective experience and is not a substitute for observational or administrative outcome measures.
4. **Item Q18 cross-loading.** The single cross-loading above 0.30 (Q18 &rarr; Communication, loading = 0.31) is near the threshold. We retained the item based on content validity, but users with strict cross-loading thresholds may consider alternative item Q18 formulations proposed in the appendix.

## 5.4 Future work

Planned future work includes: (a) translation and cross-cultural validation in three additional languages (Korean, Spanish, Portuguese); (b) evaluation of responsiveness to change over time in a longitudinal subsample; and (c) development of short forms (5-item and 10-item) with comparable coverage but reduced respondent burden.
