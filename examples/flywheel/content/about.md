Flywheel started in 2025 as an internal tool at a subscription analytics consultancy, where three of us kept building the same spreadsheet for every client: pull billing events, bucket them into cohorts, and figure out which percentage of "churn" was actually a card decline nobody retried. After doing it for the ninth client, we stopped calling it a spreadsheet and started calling it a product.

## What we believe

A churn number without a cause attached is not useful — it's a mood. Every account that stops paying left through a specific door: a failed charge, a downgrade, a support ticket that never got answered, or a trial that quietly expired. We built Flywheel around the belief that revenue teams deserve to know which door, in real time, without exporting a CSV and pivoting it in a spreadsheet at 11 p.m. before a board meeting.

We also believe recovery tools should show their work. Every dollar Flywheel claims to have recovered is tagged to the specific retry, nudge, or win-back email that triggered it, and that tag survives all the way into your revenue reporting. If a number can't be traced back to a workflow, we don't count it.

## The team

Flywheel is built by a distributed team of fourteen, based mostly in Austin and Lisbon, with backgrounds in payments infrastructure, product analytics, and customer success. We ship weekly, read every support ticket ourselves, and still keep the original nine-client spreadsheet pinned in a shared drive as a reminder of the problem we started with.
