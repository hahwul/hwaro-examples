## Who We Are

Welcome, darling, to **Memphis Milano** — a design zine that refuses to apologize for its color choices.

We are unapologetic maximalists. We believe that a room — or a webpage — should make you feel something the moment you walk in. Shock, delight, mild confusion? All acceptable. Beige? *Never.*

This site is dedicated to the radical proposition that **more is always more**.

---

## Our Philosophy

The Memphis Group burst onto the scene in Milan in 1981 and the design world has never quite recovered. We think that is a good thing.

Here is what we believe:

- **Color should clash** — if your palette is "harmonious," you have not gone far enough
- **Geometry is decoration** — circles, triangles, zigzags, and checkerboards are not accidents, they are *choices*
- **Typography should shout** — big, bold, black-outlined letters that demand to be read
- **Rules exist to be broken** — symmetry is a suggestion, minimalism is someone else's problem
- **Joy is a design requirement** — not an afterthought

## About This Site

Memphis Milano is built with [Hwaro](https://github.com/hahwul/hwaro), a static site generator with the good sense to stay out of the way and let the CSS do the talking.

Every post is written from the perspective of an enthusiast who believes the 1980s got something profoundly right about the relationship between people and objects, and that the austere reaction against it threw out a great many colorful babies with the bathwater.

### What We Cover

- The history and principles of the Memphis Group and postmodern design
- How to apply maximalist thinking to digital interfaces
- Color theory for people who think "too much" is a myth
- Profiles of designers, objects, and movements that refused to behave
