For many operations, standard Unix cron expressions do not provide enough control. Gnomon v1.2.0 addresses this by introducing calendar-precision cron scheduling. This new cron parser supports complex schedules such as 'last Friday of the month', 'nearest weekday to the tenth', and specific holiday exclusion lists. These schedules are resolved dynamically based on timezone definitions that are synchronized across all scheduling instances.

<!-- more -->

This version also introduces a distributed lock manager. While Gnomon's database-backed design prevents duplicate task execution, certain workflows require locking resources across multiple distinct jobs. The new lock manager allows jobs to request exclusive or shared access to named locks before executing, holding the locks until the job completes or the lease expires. If a lock cannot be acquired immediately, the job is put back into a waiting state with a configurable backoff. This ensures that resource-intensive workflows do not clash, guaranteeing consistency without manual operator intervention. We look forward to seeing this feature in production.
