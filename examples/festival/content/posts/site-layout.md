The festival grounds occupy thirty-two hectares on the river plain east of the rail station. Three principal stages anchor the layout, with food vendors and rest areas distributed along the connecting paths.

<!-- more -->

The Mainstage faces east across the open meadow. Capacity at the front barrier is fourteen thousand standing, with sightlines preserved to a distance of approximately two hundred meters before the gentle slope of the field begins to attenuate sound levels. The Riverbank Stage sits at the western edge near the water and runs a quieter program of acoustic and instrumental sets. The Tent Stage, fully covered, runs late-night programming until 02:00 each night.

Wayfinding signage uses three colors keyed to stage, supplemented by numbered marker posts at fifty-meter intervals along the main paths. Medical posts are positioned at each stage and at the central hub near the entrance. Water refill stations operate without charge throughout the site.

Camping is divided into family, quiet, and general zones. The quiet zone enforces a 23:00 noise reduction and is sited furthest from the stages. Vehicle access is restricted to the loading hours of 06:00 to 10:00, with a shuttle running on a fifteen-minute interval from the long-term parking field.

Accessibility provisions include raised viewing platforms at all three stages, accessible toilets at every cluster, and a charging station for mobility devices near the central hub. The site team can be reached on channel four of the festival radio or through any staff member wearing a yellow vest.

A printed site map is available at the main gate. A digital version with live updates is available through the festival app, including queue times for the larger food vendors and any program changes communicated by stage management.
