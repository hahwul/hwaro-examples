The festival operates under a sound permit issued by the regional authority and a memorandum of understanding with the village council. Both documents set limits that shape the program from soundcheck through the final set.

<!-- more -->

Daytime levels at the Mainstage are capped at 102 dB(A) measured at the front-of-house position, with a 100 dB(A) average over any thirty-minute window. Levels at the property line, measured at four monitoring points, must remain below 65 dB(A) during the day and 55 dB(A) after 22:00. The monitoring stations report continuously to the production office, and the system holder may attenuate a stage if a station shows sustained excursion.

Curfew times are firm. The Mainstage and Riverbank Stage end at 23:00 each night. The Tent Stage, treated as an enclosed venue under the permit, may run until 02:00 with closed sides after midnight. A grace period of four minutes is granted for fade-out and applause. Any encore that pushes past the grace period is the responsibility of the artist's manager and may affect future bookings.

Soundchecks begin at 09:00 and must conclude by 11:30 to respect the morning quiet hours. Bands arriving outside this window perform a line check only. Monitor engineers coordinate with the systems team for any in-ear changes during the program.

Earplugs are available without charge at every bar and information point. The festival recommends use during all amplified sets and especially at positions within fifteen meters of the Mainstage stacks. A free hearing screening is offered on Sunday afternoon at the welfare tent, run by volunteers from the regional audiology school.
