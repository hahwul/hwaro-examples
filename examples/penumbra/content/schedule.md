The night runs on a loose but real timetable. Slots start close to on time because the closing set genuinely needs its full run before the venue's midnight-and-a-bit curfew. Between sets the lights come up a little for the gear swap — a fifteen-minute window to trade modules, cables, and opinions.

## How the night flows

The programme is deliberately front-loaded with structure. Halva Circuit and Field Notation give the room something to hold onto while people are still arriving and finding the floor. Nul Modem is the physical peak, placed late enough that the room is full and warm. Slow Antenna exists to undo all of that — a slow decompression so nobody walks out into Rotterdam Noord still buzzing at 132 BPM.

## The gear swap

The two swap windows are the social heart of Penumbra. The rule is simple: bring one thing you are willing to part with — a module, a pedal, a fistful of patch cables, a half-finished DIY kit — and leave with something someone else brought. No money changes hands. If nothing catches your eye, you keep what you came with. Label your item with a sticky note saying what it does and, honestly, why you are letting it go.
