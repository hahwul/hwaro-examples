## What Penumbra is

Once a month, after the shops on the Nieuwe Binnenweg have pulled their shutters, a handful of people carry cased modular rigs down a stairwell in Rotterdam Noord and plug into a shared power strip. That is Penumbra. There is no headliner and no stage in the usual sense — just a long table, a quiet PA, and four acts who patch live while everyone else leans in to watch the cables.

The night runs in four slots. The early sets tend toward rhythm and structure; by the closing set the room has dropped to candle-light and a single drone. Between slots there is a gear swap: bring a module you have fallen out of love with and leave with someone else's. Entry is free, the door opens at 20:00, and the whole thing wraps when the last oscillator drifts out of tune.
