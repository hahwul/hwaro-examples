Penumbra happens at **De Nachtschakelaar**, a basement rehearsal room and occasional listening space under a former electrical-supply shop in Rotterdam Noord. The name means "the night switch," which the previous tenants left painted on the door and we were never going to change.

## Getting there

- **Address:** Zwaanshalskade 12-K (basement), 3035 KS Rotterdam
- **Nearest station:** Rotterdam Centraal, then tram 4 toward Marconiplein, three stops to Zwaanshals
- **By bike:** Racks along the Zwaanshalskade; the door is the unmarked grey one beside the bakery
- **By car:** Paid street parking after 18:00; the zone fills up, so allow time

The entrance is genuinely easy to miss. Look for a single magenta bulb over a grey door — we switch it on at 19:45. Walk down the stairs; the room is at the bottom.

## What it is like inside

One long room, low ceiling, a concrete floor softened with rugs. There is a shared power distribution rig along the back wall with plenty of sockets — bring a short extension lead if your case is fussy. The PA is modest and run quiet by design: this is a listening room, not a club. There is a table for the gear swap and a kettle that is always on.

## Accessibility

We want to be honest about this: the room is a basement reached by one flight of fourteen stairs, and there is currently no lift. If stairs are a barrier for you, email us at least a week ahead and we will arrange help carrying gear and, where possible, reserve a spot near the entrance. The single toilet is on the same level as the room. The floor is level once you are down. Lighting is deliberately low, dropping further for the closing set — if you need more light to move safely, tell a host and we will keep a lamp on near you.

## House notes

Sets are quiet enough to talk over near the back, but please keep the front two rows for people who came to listen closely. No smoking indoors; the courtyard out back is the spot. The night ends at a firm 00:15 because of the residential curfew — when the last set finishes, many hands make the pack-down quick.
