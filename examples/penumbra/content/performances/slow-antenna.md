Slow Antenna closes every Penumbra, and by the time Jonas Vrede starts the overhead lights are off and the only illumination is the LEDs on the rack. His set is a single evolving drone that he spends forty-five minutes coaxing, layering, and eventually letting decay into silence. People sit on the floor. Some fall asleep, which he takes as a compliment.

<!-- more -->

The modular provides the raw material — three oscillators tuned to a wide, slightly detuned chord — but the character comes from what happens after. He runs the whole mix through a chain of guitar pedals: a big reverb, a modulated delay, and a shimmer pedal that adds an octave up until the sound feels like it is lifting off the table.

There is almost no rhythm. What movement there is comes from slow filter sweeps and the beating of the detuned oscillators against each other. It is the anti-Nul-Modem, deliberately placed to bring the room back down before everyone files out into the cold.

### The closing drone

```text
[osc x3] tuned to C2 / G2 / C3 (each ~4 cents apart)
  -> [mixer] -> [low-pass filter] -> pedal chain:
     -> [reverb: decay 12s] -> [delay: 1/1, 40% fb, chorus on]
     -> [shimmer: +1 oct, 30% mix] -> PA
modulation: one slow LFO (~0.03Hz) -> filter cutoff
ending: pull osc tunings to unison, then cut mixer
```

Bringing the three oscillators to unison at the very end collapses all that shimmer into a single pure tone — and then he simply turns the mixer down.
