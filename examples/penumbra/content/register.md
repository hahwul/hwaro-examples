Penumbra is free, but the room only holds ninety people comfortably and the last few editions have filled up. Reserving a spot means we can plan the power distribution, the swap table, and how many chairs to set out. If your plans change, release your spot so someone on the waitlist can take it.

## How to reserve

Send a short email to **patch@penumbra.example** with:

- Your name (or the name you go by)
- Whether you are bringing a rig to play in a future edition, or coming to listen
- One line on what you are bringing to the gear swap, if anything

We reply within a few days with a confirmation and the door code. That is the whole process — no ticketing platform, no fees, no account to create.

## What to bring

- **Yourself, and headphones** if you like to check tunings quietly before a set
- **One item for the swap** — a module, pedal, cables, or a DIY kit you have given up on
- **A short extension lead** if your case has awkward power needs
- **Cash for the kettle jar** if you want coffee or tea; it runs on trust

## Want to play a future edition?

Slots are booked two or three editions ahead. If you patch live and want one of the four spots, email the same address with a link to a recording — a phone video of a patch running is completely fine, we care about how you build a set, not production polish. We program a mix of first-timers and returning acts every month, and we especially want to hear from people who have never played out before.

## Etiquette

Arrive close to doors if you can; the stairwell gets congested if everyone comes at 22:00 for the peak set. During sets, keep close talking to the back of the room. And when the closing drone fades out at quarter past midnight, stick around for ten minutes to help coil cables and carry cases up the stairs. That is the deal, and it is why the night keeps happening.
