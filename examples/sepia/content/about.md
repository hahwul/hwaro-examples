Nerys Calder has photographed working harbours since 2014, mostly along the Cael Estuary and the smaller inlets that feed it. The subjects are the ones a coastal town runs on and rarely photographs itself: quota chalkboards, ice houses, rope lofts, night shifts on a container feeder, boatyards that only exist for the forty minutes either side of low water. Nothing here is staged. Access is negotiated in person, over a season, usually starting with a boat that needs an extra pair of hands before it needs a photographer.

## Method

Every roll is developed by hand in small batches and contact-printed before a single frame is enlarged. The sheet — not the finished print — is the primary record; it is what gets dated, filed, and (eventually) exhibited alongside the prints it produced. A keeper is marked with a grease pencil circle directly on the sheet at the first pass, before fatigue or hindsight can second-guess the choice. That circle is never redrawn. If the frame turns out to be the wrong one, a second sheet gets made from the next roll instead.

This site mirrors that process exactly: the sheets published under [Sheets](/sheets/) are scans of the actual annotated sheets, numbered as the negatives were cut, captioned as written on the negative sleeve at the time. Nothing is cropped past what the pencil allows. [Low Water, Drummond's Reach](/sheets/low-water-drummonds-reach/) is the most recently opened project and the best starting point if you want to see the method end to end.

## Terms

Prints are available by appointment and made to order from the original negative — there is no open edition and no stock library. Editorial use of a specific frame can usually be arranged faster than a print; write ahead with the sheet name and frame number rather than a general inquiry, since the negative has to be pulled from the flat file either way.
