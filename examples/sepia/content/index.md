Sepia is the working index for Nerys Calder's harbour photographs — not a portfolio in the usual sense, but the same contact sheets kept taped inside the darkroom cupboard, moved online frame for frame. Every roll is numbered as it was shot, every keeper is circled in grease pencil before a single print is made, and nothing here has been cropped tighter than the pencil mark allows.

The subject has stayed the same since 2014: harbours that still work for a living, photographed at the pace the tide sets rather than the pace a gallery would ask for. Six projects are open on the desk right now, from a herring harbour running a tenth of its old quota to a boatyard with forty minutes either side of low water to careen a hull.
