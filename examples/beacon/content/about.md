## Introduction

The Beacon Protocol defines the standards for communication during high-priority incidents. It ensures that critical information is transmitted clearly, concisely, and securely.

### Security Levels

1. **Critical (Red):** Immediate action required. Potential for system loss or major data breach.
2. **Warning (Orange):** Impending threat or degraded service. Investigation mandatory.
3. **Info (Blue):** Operational updates or routine maintenance. No immediate action needed.

### Communication Channels

All alerts are broadcasted over the encrypted Beacon Network. Direct neural links or secondary fiber arrays are used for redundancy.

### Protocol Adherence

Failure to comply with Beacon Protocol during a Level 1 (Critical) incident may result in immediate containment protocols for the non-compliant sector.
