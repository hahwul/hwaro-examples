Years ago, at the back of a saddler's closing-down sale, I bought a half skin of goat dyed a violet so deep it looked black until the light moved. It has waited on the bench through every project since, too good to use, which is the most useless kind of good. This January I finally cut into it.

<!-- more -->

## Sewing on tapes

The book is a personal journal — 240 leaves of 90 gsm ivory wove, sewn in ten signatures on three linen tapes. Tape sewing is the workhorse of casework: the thread runs along the inside of each fold and crosses the tapes on the outside, so the swell stays modest and the spine rounds sweetly under the hammer later.

```text
signatures : 10 of 6 folios, 90 gsm ivory wove, grain long
tapes      : 3 linen tapes, 12 mm wide
thread     : 18/3 linen, waxed; kettle stitch at head and tail
endpapers  : marbled keeper no. 2, made-and-hooked around the first section
headbands  : sewn silk over cord, violet and unbleached white
note       : the marbled sheet is from the July session at the sink
```

The endpapers are one of the two keepers from the [marbling session](@/bindings/marbled-endpapers-from-the-kitchen-sink.md) — held back half a year for exactly this book, though I did not know it at the time.

## Paring

Quarter leather means the goatskin covers only the spine and a strip of each board, with the marbled paper siding the rest. The whole game is the pare: the turn-ins and the joint area have to come down from a sturdy 1.2 mm to something under 0.5, or the boards will not close without a fight. I pared with the French knife and finished with the little spokeshave, checking thickness the traditional way — holding the skin up to the window until the light comes through it grey.

Paste the leather, work it over the rounded spine, set the joints with the folder, tie up, and walk away. Walking away is a technique. It has taken me years to learn it.

The finished book sits by the reading chair now, and the offcut of that violet skin stays on the bench — it is, quite literally, where this journal's accent colour comes from.
