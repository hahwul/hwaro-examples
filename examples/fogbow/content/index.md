A fogbow is a rainbow with the colour wrung out of it — pale, patient, easy to miss unless you are already looking up. Good binding work behaves the same way: the best stitch is the one nobody notices holding for a hundred years.

This journal records what happens at one bench in Greyhaven — notebooks sewn for friends, endpapers marbled at the kitchen sink, and borrowed books that go home stronger than they arrived. Entries are numbered by folio and sewn in as they happen, newest at the front, the way any working journal refuses to be read backwards.
