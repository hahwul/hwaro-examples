Most pieces from this bench are built to be looked at more than handled; this one is built to be pulled through hair every morning and survive it. The blank is boxwood, dense and tight-grained enough to hold a lacquer edge without the wood telegraphing through, and the teeth are left entirely bare — no lacquer at all below the spine, just oiled wood — because a coated tooth is a tooth that eventually chips, and a chip in vermilion lacquer shows.

<!-- more -->

The color is *shu-urushi*, vermilion lacquer colored with a fine cinnabar-red pigment stirred into clarified urushi until the tone is even through the film rather than sitting as a surface tint. Only the spine and body take the full coat schedule; the teeth are masked at every stage, which is part of why this piece runs fewer coats and fewer weeks than the others currently listed — there is simply less surface asking to be built up. After the last vermilion coat, the spine's top rim is dusted with *kindami*, fine gold powder pressed into a nearly-cured coat and burnished, then sealed under a single clear top coat so the gold survives contact with hair and hands.
