Undertow is a first poetry collection built almost entirely from short, sparse pieces — many under ten lines, several under five. The poet's only request was that the book not feel padded to justify its spine width, which meant the interior typography had to do as much work as the jacket.

<!-- more -->

Sparse poems are an unforgiving typesetting problem: too much white space around a five-line poem and it looks lost on the page; too little and it reads as stingy. I set the interior on a wide outer margin and a narrow, deliberately low text block, so each poem sits closer to the bottom third of the page than the centre — closer to where the eye actually lands when a book falls open. Line breaks were checked against the poet's own readings, not just the page proof, since two poems changed their break points entirely once I heard where she paused.

The jacket carries the same restraint: a single flat field of deep water-blue, plum title type set small and left-aligned near the foot rather than centred at the top, and no cover copy at all beyond the poet's name. Thistle & Rowe printed the whole run on an uncoated stock heavy enough to hold the ink without show-through from the poems' more concentrated blocks of text on facing pages — a printer's concern I hadn't fully priced in until the first proof came back translucent in three places.

The book went into a second printing eleven weeks after publication, small by most standards, notable for a debut poetry collection from a press this size.
