## Summary

Editorial designer, twelve years in, working across magazine art direction and book jacket design for independent publishers and small presses. I take on four to six commissions a year — enough to sit with each one properly, from first grid sketch through to a printer's proof I've checked by hand. Recent work spans a masthead-to-margins redesign for a literary quarterly and jackets for two poetry collections that needed to hold silence as well as they hold type.

## Experience

**Founder & Principal Designer — Hallmark Studio**
*2019 — present, Bristol*

Independent practice taking on editorial identity, magazine redesigns, and book jacket commissions for publishers who want a considered type system rather than a template. Recent outcomes:

- Rebuilt the identity and grid system for *The Quarterly Review*, a forty-year-old literary magazine, across a six-issue rollout with no disruption to its subscriber base.
- Designed jackets and interior typography for two poetry collections at Thistle & Rowe, both printed on uncoated stock with hand-set running heads.
- Led a four-cover seasonal series for *Field Notes* magazine built on a single specimen system reused across photography, illustration, and type-only treatments.

**Art Director — The Quarterly Review**
*2015 — 2019, London*

Ran the in-house design desk for a 40,000-circulation literary quarterly. Rebuilt the review's book-cover feature well, introduced a consistent kicker and rule system across sections, and trained two junior designers who went on to lead their own desks.

**Junior Designer — Harrow House**
*2013 — 2015, London*

First jacket credits, working under the house's then-creative director on debut fiction. Learned the unglamorous half of the job: what a printer will and won't hold, how a spine reads on a shelf next to forty others, and why a title needs to survive being three centimetres tall.
