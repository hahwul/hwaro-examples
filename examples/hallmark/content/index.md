I design the pages that carry other people's words: magazine grids, book jackets, and the quieter typographic decisions in between. Twelve years in, most of my work still starts with the same question — what does this page want to say before a reader reads a single line of it?

I trained as a compositor before I trained as a designer, which shows in how I work: proofs on paper, corrections by hand, one typeface doing more than five ever could. Recent commissions include a full identity and grid rebuild for a forty-year-old literary quarterly and jackets for two small-press poetry collections.
