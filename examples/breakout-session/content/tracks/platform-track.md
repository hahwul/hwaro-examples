## What this track covers

The Platform Track is for engineers who build the tooling other engineers use. Seven sessions and three workshops.

Expected background: has written a non-trivial Makefile, CI pipeline, or build plugin in anger. Has strong opinions about at least two package managers.

## Sessions

1. **Day 01 &#183; 10:00 &#183; Every build system is a graph.** The theory underneath Make, Bazel, Buck, and friends &mdash; and why the graph matters more than the DSL.
2. **Day 01 &#183; 14:00 &#183; Local-first CI.** Running a green build on your laptop before you touch the cloud CI.
3. **Day 01 &#183; 16:30 &#183; Hermetic builds without hermit sealant.** Practical approaches when a fully sealed build is not worth the tax.
4. **Day 02 &#183; 13:00 &#183; Five command-line tools you haven&rsquo;t met.** Lightning session, no slides.
5. **Day 02 &#183; 15:00 &#183; Make Is Still Good, Actually.** A live-coded defence of GNU Make in 2026.
6. **Day 03 &#183; 10:00 &#183; The incremental-everything pipeline.** How modern build systems cache at file, target, and package granularity.
7. **Day 03 &#183; 14:30 &#183; Developer environments in 2026.** `devcontainer`, `nix develop`, `mise`, and the trade-offs between them.

## Workshops

- **Workshop 03.1** &mdash; Bazel hands-on, four hours.
- **Workshop 03.2** &mdash; Migrating a real project to incremental builds, six hours.
- **Workshop 03.3** &mdash; `nix develop` for the pragmatist, three hours.

## Speakers

Eliot Tan, plus rotating maintainers from the Bazel, Nix, and Buck communities.
