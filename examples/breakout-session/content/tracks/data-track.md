## What this track covers

The Data Track is for engineers who live in databases, streaming systems, and the query plans that make them go. Seven sessions and three workshops.

Expected background: comfortable with SQL, has read an EXPLAIN plan before, and has at least glanced at the source of a storage engine.

## Sessions

1. **Day 01 &#183; 10:00 &#183; Postgres for people who already like Postgres.** Advanced features most engineers never reach for: partial indexes, exclusion constraints, the planner&rsquo;s statistics collector.
2. **Day 01 &#183; 14:00 &#183; The small-data revolution.** Why DuckDB, SQLite, and a single-machine Postgres will handle more than you think.
3. **Day 01 &#183; 16:30 &#183; Wire protocols are a feature.** How Postgres&rsquo; wire protocol enables extensions that wouldn&rsquo;t fit in the server itself.
4. **Day 02 &#183; 11:30 &#183; Postgres Indexes You&rsquo;re Not Using (And Should).**
5. **Day 02 &#183; 14:30 &#183; Query Plans That Don&rsquo;t Suck.** Live EXPLAIN ANALYZE teardowns.
6. **Day 02 &#183; 16:30 &#183; Exactly-Once Is a Promise, Not a Fact.** On streaming semantics in production.
7. **Day 03 &#183; 11:00 &#183; Kafka without the platform team.** Running streaming infra with a small ops footprint.

## Workshops

- **Workshop 02.1** &mdash; Postgres plan-tuning hands-on, four hours.
- **Workshop 02.2** &mdash; Streaming hands-on with Kafka + Flink, six hours.
- **Workshop 02.3** &mdash; Small-data hands-on (DuckDB + SQLite), three hours.

## Speakers

Nadia Pashman, Priya Venkat, Jakub Zemel, and additional speakers. Postgres committers make up roughly half the track by session count.
