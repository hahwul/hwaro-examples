## What Breakout is

Breakout Session is a small, opinionated developer conference held every spring in Berlin. Four tracks, twenty-eight sessions, three days. No sponsors on stage, no recorded talks, and no slides longer than ten slides.

Every session is either a live-coded build, a teardown of real-world code, or a moderated discussion. We do not accept talks that consist of &ldquo;here is what we learned&rdquo; retrospectives or &ldquo;here is how our tool works&rdquo; product demos. Both of those are fine talks; they are not Breakout talks.

## Format

- **Live-coded builds.** The speaker writes code on stage, in a visible editor, and commits every meaningful change. The final state of the repository is pushed to a public mirror before the session ends.
- **Code teardowns.** The speaker walks through real code &mdash; theirs or someone else&rsquo;s &mdash; with a pointer and a microphone. The code is published in advance so the audience arrives with their own notes.
- **Moderated discussions.** Small panels (three or four people) on a focused technical question, moderated by a programme committee member. No keynotes on discussion days.

## Tracks

The four tracks are:

1. **Systems Track** &mdash; kernels, runtimes, networking, observability, performance.
2. **Data Track** &mdash; databases, streaming, storage, query optimisation.
3. **Platform Track** &mdash; build systems, CI, developer tooling, local-first infra.
4. **Craft Track** &mdash; API design, code review, documentation, engineering culture.

Each track has seven sessions across the three days, plus two or three hands-on workshops. Workshops are optional and ticketed separately.

## Code of conduct

Be curious. Be exact. Be kind. The full code of conduct is in the registration packet. Violations are handled by the programme committee on the same day, through a single named contact whose name is printed on the back of every badge.

## What we don&rsquo;t do

- **Sponsored talks.** Sponsors have a table in the foyer. They do not have a slot on the stage.
- **Recordings.** Sessions are streamed live and the stream is preserved for forty-eight hours. There is no permanent archive; the whole point is to be there.
- **Hero keynotes.** We have one short Day 01 opening and one Day 03 closing. Neither is longer than twenty minutes. The real content is in the breakouts.

## Tickets

General admission is EUR 480, which includes three days of sessions, lunch, and after-hours Hack Nights. Workshop tickets are EUR 80 each. A small number of diversity scholarships are available; applications open in November.

## History

Breakout Session was founded in 2017 by three engineers who had run the Berlin hackathon circuit for five years and wanted a conference where the speakers did, rather than described, their work. The first edition had sixty attendees and four tracks; the fourth edition had four hundred and forty attendees and the same four tracks. We keep the number of tracks fixed.
