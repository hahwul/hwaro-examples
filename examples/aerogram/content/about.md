Aerogram is written by a small, rotating desk of cartographers, surveyors,
and one map historian who insists on reading primary sources in their
original marginalia. We do not sell subscriptions and we do not track opens.
The "subscribe" panel on the homepage is a static mock-up built for this
example site &mdash; there is no mailing list behind it, only a form that
politely declines to submit.

## What a dispatch is

Every issue runs long enough to make one argument well and short enough to
finish before the coffee does. We write about the decisions built into every
map: which projection, which datum, which coastline was surveyed at high
tide versus low, which blank spaces were left blank on purpose. Field
surveying gets equal billing with the printed page, because most of what
ends up wrong on a map started out wrong in the field, or right in the field
and wrong in the transcription.

## Editorial marks

Each dispatch is filed with a dispatch number and a coordinate pair in the
byline, in the surveyor's habit of noting exactly where a reading was taken.
The coordinates are sometimes literal, sometimes a wink at the subject
&mdash; [Issue No. 07](@/issues/interrupted-goode-homolosine.md) is filed
from Null Island, cartography's favorite placeholder for "we don't actually
know."

## Reaching the desk

We take corrections seriously, particularly on datums and elevation figures,
which are the fastest way to lose a surveyor's trust &mdash; see
[Issue No. 03](@/issues/datum-shift.md) for how expensive that kind of error
gets. Route any correspondence through [the archive](@/issues/_index.md) or
the tags index below and you will eventually find the right dispatch to
reply against; there is no other inbox to speak of in this example.
