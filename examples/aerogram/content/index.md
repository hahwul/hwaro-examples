Aerogram began as an internal circular passed between three surveyors who
kept finding the same argument in different countries: that a map is never
neutral, and that most readers never think to ask what a projection quietly
decided to leave out. It has since grown into a monthly letter for anyone who
likes their geography with the seams showing &mdash; forgotten atlases,
disputed datums, and the particular stubbornness of measuring a coastline
that will not sit still.

Each dispatch is short enough to read over coffee and specific enough to
argue with. We favor primary evidence: field notebooks, survey marks,
soundings, the marginal notes cartographers leave when they think no one is
reading closely. No trend pieces, no "future of mapping" think-pieces &mdash;
just the ground, and the paper that tried to describe it. New here? Start
with [the argument that started all this](issues/mercator-problem/), or
read [the masthead](about/) for how a dispatch gets filed.
