# Canopy: Editorial Design for a Botanical Journal

Canopy is a quarterly journal dedicated to botanical illustration, plant science, and garden design. The publication needed a design system that honored the tradition of botanical publishing while functioning seamlessly across print and digital formats.

## The Challenge

Botanical publishing has a rich visual heritage stretching back centuries. Readers of Canopy expect a certain reverence for this tradition -- elegant typography, generous white space, and layouts that let detailed illustrations breathe. At the same time, the journal needed to work as a responsive digital publication accessible on tablets and phones.

Bridging these requirements without compromising either was the central design challenge.

## Typography

We selected a transitional serif for body text, chosen for its sharp detail at reading sizes and its visual kinship with the typefaces used in eighteenth-century botanical texts. Headings use a refined sans-serif with a tall x-height and open counters, providing clear hierarchy without competing with the illustrations.

The type scale follows a modular ratio of 1.25, creating consistent proportional relationships across all heading levels, captions, and body text.

## Grid System

The print edition uses a twelve-column grid with generous margins, allowing full-bleed illustrations to sit alongside precisely structured text columns. Pull quotes and marginal notes occupy dedicated zones that never intrude on the primary reading flow.

For the digital edition, the grid collapses gracefully to single-column layouts on narrow screens while preserving the proportional relationships established in print. Illustrations reflow to full-width positions, maintaining their visual impact at every viewport size.

## Color

The palette draws from natural pigments: a deep forest green for headings and accents, a warm cream for page backgrounds, and a muted terracotta for secondary elements. These colors reference the watercolor washes and hand-tinted plates of historical botanical illustration.

## Result

The first issue of the redesigned Canopy received praise from both subscribers and the broader design community. Readers noted that the publication felt "simultaneously modern and timeless" -- precisely the balance we set out to achieve. Subscription renewals increased by twenty-two percent in the first year.
