Viridian was started in 2015 by garden designer Josefin Aldercroft, after a decade spent watching perfectly good front lawns brown out every July while their owners argued with hosepipe bans. The practice takes on one kind of commission almost exclusively: gravel gardens, matrix-planted with drought-adapted species, designed to need a hose for a single establishing summer and nothing after that.

## The method

Every Viridian garden follows the same sequence, regardless of site. We strip organic topsoil where it exists rather than build it up — most drought planting resents rich ground far more than it resents poor ground. We plant into the subsoil or a thin sand-and-grit bed, dress the surface with 20 to 30mm of gravel matched to the local stone where possible, and water by hand through the first growing season only.

The plant palette leans hard on Mediterranean and steppe genera that have spent several million years solving this exact problem: *Stipa*, *Cistus*, *Phlomis*, *Salvia*, *Eryngium*, *Euphorbia*, and *Festuca* account for most of what goes in the ground. We use them in matrix drifts rather than traditional borders — repeated through a gravel field the way they would self-organise on an open hillside, rather than arranged as discrete specimens against a backdrop of mulch. The [gardens](/gardens/) documented on this site each carry a full planting list and a twelve-month bloom calendar built from this palette.

## What we don't do

We don't install irrigation. If a site genuinely cannot support drought planting — deep shade, permanent damp, heavy clay that never drains — we say so and suggest a different designer rather than force gravel planting where it doesn't belong. We also don't guarantee instant impact: a Viridian garden usually needs one full season before it looks like anything, and two before it looks finished. Clients who need a garden to perform for a party in six weeks should look elsewhere.

## Working with us

Commissions run from initial site visit through planting plan, procurement, and a single supervised planting week, typically in early autumn or early spring depending on the site's exposure. We work across a roughly two-hour driving radius and take on between four and six new gardens a year, which keeps the practice small enough that Josefin is on-site for every planting.

Enquiries go to studio@viridian-gardens.example, or by post to Viridian Garden Design, 14 Coombe Yard, care of the address on file. We reply to every enquiry, though slowly during planting season. [Amberlow Forecourt](/gardens/amberlow-forecourt/), our longest-running site, is usually the easiest place to start if you want to see what ten unwatered years actually looks like.
