Quarry Court is built on backfill from the limestone workings that once ran behind the property — crushed stone under a few centimetres of sharp sand, with almost no organic matter to hold water even if we had wanted it to. Rather than fight the ground, we designed for it.

<!-- more -->

The forecourt runs three matrix blocks of *Cistus ladanifer* for early summer flower, underplanted with *Phlomis fruticosa* for its felted grey foliage and the whorled yellow flower spikes that follow in June. Both are Mediterranean shrubs used to exactly this kind of thin, alkaline ground, and both resented the one thing we almost did to them: adding compost. We planted bare-root stock straight into the crushed stone with no amendment at all.

A 30mm dressing of local limestone gravel, colour-matched to the quarry fill beneath it, keeps the soil surface cool enough for root establishment and cool enough, in turn, that we lost only two plants of the sixty originally set out. Watering ran twice weekly from May to September in year one and has not run since.

By the second June the Phlomis had filled in enough that the gravel mulch was barely visible under the canopy, and the client stopped asking when the sprinkler line was going in. There is no sprinkler line. There never was one.
