The Hearth keeps a long table set after the watch bells, and any traveler who arrives between then and the third crow may sit. The custom is older than the building itself. Those who follow it pay no coin for the first plate.

<!-- more -->

### The Custom

The midnight feast is not a charity. It is an old pact between the inn and the road. Every traveler who eats at the late table is asked to leave one small thing behind: a story, a coin from a far province, a worn map, or news from the towns they passed. The collection sits in a cedar box near the chimney and is opened only when the box is full.

When the box is opened, the contents are read aloud and sorted. Maps and letters are filed for the next courier. Stories are written into the inn's ledger. Coins are weighed and traded for ale to be served at the next opening. Nothing is kept for profit.

### Rules of the Table

* No drawn steel within reach of the bread.
* No questions asked about a guest's road.
* No claims of grudge against another guest while seated.

The cook prepares a single pot stew, brown bread, and a wedge of hard cheese. The fare does not change with the season. Those who want richer plates may order from the regular kitchen during the day.

### A Note for Strangers

If you arrive late and the door is barred, knock three times slowly and once quickly. The night watcher will open without speaking. Find a seat at the long table and wait. You will be served when the others are served. Do not ask the names of those beside you unless they offer first.
