In the world of bespoke tailoring, a jacket is defined not by its outward cloth, but by its hidden anatomy. Modern ready-to-wear jackets rely on fusible interlinings, which are glued to the outer fabric under heat. While efficient, this process robs the garment of its drape and limits its lifespan. The bespoke master instead constructs a floating canvas, a natural horsehair and wool matrix stitched loosely to the wool.

<!-- more -->

This canvas acts as the skeleton of the coat. Horsehair provides spring, allowing the chest to retain its shape, while wool offers softness against the body. Because the canvas is attached with thousands of temporary basting stitches and permanent pad stitches, it behaves like a living layer. It moves independently of the outer cloth, adapting gradually to the wearer’s posture and body heat.

Over time, a canvassed jacket fits better than when it first left the cutting table. Investing in such craftsmanship ensures that a garment does not degrade, but matures alongside its owner.
