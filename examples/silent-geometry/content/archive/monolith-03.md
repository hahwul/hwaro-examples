Negative space is not absence. It is a load-bearing element with weight equal to any rendered glyph or rule. In Silent Geometry, the void is measured, not arbitrary.

### Margins as Architecture

A margin is not a buffer against the edge of the page. It is a column without content, a deliberate pause that grants the eye a place to rest. The width of that pause is calibrated against the body type, the line height, and the expected reading distance. Two ems of vertical clearance reads differently than two ems of horizontal clearance, and we treat them as distinct quantities.

### Silence Between Sections

A section break is not a horizontal rule. The rule is decorative scaffolding that we do not require. Instead, we double the vertical rhythm and trust the reader to register the transition. This demands restraint from the writer, who must compose sections that earn their separation rather than rely on a typographic crutch.

### Refusing the Filler

There is no ornamental texture, no faint background pattern, no gradient to soften the boundary between header and body. The plate is flat. The structure stands or fails on its proportions alone, and proportion is the only thing we are willing to argue about.

### Measurable Emptiness

Every block of empty space in this archive is documented in the same way as a typographic scale. Twenty-four pixels above a heading is not a gap; it is a decision. When the decision changes, it is logged, justified, and replaced. Emptiness is not free. It is the most expensive surface we maintain.
