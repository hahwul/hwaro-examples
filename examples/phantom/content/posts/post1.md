There is a house that no map records, standing where the road gives up and the trees close in. It has been there longer than anyone can remember, and yet no one can say who built it or when.

## The Approach

The path to the house is not a path at all. It is an absence of undergrowth -- a narrow channel where nothing grows, as though the earth itself recoils from the direction of the structure. Walking it feels less like traveling and more like being drawn forward by something patient.

The fog here does not behave the way fog should. It gathers in columns. It holds still when wind moves through it. Occasionally, in the corner of your vision, it takes shapes that dissolve the moment you turn your head.

## The Threshold

The front door stands open. It has always stood open, according to the few who have spoken about this place. The wood of the frame is dark with age but shows no rot. The hinges are silent -- not oiled, but silent in the way that things are silent when they have forgotten how to make sound.

Beyond the door is a hallway that extends further than the exterior dimensions of the house should allow. The walls are bare. The floor is clean. There is no dust.

## What Waits Inside

No one who has entered has given a complete account of the interior. The descriptions contradict. Some speak of endless rooms, each smaller than the last. Others describe a single vast chamber with no ceiling. One account mentions a staircase that descends without end.

The only detail that remains consistent across every telling is the silence. Not quiet -- silence. The total, absolute absence of sound, as though the house exists in a space where sound was never invented.

Those who return from the house do not speak of it willingly. When pressed, they say only this: the house is not empty. Something lives there. Something that has been waiting. Something that is very, very patient.
