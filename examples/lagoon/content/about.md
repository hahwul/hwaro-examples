## About Lagoon

Lagoon is a travel journal built on the belief that the best stories happen where the land meets the sea. This site collects dispatches from coastlines, harbors, islands, and the open water -- the kind of places where salt air sharpens the senses and slows the clock.

Every post here is a record of time spent somewhere worth remembering. Some entries trace the contours of well-known destinations. Others chart routes that barely appear on maps. What connects them is the pull of the coast: the light on the water in the early morning, the particular quiet of a fishing village at dusk, the taste of something unfamiliar ordered from a menu you cannot read.

### What you will find here

- **Destination guides** -- practical notes on places visited, written from experience rather than research
- **Travel narratives** -- longer pieces that try to capture what it felt like to be somewhere, not just what it looked like
- **Coastal culture** -- food, markets, local customs, and the rhythms of life near the water

### Who writes this

A single traveler with a preference for slow ferries over fast flights, local markets over hotel restaurants, and early mornings over late nights. The writing here aims for honesty over enthusiasm. Not every destination is paradise, and the interesting parts are often the ones the guidebooks leave out.

### About the name

A lagoon is a body of water separated from the sea by a narrow barrier -- a place that is neither fully ocean nor fully land. It seemed like the right word for a site that lives between the structured world of travel guides and the open water of personal narrative.
