A vanguard interface uses typography the way a poster does. There is a small, identifiable headline voice that announces position, and a quieter body voice that supports it. The two are deliberately different.

## The Two Faces

- **Headline face**: a condensed sans, set in a single weight, optical-sized for the role. It appears only on titles and primary-action buttons.
- **Body face**: a steady humanist sans or a low-contrast serif. Used for everything else, and never asked to do work the headline face is doing.

## Where Most Designs Slip

Mixing four or five sizes of the headline face on a single page reads as restless. The vanguard discipline is to pick one or two sizes — usually a hero size and a section size — and never go between them. If a piece of text feels like it needs a third size, it probably should be set in body face instead.

The headline does not earn its weight by being everywhere. It earns its weight by being rare.
