When a page feels unfinished, the instinct is to *add*. A shadow, a gradient, a border, an icon, a hairline, a second accent. This instinct is almost always wrong. Pages do not feel unfinished because they are missing decoration. They feel unfinished because the hierarchy has not yet done its work.

## A short loop

1. Remove the most recent element.
2. Check whether the page is worse.
3. If it is not, do it again.

You will stop removing things at roughly the point where the page starts to argue for itself. That is the point.
