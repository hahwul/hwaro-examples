This digital critical edition of The Great Gatsby is created as a specimen of typographic excellence, merging the aesthetic ideals of the 1920s Art Deco movement with modern web technology. It acts as an open resource for students, scholars, and lovers of American literature. By employing clean, high-contrast display headings and readable body text, this edition attempts to capture the dual nature of the novel's themes: the outer veneer of glittering luxury and the inner decay of personal integrity.

The project features analytical chapter commentaries, detailed profiles of primary and secondary characters, and annotations on historical context. Each section is meticulously designed using Hwaro, demonstrating the power of structured static site engines in digital publishing. Our hope is to provide an immersive reading experience that respects the classic text while introducing new avenues of structural interpretation. All editorial notes, character maps, and historical annotations are carefully curated by the fictive society of American Literary Archives.
