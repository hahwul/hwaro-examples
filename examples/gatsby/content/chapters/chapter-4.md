The cultural landscape of the 1920s, known as the Jazz Age, provides the vibrant backdrop against which Gatsby's parties take place. This era was characterized by dramatic changes in music, fashion, and social norms, fueled by post-war economic growth and the rise of consumer culture. Fitzgerald masterfully integrates these elements into the novel, using the syncopated rhythms of jazz to mirror the frantic energy of the characters' lives.
<!-- more -->

During this period, traditional values were challenged by the younger generation, who embraced new forms of entertainment, dance, and dress. The popularity of jazz music, which originated in African American communities, spread rapidly across the country, influencing the fashion and language of the youth. In Gatsby's mansion, the orchestra plays the latest hits of the summer, creating an atmosphere of luxury and excitement that draws hundreds of uninvited guests. We can analyze the musical themes using a simple text pattern matching script in Python:

```python
def find_musical_references(text):
    keywords = ["jazz", "orchestra", "rhythm", "music", "song", "dance"]
    matches = [word for word in keywords if word in text.lower()]
    return f"Found {len(matches)} musical references: {matches}"

print(find_musical_references("The orchestra played a lively jazz rhythm that kept everyone dancing."))
```

Through these musical and cultural descriptions, Fitzgerald captures the essence of a society in transition, caught between the nostalgia for a simpler past and the allure of a fast-paced, modern future.
