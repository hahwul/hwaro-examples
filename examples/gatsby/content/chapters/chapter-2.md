Perhaps the most famous symbol in American literature, the green light at the end of Daisy Buchanan’s dock represents Jay Gatsby's dreams and his hope for the future. Nick first sees Gatsby standing on his lawn, stretching his trembling arms toward the dark water where the tiny green speck glows in the night. It is a symbol of distance, of an object that is visible but completely untouchable, mirroring Gatsby's relationship with Daisy.
<!-- more -->

As the story unfolds, we learn that the green light is not just a sign of love, but a marker of Gatsby's tragic belief that he can rewrite history. The green light represents the promise of the American dream, yet it is also a reminder of the inevitable disappointment that comes when the dream is realized. When Gatsby finally reunites with Daisy, Nick notes that the colossal significance of the light has vanished forever, reduced back to a simple green light on a dock. It is this transition from infinite possibility to mundane reality that lies at the heart of the novel’s tragic vision.
