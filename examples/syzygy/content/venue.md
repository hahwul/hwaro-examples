Syzygy runs at **The Foundry**, a decommissioned power station in Tallinn's
Kalamaja district turned event space. It kept its bones: exposed steel
trusses, a turbine hall you could land a plane in, and a boiler room that
now runs the espresso machines instead of the district heating.

## Halls

- **Main Hall** — the former turbine hall. Keynotes, and every talk
  scheduled opposite nothing else, run here. Fixed seating for 420, a
  15-metre projection wall, and enough headroom that the PA system never
  has to work hard.
- **Annex** — the old control room, one floor up an open steel stair.
  Workshops, the panel track, and both RISC-V sessions run here. Bench
  seating, power at every row, and a whiteboard wall that speakers are
  encouraged to actually use.
- **Turbine Hall foyer** — coffee, lunch, and the Monday reception happen
  in the walkway between the two halls, under the original crane rail.

## Getting there

The Foundry is a 20-minute walk from Tallinn Old Town, or four stops on
tram 4 from Balti Jaam to the Kalamaja stop, then a five-minute walk
downhill toward the water. From Lennart Meri Tallinn Airport, a taxi is
about 15 minutes outside of morning traffic; there is no direct tram.
Bicycle racks are at the north entrance, by the old coal chute.

## While you're there

Registration opens at 08:00 each day at the north entrance. Badge pickup
is by name, not QR code — bring photo ID for the first pickup only.
Cloakroom is staffed all three days, no charge. Lunch on all three days
covers vegetarian and vegan by default; flag any other requirement at
registration and the caterer will find you before the first break.

Venue wifi is `foundry-syzygy`, password `turbine-hall-1926`, no captive
portal. If the network drops mid-talk, it is almost always the crane
rail's old steel interfering with the mesh nodes near the foyer, not
your laptop — give it thirty seconds before you reboot anything.
