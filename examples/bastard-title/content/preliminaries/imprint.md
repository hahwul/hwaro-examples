# The Imprint

<p class="lede">The imprint is the small block of text on the verso of the title page that records who made the book, where, and when. It carries the copyright notice, the printing history, the cataloguing data, and the legal apparatus that anchors the volume in time and place.</p>

## What It Records

<p>An imprint typically lists the publisher's name and address, the year of publication, the edition or printing number, the copyright holder, and an ISBN. In academic and trade books it also carries the printer, the paper grade, and a catalog-in-publication record contributed by a national library. None of this matters to most readers, but every line matters to someone -- a librarian, a translator, a future bibliographer.</p>

## Typography

<p>The imprint is set in the smallest type the page allows, with generous leading. The aim is precision rather than display. Sans-serif faces are common in modern editions; older books used the same serif as the body, sized down. The block is justified to the same measure as the title page above it, so that the recto and verso read as a single spread.</p>

<blockquote>The imprint is the book's address -- a quiet record of who is responsible, written in the smallest type the page can hold.</blockquote>
