Zephyr is written by Mireille Vantage from a narrow desk beneath a north-facing
window, where the light is even and the inkwells never dry out. I am not a
retailer, a chemist, or an influencer. I am a person who writes by hand every
day and who got tired of reviews that describe an ink's *vibe* without ever
telling you how it behaves after the novelty wears off.

## How a review is made

Every pen and ink gets the same treatment. It is inked, then carried through a
full week of ordinary writing — letters, lists, the margins of whatever I am
reading — before a single word of the review is drafted. I test on paper you can
buy without a specialist order, and I note the paper every time, because half of
what people blame on an ink is really the page.

I keep the notes in a plain text log so the criteria never drift. The template
looks like this:

```toml
[test]
pen        = "Marisol 74, 14k medium"
paper      = ["Tomoe River 52", "Rhodia 80"]
days_used  = 7
flow       = "medium-wet"       # dry · medium · wet
shading    = "high"             # none · low · medium · high
sheen      = "faint copper"
dry_time_s = 90                 # seconds on coated paper
water_test = "smears, ghost legible"
verdict    = "keep"             # pass · keep · grail
```

## What you will not find

No scores out of ten, no affiliate links, no bottles sent in exchange for
kindness. If something disappoints me, the review says so plainly. When a pen or
ink was a gift or a loan, I note it at the foot of the piece.

Corrections, disagreements, and recommendations are always welcome — write to the
desk and I will read every line.
