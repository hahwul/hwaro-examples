Welcome to Zephyr, a journal kept the slow way. Every review begins the same way
it would at my desk: a pen filled, a fresh page squared to the light, and a
single line drawn until the nib tells me what it wants to say. I write one
review at a time because ink deserves that much attention — how a wet line pools
and dries, how a broad nib shades from shoulder to hairline, whether a paper
flatters or fights the flow.

You will not find scores out of ten here, only swatches, plain language, and
honest notes on how each pen and ink behaves after a week of real use. I test on
paper you can actually buy, and I say so plainly when something disappoints me.
There are no affiliate links here, and when a pen or ink arrived as a gift I say
so at the foot of the page. Start with the two most recent entries below, then
wander the
[ink cabinet](@/inks.md) or read [the full journal](@/reviews/_index.md).
