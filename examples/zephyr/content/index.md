Welcome to Zephyr, a journal kept the slow way. Every review here begins the
same way it would at my desk: a pen filled, a fresh page squared to the light,
and a single line drawn until the nib tells me what it wants to say. I write
one review at a time because ink deserves that much attention — the way a wet
line pools and dries, how a broad nib shades from shoulder to hairline, whether
a paper flatters or fights the flow.

You will not find scores out of ten here. Instead you will find swatches,
plain language, and honest notes on how each pen and ink behaves after a week
of real use — grocery lists, letters, the margins of books. I test on paper you
can actually buy, and I tell you when something disappointed me.

If you are new, start with the two most recent entries below, then wander the
[ink cabinet](@/inks.md) or read through [the full journal](@/reviews/_index.md).
Pull up a chair.
