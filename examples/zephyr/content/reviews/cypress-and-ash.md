Most green inks are either a highlighter accident or a bottle of pond water.
Cypress & Ash is neither. It is a low, dusty forest green with so much grey worked
into it that the shadows of each stroke read almost as slate — hence the name, and
hence the quiet good manners that let it pass for a serious writing colour.

<!-- more -->

## Shading and character

This is a shader's ink. A wet medium or broad nib rewards you with a two-tone
line: saturated green in the pools, pale ash where the ink runs thin. On coated
Tomoe River the effect is almost painterly; on Rhodia it calms down into a
handsome, even green that would not raise an eyebrow in a meeting.

## Practicalities

Flow is medium and forgiving, dry times sat around twenty-five seconds, and I saw
no feathering on any decent paper. Water resistance is poor — a drop lifts most of
the colour — so treat this as a fair-weather, indoor ink and keep the blue-black
for the envelopes.

I reached for it expecting an autumn novelty and found myself still inking it in
July. That is the mark of a green that earns its shelf: not a costume, but a
colour you actually want to think in.
