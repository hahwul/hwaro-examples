Capped, the Wren Pocket 40 is barely longer than a house key. Posted, it becomes
a properly balanced full-length pen — which is the entire trick of a pocket pen,
and the Wren pulls it off better than tools costing three times as much.

<!-- more -->

## Small pen, real nib

The steel fine is nail-firm and slightly toothy, giving that pleasant pencil-like
feedback that suits quick standing notes. It started first time every morning for
a month, even after sitting capped and unused over a long weekend — no small feat
for a pen this size, where dry-out is the usual curse.

## The catch

It takes short international cartridges, and a converter fits only just, robbing
you of most of the reservoir. I keep mine loaded with a cartridge of Foxglove Red
and a spare rattling in the coat. That is the honest limitation: this is a pen for
capture, not for a marathon letter.

For the shopping list scrawled on a bus, the address taken down in the rain, the
idea that must be caught before it evaporates — the Wren is exactly enough pen and
not one gram more. It has earned its permanent spot in the left breast pocket.
