Some violets shout. Iris Nocturne murmurs. Laid down with a wet broad nib it
arrives almost black at the shoulder of each stroke, then relaxes into a bruised
plum where the line thins — the kind of shading you notice a day later, when the
page has dried and the light has moved.

<!-- more -->

## In the wet, in the dry

Straight from the bottle the flow is generous without being a flood. On Tomoe
River it pooled beautifully and took a patient ninety seconds to set; on the
toothier Midori MD it dried closer to forty and lost a little of its drama, which
is the usual bargain. There is a faint copper sheen at the darkest pools if you
tilt the page to a lamp — restrained, tasteful, never a puddle of glitter.

## Living with it

I filled a medium nib and wrote a week of letters and to-do lists. It never
hard-started, never railroaded, and wiped out of the pen with plain water in
under a minute. Water resistance is middling: a splash smears but the ghost of
the text survives, so it is fine for envelopes if not for cheques.

If you have wanted a violet you can actually write with every day rather than
save for flourishes, this is the one. It is the ink this whole desk is named
after in spirit — quiet, cool, and a little nocturnal.
