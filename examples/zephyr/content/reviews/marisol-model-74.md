The Marisol 74 is the sort of pen that disappears in the hand, which is the
highest compliment I know how to give. Matte resin barrel, a cap that posts
without wobble, and a piston that turns with a soft, oiled resistance rather than
the gritty click of lesser mechanisms.

<!-- more -->

## The nib

The 14k medium is the reason to buy this pen. Out of the box it ran a touch dry,
so I set it on a loupe and eased the tines apart by a hair; after that it laid a
consistently wet, faintly soft line with just enough spring to reward a light
hand. It is not a flex nib and does not pretend to be, but it gives back a little
line variation on the downstrokes if you ask nicely.

## Filling and balance

The piston holds 1.4 ml — enough for a week of my scrawl — and draws cleanly
from a shallow bottle without a converter's stinginess. Posted, the 74 is
slightly tail-heavy; unposted, it is perfectly neutral, and that is how I have
kept it.

I inked it with Aged Umber for the review and never felt the need to change.
Against the field of anonymous black gift pens, the Marisol 74 is what a tool
looks like when someone cared about the fifth hour of writing, not the first.
