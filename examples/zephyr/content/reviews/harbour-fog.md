Blue-black is the little black dress of the ink world, and Harbour Fog wears it
without fuss. In the bottle it reads nearly navy; on the page it dries to a cool,
slightly grey blue-black that looks equally at home in a ledger and a love
letter. There is no sheen, no shimmer, and no drama — and that is precisely the
point.

<!-- more -->

## Behaviour

Flow is medium-dry, which makes it a gift on cheap paper. I ran it through a fine
nib across a Field Notes memo book and a Leuchtturm journal and saw no
feathering and only the faintest show-through on the thinner stock. Dry times
were quick — around fifteen seconds on both — so left-handed writers should get
along with it well.

## The useful part

Harbour Fog is properly water resistant. I ran a wet thumb across a dried line
after an hour and lifted only a whisper of grey; the text stayed put and legible.
That moves it out of the "pretty" category and into the "trust it with the
important form" category, where far fewer inks earn a place.

It is inexpensive, it comes in a generous bottle, and it does the job every
single time. If you only ever buy one bottle of ink, buy this one, then come
back for the violet later.
