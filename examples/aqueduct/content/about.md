# About Aqueduct

Aqueduct is a technical writing project dedicated to the craft of software engineering -- specifically the structural decisions that determine whether a system endures or collapses under its own weight.

## Philosophy

The Roman aqueducts were not merely functional. They were monuments to careful planning, material selection, and an understanding of the forces at play. The Pont du Gard still stands after two thousand years, not because it was over-engineered, but because every stone was placed with intention.

We take the same approach to writing about software:

- **Diagrams before code.** If you cannot draw it, you do not understand it.
- **Trade-offs over dogma.** Every pattern has a cost. We name it explicitly.
- **Incremental depth.** Start with the broad strokes, then drill into the details.

## Topics

This site covers system design, distributed systems, infrastructure patterns, and architecture decisions. Posts are structured as step-by-step walkthroughs with diagrams and flowcharts at their core.
