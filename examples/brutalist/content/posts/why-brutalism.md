The modern web is drowning in sameness. Every site looks like every other site. Rounded corners, soft shadows, pastel palettes, and the same sans-serif font repeated endlessly.

<!-- more -->

## The Problem

We have optimized for comfort at the expense of character. Design systems and component libraries have made everything frictionless, predictable, and forgettable.

## The Alternative

Brutalism rejects this. It says: here is the content. Here is the structure. Nothing is hidden. Nothing pretends to be something it is not.

> Architecture should be honest. The web should be too.

## What Brutalism Is Not

Brutalism is not laziness. It is not the absence of design. It is a deliberate choice to strip away everything that does not serve the reader. Every thick border, every uppercase heading, every sharp edge is intentional.

## The Rules

1. **Structure over decoration** -- borders define space, not gradients
2. **High contrast** -- black on white, no middle ground
3. **System fonts** -- no external font loading, no render blocking
4. **Content density** -- every element earns its place
5. **Honest markup** -- what you see is what exists in the DOM
