# About Borealis

Borealis is a Nordic blog born from long winter nights and the silence of the far north. It is a place for stories that belong to the Arctic -- tales of science, myth, and the natural world, written beneath skies that burn with impossible color.

## The Concept

The name comes from the aurora borealis, the northern lights. That phenomenon captures everything this site aspires to be: something beautiful, mysterious, rooted in science, and deeply connected to the landscape and culture of the Nordic regions.

Here you will find writing about the physics of auroral displays, the folklore of the Norse and Sami peoples, the wildlife that thrives in polar darkness, and the landscapes that define the Arctic experience -- fjords carved by ancient glaciers, tundra that stretches beyond sight, and coastlines where the Atlantic meets ice.

## The Atmosphere

Every element of this site draws from the Arctic night sky. The dark background reflects the long polar night. The green and purple accents mirror the aurora's most characteristic colors. The design is spare and quiet, like the landscapes it represents.

## The Writing

The posts on Borealis aim to combine accuracy with atmosphere. The science is real. The myths are drawn from genuine Nordic tradition. The descriptions of wildlife and landscape are grounded in the ecology and geography of the Arctic. But the writing tries to capture something beyond facts -- the feeling of standing on a frozen shore at two in the morning, watching the sky move.

## Built With

This site is built with [Hwaro](https://github.com/hahwul/hwaro), a fast and flexible static site generator. The design uses no images -- only CSS, carefully layered shadows, and animation to evoke the aurora borealis.
