Gantry Yards was a freight marshalling yard for sixty years before the last siding closed in 2009. Fourteen acres of ballast, rail, and gantry footings sat behind a chain-link fence for a decade and a half while the city argued about what to do with a parcel too long and too narrow for most conventional uses.

<!-- more -->

## What was there

Eleven sidings converged on the yard at a shallow angle, feeding a marshalling fan that once sorted wagons for three different lines. Most of the rail was lifted for scrap in 2011, but the ballast beds stayed, along with two gantry crane footings, a weighbridge house, and a drainage culvert that had been quietly doing its job, unmaintained, for fifteen years.

## The design

Rather than strip the yard flat, we graded along the grain of the old sidings. The eleven-siding fan becomes eleven shallow lawns and planting beds, separated by low ballast-faced retaining walls built from rail stone crushed on site. The weighbridge house is retained as a park pavilion; one gantry footing survives as a climbing frame, the other as a bench base. The drainage culvert was daylighted and now runs as an open rill the length of the park, exactly on its original alignment.

## Grading and elevation

The site rises gently from the river embankment up toward the old yard throat, a difference of about four and a half metres across the full length. Grading kept close to this existing fall — regrading a marshalling yard rarely requires much more than smoothing what the original engineers already leveled for wagon movement. Spot elevations from the site survey are noted in the plan margin, from 31.2m near the embankment planting up to 35.7m at the yard throat.

## Planting

Birch and goat willow, both of which had already self-seeded through the ballast during the derelict years, form the first planting phase. A wildflower mix suited to free-draining, low-nutrient ballast substrate covers the lawns between the old siding beds — no topsoil import was needed.

## Status

Phase 1, covering the eastern two-thirds of the yard including the daylighted culvert, opened in spring 2025. Phase 2, a play terrace on the former weighbridge apron, is scheduled for completion in late 2026.
