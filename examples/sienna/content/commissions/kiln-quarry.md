Kiln Quarry supplied lime for the valley's kilns from the 1890s until 1978, when the seam ran out and the pit was fenced off and left. What the quarrymen left behind was not a hole so much as a set of benches — six or seven distinct working levels cut into the rock as the pit deepened, each wide enough to have once carried a narrow-gauge tramway.

<!-- more -->

## Reading the benches

A quarry closed mid-working is a gift to a landscape architect, in a way a flat derelict lot never is: the benches are already a section drawing. Our survey recorded seven distinct levels between the quarry rim and the flooded floor, stepping down in increments of roughly five to six metres, with two of the lower benches partially submerged by decades of unmanaged groundwater inflow.

## The design

The benches become terraced seating for an open-air amphitheatre, using the existing rock steps rather than cutting new ones. Where the stone face is stable we've left it exposed and simply cleared the scrub; where it had begun to spall, a dry-stone facing rebuilt from quarry spoil stabilizes the line without disguising that it was cut, not built. The flooded floor becomes a reflecting pool, its water level regulated by a low sluice rather than pumped dry — pumping would have meant fighting the spring feed indefinitely.

## Elevation

The rim sits at 58.4m; the pool floor at 24.8m — a full working section of the old quarry preserved in the new grading, with benches at roughly 52.8m, 47.2m, 41.6m, 36.0m, and 30.4m along the way. These are marked as spot elevations in the site plan margin, matching the original quarrymen's working levels wherever the survey could still find them cut into the rock face.

## Planting

Rock-face planting is deliberately sparse — hart's-tongue fern and wall pennywort in the joints, both already established on the shadier faces before we arrived. The upper rim gets a beech and yew shelterbelt; the amphitheatre benches themselves stay unplanted, kept as stone terracing for seating.

## Status

Groundworks and dry-stone facing are underway through 2025, with the sluice and pool edge scheduled for completion by spring 2026.
