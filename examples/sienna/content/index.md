Sienna is a landscape architecture practice working only on post-industrial ground — rail yards, quarries, gasworks, and docks the city no longer has a use for. We do not treat these sites as blank lots. Before a design exists, we survey what the ground has already done: how the water moves through it, what has seeded itself into the ballast, where the old alignments still show through the topsoil.

Every commission becomes a public park, but the industrial geometry stays legible in the grading. A berm follows an old rail cutting. A lawn steps down former quarry benches. A boardwalk traces the footing ring of a demolished gasholder. We regrade the site's working history into somewhere to sit, not erase it.

Founded by Rowan Ashdown in 2014, the practice is based in the Kell Valley and works with local authorities, rail operators, and port trusts across the region.
