## Flight Overview

Westbound transatlantic service from London Heathrow to New York JFK. Headwinds of 80 knots added 45 minutes to the typical block time. Departed from Runway 27R with a Compton departure.

<!-- more -->

## Route Details

Standard North Atlantic Track routing via NAT track Sierra. The westbound crossing followed a more southerly route to minimize headwind exposure, dipping below the core of the jet stream.

## Conditions

A deep low-pressure system south of Iceland intensified the jet stream. At FL390, headwinds peaked at 112 knots near 30W. The A350 handled the turbulence zone with characteristic smoothness. Clear skies for the descent into JFK with a visual approach to Runway 31L.

## Notes

Despite the strong headwinds, the A350-900's fuel efficiency kept consumption within acceptable margins. The track routing avoided the worst of the turbulence at the cost of additional distance. This remains one of aviation's great routes.
