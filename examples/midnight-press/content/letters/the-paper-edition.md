> Dear Editor,
>
> I have been a subscriber since 2017. I love the quarterly. But I notice that the print edition costs more, year over year, and the digital archive is excellent. Why do you continue to print on paper? It seems indulgent. — *G. K., Daejeon*

Dear G. K.,

It is indulgent. We agree.

The print edition costs more to produce every year. Paper costs more. Ink costs more. The bindery's electricity costs more. The postal service, having lost most of its non-print volume, costs more. We have raised the subscription price three times since 2017 to keep the press running, and we will have to raise it again before 2030.

We continue, despite the cost, for three reasons.

The first reason is that the print edition is the *primary* edition. The digital archive is a service to readers who cannot or do not wish to subscribe to paper. The digital archive is excellent because we spend almost as much effort on it as on the paper. But the paper is the version we are designing for. The paper is what the type director composes for first. The paper is what the bindery produces. The paper is what we, the editors, hold in our hands when we read the issue back after it is published.

The second reason is that the print edition is *finite*. We print eight hundred copies. When the run is gone, the run is gone. Subscribers know which issue is theirs. Readers receive a particular numbered object. The object decays. The object can be lent. The object can be returned. The object exists as a thing in the world, not as a server entry.

The third reason is that the print edition supports a small bindery that has employed three to five people for thirty-two years. The bindery would not survive without the print edition. Two other small print magazines work with the same bindery. If we stop, the bindery may stop. If the bindery stops, the other magazines also stop. The press is a small ecosystem, and we are part of it.

We will keep printing as long as the readers will keep subscribing. We are encouraged to learn that you are still among them.

Yours,

— *J. Y. Lee, Editor*
