The Midnight Press was founded in 1998 by a group of three graduate students at Yonsei who wanted a publication that would print essays of unfashionable length, in a language unafraid of long sentences, at a price its readers could ignore. Twenty-eight years later, two of the founders are gone, one remains, and the press continues.

## The press

We publish four times a year. Each issue is composed in a small bindery in Mapo from late October through February, March through May, June through August, and September. The press lies idle for two weeks each year — the bindery closes for Chuseok and the editor sleeps.

Print runs are deliberately small. We print 800 copies of each issue. They are shipped to subscribers by post. There is no kiosk distribution, no airport edition, no online paywall — only the printed quarterly and the public archive you are reading now.

## Submissions

We read submissions from October to February. Manuscripts should be 1,500 to 6,000 words. Send them by email to *editor@midnightpress.example*. We do not accept simultaneous submissions. We respond within ninety days, always personally, always by the editor's own hand.

## A note on the dark

We have published in dark inks since 2003, when the bindery moved to oil-based blacks for the print edition. The web edition matches the print edition. Reading is meant to be a quiet act, performed at a quiet hour, in a room that does not blaze. We do not believe a screen should imitate the page — we believe the screen should excuse itself.

— *Lee*
