## About This Blog

**Crossword Grid** is a notebook kept beside the graph paper. It is about the craft of building crosswords: how a Sunday theme gets engineered, why the same short words keep turning up in the corners, and how a constructor smuggles a joke past the editor and into the grid.

If you have ever stared at a clue, groaned at the answer, and then grinned in spite of yourself, you already understand the appeal. A good puzzle is a conversation between two people who will never meet — the constructor who hid the trick, and the solver who finds it.

---

## About the Constructor

I have been building grids for longer than I have been good at it. I started the way most people do: filling in someone else's puzzle in pen, getting it wrong, and switching to pencil. Eventually I wanted to be on the other side of the page.

I write about:

- **Theme construction** — how a single idea becomes a symmetric, fillable grid
- **Crosswordese** — the small vocabulary of vowel-heavy words that hold a grid together
- **Misdirection** — the question mark clue, the false capital, the pun you should have seen coming
- **The economy of the corner** — why the hardest square is usually the one you skipped

Every post here is filed like a clue: numbered, cross-referenced, and shorter than it looks.

## House Style

- Black squares are load-bearing, not decoration.
- A theme should reward the solver, not punish them.
- If the answer makes you angry and then makes you laugh, it was a fair clue.

> "A crossword is the only place where ERAS, ORE, and ALOE are considered good company."
