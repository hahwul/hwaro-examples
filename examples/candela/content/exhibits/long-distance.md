Long distance was the real cost of calling a BBS outside your own area
code, and this drawing is a small monument to that arithmetic. NULLPOINTR
posted it with a caption, preserved in the file description field, that
just reads "for everyone dialing in from the 217." Candela's second and
third lines pulled callers from three surrounding area codes once the
sysop started advertising in a regional callers list, and several of them
paid real per-minute rates to reach a board that was, by any reasonable
measure, a hobby running out of a closet.

The payphone itself is built almost entirely from box-drawing corners and
underscores, no shading, no diagonals except the two that suggest depth on
the booth's side panel. It is one of the more geometrically disciplined
pieces in the collection — every interior line is either perfectly
horizontal or perfectly vertical, which was a point of pride for
NULLPOINTR, whose later posts in the art crew's private message base
repeatedly criticized "wobble" in other members' work. We left the
twenty-five-cent deposit line in the drawing exactly as posted; by the
time the board closed, a local call cost a dime and long distance cost
considerably more.
