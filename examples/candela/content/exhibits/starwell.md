By early 1996 a handful of terminal programs on the board's caller list had
started advertising extended character support, and PHOSPHOR_JANE was the
first uploader to test it in public. This file uses the shade block
characters — the three densities that had been sitting unused in code
page 437 since the board opened — to fake a gradient on the antenna mast
where earlier art could only offer flat asterisks or hyphens.

It caused a small argument in the message base. Callers on older terminal
emulators saw the shade blocks render as broken boxes or blank cells, and
one regular posted a fairly heated message accusing PHOSPHOR_JANE of
uploading art that "half the board can't even see." PHOSPHOR_JANE's reply,
preserved in the same thread, is worth quoting: "it still looks like a sky
if you squint, and someday everyone's terminal will draw it right." The
antenna array in the drawing was a real fixture on the roof above Node 4 —
a mismatched cluster of TV aerials the co-sysop used to boost the modem
line's reception during storms, which is also why the piece is dated the
same week as a documented lightning strike two blocks over.
