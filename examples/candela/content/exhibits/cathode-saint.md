CRT_WITCH uploaded this face inside a monitor bezel to the art door with no
description at all, which was unusual for a board where most uploads got at
least a line of context. It spread anyway. Within a month it had been
quoted in four other users' signature blocks, reposted to a sister board in
the next area code, and — according to a thread we found in the door-game
high score comments, of all places — used as a shutdown splash screen on
at least one caller's home BBS.

Part of the appeal was technical. CRT_WITCH combined block shading with the
diamond and underscore glyphs from the geometric set to suggest a face
without drawing one directly: two diamonds for eyes, a short flat brow of
underscores for a mouth, nothing where a nose would go. Left alone, your
eye fills in the rest, the same trick a lot of period logo design leaned
on. The double bar beneath the monitor stand — meant to read as a desk
edge — became something of a signature; CRT_WITCH used the same double-bar
base under six later pieces, none of which spread quite as far as this
one did.
