DIALTONE was the sysop, and this is the closest thing the archive has to a
self portrait. It went up nine days after the board's second line came
online, posted to the general file area under the description "what it
actually looks like, in case you were wondering." The rack it depicts sat
in a converted closet: a clone tower running the BBS software, a fourteen
four modem shared across two phone lines with a switchbox, and a drive
array assembled from three mismatched hard disks that DIALTONE's post
history refers to, affectionately, as "the orphans."

We kept this one at the top of the collection because it is the only
exhibit that documents the hardware rather than an idea. Everything else in
this archive passed through that closet at some point — every upload, every
door-game high score, every argument in the message base — and this
drawing is the closest we have to a floor plan. The blinking-light rows are
static in the text file, which did not stop at least two callers from
writing in to ask whether the drive lights were supposed to animate.
