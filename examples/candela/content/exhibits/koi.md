This is the last file anyone uploaded to Candela BBS. The board's closing
notice went up the following evening — the sysop had accepted a job two
states away, the phone lines were being disconnected at the end of the
month, and there was no co-sysop willing to take over both the hardware
and the long distance bill. MOTHDUST, a semi-regular caller who mostly
posted in the aquarium-keeping message area rather than the art door,
uploaded this pond without any announcement that it would be the last
piece anyone left behind.

The frame number in the caption is not decorative. MOTHDUST had been
posting a slow sequence of near-identical pond drawings over several
months, each one nudging the koi a little further across the frame to
fake motion one screen at a time, the same flip-book logic as a zoetrope.
Frame twelve is where the sequence stops. We have no way to know whether
MOTHDUST intended to keep going and simply never got the chance, or
whether twelve was always meant to be the last card in the deck. Either
reading fits a board that closed the way most of them did: not with a
final message, but with an ordinary upload that turned out, in hindsight,
to be the end of something.

For the piece that opened the board's art door in the first place — and
gave Candela its name — see [Glasswing](/exhibits/glasswing/).
