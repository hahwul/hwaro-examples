Candela BBS ran two, then three, then four phone lines out of a spare
bedroom and a closet from August 1993 to April 1998. Like most boards of
its size it never had more than a few hundred registered callers at any
one time, and it left almost nothing behind on purpose — no press
coverage, no surviving flyers, nothing but a handful of drive images that
outlived the hardware they were pulled from by accident rather than
design.

This site is a restoration project, not a re-creation. Every piece in the
[Exhibits](/exhibits/) section was extracted from one of three drive images
recovered in 2024: two IDE disks from the original node closet and a stack of
5.25-inch floppies a former co-sysop had kept in a shoebox for reasons
nobody, including the co-sysop, could fully explain. Where a file's
original SAUCE record survived, we kept the artist handle and upload
timestamp exactly as recorded. Where it didn't, we cross-referenced
message-base mentions and file-listing snapshots to reconstruct dates as
closely as the evidence allowed.

## What we changed

We flattened every piece to a single phosphor-green trace on black,
regardless of what the original terminal color codes specified. Most
callers in 1993 were looking at these files on monochrome or amber
monitors anyway, and a handful of pieces used sixteen-color codes in ways
that read as genuinely chaotic outside the specific terminal emulator they
were built for. Monochrome is the more honest presentation for a general
audience, even where it isn't the most literal one.

## What we didn't change

Line breaks, spacing, and character choice are preserved exactly as
found, including the handful of pieces that only display correctly once a
terminal supports characters outside the original ASCII range — a
distinction the archive keeps visible by labeling each exhibit's medium
as either ASCII or Unicode. We did not correct misspellings in captions,
did not straighten misaligned frames that were clearly intentional
glitches, and did not add color, motion, or sound that wasn't present in
the source file.

## Get in touch

If you ran a node on Candela, uploaded to it, or have a drive image we
haven't seen, we would like to hear from you. The restoration effort is
ongoing and incomplete — several handles mentioned in the surviving
message bases have no attributed work in this archive yet, which almost
certainly means their uploads are still sitting on a disk somewhere,
unread.
