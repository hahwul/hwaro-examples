Most subscriptions ship you the roast they happen to have. We start at the
other end of the chain: with the machine you already brew on. A pour-over cone
pulls delicate, high-grown light roasts into focus. A moka pot wants a darker,
oilier bean that can stand up to steam pressure. An automatic drip forgives a
lot but rewards a balanced medium. When the roast and the brewer disagree, you
taste it as sourness, ash, or a flat cup you cannot quite fix at the grinder.

So every bag we send is chosen for the way your gear extracts. We track roast
level, grind window, and the rest days a bean needs to degas before it peaks in
your setup. You register your brewer once. From then on the roster rotates, the
origins change with the harvest, but the fit stays constant &mdash; a bag that
lands ready for the counter it was picked for. Skip a week, swap a grind, or
add a second brewer any time. The match follows you.
