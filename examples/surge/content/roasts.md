Our roster turns over with the harvest calendar, so what ships in March is not
what ships in October. What stays constant is the roasting intent: each coffee
is developed for a specific brewing method, and we label it plainly so you know
why it landed in your bag.

## Light roasts

Grown high, roasted short, dropped just past first crack. These keep their
origin character &mdash; the florals, the fruit acids, the tea-like clarity &mdash;
and they reward the slow, even flow of a pour-over.

> Meridian Bloom. Kenyan peaberry, washed. Blackcurrant and bergamot, a syrupy
> finish that lingers past the swallow.

Also on the light bench this season: **Altitude 1900**, a washed Ethiopian
Yirgacheffe with jasmine and lemon, and **Paper Kite**, a delicate Panama
honey-process lot we ration to a few hundred bags.

## Medium roasts

The forgiving middle. Developed through the gap between first and second crack
for sweetness and body without losing the origin entirely. These are our
best all-rounders for drip and immersion.

- **Copper Standard** &mdash; Colombia and Guatemala. Caramel, milk chocolate,
  toasted almond. The house default.
- **Riverbend** &mdash; a washed Brazil and Honduras blend, heavy on hazelnut,
  built to taste good even when you eyeball the ratio.

## Dark roasts

Taken into second crack for a low-acid, syrupy cup that punches through milk and
survives the pressure and heat of a moka pot or espresso machine.

- **Night Shift** &mdash; our espresso workhorse. Dark cocoa, molasses, a clean
  bittersweet finish under nine bars.
- **Ironwood** &mdash; a moka-pot special, roasted oily and fine-grind-ready,
  with dried fig and a smoky edge.

Every bag lists origin, process, roast date, and the brewers it was tuned for.
If a coffee on the roster suits your gear better than what you are getting, we
swap it in automatically &mdash; or you can lock a favorite and keep it.
