Surge started in a rented warehouse bay in the Rille River district with one
drum roaster, a cargo bike, and a nagging frustration. Our founder, Marta
Vensel, spent nine years behind a cafe bar dialing in espresso, then went home
and watched friends brew the same beans into disappointment on wildly different
gear. The coffee was never the problem. The match was.

## The idea

The insight was almost boring: roasters develop a coffee with a brewing method
in mind, but subscriptions ship it to everyone regardless of what is on their
counter. A gorgeous light-roast Ethiopian tuned for pour-over turns thin and
sour in a moka pot. A dark espresso blend goes flat and ashy in a drip machine.
So we flipped the order of operations &mdash; ask about the brewer first, then
choose the roast &mdash; and built the whole company around it.

## How we roast

We roast to order in batches of twelve kilos or less, log every curve, and cup
each roast against the extraction class it was built for before it ships. Beans
leave the roastery within eighteen hours, timed so they hit their rest window on
your shelf. Bags are compostable; spent chaff and grounds go to a community
garden two blocks over.

## Who we are

Today we are a team of six: three roasters, two people who keep the logistics
honest, and a green-buyer who spends half the year at origin. We work with a
short list of importers who pay above the commodity market and can tell us how a
lot was grown and processed &mdash; because that is what lets us match it to your
machine with a straight face.

If you brew coffee and have ever suspected the bag and the machine were arguing,
that argument is the thing we exist to end.
