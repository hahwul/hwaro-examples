This is the step that makes Surge different from every other subscription. Two
minutes now saves you months of blaming the beans for your machine's quirks.

## What we ask

For each brewer you own, we capture four things:

1. **Method and model** &mdash; the extraction class it belongs to.
2. **Grinder** &mdash; burr or blade, and roughly how fine it can go.
3. **Water** &mdash; filtered, hard, or soft, since minerals shift extraction.
4. **How you drink it** &mdash; black, with milk, or as a base for iced.

From those answers we assign a roast band, a grind setting, and a rest window,
then store them against your account.

## What each class gets

- **Pour-over** &mdash; light roasts, coarser grind, long rest. Clarity first.
- **Immersion** &mdash; medium roasts that hold up to a full four-minute steep.
- **Drip** &mdash; balanced mediums ground for a flat-bottom basket.
- **Moka pot** &mdash; dark, fine, low-rest beans that survive steam pressure.
- **Espresso** &mdash; medium-dark blends with the body for a nine-bar pull.

## Multiple brewers, one account

Plenty of subscribers keep a pour-over for weekends and an espresso machine for
weekday mornings. Register both and assign which brewer receives each delivery.
The roster fills each slot independently, so your Saturday cone gets a bright
Kenyan the same week your espresso gets a chocolatey dark.

## Changed your setup?

New grinder, new machine, moved to a house with harder water? Update the profile
and the change takes effect on your next order &mdash; roast band, grind, and
timing all re-tune together. No support ticket, no re-subscribing. The whole
point is that the coffee keeps up with your counter.
