# About Savanna

Savanna is a nature journal dedicated to the wildlife and landscapes of the African continent. From the sweeping grasslands of the Serengeti to the alkaline shores of Lake Nakuru, we document the lives, behaviors, and conservation challenges facing some of the planet's most remarkable species.

Our writers and field correspondents spend weeks embedded in national parks and wildlife reserves, observing animals in their natural habitats and speaking with the researchers and rangers who dedicate their lives to protecting them. Every story we publish is grounded in firsthand experience and careful research.

## Our Mission

We believe that understanding wildlife begins with storytelling. By sharing vivid, detailed accounts of animal behavior and ecology, we hope to foster a deeper connection between readers and the natural world. Conservation is not an abstract idea -- it is the daily work of protecting real creatures in real places.

## What You Will Find Here

Each article on Savanna focuses on a specific species or ecological phenomenon. Posts include structured metadata -- the species name, primary habitat, and current conservation status -- so readers can quickly orient themselves. We cover predators and prey, migratory herds and solitary hunters, birds and mammals alike.

Whether you are a seasoned wildlife enthusiast or discovering the African savanna for the first time, we invite you to explore these stories and learn what makes this landscape one of the most extraordinary on Earth.
