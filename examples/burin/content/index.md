Burin is a one-person type foundry. Every release starts as an engraved original — a bookplate cut for a private library, a banknote counter meant to defeat forgers, a chart-maker's lettering scored into copper with a burin — and is redrawn stroke by stroke into a working digital revival, kept close to the source's own irregularities rather than smoothed into a modern default. Five typefaces are in active release in the [full catalogue](/typefaces/); each one credits its plate, its engraver where known, and the year the line was first cut.
