How a cut becomes a release

  1. 01

    Source

    Every typeface starts as a single engraved object — a bookplate, a banknote counter, a chart cartouche — photographed under raking light so the burin's own cut is visible, not just the ink it left.

  2. 02

    Redraw

    Letterforms are traced stroke by stroke, keeping the taper, the register drift, and the tool's own irregularities rather than smoothing them into a modern default.

  3. 03

    Release

    Each face ships alone, with its plate documented in full — engraver, year, and archive — once every letter it needs has been redrawn and proofed.
