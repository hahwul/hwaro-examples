The market desk read a calm tape into the late edition. Front-page reads closed above the line, feed subscriptions added a modest column, and latency fell for the third edition running. The desk set the figures in the usual stock-table style, with up marked in solid ink and down marked in red.

{% alert(type="reading") %} Closing figures are true at the moment of going to press and are not revised after the edition is set. {% end %}

## How the Tape Is Set

Every listing is composed by hand from the day's logs, then locked into a hairline-ruled table. The desk holds to three rules:

1. **Figures align right** so the eye can run down a column without drifting.
2. **Change reads against the prior edition**, never against an arbitrary baseline.
3. **One accent only** — red marks a close or a correction and nothing else.

## The Day in Brief

- Front-page reads climbed six percent on the strength of two systems reports.
- Build latency fell below ten milliseconds and stayed there.
- Open corrections cleared from five down to one before the edition went out.

A static broadsheet cannot chase the market intraday, and the desk considers that a feature. The tape is read once, set once, and pressed, leaving readers with a figure they can quote without fear it has quietly moved beneath them.
