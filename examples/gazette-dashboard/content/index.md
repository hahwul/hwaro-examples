<h2 class="lead-headline">Data Desk Holds Its Composure as Traffic Surges Through the Late Edition</h2>
<div class="byline">By <b>Staff Correspondent</b> &middot; Dateline, The Composing Room</div>

<div class="lead-body">

The presses ran clean through the night. Readings filed from every desk arrived in order, and the index closed the day a fraction above its opening mark. Editors report no faults on the wire and a press run measured in single-digit milliseconds, a figure the composing room calls steady rather than spectacular.

Correspondents covering the traffic beat note that readership climbed past four thousand on the strength of two reports from the systems desk. The market table, set below, shows the day's listings with the customary hairline rules and right-aligned figures.

A spokesperson for the data desk declined to predict tomorrow's numbers, observing only that a static broadsheet keeps its promises: what is set, stays set, and renders the same on every reader's table.
