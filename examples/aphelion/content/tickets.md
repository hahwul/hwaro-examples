Aphelion keeps ticketing as unadorned as the programme. There is no online seat map and no dynamic pricing — one price per tier, sold at the box office window or held by name over the phone until thirty minutes before curtain.

<!-- more -->

## Prices

| Pass | Covers | Price |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Single screening | One film, any programme | kr 140 |
| Concession | Students, seniors, under 26 | kr 95 |
| Day pass | All programmes on one calendar day | kr 220 |
| Festival pass | All five screenings, 29 Jan – 1 Feb | kr 780 |

Festival passes are the only tickets that guarantee a seat automatically transferred if you miss a screening's admission window — every other tier releases the seat once its own film has started.

## How booking works

1. **Reserve by name.** Call or write the box office and a ticket is held under your name until thirty minutes before the screening.
2. **Pay on arrival.** Cash and card are both accepted at the window; there is no card fee.
3. **Collect early for the longer films.** *The Hurtigruten Hours* and *The Long Return* both run past three hours — arrive at doors to get a seat before the room fills, since unreserved seating within a tier is first-come. See the [full lineup](/screenings/) for every running time, and [venue](/venue/) for directions and house-lights timing.

## Refunds and transfers

Aphelion does not offer refunds once a screening has begun, in keeping with the no-late-admission policy most of the films require. Before a screening starts, a ticket can be transferred to any other programme in the same festival at no charge, subject to seats remaining — write to the box office with your booking name and the screening you would prefer instead.
