Aphelion takes its name from the point in Earth's orbit farthest from the sun — the week when daylight is thinnest and lowest at these latitudes. For four days in Bergen we clear a single screen for cinema that moves at the speed of weather: unbroken takes, tidal pacing, the patience of people who wait for ferries, for light, for winter to turn.

There are no double features and no red carpet. One film plays at a time, in full, with the house lights down until the last frame. Doors open thirty minutes before each screening; once the lights drop, the room holds still until they rise again. Full details on the room itself are on the [venue page](/venue/), and [tickets](/tickets/) covers pricing and the transfer policy.
