Every Aphelion screening plays in the same room: the single auditorium at Cinemateket Bergen, a converted herring warehouse on Bryggen fitted with 214 fixed seats, a proper masking curtain, and a projection booth that still runs both 35mm and DCP. There is no second screen and no simultaneous programme — for four days, the building has one film in it at a time.

<!-- more -->

## Getting there

The auditorium sits a five-minute walk from the Bryggen ferry quay and two stops from Bergen bus station on the city light rail. If you are arriving by the coastal ferry the way *The Hurtigruten Hours* does, the terminal is close enough to walk the route in reverse — several regulars do exactly that between the opening and closing films.

## Inside the room

- **Seating** is unreserved within your ticket's price tier; doors open thirty minutes before each screening and seats fill from the front.
- **House lights** stay at quarter for the first five minutes of any dark-adapted film, and drop fully once the projectionist confirms the room has settled — a slower fade than most cinemas use, kept deliberately for the durational programme.
- **No concessions** are sold or permitted inside the hall itself. A small counter in the foyer serves coffee and aquavit before doors and during the one scheduled interval (the closing film, *The Long Return*).
- **Wheelchair access** is step-free from the Bryggen side entrance, with two reserved spaces at the rear of the auditorium; write ahead to hold one, as the room does not take day-of transfers once a screening begins.

## Latecomer policy

Because most of this year's films are built from unbroken long takes, each screening's page states its own latecomer rule — some hold the door for a natural pause in the footage, others do not admit after the title card. Check the [full lineup](/screenings/) before you plan a tight connection from the ferry, and see [tickets](/tickets/) for how transfers work if you miss a window.
