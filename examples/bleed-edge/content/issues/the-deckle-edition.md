For each issue of BLEED EDGE, we produce two editions. The standard edition is trimmed to specification: 210 by 297 millimetres, with a clean guillotine cut on all four sides. The deckle edition is not trimmed. It ships with the 3mm bleed intact, the crop marks outside the intended page, and the original press-sheet edges visible.

## What a Deckle Edition Is

A true deckle edge is the natural rough edge of hand-made paper &mdash; the edge where the pulp slurry reaches the frame of the mould and settles, uncut. Our editorial use of the term is a stretch. The deckle edition has a machine edge, not a hand edge. But the effect is similar: an edge that the reader is not used to seeing on a commercial publication, an edge that reveals the material origin of the object.

## The Production Process

Our deckle copies are pulled from the press run at the same time as the standard copies, before they enter the guillotine. A count of 200 copies per issue is marked on the press sheet with a red grease-pencil line, and these copies are diverted. The guillotine trims the remaining 2,200 copies. The 200 deckle copies are folded, bound, and shipped untrimmed.

The binding on a deckle copy is more difficult. Without a trim, the signatures are slightly mis-registered &mdash; one page will be a few millimetres taller than its neighbour, because no two press sheets are identical. The binder accepts this variation and glues the spine with the signatures slightly fanned. The result is a book whose edges are visibly imperfect, and whose imperfection is the edition.

> The deckle edition is not a finer object than the standard edition. It is a less-finished object, and therefore a closer one.

### The Price Difference

A deckle copy costs approximately 40% more than a standard copy. This is not because the production is more expensive &mdash; it is in fact slightly cheaper, since the guillotine pass is omitted &mdash; but because the edition is limited. 200 copies per issue are produced, no more, and each is numbered by hand in pencil on the colophon page.

## What the Deckle Edition Reveals

In the deckle edition, the reader can see:

- The **crop marks** at the four corners, beyond what would have been the trim
- The **registration marks** at the outer midpoints
- The **colour bars** along the outer edge: small swatches of each printed ink, used by the press operator to confirm colour density across the run
- The **folio**, or sheet identifier: a small mark printed at the bottom of each signature, normally cut away by the guillotine

These are the pre-press and press-room artefacts, the signage of the production process. They are visible in the deckle edition and invisible in the standard edition. A reader who has only ever seen the standard edition has seen a different magazine.

### Distribution

Deckle editions ship direct from the press, numbered by hand, in a paper band. They are available only by subscription and are not sold at retail. The current waiting list is 14 issues long.
