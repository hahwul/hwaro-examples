## Why teams pick Hertz

Most status pages are built to reassure, not to inform. They stay green through
degraded latency, quietly redefine "operational," and bury the one incident
your customers actually noticed. Hertz takes the opposite bet: a status page is
only worth having if people believe it.

That belief is earned with small, boring choices. Every uptime figure on a
Hertz page is a real ratio of successful checks to total checks over a fixed
window, printed in monospace so nobody can round it into fiction. Incidents are
timestamped the moment you open them and stay in the record after they close.
Subscribers hear from you by email before they hear from each other on social.

Hertz is built for teams of two to twenty who would rather spend an afternoon
than a quarter on this. There is no query language to learn and no agent to
install. Point a check at a URL, name the services your users care about, and
you have a page you can link from your docs, your login screen, and your next
apology. Honest by default, quiet by design.
