## The question

When a regulation is drafted by a model and approved by a committee that did not read every line of it, who has written the rule?

## Panelists

- **01. Tessa Rein** &mdash; Former General Counsel, Federal Communications Council
- **02. Dr. Mehmet Demir** &mdash; Professor of administrative law, Istanbul
- **03. Iris Stern** &mdash; Managing editor, *Breaking Tape*
- **04. Judge Bronwen Oates (Ret.)** &mdash; US Court of Appeals, Second Circuit
- **05. Ren Oyelaran** &mdash; AI safety researcher, Proctorate Labs

## Moderator

Beth Albers, making her second appearance on the Day 02 programme. Closes out the main stage at 18:30.

## Format

Ninety-minute panel. Opening five minutes each, then structured cross-examination by the moderator, then a twenty-five-minute audience Q&amp;A.

## Moderator&rsquo;s brief

> &ldquo;We know the panel will agree that accountability is required. The question is whether we can name the person who is accountable &mdash; not the role, not the institution, the person. If every panelist ducks that question, we end early and we do not vote.&rdquo;

&mdash; Beth Albers

## Vote

Yes, and for this one the audience votes three times: at the start, at the midpoint, and at the end. The midpoint vote is a novelty this year; the moderator proposed it to the programme committee in January and the committee approved it.
