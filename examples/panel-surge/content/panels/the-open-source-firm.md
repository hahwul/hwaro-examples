## The question

Can an open-source project be governed, or does governance always descend, in the long run, into benevolent dictatorship or hostile fork?

## Panelists

- **01. Karim el-Ouafi** &mdash; Maintainer, libpattern (15 years)
- **02. Dr. Margaret Yue** &mdash; Foundation director, Open Infrastructure Forum
- **03. Jakub Zemel** &mdash; Engineering lead, Amber Labs
- **04. Priya Venkat** &mdash; Open-source policy, European Commission
- **05. Tomo Bevan** &mdash; Author, *The Hostile Fork*

## Moderator

Beth Albers, formerly of the Ford Foundation. Moderates one panel per day at Surge; has moderated the morning open-source panel for five consecutive years.

## Rules of engagement

This is a discussion format, not a debate. The moderator sets the order; each panelist has up to three minutes on each of five sub-questions. Sub-questions are:

1. When does a BDFL stop working?
2. What does a foundation actually change?
3. Are hostile forks always disasters, or sometimes the point?
4. How do you retire from the project you wrote?
5. What is the most interesting new governance experiment of the last five years, and why?

## Notes

The panel was proposed in response to last year&rsquo;s *libmatrix* governance crisis, which is discussed openly on stage with the consent of the two parties who were at its centre.
