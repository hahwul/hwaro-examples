This is the language specification, not a guide — read the tutorial first if you're new to Gneiss. Each clause page opens with a railroad diagram of its exact grammar, followed by its semantics and enough worked examples to cover the cases that trip people up. Clauses are numbered in the order Gneiss expects them to appear in a query: `MATCH`, then `WHERE`, then `WINDOW`, then `EMIT`.
