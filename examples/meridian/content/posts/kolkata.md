India Standard Time sits at UTC+5:30, one of the world's notable half-hour offset time zones. A single timezone covers the entire subcontinent, from Gujarat in the west to Arunachal Pradesh in the east -- a span of nearly 30 degrees of longitude.

<!-- more -->

## The Half-Hour Question

India's choice of a half-hour offset dates to 1906, when IST was established to replace the two separate time zones (Bombay Time and Calcutta Time). The 82.5 degrees east meridian, passing through Mirzapur in Uttar Pradesh, was chosen as the reference.

## Howrah Station Clock

Kolkata's Howrah Station, one of the busiest railway stations in the world, has a clock tower that has guided millions of passengers since 1906. Indian Railways operates the fourth-largest railway network in the world, all synchronized to IST.

## Key Timezone Facts

- **Longitude:** 88.3639 E (Kolkata) / 82.5 E (Standard Meridian)
- **Standard Time:** IST (UTC+5:30)
- **Daylight Saving:** Not observed
- **Single Timezone:** Covers 68 E to 97 E longitude
