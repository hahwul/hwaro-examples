New York operates on Eastern Standard Time, five hours behind UTC. As home to Wall Street and the New York Stock Exchange, the city's clock governs the rhythm of global financial markets.

<!-- more -->

## The Market Bell

The opening bell at the NYSE rings at 09:30 EST, and the closing bell at 16:00 EST. These two moments ripple across every trading floor in the world. When New York sleeps, Tokyo wakes; when Tokyo closes, London opens.

## Grand Central's Clock

The four-faced opal clock atop the information booth in Grand Central Terminal is valued at between $10 and $20 million. Installed in 1913, it has guided millions of commuters through one of the world's busiest transit hubs.

## Key Timezone Facts

- **Longitude:** 74.0060 W
- **Standard Time:** EST (UTC-5)
- **Summer Time:** EDT (UTC-4)
- **DST Change:** Second Sunday of March / First Sunday of November
