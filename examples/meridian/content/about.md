Meridian is a world clock dashboard inspired by the split-flap departure boards found in airports and train stations around the globe.

## The Name

A meridian is a line of longitude -- an imaginary great circle on the Earth's surface running from pole to pole. The Prime Meridian at Greenwich, London (0 degrees) is where our global timekeeping begins. Every timezone is measured as an offset from this line.

## Design Philosophy

The design draws from the mechanical precision of mid-century transit infrastructure. The monospaced typography, the dark backgrounds, the precise grids -- all echo the controlled environment of an operations center tracking movements across the globe.

## How It Works

Cities are grouped by their UTC offset region. Each entry shows its local time characteristics, cultural relationship with time, and geographic position along the world's meridian lines.
