This database is a field telemetry station tracking the active range, genealogy, pack hierarchy, and collar diagnostics of the Whitewolf pack. Stationed in the remote headwaters of the Yukon Valley, the project monitors spatial distribution, environmental adaptations, and interactions with neighbouring packs. 

The three-panel console provides a live dashboard view: the top filtering bar narrows down pack records, the left panel inventories the active collars with their transmission status, and the central viewport presents full diagnostic sheets, GPS coordinates, and historical context. All telemetry readouts, collar identifiers, and coordinate data are formatted in uppercase monospace to represent direct receiver outputs.

Researchers use this terminal to compile behavior logs, map territorial shifts during the winter migrations, and analyze genetic relationships. Each wolf profile is cross-referenced with collar battery levels, altitude readings, and sensor logs to ensure equipment integrity. Operations are funded by the Northern Yukon Ecology Center. Additionally, field biologists submit daily reports through encoded satellite uplinks, contributing to a broader understanding of sub-arctic pack dynamics and resource allocation. Future system updates will integrate automated alert triggers for rapid temperature drops.
