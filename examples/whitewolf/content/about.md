The Whitewolf Telemetry Initiative was established in response to shifting migratory patterns of caribou herds and territorial encroachment in the Yukon headwaters. To monitor wolf behavior without disrupting pack dynamics, researchers deployed custom GPS-collars utilizing low-frequency satellite transmission channels. 

Each collar weighs less than four hundred grams and is powered by a high-density lithium battery supplemented by miniature solar arrays. Every collar broadcasts coordinates, heart rates, skin temperatures, and activity signatures. The system records the pack's movements across rugged terrains and during severe sub-zero winter temperatures, transmitting alerts if signals are lost.

The field console is designed for low-bandwidth connections, rendering crucial text-based diagnostic data rather than heavy visual assets. This ensures researchers at remote base camps can access the wiki and collar inventory through satellite terminals. The database remains a collaborative record managed by the Yukon Wildlife Service and the Department of Ecological Sciences. By keeping the visual layout minimal and optimizing database queries, the system reduces power consumption on remote receiver terminals. Continued research relies on consistent updates from these remote stations to track long-term ecological adaptations.
