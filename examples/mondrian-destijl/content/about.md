## About This Journal

The **De Stijl Journal** is a small publication devoted to a single, stubborn idea: that art could be reduced to its absolute elements — the horizontal, the vertical, and the three primary colours — and that in this reduction it would become *universal*.

It takes its name from the Dutch movement founded in 1917 around the magazine *De Stijl* ("The Style"). Its protagonists — Piet Mondrian, Theo van Doesburg, Bart van der Leck, Gerrit Rietveld, and others — believed that the right angle was not a constraint but a liberation. Strip away the particular, the sentimental, the merely decorative, and what remains is structure. Pure relationship. Equilibrium.

This site is built in that spirit. There are no curves here, no diagonals, no soft edges. Only a white field, heavy black rules, and rectangles of red, blue, and yellow.

## About the Author

I write under the name **the Neoplasticist**, which is less a person than a position. I trained as a painter, lost faith in representation, and found a strange calm in the grid. I am interested in the moment a movement becomes a doctrine — and in what gets lost when it does.

I write here about:

- **Neoplasticism** — Mondrian's theory of pure plastic art and the discipline of the right angle
- **Primary colour** — why red, blue, and yellow, and what political weight the choice was made to carry
- **The internal feuds of De Stijl** — including the famous rupture over a single tilted line
- **Reduction as a method** — what it means to take things away until only the essential is left

## A Note on Method

I believe a journal about De Stijl should *look* like De Stijl. So this one is composed, not merely styled: every block is placed against another, every colour answers a line. If the layout feels severe, that is the point. The severity is the argument.

> "The truly modern artist sees the metropolis as abstract living converted into form." — Piet Mondrian
