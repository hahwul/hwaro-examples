## What Is This Place

Vapor is a vaporwave-inspired art blog and digital gallery dedicated to the
exploration of surreal consumerism, defunct aesthetics, and the strange beauty
of technological decay. We exist at the intersection of Greek antiquity and
the Windows 95 desktop, between the shopping mall and the void.

## The Aesthetic

Our visual language draws from:

- **Classical sculpture** -- marble busts, broken columns, the remnants of
  civilizations that also believed their commerce was eternal
- **90s digital culture** -- early web design, system dialog boxes, pixelated
  icons, and the optimism of a world just discovering hyperlinks
- **Consumer detritus** -- shopping channels, product packaging, brand logos
  divorced from their products, price tags on the intangible
- **VHS artifacts** -- tracking errors, scan lines, the warm distortion of
  magnetic tape slowly forgetting what it was asked to remember

## Why Vaporwave

Vaporwave is not simply an aesthetic. It is a lens. By slowing down, chopping
up, and recontextualizing the sounds and images of consumer capitalism, we
reveal the surreal foundation beneath the ordinary. The muzak was always
strange. The mall was always a temple. The dolphin was always a deity.

## The Blog

Posts on Vapor explore topics ranging from retro computing culture to critiques
of consumer society, from the history of dead malls to the philosophy of
digital nostalgia. Each piece is an artifact recovered from a timeline that
may or may not be our own.
