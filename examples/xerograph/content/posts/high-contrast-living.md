## The Binary Advantage

Remove the middle ground and see what remains. Most decisions that paralyze us do so because we have invented too many options. The photocopier knows only two states: black or white. Toner or no toner. Mark or unmarked surface.

This is not simplistic thinking. It is clarified thinking. The threshold is set, and everything above it becomes one thing, everything below becomes another. Ambiguity is eliminated not through force but through resolution.

## Edges Define Form

In high-contrast imagery, objects are defined entirely by their edges. There are no soft transitions, no gentle falloffs from light to shadow. A shape either exists or it does not.

This produces a visual language of extraordinary directness. A face in high contrast is not a portrait — it is a declaration. The viewer is not invited to contemplate subtle emotional gradations. They are confronted with presence or absence, figure or ground.

## The Courage of Commitment

Choosing high contrast is an act of creative courage. It means accepting that some information will be lost. It means trusting that what survives the process is sufficient — more than sufficient, essential.

Every creative decision is a threshold operation. What do you keep? What do you discard? The artist who works in high contrast has already answered these questions before picking up the tool. The medium itself enforces the discipline.
