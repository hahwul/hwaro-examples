## The System

Cruciform is a design system built on one core idea: **every element exists at an intersection**. Where a vertical grid line crosses a horizontal one, that crossing point becomes the anchor for content, navigation, and structure.

This is not a metaphor. The grid lines are visible. The intersection markers are rendered. The underlying structure is part of the design itself, not hidden scaffolding that disappears when the paint goes on.

## Swiss Roots

The International Typographic Style emerged from Switzerland and Germany in the 1950s. Its practitioners -- Josef Muller-Brockmann, Armin Hofmann, Emil Ruder -- established principles that remain foundational:

- **The grid as organizing principle.** Not a suggestion, but the primary structural element.
- **Asymmetric layouts** that create dynamic tension through mathematical relationships.
- **Sans-serif typography** at the service of clarity. Helvetica was not a fashion choice; it was a commitment to neutrality.
- **Photography over illustration.** Objective representation over subjective interpretation.
- **Flush-left, ragged-right text.** Uniform word spacing over justified columns.

## Urban Crossroads

Cruciform draws a second thread from the geometry of cities. Urban crossroads are intersections -- points where paths converge, where decisions happen, where energy concentrates. The plus sign (+) is both a typographic mark and an aerial view of a four-way crossing.

Every page in this system is a crossroads. The header bar crosses the sidebar line. Content blocks sit at intersections. Navigation markers use the cross symbol. The entire visual language reinforces this single, consistent metaphor.

## Technical Foundation

The system is built with:

- **CSS Grid** for structural layout with visible grid lines
- **CSS custom properties** for the intersection red / grid black / cross white / point gold palette
- **Pseudo-elements** (::before, ::after) to render cross markers at intersection points
- **Border-based** visual structure -- no gradients, no shadows beyond subtle accents
- **Helvetica / Arial / sans-serif** type stack in the Swiss tradition
- **8px base unit** for all spacing, ensuring every measurement aligns to the grid

## Philosophy

Decoration is noise. Structure is signal. In a world of rounded corners and soft shadows, Cruciform insists on straight lines and sharp intersections. The grid is not a constraint -- it is the design.
