*Virga* is rain that falls but never reaches the ground &mdash; a grey streak
hanging under the cloud, evaporating in the warm air below. It seemed the right
name for a paper about films where the weather never quite lands: the noir where
the downpour is a mood and not a plot point, the slow cinema where a shot of wet
asphalt is allowed to be the whole scene.

We publish long. A review here runs to the length the film earned, and no
shorter. This edition leads with the wettest picture of the year and keeps
going &mdash; through sodium light, through the grammar of downpour, through the
patience it takes to watch a room fill with dark. Read [the full run](essays/),
or go straight to the [archive](search/).
