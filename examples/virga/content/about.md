*Virga* is a nocturnal broadsheet of film criticism. It covers two overlapping
weathers: **rain-soaked noir** &mdash; the crime picture where water does the
emotional work the dialogue won't &mdash; and **slow cinema**, where duration
itself is the argument. The two meet more often than you'd think. A held shot of
a flooded intersection is both a genre cliché and an act of patience; we are
interested in the seam between them.

## The house style

We review long and we review late, usually after the second viewing, always
after dark. Our house rule is the only one that matters: **the star rating is a
footnote, not a verdict.** You will find a run of ornaments beside every review
&mdash; one to five &mdash; but they exist to give a busy reader a shape, not to
end an argument. The prose is the review. If a piece earns four marks and spends
two thousand words explaining the missing fifth, that is not a contradiction.
That is the job.

## Who writes it

The paper is written by a small standing desk of critics &mdash; Marisol Vane,
Théo Cassin, August Renn, Ilse Brandt, and Dagny Oorloff among them &mdash; who
share a projector, a bad kettle, and a stubborn preference for the 21:00
screening over the 13:00 one. Every film named in these pages is invented. The
directors, the runtimes, the festival premieres: all fiction. What is real is
the way the pictures are watched, which is slowly, and twice.

## Getting in

There is a [search page](@/search.md) for the archive and an [essays desk](@/essays/_index.md)
for the full run, newest first. Each piece is filed to a desk &mdash; reviews,
essays, dossiers &mdash; and threaded with the motifs it keeps returning to.
Follow a motif and you can read the paper sideways, by preoccupation rather than
by date.
