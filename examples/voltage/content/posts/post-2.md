The intermittency of renewable energy sources creates a fundamental grid stability challenge. Energy storage at scale is the missing piece that makes a renewable-dominant grid viable.

## Battery Technologies Compared

| Technology | Energy Density | Cycle Life | Response Time |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|--------------|
| Lithium-ion | High | 4,000-8,000 | Milliseconds |
| Flow batteries | Low | 10,000+ | Seconds |
| Sodium-sulfur | Medium | 4,500 | Seconds |
| Compressed air | Very low | Unlimited | Minutes |

## Lithium-Ion Dominance

Lithium-ion batteries currently dominate grid storage installations due to their high energy density, falling costs, and millisecond response times. A typical grid-scale installation uses thousands of individual cells arranged in racks within climate-controlled enclosures.

The main chemistries deployed are:

- **LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate)** -- longer cycle life, better thermal stability
- **NMC (Nickel Manganese Cobalt)** -- higher energy density, lower cycle life

## Flow Batteries for Duration

For storage durations beyond 4 hours, flow batteries offer compelling economics. Energy capacity scales independently of power capacity -- you simply increase the electrolyte tank volume.

Vanadium redox flow batteries have demonstrated over 20 years of operation with minimal degradation, since the electrolyte never chemically transforms.

## Grid Services

Storage systems earn revenue through multiple grid services:

1. **Frequency regulation** -- rapid charge/discharge to maintain 50/60 Hz
2. **Peak shaving** -- reducing demand charges during high-consumption periods
3. **Arbitrage** -- charging during low-price hours, discharging during peaks
4. **Black start** -- providing startup power after grid-wide outages

The economic viability of storage depends on stacking these revenue streams effectively.
