Before the kettle sings,
before the floorboards remember my weight,
there is a moment
when the house belongs to no one.

I stand at the window.
The garden is a rumor of green,
half-hidden in mist.

The cup warms both hands.
Steam rises and vanishes,
teaching me something
I already knew about impermanence.

---

Each morning is a draft,
revised by noon,
abandoned by evening.

I have learned not to grip
the early hours too tightly.
They are best held
the way one holds water:
with open palms.
