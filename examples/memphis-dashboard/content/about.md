BLAST.fm is an engagement dashboard that refuses to be quiet. It tracks visitors, plays, reactions, and listening streaks, then shows them off in the loudest way a grid will allow.

The look is pure Memphis Milano: the 1980s postmodern movement that traded restraint for squiggles, polka dots, and clashing flat color. We kept the playfulness and bolted it onto a layout you can actually read at a glance.

## what we believe

1. **Flat color is a feeling.** Cobalt, red, yellow, teal, and pink, blocked solid. No gradients, ever.
2. **Borders should be loud.** Three to four pixels of pure black around everything, with chunky offset shadows so cards feel like stickers.
3. **A little tilt goes a long way.** A card rotated two degrees is the difference between a spreadsheet and a party.

## colophon

- **Engine**: Hwaro, a static site generator built in Crystal.
- **Display type**: Space Grotesk.
- **Body type**: DM Sans.
- **Palette**: warm paper, near-black ink, and five flat accents.

We made it loud on purpose. Turn it up.
