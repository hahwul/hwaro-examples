## The Challenge

Meridian Press needed a publishing platform that could handle long-form investigative reporting without forcing reporters into a rigid CMS workflow. The existing tooling fragmented research notes, drafts, and final layouts across three separate systems, and editors spent hours each week reconciling versions.

## Our Approach

We spent two weeks embedded with the editorial team, observing how stories moved from pitch to publication. The pattern was clear: writers wanted plain text, editors wanted side-by-side comments, and designers wanted visual control over the final layout. We designed a single document model that supported all three modes.

### Deliverables

- Block-based document editor with markdown shortcuts
- Editorial review interface with inline comments and revision history
- Layout designer with typographic controls and asset placement
- Reader-facing site with subscriber paywall and archive search
- Component library covering 84 reusable patterns

## The Result

Meridian launched the platform with a flagship investigative series on municipal contracting. The editorial team reported that production time per article dropped from twelve hours to under five, and the design team shipped layout variants without engineering involvement.

The platform now hosts the full Meridian back catalog, including 600 archived pieces migrated from the previous system. Search response times stayed under 200 milliseconds across the corpus.

> Reader retention on long-form pieces increased by 28 percent in the first quarter after launch, measured by scroll depth and return visits.
