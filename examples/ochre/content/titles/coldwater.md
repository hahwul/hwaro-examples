Amaranth's six-part crime limited series *Coldwater* is set around a reservoir that flooded a town in 1974, and every episode returns to shots of the drowned buildings visible through clear winter water. The titles needed to feel like they were shot through that same water — not falling, not floating, but settling, the way silt settles once a current stops disturbing it.

<!-- more -->

**Coldwater Drift**, `cubic-bezier(0.11, 0.83, 0.32, 1)`, decelerates hard and early and never overshoots its target. Nothing in this sequence arrives with force; everything arrives by running out of energy. It is the gentlest curve in the studio library and the one we reach for most often when a director says some version of "I don't want it to feel animated at all."

## Beat sheet

**00:00 – 00:06 — Underwater plate.** A slow push through practical underwater photography of a submerged townscape built at one-eighth scale in the studio tank. No type yet. The score is a single sustained low string.

**00:06 – 00:16 — Series logotype.** COLDWATER drifts down from the top of frame at a third of normal gravity, each letter's fall governed by Coldwater Drift so it decelerates well before it reaches its resting baseline and appears to be met by resistance rather than a floor. The letters never bounce or correct — once slow, they stay slow.

**00:16 – 00:28 — Cast cards, suspended.** Six cards drift laterally across frame rather than fading, each riding Coldwater Drift horizontally so they appear to lose momentum mid-crossing, the way a dropped object loses speed once it is fully submerged. Cards use a heavy slab serif, chosen because its blunt terminals read clearly even softened by the tank's practical water caustics layered on top in post.

**00:28 – 00:35 — Title card and hold.** COLDWATER settles dead-center on the same curve stretched to its full seven-second duration, coming to rest so gradually that most viewers cannot identify the exact frame on which it stops moving. It holds for one second, motionless, before a hard cut to the cold open.

Amaranth's brief asked for "prestige, but wet." Coldwater Drift turned out to be doing most of that work by itself — a curve that never overshoots reads as considered even when the imagery underneath it is doing something as ordinary as a scale model in a studio tank.
