## Typography

This publication is set in **Playfair Display** (Claus Eggers Sorensen, 2011) for the display and **Lora** (Olga Karpushina and Alexei Vanyashin, 2011) for the body. Both are contemporary revivals of transitional serif faces &mdash; Playfair a nineteenth-century Didone, Lora a gentle Caslon-adjacent text face. Together they produce a reading voice that is simultaneously authoritative and warm, which we take to be the correct voice for a publication about worn books.

The display type is set in the 900 weight, with the italic reserved for the secondary voice. The body text is set in Lora at 17 point on 30 point leading &mdash; generous, slow, designed for the second reading rather than the first.

## Why The Spine Cracks

A book's spine cracks because the book has been opened. Every time a reader opens a bound codex wider than 110 degrees, a small amount of stress is placed on the glue or the thread at the gutter. Over hundreds of openings, the stress accumulates; the glue begins to release at its weakest point; the thread, if the book is sewn, begins to cut through the paper at the innermost margin. The spine begins to separate from the textblock.

This is usually framed as damage &mdash; the reader has not been careful, the book has been treated roughly, the spine has *cracked* in a pejorative sense. We wish to propose the opposite reading. A book with an uncracked spine is a book that has not been read, or has been read only once. A book with a cracked spine is a book that has been loved.

> The measure of a book is not its condition on the day you buy it. It is the condition on the day you pass it on.

## Production

Printed on 80gsm cream stock, folded into 16-page signatures, sewn on five raised bands with waxed linen thread, perfect-bound with flexible PVA glue at the spine. The cover is 170gsm uncoated buckram-textured board, deliberately lightweight so that the book lies flat on the first opening and cracks at the spine on the second. A book intended to break.

## Distribution

Copies ship direct from the bindery, untrimmed on three edges. Each copy is numbered in pencil on the final leaf. When a reader finishes a copy, we request they pass it on &mdash; with their marginalia intact &mdash; to the next reader. The publication is a chain letter in book form.
