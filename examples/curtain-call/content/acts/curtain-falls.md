## FINALE

### The Curtain Falls

*The orchestra swells. The lighting state shifts to deep violet and gold. The entire company stands in a single line across the stage.*

This is the moment we have been building toward. Not with dread, but with the quiet satisfaction of a story well told.

### The Last Words

There are no grand speeches in a true finale. The work speaks for itself. What remains is simple:

**Thank you.**

Thank you to every performer who gave their craft and their courage. Thank you to every crew member who ensured the machinery of theatre ran without a sound. Thank you to every audience member who chose to be present, who chose to witness, who chose to believe.

### The Descent

The curtain begins its slow descent. It does not fall -- it lowers, with the same deliberate grace that defined this entire production.

From the audience, you see the feet of the performers disappear first. Then the costumes. Then the faces. Until all that remains is a wall of deep crimson fabric and the echo of the final chord.

### After the Curtain

The house lights will rise. The audience will gather their things. Conversations will begin -- about favourite moments, about what surprised them, about what moved them.

The theatre will empty. The stage will be struck. The costumes will be packed. The set will be dismantled.

But the production does not end. It continues in every person who witnessed it. It lives in the space between memory and imagination, where all great theatre resides.

### Fin

*The curtain reaches the stage floor. The house lights rise. The theatre breathes.*

*FINALE concludes. End of programme.*
