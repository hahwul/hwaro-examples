## ACT V, Scene iii

### Final Bow

*The full company assembles on stage. The orchestra plays the overture theme one final time.*

One by one, then all together. The cast steps forward into the light to receive the recognition they have earned.

### The Ensemble

No production succeeds on the strength of a single performer. It is the ensemble -- the chorus, the supporting cast, the understudies, the swing performers -- who create the world the audience inhabits.

Tonight, they take their bow first. As they should.

### The Principals

Next come the leads. The performers who carried the narrative weight, who returned night after night to inhabit characters larger than themselves. Their bow is deep, earned through repetition and dedication.

### The Creative Team

Behind every performer stands a director, a designer, a choreographer, a musical director. They do not often take the stage, but tonight they join the company. The audience sees, perhaps for the first time, the full scope of what it takes to bring a production to life.

### The Crew

And finally, the crew. The stage managers, the technicians, the dressers, the front-of-house staff. Without them, there is no theatre. Their bow is the most important of all.

### The Ovation

The audience rises. Not because they are prompted, but because they understand what they have witnessed. The applause builds -- not merely for tonight, but for every night that led to this one.

---

*Scene iii concludes. The company holds the stage together.*
