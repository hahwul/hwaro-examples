## On the Opening

The opening set is the most important in the night. It sets the tone, the tempo, and the patience of the floor. Kiyomi has held the opening slot at the Reserve since 2021 and has a reputation for warming the ballroom without pushing it - the tempo creeps up from 118 toward 124 across the ninety minutes; the crowd finds its feet without noticing that it has done so.

## A Short Biography

Kiyomi began selecting in the bathhouses of Shimokitazawa in the early 2010s, moved to Berlin in 2015 to apprentice at Hard Wax, and returned to Tokyo periodically for residencies at Contact and Vent before joining the Reserve as a founding resident of the present rotation in 2021.

Her selections lean toward the American and Italian house catalogues of 1988 to 1995, with excursions into the deeper end of the Dutch catalogue (Ferrostaal, RKDS, the first two Delsin 12-inches) and the occasional contemporary release when it merits inclusion.

## A Note from the Resident

> "I open the floor because that is the role that suits me. I do not need to be the loudest voice in the night. I need the dancers to arrive, to settle, and to feel that by 01:30 they have been somewhere already, and that the rest of the night is still to come."

The opening set is designed to pair with the room's warm-up lighting rig, which holds the ballroom at a pale amber till 01:30 when the disco ball takes over. Please arrive on time to hear it in full - the first twenty minutes are as considered as the last twenty.
