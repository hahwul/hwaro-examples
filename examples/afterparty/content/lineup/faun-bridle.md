## On the Garden

Room D (the Garden) is the outdoor covered courtyard. It has its own bar and its own soundsystem; the courtyard opens onto the night air and is heated against the spring chill. Faun's six-hour set covers the full session and carries the quiet edge of the programme - ambient, field recordings, drone, and downtempo - as a counterpoint to the club's interior rooms.

## A Short Biography

Faun Bridle began as a sound artist rather than a club selector, with installations at the Glasgow Centre for Contemporary Arts and the Arts Council collection in 2014 and 2016. The transition to club rooms came in 2019, initially through a series of dusk-to-dawn sets at a Glasgow festival, and from 2021 as a resident at the Reserve's Garden.

Her practice brings an installation artist's patience to the long format - six-hour sets are assembled with the same care as a gallery piece, and the session comprises three two-hour movements with distinct textures.

## A Note from the Resident

> "The garden is where dancers come when they need to not dance for a while. The music has to accommodate that - it cannot demand attention, but it also cannot be wallpaper. It has to be the third thing in the conversation."

Faun's sets often incorporate field recordings made earlier in the week. Tonight's programme includes approximately thirty minutes of material recorded in Kensal Green cemetery on the preceding Tuesday morning, and a longer section built around a recording of the soundsystem being installed at the venue yesterday afternoon.
