## On the Tape Room

Room C is the Tape Room. It is not a dancefloor; it is a listening room, with seating for sixty and standing space for another twenty. The soundsystem is configured for reference listening rather than dancing - closer to a hi-fi parlour than a nightclub.

Suff-Mode curates a six-hour continuous programme of recorded mixes from residents past and present. The programme is assembled anew for each session and features between twelve and eighteen mixes, each running twenty to thirty-five minutes, connected by short tape interludes recorded in the Reserve's own studio.

## A Short Biography

Suff-Mode operated a radio programme out of a community station in south Manchester from 2002 to 2016 and has been a resident at the Reserve's Tape Room since 2019. The Tape Room itself was built for Suff-Mode's curation and is the only one of the four rooms that was not part of the original venue.

## A Note from the Resident

> "The Tape Room is for the dancers who want to take a break from dancing. Come in, sit down, drink some water, listen to a thirty-minute mix recorded six years ago by a resident who has since moved to Tokyo. Nobody is going to ask you to leave. Stay for an hour or stay for the whole night."

This session's programme includes mixes from three former residents who will not be in attendance tonight (Lene Arrufat in 2019, Joss McFee in 2020, Verwin Kay in 2022), a live recording of a Dex Marquis set from March 2021, and a previously unreleased thirty-five minute mix from Kiyomi Nero recorded in her Berlin studio last month.
