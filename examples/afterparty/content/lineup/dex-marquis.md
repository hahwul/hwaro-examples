## On the Headline

Dex's 03:00 slot has been fixed since the first session in 2018. The set runs two full hours, peaks at 138 bpm around 04:15, and holds that peak for the better part of thirty-five minutes before beginning the descent toward the closing set at 05:00. The room behaves differently under Dex than under anyone else on the rotation; it is worth arriving before 03:00 to secure a position near the sound.

## A Short Biography

Dex Marquis has operated a record shop and sound system in Hackney Wick since 2012. The Reserve sessions began as an overflow for the soundsystem's in-store parties; they moved to the arches in 2019 and have been the resident Saturday programme there since 2020.

His selections are characterised by a preference for heavier Detroit and Rotterdam catalogues, a careful rotation of pressed-copy versions over digital, and the occasional unreleased dub from the Hackney Wick sessions which do not appear in any commercial release.

## A Note from the Resident

> "People ask me whether the set gets boring after a hundred and however many Saturdays. No. The floor is different every time; the tracks sit differently depending upon who is in front of you, and when you are in the week, and whether the rain has been heavy. You learn to listen to the floor as much as you play to it."

The headline set is the principal reason many dancers attend the session and is the one slot where phones will absolutely not be tolerated upon the floor. This is a long-standing preference of the resident, not a venue policy; the preference is widely known and widely honoured.
