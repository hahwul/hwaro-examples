Cinnabar is a one-person hot shop working glass the way it has been worked for two thousand years: gathered molten from the furnace on the end of a blowpipe, shaped by breath and gravity against a wet block, and walked step by step toward a form that will hold. The furnace runs day and night at 1100°C — everything here, from the tool wall to the bench schedule, is built around that number.

This is a working record, not a shop window. Each entry in the vessel log follows one piece from its first gather to the moment it goes into the annealer, with the reheats, tool changes, and small failures that never make it into a finished-piece photograph. Weights, temperatures, and anneal schedules are logged alongside the notes, the same way they are read off the pyrometer on the bench. Start with the [vessel log](vessels/) or read more about the [studio and the furnace](about/).
