The Heirloom Seal of the Realm (传国玉玺) is perhaps the most famous jade artifact in history. Carved from the legendary He Shi Bi jade, this seal represented the divine right to rule all of China.

## The Legend of He Shi Bi

The story begins with a man named Bian He, who discovered an uncut jade stone in the mountains of Chu. He presented it to the king, but court jewelers dismissed it as ordinary rock. Bian He lost a foot as punishment for "deception."

> "The world sees stone where the worthy see jade. True value requires true vision." — Records of the Grand Historian

Only after two kings had rejected it was the stone finally cut open, revealing jade of such extraordinary quality that it became the most prized treasure in all of China.

## From Stone to Seal

When Qin Shi Huang unified China in 221 BCE, he ordered the He Shi Bi carved into an imperial seal. The inscription read:

1. **受命于天** — "Having received the Mandate of Heaven"
2. **既寿永昌** — "May the dynasty prosper eternally"

This seal passed through dynasty after dynasty, its possession legitimizing each new emperor's claim to the throne.

## Jade as Authority

The choice of jade — not gold, not silver — for the ultimate symbol of power reveals deep cultural values:

- **Permanence** — Jade endures where metals corrode
- **Purity** — The stone's clarity reflects moral virtue
- **Resonance** — When struck, jade produces a clear, musical tone, symbolizing wisdom

The imperial jade seal teaches us that true luxury is not about flash, but about substance that endures through ages.
