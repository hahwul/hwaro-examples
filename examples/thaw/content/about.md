Thaw is kept by Marren Osk, a hedgerow forager and former nursery grower
working the mixed woodland and river-valley edges of a cool temperate
region — the kind of country where the frost line retreats a little more
every week from February onward and something new is always just breaking
ground. The name comes from that stretch of the year: the notes started as
a way to remember what came up first, second, and third once the thaw set
in, and grew from there into a full-season log.

Every entry follows the same shape on purpose. The common name and the
Latin name come first, because common names drift by region and the Latin
binomial is the only thing that travels. Then the season-band, so the
harvest window is legible before you read a single sentence of prose. Then
field marks, habitat, the look-alikes worth knowing, and one honest way to
use what you have picked.

Nothing here replaces a regional field guide, a mycological society, or a
second and third opinion before you eat something you found on the
ground. Thaw is a set of notes from one patch of country, written down
carefully so the next season is easier than the last one. Start with the
[full field-note log](/field-notes/) if you want to see everything in one
place, sorted newest first.

Corrections and regional notes are always welcome — reach the desk at
notes@thaw.example.
