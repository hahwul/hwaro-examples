A Newspaper for Your Documentation, Set in Ink & Hairlines

Newsprint dresses technical writing in the clothes of a broadsheet daily: a thundering nameplate, ruled columns, small-caps subheads, and pull-quotes with the authority of an editorial. Built on the Hwaro static site generator, it renders in milliseconds and reads like the morning paper.

By the Editorial Desk · The Crystal Press · Begin reading →

Documentation deserves better than another flat dashboard. Newsprint treats every page as a printed article — with a dateline, a deck, ruled columns, and the visible grid of a composing room. Nothing here is decorative for its own sake; the rules and the rhythm exist to make long-form technical prose easy to scan and a pleasure to read.

The theme ships as a complete documentation site. There are eight reference articles, three longer guides, and an about page, all written in plain Markdown and rendered by Hwaro's Crinja templates. You write the words; the press handles the layout, the type, and the typesetting.

Everything is editable. The palette lives in CSS variables, the nameplate is a single template, and the front page you are reading now is one file. Fork it, change the title in config.toml, and you have your own daily gazette. No build step beyond hwaro build, no runtime, no JavaScript framework demanding tribute.
