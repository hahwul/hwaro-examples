# About Plasma

Plasma is a science visualization platform dedicated to exploring the fourth state of matter. Our mission is to make the complex physics of plasma accessible through clear data presentation, interactive visualizations, and rigorous scientific writing.

## What is Plasma?

Plasma is the most abundant form of ordinary matter in the universe. It is created when a gas is heated to such extreme temperatures -- or subjected to such strong electromagnetic fields -- that electrons are liberated from their atomic nuclei. The result is a hot, electrically conductive collection of ions and free electrons that exhibits collective behavior governed by electromagnetic forces.

You encounter plasma more often than you might realize: in lightning bolts, neon signs, the solar wind, fluorescent lamps, and the vast interstellar medium between stars.

## Our Focus Areas

### Magnetic Confinement Fusion

We track and analyze experimental data from tokamak and stellarator devices worldwide. Magnetic confinement fusion aims to harness the same energy source that powers the Sun by confining hydrogen plasma at temperatures exceeding 100 million degrees using carefully shaped magnetic fields.

### Plasma Diagnostics

Understanding plasma behavior requires sophisticated measurement techniques. We cover Thomson scattering, interferometry, spectroscopy, and magnetic diagnostics -- the tools that allow physicists to peer into the heart of a fusion reactor.

### Magnetohydrodynamics

MHD theory describes the macroscopic behavior of electrically conducting fluids. We explore instability modes, equilibrium configurations, and the delicate balance between plasma pressure and magnetic confinement.

### Space and Astrophysical Plasma

From solar flares to magnetospheric substorms, plasma physics governs some of the most spectacular phenomena in the cosmos. We examine the connections between laboratory plasma experiments and the natural plasma processes observed throughout the universe.

## The Platform

This site is built with [Hwaro](https://github.com/hahwul/hwaro), a fast static site generator written in Crystal. The design draws inspiration from fusion reactor observation windows -- dark interfaces with luminous data readouts, evoking the experience of watching a plasma discharge through the viewport of an experimental chamber.

All content is written by researchers and enthusiasts committed to accuracy and clarity. We believe that the beauty of plasma physics lies in its fundamental nature, and our role is simply to present it well.
