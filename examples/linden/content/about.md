Linden is a working reference for growers in USDA zones 3 through 5, where the ground freezes hard, the frost-free window can close before squash sets fruit, and a single late frost in June is not unusual. It was started in 2019 by a small group of market growers and homestead orchardists around the upper Great Lakes and northern prairie who were tired of reading permaculture guides written for zone 8 gardens and quietly discarding half the advice.

Every method in this wiki has been built and planted by a contributor, then left to survive at least one winter before it was written up. We would rather publish six methods we trust than sixty we have only read about. When a method fails somewhere it was expected to work, we note the failure in the entry rather than deleting it — a swale that silted up in year two is still useful information.

## How an entry is built

An entry starts as a field note: sketches, a materials list, and a season-by-season log kept by whoever built the thing. Before it is published, at least one other contributor tries to reproduce it on their own ground, in a different microclimate if possible. Guild entries are not published until the planting has gone through a full year, because the interactions that matter — root competition, shade timing, which pollinators actually show up — do not appear in the first season.

The full [list of methods](/methods/) is sorted alphabetically rather than by category, because most growers arrive already knowing whether they need a guild, an earthwork, or a structure — the search field under the homepage statement is the faster way in if you are not sure of a title.

## Reading the guild matrices

Guild pages close with a companion-planting matrix: a bordered grid of every plant in the guild against every other. A filled circle means the pair has performed well together across multiple seasons and sites. A hollow circle means the pair is neutral, unproven, or better kept at a distance. The matrix is not a universal rule — it reflects what has actually happened in zone 3 through 5 plantings, and your frost dates, soil, and wind exposure will shift results.

## Contact and contributions

Linden is maintained by volunteer growers and does not accept sponsored content. If you have built and tested a method that belongs here, write up a season-by-season log and send it through the address on the project's public listing. We read everything, but a method still needs its winter before it goes up.
