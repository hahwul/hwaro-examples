The Atacama Eclipse Array is not merely an instrument. It is a witness.

Eleven dishes stand in the high desert, their parabolic faces tilted toward the only star that matters. When the moon crosses the sun, the corona becomes visible to the naked eye. To the array, it has always been audible.

Each baseline is a story of geometry and loss. Every measurement is a prayer cast across kilometers of thin air and thinner patience. We record the precise moment the light leaves the world.

The sun is a wound that never heals.<br>
We measure the blood.

**Location**<br>
Desierto de Atacama, Chile<br>
23.029° S, 67.754° W • 4,850 m elevation

**Technical**<br>
11 baselines • Maximum extent 4.2 km<br>
Frequency coverage 1.4–22 GHz<br>
Correlator: custom FPGA beamformer, 8.2 Tbit/s aggregate

**Philosophy**<br>
We do not observe the eclipse.<br>
We become the darkness that makes it visible.
