Before a single word is read, the typeface has already spoken. Serif or sans-serif, tight or loose, heavy or light — these choices tell the reader what kind of experience to expect. A newspaper set in Futura would feel like science fiction. A tech blog in Garamond would feel like a period drama. Typography is not decoration; it is the voice of the text made visible.

The broadsheet newspapers of the nineteenth and twentieth centuries understood this instinctively. Their mastheads were set in bold, commanding serifs that conveyed authority and permanence. Body text was small but meticulously kerned, designed to pack maximum information into minimum space without sacrificing legibility.

## The Serif Renaissance

For a decade, the web favored sans-serif fonts — clean, modern, and easy to render on low-resolution screens. But as display technology improved, serifs returned. Google Fonts made typefaces like Playfair Display and Lora available to everyone, and designers rediscovered the warmth and readability that serifs bring to long-form text.

A well-chosen serif font does something remarkable: it slows the eye just enough to encourage careful reading. The small flourishes at the ends of strokes create a gentle rhythm that guides the reader along the line, reducing fatigue and increasing comprehension.

## Hierarchy and Rhythm

Great typography is not just about choosing the right font. It is about establishing a visual hierarchy — headlines that command attention, subheadings that orient the reader, body text that flows naturally, and captions that whisper context.

Line height, letter spacing, and column width all contribute to the reading experience. A line that is too long forces the eye to travel too far; too short, and the constant line breaks disrupt the flow. The sweet spot — roughly 50 to 75 characters per line — has been known to typographers for centuries.

The gazette tradition offers a masterclass in these principles. Every element of its design serves a purpose: to make the act of reading as effortless and pleasurable as possible.
