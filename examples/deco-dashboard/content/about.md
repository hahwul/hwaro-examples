The Gilded Exchange is a ceremonial registry dressed in the manner of the great Deco halls of the 1920s — gold geometry struck upon near-black, where every holding is entered with the gravity of a charter.

We believe a ledger may be both exacting and ornamental. Numbers are kept to the line; the ornament is kept to the gold.

## The House Principles

1. **Symmetry above all.** The masthead is centered, the panels are flanked by hairline rules, and the eye is drawn upward through stepped, ziggurat frames.
2. **Gold is a vow, not a glimmer.** Our gold is a single flat solid — never a metallic gradient. Ornament is drawn in line, not in sheen.
3. **The ledger is sacred.** Every holding is reconciled, witnessed, and sealed before it is shown upon the floor.

## The Charter Hand

- **Engine** — the Hwaro static generator, compiled in Crystal.
- **Display** — Cinzel, set wide in small-caps for every heading and seal.
- **Body** — Cormorant Garamond, an elegant serif for the prose of the registry.
- **Palette** — Noir, gold, and cream, with a single emerald accent held in reserve.
