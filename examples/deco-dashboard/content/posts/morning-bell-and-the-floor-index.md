At the appointed hour the bell is struck, and only then may the floor index be read aloud. The ceremony is older than the building, and the Exchange keeps it because numbers gain their weight from ritual as much as from arithmetic.

## The Order Of The Bell

The opening proceeds in a fixed sequence, witnessed by the clerk of the registry:

1. The vault is unsealed in the presence of two witnesses.
2. The overnight reconciliation is read against the closing ledger.
3. The floor index is calculated and announced from the dais.
4. The first lots are released for the day's exchange.

The index itself is a weighted figure. The clerk records it thus, in the house notation:

```
INDEX = (sealed_lots * confidence) / open_obligations
      = (312 * 0.73) / 0.118
      = 1924  ; struck at the morning bell
```

> A number without a ceremony is merely a rumour. The bell makes it a record.

By noon the quorum convenes, the index is confirmed, and the floor settles into the steady rhythm of the trading day. The figure announced at the bell stands for the morning; any revision waits for the evening reconciliation, when the vault is sealed again.
