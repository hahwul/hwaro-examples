The vault is the conscience of the Exchange. Every gram of bullion entered into the reserve is weighed twice, recorded in the master ledger, and sealed under two signatures. Nothing is held on trust alone.

## The Reckoning Of The Reserve

Each reserve entry carries the same fields, kept in the order the clerks have used since the house was founded:

- **Weight** — measured to the gram, witnessed by the assayer.
- **Origin** — the holding or member from which it was lodged.
- **Seal** — the date of sealing and the two signatures.
- **Standing** — sealed, pending, or under review.

<div class="deco-alert">
<strong>Notice</strong> &mdash; No member may enter or withdraw bullion without a counter-signature from the clerk of the registry.
</div>

The reserve currently stands at 88.4 kilograms, reconciled against the master ledger to the gram. When a discrepancy appears — and over the years a handful have — the floor is paused, the vault is recounted, and no lot moves until the figure agrees.

> Gold does not lie, but ledgers can be careless. We keep both honest by keeping both witnessed.

It is a slow discipline, and deliberately so. The Exchange would rather close the floor for an hour than enter a single gram it cannot vouch for.
