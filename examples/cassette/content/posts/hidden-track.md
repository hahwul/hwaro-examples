Long after the closing groove fades, the tape keeps turning. A breath of static, a soft thump, and then a voice that was never printed on the sleeve.

Hidden tracks were a small ritual of trust between artist and listener. They rewarded patience. You had to keep the deck running through the run-out, leaving the room quiet enough to catch what came next.

> "The silence was part of the song. You just had to wait long enough to hear it."

This third entry is for the listeners who never hit stop. The ones who let the spool keep rolling, who heard a chorus emerge from the noise floor and felt like they had been let in on a secret.

Pour another cup. Side A and Side B both have endings, but the cassette never really stops giving until the leader pulls free.
