An everyday Japanese soup that comes together in minutes. The trick is to never boil the miso — heat kills its delicate flavor.

<!-- more -->

## Ingredients

- 500ml dashi stock (or 500ml water with 1 tsp dashi powder)
- 2 tbsp white miso paste
- 100g soft tofu, cut into small cubes
- 1 sheet dried wakame seaweed (about 5g)
- 1 stalk green onion, thinly sliced

## Steps

1. Soak the dried wakame in a small bowl of water for 5 minutes. It will expand considerably. Drain and set aside.
2. Bring the dashi stock to a gentle simmer in a small pot. Do not let it reach a rolling boil.
3. Add the tofu cubes and wakame. Let them warm through for 2 minutes.
4. Remove the pot from heat. Place the miso paste in a ladle, lower it halfway into the broth, and use chopsticks or a small whisk to dissolve the miso into the liquid. This prevents lumps.
5. Return the pot to low heat briefly — just enough to warm everything through. Remove from heat again the moment you see the first wisp of steam.
6. Ladle into bowls and scatter green onion on top.

## Notes

- Never boil miso soup after adding the miso. Boiling destroys the living cultures and dulls the flavor.
- White (shiro) miso is milder and sweeter. Red (aka) miso gives a deeper, saltier result. Both work well.
