The best fried rice starts with yesterday's rice and a screaming hot wok. Fast, satisfying, and endlessly adaptable.

<!-- more -->

## Ingredients

- 400g cold cooked rice (day-old, refrigerated)
- 3 eggs, lightly beaten
- 2 stalks green onion, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- Salt to taste

## Steps

1. Heat a wok or large skillet over the highest heat you have. Add the oil and swirl to coat.
2. When the oil begins to shimmer, pour in the beaten eggs. Let them set for about 10 seconds, then scramble roughly into large pieces. Remove from the wok and set aside.
3. Add the cold rice directly to the hot wok. Press it flat against the surface and let it sear without stirring for 30 seconds. Then toss and repeat. Do this for 3-4 minutes until the grains are separate and slightly crispy at the edges.
4. Drizzle soy sauce around the edge of the wok so it hits the hot metal first, then toss the rice through it.
5. Return the scrambled eggs to the wok. Add the green onions. Toss everything together for 30 seconds.
6. Taste for salt. Serve immediately.

## Notes

- The single most important factor is using cold, dry rice. Freshly cooked rice will turn to mush.
- A carbon steel wok at maximum heat is ideal, but a cast iron skillet works well too.
