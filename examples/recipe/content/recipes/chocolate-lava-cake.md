A rich, molten-centered chocolate cake that looks far harder than it actually is. The key is timing — pull it from the oven just before it fully sets.

<!-- more -->

## Ingredients

- 150g dark chocolate (70% cacao), chopped
- 120g unsalted butter
- 2 whole eggs plus 2 egg yolks
- 60g sugar
- 30g all-purpose flour
- Butter and cocoa powder for the ramekins

## Steps

1. Preheat the oven to 220C (430F). Butter four ramekins generously, then dust with cocoa powder, tapping out the excess.
2. Melt the chocolate and butter together in a heatproof bowl over barely simmering water. Stir until smooth, then remove from heat and let cool slightly.
3. In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs, egg yolks, and sugar until the mixture is pale and slightly thickened, about 2 minutes by hand.
4. Fold the chocolate mixture into the egg mixture. Sift the flour over the top and fold gently until just combined — do not overmix.
5. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared ramekins. At this point, they can be refrigerated for up to 8 hours if you want to bake them later.
6. Bake for exactly 12 minutes. The tops should be set and slightly cracked, but the centers should still feel soft when you press gently.
7. Let cool in the ramekins for 1 minute. Run a knife around the edge, invert onto plates, and serve immediately.

## Notes

- Use good chocolate. This recipe has so few ingredients that quality is everything.
- If baking from cold (refrigerated), add 2 minutes to the bake time.
- A scoop of vanilla ice cream alongside is never wrong.
