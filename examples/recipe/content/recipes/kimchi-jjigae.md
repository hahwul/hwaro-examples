A deeply comforting Korean stew built on well-fermented kimchi and pork belly. The older the kimchi, the better the result.

<!-- more -->

## Ingredients

- 300g well-fermented kimchi, roughly chopped
- 150g pork belly, sliced thin
- 1 block soft tofu (about 300g)
- 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 400ml water or anchovy stock

## Steps

1. Heat sesame oil in a pot over medium heat. Add the pork belly slices and cook until the fat begins to render, about 3 minutes.
2. Add the chopped kimchi and stir-fry together for 4-5 minutes until the kimchi softens and absorbs some of the pork fat.
3. Sprinkle in the gochugaru and minced garlic. Stir for 30 seconds until fragrant.
4. Pour in the water or anchovy stock. Add soy sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer.
5. Let it simmer uncovered for 15-20 minutes. The broth should reduce slightly and turn a rich red.
6. Slide the tofu in large spoonfuls into the stew. Simmer gently for another 5 minutes — do not stir too aggressively or the tofu will break apart.
7. Serve immediately in the pot with steamed rice on the side.

## Notes

- Use kimchi that has been fermenting for at least 2-3 weeks. Fresh kimchi will not give the stew enough depth.
- Substitute pork belly with canned tuna for a lighter, quicker version.
