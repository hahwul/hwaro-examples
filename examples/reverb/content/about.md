Reverb is a cryptography podcast about the machinery of secrecy — how it was built, how it held, and eventually how it fell. Every [episode](../episodes/) picks one cipher or one cryptographic idea and works through it from the ground up: what problem it was solving, how the design decisions were made, and the specific insight that eventually broke it, if one exists yet. We start with a Caesar wheel and end wherever the current season takes us; season one runs from shift ciphers to lattice-based schemes built to survive a quantum adversary.

## Format

Episodes run forty to fifty minutes, released roughly every eight to ten weeks. Each one is built around a small, runnable code example — you'll find it in the show notes for that block, not just described but actually implemented, so you can break it yourself before we tell you how. We keep episodes ad-free and self-funded; if you want to support the show directly, the best way is sharing an episode with the one person you know who'd argue with it.

## Who makes it

Reverb is hosted and produced independently, with a different subject-matter guest joining for nearly every block — cryptohistorians, applied cryptographers, machine restorers, and current contributors to post-quantum standards work. Guests are chosen for depth on that episode's specific topic rather than general fame, which means the reading list changes every time and the bibliography is usually longer than the transcript.

## Show notes convention

Every episode card on this site shows its preview text scrambled with a Caesar-family cipher — specifically ROT13, a shift of thirteen, the one shift cipher that's its own inverse. Hover or focus a card to decrypt it in place. It's a small habit borrowed from decades of spoiler-tagging on cryptography forums, and it felt wrong to build a cipher podcast's homepage without at least one working cipher on it.

## Get in touch

For guest suggestions, corrections to a claim made on air, or a cipher you think deserves its own block, reach the production team through the contact link in any episode's platform listing. Corrections that hold up get a credit at the top of the next episode's show notes.
