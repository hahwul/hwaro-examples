# Unlicensed Chrome Clinic Network Busted in District 12

Tactical enforcement units raided seven locations across District 12's entertainment quarter early this morning, shutting down what authorities describe as the largest unlicensed cybernetics installation network discovered in the past three years.

## The Operation

The clinics operated behind legitimate fronts -- tattoo parlors, body modification studios, and one karaoke bar -- and offered cybernetic implant installation at roughly 30% of licensed clinic prices. Investigators estimate the network served over 2,000 clients in its eighteen months of operation.

Equipment seized includes surgical-grade robotic arms, neural interface calibration tools, and crates of implant hardware from at least six different manufacturers. Notably, a significant portion of the implant stock consisted of refurbished units -- devices removed from deceased users and repackaged for resale.

## Health Concerns

The District 12 Health Authority has issued an advisory for anyone who received cybernetic work from unlicensed providers in the area. Improperly installed implants carry risks including:

- Neural rejection syndrome
- Firmware incompatibilities leading to sensory distortion
- Infection from improperly sterilized bio-interface ports
- Long-term degradation of surrounding organic tissue

Free diagnostic screenings are being offered at licensed clinics throughout the district for the remainder of the month.

## The Demand Problem

While authorities frame this as a public safety victory, the bust highlights an uncomfortable reality: licensed cybernetic procedures are financially out of reach for most residents of the lower districts. A basic optical enhancement costs three months of median district wages at official prices.

"You shut down these clinics, people do not stop wanting chrome," said community advocate Lin Zhao. "They just find somewhere more dangerous to get it done. The real problem is that essential augmentation is priced as luxury."

## What Happens Next

Twelve individuals were detained during the raids. Under current statutes, operating an unlicensed cybernetics clinic carries a mandatory minimum sentence of eight years. However, legal observers note that previous cases have resulted in plea agreements, particularly when operators cooperate with investigations into implant supply chains.

The seized equipment and implant stock will be analyzed by the Cybernetics Safety Board over the coming weeks.
