Good documentation is as little documentation as possible.

A manual in the Dieter Rams tradition. Six principles, written without ornament, indexed without ceremony. Every rule earns its line. Nothing in this house is decorative.

01

The six principles, in order.

06 entries
01

Good docs are useful.

The first test. If a page does not help a reader do something, the page does not belong in the manual.

5 min
02

Good docs are honest.

They do not overstate capability. They do not hide failure modes. They name what is true now, not what was once true.

6 min
03

Good docs are unobtrusive.

They are tools, not statements. They serve the reader's attention rather than competing for it.

5 min
04

Good docs are long-lasting.

They are written to age well. They survive a rewrite of the underlying system without requiring a rewrite of themselves.

7 min
05

Good docs are thorough.

They do not stop at the first paragraph. They cover the edge case the reader will hit at 02:00.

6 min
06

Good docs are as little as possible.

The final principle, and the hardest. Subtract everything that is not necessary. Subtract again.

4 min
