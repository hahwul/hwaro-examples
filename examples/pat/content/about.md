Pat is a registered patent agent with the United States Patent and Trademark Office, focusing on cutting-edge computer engineering patents. After obtaining a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Engineering, Pat spent five years designing cryptographic accelerators for high-performance servers. This hands-on experience in chip-level implementation provided a solid foundation for transitioning into intellectual property law. Pat specializes in drafting patent specifications that stand up to rigorous inter partes reviews and examiner scrutinies, particularly in fields with complex algorithms and physical side-channel vulnerabilities. The practice is dedicated to helping pioneering research groups, startups, and established enterprises protect their proprietary security enclaves and cryptographic pipelines. In addition to drafting original utility applications, Pat conducts comprehensive patentability analyses and state-of-the-art searches. By bridging the gap between hardware engineering and patent law, Pat translates low-level RTL structures and high-level cryptographic protocols into precise claim language. The office operates out of San Francisco, California, providing specialized services to the global security community.
