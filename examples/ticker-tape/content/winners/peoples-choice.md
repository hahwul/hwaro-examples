## THE CITATION

*Read from the reviewing stand at City Hall, 14:15, to a full minute of cheering.*

In August, the Red Hook Fire House received 1,284 calls in six days. The station is normally staffed for about 50 calls in the same window. No member of the crew went home. No member of the crew complained.

### The Work

They ran pumps during the flood. They evacuated the rehab floor of the hospital on the second day. They carried an elderly neighbor down seven flights when the elevator stopped. They coordinated with the Coast Guard when the second wave came in. They did not sleep. They barely ate. They kept going because nobody else was coming.

### The Parade

Twenty-two of them march together, in uniform but with no helmets. Helmets are what they wear when they are working. Today they are not working.

> "We did our jobs. That is what the job is." &mdash; Captain Ruiz, on the microphone, before being drowned out by the applause

### The Citation

*For extraordinary service above the call of duty. For uninterrupted presence when the city most needed it. For a week that no training manual had prepared anyone to survive.*
