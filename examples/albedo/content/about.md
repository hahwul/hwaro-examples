Albedo is a small vexillology studio working a narrow, invented brief: flags for polar research stations that have declared some form of self-governance, usually over something specific — a survey meridian, a freshwater tarn, a stretch of exposed rock. None of the [six micronations catalogued here](@/flags/_index.md) exist. The stations, councils, and disputes they represent are fiction, built to give each flag a reason to look the way it does.

Every plate follows the same drafting standard. A field of no more than three flat colors, laid out on a grid derived from the flag's own hoist-to-fly ratio. One charge, never more — a line, a peak, a disc, a diamond, a circle, a pennant — chosen so it still reads correctly at flagpole distance in poor light, which is the only test that matters for a flag that might actually have to fly outdoors. And a construction sheet precise enough that anyone could redraw the flag from the numbers alone, without ever seeing the source file.

The ratio is drafted first, before any color or charge decision. A flag's proportions are its posture — a narrow 1:2 field reads as a banner unfurled in wind, while a squarer 2:3 field sits still and reads as a badge — and getting that decision wrong is the one mistake no amount of good charge design can fix afterward. Every plate in the catalog states its ratio openly, in the corner, the way a drafting sheet states its scale.

New briefs are considered from any provisional council, real or invented, willing to describe what their station is actually claiming.
