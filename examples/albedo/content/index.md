Albedo drafts flags for polar research stations that have gone their own way — provisional councils, water-rights condominiums, survey teams marking a fixed meridian and deciding the mark deserved a banner. Every plate is built from a flat field, a single restrained charge, and a construction sheet exact enough to redraw the flag from measurements alone.

No renders, no gradients, no photography — only vector geometry and the hoist-to-fly ratio it was drafted to. Six plates are ratified so far, drawn against the studio's own [drafting standard](@/about.md).
