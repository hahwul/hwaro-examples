A nunatak is a ridge or peak that pokes clear through an ice sheet: enough exposed rock to argue jurisdiction over, which is exactly what the founding claim rested on. The commonwealth's charter names the exposed rock itself as the territory, ice included only as the boundary that happens to move.

<!-- more -->

I drew the peak first and built the field around it, rather than the usual order. At 1:2 the fly is twice the hoist, which is an unforgiving ratio for a single centered charge — anything symmetrical reads as a badge stuck in the middle of a banner. Pushing the peak's apex to the golden section of the fly, rather than dead center, was what finally made the flag read as landscape instead of emblem. The thin frost-teal line along the bottom edge is the sea line, kept to two percent of the hoist so it stays a line and never becomes a second stripe.

Basalt indigo was chosen over the studio's usual charcoal because the commonwealth sits close enough to open water that its skies run blue-black rather than true black for most of the flying season — a small allowance for local light that the construction sheet notes but does not generalize into house style. Ratified after two redrafts; the first peak was judged "too tidy" by a council that wanted their rock to look difficult to climb.
