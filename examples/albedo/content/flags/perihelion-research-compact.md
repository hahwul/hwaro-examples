The compact governs a solar observation platform built to track the sun through the weeks it never fully sets — the flag needed a sun on it, and the only interesting design question was where to put the horizon.

<!-- more -->

I set the disc tangent to the horizon line rather than resting on it or floating above it, which took four drafts to get right at this scale. Too high and it reads as a full noon sun, which misrepresents a station whose entire mandate is the low-angle light; too low and the ring charge collapses into a smudge against the field. Tangent gives the flag a held breath, the compact's chair said, which is the best review note this studio has ever been given.

The ring around the disc is not decorative — it is the halo the low sun actually throws through humid polar air, and cutting it would have made the charge into generic clip-art sun. Horizon-grey was chosen over white specifically so the gold reads warm rather than garish; against a pure white field the same gold looked closer to hazard tape than sunlight. The construction sheet holds the disc, ring, and horizon to a strict proportion by radius and offset, so the plate scales cleanly from a lapel pin to a mast flag without redrafting.
