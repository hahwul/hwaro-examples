Tarn Point is the rare station on this coast with standing freshwater year-round: a tarn fed by meltwater that never quite freezes solid, which the two research groups sharing the point built their entire condominium arrangement around water rights to.

<!-- more -->

The diagonal split was the condominium's own idea, arrived at during a shared-governance dispute the studio was not invited to resolve but ended up drawing the flag for anyway: neither party wanted the other's color to dominate the field, so the diagonal gives each roughly half the area without either reading as background to the other's foreground. The circle — the tarn itself — sits in the white field rather than straddling the seam, filled in the same blue as the diagonal so the water on the flag matches the water on the ground regardless of which side of the line you are standing on.

At 1:2 the diagonal reads cleanly even at a shallow viewing angle, which matters here more than most stations: the flagpole stands on a bluff above the tarn, and from the water it is seen edge-on more often than face-on. The teal ring around the circle is the one departure from the two-party palette — a shared color neither research group can claim, which the condominium's charter now cites, only half joking, as a precedent for future disputes.
