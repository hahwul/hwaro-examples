description = "Lattice resonance patterns in the recovered shard."
date = 2023-10-08

The shard recovered from the survey site continues to emit at intervals that resist fit to any known periodic source. Field teams initially flagged the readings as instrument drift. Bench analysis has ruled that out.

The crystal lattice presents a triclinic structure with no observed twinning. Under polarized illumination, the optical axes shift through approximately four degrees over the course of a measurement, which is well outside the tolerance for a stable mineral specimen at room temperature. The motion is not thermal. We have held the sample chamber at 277 K for the duration of the most recent recording and the axis drift continues unchanged.

Spectral output peaks near 0.43 micrometers and again near 1.2 micrometers, with weaker emission across the intervening band. The two peaks are not harmonically related. Of greater interest, the relative intensity between them inverts on a cycle that we have so far been unable to characterize. A first attempt to fit the inversion to a sum of two sinusoids produced residuals an order of magnitude larger than the signal itself.

We are now treating the emission as a structured channel rather than a passive material property. The working assumption is that the shard carries information impressed on it by a process we have not yet identified. Whether that process is internal to the lattice or external and ongoing remains open.

Standard containment procedures remain in effect. The shard is held under a hard vacuum, on a non-conducting mount, with no instrumentation in physical contact. All readings are taken through the chamber window.
