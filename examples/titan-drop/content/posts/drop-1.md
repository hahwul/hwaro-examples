Commencing the first stage of structural deployment. Atmospheric entry vectors look solid. We are monitoring the heat shielding closely as the velocity peaks near the stratosphere. The initial telemetry data confirms that the ablative material is functioning exactly as simulated, dissipating the immense thermal energy generated by atmospheric friction. The guidance computers are constantly adjusting the trajectory, making micro-corrections to maintain the optimal angle of attack. Any deviation at this stage could result in catastrophic structural failure, so redundancy protocols are active across all primary sensor arrays. The communications link remains stable, though we anticipate a brief blackout period due to plasma ionization around the hull. Ground control is standing by to reacquire the signal as soon as the craft clears the upper atmosphere. The tension in the control room is palpable, but the engineering team remains confident in the design. This descent marks the culmination of years of rigorous testing and development.
