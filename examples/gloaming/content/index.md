Gloaming is the hour the bees come home. Every evening from March to October the flight boards of these two hives fill with returning foragers, heavy and slow in the last light, and every season I write down what the colonies did while I was pretending to be in charge. Two hives, one roof, six storeys above a working harbour: **Lark** on the east parapet, which wakes with the sun, and **Vesper** on the west, which forages late and sulks all morning. The log runs season by season — hefting in winter, [swarm arithmetic in spring](/apiary/swarm-logic-on-a-windy-roof/), [the linden fortnight in summer](/apiary/the-linden-fortnight/), mite counts in autumn — and none of it is advice. It is only what happened, written down close to the day it happened, before the roof taught me the next thing. If you want the cast of characters first, start with [the roof itself](/about/).
