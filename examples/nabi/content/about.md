Nabi means butterfly. We picked the name for two reasons, and only the second one is precious: butterflies show up exactly when a season is ready and not a day before, and we liked the idea of a food magazine that refused to run a strawberry feature in November just because the calendar said "spring issue."

So Nabi runs quarterly, more or less, but the quarters follow the markets, not the printer's schedule. An issue ships when the greens are actually in, or the persimmons are actually stringing, or somebody's rooftop jangdok is actually three years old and ready to talk about. If a season runs long, so does the wait. Readers who want punctual can find another magazine.

## What we cover

Every feature comes from time spent standing next to someone working — a market vendor before the shutters are up, a fermenter checking a jar they've sworn not to check, a noodle counter mid-July rush. We are not a recipe outlet, though recipes show up when the story needs one. We are more interested in the hour before the recipe: the sourcing, the timing, the small superstitions vendors and home cooks have built up over decades of getting it right and occasionally getting it wrong.

Two rubrics repeat across issues. MARKET DAY covers open-air markets, seasonal produce, and the people who sell it. FERMENT covers everything that needs time and a closed lid: doenjang, gochujang, kimchi, and the patience all three demand. A small butterfly mark sits beside every kicker in the magazine — a habit that started as a joke about the name and stuck because readers liked spotting it.

## The masthead

- **Soojin Ha** — market columnist, based in Seoul, has been covering Dongmyo and Gyeongdong markets since 2019.
- **Minho Jang** — fermentation writer, keeps his own jangdok collection and will not stop mentioning it.
- **Yuna Baek** — food culture writer, prefers to report from a counter seat rather than a press table.
- **Areum Cho** — contributing writer covering the southern provinces, currently obsessed with anything dried on a string.
- **Daeho Sim** — community and kimjang beat, lives in the building he writes about.

## Getting in touch

Nabi does not run advertising and is not looking for sponsored features. If you know a market vendor, a fermenter, or a kitchen with a story that fits, write to the editors and describe what makes the timing right — not just the subject, but the season it belongs to. We read everything, slowly, the way we do most things here.
