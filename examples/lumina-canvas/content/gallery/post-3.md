Static Field is a portrait series shot entirely under aging fluorescent fixtures, the kind found in disused office floors and municipal stairwells. The fixtures cycle at the supply frequency, producing a flicker that ordinary shutter speeds either average out or sample arbitrarily. The series uses that sampling deliberately.

Each portrait was captured at one one-hundredth of a second, a shutter duration short enough to land inside a single phase of the lamp cycle. The subjects were asked to hold a fixed gaze for thirty consecutive frames, and the images selected for the series were chosen for the moments when the lamp was at its lowest output. The result is a flesh tone shifted toward green-yellow, deeply unflattering by conventional standards, but consistent with the actual chromatic environment of the room.

The framing is uniformly tight. Each subject occupies the central third of the frame, with the surrounding architecture compressed into negative space by a 135mm lens at full aperture. The narrow depth of field pulls the wall texture into a soft, granular field that gives the series its title. No retouching was applied beyond minor dust removal.

The intention of the work is to document the chromatic conditions of spaces designed not to be looked at, and the people who pass through them.
