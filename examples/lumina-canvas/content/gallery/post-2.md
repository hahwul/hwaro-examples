Spectral Drift is a sequence of seven images shot at the threshold of usable light, where sensors begin to read noise as colour. Each frame was captured at base ISO with extended exposure, then processed through a custom Lab-space curve that exaggerates the chromaticity of the deepest shadows without lifting their luminance.

The work began as a study of dusk along an industrial waterfront. The intent was not to document the location but to record how a single environment fractures into distinct chromatic temperatures as ambient light fades. In the brightest frame the harbour reads as cool grey-blue, dominant wavelength near 470 nanometres. Twenty minutes later the same frame contains a magenta cast no eye saw at the time, an artefact of the sensor responding to residual UV scattered through humid air.

Each image is presented at native 16:9 with no crop. The decision to leave the frames uncropped reflects the working method: composition was committed at capture, and post-production handled only tonal grading and dust removal. The viewer sees the same field the camera saw, with the chromatic bias the sensor introduced left intact.

The series is best read in sequence rather than as individual frames. The drift between adjacent images is the actual subject of the work.
