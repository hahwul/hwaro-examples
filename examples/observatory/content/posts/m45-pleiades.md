The Pleiades (M45) is an open star cluster containing middle-aged, hot B-type stars. It is the most visible cluster to the naked eye and a primary target for wide-field observation.

## Observation Log

- **Equipment:** 80mm Refractor, 40mm Wide-angle eyepiece.
- **Conditions:** Bortle 3, Seeing 3/5, Transparency 4/5.
- **Notes:** The brightest stars (Alcyone, Atlas, Electra, Maia, Merope, Taygeta, Pleione) showed a brilliant blue-white hue. Subtle reflection nebulosity around Merope was faintly visible during the peak of the observation.

This cluster is relatively young, estimated at 100 million years. Its motion through the interstellar medium is bringing it into contact with a dust cloud, resulting in the reflection nebulosity we see today.
