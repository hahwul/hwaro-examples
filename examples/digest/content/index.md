Every finding that runs on this page is filed the way a lab notebook logs a specimen, not the way a newsroom ranks a story. Fig. 1 is simply the plate most recently entered into the log — the newest measurement on the bench — not a judgment about which finding matters more than the others sitting beside it.

Each plate carries the same four things: a discipline kicker in the corner (MATERIALS, MICROBIOLOGY, and their near neighbors), a one-line caption pulled straight from the paper's own claim, a staff byline, and a body written without the paper's hedging or its jargon, though never without its caveats. If a result only held up in a lab flow model and not yet in a living body, that gets said plainly, in the brief itself, not buried in a footnote.

New plates are filed as the underlying papers clear our own read-through — usually within a week of publication, sometimes longer if a method section needs a second staff writer to check it. Read [how filing actually works](about/) for the full version of that process.
