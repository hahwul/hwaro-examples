The Digest exists to do one narrow job well: take a new finding in materials science or microbiology and file it as a short, honest brief that a reader without a subject background can actually use. We do not cover every paper that crosses our desk, and we do not cover a paper the week it appears in preprint — we wait for a version we can read closely, check the methods section of, and where possible ask the authors a clarifying question about before we file anything.

## The plate convention

Every brief is labeled Fig. 1 through Fig. 7 on the front page, in the order it was filed, the way a lab notebook numbers its plates rather than the way an editor might rank a lead story. The number is a catalog reference, not a rating. A discipline kicker in the corner — MATERIALS, MICROBIOLOGY, POLYMERS, CERAMICS, VIROLOGY, and the other near neighbors of those two core beats — tells you the field at a glance. Tags underneath each brief go finer-grained, down to the specific method or system involved.

## Corrections and caveats

If a finding turns out to be wrong, retracted, or meaningfully revised by later work, we file a correction on the original plate rather than deleting it. Every institute, journal, and researcher named on this site is fictional; no real study, real person, or real publication is being described or implied.

## Staff

Current bylines include Priya Nakamura-Voss and Desmond Achebe on the materials and microbiology beats respectively, with Liesel Marchetti and Oscar Tran covering both as findings warrant. Tips and corrections can be filed the same way a brief is: plainly, with a source attached.
