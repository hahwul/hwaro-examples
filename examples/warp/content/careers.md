Warp does not have salaried openings, and it probably never will. Three people means every decision gets made by someone who actually understands the whole cabinet — the code, the art, and the wiring — and we have watched that clarity disappear at other studios the moment headcount climbs into double digits. If that changes, this page will say so first. It hasn't changed in six years.

What we do have is contract work, twice over.

## Contract sound design, per game

Priya composes the score, but a full game needs more foley and interface sound than one person composing on FM hardware can reasonably cover in a shipping window. We bring in a contract sound designer for two to four weeks per title, paid a flat day rate, credited on the cabinet's attract screen by name. You should already own the strange half of your sample library — the part with contact-mic recordings of vending machines and subway brakes — because that is closer to what NOVA WAKE needs than a stock UI-blip pack.

## Playtest weekend crew, seasonal

A few times a year, usually before a festival showing, we run playtest weekends at Satellite Skate and pay a day rate for structured feedback: timed sessions, a short written report, and honesty about the part that made you want to walk away from the cabinet. No arcade experience required. Strong opinions about difficulty curves are worth more to us than a resume.

## How to apply

Send three sentences about a cabinet or console game you'd defend to the death to careers@warp.game, with the role you're interested in in the subject line. We don't want a resume — we want to know you can talk about why a game feels the way it feels. Three people read every reply personally, so give us a few days.
