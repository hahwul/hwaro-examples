We are three people, and we have a rule about staying that way: if a fourth desk won't fit past the arcade cabinet blocking the workshop doorway, we're still the right size. It's a joke that has held up for six years, mostly because none of us wants to spend more time managing people than making games.

"Retro-cosmic" is a description we accept but didn't choose for ourselves — we'd rather call it honest. Every visual choice on a Warp cabinet traces back to a hardware reason, not a nostalgia trend: the scanlines exist because Cathode genuinely redraws at a fixed rate, the palette is narrow because the cabinet's amplifier only has so much headroom before the CRT bleeds color into itself. The cosmic settings aren't a coat of neon paint over a normal game, either — Mireille has been reading actual orbital mechanics textbooks since before the studio existed, and it shows up in how debris fields behave and why a basketball hoop can drift.

We still operate out of Satellite Skate. The rink's old sound system runs our test cabinets on Thursday playtest nights, and its regulars have been our harshest and most reliable QA department since 2019.
