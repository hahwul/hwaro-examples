## Formal Truth
Structural integrity within the refined environment of logic.

## Premises and Inference
An axiom is not a conclusion; it is the agreement that lets a conclusion be reached. The reference begins with a small set of stated premises, each one isolated and minimal, and traces the inferences that follow from their combination. When two axioms disagree, the disagreement is named rather than resolved.

## Notation and Convention
Symbols carry meaning only by agreement. Every operator used in this reference is defined on the page where it first appears, and the definitions are gathered in an appendix so that no proof depends on memory. Conventions that vary between sources, such as the binding precedence of negation, are made explicit in the margin.

## Working Through a Proof
A reader should be able to verify each step without consulting outside material. Lemmas are stated in full before they are invoked, and any appeal to a prior result names the section and line. The aim is not brevity but durability: a proof that still reads cleanly when the subject is unfamiliar will continue to read cleanly years later.
