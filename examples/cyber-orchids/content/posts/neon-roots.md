Beneath the glowing surface of the Cyber Orchid lies a complex network of neon roots. These aren't physical wires, but pathways of highly optimized data transmission that sustain the digital organism.

To understand the bloom, we must first trace the roots.

### Translucent Pathways

The roots function using a principle similar to glassmorphism. They are translucent, allowing data to be visible as it flows, yet they maintain a distinct structure.

They utilize a `backdrop-filter` effect, softening the harshness of the raw data below while reflecting the vibrant colors of the network above.

> "The root system is not just for sustenance; it is the foundation of the network's aesthetic." - Lead Digital Botanist

By studying these neon pathways, we hope to replicate their efficiency and beauty in future network designs.
