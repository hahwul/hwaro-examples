The specimens gathered here are not a survey. Each essay picks one revival — a typeface pulled back from a worn proof, a set of damaged punches, or a single surviving book — and asks what the people who redrew it kept, what they discarded, and what they quietly invented in the gap between the two. Essays are filed under two kickers: **REVIVAL**, for a historic face redrawn for a later century, and **FOUNDRY**, for the working practice of the house that cut it first. Read in either order; each stands alone.
