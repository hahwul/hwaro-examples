Minimalism has been a dominant trend in UI design for years, characterized by clean lines, ample whitespace, and a focus on essential elements. But as technology evolves and user expectations change, what does the future hold for minimalist UI? In this article, we examine the evolution of minimalism, from its early days to the current "neo-brutalist" and "bento" variations. We discuss how designers are finding new ways to inject personality and visual interest into minimalist interfaces without sacrificing clarity or usability. We also predict upcoming trends that will shape the future of minimalist web design.
