The bento grid design has taken the web by storm, offering a clean and structured way to present information. Inspired by the traditional Japanese bento box, this layout style divides content into distinct, often rounded, compartments. This approach not only looks modern and aesthetically pleasing but also improves usability by clearly separating different pieces of information. In this post, we explore the origins of the bento grid, its key characteristics, and how you can implement it in your own projects using CSS Grid. We'll also look at some stunning examples of bento grids in the wild.
