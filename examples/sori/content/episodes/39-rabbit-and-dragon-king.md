The Dragon King under Ojak crossing has fallen sick, and the one cure his physicians can name is a rabbit's liver, fresh, still warm. A turtle volunteers to fetch one, flatters a rabbit on the riverbank with talk of a palace made of coral and appointments to be had at court, and carries it down through the water on his own back before the rabbit understands what the invitation actually was.

<!-- more -->

By the time the palace guards explain the arrangement, the rabbit is already standing in the throne room with no way back to the surface on its own. So it does the only thing left available to a small animal cornered by a king: it lies, calmly and specifically. Rabbits, it explains, keep their livers outside their bodies on land, airing them out on rocks and hanging them from branches, precisely so they never spoil — a sensible precaution, surely His Majesty's physicians would agree. It only forgot to bring this one along, what terrible luck, but it would be happy to swim back up and fetch it, if someone could carry it.

Grandfather Yeo, a former ferryman who has crossed this exact stretch of river more times than he can count, tells the getaway in hwimori — the fastest jangdan there is, four tight beats compressed almost on top of each other, the pattern a drummer reaches for when a pansori scene needs to feel like it is outrunning itself.

> Down to the palace, up from the palace, the same water either way —
> *only one of us, going up, still has both eyes open.*

He grins through the whole final verse. He has clearly gotten away with something himself, at some point, and has never quite let go of how it felt.
