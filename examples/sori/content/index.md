Every tale on this show arrived first as a half-remembered tune — a line an elder hummed while shelling beans, a chant a grandmother used to still a crying child, a work-song sung in rhythm with a hoe. Before Sori was a podcast it was a notebook: two years of Sunday visits to hill villages by our collector, Yun Areum, filling pages with lyrics nobody had written down.

<!-- more -->

Each episode pairs one story with the song that used to travel inside it. Our sound artist, Baek Ho-yeol, keeps a janggu and a battered gayageum in the shed behind the community hall and rebuilds each melody from what elders remember, one verse at a time, until it is close enough to sing.
