Relays are electrically operated switches that are fundamental to modern electrical systems. They consist of an electromagnet, an armature, a spring, and a set of electrical contacts. When current flows through the electromagnet, it attracts the armature, causing the contacts to close or open. This allows a low-power circuit to control a high-power circuit safely and efficiently. Relays are essential in automation, automotive systems, and industrial control panels where isolation between control and load circuits is necessary. Always ensure the relay is rated for the correct voltage and current to prevent overheating or failure. In complex wiring diagrams, relays are often denoted by a coil symbol and one or more switch symbols. Understanding how to read these symbols is the first step in troubleshooting any relay-based circuit.
