## Mission Overview

Operation designation **REDWORLD-2** marks the transition of Mars IV-B from initial survey operations into active atmospheric reconstruction. Phase 2 focuses on large-scale nitrogen injection via orbital dispersal platforms and expanded solar mirror arrays to drive surface temperature increases.

## Objectives

- Deploy 12 additional ATMOS-INJECT satellites at 180 km orbital altitude
- Increase atmospheric pressure from 0.61 atm to target 0.84 atm within 24-month window
- Establish northern polar ice melt cascade via focused mirror array sector B
- Commission three deep-bore volcanic activator stations at Olympus Mons base

## Risk Assessment

**THERMAL RUNAWAY — LOW.** Current CO2 concentrations remain manageable. Mirror array output is calibrated to avoid runaway greenhouse escalation. Continued monitoring required as N2 levels increase.

**DUST STORM INTERFERENCE — MODERATE.** Seasonal dust storm cycles in the Hellas Basin region may disrupt ATMOS-INJECT-02 and ATMOS-INJECT-03 orbital windows. Contingency thruster reserves are pre-loaded.

**WATER DELIVERY — ON SCHEDULE.** CRYO-COMET-03 impact is confirmed in 6 days, delivering approximately 2.4 billion tonnes of H2O to the northern basin target zone.

## Next Milestone

Phase 3 initiation is contingent on achieving 1.0 atm baseline pressure. Projected date: **Year 52, Day 180**. Phase 3 will commence biosphere seeding operations using engineered cyanobacteria strains optimized for high-CO2, low-temperature environments.
