Moire is interference made visible. Lay one fine grid of straight black lines over a second identical grid, rotate the top one by a degree or two, and a slow new pattern blooms across the overlap — dark knots where the lines collide, bright channels where they part. Neither layer contains that pattern. It only exists in the eye.

For the decor card we render two SVG line fields, both pure solid strokes, and rotate the upper one by a fraction of a turn. The third pattern appears for free.

## why it belongs on a dashboard

- It is the cheapest possible illustration of **constructive and destructive interference**.
- It demonstrates **amplitude** without a single curve: the knots are crests, the channels are troughs.
- It stays readable because we cap it inside a bordered panel with a solid caption bar.

> Two ordered things, slightly misaligned, become a disordered third. Perception is the misalignment.

We let the top layer drift a couple of pixels and a couple of degrees, very slowly. The knots crawl. Under reduced motion the layers lock and the moire holds a single frozen frame — still legible, still striking.
