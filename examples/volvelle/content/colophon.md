## About this publication

This publication was composed using Hwaro, a static site generator. Its design draws on the visual traditions of the medieval manuscript, with concentric ring imagery and decorated capitals evoking the volvelle instruments that are its subject.

## Typography

**Display faces.** Chapter titles and headings are set in Cinzel, a typeface inspired by Roman inscriptional lettering, and Cormorant SC (small capitals), a face with the organic weight variation characteristic of calligraphic manuscript hands. Together they evoke the decorated capitals and formal titling of illuminated manuscripts.

**Body text.** The running text is set in Crimson Pro, a humanist serif face designed for sustained reading, with EB Garamond as a fallback. Both faces descend from the typographic tradition of the Renaissance, when the first printed volvelles were produced. Their open letterforms, moderate contrast, and generous proportions support extended reading in the manner of a scholarly codex.

## Visual design

SVG rotating volvelle disc diagrams with layered information rings are the primary decorative and illustrative elements. These diagrams depict concentric data rings in the style of medieval astronomical instruments, with zodiac signs, month names, and labeled divisions rendered in the display typefaces. SVG astronomical calculation wheel patterns appear as data display elements.

The color palette (warm parchment, gold, umber) evokes the materials of the medieval scriptorium. No CSS gradients are used. All decorative elements are inline SVG.
