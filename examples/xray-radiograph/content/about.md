## The Light-Box

Every radiologist knows the ritual. You clip the film to the illuminated panel, the room dims, and suddenly the ordinary — bone, tissue, the dense architecture of a living body — becomes visible in a new register. Not flesh but structure. Not surface but depth.

This blog is that light-box.

I write about design, writing, and software — not their appearances, but their *underlying geometry*. The load-bearing decisions most readers never notice. The constraints that shaped the form. The invisible scaffolding that makes a thing hold together or quietly fail.

## What Gets Examined Here

- **Visual structure** — why certain layouts feel trustworthy and others make you flinch without knowing why
- **Writing mechanics** — sentence rhythm, paragraph density, the physics of attention
- **Software architecture** — the choices baked into systems before a single user arrives
- **Reading practice** — how to look at a finished thing and reconstruct the decisions that produced it

---

## Technical Details

This site is built with [Hwaro](https://github.com/hahwul/hwaro). The design concept is a medical X-ray film on an illuminated viewing light-box: deep blue-black film, bone-white luminous text, clinical monospace labels, and glowing box-shadows that suggest backlit panels rather than decorative effects.

No Google Fonts. No external dependencies beyond a client-side search library. The aesthetic is intentional and the constraints are load-bearing.
