Sfumato -- from the Italian *sfumare*, meaning "to vanish" or "to evaporate like smoke."

This is a theme built on the principle that not everything needs to be sharply defined. In painting, Leonardo da Vinci developed sfumato as a technique of layering thin, translucent glazes to soften transitions between tones. There are no hard edges; forms emerge from shadow as if materializing from mist.

## The Design

This theme translates that philosophy into digital space. Rather than sharp borders and loud contrasts, it favors:

- **Layered shadows** that create depth without relying on color blending
- **Muted, warm tones** drawn from the palette of Renaissance underpaintings
- **Restrained typography** that prioritizes readability and quiet elegance
- **Negative space** that allows content to breathe

## The Constraints

No gradients. No decoration for its own sake. Every shadow, every spacing decision, and every color choice serves the same purpose: to let the content emerge softly from the dark.

> "The most praiseworthy form of painting is the one that most resembles what it imitates."

The design is deliberately minimal. There is no sidebar, no widget, no distraction. Just words, shadows, and the space between them.
