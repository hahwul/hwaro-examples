This edition sets Flyleaf complete and in reading order, across six chapters. The text follows the author's final revision, prepared the year the Salt Line itself was struck from the timetable it once kept. Chapter openers carry the folio of the original hand-bound proof, kept for readers who like to know where a page would have fallen in paper.

Search above to jump to a name, a station, or a year without leaving the title page. The [Contents](@/chapters/_index.md) below is the whole book at a glance; the chapters chain forward and back from wherever you begin.

Flyleaf was first set by a small university press that also received, over eleven years, the crates the novella describes. No claim is made that the two archives are the same archive. The publisher asks only that readers of this edition treat both — the paper one and the invented one — with the same patience: turn the leaf, hold it to the light, and see what was pressed there before anyone thought to look.
