## I

The Registry does not so much burn as smoke for two days and then stop existing, which is, Anya thinks standing across the street with the rest of the staff, precisely the outcome the Directorate would have chosen if arson were a line item you could requisition through normal channels. The official notice cites a fault in the third-floor wiring, installed, coincidentally, the same decade as the reading room's fluorescent tubes that never finished warming up. Nobody on the pavement says the word arson. Several people, Anya included, are careful not to look at each other for slightly too long.

<!-- more -->

What she understands within the hour, faster than the grief, is arithmetic. Whatever she failed to copy — through illness, through a night she went home early, through the ordinary attrition of forty thousand records handled by one tired woman with a lamp — no longer exists anywhere at all, not in corrected form, not in true form, not anywhere except the version the Directorate will now write from memory and call restoration. The crates she has already shipped north are not a supplement to the Registry's archive. As of this morning, mostly, they are the archive.

## II

Two colleagues are "reassigned to provincial stations" within the week, which is the phrase used for people the Directorate wants elsewhere and watched. Anya is not among them, a fact that frightens her more than being named would have, because it suggests either that she has been entirely overlooked or that she is being allowed to keep working precisely so that whatever she is doing can eventually be traced to its far end. She writes to [Dr. Şoim](@/chapters/the-flyleaf-trade.md) that week for the first time in her own name rather than Pavol's old code, a small, deliberate risk, to ask a single question: how much of the last eleven years has actually arrived. The answer, when it comes, is not the number she was hoping for. She decides, reading it twice at her kitchen table, that the only response available to her is to carry more, not less, and to stop waiting for a safer month that the last four weeks have made clear is never coming.
