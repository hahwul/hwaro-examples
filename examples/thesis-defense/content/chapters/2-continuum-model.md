This chapter develops the theoretical framework for understanding the electronic structure of twisted bilayer graphene. We begin with the Bistritzer-MacDonald continuum model and derive the conditions under which flat bands emerge at the magic angle.

## The Bistritzer-MacDonald Model

When two graphene layers are stacked with a small twist angle theta, the resulting moire superlattice has a period L = a/(2 sin(theta/2)), where a = 0.246 nm is the graphene lattice constant. For theta approximately equal to 1.1 degrees, L is approximately 13 nm, containing roughly 11,000 carbon atoms per unit cell.

The continuum model replaces this enormous atomistic problem with an effective low-energy Hamiltonian that operates on the Dirac cones of the two layers, coupled by interlayer tunneling that varies spatially with the moire periodicity.

## Flat-Band Formation

The central result is that at a sequence of magic angles (theta_1 approximately equal to 1.08 degrees, theta_2 approximately equal to 0.5 degrees, ...), the Dirac velocity of the moire bands vanishes. The bandwidth at the first magic angle is reduced from the bare graphene bandwidth of approximately 8 eV to less than 10 meV -- a flattening ratio exceeding 800:1.

This extreme band flattening amplifies the ratio of Coulomb interaction energy U to kinetic energy W, pushing the system into the strongly correlated regime (U/W >> 1) even though the underlying material is a simple carbon allotrope.

## Topological Properties

We demonstrate that the flat bands carry a nontrivial fragile topology characterized by a Z2 invariant that depends on the valley index. This topology has observable consequences: it protects certain edge states and constrains the possible symmetry-breaking patterns of the correlated insulating states (Chapter III).

The Berry curvature distribution within the moire Brillouin zone is computed numerically, revealing concentration at the high-symmetry points (Gamma_M, K_M, K_M') with a pattern that reflects the C3 rotational symmetry of the moire lattice.
