### OBSERVATION DATA

The current capture documents emission lines from neutral carbon (C I) and singly ionised carbon (C II) in a low-density discharge cell. The Swan band system, normally associated with diatomic carbon (C2), is suppressed under the present excitation regime, leaving the atomic transitions cleanly resolved.

#### SPECTRAL PEAKS
1. **C II:** 658.3 nm (resonance doublet, 2s2p2 to 2s23p)
2. **C I:** 538.0 nm (triplet, 2p3s to 2p4p)
3. **C I:** 477.2 nm (intercombination)
4. **C II:** 426.7 nm (4f to 3d, faint)

<div class="spectral-divider"></div>

Line ratios indicate an electron temperature near 9,400 K. Comparison with reference catalog DB-C12 yields a correlation index of 98.4%. Background continuum has been subtracted using the rolling-median filter described in the methods note. No anomalous broadening is recorded; instrumental width remains the dominant contribution at 0.04 nm FWHM.

The sample is consistent with carbon-rich circumstellar dust shells observed in late-stage AGB stars. Recalibration against the on-board hollow cathode lamp confirmed wavelength accuracy to within 0.01 nm across the visible band.
