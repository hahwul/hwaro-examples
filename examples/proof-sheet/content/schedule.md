## What Happens In Each Proof

### First Proof

The first proof is a clean setting of the manuscript, front to back, with no corrections yet made. It is the most error-rich version of the book. The author and the editor read it separately, mark corrections independently, and then meet (in person or by post) to resolve any disagreements. The first proof is the only proof in which large structural changes are still possible: moving chapters, cutting sections, inserting new material.

At this stage a change costs only the compositor's resetting time. After this stage it begins to cost plates, and after the third stage it costs reprints.

</div>

### Second Proof

The second proof is the first proof with all corrections applied. It is much cleaner, and most of the errors in it are new errors introduced while correcting the old ones &mdash; what the trade calls "second-proof errors." The author should read it against the first proof, not against the manuscript. Structural changes are discouraged at this stage; only textual corrections.

### Third Proof

The third proof is the book, give or take a dozen lingering commas. The author reads it once for sense and once backward for spelling, which is the oldest trick in the trade for catching the last errors. The third proof is returned with the author's signature on the final page, below which the compositor stamps "FOR PRESS" in red.

## If A Proof Is Late

<div class="proof-margin">
A late first proof is a recoverable event. A late third proof is a miracle; it means the book will not make its autumn slot at the bookseller.
</div>

A proof that comes back a day late costs us a day of the compositor's time and is recoverable. A proof that comes back a week late costs us a week, and the second proof is delayed accordingly. A proof that comes back a month late is, effectively, a new book; we reset from scratch, because we will have lost the working galley and reassembled it badly.

The binder has been booked for 22 June. The jacket printer has been booked for the same week. If we miss either booking, the book moves from autumn to Christmas. If we miss Christmas, it moves to spring. If we miss spring, the book does not happen this year. Please return the proofs on time. We will do the same.
