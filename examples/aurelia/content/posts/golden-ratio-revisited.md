The golden ratio shows up in design talks, logo redesigns, and grid systems. Most of the appearances are decorative. The ratio has a small number of genuine uses; the rest is mysticism.

## What 1.618 Is Good For

- **Modular type scales.** Headlines step up by ~1.618× from body size. The progression reads as proportional without feeling mechanical.
- **Layout proportions.** Splitting a page into a 38/62 column layout is more interesting than 50/50 and less arbitrary than 33/67.
- **Image crops.** A rectangle in golden proportion (e.g., 1000×618) frames almost any subject pleasantly.

## What It Is Not Good For

- Logo construction. Building a mark by inscribing it in a Fibonacci spiral is mostly post-hoc rationalization.
- Color theory. The ratio has no meaningful application to hue or saturation distance.
- Animation timing. Easing curves are governed by perception of motion, not by static proportions.

The ratio is a useful default for two or three decisions. Treat it as a starting point, not a hidden truth.
