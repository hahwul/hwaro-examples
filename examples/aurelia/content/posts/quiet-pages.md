The most useful pages I have built are the ones nobody notices. They open quickly, present the writing in a single legible column, and step out of the way.

A quiet page resists temptation. No animated table of contents that follows the scroll. No accent bars that pulse on hover. No "estimated reading time" pinned to the corner. Each of those features is defensible in isolation; together, they shout over the writing.

## A Working Checklist

- One column, 60–75 characters wide.
- Typography in a steady reading face. No display font for body text.
- Three font sizes maximum: title, body, caption.
- Colored accents only on links; never on punctuation, never on margins.
- No animation that triggers on scroll.

This is not minimalism for its own sake. The point is that the writing should be the most interesting thing on the page. Anything else is a competitor.
