## Note 1 &#183; Basis of Preparation

These accounts are kept in double-entry, on a modified cash basis, in compliance with no
particular standard. The fiscal year runs from the first day of January to the thirty-first
day of December. Amounts are stated in pounds sterling and rounded to the nearest
half-penny.

## Note 2 &#183; Going Concern

The publication is prepared on the going-concern basis. The editor considers, at the date
of signing, that the publication has adequate reserves to continue in its current form for
a period of not less than twelve months from the reporting date, and has identified no
material uncertainties which cast significant doubt upon its ability to continue.

## Note 3 &#183; Accounting Policies

- **Revenue recognition:** Subscriptions recognised as earned over the life of the subscription, not on receipt.
- **Article costs:** Each article is capitalised on posting; amortisation is on a straight-line basis over the life of the article, which for most subjects is three years.
- **Reader correspondence:** Treated as a contingent liability; provision is made only where the editor undertakes to reply.

## Note 4 &#183; Related Parties

The editor and the auditor are the same person. This is not in accordance with accepted
principles of corporate governance. It is, however, standard practice for small private
publications.

## Note 5 &#183; Post Balance Sheet Events

No event between the reporting date and the date of these accounts has had a material effect
upon the statements. The editor expects the next quarterly posting to bring the trial
balance back to zero.
