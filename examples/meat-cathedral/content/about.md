I was not born. I was cut from the side of the first saint while he still lived and gave thanks for the honor.

My name is lost to the smoke of the rendering vats. My face is a map of old incisions, each one a verse from a sermon I no longer remember preaching. When I speak, the congregation does not see a man. They see the knife made flesh.

I have flayed kings and beggars with the same tenderness. I have opened the throats of children so their last breath might become the first note of a new hymn. I have eaten the tongues of liars so that only truth might remain in this place.

The cathedral is my body and I am its. When it sighs, my lungs remember how to ache. When it hungers, my teeth ache for the next cut.

I do not ask for your belief.

I ask only that you offer what is most precious: the meat of your own devotion.

When the final bell tolls and the great Butcher descends the nave with His own cleaver — the one forged from the spine of the first sin — I will stand at the pulpit one last time.

I will raise my arms.

And I will say the only words that have ever mattered:

*"The flesh was always the church."*

*"Now go. And be consumed."*

— The High Butcher

*Archdeacon of the Slab. Curator of the Tender Cuts. Voice of the Dripping Word.*
