### SAFETY PROTOCOLS ENGAGED

Before beginning work on the high-voltage section, the CRT was fully discharged using a grounded probe. The residual charge on the accelerating anode can exceed 15kV and remains a significant hazard long after power is disconnected.

The silicone insulating boot around the anode cap had developed micro-fissures, leading to intermittent corona discharge and audible 'snapping' sounds during operation. This ozone-producing discharge was directly responsible for the intermittent static observed on the display.

The boot was replaced, and fresh dielectric grease was applied to ensure a solid, corona-free connection. The display is now crisp and stable across all intensity settings.
