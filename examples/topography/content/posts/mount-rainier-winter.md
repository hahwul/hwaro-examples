Mount Rainier's imposing silhouette dominates the Cascade Range skyline. Our winter expedition via the Disappointment Cleaver route tested our limits and rewarded us with breathtaking views from the summit.

<!-- more -->

## Route Overview

The Disappointment Cleaver route is one of the most popular climbing routes on Mount Rainier, offering a direct line to the summit while avoiding some of the more heavily glaciated areas. In winter, the route becomes significantly more challenging due to snow coverage and avalanche risk.

### Key Statistics

- **Starting Elevation**: Paradise (1,645m)
- **Summit Elevation**: 4,392m
- **Total Elevation Gain**: 2,747m
- **Round Trip Distance**: 14.5 km
- **Duration**: 2 days

## Day 1: Paradise to Camp Muir

We began our ascent from Paradise in the early morning hours. The trail through the lower snowfields was well-packed from previous parties, allowing us to make good time. As we gained elevation, the views of the surrounding peaks began to emerge.

The climb to Camp Muir involves navigating through:
- Panorama Point
- Pebble Creek
- The Muir Snowfield

Weather conditions were excellent with clear skies and minimal wind. We reached Camp Muir (3,108m) by early afternoon, giving us time to rest and prepare for the summit push.

## Day 2: Summit Push

We departed Camp Muir at 1:00 AM, headlamps cutting through the darkness. The route follows:

1. Cowlitz Glacier
2. Cathedral Gap
3. Ingraham Flats
4. Disappointment Cleaver
5. Summit crater

The most technical section came on the Disappointment Cleaver itself, where we encountered exposed rock and steep snow slopes. Proper rope team techniques and careful crampon placement were essential.

## Summit Success

We reached the summit at 7:30 AM, just as the sun was rising over the eastern horizon. The 360-degree views were spectacular, with Mount Adams, Mount Hood, and even Mount Baker visible in the distance.

The crater rim was surprisingly exposed to wind, but the Columbia Crest summit itself provided some shelter. After spending 30 minutes at the top, we began our descent.

## Lessons Learned

- **Acclimatization is crucial**: Even though Rainier isn't exceptionally high, the rapid elevation gain can cause altitude issues
- **Weather windows are short**: Winter conditions can change rapidly
- **Team communication**: Clear communication with rope partners is essential on technical terrain
- **Equipment maintenance**: Keeping gear free of ice buildup requires constant attention

This climb reinforced the importance of proper preparation, physical fitness, and respect for the mountain environment.

## Gear Notes

Essential equipment for this route included:
- Mountaineering boots with integrated gaiters
- 12-point crampons
- Ice axe and backup tool
- Climbing harness with glacier travel gear
- Four-season tent rated for alpine conditions
- -20°C sleeping bag

Would highly recommend this route for intermediate to advanced mountaineers looking to test their skills in a winter alpine environment.
