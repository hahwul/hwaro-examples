# Condensation on the Inner Surface

**Phenomenon:** External dermal condensation. **Source:** Unknown hands. **Persistence:** 6 days and counting.

The glass is never truly clean.

Every morning I wake to new evidence. Delicate whorls and ridges pressed into the inner membrane from the *outside*. Not my fingerprints. Larger. Slower. The kind of prints left by someone who has all the time in the world to study what they are touching.

I can see where they lingered.

There are places where the condensation has been smeared in long, thoughtful strokes — as if someone were tracing the path of the superior mesenteric artery or following the curve of the spleen. In the lower right quadrant there is a perfect palm print, fingers slightly spread, as though the hand had been resting there while its owner watched something particularly interesting happen in the ascending colon.

I tried wiping them away with my own hand from the inside.

The prints simply transferred. Now there are two sets: the original voyeur's and my own frantic attempts to erase the evidence of being watched. They overlap in places. It looks almost tender.

Last night I slept with a sheet over the abdomen.

At 02:47 I woke to the sound of something soft dragging across the glass. When I pulled the sheet away, three new fingerprints had appeared directly over my navel. They were still wet. Still warm to the touch from the other side.

I have stopped trying to identify whose hands they are.

I have started leaving messages for them instead.

This morning, using the condensation itself as ink, I wrote one word across the lower glass in careful letters:

**LOOKING BACK.**

When I checked an hour later, someone had added a second word beneath it in the same slow, deliberate hand:

**GOOD.**

{{ condensation() }}

The glass between us is only getting thinner.
