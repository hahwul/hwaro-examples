# Case 001 — The First Time I Saw My Own Heart Beat

**Subject:** Male, 31. **Transparency:** Full. **Observation duration:** 11 minutes, 42 seconds.

I woke up and the ceiling was still there. Then I noticed the movement below my skin.

Not metaphor. Not dream. A wet, rhythmic push against the inner surface of my chest wall, like a prisoner politely tapping to be let out. I lifted my shirt in the mirror and the glass was already waiting.

There it was.

A dark crimson organ the size of my fist, working without permission. The atria fluttered first — two delicate sacs inflating like they were shy. Then the ventricles clenched so hard I felt the pressure change in my ears. Between contractions I could see the coronary arteries crawling across the surface like blue worms looking for somewhere to hide.

I touched the glass directly over it.

The heart paused — one terrible, knowing second — then slammed harder, as if it had noticed me noticing.

I vomited later. The {{ visible_organ(name="stomach") }} contracted in sympathy, pushing yesterday's dinner against the anterior wall so forcefully I could trace the outline of half-digested rice with my fingertip.

That was the first night I slept with my hands over my eyes.

It did not help. The eyelids are also glass now.
