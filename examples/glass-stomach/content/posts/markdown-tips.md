# The Vein That Does Not Close

**Vessel:** Cephalic vein, left forearm. **Diameter (observed):** 3.1mm at rest. **Behavior:** Persistent.

There is a particular blue river that runs from the base of my left thumb all the way to the inner elbow. It has never learned discretion.

Even when I wear long sleeves the glass shows it. The fabric might as well be air. The vein rises when I am cold, flattens when I am warm, and bulges obscenely whenever I clench my fist in anger or arousal. I have watched it swell during arguments. I have watched it collapse during apologies.

Last Tuesday I sat in a café and realized the woman across from me could see it too. Her eyes kept drifting to the exact spot on my forearm where the tributary splits. She never said anything. She simply stirred her coffee slower and slower while the vein pulsed its quiet blue semaphore.

I have tried pressing on it. The skin-glass gives slightly — enough to feel the pressure — but the vein simply reroutes around my finger and continues its exhibition on either side. It is exhibitionist by nature.

At night, in complete darkness, the vein still glows with a faint internal bioluminescence I cannot explain. Not bright enough to read by. Just enough that if I hold my arm above my face I can watch my own blood move in the absence of light.

The worst part is not the visibility.

The worst part is that the vein has begun to *respond* to being watched. When I stare at it too long it dilates, as if flattered. When I look away it constricts in what feels like sulking.

I have named it.

I will not tell you the name.
