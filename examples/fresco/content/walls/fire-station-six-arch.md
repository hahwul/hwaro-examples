The arch above Fire Station Six's apparatus bay is the smallest wall Fresco has taken on in three years, and the one with the loudest sketch sessions to date — every firefighter on the day shift has an opinion about whether the mural should show the trucks, the crew, or neither.

<!-- more -->

## Community sketch sessions

Two sessions ran in April, both on the apron in front of the bay doors, both interrupted at least once by an actual call. The design that survived both sessions drops the trucks entirely: two long, low bird shapes crossing the arch nose to tail, a nod to the swifts that roost under the station's eaves every spring and that the crew has been quietly protecting from pest control for a decade. Firefighters wanted their names somewhere on the wall; the compromise is a narrow band along the base, in a font small enough to require actually walking up to read it.

## Wall survey and permit

Halloway's fire commission owns the building, which put the permit through a different office than any prior Fresco wall — the Municipal Facilities Board rather than the Bureau of Public Walls. The arch itself is precast concrete, patched twice in the last twenty years with a visibly different concrete color; a full skim coat is scheduled before priming to keep those patches from telegraphing through the paint.

## What's next

Scaffold goes up the second week of June, weather depending. This page will update through priming, paint days, and the scaffold strike once the crew is actually on the wall — for now it is two bird shapes on a stack of tracing paper and a color proof pinned in the station's break room.
