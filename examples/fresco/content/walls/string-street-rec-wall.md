The String Street Rec Center has been wrapped in plywood hoarding since a roof repair in 2023 stalled out; the hoarding was never taken down, and it has been tagged and buffed by the parks department on a loop ever since. This wall replaces the loop with something the parks department has agreed, in writing, to stop buffing.

<!-- more -->

## Community sketch sessions

Three sessions, all after-school, all run by the rec center's own program staff rather than Fresco directly — a first for the studio, and one that produced a much rowdier stack of tracing paper than usual. The winning motif came from a twelve-year-old regular: a field of overlapping diamonds sized to the actual floor tiles inside the gym, so the wall reads, from across the street, like the gym turned inside out.

## Design proof and permit

Because the hoarding is technically temporary construction fencing rather than a permanent facade, the permit ran through the parks department's temporary-structures desk instead of the usual public art office — a faster process, but one that requires the mural to be removable without damaging the plywood underneath, since the actual roof repair is tentatively back on for 2027.

## Scaffold and paint days

Scaffold went up on January 7. The panel is broken into eleven bays across three plywood sheets, primed with an exterior acrylic primer rated for repeated freeze-thaw since the wall faces north and holds ice through February. Paint days are running two bays at a time; six of eleven are blocked in as of this update, with the diamond field's edges and the gym-floor numbering saved for the final week.

## Status

On scaffold, week two of an estimated four. Photos of the finished wall and the scaffold-strike dedication will replace this note once the last bay is painted.
