The Ridgewood Branch Library's east gable had been blank brick since the building went up in 1961, forty feet of wall visible from three blocks away and, according to the branch's head librarian, "the first thing anyone sees when they're already late for story time." It is also the wall that made Fresco a civic muralist rather than just a muralist who occasionally worked for the city.

<!-- more -->

## Community sketch sessions

Three Saturday sessions ran on the library's front steps, open to anyone, no sign-up required — the format every Fresco commission since has copied almost exactly. The design that emerged was, in the end, embarrassingly simple: an oversized sun rising over a stack of open books, drawn first by a six-year-old in the first ten minutes of the first session and never meaningfully improved on by the eleven weeks of sketching that followed. Sometimes the first idea is the idea.

## Wall survey and permit

Ridgewood's library board owns the gable but not the surrounding land, which required sign-off from both the library foundation and the Bureau of Public Walls before a permit could be filed — a two-office approval process that became the template for every commission after it, and that Fresco now budgets four months for by default.

## Scaffold and paint days

Fourteen days on scaffold in February, cold enough that the elastomeric primer needed a full extra day to cure between coats. The brick took paint unevenly at first — old mortar joints drank twice the paint that the brick faces did — until the crew started priming joints and faces as two separate passes, a technique now standard on every masonry wall Fresco paints.

## Scaffold day

The scaffold came down on a Tuesday afternoon during regular library hours, with no dedication planned — until the same six-year-old whose sketch became the wall showed up with her whole after-school reading group, and the head librarian produced a folding table and a thermos of cider before anyone had planned to do so. It has been a tradition every wall since.
