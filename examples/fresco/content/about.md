Fresco is the working name of muralist Dahlia Reyes-Okafor. The studio has operated out of a converted storefront in the Dovewell rowhouses since 2016, and has painted on civic property — libraries, transit piers, harbor infrastructure, parks buildings — almost exclusively since 2018, when [a library gable in Ridgewood](/walls/ridgewood-reader/) became the first wall too big to reach without a rented lift.

## The studio

Fresco is a practice of one full-time painter and a rotating crew of two to five, hired per wall from a standing list of local painters, sign-writers, and art students who like scaffolding more than the average person should. Every commission is public in two senses: the wall belongs to the city, and the design is drawn in public, at a folding table, with whoever from the neighborhood wants a marker in their hand.

## How a wall happens

1. **Community sketch session.** Two to three open sessions on-site, usually a Saturday. Anyone can draw, argue about color, or just watch. Fresco leaves with a folder of tracing paper, not a finished design.
2. **Wall survey and permit.** A structural check of the surface, a call to the Bureau of Public Walls for the permit packet, and a color proof mailed to whichever community board has jurisdiction.
3. **Design proof.** A quarter-scale gouache proof, painted flat, pinned up at a second public session so the neighborhood sees the actual colors before a drop of masonry paint goes on the wall.
4. **Scaffold and prime.** Pressure wash, patch, prime with an elastomeric masonry primer built for the substrate — brick, silo steel, precast panel — and tape off anything that isn't getting painted.
5. **Paint days.** Usually eight to twenty days depending on wall size and weather. Fresco blocks in flat color first, largest shapes to smallest, then works back in with edges and a single day of fine line work.
6. **Scaffold strike.** The scaffold comes down, the anti-graffiti clear coat goes on, and the same folding table comes back out for a small dedication — cider in paper cups, a photo with whoever showed up to the very first sketch session.

## Materials

Every wall is painted in an exterior elastomeric masonry coating chosen for whatever substrate it needs to bridge — the flex coat used on brick is not the one used on galvanized silo steel. Fresco keeps a standing palette of about eighteen colors mixed in five-gallon batches so that a mural finished in 2019 can still be touched up in a matching color a decade later. A UV-stable anti-graffiti clear coat goes on every wall in the weeks after scaffold strike; it is reapplied on a five-year cycle at the city's expense, which is written into every commission contract before the first sketch session happens.

## Get in touch

Fresco takes on two to three civic commissions a year, almost always through a city public art office, a library foundation, or a neighborhood association with wall access and a modest budget. Site visits are free; proposals are not rushed.
