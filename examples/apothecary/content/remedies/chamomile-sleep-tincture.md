Chamomile (*Matricaria chamomilla*) is perhaps the most trusted sleep herb in Western herbalism. Combined with passionflower (*Passiflora incarnata*), which calms the racing mind, this tincture addresses both the physical and mental aspects of sleeplessness.

## When to Use

This preparation is best suited for:

- Difficulty falling asleep due to overthinking
- Mild anxiety that surfaces at bedtime
- Restlessness after a long or stressful day
- Children over age 6 (at half the adult dose, with practitioner guidance)

## Notes on Ingredients

Use whole dried chamomile flowers rather than tea bag dust -- the essential oils are better preserved in whole flowers. German chamomile is the standard medicinal variety, though Roman chamomile (*Chamaemelum nobile*) works similarly.

Passionflower can be sourced dried from herbal suppliers. The aerial parts (leaves, stems, and flowers) are used. It has a mild, grassy taste that blends well with chamomile's apple-like sweetness.

## Variations

- Add dried lavender buds (1/2 part) for additional calming effect
- Replace vodka with vegetable glycerine for an alcohol-free version (steep 6--8 weeks)
- For acute anxiety, take the same dose during the day as needed
