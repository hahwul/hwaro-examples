Peppermint (*Mentha piperita*) is the archetypal digestive herb. Its menthol content relaxes the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, easing cramping, bloating, and that uncomfortable fullness after a heavy meal.

## The Blend

This three-herb combination covers the main aspects of digestive discomfort:

- **Peppermint** relaxes spasms and moves trapped gas
- **Fennel** is carminative -- it prevents and expels gas, and has a sweet anise flavour that rounds out the blend
- **Chamomile** soothes inflammation and calms nervous digestion (the kind that acts up when you are stressed)

## How to Use

Drink a cup after meals, or whenever digestive discomfort arises. For chronic digestive issues, drink three cups daily between meals for two weeks, then reassess.

## Preparation Tips

- Always cover your cup while steeping mint tea -- the essential oils are volatile and will escape with the steam
- Crushing the fennel seeds just before use releases their aromatic oils
- Use dried herbs for this blend; fresh mint makes a pleasant drink but the dried leaf is more concentrated

## Cautions

Avoid peppermint if you have gastroesophageal reflux (GERD) -- the muscle-relaxing effect can worsen reflux by relaxing the lower esophageal sphincter. In that case, substitute ginger for peppermint in this blend.
