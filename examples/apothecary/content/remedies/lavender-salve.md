---

This all-purpose healing salve combines two of the gentlest and most effective skin herbs in the Western tradition. Lavender (*Lavandula angustifolia*) is antimicrobial and analgesic, while calendula (*Calendula officinalis*) promotes tissue repair and reduces inflammation.

## Uses

- Minor cuts, scrapes, and abrasions
- Small kitchen burns (after cooling under water)
- Dry, cracked skin on hands and feet
- Chapped lips (use as a lip balm)
- Insect bites and stings
- Diaper rash (the calendula component is especially gentle)

## Making the Infused Oil

The quality of your salve depends entirely on the quality of your infused oil. Two methods:

**Slow infusion (preferred):** Fill a jar with dried herbs, cover with olive oil, and place in a sunny window for 4--6 weeks. Shake every few days. The oil will take on a golden-green colour and a rich herbal scent.

**Quick infusion:** Place herbs and oil in a double boiler or a glass jar set in a pot of water. Heat on the lowest setting for 2--3 hours. The water should barely simmer. This method is faster but produces a slightly less potent oil.

## Beeswax Ratio

The amount of beeswax determines the firmness of your salve:

- **Soft salve** (for tins): 1 part beeswax to 8 parts oil
- **Firm salve** (for tubes): 1 part beeswax to 5 parts oil
- **Lip balm consistency**: 1 part beeswax to 4 parts oil

Test by dripping a small amount onto a cold plate. Adjust as needed before pouring into containers.

## Shelf Life

Stored in a cool, dark place, this salve keeps for about one year. If the oil smells rancid, discard and make a fresh batch.
