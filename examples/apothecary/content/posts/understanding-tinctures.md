A tincture is a concentrated liquid extract of herbs, typically made with alcohol as the solvent. It is one of the most efficient ways to preserve and deliver the active compounds of a plant.

## Why Tinctures

Tinctures have several advantages over teas and infusions:

- **Shelf life** -- a properly made tincture lasts for years
- **Concentration** -- a few drops deliver the equivalent of several cups of tea
- **Portability** -- easy to carry and dose precisely
- **Extraction** -- alcohol dissolves compounds that water alone cannot

## The Folk Method

The simplest approach, and the one most home herbalists use:

1. Fill a clean glass jar halfway with dried herb (or fully with fresh herb, chopped)
2. Pour 80-proof vodka over the herb until it is fully submerged with an inch of liquid above
3. Cap tightly and label with the herb name and date
4. Store in a cool, dark place for four to six weeks, shaking daily
5. Strain through cheesecloth, squeezing well, and bottle in dark dropper bottles

## Menstruum Ratios

For more precise work, herbalists specify the ratio of herb to solvent (the menstruum):

| Preparation | Ratio | Alcohol % |
|-------------|-------|-----------|
| Dried herb, standard | 1:5 | 40--60% |
| Fresh herb, standard | 1:2 | 75--95% |
| Roots and barks | 1:5 | 50--60% |
| Leaves and flowers | 1:5 | 40--50% |

## Dosage

A typical adult dose is 30--60 drops (1--2 dropperfuls) taken in a small amount of water, two to three times daily. Adjust based on the specific herb and your practitioner's guidance.
