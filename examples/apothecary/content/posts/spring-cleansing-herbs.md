As winter recedes, herbalists have long turned to a handful of gentle plants to support the body's transition into warmer months. Spring cleansing is not about deprivation -- it is about inviting movement and renewal.

## Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale)

The entire dandelion plant is useful. The root supports liver function, while the leaves act as a gentle diuretic. A simple tea of dried dandelion root, simmered for ten minutes, is one of the oldest spring tonics in European herbalism.

## Nettle (Urtica dioica)

Stinging nettle is rich in iron, calcium, and silica. Harvested young in early spring (with gloves), the leaves make a nourishing infusion. Steep a generous handful in hot water for four hours for a mineral-rich drink.

## Cleavers (Galium aparine)

Also called goosegrass, cleavers supports the lymphatic system. It is best used fresh -- juice the stems and leaves, or make a cold infusion by steeping them in cool water overnight.

## Burdock (Arctium lappa)

Burdock root is a blood purifier in traditional terms. It supports the skin and liver. Slice the fresh root thinly and simmer it into a decoction, or add it to soups and stir-fries as the Japanese do with gobo.

## A Simple Spring Protocol

1. Morning: dandelion root tea
2. Midday: nettle infusion (prepared the night before)
3. Evening: a small glass of cleavers juice or cold infusion

Continue for two to three weeks. Pair with fresh seasonal greens and plenty of water.
