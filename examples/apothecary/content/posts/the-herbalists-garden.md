You do not need acres of land to grow your own medicine. A small raised bed or a collection of pots on a sunny balcony can supply a household with many of the most useful herbs.

## Essential Plants for Beginners

These ten plants are hardy, forgiving, and cover a wide range of common needs:

- **Chamomile** -- calming tea, digestive aid, skin wash
- **Lavender** -- sleep support, anxiety relief, wound care
- **Peppermint** -- digestive comfort, headache relief, decongestant
- **Lemon balm** -- nervous tension, cold sores, gentle sedative
- **Calendula** -- skin healing, anti-inflammatory, gentle enough for children
- **Thyme** -- respiratory infections, coughs, antimicrobial
- **Rosemary** -- circulation, memory, joint pain
- **Sage** -- sore throats, hot flashes, digestive tonic
- **Echinacea** -- immune support at the first sign of illness
- **Yarrow** -- fever management, wound healing, blood flow

## Layout Considerations

Group plants by their water and sun needs rather than by use. Mediterranean herbs (rosemary, thyme, sage, lavender) thrive in lean, well-drained soil with full sun. Mints and lemon balm prefer partial shade and richer, moist soil -- and should be contained, as they spread aggressively.

## Harvesting

The general rule: harvest aerial parts (leaves, flowers) just as the plant begins to bloom, when essential oil content peaks. Harvest roots in autumn after the plant has died back, when energy has returned to the root system.

Dry herbs in small bundles hung upside down in a warm, well-ventilated space out of direct sunlight. Store in labeled glass jars away from heat and light.
