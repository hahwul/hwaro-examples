Eight essays, one argument: a city does not forget by accident. It forgets by decision — a market rerouted around, a ruin excavated beside another ruin, a stream buried under a road and later dug back up. This collection follows that decision through Istanbul, Naples, Beirut, Tokyo, Detroit, Lagos, and Seoul, in the order below, beginning with a foreword on why cities are harder to preserve than buildings.
