The clocks roll back tomorrow, and the late afternoon already feels like an evening borrowed from December. At 4:50 the western sky carries a low band of orange that thins to gray by the time it reaches the building tops. Light angles in horizontally and finds every imperfection in the brick: the dropped courses, the patched window frames, a line of efflorescence climbing the corner of a chimney.

The park benches fill briefly, then empty. A runner finishes a slow lap and stops to retie a shoe under a sycamore that has turned almost completely yellow. Leaves drop in small handfuls when the wind picks up. The wind is cold but not sharp. It carries a smell of damp stone from the fountain, which is still running because the maintenance schedule has not caught up to the season.

A few minutes after five, the streetlights cut on. They warm up over the course of a minute, going from a dim amber to a steadier white. Cyclists start to add lights, blinking red on the rear and steady white on the front. The traffic on the cross street thickens but does not slow. Headlights on cars turn on as a rolling wave that follows the dimming sky.

By the time the orange band is gone, the air has dropped two degrees. The bench wood holds the warmth of the afternoon for a little longer. Tomorrow at this hour the sky will already be dark, and the same scene will play in compressed time.
