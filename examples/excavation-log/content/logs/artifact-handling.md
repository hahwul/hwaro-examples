Standard procedures for handling recovered artifacts to ensure preservation.

-   **Immediate Stabilization:**
    Upon removal from the soil matrix, assess the artifact's stability. Fragile items should be supported with custom mounts before transport.
-   **Cleaning Restrictions:**
    Do not clean artifacts in the field. Adhering soil may contain critical contextual information or trace residues. Wait for laboratory analysis.
-   **Packaging:**
    Use acid-free tissue paper and archival-quality polyethylene bags or boxes. Ensure the artifact is immobilized within its container to prevent abrasion.
-   **Labeling:**
    All containers must clearly display the site code, unit designation, depth, date of recovery, and the excavator's initials.

-   **Transportation:**
    When transporting artifacts from the field to the laboratory, ensure all containers are securely packed in rigid, padded boxes. Prevent any movement within the boxes that could cause damage during transit. The vehicle must maintain a stable environment, avoiding extreme temperature fluctuations.
-   **Documentation Handover:**
    Upon arrival at the laboratory, all artifacts must be accompanied by their corresponding field records. A formal handover process, including a thorough inventory check against the field logs, is mandatory to ensure the chain of custody remains unbroken and all findings are accounted for.
