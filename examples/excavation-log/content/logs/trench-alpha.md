Initial excavation report for Trench Alpha.

We broke ground on Trench Alpha early this morning. The topsoil (Layer 1) was cleared to a depth of 15cm, revealing a dense, dark loamy matrix. Initial findings include scattered ceramic sherds and a small bronze fragment, tentatively identified as a fibula, located at grid coordinate [N14, E22].

Further excavation into Layer 2 (15-30cm) exposed a linear feature consisting of loosely packed limestone cobbles. This may represent the remains of an ephemeral structure or drainage channel. Excavation will proceed carefully to delineate the extent of this feature.

Initial screening of the soil matrix has indicated a moderate presence of organic matter, which we will continue to monitor closely as the depth increases. This specific area of the grid appears highly promising as it correlates well with the subsurface anomalies detected during the preliminary resistivity survey. Due to the high density of small artifacts, the excavation pace has been deliberately slowed to ensure careful documentation and preservation of the context. All findings have been mapped and cataloged in the daily registry for future laboratory analysis. The trench will be covered and secured for the night, with excavation set to resume at sunrise tomorrow.
