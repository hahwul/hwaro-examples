Initial survey protocol for suspected artifact clusters.

1.  **Grid Establishment:**
    Establish a 5x5 meter grid over the target area using GPS coordinates and physical markers. Ensure baseline alignment with true north.

2.  **Surface Collection:**
    Systematically collect and catalog all visible artifacts on the surface within each grid square. Bag and tag each item with its corresponding grid coordinates.

3.  **Soil Resistivity:**
    Conduct a soil resistivity survey across the established grid. Record readings at 0.5-meter intervals to identify potential subsurface anomalies.

4.  **Test Pitting:**
    Excavate 1x1 meter test pits at the center of identified anomalies or at regular intervals if no clear anomalies are detected. Screen all excavated soil through a 1/4-inch mesh.

5.  **Documentation:**
    Every test pit and surface collection must be thoroughly documented. This includes comprehensive photographic records, detailed soil profiles, and meticulously drawn stratigraphic sections. Ensure all documentation utilizes standardized forms and terminology to facilitate post-excavation analysis.

6.  **Environmental Sampling:**
    Soil samples for flotation and chemical analysis must be collected from every context encountered during test pitting. These samples will be crucial for understanding the site's environmental history and detecting trace evidence of human activity that is invisible to the naked eye. Careful handling is required to avoid contamination.
