The last feature release of the 1.x line before the renderer rewrite, and a quiet favorite: scrollback stops being the thing that eats your RAM.

<!-- more -->

## added

- **Compressed scrollback.** History beyond the live viewport is packed into lz4 blocks of 512 lines. The compression is invisible — search, selection, and `scroll:prev_prompt` all decompress lazily — but the footprint is not: one million lines of typical build output now sits around 60 MB instead of 240 MB.

```toml
[scrollback]
lines = 1000000
compression = "lz4"     # lz4 | none
block_lines = 512
```

- `semaphore stats` reports per-pane scrollback size, compression ratio, and atlas usage, formatted for humans or as JSON with `--json`.

## fixed

- **IME preedit for CJK input.** Composition text now renders inline at the cursor instead of in a floating overlay, and double-width preedit no longer drifts the caret one cell to the right by the end of a sentence. Tested against Korean (Hangul), Japanese (Mozc), and Chinese (Rime) engines.
- Bracketed paste of more than 64 KB no longer splits into multiple paste events on Windows.
- `ctrl+shift+f` search highlights survive a pane resize instead of pointing at pre-wrap coordinates.

## notes

v1.9.2 supports in-place downgrade to v1.9.1 — compressed scrollback is dropped on downgrade, not corrupted. This is the recommended release for anyone waiting out the [2.0 renderer transition](/releases/v2-0-0/).
