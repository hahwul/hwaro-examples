## About This Journal

Spire is a written record of encounters with Gothic architecture -- the soaring naves, the intricate tracery, the flying buttresses that defy gravity and expectation alike. Each entry is a meditation on stone, light, and the human impulse to build upward.

This journal documents travels through the cathedral cities of Europe and beyond, examining how these structures shaped the urban landscapes around them and continue to command the skyline centuries after their construction.

## What You Will Find Here

- **Cathedral studies**: Close readings of individual buildings, their construction histories, and their architectural significance
- **City explorations**: Walking the streets around great churches, understanding the relationship between sacred architecture and civic life
- **Architectural history**: The development of the Gothic style from its Romanesque origins through the Flamboyant and Perpendicular periods
- **Craft and material**: Stone, glass, iron, and timber -- the materials and methods that made the impossible possible

## The Name

A spire is more than an architectural feature. It is a statement of ambition, a marker visible for miles, a point where the work of human hands reaches closest to the sky. Every great cathedral city is defined by its spires.
