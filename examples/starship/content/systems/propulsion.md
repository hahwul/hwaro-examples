The primary propulsion system of the Class-IV Deep-Space Cruiser is powered by an antimatter fusion reactor utilizing magnetic nozzle coils. The propulsion core operates by combining deuterium particles with antiprotons inside a highly pressurized containment chamber. The resulting high-energy plasma flow is directed through three electromagnetic nozzles to generate thrust.

To maintain warp field stability during high-speed transit, the containment fields must be calibrated within a tolerance of zero point five percent. If magnetic field strength drops below this threshold, the reactor computer will trigger an emergency containment purge. This procedure vents the active fuel mixture into space to prevent a catastrophic core breach.

Engineers must monitor the thermal output of the magnetic nozzle coils continuously. High power output can lead to magnetic coil degradation over long voyages. Regular maintenance involves flushing the coolant channels with liquid helium and testing the magnetic injectors for flux leakage. Ensure all diagnostic checks are logged before initiating jump procedures.
