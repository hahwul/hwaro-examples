  <p>In the winter of 1976, a private collector of rare instruments and rarer voices attended the final performance of a beloved soprano. When the curtain fell for the last time, he could not accept that the music had ended.</p>

  <p>Three months later, in a disused theater on the edge of the old quarter, the first performance of the Stuffed Opera took place. The audience numbered eleven. The soprano was no longer capable of fatigue. The reviews were rapturous and deeply confused.</p>

  <p>We have not missed a single scheduled performance in forty-seven years.</p>

## Our Method

Every artist who joins the company does so with full knowledge of the terms. We preserve not only the form, but the intention. The voice. The gesture that was meant to break hearts.

Our taxidermists are artists in their own right. The sawdust we use is harvested from the same forests that supplied the original opera houses of Europe. The glass eyes are ground by a single remaining artisan whose grandfather supplied the original company in 1977.

Nothing is wasted. Nothing is forgotten. Nothing is allowed to leave.

## The Collection

At present the permanent ensemble consists of:

- 3 sopranos (2 lyric, 1 coloratura)
- 2 mezzo-sopranos
- 1 contralto (the famous "Throat of Amber")
- 4 tenors
- 2 baritones (one of whom has not blinked since 1983)
- 1 bass (retired to the role of Commendatore in 1991)
- 1 nightingale, 1 swan, 1 raven (the famous avian trio of Act II in *The Magic Flute Preserved*)

New artists are accepted only when an existing member of the company can no longer fulfill their role to the artistic director's satisfaction. This has occurred exactly twice.

## Visiting

The theater is open every evening at 7:40pm. The performance begins at 8:00pm precisely and concludes when the final member of the audience has departed.

There is no box office. There are no tickets.
