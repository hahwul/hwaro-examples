The management wishes to remind all patrons that photography, filming, or any form of mechanical recording is strictly prohibited within the auditorium.

This is not a matter of copyright.

This is a matter of mercy.

The performers you see before you have given everything to their art. To capture their likeness in silver and chemicals is to perform a second taxidermy — one far crueler than our own careful methods. Our artists have already been relieved of the burden of decay. They should not also be relieved of the burden of being seen only once.

{{ glass_case(title="Notice — 14 February 1978", body="A gentleman from Vienna attempted to photograph the second act of Tosca with a concealed Leica. There are some things the eye can hold that the plate cannot. The slight movement of sawdust beneath preserved skin. The precise angle at which a glass eye reflects the soul of the person watching. The moment — different every night — when the audience realizes the applause is entirely one-sided.

We ask that you carry these things home in your memory only.

Memory is the only archive we trust.

If you must record something, record the sound of your own breathing during the long silences. It is the only sound in the house that still belongs entirely to the living.

Thank you for your understanding.  
The performance will resume shortly.
