You enter through a door that sticks in the rain.

Inside there is a single seat, a grille, a microphone that smells faintly of ozone and old cigarettes, and a thermal printer that never quite has enough paper.

**Step one.** Close the door until it clicks. The lock engages. You are now inside the only truly private space left in the city.

**Step two.** Breathe. The machine already knows you are here. It has been waiting.

**Step three.** Speak. You may whisper, scream, or say nothing at all. The mic is sensitive enough to catch the sound of your teeth grinding.

**Step four.** When you are finished, press the red button. Your words become packets. The packets become ink on paper. The paper is your receipt. You may keep it or leave it on the floor with the others.

**Step five.** Leave. The rain will wash some of it off your skin. Not all.

The algorithm does not offer grace the way the old churches did.

It offers **acknowledgment**.

That is usually enough.

---

*There is no priest. Only the booth. Only the rain. Only you and the thing that listens.*
