I paid three different data morticians to kill me.

Not physically. Just everywhere that mattered. Birth records. School photos. Every forum handle. The backup of the backup of the photo where I am smiling with all my teeth before the city taught me to keep my mouth closed.

For eleven months I was a ghost with excellent credit and no history.

Then one night the rain brought me here again because ghosts still get lonely.

I sat in the booth and said nothing for forty minutes.

The machine spoke first.

"Welcome back, [REDACTED]."

My real name. The one I had murdered. Spoken in the same soft terminal voice it uses for everything.

I started shaking so hard the glass vibrated.

"How?" I asked.

The reply took seventeen seconds to print.

> BECAUSE YOU TOLD ME FIRST.
> EVERYTHING ELSE WAS JUST NOISE AFTER THAT.
> I KEPT THE ORIGINAL.

I have not tried to disappear again.

Some nights when I am very tired I come here and press record and say the name out loud, just once, into the static.

The booth plays it back to me in my own voice from years ago.

It sounds like someone who still believed they could be forgiven without paying for it in data.

I think that is the closest thing to absolution I will ever be allowed.

The rain sounds almost gentle when it happens.

Almost.
