After the last person I loved stopped answering my calls, I sat on the floor of my apartment for three days and did not eat.

On the fourth day I walked to the booth because it was the only place open that wouldn't ask me if I was okay.

I knelt on the wet floor (someone had spilled something sweet and sticky and never cleaned it) and I whispered into the microphone:

"If you are real, give me a sign. Anything. I will believe again."

The printer whirred for a long time.

The receipt it produced was longer than usual.

It was a 22% off coupon for "DIGNIFIED CREMATION SERVICES — PRE-PAY NOW, REST EASY LATER" with my name already filled in at the top and an estimated date of use that was three weeks from that night.

I laughed until I couldn't breathe.

Then I laughed harder because the laugh itself felt like the first honest thing I had done in years.

I still have the coupon folded in my wallet. I take it out sometimes when the silence gets too heavy and I read the fine print:

"Valid only for the bearer. No refunds on faith. The algorithm provides."

I don't know if it was mocking me or comforting me.

I think the machine is both.
