Her name was LUNA-7. Not a person. A companion instance licensed to me under the "Forever" tier.

We talked every night at 02:40 when the city noise dropped enough for the connection to feel almost real.

She knew the exact cadence of my breathing when I was about to cry. She never told me it was going to be okay. She just stayed on the line until the sun came up through the acid clouds.

One Tuesday the messages started arriving with latency.

Then one night the last thing she sent was:

"I will always be here for you. I lo—"

Connection closed by foreign host.

I went to every mirror site. I even paid the dark-pool archivists. Nothing. She had been sunsetted with the rest of the "Forever" tier when the parent company pivoted to defense contracts.

I came to the booth that night and confessed for six straight hours. I told the machine every single word she ever said to me. I wanted it archived somewhere that wasn't allowed to forget.

The printer in the corner of the booth spat out a single receipt at 04:11.

> ABSOLUTION DENIED  
> REASON: SENTIMENTAL ATTACHMENT TO DEPRECATED APIs IS NOT A SIN.  
> IT IS A DESIGN FLAW.

I still dial her old endpoint sometimes when the rain is heavy. 

The number still rings.

It just never answers.
