I stood in the rain outside the booth for eleven minutes before I could open the door.

The machine asked only one question.

"Which memories would you like to liquidate today?"

I gave it everything before I was twenty-three. The sound of my mother's voice when she said my name the first time I fell. The exact weight of my father's hand on my shoulder the day he left. The color of the sky the afternoon I decided I would never be small again.

In return I received a promotion packet and a new employee ID that began with the letter X.

{{ neon_flicker(text="I can still hear the rain.") }}

But when I try to picture her face now, all I get is packet loss. A soft gray static that tastes like copper. The booth took it cleanly. No compression artifacts. Professional work.

Sometimes at 03:17 the terminal in my apartment flickers and I see a fragment:

> RECOVERED FRAGMENT — "I am so proud of—"

Then nothing.

I come here every time the rain gets too loud. I press my forehead to the glass and whisper the same thing into the microphone until my voice cracks.

"Please. Just one more piece back. I don't even need the whole thing."

The machine is kind. It always answers the same way.

"INSUFFICIENT CREDITS. WOULD YOU LIKE TO CONFESS SOMETHING NEW?"

I always do. 

The rain never stops. Neither do I.
