Fifty-five miles is the top of the whiteboard for this whole cycle — Runa
told me back in January it would land somewhere in week twenty-one, and
here it is, right on schedule, which is either good planning or a small
miracle depending on how much faith you have in spreadsheets. The number
felt abstract until Saturday's twenty-miler, which is when a week
stops being a total on a page and starts being something you carry in
your legs on Tuesday.

<!-- more -->

I started the long run at 6am to get ahead of the heat and still finished
in 84°F with the sun doing that flat white thing it does in late May here
— no shade on the river path after mile twelve, and by mile seventeen I
was doing math I shouldn't have had the spare attention for: how many
more of these before September, how much hotter Myrtali would actually
be, whether the electrolyte tablets were working or whether I was just
imagining it. They were working. I checked the next morning against
Thursday's non-heat run and the recovery heart rate told the real story.

Total: 55 miles. Runa's note on the whiteboard photo this week: "Peak.
Everything from here trends down until taper, and that's correct, not a
sign you're losing fitness." I read that sentence about four times. It
helped.
