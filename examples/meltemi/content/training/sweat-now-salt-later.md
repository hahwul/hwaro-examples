Myrtali in September runs somewhere between 82 and 88°F at race start,
which means nothing I can do here in April will fully rehearse it — but
Runa insists on trying anyway. Twice this week I ran the treadmill in the
smallest room in the apartment with the door shut and a space heater
running, which turned a routine easy run into forty minutes of active
regret. The point, apparently, is teaching the body to sweat sooner and
sweat more, so that come September it isn't improvising.

<!-- more -->

I weighed myself before and after Thursday's heat session out of
curiosity and lost just under two pounds of water in forty minutes,
which is either impressive or alarming depending on how you want to look
at it. Runa's answer, when I sent her the number: "Good — now go buy
proper electrolyte tablets and stop guessing." I did. They taste like a
swimming pool. I am assured this is normal.

Total: 47 miles, and the first week Coach Runa added a line item that
wasn't a distance: "electrolytes — every session over 60 min, not just
long runs." It's a small addition to the whiteboard but it changes the
whole shape of a hot run — less about gutting it out, more about arriving
prepared, which is I think the actual point of this entire training
block.
