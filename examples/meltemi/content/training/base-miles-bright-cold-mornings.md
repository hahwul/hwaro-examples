Coach Runa's note in the corner of the whiteboard this week: "No hero
miles. Nobody wins the marathon in January." I believe her, mostly,
though there is a specific temptation on a cold clear morning to run the
easy miles a little less easy, just to see. I did not give in, except
maybe once, on Wednesday, when the strides at the end of the run turned
into something closer to a tempo without my permission.

<!-- more -->

The river path at this hour belongs to about six of us — a woman with two
huskies, an older man who walks backward for the first half mile as some
kind of ritual, and whoever else is awake enough to be out here at
5:40am in January. Nobody talks. It's a good kind of solitude, the sort
that makes the whole enterprise feel less like training for a race in a
country I haven't been to yet and more like just a thing I do before the
day starts.

Either reading works for me.
