Runa's plans build the long run up in increments small enough that I
never quite notice the jump until I look at three weeks of the whiteboard
side by side: eight, then nine, now fourteen. The ridge road out past the
reservoir is the closest thing this town has to open country, and running
it alone for two and a half hours on a Saturday morning has become the
week's actual centrepiece, the thing everything else is arranged around.

<!-- more -->

The speed session on Tuesday — eight times 800 metres, short recovery —
was the hardest single workout of the build so far, not because the pace
was fast but because Runa dropped the rest interval down to 90 seconds
without warning me in advance. I found out by looking at the whiteboard
photo Monday night and spent the rest of the evening negotiating with
myself about whether that was fair. It was fair. It was also miserable.

Total: 41 miles. The whiteboard note this week just says "building" —
one word, underlined twice. I've started keeping a small paper log of my
own next to the laptop, mostly so I can flip back and see the numbers
climbing in a way that a spreadsheet somehow doesn't make as convincing.
