There is exactly one hill in this town worth training on — Bellamy
Street, four-tenths of a mile at just over seven percent grade, lined
with the kind of houses that have a dog in every yard. Runa's logic:
Myrtali's marathon climbs through the olive terraces above Kastelos
around kilometre thirty, well past the point where anyone's legs want to
climb anything, so the hill work needs to start now, while it's still
theoretical.

<!-- more -->

Seven repeats on Tuesday, jogging back down between each. By the fifth
one my splits had drifted from 1:42 to 1:58, which Runa says is fine —
"you're not training to hold pace on hills yet, you're training your legs
to remember what hills are." The dogs on Bellamy Street were, as always,
extremely invested in my presence and contributed nothing useful to my
splits.

Total: 34 miles. First week the total has crossed thirty, and the first
week the whiteboard photo included a second line underneath the number:
"good — more of this in March." I taped a printout of Myrtali's course
elevation profile above my desk the same day. It is not a large hill by
the standards of actual mountains, but it is the biggest thing on that
course by a wide margin, and it arrives at kilometre thirty with the wind
already working against everyone.
