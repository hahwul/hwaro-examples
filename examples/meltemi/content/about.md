## The wind it's named for

The meltemi is the dry north wind that scours the Aegean from roughly
June through September — clear skies, cooling air, and a steady fetch
that can turn a flat harbour into a washing machine by mid-morning. On
Myrtali it funnels between the island's two ridgelines and runs straight
down the coast road that the marathon course follows for eleven of its
forty-two kilometres. Training for that wind, not just the heat, is most
of what this log is actually about.

## The whiteboard

Coach Runa Beck writes each week's plan on an actual whiteboard in her
garage in Larimet, photographs it Sunday night, and sends it over. Six
columns, one rest day, a running total circled in the corner. This site
is a fairly literal translation of that photo: the mileage numeral on
every entry is the number she circles, set as big as the page will allow,
because that's how it looks pinned to the wall above her desk.

I run in the early morning before work, alternating flat river-path miles
with the one hill in town steep enough to be useful. Twice a week, when
the plan calls for heat work and the weather won't cooperate, I do a
treadmill session in a closed room with the heater on, which is exactly
as unpleasant as it sounds and exactly as necessary.

## The race

The Myrtali Marathon starts at 06:30 on the harbour front, loops north
along the coast road through three fishing villages, climbs through the
olive terraces above Kastelos, and finishes back on the harbour with the
meltemi — by then usually blowing a steady force four — square in the
face for the last six kilometres. This will be my third marathon and the
first one run somewhere the wind gets a vote.

## Using the log

Entries are filed by week under [Training](/training/), each one tagged
by what the week emphasised — heat, hills, long runs, and so on. The
[tag index](/tags/) is the fastest way to follow one thread across the
whole build, and [search](/search/) covers every word that has gone into
this log so far, including the parts about the heater.
