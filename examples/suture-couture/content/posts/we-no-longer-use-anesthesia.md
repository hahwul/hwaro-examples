Anesthesia was a lie we told ourselves so we could sleep at night. We sleep very well now.

The moment we stopped numbing the flesh, the clothes began to mean something. Every twitch, every held breath, every time the scalpel meets resistance — these are the new drapes. The body tells us where the garment wants to go. We simply follow the screaming.

There is a particular quality of light that only appears when a woman is experiencing exactly the right amount of pain. It makes the gold thread sing. It makes the bruises arrange themselves into the most exquisite ombre.

We do not offer aftercare. The memory of the procedure is the only accessory that matters.

This season the most coveted look is the one the client cannot stop remembering. Even when she tries. Especially when she tries.
