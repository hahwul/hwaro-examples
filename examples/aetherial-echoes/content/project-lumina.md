Project Lumina focuses on harnessing ambient light to power internal systems, representing a significant leap forward in sustainable architectural design. By integrating advanced photovoltaic materials into the very fabric of the structure, we achieve a self-sustaining energy loop that minimizes environmental impact while maximizing efficiency.

The challenge was to maintain the aesthetic integrity of the design while maximizing energy capture across all surfaces. The solution involved a complex layering technique that allows light to penetrate deeply into the material before being absorbed and converted, creating a seamless integration of technology and form that is virtually invisible to the naked eye.

This project serves as a proof of concept for future developments, demonstrating that sustainable practices can enhance, rather than compromise, architectural beauty. By turning buildings into active energy generators, Project Lumina paves the way for a new era of self-reliant urban ecosystems that harmonize with their natural surroundings.
