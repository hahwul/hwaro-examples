Before a single word is read, typography has already spoken. The weight of a letter, the curve of a serif, the space between lines — these choices create an emotional landscape that shapes every interaction.

## Serif vs. Sans-Serif

The debate is ancient, but the truth is nuanced. Serifs carry heritage, authority, and warmth. Sans-serifs project modernity, clarity, and efficiency. The magic happens when you pair them — using each where its strengths matter most.

A serif headline over sans-serif body text creates a visual rhythm: the headline draws you in with personality, the body text keeps you reading with comfort.

## The Power of Weight

Light and regular weights whisper sophistication. Bold weights demand attention. The contrast between weights within a single typeface creates hierarchy without changing fonts at all.

Consider how a 300-weight heading feels compared to a 700-weight one. The lighter version suggests exclusivity and calm. The heavier version projects confidence and strength. Both are valid — the choice depends on what you want your reader to feel.

## Spacing as Luxury

Letter-spacing is the invisible signature of premium design. Wide tracking on uppercase text transforms ordinary words into statements. Generous line-height gives paragraphs room to breathe.

In design, what you leave out matters as much as what you put in. White space around typography is not emptiness — it is emphasis.
