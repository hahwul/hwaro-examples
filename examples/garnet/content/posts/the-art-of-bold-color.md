Color is not decoration — it is communication. The deep reds and burgundies that define the garnet palette carry centuries of meaning: passion, power, warmth, and depth.

## Why Red Commands Attention

In a world saturated with pale interfaces and safe neutrals, a bold color palette is an act of courage. Red wavelengths are the longest visible to the human eye, literally the first thing we see. There is nothing accidental about its impact.

## The Burgundy Spectrum

True sophistication in red comes from restraint within richness. The burgundy spectrum — from deep wine to warm rose — offers a range that can be both dramatic and inviting:

- **Wine (#722F37)** — A classic that anchors any composition
- **Burgundy (#800020)** — The heart of luxury design
- **Garnet (#733635)** — Warmth with a hint of earth
- **Rose Gold (#B76E79)** — Modern softness meets tradition

## Applying Bold Color

The key is contrast. A deep red backdrop makes white typography sing. A burgundy accent against dark surfaces creates depth without clutter. The goal is never to overwhelm, but to create focal points that guide the eye through the experience.

Bold color, used well, does not shout. It resonates.
