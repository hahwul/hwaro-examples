This is the working log of a [small station](/about/) in the Salish foothills,
grid CN87. Everything below the banner is a real entry in the book: the UTC
timestamp I keyed the contact, the frequency, the mode, and the station on the
other end. Click any row to read the story behind it.

Some nights that story is a rare prefix pulled out of the noise on twenty
metres. Some weekends it is five watts and a wire hung off a granite summit.
And some evenings it is nothing more romantic than a soldering iron and a
length of 18-gauge wire, trimming an end-fed half-wave until the analyser
finally goes flat. The notebook mixes all three, because at this station they
are the same hobby: chase signals, then build better ways to hear them.

The log is kept the old way and the new way at once. Paper dupe sheet by the
key, `ALL.TXT` scrolling on the laptop, and a few short scripts that reconcile
the two before anything goes up to LoTW. Those scripts live here too, in the
[logbook](/logbook/), free to copy. Press `Ctrl`/`Cmd` + `K` to grep the whole
book by callsign, band, or antenna.
