The 3B9GT team had been on Rodrigues Island for four days before I caught even a
trace of them. Rodrigues sits alone in the Indian Ocean, roughly the antipode of
nowhere useful from CN87, and the short path runs straight through the dead zone
at this point in the cycle. The only way here is the long path — signal leaving
my wire pointed at the sunset, crossing the pole, and arriving from the
southwest at exactly the wrong-looking bearing.

<!-- more -->

At 2008Z the waterfall lit up. They were running FT8 in Fox/Hound mode, which
turns a pileup into an orderly queue: the DX transmits multiple streams, and you
hold your frequency and wait to be picked. The discipline is different from a
phone pileup. You do not call louder — you call *once*, on a clear slot, and
then you stop and let the decoder do the shouting.

Three cycles in, my report went green:

```
2013  Rx  3B9GT KD7GST -14
2013  Tx  3B9GT KD7GST R-11
2014  Rx  3B9GT KD7GST RR73
```

Fifty-one hundred watts of collective effort on that island, and here it lands
as a -14 on a valley station running a hundred watts into a piece of wire. The
long path held open for maybe nine minutes before the band folded. LoTW
confirmed the contact three days later. New one on 20, new one on the book.
