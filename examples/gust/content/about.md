Gust is the personal log of **KD7GST**, an amateur radio operator working from
a rented cabin in the Salish foothills, maidenhead grid **CN87uk**. I have held
a ticket for eleven years, upgraded to Extra in 2021, and spend most of my air
time chasing DX on the low bands and hauling small radios up hills.

## The bench

The main desk is an IC-7300 into a homebrew 40-metre end-fed half-wave, hung as
a sloper off a fir at about nine metres. For portable work I carry a five-watt
QRP transceiver, a linked dipole on a squid pole, and a straight key that has
been to the top of more summits than I have. A uSDX handles quick FT8 sessions
when the big rig is warm from a contest.

Antennas are the real hobby. If a page here is about a magnetic loop crammed
onto a small lot, or a 49:1 unun wound on a T140 core, that is me trying to
outrun the noise floor of a valley station.

## How I log

Every contact goes into the paper book at the key first — callsign, UTC, band,
mode, and the honest signal report. It is copied into the digital log the same
evening and reconciled with `ALL.TXT` from WSJT-X using a
[small de-duping script](/logbook/adif-deduper-python/). Confirmed contacts are
uploaded to LoTW weekly.

## Working me

I am usually QRV at grey line on 40 and 20 metres. Calling frequencies drift, so
check the log for where I have actually been. Answer a CQ, send an honest
report, and you will show up in the book. Skeds are welcome by post through the
usual bureau.
