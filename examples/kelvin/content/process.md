We build in a fixed order because the order is what makes the house work. The
envelope decisions come first and everything cosmetic comes last. Here is the
whole sequence, start to finish.

## 1. Site read

We begin with your coldest week, not your prettiest one. The 99% design
temperature for your location, the prevailing winter wind, and the ground snow
load together set the wall assembly before we draw a single window. A house for
a Montana ridge and a house for a coastal island are not the same house, and we
will not pretend they are.

## 2. Timber frame

Douglas-fir posts and beams are cut and joined in our shop with mortise-and-tenon
joinery. Because the frame carries the roof, the walls are freed to be nothing
but insulation and air-sealing — no studs fighting the thermal layer for space.

## 3. The envelope

This is the part we are strict about. Dense-pack cellulose fills the wall cavity,
a taped membrane forms the continuous air-control layer, and a rainscreen gap
keeps the cladding drying outward. Every pipe, wire, and duct penetration gets
gasketed. Then we run a blower-door test before the cladding hides anything.

```text
Blower-door target:   n50 <= 0.6 ACH
Test pressure:         50 Pa depressurize + pressurize
Result on file:        signed, before delivery
Fail condition:        find the leak, re-seal, re-test
```

## 4. Fit-out and delivery

Only now do fixtures, cabinetry, and finishes go in. We tow the finished house
to your pad, level it on its piers, and commission the heat-recovery ventilator
and heat pump on site. You get the final blower-door number in writing, along
with a short manual for living with a tight house.
