Every Kelvin is built to the same envelope standard. What changes between models
is the footprint, the number of sleeping lofts, and whether you want to climb a
ladder to bed. Wall and roof R-values are effective figures — thermal-bridge
corrected — not the number stamped on a batt of insulation.

## The Fen — 18 m²

Our smallest build and the easiest thing we tow. A single sleeping loft sits
over a full galley kitchen, and a double-height south window pulls in enough
winter sun to noticeably offset the heating load on clear cold days. The Fen
suits one person or a close couple, a backyard studio, or a first place on
rural land.

| Spec | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Footprint | 18 m² |
| Wall | R-38 effective |
| Roof | R-52 effective |
| Glazing | Triple, argon-filled |
| Sleeps | 2 |

## The Kiln — 24 m²

The model most people order, and the one we would build for ourselves. Two
sleeping lofts, a proper bathroom with a short soaking tub, and — the detail
that changes everything in January — a mudroom airlock so the front door never
dumps outdoor air straight into the living space. The Kiln is the reference
house behind the performance numbers we quote.

| Spec | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Footprint | 24 m² |
| Wall | R-42 effective |
| Roof | R-60 effective |
| Glazing | Triple, argon-filled |
| Sleeps | 4 |

## The Larch — 30 m²

No ladder, no loft. A single-level plan drawn on a park-model chassis for people
who want to live small without climbing to bed on a cold night. The Larch is our
most requested build from readers approaching retirement, and it carries our
thickest wall and roof assemblies because a longer footprint gives us the room.

| Spec | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Footprint | 30 m² |
| Wall | R-44 effective |
| Roof | R-64 effective |
| Glazing | Triple, argon-filled |
| Sleeps | 3 |
