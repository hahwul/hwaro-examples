Kelvin is a two-person timber-framing shop in the Cascade foothills, plus a
rotating apprentice or two who stay long enough to learn how to tape a membrane
properly. We build a handful of tiny houses a year and test every one of them
before it leaves the yard.

## How we started

The shop began after a winter spent house-sitting a well-loved, badly-built
tiny house on a friend's land. It was gorgeous and it was freezing. The wood
stove ran all day, the windows wept, and the loft was the only warm place in the
house. We spent that January reading building-science papers instead of
sleeping, and by spring we had decided to build the version that should have
existed — small, but serious about the shell.

## What we believe

> A house's job in winter is to hold a temperature honestly. Everything else is
> decoration on top of that job.

We think small homes deserve the same envelope care that gets lavished on
expensive passive houses, and that the reason they rarely get it is habit, not
cost. Insulation and air-sealing are among the cheapest things in a build. We
would rather put the money there and let the finishes be simple.

## The people

**Rowan Ledbetter** runs the frame shop and cuts the joinery. Fifteen years of
timber framing, a stubborn streak about grain direction, and the person who will
tell you your window is in the wrong place.

**Mira Osei** handles the building science, the blower-door testing, and the
part where the house actually gets delivered and commissioned. If a Kelvin holds
its heat, it is because Mira found the leak first.

We are based near the town of Alder Fork and deliver across the cold-climate
west. To talk through a build, read [the process](/process/) and then reach out
