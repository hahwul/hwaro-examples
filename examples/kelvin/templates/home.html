The build

Framed dry, sealed tight, delivered warm.

  1. 01

    Site read

    We start with your coldest week, not your prettiest one. Design-temperature, prevailing wind, and snow load set the wall assembly before we draw a single window.

  2. 02

    Timber frame

    Douglas-fir posts and beams cut in our shop, joined with mortise and tenon. The frame carries the roof so the walls are free to be all insulation.

  3. 03

    The envelope

    Dense-pack cellulose, a taped membrane, and a rainscreen gap. Every penetration gets gasketed and blower-door tested before the cladding goes on.

  4. 04

    Delivery

    We tow the finished house to your pad, level it, and commission the heat-recovery ventilator on site. You get the blower-door number in writing.

Thermal performance

Numbers we put in the contract.

  • 0.6ACH₅₀

    Airtightness, blower-door tested on every house before it leaves the yard.

  • R-42wall

    Effective, thermal-bridge corrected — not the number printed on the batt.

  • 15kWh/m²a

    Modelled annual heating demand for the Kiln at a minus-24 design temperature.

  • −27°held

    Coldest overnight low a delivered Kelvin has ridden out on a single mini-split.
