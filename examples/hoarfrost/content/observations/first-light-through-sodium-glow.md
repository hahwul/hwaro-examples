This is the first entry, so it is also the argument for the rest of them: eleven degrees below freezing, the east parapet swept clear of the week's snow with a kitchen broom, and Orion standing almost straight up over the water tower two buildings east — bright enough that even the stairwell fixture two floors down could not argue with it.

<!-- more -->

Betelgeuse held its rust color even through the haze; Rigel, on the opposite corner, stayed hard and blue-white the whole hour. The belt is the easiest thing in the sky to find from here and I used it exactly as intended, as a pointer — down and left to Sirius, blazing low over the rooftops, and a patient half-degree below the belt's center star to the soft, unresolvable smear that is the top of the Orion Nebula trying to reach a pair of 10x50s through a Bortle 8 wash. No structure, no color, just a slightly fuzzier star than its neighbors. That fuzz is the whole report.

I am starting this log because I kept telling myself the sky here was not worth writing down, and I no longer believe that. Orion crossed the glow tonight the way it has for a hundred winters somewhere darker. The account starts here.
