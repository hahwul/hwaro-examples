Most nights the sky over this block reads somewhere between Bortle 7 and Bortle 9 — a sodium-and-LED wash that swallows the Milky Way whole and leaves only the brightest few hundred stars standing. This is not a complaint about that. It is a record of what is still possible to see from here: the seven stars of a dipper, the belt of a hunter, the slow crawl of a planet past a bright one, logged from a rooftop parapet, a fire-escape landing, and a kitchen balcony rail, with a pair of binoculars and a small refractor that both fit in one tote bag.
