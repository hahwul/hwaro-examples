## About This Journal

Riverbank is a nature journal dedicated to recording the quiet, unhurried life along a stretch of lowland river. The entries collected here span multiple seasons and aim to document the subtle changes that occur along the waterway -- from shifts in bird migration patterns to the slow rise and fall of water levels throughout the year.

## Purpose

The goal is simple: to pay close attention. By recording regular observations, patterns emerge that might otherwise go unnoticed. The seasonal return of certain bird species, the timing of plant blooms along the bank, the clarity of the water after rainfall -- these details, taken together, form a portrait of a living system.

## Method

Each entry is written during or shortly after a visit to the river. Observations are recorded with the date, location, and weather conditions. This metadata provides context that helps track changes over time.

## The River

The observations here come from a stretch of lowland river bordered by deciduous woodland, reed beds, and open meadow. The riverbank supports a diverse community of species, from common kingfishers and grey herons to otters, water voles, and a wide variety of aquatic insects.
