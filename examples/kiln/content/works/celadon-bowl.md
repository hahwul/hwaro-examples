This bowl is part of an ongoing study of Goryeo-period celadon. The body is a high-iron stoneware that responds well to reduction firing, taking on a warm grey core that shows through the translucent glaze along the rim and foot.

The form follows a wide tea bowl profile, slightly waisted at the foot to balance in the hand. The wall is thrown thin enough to feel weightless when full of hot tea, but thick enough at the base to retain heat across a long pour.

The glaze is a feldspathic celadon stabilized with a small percentage of bone ash. Reduction begins at 950C and is held until cone 10 begins to bend. The result is the characteristic blue-green that shifts toward grey in thicker pools and toward jade in the thinnest applications.

The foot was wiped of glaze and rests directly on the kiln shelf. A faint blush of iron flashing remains where the wadding lifted away after firing, a record of the bowl's position in the chamber.
