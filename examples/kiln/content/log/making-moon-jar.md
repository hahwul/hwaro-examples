Creating a traditional Korean Moon Jar requires patience, precision, and an understanding of porcelain's unique properties. This log documents the entire cycle.

<div class="mt-12">
  <div class="process-step">
    <h3 class="text-lg font-serif font-medium text-stone-900 mt-0 mb-2">1. Throwing & Joining (제작)</h3>
    <p class="text-stone-600 text-sm m-0">
      Due to its immense scale, the jar is thrown as two separate bowls on the wheel. Once the clay has dried slightly to a leather-hard state, the upper half is carefully inverted and joined to the lower half. The seam is then smoothed out, blending the two pieces into a single, cohesive form. The inherent difficulty lies in matching the exact dimensions and preventing the heavy top half from sagging.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="process-step">
    <h3 class="text-lg font-serif font-medium text-stone-900 mt-0 mb-2">2. Drying & Bisque Firing (건조 및 초벌)</h3>
    <p class="text-stone-600 text-sm m-0">
      The piece must dry very slowly to prevent the seam from cracking due to uneven shrinkage. Once bone-dry, it goes into the kiln for the initial bisque firing at 800°C. This hardens the clay and prepares it to absorb the glaze while remaining porous enough to draw the liquid in quickly.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="process-step">
    <h3 class="text-lg font-serif font-medium text-stone-900 mt-0 mb-2">3. Glazing (시유)</h3>
    <p class="text-stone-600 text-sm m-0">
      A clear, soft matte glaze is applied. Dipping such a large piece requires two people and a massive vat of glaze. The goal is a perfectly even coat that will pool slightly at the seam, emphasizing the organic nature of the join. The foot is then wiped clean to prevent the piece from fusing to the kiln shelf during the final firing.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="process-step">
    <h3 class="text-lg font-serif font-medium text-stone-900 mt-0 mb-2">4. Final Firing (재벌 소성)</h3>
    <p class="text-stone-600 text-sm m-0">
      The piece is loaded into the kiln for the high-temperature glaze firing. We fire to 1250°C in an oxidation atmosphere. At peak temperature, the glaze melts into a smooth, glassy surface, and the porcelain vitrifies, becoming fully dense. The slow cooling process is crucial to ensure the glaze fits perfectly without crazing.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>
