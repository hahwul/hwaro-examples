A reduction firing is decided long before the burners come on. The choices made while loading the kiln determine which pieces flash, which stay clean, and which come out cooler than the cone records suggest. This note captures the loading plan for the spring firing of the 25 cubic foot downdraft.

## Atmosphere Zones

The downdraft has three working zones with measurable differences in atmosphere and temperature.

The front zone, near the burner ports, runs hottest and reduces most aggressively. Iron-bearing pieces placed here develop the strongest flashing, and celadons can dip toward grey if the reduction is heavy. Bowls that benefit from movement in the glaze go in this zone.

The middle zone is the most balanced. Temperature swings are smaller and the atmosphere stays closer to neutral except during the heaviest reduction periods. Production work that needs to match across firings goes here.

The back zone, near the flue, runs coolest and tends toward neutral atmosphere. White porcelains and pieces that need stable color sit in this zone. Tall vases also fit better here because the back wall has no bagwall obstructing the upper shelf.

## Shelving Pattern

The lower deck holds heavy work close to the floor: large bottles, tall jars, and any piece taller than 30 centimeters. Half shelves are used so that a single tall piece does not cost two full shelf positions.

The middle deck holds bowls and cups in two rows. A waster slab goes underneath any piece with a known glaze runner.

The top deck holds test tiles, small cups, and decorative pieces. The space above the top deck is left open for flame travel toward the flue.

## Padding and Spacing

A minimum of three centimeters separates each piece from its neighbors. Pieces with active glazes get five centimeters and a waster slab underneath. The bagwall is rebuilt with fresh kiln brick before each firing because soda volatiles erode the inner faces over time.

The load list is photographed before the door bricks go up. The photograph becomes the reference for unloading and for connecting any defects back to position in the chamber.
