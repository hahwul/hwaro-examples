Tabloid started as a photocopied handout left on the bar at a taproom that has since closed, which we consider a coincidence and not a warning. We rate beer. We rate the rooms that pour it. We have never once softened a review because a brewery bought us a round, and several breweries have stopped speaking to us as a direct result. The whole run so far lives in the [pours archive](pours/); if you just want to jump straight to the loudest verdict we've filed, start with [Molasses Bomb](pours/molasses-bomb/).

## What we actually do

Every issue runs a fixed set of pours: small-batch releases, one-off collaborations, and the occasional flagship that's been coasting on reputation for too long. Each review carries a style kicker, a one-line dek, a byline you can hold accountable, and an ABV stamped on the corner like a bottle cap — so you know exactly what you're getting into before the first paragraph starts an argument.

We also review the room. A beer poured into a proper glass by someone who cares tastes different from the same beer rushed across a folding table at a beer fest, and pretending otherwise is how tasting notes turn into marketing copy. The room gets graded as hard as the glass.

## Corrections and complaints

We run corrections when we're wrong about a fact — an ABV, a release date, a brewery's actual name. We do not run corrections when a brewer disagrees with our opinion of their beer; that's not a correction, that's a different opinion, and you're welcome to start your own zine.

## Get in touch

Send tips, samples, and polite disagreements to the desk. We read everything. We publish what's true and interesting, in whichever order gets the sentence to land harder. Looking for something specific? [Search the archive](search/) by beer, style, or room.
