Gose is supposed to be a gateway sour — light, salty, easy on people who think "tart beer" means "mistake." Marrow Ale Works did not get that memo, and their cherry gose puckers hard enough that our photographer set his glass down mid-sip and just said, quietly, "oh."

<!-- more -->

The color is gorgeous, a cloudy rose-gold that photographs better than most beers twice its price. The nose is all fresh cherry and a faint brine, and the first sip delivers exactly what the nose promises before the acid kicks the door in. This isn't a gentle tartness. It's a full pucker, sea-salt forward, with the cherry riding underneath like it's trying to apologize for the sourness on its way past.

At 4.1% it's session strength on paper, but the acidity does more work than the alcohol ever will — one glass felt like plenty, and we say that as people whose job is finishing the glass. It's a beer built for a hot patio and a plate of something salty and fried, not for sipping slowly while you think about your day.

Marrow Ale Works only runs this batch through the summer, and honestly, that's the right call. Some beers are built for a season, not a shelf.

**Verdict:** thrilling in small doses, humbling in large ones. Order it with food, and maybe don't make eye contact with the bartender when your face does what it's going to do.
