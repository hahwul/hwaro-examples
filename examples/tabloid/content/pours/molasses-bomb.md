Molasses Bomb ships once a year, in a wax-dipped bottle, with a release-day line that wraps around Hollow Barrel's parking lot before the doors open. We stood in it. We are not proud, and we are also not sorry.

<!-- more -->

This is not a beer you drink so much as a beer you sit with. It pours black and viscous, holds a thin ring of tan foam for about four seconds, and then gives up entirely, which feels correct — foam has no business surviving contact with something this heavy. The nose is bourbon vanilla and burnt brown sugar, with a date-syrup sweetness that borders on dessert-wine territory before the alcohol reasserts the fact that this is, in fact, beer.

At 11.2%, one bottle serves four people comfortably and one person recklessly. We split ours three ways over ninety minutes and still felt it arguing with us the next morning. The bourbon barrel is doing real work here — oak tannin, a little coconut, a finish that goes on so long you'll have forgotten what you asked the bartender before it answers.

Hollow Barrel says it cellars well for three to five years. We believe them, but we also do not believe any bottle of this survives that long once opened in a room with other people in it.

**Verdict:** buy it for the cellar, drink it in the parking lot with strangers instead. Nobody has ever regretted the second option.
