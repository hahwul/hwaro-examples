Most taprooms build a flight board with a theme: four hops, four malts, a tidy little tasting arc that ends with a stout so you leave on a full note. The Tin Whistle has abandoned all pretense of a narrative. Their flight board lists fourteen beers with no grouping, no order, and a footnote that just says "pick six."

<!-- more -->

We picked six. It was the wrong six, and it was also correct, because there is no right six on a board this unhinged — a barrel-aged barleywine sitting two lines below a grapefruit radler, a coffee milk stout next to a bone-dry saison that tastes like it's still mad about something. Average ABV across our flight landed around 6.1%, which tells you nothing useful about the experience of drinking a 3.8% wheat beer immediately followed by a 9% triple.

What saves it is the pour size — proper two-ounce tasters, not the stingy one-ounce thimbles some places try to pass off as generosity — and a staff that will happily talk you out of a bad pairing if you ask before you order. We did not ask before we ordered. Lesson learned, mouth confused, would absolutely do it again.

**Verdict:** bring a friend, split two boards, and let chaos pick your order for you. The Tin Whistle isn't teaching you anything about beer. It's teaching you to have a good time regardless.
