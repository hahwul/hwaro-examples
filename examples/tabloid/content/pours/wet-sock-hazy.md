The can art on Wet Sock Hazy is a cartoon foot mid-stride, mid-crime. Slant Rule Brewing knows exactly what they named this beer, and they are daring you to order it anyway. We ordered it anyway.

<!-- more -->

The nose hits first: wet laundry, orange pith, a little cut grass, and underneath all of it, weed — the good kind, not the parking-lot kind. It should not work. It works. The first sip is thick enough to eat with a spoon, loaded with tangerine and pineapple, and the hop bitterness stays polite until the finish, when it turns around and reminds you this is still an IPA, not a smoothie with a permit.

At 6.8% it drinks dangerously easy for something this cloudy. We watched a table of four go through a four-pack in the time it took us to write two paragraphs of notes, which we are choosing to read as a compliment to Slant Rule and an indictment of our own pace.

The taproom pours it into a stemless glass that does the beer no favors visually — it looks like a smoothie left out overnight — but nobody in the room cared, and neither did we by the second round.

**Verdict:** the name is a dare, the beer is a reward. Buy two cans, one to drink and one to leave on the counter so your roommate asks what it is and you get to explain, again, that yes, it's supposed to smell like that.
