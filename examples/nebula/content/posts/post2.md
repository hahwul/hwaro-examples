## Veil of Cygnus

The Cygnus Loop is a large supernova remnant in the constellation Cygnus, the debris of a star that exploded between ten and twenty thousand years ago. The shockwave from that ancient detonation continues to expand, colliding with and exciting the surrounding interstellar medium, creating the intricate lacework of glowing gas we observe today.

### Artist Statement

I have always been drawn to the idea that destruction can create something beautiful. The Veil Nebula is the ghost of a dead star, yet it glows with an intensity and intricacy that surpasses most living things. Each filament traces the boundary where the shockwave meets undisturbed gas -- a frontier of transformation.

### Technique

This work uses generative algorithms seeded with actual positional data from the Western Veil (NGC 6960) and Eastern Veil (NGC 6992). The filamentary structures are computationally grown using reaction-diffusion models, then colored using emission-line ratios from spectroscopic surveys. The result is neither photograph nor pure abstraction, but something between.

### Exhibition Notes

Commissioned for the Deep Sky Collective's annual showcase. The piece spans a panoramic format to convey the vast arc of the remnant across the sky.
