Forced upon King John by rebellious barons, the Magna Carta established for the first time the principle that everyone, including the king, was subject to the law. Though many of its specific provisions addressed feudal grievances, its broader legacy reshaped the relationship between rulers and the ruled.

<!-- more -->

The Magna Carta's influence far exceeded its original intent. Clauses guaranteeing the right to a fair trial and limiting arbitrary taxation became cornerstones of English common law. Centuries later, the document inspired the framers of the United States Constitution and the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. The principle it enshrined -- that power must be exercised within legal bounds -- remains one of the most important ideas in political history.
