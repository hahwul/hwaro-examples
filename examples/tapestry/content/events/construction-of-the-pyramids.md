The Great Pyramid of Giza, built as a tomb for Pharaoh Khufu, stood as the tallest man-made structure in the world for over 3,800 years. Its construction required an estimated 2.3 million limestone blocks, each weighing an average of 2.5 tons.

<!-- more -->

The organizational feat behind the pyramid's construction reveals a sophisticated state apparatus capable of marshaling tens of thousands of laborers, architects, and engineers. Recent archaeological findings suggest that the workers were skilled laborers, not slaves, housed in a purpose-built settlement near the construction site. The precision of the pyramid's alignment with the cardinal directions -- accurate to within a fraction of a degree -- speaks to the Egyptians' advanced understanding of astronomy and geometry.
