Johannes Gutenberg's development of movable type printing in Europe transformed the production of books from a painstaking manual craft to a scalable mechanical process. The first major work printed, the Gutenberg Bible, demonstrated the technology's potential to produce high-quality texts at unprecedented speed.

<!-- more -->

The printing press is often called the most important invention of the second millennium. Within fifty years of Gutenberg's innovation, an estimated twenty million volumes had been printed in Europe. The resulting democratization of knowledge fueled the Renaissance, enabled the Reformation, and accelerated scientific progress. For the first time, ideas could spread faster than any authority could suppress them, fundamentally altering the balance of intellectual power in European society.
