The storming of the Bastille on July 14, 1789, became the defining symbol of the French Revolution -- a movement that overthrew the monarchy, declared the rights of man, and sought to rebuild society on principles of liberty, equality, and fraternity.

<!-- more -->

The French Revolution's impact extended far beyond France. Its ideas about popular sovereignty, constitutional government, and individual rights spread across Europe and the Americas, inspiring both democratic movements and conservative backlashes. The revolution also demonstrated the volatility of radical political change, as it moved through phases of reform, terror, and ultimately Napoleonic dictatorship. Its legacy remains contested, but its role in shaping modern political thought is beyond dispute.
