According to tradition, Rome was founded by Romulus on the Palatine Hill. Whether rooted in myth or historical memory, this date marks the beginning of a political entity that would grow to dominate the Mediterranean world and shape Western civilization for millennia.

<!-- more -->

Rome's evolution from a small settlement of huts on the Tiber to the capital of a vast empire is one of history's most remarkable trajectories. The Roman Republic's institutions -- the Senate, legal codes, the concept of citizenship -- laid groundwork for political thought that endures today. Roman engineering, from aqueducts to roads, created infrastructure that literally connected the ancient world and remained in use for centuries after the empire's fall.
