The dwarven clans are among the oldest of the settled races, predating the Great Migration of humans by several ages. Their society is organized around hereditary lines and sworn craft-bonds, with most dwarves tracing their identity through both blood-kin and the workshop where they earned their first mark.

## Characteristics

*   **Longevity:** A dwarf typically lives between three and four centuries, with master smiths and lorekeepers occasionally reaching five.
*   **Patience:** A dwarven project measured in decades is considered ordinary. Their architecture and metalwork reflect this temporal scale.
*   **Oath-Binding:** Verbal contracts carry the same weight as written ones, enforced by clan honor and a strict tradition of arbitration.

### Clan Structure

Each clan is led by a council of three: the eldest smith, the eldest miner, and the eldest scribe. Decisions affecting trade or warfare require the consent of all three. This balance has held for over two thousand years, though its origins are disputed by the historians of the Stone Library.
