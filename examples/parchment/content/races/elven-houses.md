The elven houses are not clans in the dwarven sense. They are philosophical traditions, each centered on a particular relationship between elven life and the wider world. A child of one house may, upon reaching adulthood, petition to join another, though such transitions are rare and require formal sponsorship.

## Characteristics

*   **Lifespan:** Elves commonly live between seven and nine centuries. The oldest among them remember the founding of the first human kingdoms as a recent event.
*   **Memory:** Elven oral tradition is exact. A spoken account passed through three generations is considered as reliable as a written record.
*   **Reserve:** Elves are slow to form bonds with shorter-lived races, not from disdain but from grief. Most learn to manage this distance over their first century.

### The Three Houses

The elven population is divided among three principal houses, with smaller houses existing as offshoots or minority traditions in remote regions.

*   **House of the Listening Wood:** Stewards of forest territories, focused on the preservation of pre-settlement ecologies.
*   **House of the Quiet Tide:** Coastal scholars and cartographers, responsible for most charted sea routes.
*   **House of the Last Lamp:** Archivists and observers, maintaining the central libraries and the long records of magical phenomena.

> "We do not forget. That is both our gift and the burden we carry across the centuries."
