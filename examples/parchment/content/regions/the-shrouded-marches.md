The **Shrouded Marches** form a long crescent of low country between the northern kingdoms and the elven forest territories. The land is poorly mapped, partly because of the persistent fog that rises from its peat bogs and partly because the boundary stones that mark its political divisions have been moved, removed, or replaced more than once in living memory.

## Geography

The Marches sit on a bed of compressed peat several meters deep, which retains heat and produces a near-constant ground-level mist from late autumn through early spring. The terrain is otherwise flat, broken only by occasional outcrops of weathered granite.

*   **The Greyfen:** The largest of the bog regions, considered impassable to wagons.
*   **The Standing Stones:** A network of boundary markers, many predating the current kingdoms.
*   **Lowmoor Crossing:** The only reliable trade route, maintained by a joint warden council.

### Points of Interest

| Location | Significance |
| :--- | :--- |
