The **Iron Coast** runs along the northeastern edge of the continent, a line of dark basalt cliffs broken at intervals by deep, sheltered harbors. The region takes its name from the iron-rich ore exposed in the cliff faces, which has supported a chain of mining and shipbuilding settlements for nearly six centuries.

## Geography

The cliffs rise between forty and ninety meters above the waterline. Behind them, the land slopes gradually upward into the foothills of the Granite Range. The harbors, formed by ancient glacial activity, are unusually deep and can accommodate the largest trading vessels.

*   **Blackrock Bay:** The principal harbor, home to the largest shipyard on the continent.
*   **The Ore Roads:** A network of cliff-side rail tracks used to transport iron from the mines to the docks.
*   **The Foundry Ward:** A district of forges and refineries that operates continuously through the working season.

### Points of Interest

| Location | Significance |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Lighthouse of the Long Watch** | The oldest standing structure on the coast, in continuous use since its construction. |
| **The Shipwright's Hall** | A guildhall and training ground for naval engineers from across the kingdoms. |
| **The Salt Caves** | Sea-carved hollows used for the long-term storage of cured fish and preserved goods. |
