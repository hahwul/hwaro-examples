## What's In This Issue

This issue of PULP FICTION is packed with more thrills than any magazine has a right to offer at this price. We do not know how we continue to do it, and, frankly, we do not ask. Our reader has paid ten cents, has got seven full-length yarns, four novellas, and three poems. The reader is ahead and so are we.

<h4>She Knew Too Much</h4>
<p class="story-by">by Oreyna Clemens</p>

## Editor's Page

<blockquote>
<p>Our reader has, on occasion, written in to ask why we print only the kind of story we print. The answer, plainly, is that the reader has continued to buy the magazine. When the reader stops buying, we will print something else. Until then we will print what sells.</p>

<p>This month's cover yarn, <em>She Knew Too Much</em> by Oreyna Clemens, is her sixth for us and, I think, her strongest. Clemens writes at a pace that most of our authors envy and most of our readers require. Her typewriter, she tells me, is a 1938 Royal, and she refuses to type anywhere but a drugstore counter, which is a habit I approve of in writers and disapprove of in husbands.</p>

<p>-- The Editor.</p>
</blockquote>
