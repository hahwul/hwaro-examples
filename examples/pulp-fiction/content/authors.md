## The New Voice

This month we introduce one new writer, Lucia Suare, whose three poems appear under the heading "Rhymes for May." Suare was born in Havana in 1926 and emigrated in 1949. Her work has previously appeared in the Spanish-language press and in a small quarterly out of Miami. Her contribution to these pages is her first appearance in a mass-market English-language magazine, and we hope it will not be her last.

## The Vanished

Every issue of PULP FICTION carries a small note about writers who have disappeared from our pages and whom we would like back. This month we list four.

| Name | Last Appearance | Last Heard From |
|---|---|---|
| Viola Rennick | June 1948 | Cleveland, 1949 |
| Owen Capdeville | March 1950 | New Orleans, 1951 |
| Patience Ling | September 1950 | Seattle, 1951 |
| Arnold Straub | January 1951 | not known |

If you are one of these writers, or if you know one of them, please write to the editor at the masthead address. We have cheques waiting and a standing offer of a cover slot.

*Viola Rennick has, since this column went to press, returned her acceptance of our standing offer. Look for her new novelette in the June issue.*

## How To Sell To This Magazine

We buy full-length yarns of between 4,000 and 6,000 words at the rate of one cent a word, paid on acceptance; novellas of between 10,000 and 14,000 words at the rate of one and a quarter cents a word, paid on publication; poems at a flat rate of ten dollars each, regardless of length, paid on acceptance.

We do not buy work that has been published elsewhere, in any language. We do not buy work that was first submitted to another magazine and rejected by them. We do buy work that has been previously written for another magazine and subsequently heavily revised, provided the revision is the author's and the previous publisher has released the work in writing.
