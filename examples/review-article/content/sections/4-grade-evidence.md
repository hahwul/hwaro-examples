## Rationale for Downgrading

### Inconsistency (all outcomes)

All outcomes showed substantial heterogeneity (I-squared 55-72 pct), downgrading certainty by one level. Heterogeneity was partially explained by comparator type (waitlist vs. active control) and therapist involvement, but residual heterogeneity remained after subgroup analysis.

### Risk of bias (daytime functioning)

Four of ten studies measuring daytime functioning had high risk of bias in the outcome measurement domain (self-report questionnaires in unblinded participants), warranting a one-level downgrade.

### Imprecision (6-month follow-up)

The 95 pct confidence interval for the 6-month follow-up effect [-0.84, -0.44] crosses the threshold of clinical significance (SMD = -0.50), making it uncertain whether the long-term benefit is clinically meaningful.

## Publication Bias Assessment

Funnel plot inspection and Egger's test (p = 0.14) did not indicate significant publication bias for the primary outcome. The trim-and-fill method estimated zero missing studies. However, the power to detect publication bias is limited with k = 22 studies.
