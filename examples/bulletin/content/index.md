Every graphic design history worth telling twice starts in a filing cabinet nobody has opened since a foundry closed, a print shop moved, or an art director retired without leaving instructions. The Bulletin exists to open those cabinets.

We are not a magazine for finished stories. We are a circular — the format tradespeople have mailed each other for a century to flag something worth checking before the paper trail goes cold: a rejected specimen, an uncredited hand, a patent fight nobody remembers winning. Each dispatch below runs short, cites its source, and says exactly where in the archive to look if you want to keep pulling the thread yourself.

New bulletins are filed as the research clears. Browse the [full archive](bulletins/), read how the desk [checks a find](about/) before it runs, or start with the lead story below.
