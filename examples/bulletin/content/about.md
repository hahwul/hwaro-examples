The Bulletin is compiled by a rotating desk of design historians, archivists, and one very patient former paste-up artist, working from trade circulars, foundry correspondence, specimen books, and in-house newsletters that were never meant to outlive the offices that printed them.

## What counts as a find

We run short items on design history's overlooked margins: a process that lost its patent fight, a letterer who signed nothing, a grid nobody got to drive. A find has to clear three bars before it runs — a primary source we can name, a date we can defend within a year, and a reason the story still matters to anyone drawing letters today.

## How we cite

Every dispatch closes with an archive citation line: collection, box, and folder where we found the primary document, or the trade publication and issue where it ran. If a claim in a bulletin can't be traced to a citation, treat it as our error and write in — corrections run in the next issue, credited.

## Get in touch

Found a folder we should see? Researchers, foundry heirs, and print-shop estate sales are our best leads. Write to the desk with what you have, however partial. We answer everything, even the boxes that turn out to be nothing.
