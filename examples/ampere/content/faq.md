If your question is not answered here, it usually means we have not heard it
often enough yet to write it down. [Ask us directly](/about/) and we will add
the answer for the next person.

## Will it work during a power outage?

Yes. The base module includes an automatic transfer switch that islands your
home from the grid within a few hundred milliseconds of an outage starting —
fast enough that most electronics never notice. Which circuits stay powered
depends on how your electrician wired the backup panel during install; most
households back up the refrigerator, networking gear, and a few general
circuits, and size the stack to run those for a full day or more.

## How loud is it, really?

Under normal charge and discharge there is no audible fan noise — the module
cools passively through its rear fins. The base module's fan spins up only
under sustained peak output, which is uncommon in day-to-day use. A
four-module stack under typical evening load measures 30 dB at one metre,
which is quieter than a running refrigerator compressor.

## Does cold weather hurt performance?

Lithium iron phosphate cells lose some usable capacity in very cold
conditions, same as any battery chemistry, but the module's internal heater
keeps the cells within their optimal operating range down to outdoor
temperatures well below freezing. We rate the stack for continuous operation
from -20°C to 45°C, and installers in cold climates typically mount the
stack in a garage rather than fully exposed to wind chill.

## Can I add modules after the first install?

Yes, and this is the whole point of the design. The mounting rail installed
on day one is rated for a full six-module stack no matter how many you start
with, and the electrical service is sized the same way. Adding a module is
typically a sub-90-minute visit; see [installation](/installation/) for the
full sequence.

## What happens to a module at end of life?

LFP chemistry does not contain cobalt or nickel, which makes recycling both
simpler and less hazardous than older lithium chemistries. We operate a
take-back program: retired modules ship back to us in the original shipping
crate at no cost, and the cells are processed through a certified battery
recycler rather than landfilled.

## What is covered under warranty?

Ten years or 6,000 cycles, whichever comes first, on capacity retention down
to 80% of the module's original rating. The base module's inverter and
electronics carry the same ten-year term. Labor for a warranty repair is
included if the original installer performed the commissioning; otherwise a
standard service call fee applies.

## Do I need existing solar panels?

No. A stack charges from the grid on a time-of-use schedule just as
effectively as it does from solar, filling overnight when rates are cheapest
and discharging through the evening peak. Solar improves the economics
significantly, but it is not a requirement to benefit from the stack.
