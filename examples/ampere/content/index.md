Every home battery on the market is sized for someone else's evening. Ours is
sized for yours, because it is not one size at all — it is a stack, and you
decide how tall.

## Why we measure in evenings, not kilowatt-hours

A spec sheet full of kilowatt-hours tells you nothing about seven o'clock on a
Tuesday, when the stove is on, the dryer is running, and the sun that filled
your panels an hour ago is gone. So instead of leading with capacity, we model
the hours that actually cost you money — the ones between your solar dropping
off and the grid's cheapest rate window opening back up — and we size a stack
against that window specifically.

The number on our homepage, 87%, is not a marketing ceiling. It is the median
outcome across four-module stacks paired with a mid-size rooftop array in our
installed base, measured against each household's own evening load, not an
assumed one. Some homes clear 95%. A few, with unusually large evening loads,
land closer to 75%. We would rather publish the honest median than a cherry
picked best case, because you are going to live with the real number either
way.

That same honesty carries into how the hardware is built. A module is a
sealed, weatherproof unit you can lift into place without a crew, and the
electronics that meter, balance, and report on the stack live in the base
module so that adding capacity later is a mechanical job, not a rewiring one.
