A senior Trade Office official briefed correspondents Thursday on the quarterly trade review, confirming that tariff adjustments on three categories of imported goods will take effect on 01 June, pending the completion of bilateral consultations with two trading partners. The official characterised the adjustments as &ldquo;calibrated, reciprocal, and consistent with the existing framework agreements,&rdquo; and declined to characterise them as a departure from prior posture.

## Key Points

The official confirmed:

- **Tariff adjustments** on three categories &mdash; specialty steel, processed dairy, and two subcategories of semiconductor components &mdash; will take effect 01 June
- **Bilateral consultations** with the two affected trading partners are ongoing and are expected to conclude by the end of May
- **Compliance notifications** to the multilateral trade body will be filed by 20 April, well within the required 45-day window
- **Domestic industry impact assessments** have been completed for two of the three categories; the third is under review

The official emphasised that the adjustments are &ldquo;calibrated to specific market distortions&rdquo; rather than being broad-based, and that the Department does not anticipate a cascade of additional measures in the current quarter.

## On The Bilateral Consultations

Asked about the state of bilateral consultations with the two affected trading partners, the official offered that discussions are &ldquo;proceeding on schedule&rdquo; and that both partners had responded to the formal notification within the expected diplomatic timeframe. The official declined to characterise the substance of the discussions, citing the on-background rubric of the briefing.

> On background: the consultations are cordial, but the positions are not yet aligned. We expect a working compromise within the month.

This formulation &mdash; cordial consultations, misaligned positions, working compromise &mdash; is the standard Department readout for bilateral negotiations that are progressing but not yet resolved. Correspondents present interpreted it as a signal that no breakthrough is imminent but that no breakdown is expected either.

## On The Compliance Filings

The compliance notifications to the multilateral trade body will be filed under the standard Article 14(b) provisions, the official confirmed. These provisions require a detailed written justification for the tariff adjustment, including a market distortion analysis and an impact assessment on affected domestic industries. The Department has completed the market distortion analysis for all three categories and the impact assessment for two.

The third impact assessment &mdash; covering the semiconductor subcategories &mdash; is under review. The official indicated that the review is expected to be completed before the 20 April filing deadline, but that an extension remains a possibility.

## Expected Next Steps

Correspondents should expect:

1. A formal public statement from the Department no later than 16 April, timed to the lift of this embargo
2. A Congressional briefing to the relevant oversight committees, to be scheduled the week of 20 April
3. A press availability with the Trade Representative, potentially on the record, the week of 27 April

The Trade Representative has not committed to the press availability but has not ruled it out. Correspondents wishing to attend should submit accreditation requests through the standard channel by 24 April.
