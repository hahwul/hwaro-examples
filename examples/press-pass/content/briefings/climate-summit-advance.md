Climate Envoy K. Malherbe briefed correspondents Thursday on the agenda and principal positions heading into next week's multilateral climate summit in Tromso, confirming that the Envoy will lead the delegation and that the delegation's brief is to &ldquo;advance, not retreat&rdquo; on the existing commitments framework.

## The Agenda

The summit agenda is organised around four working tracks, each with a dedicated session and a working-group report due at the plenary:

1. **Mitigation commitments** &mdash; reviewing the national contribution schedules submitted under the last two summits and assessing compliance with the 2030 milestones
2. **Adaptation financing** &mdash; specifically the replenishment of the Climate Adaptation Fund, which is currently short of its 2026 funding target by an estimated 18%
3. **Loss and damage operationalisation** &mdash; moving the loss-and-damage facility agreed in principle three summits ago toward full operational status
4. **Technology transfer mechanisms** &mdash; a standing item, but with renewed attention given the expected debate over carbon capture technology licensing

## Principal Positions

The Envoy was explicit that the delegation's brief is to advance the existing framework:

> We are not here to re-litigate settled matters. We are here to move the framework forward, in particular on adaptation financing and on loss and damage, where operational progress has been slower than the political commitments would suggest.

Asked whether the delegation expected pushback on adaptation financing, the Envoy acknowledged that &ldquo;several delegations have signalled they wish to revisit the contribution formula,&rdquo; and that the delegation's position would be to &ldquo;listen carefully, explain our reasoning, and resist any formulation that reduces total committed capital.&rdquo;

## Specific Commitments Expected

The Envoy confirmed that the delegation will announce:

- A **contribution increase** to the Adaptation Fund of not less than 12% over the 2025 commitment
- A **new bilateral technology partnership** with two developing-country partners, to be announced at the summit's second plenary
- A **public commitment** to the loss-and-damage facility's operational target, subject to the facility's governance framework being agreed at the summit

The contribution increase will be funded from the Department's existing climate appropriation; the Envoy declined to specify the specific budget lines but confirmed that no new appropriations request is contemplated.

## Delegation Composition

The delegation will be led by the Envoy and will include:

- Two career diplomatic officers from the Climate Office
- A senior representative from the Treasury, attending in a technical capacity
- Two scientific advisers, one from the national meteorological service and one from the national laboratories
- A press officer (the Deputy Press Secretary for Climate)

Correspondents accredited to the summit may request bilateral interviews with delegation members through the summit press desk. The Envoy has committed to two press availabilities during the summit itself: one at the opening plenary and one at the closing plenary.

## Follow-Up

Questions are to be submitted through climate@press-pass.test. A follow-on briefing is expected the week following the summit, on or around 01 May, to review outcomes and answer remaining questions.
