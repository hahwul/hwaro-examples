The accreditation desk operates from Briefing Room 4B between 0700 and 0800 each Thursday. Correspondents wishing to attend the weekly briefing must present (a) a credential from their outlet dated within the current calendar year, (b) government-issued photographic identification, and (c) a completed accreditation request form submitted via the online portal no later than 1700 the preceding Wednesday.

## Who We Accredit

We accredit working journalists employed by or contracted to a recognised news outlet. Recognition is extended to outlets that (a) publish original reporting on a regular schedule, (b) employ at least one credentialled editor, and (c) have been in continuous operation for not less than six months.

We do not accredit:

- Advocacy organisations using journalistic format as a vehicle for campaign communications
- Individual bloggers without an editorial apparatus behind them (exceptions are made for established independent correspondents)
- Social media accounts operated as their sole publication channel (some exceptions)
- Representatives of state media operating under direct government editorial control

These are judgement calls, and they are adjudicated by the Accreditation Officer. Decisions may be appealed once, in writing, to the Press Secretary.

## Ground Rules

Every briefing is conducted under one of three rubrics, announced at the outset:

1. **On Record.** Everything said is quotable with full attribution to the speaker.
2. **On Background.** Quotable with attribution to &ldquo;Press Office sources&rdquo; or equivalent anonymous formulation.
3. **Off The Record.** Not for publication, not for attribution, not for circulation to colleagues.

We also use the hybrid rubric **Deep Background**, in which material may inform reporting but may not be quoted or attributed in any form. This is the default for material relating to classified operations or personnel matters.

## Embargoes

An embargo is a binding promise. When the Press Office releases a document, a readout, or an advance text under embargo, the correspondent agrees not to publish, broadcast, tweet, or privately circulate the material before the stated time. This is not a gentlemen's agreement; it is a condition of accreditation.

A confirmed embargo violation results in:

- Loss of the violating correspondent's accreditation for the remainder of the calendar year
- Review of the outlet's standing accreditation
- A public notice posted to the accreditation roster

Violations are rare, but they are treated seriously. Every correspondent accepting this accreditation accepts these terms.
