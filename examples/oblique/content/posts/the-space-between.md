Whitespace is misnamed. It is not white and it is not space. It is structure.

## Active and passive space

There are two kinds of whitespace. **Passive whitespace** is the default: the gaps left over when you place elements on a page. **Active whitespace** is intentional: the space you *choose* to leave empty.

The difference between amateur and professional design is almost entirely the difference between passive and active whitespace.

## What space communicates

Large space between elements says they are separate. Small space says they are related. No space says they are the same thing.

This is Gestalt proximity, and it is the most powerful layout tool available. More powerful than color. More powerful than size. More powerful than borders.

If two elements need a border between them to feel separate, they are too close together.

## Margins and meaning

On this page, the space above each heading is larger than the space below it. This is not aesthetic preference. It is semantic structure. The heading belongs to the content that follows it, not the content that precedes it.

That relationship is established entirely through space.

## The courage to leave empty

The hardest decision in design is to leave something empty. Clients want to fill. Stakeholders want to use every pixel. The instinct is always to add.

Resist.

Empty space is not wasted space. It is the space that makes everything else legible.
