# Exception Handling

**Document ID:** GOV-003 | **Effective Date:** 2025-09-01 | **Owner:** Chief Compliance Officer

**Classification:** Internal | **Review Cycle:** Annual | **Next Review:** 2026-09-01

---

## Article 1. Purpose

Personnel may, in limited circumstances, require relief from a specific policy requirement. This document establishes the formal process for requesting, evaluating, granting, and tracking such exceptions. No personnel may waive a policy informally.

## Article 2. Eligibility

An exception may be requested when one of the following conditions applies:

1. Strict compliance is technically infeasible within a defined timeframe
2. Compliance creates a greater risk than the requirement is intended to mitigate
3. A regulatory or contractual obligation conflicts with internal policy
4. A time-bounded business need outweighs the residual risk

## Article 3. Submission Requirements

The exception request shall include the requesting party, the specific policy clause, the business justification, the residual risk assessment, the compensating controls proposed, and the requested expiration date. Requests without compensating controls shall be returned without review.

## Article 4. Approval Authority

| Risk Level | Approver | Maximum Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Policy Owner | 6 months |
| Moderate | Department Head + Policy Owner | 6 months |
| High | Chief Compliance Officer | 3 months |
| Critical | Governance Committee | 1 month |

## Article 5. Tracking and Review

All approved exceptions are recorded in the central exception register. The register is reviewed monthly by the Office of Governance and Compliance. Expiring exceptions trigger an automatic notice to the requesting party 30 days in advance. Exceptions shall not be renewed indefinitely; a renewal exceeding the original duration constitutes a material change to the underlying policy.
