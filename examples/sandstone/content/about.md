## The Journal

Sandstone is an architecture journal devoted to buildings conceived in dialogue with arid landscapes. We document structures that draw their logic from the desert --- its materials, its light, its silence.

## Editorial Focus

Each featured work is presented with attention to siting, material origin, and climatic response. We record location coordinates and construction era alongside descriptions of how buildings negotiate extreme heat, scarce water, and vast horizons.

Our coverage spans ancient earthen citadels and contemporary desert pavilions alike. The common thread is an architecture that listens to its terrain rather than imposing upon it.

## Submissions

We welcome contributions from architects, photographers, and historians working in arid regions. Documentation should include high-quality photography, location data, and a written account of the design rationale.

## Contact

For inquiries or submissions, reach out through our editorial channels.
