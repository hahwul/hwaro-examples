Color is the soul of tropical design. The right palette can transport viewers to sun-drenched beaches and lush rainforests with a single glance.

## Warm Foundations

Every great tropical palette starts with warmth. Deep crimsons, fiery corals, and sunset oranges form the emotional core. These colors evoke passion, energy, and the heat of island life.

### The Hibiscus Spectrum

The hibiscus flower alone offers a masterclass in color harmony:

- **Deep Crimson** — The heart of the petal, rich and commanding
- **Hot Pink** — The outer edges, playful and energetic
- **Coral** — The transitional tone, warm and inviting
- **Gold** — The stamen, a touch of luxury and contrast

## Cool Accents

Tropical palettes need contrast to breathe. Ocean teals, leafy greens, and twilight purples provide visual relief and depth. These cooler tones prevent the warm colors from overwhelming the viewer.

## Practical Application

When building a tropical color scheme for the web:

1. Use warm colors for primary actions and focal points
2. Reserve cool tones for backgrounds and secondary elements
3. Let white or cream space balance the visual intensity
4. Test contrast ratios to maintain readability

The goal is not restraint — it's orchestrated abundance.
