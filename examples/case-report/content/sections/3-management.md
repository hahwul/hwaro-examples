## Emergency Department Management

Immediate interventions on ED arrival:

1. **Airway:** Rapid sequence intubation (RSI) for GCS 8 with ongoing seizure risk
2. **Hypertonic saline:** 100 mL 3% NaCl bolus over 10 minutes, repeated once after 20 minutes when Na+ remained at 114 mmol/L
3. **Potassium replacement:** 40 mEq KCl in 100 mL NS over 2 hours via central line
4. **Fluid restriction:** Strict nil by mouth; IV fluids limited to 3% NaCl and medication diluents only
5. **Seizure prophylaxis:** Levetiracetam 1500 mg IV loading dose

## ICU Management Protocol

The patient was transferred to the ICU 4 hours after ED arrival (Hour 14 from race start). At this point, Na+ had risen to 119 mmol/L following the two hypertonic saline boluses.

### Sodium Correction Protocol

The correction target was set at 10 mmol/L per 24-hour period, with an absolute ceiling of 12 mmol/L in any 24-hour window.

| Time Window | Starting Na+ | Target Na+ | Actual Na+ | Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| H14 - H24 | 119 | < 129 | 124 | 5 mmol/10h |
| H24 - H38 | 124 | < 134 | 128 | 4 mmol/14h |
| H38 - H48 | 128 | < 138 | 133 | 5 mmol/10h |
| H48 - H62 | 133 | < 143 | 136 | 3 mmol/14h |
| H62 - H72 | 136 | Maintenance | 140 | 4 mmol/10h |

### Desmopressin Administration

Between hours 14-20, sodium rose from 119 to 125 mmol/L (6 mmol/L in 6 hours) -- exceeding the target rate. A single dose of desmopressin 2 mcg IV was administered at hour 20 to transiently reduce free water clearance and slow the correction rate. Sodium subsequently stabilised at 124 mmol/L for 4 hours before resuming a controlled upward trajectory.

### Ventilator Management

The patient was ventilated on volume-controlled mode with the following settings:
- Tidal volume: 480 mL (6 mL/kg IBW)
- PEEP: 5 cmH2O
- FiO2: 0.3
- RR: 14

Sedation was weaned on day 2 as mental status improved. The patient passed a spontaneous breathing trial on day 3 and was extubated without complication.
