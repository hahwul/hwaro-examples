## Hospital Course Summary

The patient's hospital course was characterised by steady improvement following controlled sodium correction:

- **Day 1-2:** Intubated, sedated. Na+ corrected from 114 to 128 mmol/L.
- **Day 3:** Sedation weaned. Patient alert and oriented. Extubated. Na+ 136 mmol/L.
- **Day 4-5:** Mobilising with physiotherapy. CK trending down (1,800 to 680 U/L). Creatinine normalised.
- **Day 6:** Neurological assessment: no focal deficits. MMSE 29/30 (1 point lost on delayed recall).
- **Day 7:** Discharged home in good condition. Na+ 141 mmol/L.

## Neurological Outcome

Detailed neurological assessment on day 6 revealed:

| Domain | Assessment | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Consciousness | GCS | 15/15 |
| Cognition | MMSE | 29/30 |
| Motor | Upper and lower limb power | 5/5 bilaterally |
| Sensation | Light touch, proprioception | Intact |
| Cerebellar | Finger-nose, heel-shin | Normal |
| Cranial nerves | CN I-XII | Intact |
| Reflexes | Deep tendon reflexes | 2+ symmetric |

No evidence of osmotic demyelination syndrome (ODS) was found. An MRI brain performed on day 5 showed no signal abnormality in the pons, thalami, or cerebral white matter.

## Follow-Up Plan

The patient was discharged with the following plan:

1. **Outpatient neurology review** at 4 weeks with repeat MRI if any new neurological symptoms
2. **Sports medicine consultation** before return to endurance training
3. **Hydration education:** Referral to the hospital's sports dietitian for individualised fluid and electrolyte planning
4. **Exercise restriction:** No endurance exercise exceeding 2 hours for a minimum of 3 months
5. **Repeat bloodwork:** Electrolytes and renal function at 2 weeks

## Three-Month Follow-Up

At 3-month follow-up, the patient reported:
- Complete return to pre-event cognitive baseline
- No residual neurological symptoms
- Gradual return to running (up to 90-minute sessions without incident)
- Adopted a sodium-containing drink protocol and thirst-guided hydration strategy
- Repeat MRI brain: normal
