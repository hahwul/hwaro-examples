The Loki project began as an internal repository of diagnostic logs and chaos testing scripts compiled during enterprise red-teaming engagements. Over time, the authors recognized that the techniques used to bypass traditional network boundaries and endpoint detection systems were rarely documented in a structured, analytical manner. Loki was established to fill this gap, offering a weekly, technical catalog of evasion mechanics and chaos simulation protocols.

Our team consists of network security engineers, kernel engineers, and system operators who believe in the power of open-source diagnostic tooling. By researching bypass methods, we are able to engineer more robust firewall configurations, custom intrusion detection signatures, and hardened kernel parameters. Each issue published in our dispatches includes a step-by-step reproduction guide, a discussion of the underlying OS or protocol mechanism being targeted, and a concrete list of defensive remediations. We invite security researchers, systems administrators, and curious operators to review our archives, download our sample test vectors, and contribute to the collective understanding of modern defensive engineering.
