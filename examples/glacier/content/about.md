## About Glacier

Glacier is a space for thinking clearly about software. It exists because writing forces precision, and precision is the foundation of good engineering.

The posts here cover the things I care about most: readable code, thoughtful system design, the tools we use daily, and the quiet discipline of debugging. None of it is flashy. All of it matters.

## Philosophy

Software engineering is a craft. Like any craft, it rewards patience, attention to detail, and a willingness to revisit fundamentals. The industry moves fast, but the principles that make systems reliable and code maintainable change slowly, if at all.

This blog leans toward substance over novelty. You will not find breathless coverage of the latest framework here. Instead, you will find considered writing about the decisions that compound over a career: how to name things well, when to choose boring technology, why plain text endures.

## Colophon

This site is built with [Hwaro](https://github.com/hahwul/hwaro), a fast static site generator written in Crystal. The design is intentionally minimal -- monospace typography, maximum whitespace, no distractions. Content is written in Markdown and version-controlled in Git.

The color palette draws from ice and open sky: cool whites, silver borders, and a single accent blue. There are no animations, no tracking scripts, no cookie banners. Just text.
