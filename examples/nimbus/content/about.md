Nimbus started as a shared notebook between two flying-club members trading text messages before every weekend flight: one of us would paste a raw METAR, the other would translate it, and we would argue about whether the ceiling was really going to hold. After a hundred of those exchanges we realized the translations were the useful part, so we started writing them down.

The site is aimed squarely at student and private pilots flying light single-engine aircraft in day VFR and early instrument training. If you have ever stared at `BKN008` and felt your stomach drop without quite knowing why, these briefings are for you.

## How to read a briefing

Each entry leads with the raw observation in a mono capsule, followed by a one-line plain-English decode. The body then walks the report group by group and, more importantly, translates it into decisions: go, no-go, or "call the briefer and think harder." The [full logbook](@/briefings/_index.md) is the place to start.

## The standing disclaimer

Nothing here is an official weather source. The reports quoted are illustrative examples chosen to teach a concept, not live data. Before every flight, get a standard briefing from an approved source, check NOTAMs, and make your own go/no-go call. The pilot in command is always the final authority &mdash; this site is just a friend who reads the code out loud.

## Get in touch

Spot an error in a decode, or have a report that confused you? Write to the club at `briefings@nimbus.example` and we will work it into a future entry.
