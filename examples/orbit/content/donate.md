Orbit carries no ads, sells no data, and takes no payment for placement. That means the entire operation — hosting, tooling, and a small stipend for the forty-two curators who do the actual reading — runs on what readers and listed site owners choose to give.

## What it costs

Running a search index by hand is cheap on servers and expensive on time. Hosting the index and search infrastructure runs a little over two hundred dollars a month. The rest of the budget — the overwhelming majority of it — goes toward curator stipends, because reading sixty pages a week closely enough to vouch for them is real work and we don't expect the desk to do it for free forever.

## Where a contribution goes

- **Passerby — $3/month.** Covers roughly one curator-hour of review time across the desk each month. Gets you a quiet thank-you in the footer, nothing more.
- **Regular — $8/month.** Our most common tier. Funds the twelve-month re-check cycle that keeps stale listings from lingering in the index.
- **Desk Patron — $25/month.** Sponsors a curator's monthly stipend directly. Patrons at this tier can suggest — never dictate — a category the desk should spend more attention on.

## What your money does not buy

No tier moves a listing up, gets a rejected submission a second look outside the normal queue, or grants access to data about other users' searches — we don't collect that data in the first place. Donating supports the desk; it does not change what the desk decides.

## If you can't give money

Two things help just as much: pointing us to a small, well-made page we might have missed, and telling other people who miss the old web that Orbit exists. Both cost nothing and both genuinely move the index forward.
