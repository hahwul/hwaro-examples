During a commercial transit flight across the northern air corridor, multiple cockpit crews reported a giant delta-shaped craft hovering silently at approximately fifty thousand feet. The encounter was documented by both optical cameras and thermal imaging systems on board two independent airliners.

<!-- more -->

The craft possessed a dark, non-reflective surface and carried three faint amber lights positioned at the vertices of its triangular frame.

According to flight log data, the object remained stationary despite strong high-altitude jet streams, exhibiting aerodynamic stability that cannot be achieved by standard lighter-than-air platforms or rotorcraft. When a military patrol interceptor approached the sector, the craft rotated slowly on its vertical axis before accelerating vertically at an estimated speed of Mach twelve.

No sonic boom was recorded on ground-based seismic monitors, indicating that the craft employed a propulsion mechanism that suppresses atmospheric pressure waves. Visual logs confirm that the flight envelope of the object did not correspond to any known domestic or foreign drone test programs, leading investigators to classify the contact as anomalous. The optical signatures are currently being analyzed by the joint sensor validation group.
