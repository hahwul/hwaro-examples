Early warning satellite arrays detected a series of high-velocity transits in low Earth orbit that do not conform to known orbital trajectories or debris decay vectors. The contacts were first recorded by infrared monitoring sensors as localized thermal signatures traveling at speeds exceeding twenty-five thousand miles per hour.

<!-- more -->

Ground-based radar tracking facilities subsequently verified the satellite telemetry logs, establishing active tracks on three independent anomalous bodies.

The anomalous objects performed complex orbital adjustments and altitude changes without displaying any visible rocket plume or cold-gas thruster emissions. Space command analysts confirmed that the maneuvers required significant delta-v changes that would completely exhaust the propellant tanks of standard orbital satellites or upper-stage boosters.

Additionally, the objects maintained a tight, triangular formation during these orbital shifts, maintaining a relative distance of exactly one point five kilometers between each unit. Attempts to establish active optical tracks using telescope arrays were unsuccessful due to the objects' extremely low radar cross-section and light-absorbent exterior coating. Telemetry files have been archived under the low Earth orbit monitoring project for long-term orbital decay analysis.
