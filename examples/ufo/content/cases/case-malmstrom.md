Security personnel at a strategic launch facility reported a glowing red disc-shaped object hovering directly above the primary security fence. During the visual encounter, launch control facility operators experienced a sudden, unexplained deactivation of all ten ICBM guidance systems, placing the missiles in a disabled status.

<!-- more -->

Backup generators failed to activate automatically, leaving the launch control capsule operating on emergency battery cells.

Security patrols dispatched to investigate the perimeter reported that their vehicle engines and radio transmitters failed as they approached the launch silos. The object remained stationary in the facility's airspace for approximately fifteen minutes, illuminated by a faint crimson light that did not register on standard thermal imaging sensors.

Immediately upon the object's vertical ascent and departure from the sector, the launch controllers regained control over the guidance systems, and all silo power feeds returned to normal operational limits. A joint engineering investigation later examined the facility's power lines, battery banks, and guidance computer systems, finding no signs of physical tampering, hardware component failures, or power grid surges. The incident logs have been archived under restricted category six files due to national defense implications.
