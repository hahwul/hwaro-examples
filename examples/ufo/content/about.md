The Unified Aerial Phenomena (UAP) Declassified Archive Registry was established in response to Section 102 of the Federal Disclosure Act, which mandates the systematic public release of sensor data, cockpit logs, and radar logs related to anomalous encounters. Our mission is to provide researchers, aerospace engineers, and the general public with verified data points collected by national defense installations and scientific survey networks.

Prior to this registry, documentation of unexplained aerial activities was fragmented across multiple jurisdictions, often locked behind security classifications. By coordinating with the Joint Analysis Directorate, this registry gathers once-restricted records into a central, unified index.

Every case file in this registry undergoes a rigorous validation process. Telemetry files, radar returns, and visual logs are scrutinized to eliminate known terrestrial explanation models, such as commercial aviation transponder echoes, weather balloons, high-altitude research probes, or satellite flares. Cases that cannot be resolved through these standard models are logged as anomalous and assigned a unique classification identifier (e.g. FILE-51).

To ensure complete transparency, files include exact geo-spatial coordinates, sensor configurations, and witness testimony excerpts, with only highly sensitive hardware signatures redacted. The archive continues to update as older directories are processed and cleared for public custody.
