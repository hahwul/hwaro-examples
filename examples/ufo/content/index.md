Welcome to the Unified Aerial Phenomena (UAP) Declassified Archive Registry. This system serves as a public repository for declassified logs, official investigation records, sensor calibration charts, and radar metadata compiled by military and civilian intelligence organizations. Following the directives of the disclosure act, these records have been sanitized to protect sensitive military hardware specifications and observer identities while maintaining the integrity of the data.

The database is structured as a searchable secure wiki. Researchers may explore case files chronologically or query logs by geographical classification tags (e.g. marine, airspace, orbital). Each entry represents a verified incident where military or certified civilian sensors recorded kinematics or optical phenomena that cannot be attributed to conventional aircraft, meteorological events, or known satellite arrays. We encourage academic researchers and systems engineers to review these records, verify sensor telemetry, and cross-reference geographic tags with public transponder networks. All documents are presented in their declassified typewriter stencils, preserving original intelligence headers, classifications, and case log timestamps.
