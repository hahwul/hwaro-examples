## THE FLASH START

Tunde Okonkwo is a reactor. In three of his last five finals he has been the first out of the blocks, and the first to 30 meters. The question with Okonkwo is never "will he start fast?" It is "can the others catch him?"

### Reaction Time

His career average reaction time is 0.128 seconds. For context, the rules consider anything under 0.100 a false start &mdash; the theoretical minimum for a human nervous system to process the gun and fire the muscle. Okonkwo runs close to that floor, legally, every race.

### The Drive Phase

From 0 to 40 meters he is the best in the world. He gets to top speed faster than anyone else in this final. The problem: at top speed he is a tenth of a second slower than Santos or Farrow.

### The Long Hundred

A hundred meters used to be a sprint. Now it is three sprints stitched together &mdash; the block clearance, the drive, the finish. Okonkwo owns the first two. Tonight he has to find the third.
