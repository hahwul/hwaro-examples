date = "2025-03-20"
+++

Building an accessible web is a shared responsibility. It ensures that our digital creations can be experienced and navigated by everyone, regardless of their abilities or the devices they use. Accessibility should not be an afterthought or a compliance checkbox; it should be integrated into the design and development process from the very beginning.

This means using semantic HTML, providing clear alt text for images, and ensuring sufficient color contrast. It also involves testing our interfaces with keyboard navigation and screen readers. When we prioritize accessibility, we create a more robust and inclusive web that benefits all users. Let's commit to building products that are truly usable by all. Empathy is the cornerstone of accessible design, requiring us to understand and accommodate diverse user needs and contexts.
