# Galerie Steinbach *—*

A complete identity and signage system for a private contemporary art gallery occupying a converted nineteenth-century bank in Berlin-Mitte.

## Brief

The client asked for an identity that would sit comfortably against the vaulted ceilings of the main hall without looking apologetic about the building or dismissive of the art it contains. Our brief was to produce a system that held its own at thirty meters, and disappeared at three.

## Response

A wordmark cut from a single geometric sans, locked to a palette of charcoal on paper-white with a single brass line drawn under the name. Signage is milled in reclaimed oak from the bank's original counters and finished with an unlacquered brass inlay. Nothing shines. Nothing rusts.

## Outcome

The gallery has held eight exhibitions under the system. It has been photographed for two architectural quarterlies. The brass has begun to tarnish, which is exactly what we wanted.
