# Atlas Press Annual *—*

An editorial and cover system for a two-hundred-and-eighty-page hardbound annual, printed in two inks on two papers and bound in linen.

## Brief

The publisher wanted a book that an educated reader would keep on a shelf for ten years. The brief placed no constraint on format, palette, or printing method &mdash; only that it must last.

## Response

A two-ink system: charcoal for type, brass-gold for the folio numbering and a single ornament at chapter breaks. The text paper is an off-white mould-made stock from a mill in Fabriano; the cover papers are a coated bone-white for the jacket and a raw linen for the case. Binding is Smyth-sewn. The spine is exposed in one signature to show the stitching.

## Outcome

The annual has won two editorial design awards and has been adopted by a major metropolitan library as a reference work. The second edition is in preparation.
