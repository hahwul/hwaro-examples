# Kore Flagship *—*

A flagship retail fitout for a fragrance house, with wayfinding and a typographic mural across the mezzanine of a newly restored trading house.

## Brief

The client wanted a space that felt like a library, not a perfume counter. The architectural brief had already committed to a double-height void above the entry; our job was to design the wayfinding, the mural, and the small print that tied them to the product line.

## Response

Wayfinding is cut from a single weight of a custom display face, engraved into the existing plaster walls and filled with brass. The mural is a six-meter typographic quotation from the founder, set solid, in a single line, broken by three painted circles the diameter of the largest bottle in the range. The circles are the only chroma in the space.

## Outcome

The store opened in autumn. Walking past it at night, with the lights down, you can read the mural from across the street. We consider this a success.
