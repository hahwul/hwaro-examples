Lyrid is small on purpose. The whole festival is capped at two hundred people so the benches stay quiet and the presses stay free. Passes are sold in a single tier — no early-bird tiers, no VIP nonsense — and bench reservations clear in the order payments land.

## Passes

| Pass | Price | Covers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Weekend | €140 | Both days, all halls, one reserved bench per day |
| Single day | €85 | One day, all halls, one reserved bench |
| Student | €70 | Full weekend; bring a valid card to the door |
| Open studio only | €25 | Sunday drop-in press room, no reserved bench |

Every pass includes the opening lecture, the closing broadside, courtyard coffee, and a printed specimen of the festival face, **Lyrid Grotesk**.

## How bench reservations work

The full-day and half-day benches seat twelve. When you buy a weekend or single-day pass you pick one reserved bench per day from whatever is still open. The [Letterpress Open Studio](/workshops/letterpress-open-studio/) is drop-in and never needs a reservation — it is included with every pass except, obviously, the ones that already are it.

1. Choose your pass and pay.
2. On the confirmation screen, pick a bench for each festival day.
3. You get a printable ticket with your bench, hall, and time.

Read the [schedule](/schedule/) first — benches run in parallel and you can only be in one hall at a time.

## The fine print

- **Refunds** up to 1 April 2026, minus a €10 handling fee. After that, passes are transferable but not refundable — send us the new name.
- **Waitlist.** Sold-out benches open a waitlist; we release no-shows fifteen minutes after each session starts.
- **Access needs.** Tell us on the registration form and we will sort seating, drawers, and the quiet room before you arrive. See the [venue](/venue/) page for step-free routes.
- **Under 16s** are welcome in the open studio with an adult, free of charge.

Registration opens **1 February 2026**. Until then, hold the date — 25–26 April, Antwerp — and decide which letter you want to cut.
