The one session with no roster and no clock: the press room stays open for six hours and you set as much or as little as you like. Compositors from the studio stand at every case to talk you through the drawer, the stick, and the quoins. If you have never touched metal type, you will pull a printed line within the hour.

<!-- more -->

## How a drop-in works

Take a composing stick, pick a case, and set a line — a name, a motto, a bad pun, whatever earns the effort of reversing every letter in your head. We help you space it, lock it into the chase with furniture and quoins, and ink up a Vandercook SP-15. You crank the bed, and the impression is yours to keep.

## The rules of the room

- **Mind the pied type.** Drop a sort, pick it up; a spilled case ruins someone's afternoon.
- **Ink is permanent.** Aprons are provided; wear sleeves you do not love.
- **One forme at a time on each press.** Two presses run in parallel, so queues stay short.

## Take home

Every proof you pull, plus a broadside the studio prints live through the day: a specimen of the festival face, **Lyrid Grotesk**, set and locked in front of you. No booking required — but the room is happiest with fewer than twenty hands at once, so the [schedule](/schedule/) lists the quiet hours.
