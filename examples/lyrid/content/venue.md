Lyrid takes over **De Zetterij**, a former newspaper composing house on the edge of the Antwerp docklands. It has been a working type studio since 2014: two press halls, a matrix library, a small forge, and the smell of machine oil that no amount of ventilation will ever fully clear. It is the right room for this and there is no other like it in the city.

## Getting there

> De Zetterij · Napelsstraat 118 · 2000 Antwerpen · Belgium

- **By tram.** Lines 7 and 24 stop at *Kattendijkdok*, a four-minute walk. Turn toward the water; the building is the brick one with the cast-iron doors.
- **On foot.** Fifteen minutes north of Antwerpen-Centraal along the Italiëlei. Flat the whole way.
- **By bike.** Racks along the quay; the studio has a floor pump if a tyre gives out on the cobbles.
- **By car.** Don't, if you can help it. The nearest paid garage is *Kattendijk P+R*, then a ten-minute walk.

## Inside the building

Hall A holds the punchcutting benches and the forge; Hall B holds the presses and the clinic tables. The matrix library on the mezzanine is open to browse between sessions — please handle the drawers with the gloves provided.

## Access

The ground floor, both halls, the courtyard, and the accessible toilet are step-free. The matrix library is up **one flight of stairs with no lift** — if you would like a compositor to bring specific drawers down to the ground floor, email ahead and we will set them out for you.

## Food and rest

The courtyard bar serves coffee, bread, and Antwerp's stubbornly good beer all weekend. There is a quiet room off Hall B with soft chairs and no machinery, for anyone who needs the ink and the noise to stop for a while. Once you have your bearings, check the [schedule](/schedule/) and [register](/register/) for a bench.
