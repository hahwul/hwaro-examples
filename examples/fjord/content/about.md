## About This Journal

Fjord is a travel journal dedicated to the landscapes of the North. It documents journeys through Norway's fjords, Iceland's volcanic coasts, the Faroe Islands' sea cliffs, and the Arctic wilderness beyond the treeline.

The writing here is slow and observational. Each entry is a record of a particular place at a particular time -- the quality of light on still water, the sound of wind across a glacier, the weight of silence in a valley that has not changed in ten thousand years.

### The Name

A fjord is a long, narrow inlet carved by glacial erosion, where the sea reaches deep into the mountains. The word comes from Old Norse and describes a landscape shaped by immense patience -- ice moving through stone over millennia. This journal aspires to a fraction of that patience.

### Colophon

This site is built with [Hwaro](https://hwaro.hahwul.com), a fast static site generator written in Crystal. The design uses a cold blue and white palette inspired by Nordic winter light, paired with clean sans-serif typography in the Scandinavian tradition.

The layout favors open space and restraint -- qualities borrowed from the landscapes it describes.
