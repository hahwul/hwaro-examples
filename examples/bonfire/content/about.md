## What Is Bonfire?

Bonfire is a place for stories that take their time. In the tradition of campfire storytelling --- where the only audience is the night sky and whoever stayed awake long enough to listen --- this site is dedicated to long-form tales, quiet reflections, and the kind of writing that asks you to slow down.

There are no notifications here. No algorithms deciding what you should read next. Just words, arranged with care, meant to be read the way you might listen to someone speak across a low fire: patiently, and with the understanding that the best parts often come near the end.

## What You Will Find

The stories here draw from the landscapes and silences of the outdoors. Mountains, rivers, forests, rain. But they are not adventure writing in the conventional sense. They are closer to meditation --- attempts to describe what happens inside a person when they stop moving long enough to notice the world around them.

Some pieces are narrative. Some are reflective essays. Occasionally you will find something that sits between the two, the way a campfire story often does: starting as fact and drifting, through the telling, into something closer to meaning.

## The Philosophy

We believe that storytelling is an act of generosity. The teller gives their time and attention. The listener gives their patience and trust. Something is exchanged that neither could have produced alone.

The best stories are not the loudest. They are the ones that stay with you after the fire has burned down to embers and everyone else has gone to sleep.

> "We tell ourselves stories in order to live."
>
> --- Joan Didion
