Meridian existed to be read from a moving car. Everything on this shelf is loud,
opaque, and unapologetic, because a window card that whispers is a window card
nobody buys. The founder, a former circus-route letterer, ran the shop like a
midway: heat up front, one cool color to keep the eye from burning out, and a
black outline hit to hold the whole thing together at forty miles an hour.

<!-- more -->

The signature move was the outline last. Where most shops laid black first and
color inside it, Meridian flooded the brights, let them dry proud, then dropped
Carbon Line on top as a keyline. It made the tangerine look like it was lit from
behind. The trade-off was registration hell, and spoiled proofs from the shop
survive in stacks, each one a lesson in how far a poster can slip before the
color stops singing.

Show Green is the only mixed color on the plate, and the reconstruction pins its
formula from a surviving fair banner:

```ink
Show Green — matched to 1958 county-fair banner
  Sunflower Bold ....... 6 parts
  Boulevard Blue ....... 2 parts
  transparent white .... 1 part
knife-mix; check against wet draw-down, not dry
```

The gamut has no subtle tints, no tint base, no grey. Meridian did not believe
in grey. Meridian believed you had about two seconds, and grey wasted both.
