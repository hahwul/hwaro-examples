Each plate in the collection reconstructs the working ink shelf of a single
mid-century print shop, drawn from order books, spoiled proofs, and the greasy
thumbprints left on a pressman's swatch cards. None of these shops existed. All
of them could have. What survives here is the gamut: the finite set of pigments
a room full of people reached for, day after day, to make the world look
printed.

Swatches carry the ink name the shop used on its own tins, the closest modern
hex we could match under daylight-balanced light, and a mixing note pulled from
the reconstructed formula book. Hover any plate to fan its chips open. New to the
collection? [Read how the reconstructions work](/about/) first.
