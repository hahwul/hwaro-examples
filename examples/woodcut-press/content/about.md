## About This Press

**Woodcut Press** is a journal kept by hand and pulled from the block. Everything here is a *relief* matter: ink rides only on the surfaces left standing after the rest of the block is cut away. What you read on the page is the part the gouge did **not** touch.

The discipline of this place is deliberately narrow. Two inks, no more: a heavy carbon black and a single spot of vermilion. The paper is oatmeal &mdash; soft, toothy, never bright white. Headlines are set in wood type, tall and slab-footed, because a relief block wants a bold face that can carry a thick coat of ink without filling in.

> "Carve away everything that is not the picture. What remains will print."

## About the Printer

I am a jobbing relief printmaker. I cut blocks of pearwood, lime, and battleship linoleum; I ink them with a hand brayer; I pull prints on a small platen press and, when the press is cold, with the back of a wooden spoon. I keep this journal the way a shop keeps a proof book &mdash; a record of what worked at the bench and what tore the paper.

I write about:

- **The block** &mdash; choosing the wood, the linoleum, the plank versus the end grain
- **The cut** &mdash; gouges, V-tools, the direction of the grain, carving against it
- **The press** &mdash; bench hooks, brayers, registration, the pressure of the pull
- **The edition** &mdash; why a relief print is a multiple and not a copy

Nothing here is reproduced. Every figure on the site is drawn the way a block is cut: solid black shape, paper-colored cuts, one strike of vermilion for the catchword.

## The House Rules

1. Two inks. Carbon black and vermilion. Never a third.
2. The paper stays oatmeal. No bright white.
3. Bold shapes carry the ink. Fine lines fill in and fail.
4. The mark of the tool is the point, not a defect to hide.
