## About Tesseract

Tesseract is a mathematical education platform dedicated to making four-dimensional geometry accessible and intuitive. We use interactive visualizations, rigorous mathematical notation, and step-by-step explanations to bridge the gap between abstract higher-dimensional mathematics and human spatial intuition.

### Our Mission

The human brain evolved to navigate three-dimensional space. We can see width, height, and depth. But mathematics tells us there is no reason to stop at three dimensions. The fourth spatial dimension -- and indeed the nth dimension -- follows the same geometric rules we already understand.

Our goal is to build that understanding systematically:

- **Analogy**: Just as a cube is to a square, a tesseract is to a cube. We use dimensional analogy as the primary teaching tool.
- **Projection**: We cannot see 4D directly, but we can project it. The same way a 3D cube casts a 2D shadow, a 4D tesseract casts a 3D "shadow."
- **Rotation**: In 4D, objects rotate in planes, not around axes. Understanding the six rotation planes of 4D space unlocks deep geometric intuition.
- **Cross Sections**: Slicing a tesseract with a 3D hyperplane reveals surprisingly familiar 3D solids.

### Mathematical Foundation

The tesseract (also known as the 8-cell or regular octachoron) is the four-dimensional analog of the cube. It is defined as the convex hull of the 16 vertices at coordinates:

```
(+-1, +-1, +-1, +-1)
```

Its Schlafli symbol is {4, 3, 3}, indicating that it is composed of cubes ({4,3}) arranged with 3 meeting at each edge.

### The Platform

This site is built with Hwaro, a fast static site generator. The visualizations use pure CSS 3D transforms to approximate what four-dimensional projections look like when collapsed onto a two-dimensional screen. No JavaScript frameworks, no WebGL -- just mathematics expressed through CSS geometry.

### Key Properties of the Tesseract

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Vertices | 16 |
| Edges | 32 |
| Square faces | 24 |
| Cubic cells | 8 |
| Vertex figure | Tetrahedron |
| Dual polytope | 16-cell |
| Symmetry group | B4, order 384 |
