Step into a Rococo salon and you enter a world where every surface tells a story. Walls adorned with painted panels depicting pastoral scenes, ceilings that seem to dissolve into sky and cloud, mirrors that multiply light and space infinitely—these were the hallmarks of 18th-century French interior design.

The Palace of Versailles and its surrounding châteaux became laboratories for this new aesthetic. Designers like Juste-Aurèle Meissonnier and Nicolas Pineau created interiors where architecture blurred into decoration, and decoration blurred into art.

### Light as Material

Perhaps the most revolutionary aspect of Rococo interiors was the treatment of light. Mirrors, crystal chandeliers, and pale-colored surfaces worked together to create rooms that seemed to glow from within.

This emphasis on luminosity reflected the period's philosophical optimism and its celebration of pleasure, beauty, and the art of living well.
