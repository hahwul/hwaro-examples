Welcome to my personal archive and project log. Here, I document my ongoing work at the intersection of forestry research and studio woodworking. This space serves as a dual-purpose repository: a place to share the empirical findings of my canopy and tree-ring studies, and a journal reflecting on the practical application of this knowledge in the woodshop. 

My research heavily informs my craft. Understanding the life cycle of a tree—its response to drought, its struggle for light, the slow accumulation of its rings—provides a profound context for the timber I select. Each log holds a record of the climate it endured, and this history is revealed as I work the wood with traditional hand tools. I prefer using hand planes, drawknives, and spokeshaves because they allow me to follow the grain, preserving the continuous fibers of the wood. This method not only honors the material but also results in furniture that is exceptionally strong, lightweight, and enduring. The pieces I create are designed for utility and permanence, reflecting the resilience of the trees themselves. I invite you to explore both the scientific observations and the crafted objects chronicled in these pages.
