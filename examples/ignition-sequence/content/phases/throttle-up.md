## Overview

THROTTLE UP is the final decision gate. Engines advance from 60% to 100% rated thrust. The launch director conducts the final GO/NO-GO poll across all stations. There is no turning back after throttle-up commit.

## GO/NO-GO Poll Stations

Each station must independently confirm GO status:

- **BOOSTER** -- Engine cluster performance within limits
- **GUIDANCE** -- Navigation solution converged and stable
- **RANGE SAFETY** -- Flight termination system armed and tested
- **WEATHER** -- All launch criteria satisfied
- **TELEMETRY** -- Data links nominal, recording active
- **FLIGHT DYNAMICS** -- Trajectory prediction within corridor
- **LAUNCH DIRECTOR** -- Final authority for commit to launch

## Throttle Profile

The throttle-up sequence follows a prescribed profile to minimize structural loads:

- T-10:00 -- Begin ramp from 60% to 80%
- T-07:00 -- Hold at 80% for stability check
- T-05:00 -- Ramp from 80% to 100%
- T-03:00 -- Confirm 100% rated thrust on all engines
- T-01:00 -- Final systems snapshot and commit

## Duration

T-10:00 to T-00:00 -- The final 10 minutes before liftoff.
