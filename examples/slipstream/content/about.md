Slipstream started in a garage in Denver in 2023, out of a specific frustration:
the fastest road bikes were priced as if only professionals rode fast. The three
of us raced Category 2 and 3 crits on weekends and paid for our own entries,
travel, and gear. We could read a wind-tunnel report. What we could not do was
find a company willing to show us one.

## The founders

**Mara Voss** spent nine years as an aerodynamicist in motorsport before she got
tired of secret data. **Deniz Aktaş** built carbon layup schedules for a
frame factory in Taichung and wanted to design the bikes, not just make them.
**Ford Whitlock** ran a bike shop for a decade and knew exactly how much of a
retail price is badge and margin. We pooled our savings, booked off-season tunnel
time when it is cheapest, and committed to one rule: publish every number.

## What "direct" actually means

Skipping the dealer network and the pro team lets us spend on the frame instead of
the story around it. A WorldTour sponsorship can cost more than a small factory's
annual output. We would rather put that money into another tunnel session and a
lower price. It also means we answer our own email — the person who tuned your
frame's drag is the person who replies when you have a question.

## Where we're going

We are a small company and intend to stay one. The next frame in development is a
gravel-adjacent all-road bike, tested to the same protocol you can read on our
[wind-tunnel report](/wind-tunnel/). We will not launch it until the data is worth
publishing. In the meantime, the three frames in [the lineup](/bikes/) are the
honest best of what we know how to build. Come ride one.
