Three frames, one design language, one honest promise: the drag number you see
is the drag number we measured. Each bike is sold as a frameset or in two build
kits. Every complete build ships with a Slipstream cockpit already dialed to the
fit numbers you enter at checkout.

## Draft — entry aero

The frame that started the company. A hydroformed 6069 alloy chassis with truncated
airfoil tubes borrowed almost line-for-line from the carbon bikes above it. At a
measured **0.221 m² CdA** and **8.6 kg** in a complete Rival build, the Draft is
faster at 40&nbsp;km/h than carbon aero bikes that cost three times as much and were
never put in a tunnel.

- **Frameset:** $2,900 · **Rival AXS build:** $4,100
- Tire clearance 32&nbsp;mm · fully internal cabling · threaded bottom bracket
- Sizes 49–61, six options, real fit range on the [geometry page](/geometry/)

## Echelon — all-round race

Our best-selling frame and the one most riders should buy. Full T700/T800 carbon
layup, a dropped seatstay junction, and a fork crown shaped to feed clean air to
the down tube. **0.204 m² CdA**, **8.1 kg** as built. The Echelon holds a
breakaway on the flat and still climbs with the group when the road tips up.

- **Frameset:** $3,800 · **Force AXS build:** $6,200
- 30&nbsp;mm clearance · integrated cockpit · UDH derailleur hanger
- Ships with a 45&nbsp;mm-deep alloy wheelset; upgrade to 50&nbsp;mm carbon at order

## Breakaway — crit weapon

The frame we take to the tunnel to prove a point. Every junction is optimized for
zero-yaw drag and shallow-yaw stability, because that is what a 45&nbsp;km/h field
sprint actually is. **0.191 m² CdA**, **7.5 kg**. It is the lowest drag figure we
have ever recorded on a road frame, and it is a production number, not a prototype.

- **Frameset:** $4,900 · **Force AXS build:** $7,400
- 28&nbsp;mm clearance · one-piece bar-stem · aero seatpost with reversible head
- Available in the raw unidirectional weave finish or asphalt matte
