## Same tunnel. Different math.

The aero road bikes at the front of a WorldTour race and the ones in your
Tuesday-night crit are shaped by the same physics. What separates them is
usually the price of the badge, not the speed of the frame. We build in the
GST Aeronautics tunnel outside Denver, run our sessions off-season when time
is cheap, and skip the team sponsorship line item entirely. The savings land
on your invoice, not a marketing budget.

Drag is the tax every rider pays above roughly 30&nbsp;km/h. At 45&nbsp;km/h it
is close to 90% of the total resistance you fight. Shaving 0.03 off a frame's
CdA is worth more to a solo breakaway than any amount of grams. So we optimized
for the number that actually decides races, published every run, and let the
weight fall where good engineering puts it.

## What we will and won't do

- **We publish raw drag data.** Every frame's CdA, the yaw sweep, the protocol,
  the date. See the [wind-tunnel report](/wind-tunnel/).
- **We fit you first.** [Geometry](/geometry/) is on the site before you ask;
  our stack-and-reach numbers are honest, not flattering.
- **We won't chase grams past the point of speed.** A 6.8&nbsp;kg superbike that
  is draggier than our Echelon is slower on every course that isn't a summit
  finish.
- **We sell direct.** No dealer margin, no demo-fleet tax. Find a
  [fit studio](/dealers/) near you and ride before you commit.
