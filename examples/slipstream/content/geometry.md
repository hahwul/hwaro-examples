Geometry decides whether a fast frame is fast for *you*. We publish full tables
for every model and size, measured on production frames, not rounded off a CAD
file. If your current bike fits, match its stack and reach here and you will feel
at home on day one.

## How to read this

**Stack** is the vertical distance from the bottom bracket to the top of the head
tube; it sets how high the bars can go. **Reach** is the horizontal distance to
the same point; it sets how long the bike feels. Get these two right and the rest
is spacers and stem length. Our fit tool at checkout converts your saddle height
and bar drop into a recommended size and cockpit spec.

## Race and endurance fit

Every frame ships in a **race** stack by default. Riders who want a taller front
end can order the same size in **endurance** trim, which adds a 15&nbsp;mm head-tube
extension and a shorter reach via a setback-neutral cockpit — no change to the
frame's handling, just a more sustainable position for long days. The Draft and
Breakaway share the Echelon's fit philosophy within a few millimetres per size;
their full tables load on each model's spec sheet under [the bikes](/bikes/).

Not sure where you land? Send us your current bike's stack and reach through the
fit form and we will confirm the size before you pay.
