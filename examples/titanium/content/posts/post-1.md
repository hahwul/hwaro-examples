Exploring high-precision milling techniques for rigid alloys. The new multi-axis CNC configuration allows for complex geometries while maintaining strict tolerances required for aerospace applications. Traditional machining methods often struggle with the extreme hardness and low thermal conductivity of titanium alloys, leading to rapid tool wear and compromised surface finishes. By implementing advanced toolpath generation algorithms and utilizing cryogenic cooling systems, we have significantly improved both the efficiency and the quality of the machining process. The new five-axis machines allow us to produce intricate components from a single solid billet, eliminating the need for complex assemblies and reducing the overall weight. This represents a major leap forward in our manufacturing capabilities, opening up new possibilities for structural design. We are currently evaluating the performance of custom carbide end mills with specialized coatings to further optimize the material removal rates. The preliminary results are highly promising, indicating a potential reduction in cycle times by up to thirty percent.
