## On-Air Standards

The foundational nature of real-time structural communication.

### Signal Integrity

Every transmission begins as a measurable property of the carrier. Loudness is held to a target of -23 LUFS for long-form material; momentary peaks are clipped at -1 dBTP at the inter-sample stage. Color signals are gamut-limited before any creative grade. The discipline is not aesthetic. It is the contract that lets one studio hand to the next without distortion.

### Continuity Discipline

A broadcast schedule is a binding promise. Each clock minute is reserved, logged, and reconciled against ad traffic, network feeds, and station-mandated slots. Drift of more than two seconds across a half-hour block triggers a written report. Continuity is the operational memory of the channel — without it, no listener can rely on what they hear at any given hour.

### Specification, Not Style

This site documents the standards by which on-air signal is produced. It does not prescribe taste. The grid you see, the monospaced type, the absence of color: each is a working surface, not a brand. The point is to make the rules legible, so anyone reading them can hold the next person who breaks them to account.
