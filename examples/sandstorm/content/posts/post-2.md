Sand dunes are not random piles of grains. They are precise mathematical structures shaped by fluid dynamics, particle physics, and geological time.

## Dune Morphology

Each dune type tells a story about its wind regime:

- **Barchan dunes** form under unidirectional wind with limited sand supply
- **Star dunes** emerge where winds blow from multiple directions
- **Linear dunes** align parallel to the prevailing wind
- **Transverse dunes** sit perpendicular to consistent winds

## The Grain Cascade

When wind pushes sand grains up the windward slope, they reach the crest and tumble down the lee side in a process called grain flow. This cascade maintains the dune's angle of repose -- typically between 30 and 34 degrees.

```
Wind direction -->

    /\
   /  \ angle of repose
  /    \  ~32 degrees
 /______\
```

The regularity of this angle across deserts worldwide speaks to a universal physical constraint governing granular materials.

## Sound of the Sands

Some dunes produce a low-frequency hum when sand avalanches down their faces. This "singing sand" phenomenon occurs when grains are uniform in size and the moisture content falls within a narrow range.

Researchers have measured these sounds at frequencies between 65 and 120 Hz -- deep enough to feel in your chest when standing on an active dune.

## Preservation and Change

Desert landscapes appear timeless but are in constant motion. A large star dune may migrate only centimeters per year, while a small barchan can travel 30 meters annually. Understanding these rates is critical for infrastructure planning in arid regions.
