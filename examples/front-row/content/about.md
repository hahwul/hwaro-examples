## A word on the front row

Front Row is a subscription of witness. It does not sell seats; it holds them. Each year, the house issues a small and deliberate number of membership contracts, and each contract reserves a particular vantage on the season &mdash; a geometry of proximity to the stage, to the directors, and to the other patrons who make up the small community we call, informally, the Circle.

This page sets out the standing rules. It is short on purpose.

## Membership

Membership is annual, opened each autumn for the following season. Admission to Tiers I through III is by application endorsed by two existing members. Tier IV &mdash; the Imperial Circle &mdash; is held by election of the board and cannot be applied for.

Membership cards are engraved and delivered by hand. A lost card is replaced once without ceremony; a second loss is noted.

## Reserved seating

Every member of the Circle has a reserved seat. The seat is yours for the season. It is not yours in the sense of private ownership, but in the older sense: it is kept for you, and it is not given to anyone else, even in your absence. If you cannot attend, the house asks only that you notify the concierge by noon on the day.

## Guests

One named guest per member per evening, unless the invitation specifies otherwise. Substitution is welcome by prior notice. A guest is bound by the same standards of attire and discretion as the member who brings them.

## Attire

House standards begin at six in the evening. For principal evenings: black tie. For the Annual Gala: white tie. For private previews and salons: smart. The house does not waive attire, and asks that members not press the matter.

## Photography and discretion

The house observes the older courtesies. Photography during performance is never permitted. Photography in the foyer, the garden, or the Verandah Room is by arrangement with the Artistic Director.

We ask &mdash; and we note, for the record, that this has never failed us &mdash; that members refrain from discussing the evening&rsquo;s company outside the company itself. The front row, like the salon, works only because the people in it trust one another to say nothing afterwards.

## Cancellation &amp; refund

A member may cancel membership at any time before the first principal evening of the season, for a refund of three quarters. After the first principal evening, membership is held for the full season. The house does not pro-rate.

## Bequest

A member may nominate a successor to take up the seat on their death or withdrawal. The successor is interviewed by the Artistic Director and, if the house agrees, the seat is continued without application. This is an old practice and accounts for roughly one in every seven memberships in the current register.

## Contact

For all matters, the concierge is available Monday to Friday, ten until eighteen, at `patrons@frontrow.example` or by telephone. In the evenings the concierge is at the Villa Cornelis itself, under the blue lamp by the garden door.
