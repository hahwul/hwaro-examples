## Tier IV &#183; Sovereign

The Imperial Circle is a single seat. It is held at the invitation of the Board of Patrons and cannot be applied for.

At any given time, the house may or may not have a Sovereign Patron. There have been ten Sovereigns in the history of the Circle, of whom three are living. A single seat is a deliberate architecture: the house considers that the Sovereign&rsquo;s privilege depends on its rarity.

## What the contract includes

- **A reserved seat** for every event in every season, in perpetuity for the term of election.
- **One named guest** per event, without restriction, with a further three seats held for the Sovereign&rsquo;s household by prior notice.
- **A reserved table** at the Verandah Room and a standing place at the Vermilion Salon.
- **Quarterly audiences** with the Artistic Director, by arrangement.
- **A bound chronicle** of the Sovereign&rsquo;s term, prepared by the house archivist and presented on the day of withdrawal.

## Election

The Sovereign is elected by a two-thirds majority of the Board of Patrons in private session. The election is not announced in advance; nor is the name of the Sovereign published outside the house register. The Sovereign&rsquo;s portrait is added to the principal foyer on the day of election and is covered with a cloth until the Sovereign attends the first principal evening of the following season.

## Term

The term is for the lifetime of the Sovereign, or until withdrawal. The house does not set a term of years. A Sovereign may withdraw at any time, on written notice to the Artistic Director, and is welcomed thereafter as a Vermilion member without further application.

## The seat itself

The Sovereign&rsquo;s seat is in the centre of the front row. It is marked, unobtrusively, by a narrow brass plaque inlaid into the armrest. The plaque bears no name, only the date 1878 &mdash; the year the seat was first held, by a young composer who later declined the next five invitations to take up the title and who is remembered as the first and, by some accounts, the truest Sovereign the Circle has known.
