# Glue Tabs and Anchor Points

<p class="lede">Every pop-up structure resolves, eventually, to a question of where the paper meets the page. A glue tab is the joint that converts the motion of the spread into the motion of the rising element.</p>

## Geometry of a tab

<p>A glue tab is a flap of paper folded perpendicular to the panel it supports. The fold is a single crease scored into the surface and bent through ninety degrees. The exposed face of the flap carries the adhesive. The opposing face becomes part of the panel itself. When the tab is pressed onto the page, the adhesive bonds the flap to the substrate, and the crease becomes a hinge.</p>

<p>The width of the tab determines how much shear the joint can absorb before tearing. A narrow tab concentrates stress along a short edge and fails first at its corners. A wider tab distributes the load across a longer line and tolerates repeated openings. Most production pop-ups specify a minimum tab width of twelve millimetres for cardstock between two hundred and three hundred grams per square metre.</p>

## Placement on the page

<p>Tab placement controls the angle and timing of the rise. A tab anchored close to the gutter receives a large rotational displacement when the spread opens, producing a tall, fast rise. A tab anchored further out receives less rotation but a longer linear pull, producing a wider, slower rise. The two tabs supporting a single panel must be placed on opposite sides of the gutter at matching distances, or the panel will twist instead of rising cleanly.</p>

## Adhesive selection

<p>Polyvinyl acetate dries clear, sets in roughly two minutes at room temperature, and remains slightly flexible after curing. It is the standard choice for hand-bound pop-ups. Hot-melt adhesives cure faster and are preferred for production runs, but they stiffen the joint and reduce its tolerance for repeated motion.</p>
