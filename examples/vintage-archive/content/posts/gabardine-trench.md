A magnificent example of mid-century outerwear design. Sourced from a textile estate in southern England, this heavy cotton-gabardine trench coat displays incredible resistance to wear and moisture.

### Historical Context
Developed originally for military utility, the double-breasted trench became a cornerstone of urban civilian elegance during the late 1960s and 1970s. The sharp storm flaps and epaulets offer a structured silhouette that remains timeless.
