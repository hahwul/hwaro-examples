<section class="plates-section">
  <h2 class="section-title">Plate Boundaries</h2>
  <p class="section-intro">The Earth's lithosphere is divided into tectonic plates that interact at their boundaries. These interactions drive earthquakes, volcanism, and mountain building.</p>
  <div class="plate-grid">
    <div class="plate">
      <div class="plate-header">
        <span class="plate-icon">[+]</span>
        <h3>Convergent Boundaries</h3>
      </div>
      <p>Where plates collide, one is forced beneath the other in a process called subduction. This builds volcanic arcs, deep ocean trenches, and towering mountain ranges like the Himalayas.</p>
      <span class="plate-tag">Compression</span>
    </div>
    <div class="plate">
      <div class="plate-header">
        <span class="plate-icon">[&lt;&gt;]</span>
        <h3>Divergent Boundaries</h3>
      </div>
      <p>Where plates pull apart, magma rises to fill the gap, creating new oceanic crust. The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is the most prominent example, splitting the Atlantic basin wider each year.</p>
      <span class="plate-tag">Extension</span>
    </div>
    <div class="plate">
      <div class="plate-header">
        <span class="plate-icon">[//]</span>
        <h3>Transform Faults</h3>
      </div>
      <p>Where plates slide laterally past one another, stress accumulates along strike-slip faults. The San Andreas Fault in California is the classic example of a continental transform boundary.</p>
      <span class="plate-tag">Shear</span>
    </div>
    <div class="plate">
      <div class="plate-header">
        <span class="plate-icon">[v]</span>
        <h3>Subduction Zones</h3>
      </div>
      <p>Dense oceanic plates descend into the mantle beneath lighter continental plates. This process recycles crustal material and generates the deepest earthquakes recorded on Earth.</p>
      <span class="plate-tag">Recycling</span>
    </div>
    <div class="plate">
      <div class="plate-header">
        <span class="plate-icon">[^]</span>
        <h3>Volcanic Activity</h3>
      </div>
      <p>Volcanism concentrates along plate boundaries and over mantle hotspots. The Pacific Ring of Fire accounts for roughly 75 percent of all active volcanoes on the planet.</p>
      <span class="plate-tag">Eruption</span>
    </div>
    <div class="plate">
      <div class="plate-header">
        <span class="plate-icon">[~]</span>
        <h3>Seismic Waves</h3>
      </div>
      <p>Earthquakes release energy as P-waves, S-waves, and surface waves. Studying their propagation through Earth's interior has revealed the structure of the core, mantle, and crust.</p>
      <span class="plate-tag">Propagation</span>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
