title = "The sixty-second cliff"
date = "2025-05-06"
description = "A look across every completion curve on Hover: the steepest drop-off almost always lands before the first minute is over."

We pulled completion curves for every episode published on Hover in the last twelve months -- a little over ninety thousand of them -- and lined them up on the same axis, time on the bottom, percent of audience remaining on the side. Almost every curve has the same feature near the start: a steep, fast drop in the first sixty seconds, followed by a much gentler slope for the rest of the runtime. We started calling it the cliff.

<!-- more -->

The size of the cliff varies by genre. Narrative and true-crime shows lose the least, typically four to seven percent, because their cold opens are usually a hook straight into the story. Interview shows lose the most, sometimes eighteen to twenty-two percent, and the pattern is almost always the same: a long stretch of thank-yous, sponsor mentions, and "so glad to have you on the show" before the actual conversation starts.

We do not think every show should cut its intro to nothing. Listeners who have been with a show for forty episodes often like the ritual of a familiar open -- cutting it can measurably hurt retention for that audience even as it helps new-listener conversion. The data argues for two different opens more than it argues for a short one.

What the cliff is genuinely useful for is catching drift. A show's cliff size is stable episode to episode until the format changes -- a new cold-open bit, a longer ad read moved earlier, a co-host talking over the hook. When we show a host their cliff size trending upward over three or four episodes in a row, it is almost always something they can point to and fix in the very next recording.

This is also the data behind cold-open scoring, which we shipped to the board as a permanent feature. Every new upload is measured against your own show's historical cliff the moment processing finishes, so you find out before the numbers do the finding out for you.
