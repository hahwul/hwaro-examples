## About Aquarium

Aquarium is a curated journal dedicated to the wonders of marine life. From the sunlit shallows of coral reefs to the crushing darkness of hadal trenches, we document the creatures that inhabit our planet's vast oceans.

Each entry in our species catalog is written with care, combining scientific rigor with a sense of wonder. Understanding the ocean's inhabitants is the first step toward protecting them.

### What You Will Find Here

**Species Profiles** --- Detailed accounts of marine organisms, including their taxonomy, habitat preferences, behavioral patterns, and conservation status. Each profile is organized by the depth zone and ecosystem where the species is most commonly encountered.

**Field Observations** --- Notes from underwater expeditions, aquarium research, and marine biology fieldwork. These observations capture behaviors and interactions that rarely make it into formal literature.

**Ecological Context** --- Every species exists within a web of relationships. We explore how organisms interact with their environment and each other, from symbiotic partnerships to predator-prey dynamics.

### Our Approach

We draw from peer-reviewed research, direct observation, and consultation with marine biologists worldwide. Scientific names follow the World Register of Marine Species (WoRMS) taxonomy. Depth ranges and habitat descriptions reflect current literature as of publication.

This is not an exhaustive database. It is a carefully selected collection, prioritizing depth of coverage over breadth.
