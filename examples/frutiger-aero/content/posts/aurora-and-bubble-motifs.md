The Frutiger Aero period is identifiable by a small set of recurring images: water droplets resting on a leaf, the curtain of an aurora borealis, soap bubbles drifting across a blue sky, and koi fish photographed from above. These motifs appeared on phone wallpapers, retail packaging, login screens, and printer boxes between roughly 2004 and 2011.

<!-- more -->

### Source Material

Most of the imagery came from royalty-free stock libraries that became commercially available alongside the rise of digital cameras. The same handful of photographers supplied the assets that shipped on consumer hardware around the world. A specific photo of a green leaf with a single water droplet appeared in product launches across at least three continents during 2007.

### Why These Subjects

The motifs were chosen because they share three useful properties for marketing imagery. They are immediately legible at small sizes. They communicate optimism without requiring cultural context. They photograph well against high-contrast backgrounds, which suited the saturated blue and green palette favored by the operating systems of the period.

Bubbles in particular paired well with the era's interface chrome. A floating bubble has a translucent body, a rim of refracted light, and a single specular highlight, the same components used to render Aero window borders. Software and photography reinforced each other.

### Disappearance

The motifs receded as the smartphone era matured. Mobile devices favored solid color wallpapers that did not compete with home screen icons. Marketing imagery shifted toward photographs of people using the products. The bubbles drifted off screen and have not returned.
