*Gathering* is the log of a single-handed passage made in June 2025 aboard *Selkie*, a thirty-two-foot cutter-rigged sloop built in 1988 and refitted, mostly by hand, over the two winters before the crossing. She carries a Hydrovane self-steering gear, sixty litres of diesel, a hand-cranked watermaker as backup to the electric one, and a paper chart for every leg of the route in addition to the plotter — a habit from a skipper who does not entirely trust anything that needs a battery.

## The route

Falmouth to Horta is one of the shorter classic Atlantic crossings, roughly twelve hundred nautical miles depending on how faithfully the wind allows a course to follow the great circle. It runs southwest across the shipping lanes off Ushant, out past the edge of the continental shelf into open water, through the belt of prevailing westerlies, across the eastern shoulder of the Azores High, and into Faial on the last of the trade-wind push before the islands' own local weather takes over. Most who sail it do so early in the season, before the high strengthens and the calms lengthen; this passage left on the third of June and made landfall twelve days later.

## Why alone

The honest answer is that a long solo passage was the only kind of quiet I had not yet tried, after some years of being fairly sure that quiet was what I was short of. It turned out to be true, though not in the way I expected — the passage was rarely silent and never idle, and the quiet it delivered was less an absence of noise than an absence of anyone else's opinion about what to do next. That is a specific kind of gift, and twelve days felt, in the end, like exactly the right length of time to receive it.
