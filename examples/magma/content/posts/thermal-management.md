Managing the immense heat generated by the active magma flow requires constant vigilance and robust protocols. Over the past month, we have implemented an updated thermal management system designed to automatically reroute excess heat away from critical infrastructure.

The new protocol leverages a network of secondary vents and liquid cooling loops that activate when ambient temperatures exceed 800 C. This system has already proven its worth during the recent spike in core temperature, successfully redistributing over 450 MW of thermal energy without any disruption to the primary power grid.

Additionally, our team has reinforced the structural supports around the main caldera observation deck with a new high-temperature alloy. We continue to prioritize safety while maximizing our data collection capabilities in these hostile environments.
