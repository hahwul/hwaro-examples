Color is the heartbeat of every Latin American celebration. From the neon pinks of a quinceañera to the deep marigold of Día de los Muertos, each hue carries meaning and emotion.

## The Psychology of Festival Colors

Latin American festivals don't follow the muted palettes of minimalist design. Instead, they embrace chromatic maximalism:

- **Magenta & Hot Pink** — Passion, femininity, and the vibrant bougainvillea
- **Marigold & Gold** — The sun, abundance, and the path between worlds
- **Teal & Turquoise** — Water, sky, and indigenous heritage
- **Coral & Sunset Orange** — Warmth, community, and shared meals

## Design Lessons

Modern designers can learn from these traditions. Bold color isn't chaos — it's intentional, cultural, and deeply communicative. The key is **contrast and rhythm**: bright colors sing louder when placed against warm neutrals.

## In Practice

This very theme draws from these principles. The palette isn't random — it's a curated celebration of the colors that make Latin culture so visually powerful.
