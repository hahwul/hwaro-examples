## Trial Design Overview

The CLARITY trial is a multi-site, parallel-group, single-blind randomized controlled trial comparing two frequencies of spaced retrieval testing against a matched re-study control in undergraduate introductory biology courses. The trial follows a superiority design with 1:1:1 allocation.

## Hypotheses

**Primary hypothesis (H1a):** Students in the weekly spaced testing arm (A) will achieve higher end-of-semester exam scores than re-study controls (C), with a minimum detectable effect size of Cohen's d = 0.30.

**Primary hypothesis (H1b):** Students in the biweekly spaced testing arm (B) will achieve higher end-of-semester exam scores than re-study controls (C), with a minimum detectable effect size of Cohen's d = 0.25.

**Secondary hypothesis (H2):** Weekly testing (A) will produce greater gains on transfer tasks than biweekly testing (B), indicating a dose-response relationship for retrieval practice.

**Exploratory hypothesis (H3):** The testing effect will be moderated by baseline cognitive load capacity, such that students with lower working memory spans benefit more from weekly testing.

## Registration Details

The protocol was registered simultaneously on OSF (osf.io/clarity-2026) and ClinicalTrials.gov (NCT06841022). The registration includes the full statistical analysis plan, primary and secondary outcomes, sample size justification, and pre-specified subgroup analyses. The registration timestamp precedes any participant contact by at least 80 days, exceeding the minimum recommended interval of 30 days.
