<section class="abstract-box">
  <h2>Abstract</h2>
  <dl>
    <dt>Background</dt>
    <dd>Spaced retrieval practice has shown robust effects on long-term retention in laboratory settings, yet evidence from large-scale classroom implementations in STEM disciplines remains limited. The CLARITY trial addresses this gap with a pre-registered, multi-site randomized controlled design.</dd>
    <dt>Objectives</dt>
    <dd>Primary: determine whether a 12-week spaced testing intervention improves end-of-semester exam performance relative to re-study controls. Secondary: assess effects on transfer tasks, examine dose-response across testing frequencies, and measure cognitive load via dual-task reaction time.</dd>
    <dt>Design</dt>
    <dd>Multi-site, parallel-group, single-blind RCT with 1:1:1 allocation to spaced testing (weekly), spaced testing (biweekly), or matched re-study control. Stratified randomization by site and prior GPA quartile.</dd>
    <dt>Participants</dt>
    <dd>N = 960 undergraduate students (320 per arm) enrolled in introductory biology courses across 4 university sites. Inclusion: age 18-25, enrolled full-time, no prior completion of the target course.</dd>
    <dt>Primary outcome</dt>
    <dd>Standardized end-of-semester exam score (cumulative, 120 items). Analysis: mixed-effects linear model with site as random intercept, arm and stratification factors as fixed effects. Alpha = 0.025 (Bonferroni-corrected for two primary comparisons).</dd>
    <dt>Keywords</dt>
    <dd><em>spaced retrieval; testing effect; cognitive load; RCT protocol; STEM education; pre-registration</em></dd>
  </dl>
</section>

## Structure of the Protocol

1. **Section 1. Study Design and Objectives** -- trial design, hypotheses, PICO framework, and study registration details
2. **Section 2. Participants and Randomization** -- eligibility criteria, recruitment, stratified randomization, and allocation concealment
3. **Section 3. Intervention and Control** -- spaced testing protocol, re-study control, materials, and fidelity monitoring
4. **Section 4. Outcomes and Analysis Plan** -- primary and secondary endpoints, statistical models, and sensitivity analyses
5. **Section 5. Data Collection Workflow** -- assessment schedule, data management, quality assurance, and stopping rules
