## Amendment 1: v2.0 to v2.1

<div class="milestone-box">
  <p class="milestone-label">Amendment Filed</p>
  <p class="milestone-date">28 January 2026 -- IRB amendment ref. 2026-0112</p>
</div>

### Change description

The secondary endpoint measurement window for cognitive load (dual-task reaction time) was adjusted from week 6 to week 8 for the mid-semester assessment point.

### Justification

Pilot data (N = 48, single site) revealed that the week 6 cognitive load assessment fell during a university-wide examination period at two of the four sites. This created a confound between intervention-related cognitive load and exogenous testing stress. Moving the assessment to week 8 places it in a period free from external examinations at all four sites.

### Impact on primary outcome

**None.** The primary outcome (end-of-semester exam score, week 14) and its analysis plan are unchanged. The primary statistical model, sample size, randomization procedure, and stopping rules are identical to v2.0.

### Impact on secondary outcomes

The cognitive load trajectory analysis now measures at weeks 4, 8, and 12 (previously 4, 6, 12). The 4-week spacing between the first two measurements (previously 2 weeks) provides a more balanced temporal distribution but reduces sensitivity to early-stage cognitive load changes.

### Approval chain

1. Steering committee approved: 20 January 2026
2. IRB amendment submitted: 22 January 2026
3. IRB amendment approved: 28 January 2026
4. OSF registration updated: 28 January 2026
5. ClinicalTrials.gov record updated: 30 January 2026

## Planned vs. Actual Deviations

No unplanned deviations from the registered protocol have occurred as of the publication date of this manuscript. All changes from v2.0 to v2.1 were made during the pilot phase, before main trial enrollment opened on 3 February 2026.
