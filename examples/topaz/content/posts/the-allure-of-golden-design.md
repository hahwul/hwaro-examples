Gold has captivated humanity for millennia. In web design, it carries the same weight — conveying luxury, warmth, and timeless appeal.

## Why Gold Works

The golden spectrum sits at the intersection of warmth and brilliance. Unlike cool tones that recede, gold advances toward the viewer, demanding attention without aggression.

Key principles for using gold in digital design:

- **Restraint creates impact** — Use gold as an accent, not a flood
- **Contrast is essential** — Pair gold with deep, dark backgrounds for maximum effect
- **Gradients add dimension** — Linear and radial gradients simulate the way light plays across metal surfaces
- **Typography matters** — Gold works best with serif and display typefaces that have enough weight to carry the color

## The Topaz Palette

This theme draws from the topaz gemstone's natural color range:

1. Pale champagne for highlights
2. Warm honey for primary accents
3. Rich amber for interactive elements
4. Deep bronze for shadows and depth

The result is a palette that feels both luxurious and organic — like sunlight filtering through crystal.
