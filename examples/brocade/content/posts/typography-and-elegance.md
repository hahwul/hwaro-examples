Typography is the voice of design. The right typeface pairing can transform a simple layout into something that feels curated, polished, and luxurious.

## Choosing Display Fonts

For headlines and titles, serif fonts with high contrast between thick and thin strokes convey elegance:

- **Playfair Display** — Dramatic contrast, perfect for editorial luxury
- **Cormorant Garamond** — Refined and classical
- **Libre Baskerville** — Warm and authoritative

## Body Text Considerations

Luxury doesn't mean sacrificing readability. Body text should be comfortable to read at length:

- Use a generous line-height (1.6–1.8)
- Keep line lengths between 50–75 characters
- Choose a font size of at least 16px for body text
- Pair a clean sans-serif or humanist serif with your display font

## The Role of Weight and Spacing

Letter-spacing and font weight create hierarchy without shouting. A light uppercase heading with generous tracking feels refined, while bold condensed text feels energetic.

```css
.elegant-heading {
  font-family: 'Playfair Display', serif;
  font-weight: 700;
  letter-spacing: 0.02em;
  line-height: 1.2;
}

.refined-label {
  font-family: 'Cormorant Garamond', serif;
  font-weight: 300;
  letter-spacing: 0.15em;
  text-transform: uppercase;
}
```

## Hierarchy Through Restraint

The most elegant typographic systems use just two typefaces — one for display and one for body. More than three fonts creates visual chaos. Let spacing, size, and weight do the work of differentiation.
