We built Traction by writing down everything our own three-person consultancy actually opened in a working week, then refusing to build anything past that list. That sounds like a limitation, and it is — deliberately. Every CRM we tried before this one started simple and grew a permissions system, a workflow builder, and a marketplace of third-party apps, because that's what happens when a product has to justify a bigger price tier every year. Traction doesn't have tiers to justify — see [the only plan](/pricing/) for proof. It has one job: hold your pipeline, your contacts, and your invoices in a place two founders can actually keep in their heads, and get out of the way for everything else.
