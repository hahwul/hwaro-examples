Every CRM we cancelled had a pricing page with a "most popular" ribbon on the middle tier, a price that quietly assumed we'd add fields we never planned to use, and an enterprise column with no number on it. We priced Traction the way we wished someone had priced something for us: one number, one plan, and it doesn't move whether you're a solo consultant or a team of eight sharing one flat fee. There's no per-contact metering that punishes a good month, no seat count to true up when you hire, and no "contact sales" column standing in for a number they'd rather negotiate in private. If the price changes for existing customers, which it hasn't since launch, we'll email you first and honor your current rate for a year. Want the full list of what's included first?
