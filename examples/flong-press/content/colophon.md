## About this publication

This publication was composed using Hwaro, a static site generator. Its dark, industrial design reflects the material culture of the stereotype foundry -- the heat of molten metal, the weight of lead alloy, and the reversed impressions of the flong matrix.

## Typography

**Display faces.** Chapter openers use heavy reversed (mirrored) type rendered in SVG to evoke the flong impression, where all text appears backwards as a negative mold of the original type. Display headings are set in Roboto Slab at heavy weight with wide letter-spacing, conveying the industrial weight of cast metal type.

**Body text.** The running text is set in Roboto Slab and Zilla Slab -- clean, geometric slab serifs that echo the precision of cast type emerging from the matrix mold. Both faces were designed for clarity at various sizes, and their mechanical regularity reflects the reproducibility that stereotyping brought to printing.

## Visual design

SVG flong/matrix mold impression patterns appear in reverse throughout the publication, simulating the negative impressions that the papier-mache flong captures from the type forme. SVG type casting diagrams illustrate the three-stage stereotype process -- from composed type through flong impression to cast metal plate.

The dark color palette (deep charcoal, brass gold, warm grays) evokes the foundry environment. No CSS gradients are used. All decorative elements are produced with inline SVG.
