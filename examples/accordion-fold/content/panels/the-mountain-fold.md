## Peak

A mountain fold is a fold where the crease rises toward the reader. Seen from the side,
the paper resembles an inverted V: two panels meeting at a ridge. The mountain fold is the
positive half of the concertina; each mountain must be followed, in the alternating
sequence, by a valley.

> Where the mountain rises, the next panel must fall.

## The Acute Angle

The working angle of a well-made mountain fold is between seventy and ninety degrees. A
fold more acute than sixty degrees will, over time, crack the fibres at the ridge; a fold
less acute than one hundred and ten will not hold.

## Reading the Mountain Panel

The mountain panel is typically the first to meet the reader's eye when the concertina is
partially extended. For this reason it carries the burden of the opening image: the
figure, the numeral, the device which, in a codex, would sit on the frontispiece.

In the current number, the mountain panels carry diagrams of the fold mechanism itself
&#8212; a small recursion which the editor found impossible to resist.
