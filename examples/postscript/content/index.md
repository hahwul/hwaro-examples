Postscript is written for the reader every other finance letter forgets: someone whose income arrives in commissions, print sales, and grant deposits instead of a biweekly direct deposit. Every issue is built around one real figure, worked out with an actual artist's ledger, printed old-style and boxed like the entry it is.

There is no six-month emergency fund advice sized for a salary you don't have, no tax guidance that assumes a W-2, and no pretending that a grant behaves like a paycheck. Just the arithmetic that irregular income actually requires, checked against real numbers before it runs.
