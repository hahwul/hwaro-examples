Ask ten working artists how they track money and you'll get ten answers, most of them some version of "I check the account balance and hope." That's not a ledger, it's a mood. A ledger answers a specific question — what came in, what went out, and what kind of money was each — in a way you can look back on in April and actually trust.

The system we're recommending this issue isn't ours; it's adapted from a studio-management course a stained-glass artist named Teo Bakhtiari has taught out of his workshop for six years. It fits on one spreadsheet tab and takes about ten minutes a week to maintain.

<!-- more -->

**The four columns are date, amount, category, and running balance.** The category column is where the system earns its keep — every entry gets one of five tags: `commission`, `sale`, `grant`, `expense`, or `owed`, the last one for invoices sent but not yet paid, which live in their own sub-total so they stop quietly inflating your sense of what you have.

A week of Teo's actual ledger looks like this:

```
DATE        AMOUNT   CATEGORY     RUNNING BALANCE
2025-10-06  +450.00  commission   2,140.00
2025-10-08  -62.00   expense      2,078.00
2025-10-09  +180.00  sale         2,258.00
2025-10-11  +0.00    owed:600     2,258.00
2025-10-14  -900.00  expense      1,358.00
```

That fourth line is the one most systems skip. A $600 invoice went out on October 11th; nothing changed hands, so the running balance doesn't move, but the `owed` tag means Teo can pull a total of everything unpaid with one filter, instead of reconstructing it from memory during a slow month when it matters most.

**Reconcile weekly, not monthly.** A month is long enough for a discrepancy to become un-diagnosable — you'll find a $340 gap and have no idea if it's a missed sale, an untagged expense, or a typo from three weeks back. A Friday-afternoon, ten-minute pass through bank and cash transactions catches the error while you still remember the context.

**What this buys you that a bank balance doesn't:** a category breakdown by quarter, which is the input every other issue of this letter assumes you have. The buffer target in [Issue No. 34](/issues/slow-month-fund/), the tax set-aside in [Issue No. 37](/issues/quarterly-taxes-without-a-w2/), and the rate floor in [Issue No. 38](/issues/pricing-your-time/) all start from "what did commission income actually total, separate from grants and sales" — a question your bank app cannot answer and your ledger can, in seconds, if you've kept the four columns honestly.
