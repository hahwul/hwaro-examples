Postscript is a letter about money for people who make things for a living and get paid the way artists get paid — in commissions, sales, grants, and the occasional check that arrives eleven weeks after the invoice. It runs roughly every six weeks and it is written by Odile Marsh, a former grants administrator who spent nine years reading other people's budgets before deciding the more useful version of that job was writing budgets down where working artists could read them too.

Every issue is built around one figure — a dollar amount, a percentage, a day count — pulled from a real ledger, rounded honestly, and printed old-style in a ruled box on the cover. That figure is never a benchmark to hit; it's a worked example, usually from an artist who shared real numbers so the arithmetic would be legible instead of theoretical. Names are changed on request. The math is not.

## What this letter is not

It is not accounting advice for your specific situation, tax law for your specific jurisdiction, or a substitute for a bookkeeper once your practice outgrows a spreadsheet. It is a set of habits and a way of thinking about irregular income that most financial writing, built for a reader with a salary, simply doesn't cover. When a term needs a jurisdiction-specific answer, the issue says so and stops there.

## How issues get made

Each issue starts with a conversation — usually with an artist who has already solved, imperfectly, the problem the issue is about. We ask for real numbers, not averages, because averages hide the shape of the problem. A studio's actual worst three months are more useful than a composite "typical" month that never happened to anyone. Figures are checked against the artist's own ledger before publication, and where a method is offered — a buffer target, a set-aside percentage, a floor rate — it's one that's been run for at least a full quarter before we write it up.

## Reaching Postscript

This example letter does not collect email addresses or send anything — the subscribe coupon on the [homepage](/) is a static mock, part of the design, not a working form. If you're studying this site as a starting point for your own newsletter, wire the form action to whatever delivery service you use and it will behave like any other subscribe box.

For everything else — corrections, questions about a method, or an artist willing to share a real ledger for a future issue — the masthead lists no working contact address, in keeping with this being a demonstration site rather than an active publication.
