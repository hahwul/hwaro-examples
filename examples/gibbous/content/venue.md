Gibbous is online first. You never have to leave your desk to take part, and the whole jam runs through the community server so remote teams are first-class citizens. But if you are near Scotland the first weekend of June, there is a physical hub, and it is lovely.

## The Glasgow hub

We take over the **top floor of the Lighthouse**, just off Buchanan Street in the city centre. It is a bright, high-ceilinged room with a view over the rooftops that turns gold about an hour before the theme drops.

- **Address:** The Lighthouse, 11 Mitchell Lane, Glasgow G1 3NU
- **Open:** Friday 5 June from 15:00, continuously until Sunday 7 June at 20:00
- **Capacity:** 60 desks, first come first served through the [registration form](/register/)

<!-- more -->

## What is in the room

Power and gigabit wired ethernet at every desk. A wall of monitors you can borrow if you would rather not hunch over a laptop for two days. A quiet corner with bean bags and a strict no-keyboard rule for when your brain needs to lie down. A coffee urn that is refilled on the hour, every hour, by a rota of volunteers who take the job extremely seriously.

Food is not provided, but the hub sits three minutes from more lunch options than you can try in a weekend, and we keep a shared map of the late-night ones pinned by the door.

## Getting there

Queen Street and Central stations are both a seven-minute walk. If you are driving, the Cambridge Street multi-storey is the closest overnight car park; the room is not accessible for vehicles, so plan to carry your kit the last few blocks.

The building has step-free access via the lift from the Mitchell Lane entrance. If you have specific access needs, note them on the registration form or message the organizers, and we will sort it out before you arrive.

## Sleeping

The hub stays open all night, but it is not a place to sleep. There is no floor space for camping and the lights stay on. Most local teams go home to sleep and come back; visitors tend to book a cheap room nearby and treat the jam like a two-day festival. Whatever you do, sleep at least once. The Sunday builds always show who did.
