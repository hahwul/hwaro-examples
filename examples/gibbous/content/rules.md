Gibbous is a friendly jam, not a gauntlet. The rules exist to keep it fair and to keep it kind. If a rule ever seems to be getting in the way of someone having a good weekend, that is a bug, and you can report it to the organizers in the `#help` channel.

## The clock

The theme is revealed at **18:00 BST on Friday 5 June 2026**. From that moment you have exactly **48 hours**. Submissions close at **18:00 BST on Sunday 7 June**. There is a hard fifteen-minute grace window for upload gremlins, and after that the button goes away.

Everything that ships in your build must be made during those 48 hours: code, art, audio, level design, and copy. The two exceptions below are the only ones.

## What you may bring

- **Engines, libraries, and tools.** Godot, PICO-8, Phaser, Bitsy, your own framework from last year: all fine, as long as they existed before the jam.
- **Reusable snippets you wrote and published before the jam.** A tweening helper, a save-system template, your personal input wrapper. Link it in your submission notes.
- **Fonts, sound libraries, and asset packs that are openly licensed.** Credit them. If it is not clearly licensed for reuse, leave it out.

## Team size

One to four people. That is the whole rule. Solo entries compete on exactly the same footing as four-person teams, and historically solo builds win about as often as they lose. Find teammates in `#team-up` before Friday, or jam alone and sleep whenever you like.

## Scope, gently

You are not required to finish anything. A jam build is allowed to be a vertical slice, a toy, a single good idea with a start screen. The judges would rather play three tight minutes than wander through thirty broken ones.

A quick pre-submission checklist that has saved a lot of teams:

```text
[ ] Builds run on a fresh machine (no missing DLLs, no dev paths)
[ ] There is a way to quit, and a way to restart
[ ] Controls are shown on screen or in the description
[ ] Audio has a mute, or starts quiet
[ ] The download is under 300 MB
```

## Judging

Every playable submission is rated by fellow jammers across four axes: **Theme**, **Fun**, **Craft**, and **Moonshine** (our name for the one surprising spark). Scores are averaged, ties are broken by Theme. Top three by combined score take the crown; a separate community-vote ribbon runs alongside. There is no cash prize. There is a very nice enamel pin.
