## Purpose

Lab Notes is a research journal for documenting experiments, observations, and findings. Each entry follows a structured protocol format with clearly defined sections: hypothesis, method, results, and conclusion.

## Methodology

All experiments are recorded with the following metadata:

- **Experiment ID**: A unique identifier for cross-referencing
- **Date**: When the experiment was initiated
- **Status**: Current state (Completed, In Progress, or Failed)
- **Tags**: Subject classification for filtering and discovery

## Data Standards

Observations are recorded as close to real-time as possible. Data tables use consistent units and significant figures. Failed experiments are documented with the same rigor as successful ones -- negative results are still results.

## Tools

This journal is built with [Hwaro](https://github.com/hahwul/hwaro), a fast static site generator. The grid paper background and monospace typography are chosen to evoke the feel of a physical lab notebook while retaining the searchability and organization benefits of a digital system.
