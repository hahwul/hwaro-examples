## OPERATIONAL OVERVIEW

**REDACTED DOSSIER** is a repository of case files, intercepts, and field observations concerning the design of information systems. Topics include:

- The geometry of readable typefaces and why the government prefers them
- Classification systems, their visual grammar, and what they reveal about bureaucratic psychology
- Typography as a control mechanism: how fonts enforce authority
- The semiotics of the rubber stamp
- Information theory as practiced by records officers and graphic designers

All materials in this archive were generated through authorized surveillance of the design community. Sources have been protected. Methods are classified.

## REGARDING THE VISUAL PRESENTATION

> The appearance of this document is not incidental. Form communicates authority. A file that looks official is treated as official. This is the first lesson of information design.

The typeface is `Courier New`. This is intentional. The monospace character grid imposes discipline on text. Every letter occupies identical horizontal space, creating a visual equality that is, paradoxically, deeply hierarchical. The typewriter was a government instrument before it was a literary one.
