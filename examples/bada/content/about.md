I swim in Marn Sound, a shallow grey inlet on a northern coast where the sea never quite warms and never quite freezes. The swimming happens off a concrete slip below the old herring station — locals call it the ladder, after the three rusted rungs bolted to its seaward edge. I have been getting in year-round since the winter of 2023, and writing it down since the first January I stayed in past the gasp.

This site is the written part. Each entry is one swim: the date, the water temperature at entry, how long I stayed, and whatever the cold shook loose on the walk home. The essays are short because the swims are short. Nothing here is advice; it is a record — [the log starts here](@/swims/_index.md), newest swim first.

## The badges

Every swim carries a small round badge showing the water temperature in degrees Celsius, measured wrist-deep with a pool thermometer on a lanyard before I commit anything more important than a wrist. The badge's colour follows the water: near-black blue at two degrees, the pale blue of a kind sky at fifteen. Scan any list on this site and you can read the whole year as a slow tide of colour — dark, darker, briefly light, dark again.

## The rules I keep

- Never alone between November and March. Ede swims Wednesdays; Halvard watches from the slip the rest of the week with a flask and visible scepticism.
- One minute per degree, and I round down. The water decides, not the calendar.
- Out before the shiver, warm clothes in strict order, sugar within ten minutes.
- The sea is not a test. Some days the log entry is *walked to the ladder, touched the rail, went home* — and that counts.

*Bada* is written by one swimmer and read, as far as I can tell, by four. If you swim cold water too, the log is yours to borrow from — especially the part about rounding down.
