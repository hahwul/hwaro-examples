Fifteen point two this morning — the highest number this thermometer will read all year, and I know it the way you know the top of a Ferris wheel: by the fact that everything after it is descent.

<!-- more -->

Around Marn Sound the sea runs a season behind the sky. The air gave up on summer weeks ago, but the water spent all of July and August quietly banking heat, and it pays out now, in September, to anyone still showing up. Today it was glassy and almost kind. Fifty minutes, the full circuit — slip to breakwater to the far moorings and home — without a single stroke swum in negotiation.

They call it warm, the walkers on the sea path, watching me wade in. It is a rumor I decline to correct. Fifteen degrees would empty a public pool in minutes; here it is the luxury we wait ten months for, and the difference between those two sentences is the whole sport.

The melancholy is real, though. Every swim from here descends the ladder I climbed all spring: fourteen by the equinox, ten by the leaf-fall, the first brutal five sometime before the year ends. I floated on my back for the last five minutes and let the swell turn me slowly, memorising the ease of it, the way you memorise a room you are about to leave. The badge on this entry is the palest blue in the log. Enjoy it. I did.
