Eleven point four. In January I would have called that tropical; today, with June air sitting mild on the sound, it is simply the temperature at which the cold goes soft — still present, still setting terms, but negotiable, like a border guard at the end of a long shift.

<!-- more -->

This is the season the winter pays for. Forty minutes, out past the herring station and along the seaward face of the breakwater, where the swell comes in long and low and lifts you the way a sleeping thing breathes. At this temperature the body stops spending everything on survival and remembers it knows how to swim: proper catch, proper rotation, breathing on threes because there is breath to spare.

Below the surface the summer sound is a different country. I could see my hands the whole stroke through, pale green and enormous, and once, near the breakwater, the quick silver scatter of a sand-eel shoal parting around me with insulting ease.

The June trap is generosity. Eleven degrees flatters you into forgetting it is still cold water, and the shiver, when it finally arrives, arrives with forty minutes of compound interest. So the rules hold even in the soft season: sugar in the bag, clothes in order, and the walk home taken briskly, even when — especially when — the day insists you have all the warmth in the world.
