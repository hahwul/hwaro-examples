The clocks went forward last night, so the light at the slip this morning was January light wearing a name tag that said spring. Water at 5.1 — the year's first five, which around here is what passes for good news you can verify.

<!-- more -->

I have a spring rule: at five degrees I stop counting minutes and start counting strokes, sixty out along the moorings and sixty back. Strokes are more honest than minutes. A minute in cold water stretches or collapses depending on fear, but a stroke is a stroke; the arm either went over or it did not.

Today the arithmetic argued back. Sixty out took me past the second mooring buoy, further than the count has ever reached, and I caught myself suspecting the sea of flattery before I remembered the chop was behind me on the way out. Sixty back, into it, delivered the correction — I landed a full ladder-length short of the slip and had to swim the difference off the books.

That unbudgeted distance is the part worth writing down. Cold-water swimming is mostly a practice of refusing to lie to yourself with numbers you chose because they were comfortable. The water was reading over my shoulder the whole time, and it marks hard.

Ede arrived as I dressed, pronounced the water "basically Mediterranean," and got in without ceremony. Spring, officially.
