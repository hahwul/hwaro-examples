Lichen is not a single organism, but a composite one. It is a stable, mutually beneficial partnership between a fungus and an alga or cyanobacterium.

The fungus provides the physical structure, anchored to a rock or bark, protecting the fragile inner layers from the harsh sun and drying winds. In return, the algae, safely nestled within the fungal web, photosynthesizes, turning sunlight into sugars to feed them both.

### Forms of Growth

They come in many forms:
* **Crustose:** Flat and crusty, tightly bound to their substrate.
* **Foliose:** Leafy and lobed, slightly raised.
* **Fruticose:** Shrubby and branched, like miniature trees.

> "A lichen is an ecosystem unto itself, a tiny world thriving where nothing else can."

It reminds us that life is often a collaborative effort, a slow blending of different strengths to survive in the harshest of environments.
