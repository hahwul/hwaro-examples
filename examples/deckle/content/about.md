Deckle began in 2013 as a stapled pamphlet passed between six readers who had grown tired of literary magazines that treated translation as a special issue rather than a standing commitment. Thirteen years and more than fifty contributors later, it has kept the shape of that first pamphlet more than its size: every issue still runs exactly six pieces, and at least half of them arrive in English from somewhere else.

## What we publish

Two forms, no more: the long essay and the short story, run at the length each actually needs rather than the length a masthead prefers. Essays tend to land between twenty-five hundred and six thousand words, in the register of [On Marginalia as a Way of Reading](@/folio/on-marginalia-as-a-way-of-reading.md); fiction in translation rarely goes past five thousand, closer to the scale of [What the River Keeps](@/folio/what-the-river-keeps.md). We are looking for pieces built to reward a single unhurried sitting — the kind of reading a quarterly, rather than a feed, still makes room for.

## On translation

A translated piece is credited to its translator at the same size as its author, on the same line where practical, because a sentence that crosses a language arrives by someone's hand and not on its own. Where a translator's choices are unusually visible in the finished piece — a pun rebuilt from nothing, a form that does not exist in English — we ask for a short note and run it in the margin rather than burying it in an introduction nobody reads.

Original-language texts are available to any reader who asks; we keep them on file for exactly this reason.

## Submissions

Deckle reads essays and fiction in translation from January through March and July through September each year, closing the window the rest of the time so the editors can actually finish reading what has already arrived. Translators may submit alongside or ahead of an author's permission; we will help sort out rights before anything runs. Query first with a paragraph on the piece and, for translations, a note on the original's publication history.

Send queries and manuscripts to the address in the margin. We answer everything, eventually, on paper if you ask us to. In the meantime, the current [folio](@/folio/_index.md) is the clearest guide to what we mean by all of this.
