Stratus is kept by one recordist working alone, without a studio, a label, or a deadline. The archive exists because certain sounds — a transformer's hum at 4am, the particular silence of a platform after the last service, ice deciding to move on a reservoir — are usually heard once, in passing, and then never listened to properly. This is an attempt to listen properly.

## Method

Every entry follows the same discipline. Arrive early, before there is any reason to be somewhere. Set the microphones and leave them alone for longer than feels necessary — most of what is worth keeping happens after the urge to check the meters has passed. Log the date, the exact spot, the weather, and the gear, because context is half of what makes a recording legible later. Take nothing that was said to a person; only what a place says on its own.

Editing is limited to trimming dead air at either end and normalizing level. Nothing is denoised, pitched, layered, or stretched. If a passing car ruins a take, the take is ruined — it is filed as a failure, not repaired into a success. The waveform printed at the top of each entry is drawn directly from the recorded file, so what you see traces what you would hear.

## Equipment

The kit is small and mostly quiet on its own: a matched omni pair for open ground, a mid-side pair when the left-right image of a room matters, a pair of contact mics and a magnetic pickup coil for anything with a current running through it, and a hydrophone for water and ice. Everything runs into a battery recorder that has outlived three replacements and gets serviced every winter, whether it needs it or not.

## Access

Recordings made on active infrastructure are taken from public land with no fences crossed and no equipment left unattended near live plant. The [full log](@/recordings/_index.md) lists every entry in the order it was made. Get in touch through the tag pages if you are trying to place a specific hum, platform, or bog.
