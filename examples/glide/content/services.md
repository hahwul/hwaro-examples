We keep the list of services short on purpose. A bureau of four cannot be everything to everyone, and a text is best served by people who do one kind of work often. Everything below is delivered by a named linguist and read a second time by a colleague before it leaves the office.

## Literary translation

Novels, short fiction, poetry, essays, exhibition texts, and criticism. Literary work is where cadence matters as much as meaning, so we translate slowly and revise in passes: a first draft for sense, a second for rhythm, a third read aloud against the source. We are comfortable corresponding with authors and editors directly, and we prefer to. A translator who can ask "did you mean the river or the crowd?" produces a better book than one who guesses.

> A translation is finished not when nothing more can be added, but when nothing more can be removed without a loss.

## Technical translation

Product documentation, developer guides, patent filings, contracts, and interface strings. Here consistency outranks flourish. For every technical client we keep a terminology base and a style sheet, so the tenth document reads like the first. Nakamura maintains these glossaries; when a term is contested, we log the decision and the reason, so a future translator inherits the argument, not just the answer.

## Four-eyes revision

You have a translation already and want a second, independent reader. We check register, terminology, consistency, and the quiet errors a first draft leaves behind — a false friend, a dropped negation, a number that drifted. Revision is billed by the hour with an estimate up front, and we will tell you honestly when a text needs retranslation rather than repair.

## What we decline

We do not take on certified sworn interpreting, real-time conference work, or machine-post-editing at volume. Those are crafts of their own, and pretending otherwise would not serve you. If a project needs them, we keep a short list of colleagues we trust and will make the introduction.
