## How pairing works

For any pair we assign a lead and a reviser. The lead drafts; the reviser reads against the source and signs off. On the rarer pairs — French to Italian, for instance — the two linguists sharing those languages simply swap roles by direction. Nothing leaves the bureau on a single pair of eyes.

## Terminology and memory

For repeat clients we keep a translation memory and a terminology base per account. This is not machine translation; it is a record of the decisions we have already made together, so that your product name, your house spelling, and your preferred rendering of a tricky term stay stable across years and across whichever of us is at the desk. You own that memory, and we hand it over on request in TMX and CSV.

## Languages we are asked for

We are regularly asked about Spanish, Dutch, and Swedish. We do not translate them in-house, but Vestergaard reads Swedish well enough to revise, and we can subcontract Spanish and Dutch to two trusted colleagues under our own review. Ask, and we will tell you plainly whether we are the right bureau for the job.
