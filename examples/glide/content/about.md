Glide began in 2016, in a rented room above a bookbinder in Ghent, when four translators who kept subcontracting to the same overstretched agencies decided to stop. We had all watched good texts pass through too many hands and arrive flattened. We wanted a bureau small enough that the person who quotes your project is the person who translates it, and small enough that we could refuse work we were not right for.

Ten years on, the arrangement has not changed much, which we take as a good sign. We are still four. We still work in pairs. We still read everything twice.

## The four of us

**Séverine Aubry** translates French and Italian fiction, art writing, and criticism into English, and English into French. She spent twelve years as an in-house translator at a Paris literary house and brings its patience with a sentence to every manuscript.

**Matteo Ferri** handles Italian and German technical and legal work — contracts, patents, standards, compliance. He is certified for patent filings in three jurisdictions and is the person who actually enjoys a well-drafted clause.

**Ingrid Vestergaard** translates Danish and Norwegian fiction and trade non-fiction into English. Two of her novel translations have been listed for translation prizes; she is our slowest reader, and means it as a virtue.

**Yuki Nakamura** works between Japanese and English on software, interface, and technical documentation. He builds and maintains the bureau's terminology bases and is the reason our tenth document reads like our first.

## How to reach us

We answer correspondence within one working day. Tell us the languages, the length, the kind of text, and the deadline you are hoping for, and attach the source if you can. We will read it, tell you honestly whether we are the right bureau, and if we are, send a fixed quote and a name.

Before you write, you may want to look over our [services](/services/), the [languages](/languages/) we cover, and the published [rates](/rates/).

> We do not have a sales team. When you write to Glide, a translator writes back.
