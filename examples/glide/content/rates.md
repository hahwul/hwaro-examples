We publish our rates because guessing wastes everyone's time. The figures below are our standard bands; the final quote depends on the text, and you always see it in writing before we begin. All prices are in euros and exclude VAT.

## Translation

| Work | Rate | Unit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Literary translation | 0.16 — 0.22 | per source word |
| Technical translation | 0.18 — 0.24 | per source word |
| Certified / patent | 0.24 — 0.30 | per source word |
| Rush (within 48h) | +25% | on the above |

Literary book-length work is more often quoted as a project fee, sometimes against a grant or an advance; we are glad to structure it that way and to help you word an application.

## Turnaround

A working translator moves roughly 2,000 to 2,500 finished words a day, and revision adds time on top. As a rule of thumb:

- **Up to 3,000 words** — three to four working days.
- **A 60-page report** — around two weeks, including revision.
- **A full-length novel** — three to five months, quoted individually.

Rush work is possible and honestly priced, but we will not promise a schedule that forces us to skip the second reading. That reading is the point.

## Terms

We invoice 30% on commission for projects above 5,000 words, the balance on delivery; smaller jobs are billed in full on delivery. Payment is due within 21 days. First-time clients and new pairs start with a short paid sample so you can judge the fit before committing the whole text.
