title = "Skeletal Structure of the UI"
date = 2025-08-05
+++

The visual layer of our application is supported by an intricate skeletal structure. We use a grid-based layout system that acts as the backbone, ensuring consistency and alignment across all screen sizes. The 'bones' of this structure are our base components: buttons, inputs, and containers. These are designed to be flexible yet strong enough to support various styling variations without breaking the overall design language. When designing new layouts, it's important to respect this underlying skeleton. Avoid overriding core styles unless absolutely necessary, as this can weaken the structural integrity and lead to unexpected visual glitches. Think of it as adding muscle to a skeleton; it needs to attach correctly to function properly. We have documented the structural constraints and guidelines extensively to help you build robust interfaces that stand the test of time.
