## THE PRESS COLLECTIVE

We are not a studio. We are not an agency. We are a collective of designers, typographers, and builders who believe that **form is never neutral**. Every color choice is a declaration. Every typeface carries a history. Every layout is an argument.

KONSTRUKT PRESS issues manifestos, dispatches, and technical studies. We document the living practice of aggressive, geometric design — not as nostalgia, but as method.

## OUR PRINCIPLES

The early twentieth century gave us tools we have barely used:

- **The diagonal is more dynamic than the horizontal.** Stability is for monuments. We are in motion.
- **Black and red are not a color scheme.** They are a political act, reclaimed for the work of making.
- **Typography is architecture.** The grid is not a cage — it is the structure that makes freedom legible.
- **No ornamentation that does not serve function.** Cut what does not work. Keep what does.

---

These are not aesthetic preferences. They are working constraints. We build to them because they produce results: pages that communicate at speed, hierarchies that cannot be misread, compositions that hold attention.

## WHAT WE PUBLISH

We publish in three registers:

**Manifestos** — short, declarative arguments about how design should be practiced and why it matters beyond the screen.

**Dispatches** — practical studies of specific techniques: how to cut a diagonal with `clip-path`, how to use `transform: skewX()` without making readers dizzy, when to break the grid and when to obey it.

**Examinations** — close readings of historical work that still teaches. Rodchenko's book covers. El Lissitzky's Proun compositions. Zwart's typography. We look at what was built and ask what it still demands of us.
