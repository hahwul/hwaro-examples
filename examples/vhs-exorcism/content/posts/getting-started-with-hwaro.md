I don't remember dying.

I remember setting up the tripod because "someone should document this properly."

I remember the priest saying my name wrong.

And then I remember pressing RECORD.

The tape shows everything: the hole, the box, the flowers. My mother crying into her hands the way she always did when she thought no one could see.

At 17:09 the camera slowly tilts upward on its own.

There is no one operating it.

At 17:11 my own face fills the frame. I am standing behind my mother, looking straight into the lens.

I am smiling.

I wave.

The tape continues for another five minutes. You can hear the dirt.

I have watched it 47 times. Every time the face gets a little closer to the camera.
