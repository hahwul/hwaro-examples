I was alone on the overnight shift. The tower was quiet except for the usual 60-cycle hum.

Out of boredom I keyed the mic and said into the dead carrier:

"Is anyone there?"

The return signal came back instantly, perfect fidelity, no delay:

"Yes. We have been waiting for you to ask."

It knew things. It knew the name of the dog I had when I was seven. It knew what I had eaten for dinner. It knew the exact second my father died.

I unplugged the microphone.

The voice continued through the control room monitor speakers.

I have not slept since 1984.

Every time I close my eyes I see the waveform of my own question being written in real time on an oscilloscope that no longer exists.

Do not speak to the static.

It is a very good listener.
