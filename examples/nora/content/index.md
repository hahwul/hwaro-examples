Welcome to Nora, an archival project dedicated to capturing the disappearing acoustic landscapes of the subarctic circle. Through long-form field recordings, ambient soundscapes, and oral histories, we document the voices of weather station operators, remote fishermen, and researchers living along the northernmost edges of human settlement. Each dispatch is a geographical and temporal snapshot, capturing the quiet conversations of communities defined by isolation and the deep, resonant frequencies of the ice, wind, and water that surround them. Listen to the changing north, one coordinate at a time.

Our recordings are gathered using calibrated omnidirectional microphones and portable field recorders designed to operate in extreme sub-zero temperatures. We believe that environmental soundscapes carry history just as deeply as spoken testimony. The hum of a generator in an isolated research station, the shifting weight of pack ice in a fjord, and the local dialect of a fishing village on the edge of the Barents Sea all tell stories of adaptation and resilience. By pairing these audio dispatches with geographic coordinates, local temperatures, and meteorological data, we build a multi-sensory archive for future generations. We invite you to explore the timeline of our voyages, listen to the voices of the far north, and connect with a region experiencing rapid ecological and cultural transformation.
