Pyramiden was once a bustling Soviet coal mining settlement on the archipelago of Svalbard. Abandoned in 1998, it remains frozen in time, its empty buildings slowly decaying in the dry arctic air.
<!-- more -->
We walk through the silent streets and empty corridors of the cultural center and the swimming pool, recording the eerie sounds of industrial decay: the rhythmic squeak of a rusted iron door swinging in the wind, the dry creak of shifting floorboards, and the low hum of wind through ventilation shafts.

We interview Vladimir, one of the few caretakers who live in Pyramiden during the winter months to maintain the remaining infrastructure. He describes the strange experience of living in a ghost town, where the only sounds are the footsteps of occasional polar bears and the cracking of the nearby Nordenskiöld glacier. Vladimir reflects on how the silence of Pyramiden is different from the silence of nature; it is a heavy, populated silence, full of the echoes of the hundreds of miners and their families who once lived here. The recordings capture the transition from a noisy industrial hub to a quiet, cold museum of the cold war.
