Siorapaluk is the northernmost naturally inhabited settlement in Greenland. Here, the landscape is dominated by rock, ice, and an overwhelming, heavy silence that is rarely experienced in the modern world.
<!-- more -->
Using highly sensitive microphones equipped with specialized wind protection, we record the wind as it sweeps down from the Greenland Ice Sheet, whistling through the gaps in the village's small wooden houses and rustling the dry, sparse summer tundra.

We sit down with Kaaralo, an elder hunter, who shares his experiences of traveling across the frozen sea ice by dog sledge. He explains how hunters listen to the wind to predict changing weather patterns. Each direction has its own voice and portends a different kind of snow. Kaaralo describes the unique acoustics of the polar night, when the cold air becomes dense and carries sounds over immense distances. Under these conditions, the barking of a dog can be heard clearly from miles away across the flat ice. Our recording documents a world where survival depends on a deep, active listening to the subtle voices of the wilderness.
