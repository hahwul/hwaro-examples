Haboob is a one-person research log. A handful of sensors sit on unremarkable IP ranges and this site collects what shows up in them: brute-force sprays against Cowrie, opportunistic exploitation of exposed Redis and SMB, and the malware samples worth pulling apart once the noise settles. Every write-up starts from a real capture, walks through the packets or the binary, and ends with something a defender can act on — a Suricata rule, a YARA signature, or at minimum a clearer picture of what a botnet is doing this month.

No vendor pitch, no "threat intelligence platform" behind it — logs, disassembly, and dated notes from sensors that stay up because someone has to watch the dust settle. New here? The [about page](/about/) covers the sensor grid, the triage tooling, and the disclosure policy in full.
