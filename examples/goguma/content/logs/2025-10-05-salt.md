In October, we concluded the coastal mudflat restoration trials featuring the Muan Clay (IB-2026-C09) sweet potato variety. This trial aimed to observe how heritage cultivars adapt to soils with high electrical conductivity and compact clay texture. Sodium levels were monitored weekly to measure salinity buildup in the topsoil.

<!-- more -->

Muan Clay demonstrated significant osmotic tolerance. While control varieties experienced leaf chlorosis and root decay at electrical conductivity levels above four decisiemens per meter, Muan Clay maintained stable growth up to six decisiemens. Our anatomical slices showed thickened epidermal cell walls in both the feeder roots and the emerging storage roots, preventing excessive sodium uptake.

Starch extraction from the harvested tubers showed that sweetness was preserved, though starch granule size was slightly smaller than those grown in inland sandy-loam. The higher concentration of soluble sugars in the root cells likely serves as an osmotic buffer, helping the plant retain moisture. These results show that heritage landraces possess key resilience traits for coastal farming.
