title = "Dryland Trials: Irrigation Scarcity and Soil Horizon Adaptation"

description = "Evaluating the growth of sweet potato accessions under simulated drought and deep soil horizon constraints."

In the early summer trial cycle, we evaluated the response of Andong Dryland (IB-2026-D12) sweet potatoes to acute water scarcity. This experiment was conducted in our outdoor deep-soil lysimeters, allowing us to observe root growth relative to dry soil horizons. Irrigation was reduced by sixty percent compared to optimal levels over a forty-day period.

<!-- more -->

Andong Dryland exhibited a remarkable capacity for deep soil penetration. While standard cultivars confined their root mass to the upper fifteen centimeters of soil, Andong Dryland extended primary lateral roots down to forty-five centimeters, tapping into residual moisture in the clay-loam horizon. This deep root system allowed the plant to maintain leaf turgor and continue photosynthesizing under intense sunlight.

By day forty, stomatal conductance had decreased, but plant survival remained at ninety-eight percent. Storage root initiation occurred despite the water stress, though individual tuber weights were reduced by approximately fifteen percent. These findings suggest that Andong Dryland is a promising candidate for dryland farming systems facing changing rainfall patterns.
