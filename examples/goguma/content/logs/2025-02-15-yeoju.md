During the mid-winter greenhouse trials, we monitored the rooting efficiency of Yeoju Purple (IB-2025-P01) cuttings. This heritage root crop is known for its high starch density and deep pigmentation, but its establishment under cold soils remains a challenge. We maintained the soil temperature at a steady ten degrees Celsius to evaluate early cellular division.

<!-- more -->

Early root initiation was delayed by four days compared to control groups at twenty degrees. However, the subsequent root structure showed increased density. Upon sectioning the root cortex after thirty days, we observed a notable accumulation of anthocyanins near the vascular bundles. This pigmentation is believed to act as an osmoprotectant under thermal stress.

Furthermore, dry matter accumulation reached thirty-two percent of total root mass, which is significantly higher than golden varieties. This suggests that the Yeoju Purple landrace prioritizes starch storage under winter planting conditions, though overall volume remains modest. Further genomic sequencing is planned to trace specific starch synthase genes.
