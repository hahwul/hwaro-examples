Vermilion designs identity systems for people who make food and drink in quantities too small for the agencies that usually handle this work, and too consequential to leave to a template. Cideries pressing a few thousand cases a year. Spice grinders running batches by hand. Canneries packing one catch at a time. We take on five to seven projects a year, on purpose, so every system gets the attention a full rebrand deserves.

## What we build

Every engagement produces the same core kit, sized to the client: a wordmark and a monogram mark built to reduce cleanly from a shop sign to a jar lid; a label or packaging grid flexible enough to cover a full product line without a redesign each time a new variety ships; and a written guideline document the client's own team can run without calling us back for every reorder. We do not design menus, websites, or anything past the point where the product leaves the maker's hands — that focus is why the systems hold up. See the full kit worked through five times over in [our case studies](/identities/).

## How we work

Every project starts on site, usually in the maker's own production space, because a label system designed without seeing the bottling line or the tin gauge tends to fail the first time volume changes. From there: two weeks of research and naming work if needed, three weeks of mark development, and a production phase that runs alongside the client's own print or can vendor so nothing gets specified that can't actually be printed at their scale.
