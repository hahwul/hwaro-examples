Amber is not as durable as it appears. Exposure to oxygen, ultraviolet light, and minor temperature swings will, over decades, surface-craze a polished specimen and dull its color.

## Stabilization Workflow

1. **Initial cleaning** — soft brush, distilled water, no solvents. Most museum-grade amber pieces have been ruined by overzealous solvent baths.
2. **UV barrier** — display cases use a film rated below 400nm. Direct sunlight is non-negotiable; even reflected daylight matters over years.
3. **Atmospheric control** — relative humidity held between 45% and 55%. Below 40% the resin micro-cracks; above 60% mold appears on inclusions.

## Long-Term Storage

For specimens not on display, conservators use sealed containers with passive oxygen absorbers and silica gel. The goal is not zero exposure — that is impossible — but slowing degradation to a rate measured in centuries rather than decades.
