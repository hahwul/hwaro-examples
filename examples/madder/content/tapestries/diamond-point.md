Point twill is the straight twill's threading turned back on itself at the top of the run — 1 to 4 and then 3 back down to 2 — so that treadled the same way, the diagonal folds into a diamond instead of running off the edge of the cloth.

<!-- more -->

I used the second pull from the same dye lot as Coldhope Twill for this one, drawn from the same pot a week later once the first exhaustion had settled the colour toward rose rather than brick. It suits the diamonds better than the deeper red would have — softer edges read more clearly at this scale than a hard colour would.

The threading turn happens every six ends, which at eighteen ends per inch puts a diamond point roughly every third of an inch — small enough that the eye reads it as texture from across a room and only resolves into diamonds up close. I wove this one slower than Coldhope, partly because point-twill floats are longer and want a gentler beat, partly because I kept stopping to check the draft against the notebook; a single threading error this early in a point repeat throws every diamond after it out of true, and I'd rather lose ten minutes checking than an afternoon unweaving.
