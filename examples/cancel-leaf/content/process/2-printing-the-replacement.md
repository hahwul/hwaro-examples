# Printing the Replacement

<p class="lede">A cancel is rarely printed alone. The replacement leaf travels through the press alongside other small jobs, gathered onto a single sheet that will later be cut down to size.</p>

## Matching the original

<p>The compositor sets new type that imitates the page it will replace: the same measure, the same leading, the same running head. Small differences are inevitable. A slightly different sort, a corrected catchword, a re-spaced line — these become the fingerprints that bibliographers later use to identify a cancel state.</p>

## After the press

<p>Once printed, the sheet is hung to dry, trimmed, and folded. The single replacement leaf is separated from the others and stacked with its siblings, ready for the bindery. The cancel has not yet entered the book; it waits on a workbench, indistinguishable to the casual eye from any other freshly printed page.</p>
