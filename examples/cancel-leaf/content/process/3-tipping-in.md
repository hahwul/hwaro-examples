# Tipping In

<p class="lede">Tipping in is the final, quiet step of the cancel. The replacement leaf is pasted along its inner edge onto the stub left behind, so the corrected page hangs in the gathering as if it had always been there.</p>

## The narrow band of glue

<p>A thin line of paste is brushed along the inner margin of the new leaf, no wider than a few millimeters. The leaf is laid against the stub, pressed lightly, and left under a weight to dry. Too much glue and the leaf will refuse to open; too little and it will lift away from the book within a generation.</p>

## What the reader sees

<p>To a reader turning the page, the cancel is invisible. The book opens cleanly, the running head is right, the catchword agrees with the next leaf. Only at the inner margin, if you look closely, do you find the seam: a faint shadow where the old paper meets the new, a small confession that the book has been corrected.</p>
