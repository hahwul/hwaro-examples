Ethereal Constructs is a series of fourteen digital paintings produced over six months, each examining the same question from a different angle: what does it mean for a shape to feel weightless without losing its presence?

The work begins from a constraint. Each piece uses no more than three primary forms and a single light source, with all secondary detail derived from the interaction between them. The constraint is not an aesthetic flourish; it is a working method, designed to force the composition to carry meaning through arrangement rather than ornament. When a form is permitted to do too many things at once, it tends to do none of them well.

The palette across the series is drawn from a narrow range of pale blues, bone whites, and a low-saturation gold reserved for the highlight. The choice was practical before it was poetic. A wider palette would have invited the eye to chase color rather than form, and the series is not about color. It is about the relationship between mass and air, and how a hard edge can imply softness when set against the right neighbor.

Each piece was developed in three stages: a graphite study at A5 scale, a digital block-in at full resolution, and a final pass focused entirely on edge quality. The third stage usually consumed more time than the first two combined. Edges, more than any other property, determine whether a form reads as solid or as suggestion.

The series concluded when the constraints stopped producing new questions. There is no virtue in extending a project past the point at which it has answered itself.
