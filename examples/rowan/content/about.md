This codex is compiled and kept, in principle, by no house at all. In practice it lives in a reading room off the Sail Hall in [Thornreach](/codex/thornreach/), maintained by a rotating clerkship the Assembly funds jointly and no single house is permitted to staff for more than two consecutive terms — a rule written after an early edition was found to describe one house's fleet in verbs and a rival's in adjectives, which is a smaller crime than it sounds and a larger one than the guilty clerk expected.

<!-- more -->

## What "entry" means here

Every codex entry — whether filed under **regions** or **houses** — carries a ledger infobox recording, where it can be established, the entity's ruler, banner, and founding year. These three facts sound simple and rarely are. A region's "ruler" may be an Assembly committee, a house with no formal claim but an armed presence, or genuinely no one, as with the customs waters of [Ashgale Strait](/codex/ashgale-strait/). A founding year is reliable for a house with kept records and closer to folklore for a region that predates the Sundering by centuries. The infobox states what is claimed and by whom; it does not adjudicate.

## Disputed claims, printed twice

Where two houses give incompatible accounts of the same fact — a toll's legitimacy, a chairing owed, a salvage block's ownership — this codex prints both accounts rather than choosing between them. Readers looking for a single authoritative history of Veyra will not find one here, because no such history exists that both [House Corvane](/codex/house-corvane/) and [House Vance](/codex/house-vance/) would sign. What exists instead is a record of what each party is prepared to claim in writing, which is its own kind of truth and, the clerkship has found, a more durable one.

## Corrections and cross-references

Cross-reference links throughout the codex — set in the accent wine — point to the fullest existing entry on a given subject, not necessarily the most sympathetic one. Corrections may be submitted to the reading room clerk in Thornreach; they are logged, dated, and weighed against the standing entry, but nothing is silently overwritten. An earlier claim, once printed, stays part of the record even after a later one supersedes it — which is, this codex holds, the only honest way to keep a gazetteer for a place still arguing with itself about its own history.
