Ashgale Strait is eleven miles of grey water between Thornreach and the outer chain, named for the wind that funnels through it hard enough to strip paint from a hull. It is also the only deep-water passage that connects the eastern spice isles to the Thornreach markets without a month-long detour around the Marches, which makes it the single most contested stretch of water in the archipelago despite belonging, on paper, to no one at all.

<!-- more -->

## A channel with two customs houses

Because the strait predates the [Compact of Thornreach](/codex/thornreach/) by eleven years, no clause of that treaty ever assigned it a sovereign. [House Corvane](/codex/house-corvane/) maintains that its navy has patrolled the strait since the Sundering and therefore holds customary right of toll; [House Vance](/codex/house-vance/) maintains that customary right means nothing without a ledger entry, and that its underwriters have insured every cargo through the strait since 19 YS, which is a claim of a different kind but asserted with equal confidence. The Assembly has twice been asked to rule and twice declined, on the reasoning that a ruling would only give the losing house a grievance sharp enough to act on.

In practice this means a ship running the strait pays two tolls: a flag-toll to whichever Corvane cutter intercepts it, and an insurance premium to Vance's underwriters that is functionally identical to a toll but is never called one. Captains who object are reminded, gently, that the alternative is the Marches route, and that the Marches do not forgive.

## The wind itself

The strait's name comes from the ash-grey chop that the funneling wind throws up even in calm weather — sailors call it "the gale that isn't," since it rarely reaches storm strength but never fully dies either. Pilots who work the strait regularly develop a feel for its rhythm that cannot be taught from any chart; House Corvane pays a premium for pilots with strait experience, and losing one is treated as a minor naval emergency.
