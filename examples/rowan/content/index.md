Veyra was, for four centuries, a single empire governing some two hundred islands from a capital that no longer exists. When it fell — an event this codex and every house in it call simply the Sundering, though no two accounts agree on its cause — no successor state rose to replace it. What remains is an archipelago of rival trading houses, each holding a chain of isles, a fleet, and a claim to legitimacy that rests less on conquest than on the ledger: on tolls collected, routes insured, and treaties signed at the neutral harbor of Thornreach, where the Assembly of Sails has kept the houses from open war for three centuries by the simple expedient of giving every dispute a hearing and almost no dispute a final ruling. This codex records the archipelago as the houses themselves would have it recorded — regions and houses alike, filed by rubric, cross-referenced where their histories collide, and left disputed wherever a clean answer would be a lie.
