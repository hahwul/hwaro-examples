Whitespace, in a brutalist layout, is not a cushion. It is a beam. It transmits load from one element to the next and is sized accordingly. A page with too much whitespace sags. A page with too little collapses. Design is choosing the right gauge.

## Rules of gauge

- A heading needs 48 pixels of clearance, or it is crowded.
- A quote needs 24 pixels, or it is indistinguishable.
- A footer needs nothing beyond the line above it.

> Whitespace that does not transmit load is ornament. Ornament, in this house, does not pour.

Treat whitespace as a material, and the page will carry its weight.
