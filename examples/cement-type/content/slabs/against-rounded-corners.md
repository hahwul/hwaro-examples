Rounded corners are an apology. They say *this element did not want to be here, so we sanded it down*. Sharp corners do not apologise. They sit where they were cast and carry their weight.

We do not round corners. We do not soften edges. We do not blur backgrounds. If the corner is too sharp for the reader, the reader is reading a different publication.

## The argument, briefly

- A sharp corner commits to its position.
- A rounded corner compromises its position.
- A compromised corner cannot carry load.

> If the corner does not meet at ninety degrees, the structure does not meet at all.

Pour straight. Cure hard. Read honestly.
