## 3.1 Parameter change from EXP-02

<div class="param-change">
  <strong>Temperature:</strong> <span class="old-val">120 C</span> <span class="arrow">--&gt;</span> <span class="new-val">95 C</span><br>
  <strong>Pd:Cu molar ratio:</strong> 2:1 (unchanged)<br>
  <strong>Cu loading:</strong> 0.47 wt pct (unchanged)<br>
  <strong>WHSV:</strong> 3.6 h-1 (unchanged)
</div>

The <span class="exp-id">EXP-02</span> catalyst (Pd:Cu 2:1) was used directly for <span class="exp-id exp-success">EXP-03</span>. After completing the EXP-02 run, the catalyst was regenerated by flowing 10 vol pct H2/N2 at 300 C for 1 h, then cooled to 95 C under N2 before re-introducing the feed. This regeneration step removes any surface carbonaceous deposits accumulated during the prior 8 h run. A control experiment confirmed that the regenerated catalyst reproduced the EXP-02 result at 120 C (selectivity: 84.2 pct vs. 84.6 pct originally).

## 3.3 Results

### 48-hour stability test

To confirm that the optimised conditions are robust, <span class="exp-id exp-success">EXP-03</span> was extended to 48 h TOS. Selectivity ranged from 90.6 to 91.8 pct over this period, with no significant trend. Conversion declined gradually from 79.1 pct (1 h) to 76.2 pct (48 h), corresponding to a deactivation rate of 0.06 pct per hour. This rate is consistent with mild coke formation and is within the acceptable range for industrial operation with periodic regeneration cycles.

## 3.4 Assessment: Optimum found

<span class="exp-id exp-success">EXP-03</span> surpasses the 90 pct selectivity target at 91.2 pct and achieves the highest yield in the chain at 71.4 pct. The 48 h stability test confirms robust performance with negligible selectivity drift. The experiment chain is concluded.
