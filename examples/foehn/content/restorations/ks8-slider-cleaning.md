The Klangwerk KS-8 has the most honest front panel of its era: thirty-two
faders, no menus, no memory, every parameter exactly where the panel says
it is. Which means when the faders go bad, the whole instrument goes bad,
because there is no other way in.

<!-- more -->

This one crackled on every single slider. Moving the filter cutoff sounded
like frying bacon; the mixer faders dropped out entirely in the bottom
third of their travel. The synth was electrically healthy underneath — the
crackle was the sound of oxide, not failure.

## Why you desolder instead of spray

The tempting shortcut is contact cleaner through the slot. It works for a
month. The solvent flushes the old conductive lubricant off the resistive
track along with the dirt, and a dry track wears out fast — you trade a
crackle now for a dead fader next spring.

So all thirty-two came off the boards. Each fader opens with four folded
tabs; inside, the carbon track gets a wash with isopropyl on lint-free
swabs, the wiper gets inspected under the loupe, and everything is
reassembled with a thin film of the correct conductive grease. Panel by
panel: mixer section first, then envelope, then filter, then the master
section, testing each board before the next came apart.

```text
Section    Faders   Condition            Action
Mixer      8        heavy oxide          clean + relube
Envelope   8        moderate oxide       clean + relube
Filter     8        light oxide, 1 bent  clean + relube, straighten
Master     8        1 broken wiper       clean + relube, donor part
```

## The broken wiper

Slider 29 — master tune, naturally — had a snapped wiper arm. Klangwerk
went under in 1988; there are no new parts. The donor was a scrapped KS-4
board bought two years ago for exactly this afternoon. Same fader family,
same track length. The rule of the parts shelf: buy the wreck when you see
it, because you will not see it again when you need it.

Reassembled, the panel feels like a mixing desk again — smooth, silent,
and precise. Total time: eleven hours. Total parts cost: one donor board
already on the shelf and four euros of grease. The KS-8 goes home tuned
and quiet, and the workshop smells like isopropyl for a week.
