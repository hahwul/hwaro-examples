## Conditions

Slack tide at German Channel. Minimal current at the cleaning station, which allowed us to settle on the sandy bottom and wait. Visibility was 25 meters with slight particulate matter from the channel flow.

## The Dive

Positioned ourselves 8 meters from the known cleaning station -- a prominent coral bommie at 12 meters depth with resident cleaner wrasses.

The first **reef manta ray** arrived at the 15-minute mark. Wingspan estimated at 3.5 meters. It made three passes over the cleaning station, hovering motionless while cleaner wrasses worked along its gill slits and ventral surface.

A second manta arrived at 22 minutes, followed by a third at 30 minutes. All three individuals had distinct belly patterns -- photographed for ID matching with the local database.

The largest individual made a close pass directly overhead at approximately 4 meters distance. At this range, the remoras attached to its underside were clearly visible -- counted four.

## Behavior Notes

- All three mantas approached from the south channel
- Cleaning hover duration: 2-4 minutes per pass
- Interval between passes: 3-8 minutes
- No feeding behavior observed (no barrel rolling)
- Mantas showed no avoidance of divers at 8m distance
- Cleaner wrasses active throughout; servicing mantas and resident reef fish between manta visits

## Post-Dive Notes

Extended bottom time was possible due to shallow depth. Air consumption was excellent at the low activity level. This site rewards patience -- arriving early and remaining still produces the best encounters.
