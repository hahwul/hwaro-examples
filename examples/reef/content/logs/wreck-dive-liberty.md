## Conditions

Entry at 05:45, pre-dawn. The wreck lies on a black sand slope starting at 5 meters and reaching 30 meters at the deepest point of the hull. Visibility was 18 meters with slight surge at the shallow stern section.

Water temperature was 26C with a noticeable thermocline at 22 meters dropping to 24C.

## The Dive

Descended to the stern at 12 meters to intercept the morning school of **bumphead parrotfish**. They arrived on schedule at 06:02 -- a group of approximately 30 individuals moving in formation along the wreck's port side. The lead fish were easily 1 meter in length, their characteristic forehead bumps prominent in the early light.

The school paused at the coral-encrusted superstructure and began feeding, the sound of their beak-like teeth scraping coral clearly audible underwater.

Moved along the hull to the midship section at 20 meters. The wreck's structure is heavily colonized -- soft corals, barrel sponges, and gorgonian fans cover nearly every surface. Counted four distinct species of anemone with resident clownfish along a single 10-meter stretch of railing.

At the deepest section, a **whitetip reef shark** was resting in the sand beneath the hull. It remained stationary during observation from 5 meters distance.

## Structural Notes

The wreck continues to deteriorate slowly. The cargo holds at midship are accessible but show signs of collapse at the edges. The forward gun mount remains intact and is the most heavily encrusted section, supporting a dense ecosystem.

Coral health on the wreck appears excellent -- no bleaching observed, and new growth is visible on recently exposed metal surfaces.

## Post-Dive Notes

Dawn timing is critical for the bumphead school. They arrive from deeper water at first light and depart within 20 minutes. Later dives miss this encounter entirely. The black sand entry requires careful buoyancy to avoid silting.
