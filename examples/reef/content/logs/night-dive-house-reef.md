## Conditions

Entry at 19:30 local time, approximately 40 minutes after sunset. No moon. Sea state calm with negligible surface current. Visibility reduced to 12 meters under torch light due to suspended plankton attracted to the reef lights.

Water temperature was 27C, dropping slightly to 26C at 16 meters.

## The Dive

The reef transforms after dark. Daytime species retreat into crevices while nocturnal hunters emerge.

Three **lionfish** were active within the first 50 meters of the dive, positioned at coral overhangs with fins spread, herding small prey against the reef structure. Their hunting coordination was deliberate -- two individuals appeared to work in tandem to corner a school of cardinalfish.

At 12 meters, found a sleeping **bumphead parrotfish** wedged into a coral alcove, fully enclosed in its mucus cocoon. The cocoon was intact and translucent, visible under careful torch positioning without direct illumination.

## Highlight: Spanish Dancer

At the 35-minute mark, spotted a **Spanish dancer nudibranch** (*Hexabranchus sanguineus*) on open sand at 14 meters. It was moving across the substrate in its characteristic undulating crawl. Body length approximately 25cm, deep red coloration.

When gently illuminated from the side, it lifted off the sand and performed a brief swimming display -- the flattened body rippling in the water column for approximately 10 seconds before settling back to the bottom.

## Post-Dive Notes

Night dives on this reef consistently produce encounters not possible during the day. The Spanish dancer sighting was exceptional -- only the second recorded at this site this season. Recommend red filter for approach to avoid disturbing nocturnal behavior.
