## The Procession

The legions return victorious from the eastern frontier. The Triumph of the Legions is the most ancient and solemn of Roman ceremonies — a sacred rite in which the conquering general, dressed in the purple and gold of a god, rides through the heart of Rome to the thunderous acclaim of the citizenry.

The procession begins at dawn at the Field of Mars and winds through the Via Sacra, culminating in the Grand Arena for the formal ceremony and oration before the assembled senate and people.

## Programme of Events

| Time | Event |

| X hora | The Triumphal Procession enters the Arena |
| XI hora | Formal Oration by the Victorious General |
| XII hora | Ceremonial wreath-laying and libation |
| XIII hora | Grand Feast in the Imperial Galleries |

## What to Expect

Witness the spectacle of ten thousand soldiers marching in perfect formation, their armour gleaming beneath the Italian sun. The air will ring with the sound of cornu and tuba as the procession enters the arena.

The ceremony includes a formal reading of the Senate's decree, the presentation of captured standards, and a grand oration delivered from the Rostra — the ancient platform of Roman speakers — erected at the centre of the arena floor.

## Dress

Guests are invited to attend in togas or formal Roman dress. The Imperial Gallery guests are expected to observe strict dress standards befitting their elevated position.

## Registration

This event is open to all citizens of standing. Seating is allocated by senatorial order, equestrian rank, and then general admission. Register early to secure your preferred section.
