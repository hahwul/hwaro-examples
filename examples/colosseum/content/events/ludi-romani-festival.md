## The Greatest Games in Rome

The *Ludi Romani* — the Roman Games — are the oldest and most celebrated festival in the Roman calendar, instituted in honour of Jupiter Optimus Maximus. For fifteen days, the entire city transforms into a theatre of celebration, competition, and sacred ceremony.

Our revival of this magnificent tradition brings together the finest elements of the ancient festival: theatrical productions in the classical mode, athletic contests in the arena, musical performances, and sacred rites at the Temple precinct.

## Festival Programme

### Days I through III: The Theatrical Days
Classical tragedies and comedies performed in the open-air theatre adjacent to the Arena. This year's productions include:
- *Oedipus Rex* by Sophocles (performed in Latin translation)
- *The Clouds* by Aristophanes
- *Medea* by Seneca the Younger

### Days IV through VII: The Athletic Days
Competition in the ancient disciplines: foot races, wrestling, discus, javelin, and the long jump. The pentathlon champion receives the olive crown and the title of *victor ludorum*.

### Days VIII through XI: The Musical and Artistic Days
Performances of classical Roman music reconstructed from historical sources. Exhibitions of mosaic, fresco, and sculpture by contemporary artists working in the ancient style.

### Days XII through XV: The Sacred Days
Processions, libations, and ceremonial feasts. The festival concludes with a grand banquet in the Colosseum Arena, under the open sky, in the manner of the ancient *epulum Jovis* — the feast of Jupiter.

## Accommodations

Guests attending the full festival may reserve chambers in the adjacent Villa Claudia, a restored Roman villa offering accommodation in period-appropriate style. Advance booking is strongly recommended.

## How to Register

Single-day and full-festival passes are available. Early registration is required for the theatrical performances, which have limited seating. Athletic competition entries close one month before the festival.
