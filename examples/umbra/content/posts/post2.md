The Void was a twelve-week exhibition at the Hale Gallery examining how humans perceive space when visual cues are reduced to near-zero. Our studio was commissioned to design the physical environment and all accompanying materials.

## Concept

Visitors entered through a narrow corridor where light levels dropped incrementally across thirty meters. By the time they reached the main gallery, their eyes had fully adapted to darkness. Artworks were illuminated by single-point light sources that cast dramatic, room-spanning shadows.

## Spatial Design

The gallery was restructured with a series of freestanding walls at calculated angles. Each wall's position was determined by its shadow projection at the fixed light source coordinates. The shadows themselves became the architecture of the space.

Wayfinding was achieved entirely through shadow -- floor-level fixtures cast directional shadows that guided visitors along the intended path without any signage or printed materials.

## Print and Digital

All exhibition materials used a strict monotone palette. The catalogue was printed on uncoated black stock with gloss-black ink, making text visible only when held at certain angles to light. The website used a similar principle, with content revealed through cursor-driven shadow interactions.

## Reception

The exhibition attracted over twenty thousand visitors and was extended twice. It was named among the year's ten most significant design installations by three major publications.
