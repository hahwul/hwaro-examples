The first study on the wall, and the one that set the rule for all the rest:
the pen touches the paper once and does not leave until the figure is done.
Here the line starts at the crown, falls down the near side of the face, and
gathers into the long curve of a back turned three-quarters away from the room.

<!-- more -->

A seated pose is unforgiving under this constraint. The eye wants to lift the
pen at the shoulder, cross the empty air, and begin again at the hip — but the
whole discipline is to *refuse* that lift. So the line has to travel where a
real contour would never go: down into the seat, around the folded forearm,
back up the spine on the far side. Those returning passages, drawn faintly,
end up reading as weight. The figure sits because the line had to double back
to get home.

I redrew this eleven times before the closing curve met its own starting point
without a visible seam. The measured path — six hundred and fifty-five units,
the shortest study in the collection — is deceptive: brevity here came from
planning the route, not from drawing less. Every centimetre had to be spent
on the way somewhere.

The method — how each line is measured and hung — is set out in the
[colophon](@/colophon.md).
