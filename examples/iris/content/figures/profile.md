The most recent study, and the one that comes closest to a portrait. A profile
is the single line's natural home: the edge of a face *is* one continuous
contour, from the hairline down across the brow, the small cliff of the nose,
the two soft steps of the lips, the chin, and under the jaw. The whole front of
the head can be drawn without a single lift — nature already obeys the rule.

<!-- more -->

The difficulty is everything the profile edge leaves out. An outline alone
gives you a silhouette, not a head. So the line, having reached the jaw, does
not stop; it travels up the throat, turns at the nape, and climbs back over the
skull to close at the hairline — and on that return journey it dips inward twice,
lightly, to place the ear and the hollow of the eye. Those two small inward
excursions are the difference between a shadow-puppet and a person.

Seven hundred and two units. I have drawn this same profile perhaps forty times
now; the length varies by less than five units between the good versions, which
tells me the pose has finally settled into the hand. When the number stops
moving, the study is finished.
