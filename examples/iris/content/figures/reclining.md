The only landscape-format study on the wall. A reclining figure is read the way
you read a sentence — left to right, along a horizon — and the single line makes
that literal. It begins at the head on the left, loops once to close the skull,
then runs the length of the body: over the shoulder, along the flank, up and
over the raised knee, and back down to the ankle before returning underneath.

<!-- more -->

Two closed loops interrupt the long horizontal — the head at one end and the
lifted knee near the other. Both are moments where the line has to circle back
on itself and rejoin the main run, and both took far longer to resolve than the
sweeping contour between them. A loop that does not close cleanly reads as a
smudge; a loop that closes too neatly reads as a bicycle wheel. The knee sat
somewhere in between for a dozen attempts.

Seven hundred and seventy-one units, most of it in that single unhurried
traverse of the torso. When I drew it I tried to keep my hand moving at a
constant speed, so the length of the path would map to the time the pose took
to see. It very nearly does.
