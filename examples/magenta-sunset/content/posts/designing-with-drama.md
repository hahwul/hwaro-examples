Drama in design isn't about chaos — it's about controlled intensity. Like a sunset that builds from gentle pastels to a blazing magenta climax, dramatic design uses progression, contrast, and rhythm.

## Building Visual Tension

Great dramatic design follows a narrative arc:

1. **Quiet introduction** — clean typography, generous whitespace
2. **Rising intensity** — color accents appear, gradients deepen
3. **Dramatic peak** — the hero section, the key visual, maximum color
4. **Graceful resolution** — returning to calm, guiding the reader forward

## Typography as Performance

In dramatic design, typography isn't just functional — it performs:

- **Display fonts** with high contrast strokes create visual weight
- **Generous sizing** makes headings feel like stage announcements
- **Warm glow effects** add dimension and atmosphere
- **Careful spacing** gives each letter room to breathe

## The Balance Point

The challenge of dramatic design is knowing when to pull back. Every element cannot be at maximum intensity — that creates noise, not drama.

The Magenta Sunset approach reserves maximum visual impact for key moments: the homepage hero, article headings, and navigation highlights. Everything else supports these moments with quiet elegance.

## Glamour with Purpose

True glamour in design serves the content. The fiery gradients of Magenta Sunset don't exist for their own sake — they create an emotional context that makes content more engaging, more memorable, and more enjoyable to read.

That's the difference between flashy and glamorous: purpose.
