Before pixels were sharp and screens were flat, images arrived through electron beams scanning phosphor-coated glass. The result was warm, slightly soft, and alive with subtle imperfections.

CRT monitors hummed. They flickered at frequencies just below conscious perception. They produced scan lines -- thin horizontal gaps where the beam had not yet returned. These artifacts were not defects. They were the texture of the medium.

Modern displays eliminated all of this in pursuit of clarity. What we gained in precision, we lost in character.
