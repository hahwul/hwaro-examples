Foundry No. 1 closed cycle IV with 2,832 tons of poured steel against an assigned quota of 2,400. The pouring brigade ran three shifts without a single furnace stall, and the overrun bonus has been entered into the ledger.

The gain came not from longer hours but from sharper sequencing. The charge schedule was rebuilt so that no furnace ever waited on cold stock.

{% alert(type="output") %}Foundry No. 1 is now the cycle-leading unit. Its sequencing method is being copied to Foundry No. 2.{% end %}

## what the brigade changed

- **Continuous charging.** Stock was staged one heat ahead, so the furnace never idled between pours.
- **Tighter tapping windows.** The tap crew worked to a fixed clock rather than to call.
- **Shared maintenance.** Relining was scheduled between shifts, not during them.

```
BRIGADE   : Foundry No. 1
QUOTA     : 2,400 T
```

The plan does not reward the loudest unit. It rewards the unit that pours. Foundry No. 1 poured.
