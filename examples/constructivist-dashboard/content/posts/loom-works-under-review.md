Loom Works No. 3 closed cycle IV at 980 tons against a quota of 1,100, an eleven-point shortfall and the only unit on the register to miss its target. The review brigade was dispatched within the day.

The shortfall was not a failure of labor. It was a failure of supply: raw fiber arrived late on six of twelve weeks, and idle looms cannot weave thread that has not reached the floor.

> A machine without material is not a worker resting. It is a quota dying in silence.

## findings of the review brigade

1. **Upstream delay.** Fiber dispatch from the Granary Collective lagged the loom schedule by an average of nine days.
2. **No buffer stock.** The works held less than two days of fiber on site, leaving no margin for late delivery.
3. **Sound machines.** Every loom passed inspection. The fault was in the chain, not the unit.

## corrective plan

- Establish a ten-day fiber buffer at the works.
- Synchronize dispatch to the loom calendar, not the rail timetable.
- Re-survey at the midpoint of cycle V.

The unit is not penalized. It is corrected. The plan moves forward, and Loom Works No. 3 moves with it.
