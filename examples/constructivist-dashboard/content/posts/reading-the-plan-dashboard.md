The Production Plan is a working instrument, not a poster to admire. Every brigade is expected to read it at the start of each shift. This dispatch explains how.

## the three figures that matter

- **Plan Fulfilment.** The headline percentage. Above one hundred means the collective is ahead of target; below means the gap must be closed.
- **Output.** The recorded tonnage, set in the largest numerals. When the numeral is large, read it first.
- **Status.** The badge. Red means a unit has exceeded its quota. Plain means the unit is under review.

## how the bands are read

On the plan-versus-actual chart, black bars are the assigned target and red bars are the recorded output. When the red bar stands taller than the black, the brigade has exceeded its quota:

```
BLACK BAR  =  assigned plan
RED  BAR   =  recorded actual
RED > BLACK = quota exceeded
```

Color is not ornament here. Red always points to output, black always holds the structure. Learn the two and the whole plan reads in a glance.

The plan rewards the unit that reads it and then exceeds it. Read first. Pour second.
