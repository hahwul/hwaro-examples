## Why This Is The Last One

We have played The Old Hall on the first Saturday of May, every year, since 2004. Twenty-two years. The room knows us and we know the room. We know which of the floorboards creaks under the kick drum. We know which of the footlights buzzes at 60hz when you plug a humbucker into it. We know which seats get the best stereo image, and we have quietly saved those seats for the people we wanted to sing to.

We are ending it because the band is not the band it used to be, not quite, and we have decided to end it before the room can tell. This is for us, and it is for anyone who has stood in the back of that room for even a single song over the years.

## A Word From Maeve

> Twenty-two years is a long time to play the same room. We have played it when I was pregnant, when Ollie broke his hand, when Duncan's bass was in the repair shop and we did the whole show on ukulele. We played it when the power cut out in 2017 and we finished the second set on the porch, acoustic, by torchlight. The room has been kind to us, every time. This is our way of being kind back.
>
> -- Maeve Kilgore
