## STORE INFO

Welcome to **The Daily Receipt**, a blog that comes out of the printer one curling strip at a time. There is no masthead here, no hero image, no clever logo lockup. There is a register, a roll of thermal paper, and an opinion that prints in all caps because the font only has one weight that matters.

Everything you read is formatted like a transaction. Posts arrive as line items. Reading time is the price. The total at the bottom is always *FREE TO READ*, and there are *NO REFUNDS ON OPINIONS*.

---

## WHAT WE STOCK

We write about the small printed things that pile up in coat pockets and glove compartments — the ephemera nobody saves on purpose but everybody ends up keeping:

- **Ephemera** — the receipt as found text, accidental poetry, and disposable record
- **Design** — dotted leaders, dashed rules, and why the humble register slip is a masterclass in constraint
- **Everyday** — what a grocery list, a parking stub, or a coffee receipt quietly says about the person holding it
- **Typography** — monospace, all-caps, and the strange dignity of a font printed by heat
- **Analog** — the chemistry of thermal paper, and the slow disappearance of every word it ever held

> "A receipt is the only document that records exactly what you wanted at one specific minute, and then politely fades before you can be held to it."

## ABOUT THE CASHIER

I go by **the cashier**. I have rung up more orders than I can count, and somewhere along the way I started reading the tape instead of just tearing it off. I am fascinated by the things printers print without thinking: the misaligned headers, the SKU codes, the cheerful `THANK YOU` that the machine has no feelings behind.

I believe the receipt is the most honest document we produce. It does not editorialize. It does not flatter. It lists what happened, totals it up, and hands it back to you while it is still warm.
