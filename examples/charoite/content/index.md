## One valley on Earth

Charoite is a silicate that has been documented from a single place: the Murun massif in Sakha, where the Chara River bends around its northwest rim. Nowhere else. The stone forms where an unusual alkaline intrusion cooked the surrounding rock into something that does not happen twice, and every polished slab that exists came out of a few hillsides above one cold river.

This archive keeps six of those slabs. Each specimen is cut, polished, weighed, and drawn by hand from the cut face, because a drawing forces you to look longer than a camera does. The plates record what the stone actually shows: fiber bands that flow like poured silk, pale quartz veins that cross the current, black aegirine crystals sitting in the swirl like stones in a stream.

The journal alongside the catalog records how the collection is kept. Field notes from the valley, saw-room decisions about which face to cut, and the plain-text records that hold it all together. It is a small archive, and it means to stay small: six stones, looked at carefully, from the only valley that makes them.
