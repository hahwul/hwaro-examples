Quartz is usually the enemy of a clean charoite face. It saws differently, polishes differently, and undercuts at the boundary if the lap is even slightly impatient. CH-007 earned its drawer because the two veins that cross it do neither: they run clean, white, and nearly parallel, straight across the violet current like wires over a river.

<!-- more -->

What makes the block worth drawing is the argument it settles. The fiber does not deflect around the veins; it passes beneath them unchanged, which says the quartz arrived late, filling fractures in a fabric that was already set. The stone records its own sequence of events, and a careful cut can make that sequence legible from across the room.
