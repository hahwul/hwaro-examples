Most charoite flows. CH-021 circles. Somewhere in its formation the fiber stopped traveling and began to orbit, and the polished section shows three distinct eddies, each wound around a black aegirine core like weather around a pressure center.

<!-- more -->

The largest eddy is a shade under five centimeters across and rings its center with alternating silk and pearl bands, tight enough that the naked eye reads them as a single luminous halo. The chatoyancy behaves unlike any other stone in the drawer: instead of a band sweeping across the face, each eddy lights in turn as the lamp moves, a slow blink passing around the stone.

The rough was bought at the workings in 2019 and sat unlabeled in a crate for six years because it looked, from the outside, like a plain grey cobble. The saw is the only honest assessor this material has.
