CH-014 is the reason this archive exists. It came off the saw as a rough 21 cm block from the Kaskad working and lost three centimeters to a fracture that, in hindsight, did it a favor: the surviving face carries the full repertoire of the deposit in one uninterrupted sweep.

<!-- more -->

The fiber runs in broad diagonal currents, deep violet troughs against pale silk crests, and the chatoyant band travels the whole 18 cm without a break. Under a moving lamp the light does not slide so much as pour, following each S-curve like water finding the same channel every time. A single aegirine crystal sits low in the composition, black and blunt, and the swirl bends around it exactly the way a stream bends around a set stone.

It was the first stone drawn for the catalog, and the plate set the standard for every drawing after it: decide what the fiber is doing, and say it in six inks, no shading, no apology.
