Collectors pass over material like CH-032, and that is precisely why it is here. The face is closer to night than to lilac: a violet-black ground threaded with only a few silk bands, and scattered across it a field of aegirine crystals, black-green, blunt, five of them large enough to name.

<!-- more -->

An archive that keeps only the prettiest faces is a jewelry box. This slab documents the other end of what the Murun contact zone produces, the portions where the alkaline fluids ran out of patience and the pyroxene moved in. Held flat under the lamp it reads almost matte; tilted, the few surviving fiber bands flash suddenly bright against the dark ground, the highest-contrast chatoyancy in the collection.
