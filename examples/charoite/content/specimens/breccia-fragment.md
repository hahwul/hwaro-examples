CH-029 is the youngest stone in the drawer and the most violent. At some point after the fiber set, the rock was shattered in place: the face shows angular clasts of finished charoite, each with its own internal current running in its own direction, locked in a darker ground and seamed with threads of white quartz where the fractures healed.

<!-- more -->

Reading it is like reading a shuffled manuscript. One clast carries parallel silk, its neighbor a truncated eddy, a third almost pure grape with no visible fiber at all, and none of the currents agree. The breccia records an event the tidy specimens omit: the deposit did not form once and rest, it was broken and re-cemented while still underground, and kept its history in the seams.

It closes the catalog deliberately. Five stones show what the deposit makes; the sixth shows what happens to it, and a collection that can hold both is telling the truth.
