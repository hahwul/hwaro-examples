The Chara in March is a road. The river freezes hard enough to drive on, which is the only reason a March buying trip makes sense: the workings above Sirenevyi Kamen are a half-day by winter track, and the same trip in July means a boat, a swamp, and a week.

<!-- more -->

Buying rough at the source is mostly the discipline of not trusting surfaces. Weathered charoite goes chalk-grey and dull, and the best block on this trip looked like something you would use to hold down a tarp. What gives good material away is the fracture: fresh breaks show silk even when the skin shows nothing, and a wet thumb across a broken corner will flash violet if there is anything underneath worth sawing.

Three blocks came home. Two are ordinary good stone that will likely be traded on. The third, the grey cobble, rang under the hammer tap like it had structure to hide, and a corner chip showed tight parallel fiber, the kind that either becomes the best slab of the year or dies on the saw. It has been assigned CH-033 and sits in the queue behind a re-polish.

The keeper's rule for field notes stands: write down what was seen at the river, not what is hoped for at the saw. The stones settle the argument themselves, some weeks later, in the cut.
