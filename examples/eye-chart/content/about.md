## WHO WE ARE

This is Eye Chart. A blog written by an optometrist who grew tired of squinting at poorly designed interfaces, unreadable typography, and software that nobody bothered to focus properly before shipping.

## WHAT WE EXAMINE

Every post here starts with a single clinical question: *can you read it?* Not just the text. The structure. The intent. The signal-to-noise ratio. The information hierarchy.

We look at:

- **Type** — sizing, weight, contrast, spacing, the difference between "set" and "readable"
- **Design systems** — the ones that clarify, the ones that obscure
- **Software interfaces** — with the diagnostic eye of someone who has spent years asking patients which is clearer, one or two
- **Writing itself** — because the best prose passes the 20/20 test without trying

## THE INSTRUMENT

This site is built with [Hwaro](https://github.com/hahwul/hwaro). The design is intentionally clinical: white field, black letters, scaling type, no decoration that does not serve legibility. The Snellen chart is not merely the inspiration. It is the argument.

---

A good design, like a good lens prescription, brings everything into focus without you noticing the correction.
