Timbre is a podcast about graphic design's back catalog — the posters, magazines, signage manuals, and manifesto covers that quietly set the terms for everything that came after them. Each episode picks a single artifact, dates it, and spends an hour asking what problem it was solving and why the solution still looks inevitable in hindsight.

We treat every episode like a museum registrar treats an accession: a plate number, a swatch pulled from the object's dominant color, a note on medium, a note on condition. The show's tagline, borrowed from the registrar's ledger, is "one artifact at a time" — not a survey, not a canon, just one object examined closely enough that its era becomes legible.

## The hosts

**Adaline Marchetti** trained as a paper conservator before moving into design history; she spent six years cataloging a type foundry's specimen archive before that archive's grant money ran out, which is more or less how Timbre started. She reads the object.

**Desmond Okafor** is a working graphic designer who has spent two decades in production — identity systems, wayfinding, editorial layout. He argues for the artifact's craft: how it was actually made, what it cost the people who made it, and whether the technique still holds up.

## How an episode gets made

We start from the object, not the movement. A subway sign comes before "the Swiss grid arrives in New York." A magazine spread comes before "digital typography finds its voice." The movement gets explained, eventually, but only after we've spent time with the thing itself — its paper stock, its printing method, the color it actually shipped in rather than the color it's remembered as.

Every episode's show notes include a swatch sampled from the artifact, labeled with a specimen code in the style of a paint or ink catalog. It is not a scientifically rigorous color match — we don't have the original in the studio — but it is our best-effort registrar's note, and we'd rather commit to a specific wrong answer than a vague right one.

## Get in touch

Timbre is produced independently out of a converted print shop in Providence, Rhode Island. Episode suggestions, corrections, and archive donations are always welcome — reach the show through the contact address in each episode's notes.
