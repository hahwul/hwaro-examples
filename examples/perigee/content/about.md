Perigee is five people working out of a single room above a hardware supplier in Fall River — a bench for casing, a bench for movement assembly and regulation, a small lathe for dials and case backs, and a corner desk where the paperwork happens. There is no showroom, because there has never been enough stock sitting still long enough to show.

## How it started

The workshop began as a repair bench. Before Perigee existed as a brand, its founder, Mireille Achterberg, spent nine years servicing moonphase complications for other people's watches — mostly older pieces whose moon-works had drifted so far out of true that owners assumed the complication was decorative. Correcting the same 59-tooth drift problem often enough eventually turned into a question worth answering properly: what would a moonphase mechanism look like if it were built, from the first gear cut, to actually track the sky. The 135-tooth moon wheel in [the P-38 caliber](/the-watch/) is the answer, and it took three years and two failed prototypes to get the finger-cam advance to index reliably at 46 hours of reserve rather than the 72 hours a bigger barrel would have made easier. The first day of cutting Run Two's bridges is recorded in [Cutting Run Two: First Bridges](/journal/cutting-run-two/).

## Why runs of three hundred

Three hundred is not a marketing number. It is roughly what one workshop of five people can case, regulate, and inspect in a year without adding a second shift or a second bench — and adding either would mean training new hands on tolerances that currently live mostly in muscle memory. Capping each run also means the tooling for a given case geometry gets used consistently within a run and then retired or revised for the next one, rather than drifting slowly out of spec across an open-ended production line. When Run One's three hundred cases sold out, we closed the waitlist rather than promise a date for Run Two before the silver had even been ordered.

## No reissues

We have had requests to reopen Run One's exact specification — a slightly different lug shape, an earlier dial texture — and turned all of them down. A Perigee is dated by its run the way a vintage bottle is dated by its harvest: Run One means something specific, and diluting that by reissuing it under a new run number would make the number on every case back mean less, not more. If a genuine improvement changes the watch, it starts a new run instead of quietly rewriting an old one.

Perigee ships worldwide directly from the workshop, with no dealer network and no showroom appointments. The fastest way to reach us is the contact address in each run's confirmation email; general questions about waitlist timing are best asked before a run opens, not after it closes.
