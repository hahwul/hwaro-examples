Mangrove is compiled and corrected by the Sable Bight Pilot Station, a volunteer body of working skippers and two retired harbourmasters who between them have taken small craft in and out of the Vane Estuary for the better part of four decades. It is not an official publication, carries no chart agency's endorsement, and is not a substitute for the current Admiralty chart, tide tables, and notices to mariners for your area — treat it as a companion to those, not a replacement.

## Scope

The handbook covers pilotage for craft under twelve metres on the stretch of coast between Sable Head and the Coot Skerries, including the Vane Estuary, Halyard Sound, and the approaches to Bittern Quay. It does not cover offshore passage-making, commercial pilotage, or vessels over twelve metres, all of which carry requirements this handbook was never written to meet.

## How corrections happen

Every method in these pages has been worked, by hand, by at least two contributors before it was written up — the worked examples are real plots, not invented numbers made to come out even. When a correction is needed, it goes through the Station's quarterly review and lands in the next edition with the affected chapter's "corrected" date updated accordingly. Chapter dates in this handbook mark the last review, not the date the method was first written.

## What the handbook assumes

Nothing here assumes a functioning GPS, a charged battery, or a signal. Every fix, every tidal calculation, and every plan can be worked with paper, a pencil, a hand-bearing compass, a watch, and the current chart and tide table for the area. Where an electronic aid genuinely earns its place — a plotter cross-check on a fix, for instance — the text says so explicitly and treats it as a check against the manual method, never as the method itself.

Corrections and reports of a chapter that no longer matches conditions on the water travel through the usual channel: a dated note left at the fairway noticeboard by Bittern Quay, addressed to the Sable Bight Pilot Station.
