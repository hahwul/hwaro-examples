Pilotage is coastal navigation by eye, checked by instrument — the discipline of knowing where you are and where the hazards are without leaving sight of land. It sits between chartwork done at a table before you leave and the full offshore navigation that takes over once the coast drops astern.

The six chapters below cover four skills: taking and using bearings, reading tides and streams, writing a plan that survives contact with the weather, and doing all of the above again once the light characteristics have replaced the landmarks. Read them in whatever order matches what you are short on — a skipper confident with fixes but new to secondary ports should start with the tides chapters, not work through from the top.
