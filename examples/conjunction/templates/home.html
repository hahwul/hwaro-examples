On the wheel, at the kiln

Demonstrations

Throwing, trimming, and glaze-line talks run continuously from the demonstration barn. Pull up a bench; nothing needs booking.

  1. 10:30

    Centring for the terrified

    A slow, unhurried half-hour on getting a lump of clay to sit still — the one skill everything else rests on.

  2. 12:00

    Salt in the fire

    How a shovel of salt at cone 10 becomes the orange-peel skin on a finished jug, explained beside a cooling chamber.

  3. 14:00

    Slip-trailing a harvest jug

    Drawing in liquid clay, freehand, at speed — the loose slipware line that never comes twice the same way.

  4. 15:30

    Reading a wood-fire

    Ash, flame path, and the accidents worth keeping — a walk around the anagama with the makers who stoke it.

Getting there

Visiting

The fair fills the old drying sheds and firing hall of Wraydale Brickworks, a mile above the market town of Kettleby. Entry is free; the kettle is always on.
