Conjunction is a **selected fair** of fifty studios. We keep it small on purpose: everyone gets a proper stand, the halls never feel like a market, and visitors can actually talk to the makers. Applications for 2026 are open until **31 January 2026**.

## Who we take

We show work that has been through a real fire and is sold by the person who made it. In practice that means:

- **Wood-fire** — anagama, climbing, and single-chamber kilns.
- **Salt and soda** — vapour-glazed stoneware and porcelain.
- **Slipware** — trailed, combed, and feathered earthenware.

We do not take bought-in seconds, transfer-decorated ware, or resellers. Collectives sharing a kiln are welcome to apply as one studio.

## What a stand includes

Each studio gets a **2.4m × 2m** pitch in either the firing hall or the drying sheds, with a table, two chairs, and power on request. The fee is **£140 for the weekend**, due only once you are offered a place — there is no charge to apply. We take a **flat 10% commission** on sales to cover the shuttle, marshals, and the tea room's endless kettle.

## How to apply

Send the following to the selection panel by the closing date:

```text
Studio name and maker(s)
Where you fire, and how
Six images of recent work (a link is fine — no attachments)
Roughly what you would bring, and a price range
Hall preference: firing hall / drying sheds / no preference
Any access needs for your stand
```

The panel — three practising potters and last year's guest selector — meets in **mid-February** and everyone hears back by the end of the month, accepted or not, with a note on why. Offered a place, you confirm with the stand fee within two weeks.

## Good to know

- Stands are staffed by you, though we can arrange cover for demonstration slots.
- Load-in is the Friday afternoon before; overnight parking for one van per studio is on site.
- If you would like to run a demonstration, say so in your application — we build the timetable from the makers who offer.

Questions before you apply are always welcome; email the panel and a real potter will answer.
