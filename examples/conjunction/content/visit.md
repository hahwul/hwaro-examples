Conjunction fills the drying sheds and firing hall of **Wraydale Brickworks**, a mile above the market town of Kettleby in North Yorkshire. The brickworks last fired clay of its own in 1971; the tall chambers that once dried bricks now make unreasonably good rooms for showing pots.

Entry is **free** and no ticket is needed. Come for an hour or stay the day — the demonstration barn and the tea room both run right through.

The halls are quietest before eleven and after four. Demonstrations run to a fixed timetable each day (see the [home page](/) for the running order); nothing needs booking, and there is bench seating in the barn.

## Getting there

**By train.** Kettleby is on the Dales line, roughly two hours from Leeds with one change at Skipton. From the station it is a twenty-minute walk uphill to the brickworks, or a short hop on the fair shuttle (below).

**By car.** Follow the brown *Wraydale Brickworks* signs from the A-road; the site is signposted from the edge of Kettleby. Please do not rely on satnav for the final mile — the lane is narrow and the postcode drops you in the wrong field.

**Fair shuttle.** A minibus runs a loop between Kettleby station, the town car park, and the brickworks gate every twenty minutes, 9.30am to 5.30pm both days. It is free and takes prams and wheelchairs one at a time.

## Parking

There is a **grass overflow field** beside the gate, marshalled from 9am. In wet weather it can be soft — sensible shoes, and a tow from the marshals if you are unlucky. Blue Badge parking is on hardstanding directly by the entrance; tell the marshals at the top of the lane and they will wave you through.

## Access

The firing hall and tea room are step-free on the ground floor, with a level threshold from the hardstanding. The drying sheds have a shallow ramp at the north door. Paths between buildings are gravel and generally firm but can rut after rain. An accessible toilet is by the tea room, and assistance dogs are welcome throughout. If you would like a quiet time to visit or need anything arranged in advance, email the organisers and we will sort it.
