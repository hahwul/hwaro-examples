Bracken & Salt is Nadia Kerr's studio, built around a single downdraught salt kiln and a stubborn belief that pots are meant to be used. At the top of the firing she introduces salt straight into the flame; it vaporises, settles on the pots, and fuses into that unmistakable pitted, orange-peel skin.

<!-- more -->

The result is glaze that grows on the pot rather than being painted onto it — each piece shows exactly where the vapour reached, brightest on the edges and thin in the shadows. Nadia throws almost entirely for the table: jugs that pour cleanly, mugs sized for a proper mug of tea, and wide serving bowls with a cobalt line brushed under the salt.

## A note on the surface

The blue you see is a slip laid down before firing, not an overglaze:

```yaml
# Cobalt liner slip — brushed, single coat
ball_clay:   60
china_clay:  25
flint:       10
cobalt_carb:  2   # a little goes a long way
water:       "to single-cream consistency"
```

Everything on the stand is food-safe, dishwasher-steady, and priced to be used daily rather than shelved. Chipped seconds from the last firing go in a basket at the front, first come first served.
