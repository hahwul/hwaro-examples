Cinder Row is a shared anagama — a single long tunnel kiln — stoked in rotation by a loose collective of six potters who split the wood, the watches, and the results. A firing runs five days and nights without pause, and someone is awake feeding it the whole time.

<!-- more -->

The pots that come out are a record of where the flame went and what it carried. Ash lands, melts, and runs; embers press against a shoulder and leave a scar; a shelf's shadow keeps one side cool and matte while the other glazes itself green. The collective treats these as the point, not the price of admission. Nothing is thrown away for being uneven.

## Firing schedule

| Day | Stage | Cone |
|----|-------|------|
| 1 | Warming, doors ajar | — |
| 2–3 | Body reduction | 6–8 |
| 4 | Hard firing, side-stoking | 10 |
| 5 | Ember soak, then seal | 11 down |

For Conjunction the group is bringing work from the autumn firing: large storage jars, a wall of tea bowls, and a few flat plates that caught heavy ash across the rim. Members take turns on the stand, so the person you speak to may well be the one who stoked the pot you are holding.
