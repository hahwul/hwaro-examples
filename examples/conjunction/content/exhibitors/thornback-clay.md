Thornback Clay is Isla Voss, working in soda — a close cousin of salt-glaze, where a solution of soda ash is sprayed into the kiln at temperature rather than shovelled in as crystals. It carries more gently on the flame, so the surfaces come out softer: fluid blushes of orange and grey drifting across white porcelain rather than the hard pitting of a salt kiln.

<!-- more -->

Isla throws thin, tall forms that give the vapour a long run — bud vases, stem cups, carafes with a pinched lip. The porcelain is left mostly bare so the soda can do the drawing, with just a wash of shino inside the rims where the pour will show. What decoration there is comes from wadding: little rolls of clay the pots stand on, which mask the flame and leave pale flared marks like the shadow of a wing.

## Spraying the kiln

The soda goes in as a fine mist near the top of the firing:

```text
cone 9 down  →  begin spraying
sodium carbonate  1 kg in 4 L warm water
spray in short bursts through the ports, rotating the kiln in the mind's eye
watch the draw trials: stop when the orange-peel just breaks
```

The palette is deliberately quiet — whites, straw, a little iron-red — so the forms and the flame patterns carry the pots. Isla will be spraying draw trials from a portable rig on the hour if the weather holds.
