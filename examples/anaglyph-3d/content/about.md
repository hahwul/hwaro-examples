## About This Blog

Put on your red-cyan glasses. Everything here is designed to *pop* — to leap off the screen the way a 1950s movie frame leaped off a theater screen into the laps of the audience.

**Anaglyph 3D** is a blog about depth: the illusion of it in design and film, the physics behind stereoscopic vision, the culture of 3D cinema, and the strange beauty of chromatic aberration. If you have ever squinted at an old 3D comic and felt your brain perform a small miracle, this is the place for you.

---

## About the Author

I am a designer and depth-obsessive who collects vintage 3D viewers, restores old anaglyph prints, and spends an unreasonable amount of time tweaking `text-shadow` offsets. My tools of choice are a pair of paper 3D glasses and a good CSS editor.

I write about:

- **Stereoscopic photography and film** — the history and the how
- **Chromatic aberration as aesthetic** — turning a lens flaw into a style
- **Design that demands your full attention** — nothing flat, nothing dull
- **The neuroscience of depth perception** — how two flat images become one 3D world
