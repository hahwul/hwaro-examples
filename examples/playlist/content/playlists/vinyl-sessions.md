## About This Playlist

These are recordings that have survived every format transition -- from vinyl to tape to CD to streaming -- and lost nothing in the process. The performances are definitive. The production, despite its age, sounds better than most things recorded this decade.

Miles Davis bookends the collection because his range defines the boundaries of the playlist. "So What" is cool, confident, and forward-looking. "Blue in Green" is fragile and introspective. Everything else lives between those two poles.

## Selection Criteria

Every track had to meet three conditions:

1. **Timeless production.** It had to sound good on any system, in any era.
2. **Definitive performance.** Not a good version of a song -- the version.
3. **Emotional honesty.** No showboating, no technical displays for their own sake.

> Great recordings do not age. They wait.

## Listening Notes

- Nina Simone's "Feeling Good" arrives at track 3 as a jolt of energy after two longer jazz pieces
- The Sam Cooke track is the emotional heart of the playlist
- Etta James closes things out with earned warmth
- If you have a turntable, these songs deserve it
