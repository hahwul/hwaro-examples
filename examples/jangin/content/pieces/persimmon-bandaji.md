Not every bandaji needs to hold a household's whole store of linens. This one was sized for a single low shelf in a bedroom — small enough that one person can lift it by the sides, which makes the dovetailed corners do real structural work rather than serve as a courtesy. Persimmon panels sit inside a pine frame, chosen for the wood's dark, almost black heartwood streaks against pale sapwood, a contrast the maker only fully knows once the log is opened.

<!-- more -->

Construction follows the larger chests exactly: through-dovetails at all four case corners, cut with a fine dozuki pull saw and pared to the scribe line with a chisel rather than a router jig, and a lid hung on the same wooden pivot hinges rather than metal butt hinges. The front carries a single recessed pull mortised into the panel rather than a separate applied handle, so there is nothing on the face of the chest that was not cut from the same four boards as the case itself. The [elm bandaji](/pieces/elm-bandaji/) shows the same case joinery at parlor scale, if a full-size chest is closer to what you need.
