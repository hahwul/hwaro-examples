## About Patina

Patina is a design journal exploring the intersection of material culture and the passage of time. The name refers to the thin layer of oxidation --- typically green or brown --- that forms on copper, bronze, and similar metals as they age.

Rather than a flaw, patina is a mark of authenticity. It cannot be rushed or faked convincingly. It is proof that something has endured.

## Philosophy

This site is built with the same principle in mind. Clean typography, restrained color, and deliberate structure. No decoration for its own sake. Every element earns its place.

We draw inspiration from:

- Japanese *wabi-sabi* and the acceptance of imperfection
- The Arts and Crafts movement's respect for honest materials
- Modernist typography and its pursuit of clarity
