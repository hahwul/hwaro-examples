## Concept

Project Beta sits as a horizontal slab projected over a sloping site. The cantilever extends nine meters past the last vertical support, an obvious structural drama that the design refuses to hide.

## Execution

The underside of the cantilever is finished in raw steel rather than the polished surfaces of the rest of the building. The change in material is the only ornamentation — a deliberate cue that the load path runs above the visitor's head, not under their feet.

Interior partitions stop short of the cantilever's free edge. Walking out toward it produces a controlled vertigo: the floor remains solid, but every other reference plane disappears.

## Material Restraint

Three materials only: cast concrete for the supports, raw steel for the cantilever underside, and oiled timber for the floor. No paint, no surface treatment beyond what the material demands to survive weather.
