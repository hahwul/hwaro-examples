## Concept

Gamma takes a deep, low-ceiling commercial floor plate and breaks it open with a single vertical light well. The well runs from roof to ground floor, lined in white plaster, glazed at the top.

## Execution

The well is the only place daylight reaches the building's interior at noon. Office floors are arranged so the desks farthest from the perimeter face the well; nobody sits more than fifteen meters from natural light.

The well's section is rectangular at the top and tapers narrower as it descends, exaggerating the perceived depth. From the ground floor, looking up, the building reads as taller than it is. From the roof, looking down, the bottom appears further away than measurement would suggest.

## What the Drawings Don't Show

The acoustic effect was not designed; it was discovered. Voices from the ground floor carry softly through the well to every level above. The result is that the building always sounds occupied, even when most floors are empty — a quality that turned out to be unexpectedly important to tenants.
