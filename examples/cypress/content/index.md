Cypress collects the procedures a home mechanic actually reaches for: the drivetrain adjustments, brake service, and wheel work that keep a bike quiet and predictable between shop visits. Browse the full [repair procedures](/repairs/) list, or read [about this reference](/about/) to see where its torque figures come from. Every page opens with the tools and specs up front, then walks the job step by step.

## How to read a procedure

Each repair page carries the same shape. A meta row up top gives you the difficulty and rough time. A spec rail on the right holds the torque and fit values you'll need mid-job, rendered in monospace so they're easy to scan without losing your place in the steps. The numbered steps themselves are bold-countered — glance down the left edge and you always know which step you're on, even with grease on your hands and the manual propped against the stand.

None of these procedures require a shop's air tools or a case of specialty lubricant. If a step needs something unusual, it's called out in the tools line at the top of the page before you start.
