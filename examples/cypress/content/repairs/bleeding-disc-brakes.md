A lever that comes to the bar, or a bite point that keeps drifting further out, means air has worked its way into the hydraulic line. A syringe bleed pushes fresh fluid through the system from the caliper up to the lever, carrying the air bubbles out with it.
<!-- more -->
Confirm your fluid type before you open anything — mineral oil and DOT fluid are not interchangeable, and mixing them damages seals. The system's fluid type is usually printed on the lever body or listed in its documentation.

## Setup

1. **Remove the wheel and pads.** Pull the wheel, then remove the brake pads and insert a bleed block in their place. This keeps the pistons from getting pushed too far out if fluid moves unexpectedly.
2. **Open both bleed ports.** Remove the bleed screw at the lever and thread in the funnel or syringe fitting. At the caliper, attach an empty syringe to the bleed nipple and crack it open a quarter turn — no more, or you'll unthread it completely.
3. **Fill the top syringe.** Fill the lever-side syringe or funnel with fresh fluid, holding the lever roughly level so gravity doesn't help air travel the wrong direction.

## Bleeding

4. **Push fluid down from the lever.** Slowly depress the plunger at the lever end while gently pulling the caliper-side syringe, watching for bubbles arriving at the bottom. Tap the lever body and hose lightly with a plastic handle to dislodge bubbles clinging to the walls.
5. **Cycle the lever.** With both syringes still attached, pull the brake lever slowly to the bar and let it return, several times. This clears air trapped in the lever's internal passages that gravity alone won't move.
6. **Close the caliper side first.** Once bubbles stop appearing at the caliper syringe, snug the bleed nipple to `2–3 N·m` before removing the syringe — this traps fluid in the line instead of letting air back in.
7. **Top off and close the lever.** Top the lever reservoir to the fill line, replace the bleed screw, and torque it to `1–1.5 N·m`. Wipe any spilled fluid immediately; most hydraulic fluids attack paint and rubber.

## Reassembly

8. **Reinstall pads and wheel.** Remove the bleed block, reinstall the pads, and pump the lever a few times to bring the pistons back to the rotor before reinstalling the wheel. Torque caliper mounting bolts to `6–8 N·m` and rotor bolts to `2–4 N·m` in a star pattern if either was disturbed.

{{ alert(type="caution", body="Hydraulic fluid is not compatible across brake families. Confirm mineral oil versus DOT before you crack a single fitting — the wrong fluid will swell seals within minutes.") }}

Bed in new pads with 15–20 firm stops from moderate speed before riding normally; the bite point will keep improving for the first few rides. If the pads themselves are worn rather than the fluid, start with [Replacing Worn Brake Pads](/repairs/replacing-brake-pads/) instead — a bleed won't fix a thin pad.
