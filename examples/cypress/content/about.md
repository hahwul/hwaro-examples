Cypress started as a stack of index cards taped inside a garage cabinet. Every time a bolt was torqued to a value worth remembering, or a bleed took three tries to get right, the fix went on a card. This site is that cabinet, typed up and organized into [repair procedures](/repairs/) by system instead of by which shelf had space.

## Who this is for

Cypress is written for the rider who is comfortable with a hex key and willing to learn the rest — not a professional mechanic, and not someone opening a toolbox for the first time. Each procedure assumes you can identify your own components (the difference between a two-piece and press-fit crank, for instance) but explains the steps that are easy to get wrong: torque sequences, bleed order, and the small tolerances that separate "fine" from "right."

## Where the numbers come from

Torque and spec values in the right-hand rail are shop-standard starting points gathered from component documentation and cross-checked against a torque wrench, not guessed. They are conservative on purpose. If your component's manufacturer publishes a different number, use theirs — carbon parts in particular vary enough between models that this reference should never be the last word on a carbon steerer or seatpost.

## Corrections

If a procedure here doesn't match what you find on your bike, trust your eyes and your service manual first. Bicycles change enough between model years that "close enough" sometimes isn't. Cypress is a starting point for the parking-lot fix and the Sunday-afternoon overhaul, not a substitute for documentation specific to your groupset.
