# On Writing Raw Logs

Engineering writeups are mostly lies. Not intentional lies — tidy lies. The author knows the answer by the time they write, so the narrative presents the debugging path as if it were a line. It is never a line.

## The value of the tangle

A raw log keeps the tangle. You get the false leads, the misread error messages, the thirty-minute tear-down of a fuse that wasn't blown. The reader learns more from the tangle than from the answer. The answer is rarely the point.

## The cost of the tangle

You look dumber in public. That is the cost. Pay it anyway.

## A rule

I have one rule for logs on this site: if the entry was written after the problem was solved, it is labeled as a writeup, not a log. Logs are written while it still hurts.
