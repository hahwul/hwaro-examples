## The Premise

Language is not merely sound arranged in time. It is also shape arranged in space. The poem on the page is already an image before it is read; the eye moves before the mind does. This journal exists in that interval.

*Concrete Poetry* takes its name from the mid-century movement that asked a simple, radical question: what if the visual arrangement of letters carried meaning equal to — or greater than — the words themselves?

---

## What You Will Find Here

This is not a blog about concrete poetry in the historical sense, though that tradition echoes through every post. It is a blog written by someone who cannot stop thinking about:

- The weight of white space between stanzas
- Why a justified margin feels authoritarian and a ragged right feels free
- What happens when you rotate a word ninety degrees
- The **letter** as a unit of visual composition, not just phonetic value
- Silence as structure — the unsaid as load-bearing wall

---

## The Approach

Each post is composed with the page in mind. Headings are not markers for navigation; they are typographic events. A blockquote is a shifted breath. A horizontal rule is a deliberate pause — not a decoration but a full stop with more room around it.

Read slowly. Let the shapes arrive before the meanings do.
