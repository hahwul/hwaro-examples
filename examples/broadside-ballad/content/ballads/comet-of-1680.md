<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">I.</span>
A sign and wonder in the sky,
Good Christians, have appeared:
A blazing star of fearful eye
Hath many hearts affrighted.
</div>

<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">II.</span>
For three full nights its tail did stream
Across the northern air,
And men forsook their nightly dream
To watch with wondering stare.
</div>

<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">III.</span>
Some said it foretold war and dearth,
And plague to visit soon;
Some said it was of comet birth,
And merely passed the moon.
</div>

<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">IV.</span>
But wiser heads, on second thought,
Did counsel all to pray,
For whether by Dame Nature wrought,
Or God's commanding sway,
</div>

<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">V.</span>
The proper course for godly folk,
When portents fill the sky,
Is prayer and alms, and mending cloak,
And setting hearts on high.
</div>

---

## Apology of the Printer

The present verses were composed in haste, upon news of the apparition reaching this press by the common carrier from Cambridge. If any error hath crept into the narration, the printer begs the reader's patience, and promises a corrected impression should the blazing star return.
