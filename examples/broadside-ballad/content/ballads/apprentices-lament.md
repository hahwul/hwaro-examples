<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">I.</span>
My Father was a yeoman free,
Who held ten acres clear,
And bound me to a Mercer's fee,
In London, far from here.
</div>

<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">II.</span>
Seven years of service I did vow,
By solemn indenture,
To rise at six, to work at plough
Of needle, thread, and venture.
</div>

<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">III.</span>
But oh, alas, the city's charms
Did lead me far astray:
The bowling-green, the taverns' arms,
The theatre by day.
</div>

<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">IV.</span>
I robbed my Master of his till,
I pawned his cloth and lace,
Until one day a Constable
Did lay me by the face.
</div>

<div class="verse">
<span class="stanza-num">V.</span>
Now at the Guildhall stands my plea,
And at the Fleet I lie,
And all young men who learn from me,
Beware the tavern's eye.
</div>

## A Register of Lost Apprentices

This press hath printed many such ballads, each in remembrance of some particular youth. The names are not always preserved, for the apprentice, being bound only by indenture and not enrolled upon the public records, may pass into obscurity unless his crime or his verse preserve him.
