A gentle arc of five stars with three teeth hanging below — and a conspicuous gap where the fourth tooth should be. The islanders never repaired it. The break is the point.
<!-- more -->

## The figure

The spine of the Comb arcs across the narrowest plate in the folio, anchored by **Chambit**, the great star at its centre. Three *jan-byeol* hang beneath it as teeth. Beneath Chambit, where symmetry demands a fourth, there is only a wisp of light: a star so faint that Mun pricked it at half depth and noted, *"seen twice in thirty nights, both times by someone else."*

The story is a widow's story. A bride combing her hair on the night before the fleet sailed snapped a tooth from her mother's comb and hid the breakage, thinking it unlucky to mention. The fleet did not return. She threw the comb into the sky so that no one would ever again keep a bad omen politely to herself. On Darae's outer islands, delivering unwelcome news promptly is still called *showing the comb*.

## Named stars

- **Chambit** — *the true comb*. The central great star, used to check the clearness of the northern horizon before a crossing.
- The **Lost Tooth** — unnamed in the tradition, deliberately. Seeing it was held to be neither good luck nor bad, but a reminder.

## Observing notes

The Comb rides low over the northern sea in midwinter and never climbs high; haze swallows the teeth first, spine last. A night when all three teeth are visible was rated safe for the strait crossing — provided [the Tide Ox](@/charts/tide-ox.md) had risen horns-first that fortnight.
