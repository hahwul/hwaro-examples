There is a common misconception that silence is the absence of something. In deep space, beyond the magnetosphere, beyond the solar wind, silence has a presence that presses against the mind like water pressure at depth.

## The Hum That Isn't

Astronauts report hearing things in the vacuum. Not sound -- sound cannot travel where there is no medium -- but something the brain manufactures to fill the gap. The auditory cortex, evolved over millions of years to never stop listening, refuses to accept zero input. It invents.

This is perhaps the most human response to the void: to populate it with ghosts of sensation.

## Measured in Decibels Below

In anechoic chambers on Earth, the background noise level drops to approximately -9 dB. People who stay in these rooms for extended periods report hearing their own blood, their own nervous system. The silence becomes so complete that the body itself becomes audible.

> In the absence of external sound, you become the sound.

Deep space takes this further. There is no air to conduct vibration, no chamber walls to reflect it. The silence is absolute in a way that no terrestrial environment can replicate.

## What Remains

Strip away sound, strip away light pollution, strip away atmosphere. What remains is not nothing. It is a canvas so vast that the mind cannot process its edges. The silence of space is not empty -- it is full of distance.
