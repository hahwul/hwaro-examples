Jinju Bibimbap is a highly historic regional variety originating from the city of Jinju in South Gyeongsang Province. Legend has it that the dish was created during the Imjin War when military cooks prepared a quick, nourishing bowl for soldiers defending Jinju Castle. Often referred to as 'kkotbap' (flower rice) due to its striking, vibrant appearance, it is defined by the inclusion of 'yukhoe'—fresh, finely slivered raw beef rump seasoned with soy sauce, sesame oil, and garlic.

The base of Jinju Bibimbap is warm white rice topped with seasonal greens, blanched bean sprouts, and fernbrake (gosari). The raw beef is placed in the center, and its deep red color contrasts beautifully with the bright vegetables. A key characteristic is the serving of a rich, hot beef broth cooked with turnip and beef tripe alongside the bowl, which is used to wet the spoon and help mix the ingredients smoothly. The flavor profile is exceptionally delicate, relying on the quality of the beef and a seasoned gochujang paste that is slightly milder than standard preparations. The combination of cold, savory raw beef and warm, seasoned rice offers a unique culinary experience that is deeply tied to historical tradition.
