Dolsot Bibimbap is a beloved modern variation that elevates the texture of the classic rice bowl through heat. The defining element of this preparation is the 'dolsot'—a heavy, thick-walled stone bowl that is heated directly on the stovetop until sizzling. Before adding the rice, the interior of the hot stone bowl is coated with a generous layer of toasted sesame oil, which crackles pleasantly as the ingredients are assembled.

<!-- more -->

As the bowl retains its heat, the rice at the bottom cooks into a golden-brown, crispy crust known as nurungji. This texture adds a wonderful crunch that contrasts beautifully with the tender vegetables and soft rice above. Typical toppings include sautéed zucchini, seasoned spinach, carrots, seasoned beef, and bean sprouts. Because the bowl is extremely hot, a raw egg yolk placed on top cooks gently as the diner mixes the ingredients. The intense heat also slightly caramelizes the gochujang sauce, creating a complex, smoky flavor profile. Care must be taken when serving, as the hot stone bowl continues to sizzle throughout the meal, keeping the food piping hot until the very last bite.
