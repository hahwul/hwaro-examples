Tongyeong Seafood Bibimbap is a coastal specialty hailing from the port city of Tongyeong, located on the southern coast of the Korean peninsula. Surrounded by rich marine ecosystems, this variation swaps out the traditional beef and land vegetables for an array of freshly caught seafood. Rather than heavy sauces, the seasoning is kept minimal to highlight the natural sweetness and brininess of the ocean's harvest.

<!-- more -->

Typically, the bowl features blanched sea squirt (meongge), tender octopus, sea urchin roe (uni), and fresh local seaweed. The rice is kept warm but not hot, ensuring the delicate textures of the raw and lightly cooked seafood are preserved. Instead of the typical spicy gochujang sauce, Tongyeong Bibimbap is frequently served with a light dressing made from chogochujang (vinegared chili paste) or seasoned soy sauce with minced garlic and green onions. Sliced cucumbers and wild greens provide a refreshing crispness, while a drizzle of sesame oil binds the ocean flavors together. Mixing the bowl gently allows the sea urchin roe to coat the rice grains, creating a luxurious, creamy mouthfeel that is highly prized by seafood connoisseurs.
