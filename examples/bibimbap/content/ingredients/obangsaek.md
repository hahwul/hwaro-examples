Obangsaek is the traditional Korean philosophy of five colors that governs the aesthetic and nutritional composition of bibimbap. Rooted in Yin and Yang and the Five Elements, this color spectrum includes white, black, green, red, and yellow. Each color corresponds to a specific compass direction, a season of the year, a natural element, and, most importantly, a set of internal organs within the human body.

<!-- more -->

When preparing bibimbap, the selection of toppings is carefully balanced to include all five colors, ensuring a meal that is both visually stunning and nutritionally complete. White toppings, such as steamed rice, radish, or bean sprouts, represent metal and the lungs. Black toppings, like wood ear mushrooms, fernbrake, or seaweed, represent water and the kidneys. Green vegetables, such as spinach, wild greens, or zucchini, represent wood and the liver. Red ingredients, including carrots, chili paste, or beef, represent fire and the heart. Finally, yellow toppings, like mung bean jelly or egg yolk, represent earth and the spleen. This thoughtful alignment of color and nutrition makes bibimbap a holistic dining experience.
