This bestiary began as a private commission and was never meant to circulate past the patron who ordered it: a record, in the old illuminated style, of creatures said to live at the edges of the settled valleys — in the standing reed marsh below the causeway, and in the frost-locked passes above the last grazed pasture. The workshop's founding scribe intended twenty plates. Six survive in a state fit to show.

Each surviving plate follows the same discipline. The verso leaf carries a full-page drawing, ruled in gold and worked from field sketches rather than invention alone; the facing recto carries a scholarly description in the compiling scribe's own hand, including the disagreements between illuminators that the workshop chose to preserve rather than smooth over. Nothing here claims to be complete. What follows is what was actually finished, in the order it was finished, bound as found.

Begin with the first plate below, or turn directly to the [full table of plates](/chapters/). The [colophon](/colophon/) records how the six were made and by whose hands.
