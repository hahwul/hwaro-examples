Six creatures survive from the working set. Each was drawn as a full plate on the verso leaf and glossed on the facing recto, in the order the workshop finished them rather than any taxonomy the compilers might have preferred. A marsh dweller and a mountain dweller often share a spread by accident of scheduling, which the guild's supervising scribe considered a fault; readers four centuries on have generally disagreed.

No attempt has been made, here or elsewhere in this binding, to impose a later order on the work — no grouping by habitat, no ranking by size or danger, no separation of bird from beast from serpent. The founding scribe's original proposal called for such a taxonomy, worked out across twenty entries before the commission ran short of time and patrons alike, but the six plates that survive were finished too unevenly, and too far apart, for any of that scheme to be honestly reconstructed. What is offered instead is simpler and, the compilers came to believe, more honest: the plates as the workshop actually produced them, one after another, each dated in its own hand.
