Registration for Perseid 2026 is open now and closes on 1 August, so that
catering and red-torch numbers can be counted ahead of the gate opening.
Three ticket tiers cover the party:

- **Full weekend pass** — all five sessions, camping for three nights, and a
  loaner red torch if you need one.
- **Peak-night pass** — a single day ticket covering arrival from midday on
  the 12th through the peak watch and departure the following morning.
- **Junior pass** — free for under-16s attending with a registered guardian,
  covering every session on the guardian's pass.

<!-- more -->

Every ticket includes hot drinks from the fire circle and mess tent, a
laminated observing chart for the shower's radiant and the season's brighter
deep-sky targets, and a place on the citizen-science tally sheet if you want
one. Loaner binoculars are available in limited numbers at check-in on a
first-come basis; if you need a telescope, the [arrival clinic](/sessions/arrival-clinic/)
is the place to have your own gear looked over rather than relying on a
borrowed one. See the [venue notes](/venue/) for parking, camping, and the
field's red-light rule before you travel.

## Code of conduct

- Red light only on the field after dusk — no exceptions for "just a second."
- Keep voices low once the counting sector is active; a quiet "meteor" and a
  pointed arm is all a call-out needs.
- No flash photography anywhere within sight of the observing field.
- Respect the marshals' calls on parking, quiet zones, and weather changes —
  they are watching the forecast so you do not have to.

Booking is handled through the farm office; a confirmation and a full kit
list follow once your ticket is paid. Places are limited to the field's
capacity, and the weekend has sold out in each of the last two years.
