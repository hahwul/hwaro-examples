Gates open at four, but the clinic proper starts once the trailers are parked
and the threshing barn has been swept out for the evening. Bring whatever you
own — a department-store refractor, an inherited Newtonian, a pair of
binoculars that have never left their case — and one of our four resident
opticians will get it collimated, its finder aligned, and its dew heater
tested before the sky is dark enough to matter.

<!-- more -->

This is also the only hour of the party when white light is allowed on the
field itself, so it is the hour to sort out everything you cannot do by red
torch: labelling eyepiece cases, charging power banks, taping down loose
tripod legs. At half past eight the site marshal walks the barn to explain
where the quiet zones sit, which gate the late arrivals should use, and why
your phone screen is the single most disruptive object on a dark-sky field.
Stew is served at the mess tent from seven; the fire circle is lit once the
last white torch is switched off, and after that, everything on Blaen Onnen
runs by starlight and red glass alone. From here, the field moves straight
into the [Opening Watch](/sessions/opening-watch/).
