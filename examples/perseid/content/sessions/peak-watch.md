This is the night the party is built around. The waning crescent moon sets
before the sky is fully dark, leaving Blaen Onnen's Bortle 3 ceiling entirely
to the shower, and the forecast zenithal hourly rate sits close to a hundred
— roughly one meteor every forty seconds for anyone facing a clear, unobstructed
sky.

<!-- more -->

The field is laid out in three sectors this time: a counting sector running
an International Meteor Organization-style tally under Ceri's direction, a
quiet sector for anyone who would rather watch than log, and an imaging
corner where tracked cameras run long wide-field exposures stacking toward a
composite of the whole night's trails. Call-outs follow the field's usual
etiquette — a quiet "meteor" and a pointed arm, nothing louder, so as not to
startle anyone mid-exposure. Ceri gives a short midnight address from the
fire circle on what the count is telling us so far, and the last committed
watchers stay through to twilight, when the sky pales from black to the deep
blue that means the peak has, for another year, passed. Expect fireballs;
expect at least one gasp from the whole field at once.
