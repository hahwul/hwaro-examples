Perseid gathers a few hundred observers on a hill farm inside the Brecon
Beacons Dark Sky Reserve for three nights timed to the meteor shower of the
same name. There is no stage, no amplified sound, and no white light on the
field after dusk — just a run of clinics and watches plotted from first dark
to last star, and a sky dark enough to make the plotting worthwhile.

The party keeps its programme deliberately small: one optics clinic, three
overnight watches, and a dawn session for the shower's last, longest
meteors. Everything else — the fire circle, the mess tent, the red-light
rule that holds from dusk until the last car leaves — exists to protect the
few hours each night when the sky is worth the drive.

Read the [venue notes](/venue/) before you pack the car, and
[register](/register/) early — the field has sold out in each of the last
two years.
