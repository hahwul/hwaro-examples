Perseid is held at Blaen Onnen, a working hill farm on the western edge of
the Fforest Fawr Geopark, inside the Brecon Beacons Dark Sky Reserve. The
observing field sits above the farmhouse on a shoulder of open pasture with
an unobstructed horizon from north-east through south — the direction
Perseus needs, and the direction from which the reserve's darkest sky
arrives.

## Getting there and settling in

Cars park in the lower yard, a five-minute walk below the field; headlights
must be switched off at the cattle grid, well before the observing area
comes into view. Tents, vans, and roof tents are all welcome on the camping
paddock next to the yard — there is no mains hook-up, so bring batteries and
power banks charged before you arrive, since generators are not permitted
anywhere on site. Composting toilets and a water point sit beside the barn,
which doubles as the mess tent, the wet-weather lecture room, and the only
place white light is allowed after dusk.

## Keeping the field dark

Every session runs on a strict red-light rule: no white torches, no unshielded
phone screens, no headlamps above amber, once the sun is down. Red-filtered
torches are issued at check-in for anyone who does not already own one, and
dimming your phone screen and covering it with the supplied red gel takes
thirty seconds and is checked, gently but firmly, by the marshals on the
gate. The field is graded but uneven underfoot; a step-free hardstanding area
beside the barn is kept clear for anyone who needs level ground for a chair,
a scope, or simply to stand. If cloud rolls in, sessions move indoors to the
barn for a talk rather than being cancelled outright.
