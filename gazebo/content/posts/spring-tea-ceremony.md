With the first crocuses pushing through the soil and the magnolia tree in full blossom, we held our annual spring tea ceremony in the walled garden. A round table was set beneath the arbour, dressed with a pale linen cloth, vintage porcelain cups, and a centerpiece of freshly picked daffodils and hellebores arranged in a low copper bowl.

The tea selection honoured the season: a first-flush Darjeeling with its bright, muscatel notes; a delicate white peony tea from Fujian; and a house blend of chamomile and lemon verbena harvested from the kitchen garden the previous autumn. Alongside the tea, we served finger sandwiches of watercress and cream cheese, warm scones with clotted cream and rhubarb compote, and a layered Victoria sponge adorned with crystallised violets.

Birdsong provided the only accompaniment as guests sipped and spoke in unhurried tones. The afternoon passed slowly, as spring afternoons should, and by the time the last cup was poured the shadows had grown long across the garden beds. Each guest departed with a small muslin sachet of the house blend, tied with twine and a sprig of rosemary, a reminder that the simplest rituals can hold the deepest pleasures.
