## About the Cauldron

The Cauldron is a reference compendium for the working alchemist. Every recipe within has been tested, verified, and documented according to the standards of the Third Circle of Practical Alchemy.

### How to Read a Recipe

Each recipe entry follows a consistent format:

- **Ingredients** are listed with precise measurements where available
- **Preparation steps** are numbered in order of execution
- **Warnings** note dangerous interactions or common mistakes
- **Variations** describe regional or seasonal alternatives

### Difficulty Ratings

Recipes are categorized by the skill required:

- **Apprentice** -- Suitable for beginners with basic equipment
- **Journeyman** -- Requires intermediate knowledge and a proper workshop
- **Master** -- Demands expert technique and rare components
- **Forbidden** -- Restricted knowledge; attempt at your own peril

### Equipment

A standard alchemist's workshop should include at minimum: a cast iron cauldron (3-gallon capacity), a mortar and pestle of unglazed stone, glass vials in assorted sizes, a silver stirring rod, and a reliable heat source. More advanced recipes may require specialized apparatus noted in their entries.
