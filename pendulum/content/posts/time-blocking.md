Most people plan their days with a to-do list. The problem is that lists have no relationship to time. Time blocking fixes this by giving every hour a job.

<!-- more -->

## How Time Blocking Works

Instead of a list of tasks, you assign each task to a specific block on your calendar. If a task needs two hours, it gets a two-hour block. No exceptions.

### Rules for Effective Time Blocking

1. **Be honest about duration.** If something takes 45 minutes, do not squeeze it into 30.
2. **Include buffer time.** Meetings run over. Tasks take longer. Plan for it.
3. **Batch similar work.** Group emails, calls, and admin tasks together.
4. **Protect your deep work blocks.** These are sacred. Decline meetings that conflict.

## A Sample Day

| Time | Block |
|------|-------|
| 08:00-08:30 | Morning routine and planning |
| 08:30-10:00 | Deep work: primary project |
| 10:00-10:20 | Break |
| 10:20-11:50 | Deep work: secondary project |
| 11:50-12:30 | Lunch |
| 12:30-13:30 | Email and communications |
| 13:30-15:00 | Meetings |
| 15:00-15:20 | Break |
| 15:20-16:50 | Deep work or collaborative work |
| 16:50-17:00 | End-of-day review |

## When Plans Break

They will. The goal is not perfection — it is awareness. When you know what you planned, you can consciously decide what to sacrifice.
