A weekly review is the single most important habit for staying on track. It takes 30 minutes and saves you hours of misdirected effort.

<!-- more -->

## The Three Phases

### Phase 1: Clear (10 minutes)

Empty your inbox — email, notes, task captures. Process each item:

- **Delete** if irrelevant
- **Delegate** if someone else should handle it
- **Do it now** if it takes under 2 minutes
- **Defer** to your task list with a due date

### Phase 2: Review (10 minutes)

Check your calendar for the coming week. Review your active projects. Ask yourself:

- What are my top 3 priorities this week?
- What deadlines are approaching?
- What commitments did I make to others?

### Phase 3: Plan (10 minutes)

Block time on your calendar for deep work sessions. Assign tasks to specific days. Identify the single most important task for Monday morning.

## When to Do It

Friday afternoon or Sunday evening. Pick one and protect that slot. The weekly review is non-negotiable.
