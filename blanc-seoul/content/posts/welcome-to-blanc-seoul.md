## A Theme Born from Light

Seoul is a city of contrasts. Ancient palaces stand beside glass towers. Narrow alleyways open into sweeping plazas. And in every corner, there is a deep appreciation for the balance between presence and absence.

Blanc Seoul captures this spirit in a web theme. Where many modern themes chase darkness and neon, this theme steps into the light, finding richness in restraint and elegance in simplicity.

## The Meaning of Blanc

**Blanc** is the French word for white. We chose it not merely for the light color palette, but for what white represents in Korean aesthetics: purity, openness, and the canvas upon which meaning is drawn.

> In Korean philosophy, empty space is not the absence of content. It is where content breathes.

## Design Principles

The theme is built on three core principles:

1. **Clarity** — Every element serves a purpose. No decoration exists without reason.
2. **Warmth** — The color palette draws from natural materials: parchment, ink, vermillion, and aged gold.
3. **Rhythm** — Typography and spacing create a reading experience that flows naturally, like a well-composed page of calligraphy.

## The Color Story

The accent color, a warm vermillion (`#D64933`), is drawn from **juhong**, the traditional Korean red found in temple paintings and royal seals. It appears sparingly, making each instance meaningful.

Gold (`#B69D6E`) serves as a secondary accent, evoking the metallic details found in traditional Korean craftswork — from celadon pottery inlays to palace door fittings.

## What You Will Find Here

This blog explores the intersection of Korean aesthetics and modern design. Posts cover architecture, typography, color theory, and the design philosophies that shape how we experience digital spaces.

Welcome to Blanc Seoul. We hope the reading feels as good as the looking.
