## Two Timelines, One City

Walk through Seoul for an hour and you will cross centuries. The curved eaves of Gyeongbokgung Palace give way to the angular geometry of Dongdaemun Design Plaza. A hanok village nestles in the shadow of a 555-meter skyscraper. This is not contradiction — it is conversation.

## The Hanok Principle

Traditional Korean architecture, the **hanok**, teaches a fundamental lesson: a building should respond to its environment, not impose upon it.

Key characteristics of hanok design:

- **Ondol** — Underfloor heating that shapes the entire spatial layout
- **Madang** — The central courtyard that brings sky into the home
- **Dancheong** — Decorative painting using five cardinal colors
- **Chunyeo** — Eaves designed to admit winter sun and block summer heat

These are not mere aesthetic choices. Each serves a functional purpose, and the beauty emerges from that function.

## The Modern Response

Contemporary Seoul architecture takes these principles into new materials and scales:

| Traditional | Modern Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Curved roof tiles | Flowing parametric facades |
| Central courtyard | Atrium and void spaces |
| Natural ventilation | Double-skin curtain walls |
| Ondol heating | Radiant floor systems |
| Wood and stone | Steel and glass |

The Dongdaemun Design Plaza by Zaha Hadid is perhaps the most visible example: a building without corners, flowing like water, that nonetheless holds echoes of the curved rooflines that have defined Korean architecture for millennia.

## Lessons for Digital Design

Architecture teaches web designers to think about:

- **Threshold** — How do users enter your space? The header is your front gate.
- **Flow** — How does movement through pages feel? Navigation is your corridor.
- **Proportion** — What is the relationship between content and negative space?
- **Material** — Typography, color, and shadow are the materials of digital architecture.

Seoul's built environment reminds us that the most enduring designs honor what came before while reaching toward what comes next.
