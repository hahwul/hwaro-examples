## A Script Designed, Not Evolved

Most writing systems evolved gradually over centuries, accumulating irregularities along the way. Hangul is different. Created in 1443 by King Sejong and his scholars, it was designed from first principles to be logical, learnable, and visually balanced.

This intentionality makes Hangul uniquely interesting from a design perspective.

## The Geometry of Hangul

Each Hangul syllable block occupies a square cell, composed of two to four individual letters (jamo) arranged in a consistent spatial structure:

```
  Initial consonant
  +----------+
  |  ㅎ  ㅏ  |  -> 하 (ha)
  |    ㄴ    |  -> 한 (han)
  +----------+
```

This block structure creates a natural grid rhythm in running text. Unlike Latin scripts, where letter widths vary dramatically (compare "m" and "i"), Hangul maintains a consistent visual density.

## Design Properties

Hangul possesses several properties that make it remarkable for typographic design:

- **Modularity** — 24 basic jamo combine into over 11,000 possible syllable blocks
- **Geometric consistency** — Circles, lines, and squares form the foundational shapes
- **Vertical and horizontal flexibility** — Text reads naturally in both orientations
- **Visual rhythm** — The block structure creates even spacing without kerning

## Korean Typographic Traditions

| Era | Style | Characteristic |
|---|---|---|
| Joseon Dynasty | Calligraphic | Brush-influenced, flowing strokes |
| Colonial Period | Mixed script | Korean-Japanese typographic fusion |
| Post-war | Modernist | Clean, geometric sans-serif |
| Contemporary | Variable | Expressive, experimental approaches |

## Type in Blanc Seoul

This theme pairs **Noto Serif KR** for Korean headings with **Cormorant Garamond** for Latin text. The combination works because both typefaces share a quality of refined elegance: high contrast between thick and thin strokes, and a sense of classical proportion.

> Good typography does not call attention to itself. It calls attention to the words.

The best Korean typography, like the best Korean architecture, achieves beauty through precision rather than ornament.
