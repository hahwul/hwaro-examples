## The Space Between

In the West, we often think of white space as "empty" — unused real estate that could hold more content, more features, more information. Korean aesthetics offers a different perspective.

**Yeobaek** literally translates to "remaining white." But its meaning runs far deeper than blank pixels on a screen. It refers to the intentional space that gives meaning, weight, and breathing room to the elements around it.

## A Painting with Half the Canvas Blank

Consider a traditional Korean ink wash painting (sumukhwa). The artist might paint a single branch of plum blossoms in the lower corner, leaving the vast majority of the silk empty. This is not laziness or incompleteness — the empty space *is* the sky, the air, the season, and the silence.

The viewer's mind fills the space with meaning. The painting becomes a collaboration between artist and audience.

## Yeobaek in Design

Applied to digital design, yeobaek suggests several principles:

### 1. Space Creates Hierarchy

When everything is dense, nothing stands out. A generous margin around a heading does more for hierarchy than increasing its font size.

### 2. Space Creates Rhythm

The alternation between content and space creates a reading rhythm. Like music, design needs rests between notes.

### 3. Space Creates Meaning

A single accent color on a white page has more impact than a dozen colors in a busy layout. Restraint amplifies.

### 4. Space Creates Comfort

Dense interfaces create anxiety. Spacious interfaces create calm. When we read comfortably, we read more deeply.

## In Practice

This theme applies yeobaek throughout:

- **Article pages** have wide margins and generous line height (1.85)
- **Post cards** use padding to let titles breathe
- **The hero section** places a few elegant elements in a field of white
- **Navigation** uses space rather than visual separators

> The space between things is not nothing. It is the most important thing.

The courage to leave space empty is the mark of a confident designer. It says: "I trust the content to speak for itself."
