## A City That Changes Its Colors

Seoul experiences four dramatically distinct seasons, each bringing a complete transformation of the city's visual character. For designers, this is a masterclass in how palette shifts can change the entire mood of a space.

## Spring: Soft Beginnings

Cherry blossoms line the streets around Yeouido and the palace walls of Changdeokgung. The city wears pastels.

**Spring palette characteristics:**
- Soft pinks and whites dominate
- Fresh greens emerge gradually
- Light is diffused and gentle
- Colors feel translucent, layered

In design terms, spring teaches us about **soft launches** — introducing color gently, letting warmth build rather than arriving all at once.

## Summer: Bold Saturation

The monsoon season brings deep greens, dramatic skies, and intense humidity that makes colors appear more saturated. Seoul in summer is lush and overwhelming.

**Summer palette characteristics:**
- Deep, saturated greens
- Dramatic contrasts between sky and foliage
- Rich earth tones after rain
- High energy, high intensity

Summer teaches **boldness** — there are moments in design when restraint should yield to impact.

## Autumn: Refined Warmth

This is perhaps Seoul's most celebrated season. The mountains surrounding the city — Bukhansan, Namsan, Gwanaksan — transform into tapestries of red, gold, and amber.

**Autumn palette characteristics:**
- Warm reds and deep golds
- Rich amber and burnt orange
- Cool blue skies for contrast
- A feeling of maturity and depth

Blanc Seoul's accent colors draw from autumn: the vermillion (`#D64933`) of turning maple leaves, and the gold (`#B69D6E`) of ginkgo-lined boulevards.

## Winter: Essential Clarity

Winter strips Seoul to its bones. The palace rooftops carry snow. The Han River reflects pale skies. Everything is reduced to essentials.

**Winter palette characteristics:**
- Cool whites and pale grays
- Dark branches against light sky
- Minimal color, maximum structure
- Quiet, contemplative atmosphere

Winter teaches the most important design lesson of all: **when you remove everything unnecessary, what remains is either beautiful or broken.** There is no place to hide.

## Designing with Seasons

A well-designed theme, like a well-designed city, should feel as though it belongs to a place and a time. The colors, typography, and spacing should tell a story that the viewer feels before they consciously understand it.

Seoul's seasons remind us that great palettes are not invented in a vacuum. They are observed, refined, and translated from the world we inhabit.
