## About This Project

Constellation is a field journal for amateur and professional astronomers. We document observations of stars, constellations, nebulae, and other deep-sky objects, providing structured data alongside narrative guides.

## What We Cover

- **Constellation guides** -- detailed maps and mythology for each of the 88 IAU-recognized constellations
- **Deep-sky observations** -- nebulae, star clusters, and galaxies visible through modest equipment
- **Seasonal sky reports** -- what to look for each season from mid-northern latitudes
- **Observational technique** -- tips on dark adaptation, equipment selection, and astrophotography

## Observation Data

Every post includes structured celestial metadata when applicable:

- **Magnitude** -- the apparent brightness of the object as seen from Earth
- **Distance** -- measured in light-years or parsecs
- **Classification** -- the IAU constellation or object type

## Equipment

Our observations are made primarily with a 10-inch Dobsonian reflector under Bortle class 3-4 skies. Binocular observations use 10x50 wide-angle units. All data is cross-referenced against the Hipparcos and Messier catalogs.
