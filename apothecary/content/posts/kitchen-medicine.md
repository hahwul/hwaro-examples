Before the pharmacy, there was the pantry. Many everyday kitchen staples have a long history of medicinal use, and they remain effective first-line remedies for minor complaints.

## Ginger (Zingiber officinale)

Fresh ginger is unmatched for nausea -- motion sickness, morning sickness, or digestive upset. Slice a thumb-sized piece and simmer in water for ten minutes. It also warms the body and supports circulation in cold weather.

## Honey

Raw honey is antimicrobial and makes an excellent wound dressing for minor cuts and burns. Mixed with lemon juice and warm water, it soothes sore throats. A spoonful before bed can ease nighttime coughs.

## Garlic (Allium sativum)

Raw garlic is a powerful antimicrobial. Crush a clove, let it sit for ten minutes (this activates the allicin), then swallow with water or mix into food. Useful at the first sign of a cold or for supporting cardiovascular health.

## Apple Cider Vinegar

A tablespoon in warm water before meals can improve digestion. Diluted, it makes a soothing gargle for sore throats. It also serves as a base for herbal oxymels -- vinegar-honey preparations.

## Turmeric (Curcuma longa)

Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant. The classic preparation is golden milk: simmer turmeric with black pepper (which improves absorption) in warm milk or a plant-based alternative. Useful for joint stiffness and general inflammation.

## Cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum)

Warming and gently antimicrobial. Cinnamon tea supports digestion and helps regulate blood sugar. Use Ceylon cinnamon (true cinnamon) for regular consumption, as cassia cinnamon contains higher levels of coumarin.
