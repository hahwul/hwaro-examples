Elderberry (*Sambucus nigra*) has been used for centuries across Europe as a winter tonic. Modern research supports its traditional use, showing it can reduce the duration and severity of colds and flu.

## The Preparation

This is a cooked syrup, which means the berries are decocted (simmered) to extract their deep purple compounds -- anthocyanins and other flavonoids that give elderberry its immune-supporting properties.

The spices are not merely for flavour. Cinnamon is warming and gently antimicrobial. Ginger supports circulation and has its own anti-inflammatory properties. Cloves are strongly antiseptic.

## Important Cautions

- Never use raw elderberries -- they contain cyanogenic glycosides that are neutralized by cooking
- Add honey only after the liquid has cooled below 40C to preserve its enzymes
- The syrup keeps for 2--3 months refrigerated
- Discard if you notice any fermentation or off smells

## For Children

Children over age 1 can take elderberry syrup. A typical dose is 1 teaspoon daily for prevention. Do not give honey-based preparations to infants under 12 months.

## Storage

Glass bottles with tight-fitting lids work best. The syrup can also be frozen in ice cube trays for longer storage -- thaw individual portions as needed.
