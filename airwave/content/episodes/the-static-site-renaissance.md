Static site generators have come a long way from basic template engines. Yuki Tanaka discusses the design decisions behind Hwaro, performance tradeoffs, and why the JAMstack philosophy still holds up.

## Topics Covered

- The evolution from Jekyll to modern static site generators
- Crystal as a language choice for performance-critical tools
- Template engines and the balance between power and simplicity
- Asset pipelines, image processing, and build-time optimization
- What comes after static: edge rendering and hybrid approaches
