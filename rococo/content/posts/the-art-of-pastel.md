The Rococo palette is unmistakable: powder pink, mint green, sky blue, lavender, and ivory—all softened and blended with white to create an ethereal luminosity. These colors were not chosen merely for decoration; they carried symbolic weight.

Pink suggested romance and tenderness. Blue evoked the heavens and serenity. Gold, applied as gilding on frames and moldings, signified wealth and divine radiance without the heaviness of solid metallic surfaces.

### Pastels in Modern Design

Today's design world has rediscovered the power of soft hues. From UI palettes to brand identities, the gentle touch of pastel colors communicates approachability, warmth, and sophistication—the very qualities that made Rococo interiors so beloved.
