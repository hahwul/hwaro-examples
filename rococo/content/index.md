Welcome to the Rococo salon—a digital homage to the 18th-century art movement defined by lightness, grace, and extravagant ornamentation. Here, asymmetrical curves meet soft pastels and gilded flourishes in a celebration of pure aesthetic delight.

The Rococo style emerged in early 18th-century France as a reaction to the heavy grandeur of Baroque. It favored intimacy over monumentality, playfulness over solemnity, and pastel hues over dark shadows.

### The Spirit of Rocaille

At the heart of Rococo lies the *rocaille*—the shell-like ornament from which the style draws its name. These flowing, asymmetrical forms appear throughout architecture, furniture, and decorative arts of the period.
