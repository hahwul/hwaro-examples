## About this publication

This monograph was composed and published using Hwaro, a static site generator. The text was set in the tradition of scholarly monograph publishing, where sustained argument and detailed technical illustration demand typography that supports extended reading.

## Typography

**Display faces.** Chapter titles and section headings are set in Crimson Text Bold and Libre Baskerville Bold -- authoritative serif faces that convey the weight appropriate to academic publishing. Their generous x-heights and moderate contrast ensure legibility at display sizes while maintaining the formal character expected of scholarly work.

**Body text.** The running text is set in STIX Two Text, a typeface originally developed for scientific and technical publishing by a consortium of scientific publishers. Its comprehensive glyph set includes mathematical symbols, and its design balances readability with the precision required for technical prose. Libertinus Serif serves as a fallback.

**Footnote apparatus.** Notes and references are set in STIX Two Text at a reduced size, with numbered markers in the body text linking to the note block at the foot of each chapter.

## Illustrations

All diagrams and plates are rendered as inline SVG with labeled callout annotations identifying structural members, force paths, and dimensional relationships. The plate-style full-page diagrams follow the convention of nineteenth-century engineering publications, in which technical illustration was considered integral to the argument rather than supplementary decoration.

## Production

No CSS gradients are used in the design. All decorative elements are produced with inline SVG. The color palette is drawn from natural materials -- parchment, walnut, and iron gall ink -- to evoke the material culture of scholarly publishing.
