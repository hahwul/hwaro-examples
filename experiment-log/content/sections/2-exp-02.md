The sole change from <span class="exp-id">EXP-01</span> to <span class="exp-id">EXP-02</span> was an increase in Cu loading from 0.30 wt pct to 0.47 wt pct, corresponding to a Pd:Cu molar ratio shift from 3:1 to 2:1. Pd loading was held constant at 1.50 wt pct. A fresh catalyst batch was prepared using the same co-impregnation protocol described in the [Protocols](/protocols/) section.

## 2.3 Results

### Conversion and selectivity

At 4 h TOS, <span class="exp-id">EXP-02</span> achieved a total butadiene conversion of 75.5 pct with a 1-butene selectivity of 84.6 pct. The yield of 1-butene was 63.8 pct.

### Comparison with EXP-01

The increase in Cu loading achieved the intended effect: n-butane selectivity dropped from 22.1 pct to 9.4 pct (-12.7 pp), while 1-butene selectivity rose from 72.4 pct to 84.6 pct (+12.2 pp). Total conversion decreased moderately from 80.3 pct to 75.5 pct (-4.8 pp), consistent with the expected reduction in hydrogenation activity when Pd surface sites are diluted by Cu. Despite the lower conversion, yield improved from 58.1 pct to 63.8 pct (+5.7 pp) because selectivity gains more than compensated.

### Stability

The EXP-02 catalyst showed stable performance over 8 h TOS. Selectivity varied between 83.8 and 85.2 pct with no trend. Conversion showed a marginal decline from 77.1 pct (1 h) to 74.6 pct (8 h), comparable to the rate observed for EXP-01.

## 2.4 Assessment and decision

The 84.6 pct selectivity represents a substantial improvement over EXP-01 (72.4 pct), confirming that Cu loading is an effective lever for suppressing over-hydrogenation. However, selectivity remains below the 90 pct target. n-Butane selectivity at 9.4 pct is still the dominant unwanted product.

Temperature-programmed desorption (TPD) of 1-butene from the EXP-02 catalyst (performed after the catalytic test) showed a desorption peak at 85 C. This suggests that reducing the reaction temperature below 100 C could further promote 1-butene desorption relative to secondary hydrogenation. At 120 C, the surface 1-butene population is in a kinetic regime where both desorption and further hydrogenation occur at significant rates. Lowering the temperature is expected to shift this balance in favour of desorption, because the secondary hydrogenation pathway has a higher activation energy barrier.

<div class="decision-node">
  <p><strong>Decision D2:</strong> Reduce reaction temperature from 120 C to 95 C while holding catalyst composition at Pd:Cu 2:1. The rationale is that the TPD data suggest the 1-butene desorption temperature (85 C) is well below the current reaction temperature, and reducing to 95 C should suppress the higher-barrier over-hydrogenation pathway while maintaining sufficient driving force for primary butadiene hydrogenation.</p>
