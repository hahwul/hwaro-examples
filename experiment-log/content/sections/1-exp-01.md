## 1.1 Rationale for the baseline

The starting point for the sequential optimisation was informed by the existing literature on Pd-based catalysts for selective hydrogenation of conjugated dienes. Monometallic Pd/Al2O3 catalysts typically achieve 60-70 pct selectivity toward monoenes at moderate temperatures (100-140 C) but suffer from over-hydrogenation to alkanes at higher conversions. Bimetallic Pd-Cu systems have been reported to improve selectivity by modifying the d-band electronic structure of Pd surface atoms, thereby weakening the adsorption energy of the monoene product and promoting desorption before further hydrogenation occurs.

The Pd:Cu 3:1 molar ratio was selected as the baseline because it represents the lower end of Cu addition in the literature, providing a conservative starting point from which Cu content could be increased if selectivity remained insufficient.

## 1.3 Results

### Conversion and selectivity

At 4 h TOS, <span class="exp-id">EXP-01</span> achieved a total butadiene conversion of 80.3 pct with a 1-butene selectivity of 72.4 pct. The yield of 1-butene was therefore 58.1 pct. The product distribution is detailed below.

### Time-on-stream profile

Selectivity was relatively stable from 2 h to 8 h TOS, fluctuating between 71.6 and 73.1 pct. No significant deactivation was observed over the 8 h run. Conversion declined marginally from 82.1 pct at 1 h to 79.8 pct at 8 h, consistent with mild coking.

## 1.4 Assessment and decision

The 72.4 pct selectivity falls short of the 90 pct target. The dominant side product is n-butane (22.1 pct), indicating excessive over-hydrogenation. Isomerisation to 2-butenes is minor (5.5 pct combined). This product profile suggests that 1-butene is adsorbing too strongly on the Pd-rich surface, undergoing secondary hydrogenation before desorption.
