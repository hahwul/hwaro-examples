Grange remains the benchmark for Australian fine wine. The 2017 vintage delivers the hallmark power and complexity that has defined this wine for over seven decades.

<!-- more -->

## First Impressions

Nearly opaque in the glass, with a deep crimson-black core. The nose is immediately commanding: dark plum, star anise, smoked meat, and cracked black pepper form the first wave. Beneath that lies dark chocolate, clove, and an intriguing note of salted licorice.

## On the Palate

The entry is dense and concentrated, almost chewy in its richness. Dark fruit, chocolate, and warm spice coat the palate with layers of flavor. The mid-palate reveals Grange's characteristic American oak signature — coconut, vanilla, and cedar spice — but the fruit has enough weight and complexity to absorb it completely.

## Power and Poise

This is a big wine by any standard, but it is not clumsy. There is a sense of deliberate architecture — each element placed with care. The tannins are firm and ripe, providing a robust frame. The acidity, while not the star, does its job in maintaining freshness and preventing the wine from becoming ponderous.

## The Finish

The finish extends well over a minute, with waves of dark spice, chocolate, and warm earth. There is a pleasant warmth that lingers without becoming hot.

## Pairing Suggestions

- Slow-smoked beef brisket
- Venison loin with blackberry reduction
- Aged cheddar with quince paste

## Cellaring Potential

Built for the long haul — 25-35 years easily. Will reward patience with increasing complexity and integration.
