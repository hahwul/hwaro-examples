Opus One consistently delivers one of Napa Valley's most refined expressions of the Bordeaux blend. The 2018 is generous, polished, and immediately appealing.

<!-- more -->

## First Impressions

Deep purple-black in color, almost inky at the core. The nose opens with an inviting cascade of ripe blackberry and cassis, followed by mocha, dried Mediterranean herbs, and a subtle violet perfume. The oak is present but well-integrated — toasty vanillin adding warmth without overwhelming the fruit.

## On the Palate

The wine enters with a plush, almost velvety texture. Ripe dark fruit dominates — blackberry compote, blackcurrant, and dark plum — but there is enough structure and freshness to keep things interesting. The mid-palate reveals coffee bean, dark chocolate, and a faint herbal quality that prevents it from becoming monolithic.

## New World Character

This is unmistakably Napa — generous, sun-kissed, and opulent. But what separates Opus One from many of its neighbors is restraint. The alcohol is well-managed, the oak judicious, and there is a sense of proportion throughout.

## The Finish

The finish is long and warming, with lingering notes of mocha, blackberry, and a pleasant dusty tannin. It leaves you reaching for the glass again.

## Pairing Suggestions

- Prime ribeye with herb butter
- Dark chocolate fondant
- Lamb chops with rosemary and garlic

## Cellaring Potential

Drinking beautifully now, with 15-20 years of evolution ahead. No need to decant extensively — 30-60 minutes opens it up nicely.
