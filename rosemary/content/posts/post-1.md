## A Simple Roast, Done Right

There is no recipe more fundamental to a cook's repertoire than a well-roasted chicken. This version leans heavily on fresh rosemary, which pairs naturally with the rich, savory flavor of chicken skin crisped in a hot oven.

The key is patience. Let the chicken come to room temperature before it goes into the oven. Season generously. And resist the urge to open the oven door too often.

## Ingredients

- 1 whole chicken, about 1.8 kg (4 lbs)
- 4 sprigs fresh rosemary
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 1 whole lemon, halved
- 1 head of garlic, halved crosswise
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- Flaky sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, softened

## Method

1. Preheat your oven to 220C (425F). Pat the chicken thoroughly dry with paper towels. This is the single most important step for crisp skin.

2. Season the cavity with salt and pepper. Stuff it with the lemon halves, garlic head, and half of the rosemary and thyme sprigs.

3. Rub the outside of the chicken with softened butter, then drizzle with olive oil. Season generously with salt and pepper. Tuck the remaining herb sprigs under the twine or simply lay them over the breast.

4. Place the chicken breast-side up in a cast iron skillet or roasting pan. Roast for 1 hour and 15 minutes, or until the juices run clear when you pierce the thigh and the skin is deeply golden.

5. Rest the chicken for 15 minutes before carving. This is not optional -- the resting period allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat.

## Notes on Herbs

Fresh rosemary is essential here. Dried rosemary has a dusty, almost medicinal quality that cannot substitute for the bright, piney fragrance of the fresh herb. If you grow your own, harvest it just before cooking for the best flavor.

The thyme is a quieter companion, adding an earthy warmth that rounds out the rosemary without competing with it.
