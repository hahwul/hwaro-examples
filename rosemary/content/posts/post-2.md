## The Art of Restraint

Lavender in baking requires a gentle hand. Too much and the flavor veers toward perfume; too little and you wonder why you bothered. This shortbread strikes the balance, using just enough dried culinary lavender to create a floral whisper that complements the butter and honey without overwhelming them.

## Ingredients

- 225 g (1 cup) unsalted butter, at room temperature
- 100 g (1/2 cup) powdered sugar
- 2 tablespoons honey
- 1 tablespoon dried culinary lavender, finely chopped
- 280 g (2 1/4 cups) all-purpose flour
- 1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

## Method

1. In a large bowl, cream the butter and powdered sugar until pale and fluffy, about 3 minutes. Add the honey and vanilla and beat until combined.

2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, and chopped lavender.

3. Add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture in two additions, mixing on low speed just until the dough comes together. Do not overmix.

4. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and shape it into two logs, each about 5 cm (2 inches) in diameter. Wrap tightly in parchment paper and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

5. Preheat the oven to 160C (325F). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.

6. Slice the dough into rounds about 8 mm (1/3 inch) thick. Arrange on the prepared sheets with a little space between each cookie.

7. Bake for 22 to 25 minutes, until the edges are just barely golden. The centers should still look slightly pale. They will firm up as they cool.

8. Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack.

## A Note on Lavender

Use only culinary-grade lavender, never the ornamental variety sold for sachets and potpourri, which may have been treated with chemicals. English lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) has the sweetest, most balanced flavor for baking.

Chop the buds finely so that no single bite delivers an overpowering burst of flavor. The lavender should be a suggestion, not a statement.
