Current satellite view reveals a perfectly circular eye, a rare and indicative sign of a stable, high-energy cyclone system. Optical and thermal imagery have been combined to provide a comprehensive look into the storm's structure.

## Thermal Inversion

Notice the extreme temperature difference between the eye and the surrounding cloud tops. Thermal imagery shows the eye is up to 10 degrees Celsius warmer than the outer eyewall.

1. **Spectral Analysis**: Blue spectrum indicates high-altitude ice crystals.
2. **Thermal Gradient**: Sharp contrast between the warm core and cold convective bands.
3. **Rotational Pattern**: Visible spiral arms extending 500 kilometers from the center.

Further analysis is being conducted on the storm's intensification as it crosses the warmer ocean current.
