A compact bottle opener forged from mild steel flat bar with a thin copper strip sandwiched in a forge weld for a visible accent line.

<!-- more -->

  <tr><th>Material</th><td>1/4" x 1" mild steel flat bar, 1/16" copper sheet</td></tr>

## Process

<div class="process-step">
  <div class="process-step-num">Step 01 -- Stock Preparation</div>
  Cut two 5-inch lengths of flat bar and one matching piece of copper sheet. Cleaned all mating surfaces with a flap disc to bare metal. Copper must be free of oxidation for a clean weld.
</div>

<div class="process-step">
  <div class="process-step-num">Step 02 -- Forge Welding</div>
  Sandwiched the copper between the steel pieces, wired them together. Applied borax flux and brought the assembly to welding heat -- bright yellow, just below the sparking point. Three solid hammer blows on the anvil face to set the weld, then worked it down with lighter blows.
</div>

<div class="process-step">
  <div class="process-step-num">Step 03 -- Shaping</div>
  Drew out the handle end to a tapered point. Forged the opener end wider and flatter. Used a hot cut to create the bottle cap slot, then refined the hook geometry with files.
</div>

<div class="process-step">
  <div class="process-step-num">Step 04 -- Grinding and Finishing</div>
  Ground the faces flat on the belt grinder to reveal the copper line. The contrast between dark steel and bright copper shows clearly after grinding. Filed all edges smooth.
</div>

<div class="process-step">
  <div class="process-step-num">Step 05 -- Patina and Protection</div>
  Soaked in a vinegar and salt solution for 30 minutes to force an even dark patina on the steel while leaving the copper bright. Sealed with paste wax after drying.
</div>

## Notes

Forge welding copper to steel requires careful temperature control. Too hot and the copper melts out. Too cold and the weld fails. The sweet spot is narrow -- practice on scrap first.

The forced patina gives a more even appearance than natural oxidation. It will continue to develop character with handling.
