There is something deeply satisfying about apple butter. It is not quite a jam and not quite a sauce -- it occupies its own category, thick and dark and concentrated with the flavor of cooked-down fruit. This version uses the oven rather than the stovetop, which means less stirring and a more even caramelization.

## Choosing Your Apples

The best apple butter comes from a blend of varieties. You want a mix of sweet and tart, firm and soft. A combination of Fuji, Granny Smith, and Honeycrisp works beautifully. Avoid anything too mealy -- you want apples with structure that will break down gradually rather than immediately turning to mush.

Plan on about five pounds of apples for a generous batch.

## Ingredients

- 5 pounds mixed apples, peeled, cored, and roughly chopped
- 1 cup brown sugar
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
- 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1 pinch of fine sea salt
- 1/4 cup apple cider

## Method

1. Preheat your oven to 300 degrees Fahrenheit. Toss the chopped apples with the brown sugar, lemon juice, spices, salt, and cider in a large Dutch oven or deep oven-safe pot.

2. Cover with a lid and roast for two hours, stirring once at the halfway mark. The apples will have collapsed into a rough sauce.

3. Remove the lid, stir well, and return the pot to the oven uncovered. Roast for another two to three hours, stirring every forty-five minutes. The mixture will darken steadily, thickening as the moisture evaporates.

4. When the apple butter is deeply brown, thick enough to mound on a spoon, and reduced by roughly half, remove it from the oven. Use an immersion blender to puree until completely smooth.

5. Taste and adjust -- a touch more lemon juice if it needs brightness, a pinch more salt to lift the flavors.

6. Ladle into clean jars while still hot. It will thicken further as it cools.

## Storage

Refrigerated, this apple butter keeps for three to four weeks. For longer storage, process in a water bath canner for ten minutes. Sealed jars will keep on the pantry shelf for up to a year.

## Serving Suggestions

Spread on warm toast with salted butter. Serve alongside sharp cheddar on a cheese board. Swirl into oatmeal or yogurt. Thin slightly with cream and use as a glaze for roasted pork. Gift in small jars with a handwritten label -- few things from the kitchen are received with more genuine appreciation.
