## The Chromatic Project

Chromatic is a visual journal dedicated to the phenomenon of chromatic aberration -- the beautiful failure of a lens to focus all colors to the same point. What physics considers an optical defect, we see as an artistic signature.

## The Photographer

Every image in this collection is an exploration of how light fractures when it passes through glass, water, and atmosphere. The work sits at the intersection of technical photography and abstract art, using prisms, vintage lenses, and deliberate mis-focus to isolate the red, green, and blue channels that compose every image we see.

## Philosophy

A perfect lens produces a perfect image. But perfection is sterile. The slight misalignment of color channels -- red drifting left, blue drifting right -- reveals the hidden structure of light itself. Chromatic aberration is not an error. It is a window into how vision actually works.

## Equipment

- Manual focus lenses from the 1960s-1980s
- Crystal prisms and dichroic filters
- Sensors modified for extended spectral sensitivity
- Post-processing that amplifies rather than corrects
