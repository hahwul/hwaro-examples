## The paper of record for the opening weekend

Blockbuster is a weekly trade published out of Hollywood and Vine since the autumn of 1953. It was founded the year Cinerama opened, and it has never missed an opening weekend.

We do one job, and we do it in print: we tell the town what is playing, who is carrying it, and whether the carpet will be walked. Everything else &mdash; reviews, profiles, weekend grosses, the gossip columns &mdash; belongs to other papers.

## What we cover

- **Premieres.** Every major theatrical opening in North America and, by arrangement, the principal European festival circuit.
- **Red-carpet calls.** Call time, venue, dress code, and attendance for each premiere.
- **Cast lists.** Principal billing for every film we cover, with studio credits.
- **Crew credits.** Director, producer, cinematographer, editor, and key department heads for every released picture.
- **Status changes.** Pictures that have been held, pulled, rescheduled, or re-titled after announcement.

## What we do not cover

- Reviews. We do not print them.
- Weekend grosses. These belong to the trade wires.
- Awards handicapping. The town has enough of these.

## The reel

Our pagination is borrowed from the projection booth. Each issue is a reel, and each film is a scene on the reel. Reel 01 opens the season in January. Reel 52 closes it on the last Friday of December. There are no holiday editions and no double issues; a premiere falling on New Year&rsquo;s Eve is printed on the 31st and circulated by hand.

## Trade subscriptions

Blockbuster is a trade weekly and is circulated only within the motion-picture industry. We do not sell single copies. A trade subscription requires a studio, agency, guild, or theatre-chain address and is payable in advance.

Write `press@blockbuster.example` for rate card and application. Applications are reviewed by the managing editor on a rolling basis.

## History

The paper was founded in 1953 by Delia Vance, a press agent for the Warner Bros. lot who resigned her position the week *Shane* opened and issued the first number of Blockbuster &mdash; four sheets, mimeographed &mdash; the Friday after. The paper was acquired by the Mars family in 1981 and remains under their editorial control.

Delia Vance is still listed on the masthead as Founder, and her desk on the third floor has been kept as she left it.

## House standards

- **Copy deadline:** Thursday noon for the Friday issue.
- **Errata:** Corrections are printed on the front page of the following reel, under the founder&rsquo;s standing rule that &ldquo;the town sees the mistake, so the town sees the amends.&rdquo;
- **Conflicts:** Writers may not hold stock in, or be related to, any studio they cover. The rule is enforced by the editor and has no exception.
