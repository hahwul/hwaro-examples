## Overview

LIFTOFF is the culmination of the ignition sequence. Hold-down clamps release, the vehicle begins its ascent, and tower clearance marks the transition from ground operations to flight operations.

## Liftoff Sequence

- T-00:00 -- Hold-down clamp pyrotechnic release command
- T+00:30 -- Positive rise rate confirmed
- T+02:00 -- Tower clearance confirmed
- T+05:00 -- Roll program initiated
- T+10:00 -- Throttle back for Max-Q
- T+60:00 -- Max-Q passage confirmed
- T+90:00 -- Throttle back to 100%

## Post-Liftoff Monitoring

Once the vehicle clears the tower, control transfers from the launch director to the flight director. Key monitoring parameters:

- Vehicle attitude and rate stability
- Engine performance margins
- Trajectory deviation from nominal
- Structural load indicators
- Communication link margins

## Mission Success Criteria

The launch is declared successful when:

1. All engines perform at rated thrust through MECO
2. Vehicle achieves target orbit parameters
3. Payload separation confirmed
4. All telemetry data captured and archived
