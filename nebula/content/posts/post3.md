## Orion's Crucible

At roughly 1,344 light-years away, the Orion Nebula is the closest massive star-forming region to Earth and one of the most studied objects in the night sky. Within its turbulent clouds, thousands of new stars are taking shape, their fierce radiation sculpting the surrounding gas into caverns, pillars, and shock fronts of extraordinary complexity.

### Artist Statement

Orion is where I first fell in love with the sky. Visible to the naked eye as a faint smudge below Orion's Belt, it was the first deep-sky object I ever observed through a telescope. That memory -- the shock of seeing structure where I expected only a blur -- is what this piece tries to recapture. The crucible is both the nebula and the moment of seeing.

### Technique

The foundation is an infrared mosaic compiled from multiple observatory datasets, revealing structures hidden by visible-light dust obscuration. Over this, hand-painted elements were layered to introduce the subjective warmth of human memory. Gold leaf accents reference the alchemical tradition of the crucible -- base matter transformed into something precious.

### Exhibition Notes

Winner of the Astral Visions Prize, 2025. This piece marks the first in a planned triptych exploring the Orion Molecular Cloud Complex.
