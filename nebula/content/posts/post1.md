## Pillars of Creation

This piece draws from one of the most recognizable images in astronomical history -- the Pillars of Creation within the Eagle Nebula, roughly 6,500 light-years from Earth. These towering columns of cool molecular hydrogen and dust are incubators for new stars, stretching approximately four to five light-years in height.

### Artist Statement

In reimagining the Pillars, I sought to emphasize the tension between destruction and creation that defines these structures. The intense ultraviolet radiation from nearby young stars is slowly eroding the pillars even as new stars form within them. This duality -- birth and dissolution happening simultaneously -- felt deeply human to me.

### Technique

The base data was sourced from publicly available Hubble observations. Color channels were remapped to emphasize the boundaries between ionized hydrogen, sulfur, and oxygen. The resulting palette shifts between deep rose and spectral teal, revealing structure invisible to the naked eye.

### Exhibition Notes

First displayed at the Interstellar Arts Festival, 2026. This work has been acquired for permanent display in the Nebula collection.
