<div class="two-column drop-cap">

In the year that Senefelder's discovery reached its full maturity, the colour plate became an object of wonder upon every parlour table. Where once the engraver's burin had reigned, now the draughtsman drew upon Bavarian stone, and the pressman struck from four or five such stones in succession - red upon the first impression, yellow upon the second, green upon the third, blue upon the fourth, and the black key last of all to bind the flat colours together into a recognisable thing.

This cabinet gathers twelve such plates, chosen from our portfolio for their fidelity to the original draught. Each is guarded by a leaf of translucent tissue, lest the colours should offset upon the facing page. The student is invited to lift the tissue gently, study the plate, and replace the guard before turning on to the next.

The volume is printed upon a demi-quarto sheet of Whatman's wove, trimmed to the octavo when bound. The reader will find the plates numbered in Roman below each figure, and the Latin binomial beneath the English common name. A colophon at the close of the volume attests to the pressmen and the stones.
