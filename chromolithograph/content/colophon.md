  <h1 class="section-title">Colophon</h1>
  <p class="section-note">The names of those whose hands have touched this volume, from the quarrying of the stones to the trimming of the leaves.</p>

<div class="prose-page">

## The Stones

Quarried at Solnhofen by the house of Kellner und Sohn, and dressed upon our own premises at Holborn by four apprentices under the direction of our stoneman, Mr T. Birkhead.

## The Draughtsmen

Miss E. Hartwell (botanical subjects upon Plates I, III, VI)
Mr J. Wolstenholme (ornithological subjects upon Plates II, IV, VIII)
Rev. F. G. Whitlock (palaeontological subjects upon Plates V, X)
Dr H. Penrose (entomological subjects upon Plates VII, XII)
Miss C. Penrose (floral cultivars upon Plates XI)
Mr A. Tennyson (mineralogical subjects upon Plate IX)

## The Pressmen

Under the foremanship of Mr E. Carrick, with Messrs Tindall, Rodway, and Hemphill at the cylinder, and Mr W. Morley at the proofing of each separation.

## The Paper

Whatman's wove of the demi-quarto, supplied by Mr W. Balston's mill at Maidstone. Fifty copies upon India paper for presentation, the remainder upon a heavier ply for ordinary distribution.

## The Binding

Sewn upon five cords by the binders of Rivière and Son, and cased in half-morocco over cloth. The arms of the press, being a stone and two crayons crossed, are blocked in gold upon the upper board. The tissue guards interleaved by the house Bindery.

## Edition

Four hundred copies for the subscribers of the press, fifty upon India paper for presentation to the Society and the learned press, and twenty copies retained for the archive of the house. The whole edition numbered in the stone upon each plate, the copy number added by hand upon the colophon leaf.
