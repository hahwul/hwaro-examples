## Description

The amethyst geodes of southern Brazil occur in basaltic lavas of the Parana Traps, where they formed by the slow percolation of silica-bearing waters into gas bubbles within the cooling rock. The outer shell is commonly of chalcedony or banded agate; the inner cavity is lined with violet quartz, whose colour depends upon traces of iron and upon the exposure of the stone to radiation during its long repose underground.

The specimen figured was broken across the equator by the vendor in Belmonte, and the half retained for drawing shows the characteristic banding of the outer shell, the pale inner lining of pale quartz, and the violet points arranged inward toward the open cavity. The pressman has chosen to impress the rough outer surface from a single green stone, the banding of the chalcedony from the yellow stone, the violet quartz from a red stone somewhat diluted, and the background cavity from a blue.

## On the Colour of Amethyst

Professor Sorby, whose petrographical investigations have transformed the study of the minerals in our own lifetime, has demonstrated that the violet colour of amethyst is due to the presence of iron in a peculiar oxidation state, and that it may be discharged entirely by gentle heating, the stone assuming the citron yellow called sometimes citrine. Specimens which have been thus altered are offered in commerce as topaz, though they are of course nothing of the kind.

## Commercial Note

Specimens may be obtained from the dealers of Bahia, or at more elevated prices from the Hatton Garden houses. The English collector is advised to choose those stones whose violet is even and deep, and whose crystal points are free from chips.
