## Description

The peafowl of Asia has furnished the subject of ornithological plates since the earliest days of our craft, and no volume of coloured illustrations may omit it without reproach. The figure before the reader represents an adult cock in full train, drawn from a living specimen at Knowsley during the breeding season of last spring.

The train is not strictly the tail; the tail proper is a short structure concealed beneath, supporting the long upper tail-coverts which constitute the magnificent ocellated display. Each of these coverts bears, near its tip, an eye-spot - the pressman has rendered these from three stones: a yellow surround, a red inner ring, and a blue centre, bound together by the key.

## Upon the Colours

The iridescence of the living bird cannot be reproduced upon paper, however finely the stones are dressed; the pressman must be content with flat, separated colour, trusting that the arrangement of these flat fields will suggest the shimmer to the mind's eye. It is for this reason that our figures avoid the temptation of graded wash, and depend instead upon the honest confession of the printer's art.

## Habits

The species is largely granivorous, though taking insects and small lizards when opportunity offers. It roosts high in trees during the night and descends at dawn to water, calling as it goes with the well-known piercing cry from which its Greek name derives.
