# M4.7 Earthquake -- Gyeongju Offshore Basin

**Event ID:** TRM-2026-0403-0841

**Magnitude:** 4.7 ML
**Depth:** 12.3 km

## Summary

A magnitude 4.7 earthquake was recorded at 08:41 UTC on April 3, 2026, with the epicenter located approximately 28 km east-southeast of Gyeongju in the offshore basin region. The event was detected by all 12 stations in the Tremor monitoring network within 3.2 seconds of the initial P-wave arrival.

## Analysis

The focal mechanism indicates a predominantly strike-slip rupture consistent with the regional tectonic stress regime along the Yangsan Fault system. The shallow depth of 12.3 km places the hypocenter within the upper crust, which is typical for events in this region.

## Impact Assessment

At intensity V on the Modified Mercalli scale, this event would have been felt by most people in the Gyeongju metropolitan area. Light objects may have been displaced and some unsecured items could have fallen from shelves. No structural damage is expected from an event of this magnitude at this distance.

## Aftershock Forecast

Based on the Omori-Utsu aftershock decay model, approximately 3-5 aftershocks of magnitude 2.0 or greater are expected within the next 72 hours. The monitoring network has been placed on heightened sensitivity for the affected region.
