## THE MIDDLE ROUNDS

By Round 4, the data collection phase is over. The fighters know each other now. The scouting report is irrelevant. What matters is what happens in the ring, tonight, between these two men.

Vega starts working the body. Short left hooks under the elbow, right uppercuts tight to the chest. Okafor tries to circle out and finds his angle cut off. The trap starts to close.

### The Body Attack

Body shots do not show up on a highlight reel. They show up in Round 10, when a fighter's breathing changes, when his feet slow half a step, when his hands drop by an inch. Vega is investing. Every punch to the ribs is a deposit on a debt that will come due later in the night.

### Okafor Responds

But Okafor is a champion for a reason. He answers the pressure with a beautiful counter-hook over the top of Vega's jab. The crowd roars. Vega nods, acknowledges the shot, and comes right back.

This is the round where a fight becomes *a fight*. Not a sparring session. Not a measurement. A real, contested exchange between two men who have decided that the only way out of the ring is through.
