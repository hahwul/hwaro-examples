## WHERE FIGHTS ARE DECIDED

The middle-to-late rounds are where championship fights live and die. The early-round learning is done. The late-round finishing has not yet begun. The fighters are tired but not yet broken, composed but no longer calculating.

Okafor steps forward. Vega plants his feet and throws a three-punch combination &mdash; jab, right hand, left hook &mdash; that Okafor is not expecting. The left hook lands flush on the temple.

### The Moment

Okafor's knees do not buckle. His eyes do not glaze. But something in his posture shifts, something the broadcast cameras will slow down and replay a hundred times in the morning. He is still in the fight. But the fight has changed.

### The Corner Between Rounds

In the corner between the eighth and ninth rounds, the trainer does not say much. He cleans the cut. He pours water. He whispers three words:

> "You are fine."

That is all a fighter needs to hear.

### What the Judges See

The scorecards tighten. Two judges may have it dead even. The third may give Okafor a narrow edge based on earlier work. None of it matters now. There are four rounds left. This fight will be decided in the last three minutes.

*Championship fights turn on single exchanges. That was the exchange.*
