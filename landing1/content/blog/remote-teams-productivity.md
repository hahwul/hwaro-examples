Managing a remote team is more than just moving meetings to video calls. After working with thousands of distributed teams, we've identified the strategies that actually move the needle.

<!-- more -->

## 1. Async-First Communication

Not every conversation needs to happen in real-time. Encourage your team to default to written updates, recorded demos, and threaded discussions. Reserve synchronous time for brainstorming and relationship building.

## 2. Clear Ownership

Every task, project, and decision should have a single owner. This doesn't mean working alone — it means someone is responsible for driving progress and communicating status.

## 3. Visible Work

When you can't see your colleagues at their desks, work needs to be visible in other ways. Use shared boards, daily standups (async or sync), and progress dashboards to keep everyone informed.

## 4. Time Zone Awareness

Overlapping hours are precious. Use them for collaborative work and meetings. Use non-overlapping hours for deep focus work. Document decisions so no one wakes up confused.

## 5. Intentional Culture

Remote culture doesn't happen by accident. Schedule virtual social events, celebrate wins publicly, and create spaces for non-work conversations.
