The Andromeda Galaxy (M31) remains the most prominent celestial object in our local group. During tonight's observation, the central bulge showed exceptional clarity through the 12-inch Schmidt-Cassegrain telescope.

## Observation Log

- **Equipment:** SCT 12", 25mm eyepiece.
- **Conditions:** Bortle 2, Seeing 4/5, Transparency 5/5.
- **Notes:** The dust lanes were clearly visible extending from the nucleus. M32 and M110 satellite galaxies were also observed as distinct fuzzy patches.

The core region appears highly concentrated, with a sharp drop-off in brightness towards the outer halo. Spectroscopic analysis confirms a blue-shift, indicating its approach towards the Milky Way at approximately 110 kilometers per second.
