The Orion Nebula (M42) is one of the most studied and photographed celestial objects in the sky. It is a diffuse emission nebula situated in the Milky Way, south of Orion's Belt.

## Observation Log

- **Equipment:** SCT 12", 10mm eyepiece.
- **Conditions:** Bortle 4, Seeing 5/5, Transparency 3/5.
- **Notes:** The Trapezium Cluster (A, B, C, D) was resolved with exceptional clarity. The "wings" of the nebula showed extensive intricate detail with some hints of green tint due to ionized oxygen.

This is the closest region of massive star formation to Earth. It contains hundreds of very young stars (less than a million years old) and protostellar disks known as "proplyds".
