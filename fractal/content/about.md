## The Gallery

Fractal is a generative art gallery dedicated to the visualization of mathematical beauty. Every piece in our collection emerges from deterministic equations -- yet produces forms of staggering complexity and infinite detail.

## The Mathematics

Fractal geometry, pioneered by Benoit Mandelbrot in the 1970s, reveals that simple recursive formulas can generate structures of infinite complexity. The equation z = z^2 + c, iterated across the complex plane, produces boundary sets of infinite length contained within finite area.

## Our Approach

Each gallery entry explores a different class of fractal geometry:

- **Escape-time fractals** such as the Mandelbrot set and Julia sets, where iteration depth maps to visual structure
- **Iterated function systems** like the Sierpinski triangle, built from recursive self-similar transformations
- **L-systems and space-filling curves** that demonstrate how simple rewriting rules produce organic complexity

## The Infinite

What draws us to fractals is the principle of self-similarity across scales. Zoom into any boundary region and discover structure that echoes the whole -- a visual proof that finite rules can encode infinite detail.
