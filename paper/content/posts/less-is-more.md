Every feature you add is a feature someone has to understand. Paper deliberately leaves things out — and that's a feature in itself.

## No Dark Mode

This might be controversial. Most modern themes ship with a dark/light toggle. Paper doesn't, because its warm light palette *is* the identity. The cream background and soft brown text are already easy on the eyes, even in dim lighting.

If you need dark mode, there are excellent themes for that. Paper is for people who want a warm, print-like experience.

## No Sidebar

Sidebars add visual noise. They compete with the main content for attention. Paper uses a single-column layout, giving your writing the full stage.

Navigation lives in the header — simple and out of the way.

## No Cover Images in Lists

Unlike many blog themes, Paper's post lists show only text: title, date, and excerpt. This keeps the focus on the writing and makes the page load faster. You can still use images inside posts — they just don't dominate the homepage.

## The Result

Fewer features mean:

- **Faster load times** — less CSS, no JavaScript for theme toggling
- **Easier maintenance** — less code to break
- **Better focus** — readers engage with your content, not your UI
- **Timeless design** — trends change, but clean typography doesn't

Sometimes the best design decision is what you choose to leave out.
