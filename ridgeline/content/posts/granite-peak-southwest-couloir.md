## Overview

Granite Peak stands at 3,904 meters, the highest point in Montana and one of the most demanding state highpoints in the Lower 48. The southwest couloir route is the most commonly attempted line, but "common" does not mean easy. This is a serious mountain day that demands respect, good fitness, and careful judgment about conditions.

## Trailhead

Start from the Mystic Lake Trailhead near Fishtail, Montana. The approach follows the West Rosebud Creek drainage for roughly 10 kilometers before the real climbing begins. Arrive early. A 4:00 AM start is not unusual for summer attempts.

## Route Description

The first segment follows a well-maintained trail along West Rosebud Creek to Mystic Lake. The lake sits in a glacially carved basin surrounded by granite walls. From the lake, continue south on a climbers' trail that grows progressively fainter.

### The Froze-to-Death Plateau

Above tree line, the route crosses the Froze-to-Death Plateau, a stark and windswept expanse of broken rock. The name is not metaphorical. Navigation here requires attention even in clear conditions, as cairns are sparse and the terrain is disorienting. A GPS device with the route pre-loaded is strongly recommended.

### The Couloir

The southwest couloir is the crux of the route. Early in the season, expect consolidated snow that requires an ice axe and crampons. By late summer, the snow melts out to reveal loose, rotten rock that is arguably more dangerous. Pick your difficulty.

The couloir gains roughly 300 meters at angles between 35 and 45 degrees. Helmets are mandatory. Rockfall from parties above is a constant hazard. Climb early, stay alert, and do not linger.

### The Summit Ridge

Above the couloir, a short but exposed scramble along the summit ridge leads to the top. The final moves are Class 4 on solid granite, a welcome change from the loose rubble below. The summit view encompasses the entire Beartooth Range and, on clear days, reaches into Yellowstone.

## Conditions and Timing

The window for this route is narrow. Late July through early September offers the best conditions, though afternoon thunderstorms are a daily threat above 3,000 meters. Monitor weather obsessively and be prepared to turn around at any point.

## Gear Notes

- Ice axe and crampons (early season)
- Helmet (mandatory at all times above the plateau)
- 30-meter rope for the summit ridge if your party is not comfortable with exposure
- GPS device with the route pre-loaded
- Layers for sub-zero wind chill at the summit
- Bivy sack in case the day runs long

## Water Sources

Mystic Lake and several small streams along the approach. Above the plateau, there is nothing. Carry at least 3 liters from the last reliable source.
