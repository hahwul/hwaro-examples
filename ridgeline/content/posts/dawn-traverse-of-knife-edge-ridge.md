## Overview

Knife Edge is the most famous ridge walk in the northeastern United States. Connecting Pamola Peak to Baxter Peak on Mount Katahdin, this narrow arete runs for roughly 1.6 kilometers at elevations above 1,500 meters. The exposure is real, the rock is solid granite, and the views in every direction are enormous.

We chose a pre-dawn start to catch first light on the ridge. It was worth every minute of lost sleep.

## Trailhead

Start from Roaring Brook Campground in Baxter State Park. The park gate opens at a specific hour that varies by season, so check current regulations before planning your approach. Reservations are required for parking and camping.

## Route Description

### Approach via Helon Taylor Trail

The Helon Taylor Trail climbs steadily from Roaring Brook through dense boreal forest. The first few kilometers are straightforward, following well-blazed trail through spruce and fir. Above tree line, the terrain opens to reveal the full sweep of the ridge ahead.

The final push to Pamola Peak gains elevation quickly over granite slabs. In dry conditions the rock offers excellent grip. When wet, every step requires deliberation.

### Pamola Peak

Pamola stands at 1,451 meters and serves as the eastern anchor of Knife Edge. From the summit cairn, the full traverse is visible: a narrow spine of granite dropping and rising across three subsidiary peaks before reaching Baxter Peak at 1,606 meters.

At dawn, the ridge catches the first light while the valleys below remain in shadow. The contrast is striking. Pinks and oranges wash across the granite while the forest floor stays dark and cold.

### The Knife Edge Traverse

The traverse itself is not technically difficult in good conditions. The moves are Class 3 at their hardest, with solid hand and foot holds throughout. What makes Knife Edge serious is the exposure. The ridge narrows to less than a meter in places, with steep drops on both sides.

Wind is the primary hazard. Katahdin creates its own weather, and conditions on the ridge can deteriorate rapidly. If the wind makes you feel unstable, turn around. The mountain will be there next time.

The traverse takes most parties 1 to 2 hours, depending on comfort level and traffic. In peak season, you may encounter bottlenecks at the narrowest sections.

### Baxter Peak and Descent

Baxter Peak is the northern terminus of the Appalachian Trail and the highest point in Maine. On a clear day, the view extends across an unbroken expanse of forest, lakes, and low ridges that stretches to the horizon in every direction.

Descend via the Saddle Trail, which drops through the broad saddle between Baxter and Hamlin peaks. The trail rejoins the Chimney Pond area and eventually connects back to Roaring Brook Campground.

## Conditions and Timing

The ridge is typically accessible from late June through October. Snow and ice can linger on the north-facing sections well into July. Afternoon thunderstorms are common in summer, so early starts are strongly recommended.

## Gear Notes

- Sturdy boots with good ankle support and grippy soles
- Helmet (optional but sensible given the exposure)
- Wind shell and insulation layer even in summer
- Headlamp for the pre-dawn approach
- 2.5 liters of water minimum
- Trekking poles for the descent (stow them for the traverse)

## Water Sources

Roaring Brook at the trailhead, Chimney Pond if you route through the basin. Nothing on the ridge itself. Carry everything you need from the last source.
