## Overview

The Enchantments Traverse is one of the premier alpine routes in Washington State. Connecting the Stuart Lake Trailhead to the Snow Lakes Trailhead, the route passes through the Core Enchantment Zone, a landscape of granite slabs, larch groves, and turquoise lakes that sits above 2,100 meters.

Most parties complete this as a very long day hike, though overnight permits (won by lottery) allow a more measured pace.

## Permits

A permit is required for overnight stays in the Enchantment Permit Area from May 15 through October 31. The lottery opens in late February and competition is fierce. Day-use does not require a permit, which is why the thru-hike approach has become so popular.

## Route Description

### Stuart Lake Trailhead to Colchuck Lake

The first 6.4 kilometers follow a well-graded trail through old-growth forest to Colchuck Lake. The lake is stunning, but resist the urge to linger if you are on a day-hike schedule.

### Aasgard Pass

From the west end of Colchuck Lake, the route climbs Aasgard Pass, a 550-meter grind up loose rock and boulder fields. There is no trail. Follow the boot-beaten path up the drainage, staying left of the waterfall. This section gains elevation at a relentless pace and is the physical crux of the traverse.

Allow 2 to 3 hours for Aasgard Pass alone. Start before dawn.

### The Core Zone

Topping out on Aasgard, the Core Zone opens up before you. This is the heart of the Enchantments. Granite basins cradle lakes with names like Isolation, Tranquil, and Perfection. In September, the larch trees turn gold against the grey rock.

The route through the Core Zone is largely cross-country, following cairns and use trails between the lakes. Navigation is straightforward in clear weather but can become confusing in fog.

### Descent to Snow Lakes

Below the Core Zone, the trail becomes more defined as it drops through the Snow Zone and past Snow Lakes. The descent is long and relentless on the knees, losing over 1,800 meters from the high point to the Snow Lakes Trailhead.

The final section follows Icicle Creek on a well-maintained trail to the parking area.

## Logistics

This is a point-to-point route, so you will need a car shuttle or a willing friend. The two trailheads are roughly 20 minutes apart by car along Icicle Road.

## Gear Notes

- Trekking poles (essential for Aasgard and the long descent)
- Headlamp with fresh batteries (many parties finish after dark)
- Microspikes (early season, for lingering snow on Aasgard)
- At least 3 liters of water capacity
- Sun protection (the granite reflects intensely)

## Water Sources

Colchuck Lake, all lakes in the Core Zone, and Snow Lakes. Filter everything. The granite environment supports minimal vegetation for natural filtration.
