# About Ridgeline

Ridgeline is a journal of mountain travel. We document trails, test gear, and share route knowledge so that others can find their way through the high country with confidence.

## Our Philosophy

We believe that good trail information saves lives and makes the backcountry accessible to more people. Every trail report on this site is based on firsthand experience. We walk the routes we write about, in conditions that range from bluebird mornings to whiteout storms.

## What We Cover

- **Alpine routes** in the Pacific Northwest, Sierra Nevada, and Rocky Mountains
- **Gear testing** across four seasons and varied terrain
- **Navigation and safety** for intermediate to advanced hikers
- **Leave No Trace** practices and trail stewardship

## Trail Difficulty Ratings

Our posts use a straightforward difficulty scale:

- **Easy** -- Well-maintained trails with moderate elevation gain, suitable for most fitness levels
- **Moderate** -- Sustained climbs, some route-finding, or exposed sections that require attention
- **Strenuous** -- Long days, significant elevation gain, and terrain that demands experience
- **Expert** -- Technical scrambling, glacier travel, or remote routes with no bailout options

## Contact

Have a trail to recommend or a correction to submit? Reach out through our social channels or open an issue on the site repository.
