In East Asian art, four plants are revered as the **사군자 (Four Gentlemen)**: plum blossom, orchid, bamboo, and chrysanthemum. Each represents a season and a set of virtues.

## The Four Gentlemen

1. **Plum Blossom (梅)** — Winter. Resilience, perseverance, blooming against the cold.
2. **Orchid (蘭)** — Spring. Refinement, scholarly virtue, hidden fragrance.
3. **Bamboo (竹)** — Summer. Integrity, flexibility, upright character.
4. **Chrysanthemum (菊)** — Autumn. Nobility, longevity, defiance of frost.

## Why Chrysanthemum?

The chrysanthemum holds a special place because it blooms in late autumn when other flowers have already faded. This timing earned it the title **"flower of the noble hermit"** — one who maintains beauty and dignity even in adversity.

In Korea, the chrysanthemum appears in:

- **Royal seals** and court decorations
- **Traditional poetry** (시조, *sijo*) celebrating autumn
- **Festival cuisine** — chrysanthemum tea and rice cakes during the Double Ninth Festival (중양절)
