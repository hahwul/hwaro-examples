There is something inherently human about imperfection. When things align too perfectly, they can feel sterile and machine-made. Introducing intentional asymmetry into design breaks that sterility. 

It forces the eye to wander, to explore the space between elements, rather than just scanning straight down a page. This layout technique relies on careful balance—where negative space carries as much visual weight as the content itself.

### Tension and Balance

When you push an element off-center, you create tension. To prevent the layout from feeling broken, you must counterbalance it. A large image on the left might be balanced by a smaller, denser block of typography on the right. 

In this theme, we achieve balance through overlapping blocks and contrasting colors, anchoring the floating elements.
