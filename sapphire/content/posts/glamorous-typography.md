Typography is the backbone of glamorous design. The right typeface pairing can elevate a simple layout into something that feels luxurious and intentional — while the wrong choice can undermine even the most beautiful visual design.

<!-- more -->

## Serif and Sans: The Classic Luxury Pairing

The most enduring approach to glamorous typography pairs a refined serif for display text with a clean sans-serif for body copy:

- **Display serifs** — Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, or Libre Baskerville for headlines
- **Body sans** — Inter, DM Sans, or Source Sans Pro for readable body text

This contrast creates immediate visual hierarchy: the serif commands attention while the sans-serif ensures comfortable reading.

## Scale and Weight as Luxury Signals

Generous sizing and deliberate weight choices communicate premium quality:

1. **Large display sizes** — Headlines at 3rem or larger create dramatic impact
2. **Refined weight contrast** — Pair bold 700 headlines with regular 400 body text
3. **Generous line height** — 1.6 to 1.8 for body text gives a luxurious, airy feel
4. **Letter spacing** — Slightly tracked uppercase text for labels and metadata

## Colour in Typography

On dark backgrounds, typographic colour choices are critical:

- Primary headings in near-white (#eeedf5) for maximum impact
- Body text slightly dimmed (#c8c4d8) for comfortable reading
- Metadata and captions in muted tones (#7a7494) for hierarchy
- Accent colours (gold, cyan) sparingly for links and highlights

## The Details That Matter

Glamorous typography is about the small things:

- **Optical alignment** — Adjusting text to look aligned rather than being mechanically aligned
- **Hanging punctuation** — Quotation marks that extend beyond the text block
- **Proper dashes** — Em dashes (—) instead of hyphens (-) for parenthetical thoughts
- **Smart quotes** — Curly quotes ("") instead of straight quotes ("")

> "Typography is the craft of endowing human language with a durable visual form." — Robert Bringhurst

In glamorous design, typography isn't just functional — it's a primary visual element that sets the entire tone of the experience.
