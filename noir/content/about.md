# About Noir

This is a place for stories that live in the margins. Crime fiction, detective tales, and the kind of writing that keeps the lights on past midnight.

## What We Cover

**Crime fiction** — from classic whodunits to modern psychological thrillers. We examine the genre's darkest corners and most brilliant plot twists.

**Detective stories** — the private eyes, the reluctant investigators, the ordinary people pulled into extraordinary circumstances. We trace their footsteps through rain-slicked streets.

**Film noir** — the shadows on the screen that shaped a generation of storytelling. We revisit the classics and spotlight the modern heirs to the tradition.

**Mystery writing craft** — for those who want to build their own labyrinth of clues and red herrings. Technique, structure, and the art of misdirection.

## The Name

*Noir* is the French word for black. In cinema, it describes the shadow-drenched films of the 1940s and 50s — stories of moral ambiguity, femmes fatales, and doomed protagonists. Here, we carry that spirit into the written word.
